1. **Noun**: bed (C, pl: beds)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A piece of furniture designed for sleeping or resting, typically consisting of a mattress supported by a frame
   - *(Sense 2)* The base or foundation upon which something rests (e.g., riverbed, flower bed)
   
   **Synonyms**: sleeping place, cot, bunk, berth, resting place
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (functional object, no direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: Universal term for sleeping furniture; suitable for all contexts from casual home discussions to formal hospitality or medical settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When specific type is relevant (specify twin, queen, bunk bed, etc.)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - go to bed
   - make the bed
   - get out of bed
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English bedd (sleeping place, garden plot)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English bedd → Proto-Germanic badjam (sleeping place) → unchanged in Middle/Modern English
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I need to buy a new bed for the guest room.
   - Get into bed, it's late!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The facility provides hospital beds for all patients.
   - Quality sleeping beds contribute significantly to resident wellbeing.

1. **Noun**: mattress (C, pl: mattresses)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A large, rectangular pad filled with resilient material (springs, foam, air) designed to support the body during sleep
   
   **Synonyms**: sleeping pad, bed cushion
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (functional object)
   
   **When to Use**: When discussing sleeping surface specifically, separate from bed frame; common in retail, medical, and household contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to complete bed including frame
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - firm mattress
   - memory foam mattress
   - mattress protector
   
   **Root Analysis**: mattress from Arabic matrah (place where something is thrown)
   
   **Etymology**: Arabic matrah → Italian materasso → Old French materas → English mattress (13c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This mattress is way too soft for my back.
   - We need to flip the mattress this weekend.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The mattress industry has evolved significantly with memory foam technology.
   - Proper mattress selection directly impacts sleep quality and spinal health.

   **Antonyms**: N/A (structural component)

1. **Noun**: sleep (U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The natural periodic state of rest characterized by reduced consciousness and physical activity
   - *(Sense 2)* A period of sleeping (countable: "had a good sleep")
   
   **Synonyms**: slumber, rest, repose, shut-eye (informal)
   
   **Antonyms**: wakefulness, consciousness, alertness
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing rest, health, daily routines, sleep quality; universal term suitable for all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Context usually clarifies; medical contexts may require more specific terminology (REM sleep, deep sleep)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - get some sleep
   - lack of sleep
   - good night's sleep
   
   **Root Analysis**: sleep from Old English slǣp (state of sleeping)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English slǣp → Proto-Germanic slepaz → unchanged meaning
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I didn't get much sleep last night.
   - I need to catch up on sleep this weekend.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Adequate sleep is essential for cognitive function and overall health.
   - Sleep deprivation significantly impacts performance and wellbeing.

   **Examples [Casual]**:
