1. **Adverb**: comfortably (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that provides physical ease and relaxation; without discomfort or pain. (Can appear in mid or end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: cozily, restfully, easily, snugly, peacefully
   
   **Antonyms**: uncomfortably, painfully, awkwardly, stiffly
   
   **When to Use**: When describing how someone rests, sleeps, or positions themselves in bed; suitable for all registers.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in technical specifications requiring precise measurements.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - sleep comfortably
   - rest comfortably
   - lie comfortably
   
   **Root Analysis**: comfortable (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin confort → comfortable + -ly suffix → modern adverb form (15c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I can finally sleep comfortably with this new mattress.
   - She settled comfortably into the soft bed.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Patients should be positioned comfortably to facilitate recovery.
   - The ergonomic design allows users to rest comfortably throughout the night.

1. **Adverb**: soundly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a deep, uninterrupted manner; thoroughly and completely, especially in relation to sleep. (Typically end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: deeply, peacefully, thoroughly, heavily, well
   
   **Antonyms**: lightly, fitfully, restlessly, poorly
   
   **When to Use**: When describing quality of sleep; emphasizes depth and completeness of rest; neutral to formal register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in medical contexts requiring precise sleep stage descriptions.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - sleep soundly
   - rest soundly
   - sleeping soundly
   
   **Root Analysis**: sound (adjective meaning "healthy, undisturbed") + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English sund (healthy) → sound → soundly (13c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The baby slept soundly all night for the first time.
   - I haven't slept this soundly in months.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants reported sleeping more soundly after the intervention.
   - The optimal sleeping environment enables individuals to rest soundly.

1. **Adverb**: fitfully (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an irregular, interrupted manner with frequent stops and starts; restlessly and incompletely. (Typically end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: restlessly, intermittently, sporadically, irregularly, uneasily
   
   **Antonyms**: soundly, peacefully, continuously, steadily
   
   **When to Use**: When describing poor quality, interrupted sleep; common in literary and formal descriptive contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in positive marketing contexts; inappropriate for describing successful rest.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - sleep fitfully
   - doze fitfully
   - rest fitfully
   
   **Root Analysis**: fitful (adjective: fit + -ful) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: fit (sudden action) + -ful + -ly → modern usage emphasizing irregular patterns (16c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I slept fitfully last night because of the noise.
   - He dozed fitfully, waking every hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The patient slept fitfully throughout the evening, indicating continued discomfort.
   - Participants who consumed caffeine late reported sleeping fitfully.
