1. **Adjective**: comfortable (comp: more comfortable, sup: most comfortable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Providing physical ease and relaxation; pleasant to use or experience, especially in terms of furniture or sleeping arrangements. (Gradable, both attributive and predicative)
   
   **Synonyms**: cozy, restful, relaxing, snug, soft
   
   **Antonyms**: uncomfortable, hard, lumpy, stiff
   
   **When to Use**: When describing the quality of a bed, mattress, or sleeping experience; suitable for both casual and formal contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in technical specifications where precise measurements are needed; not appropriate when discussing medical-grade equipment requiring exact standards.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - comfortable bed
   - comfortable mattress
   - make oneself comfortable
   
   **Root Analysis**: comfort (noun) + -able (suffix meaning "capable of")
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → Old French confort (strengthening) → Middle English comfortable (late 14c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This bed is so comfortable I could sleep for days.
   - I need a more comfortable mattress for my back.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The hotel provides exceptionally comfortable sleeping accommodations.
   - Research indicates that comfortable bedding significantly improves sleep quality.

1. **Adjective**: queen-size (non-gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Designating a bed or mattress of standard medium-large dimensions, typically 60 x 80 inches (152 x 203 cm). (Non-gradable, attributive only)
   
   **Synonyms**: medium-large bed, standard double (regional)
   
   **Antonyms**: twin-size, king-size, single
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying bed dimensions in retail, real estate, hospitality; standard terminology across English-speaking markets.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not applicable as a predicative adjective; avoid when exact custom measurements are needed.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - queen-size bed
   - queen-size mattress
   - queen-size frame
   
   **Root Analysis**: queen (royal title) + size (dimensions)
   
   **Etymology**: Modern commercial term (20c.) derived from king-size, using royal hierarchy for sizing convention
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We just bought a queen-size bed for our bedroom.
   - Do you have queen-size sheets?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The master bedroom accommodates a queen-size bed with adjacent nightstands.
   - Standard hotel rooms feature queen-size bedding configurations.

1. **Adjective**: orthopedic (non-gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Designed to provide proper support for the bones and muscles, particularly the spine; medically designed for musculoskeletal health. (Non-gradable, attributive)
   
   **Synonyms**: therapeutic, medical-grade, supportive, ergonomic
   
   **Antonyms**: standard, conventional, unsupportive
   
   **When to Use**: When describing mattresses designed for back support and medical conditions; common in healthcare and specialized retail contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid as casual marketing term without genuine medical design features.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - orthopedic mattress
   - orthopedic support
   - orthopedic bed system
   
   **Root Analysis**: ortho- (Greek: straight, correct) + pedic (Greek: child, but evolved to mean "related to correction")
   
   **Etymology**: Greek orthos (straight) + paideia (rearing of children) → modern medical terminology (19c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My doctor recommended an orthopedic mattress for my back pain.
   - I switched to an orthopedic bed last year.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Orthopedic mattresses are clinically proven to reduce pressure points.
   - The facility provides orthopedic sleeping surfaces for post-operative patients.
