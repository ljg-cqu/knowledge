1. **Verb**: enroll
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive/Intransitive) To officially register or become a member of an educational institution or course
   - To add someone's name to a list of members or students
   
   **Synonyms**: register, sign up, join, matriculate, admit
   
   **Antonyms**: withdraw, drop out, leave, unenroll
   
   **When to Use**: Administrative and registration contexts; formal and informal discussions about starting school or courses
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For casual attendance without formal registration (use "attend" or "go to")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - enroll in a course
   - enroll at university
   - enroll students
   
   **Root Analysis**: en- (in) + roll (list, register)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French enroller (to register on a roll) → English enroll (14c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm enrolling in art class next semester.
   - Have you enrolled yet?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Students may enroll in up to six courses per semester.
   - The institution enrolled 2,000 new students this year.
