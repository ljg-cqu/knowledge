1. **Functional Expression**: Could I ask a question about...? (function: requesting clarification)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to politely request permission to ask for information or clarification
   - *Formality*: formal/neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: May I ask about...?, I'd like to know about..., Can I ask something about...?, I have a question about...
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (polite request)
   
   **When to Use**: Classroom settings with teachers/professors; when unsure about content; maintaining politeness with authority figures
   
   **When NOT to Use**: With close peers (too formal); when quick clarification is needed (be more direct)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could I ask a quick question?
   - May I ask about...?
   - I have a question about...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Uses negative politeness (minimizing imposition through indirectness); modal "could" softens the request; question form shows deference to instructor's authority
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Of course, go ahead.
   - Sure, what would you like to know?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Could I ask a question about the homework?
   - May I ask something about yesterday's lecture?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could I ask a question about the methodology section?
   - May I inquire about the assessment criteria?

1. **Functional Expression**: I'm not sure I understand... (function: requesting clarification)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Politely indicates lack of comprehension and requests explanation
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: I don't quite get..., Could you explain...?, I'm having trouble understanding..., I'm confused about...
   
   **Antonyms**: I understand perfectly, That's clear, I got it
   
   **When to Use**: When confused about course content; seeking clarification without appearing demanding; suitable for all academic contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you haven't tried to understand first; repeatedly for the same concept (suggests inattention)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm not sure I follow...
   - I don't quite understand...
   - I'm having difficulty understanding...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Hedges with "not sure" to soften admission of confusion; saves face by expressing uncertainty rather than complete lack of understanding
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Let me explain it differently...
   - Which part is unclear?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm not sure I understand the assignment requirements.
   - I don't quite get how this formula works.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I'm not entirely certain I understand the theoretical framework.
   - I'm having difficulty comprehending the methodology.

1. **Functional Expression**: Would it be possible to...? (function: requesting)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Very polite way to request something, inquiring about feasibility
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: very indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: Could I possibly...?, Is there any chance I could...?, Would you be able to...?, Can I...?
   
   **Antonyms**: I need you to..., You must..., I demand...
   
   **When to Use**: Making requests to professors or administrators; requesting exceptions or accommodations; formal academic situations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual peer interactions (too formal); emergency situations (too indirect)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Would it be possible for me to...?
   - Is it possible to...?
   - Could it be possible...?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Very high negative politeness; "would" + "possible" creates maximum indirectness; allows recipient easy refusal without face threat
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, that should be fine.
   - I'm afraid that won't be possible.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Would it be possible to get an extension on the paper?
   - Is it possible to change my presentation date?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Would it be possible to schedule a meeting to discuss my research proposal?
   - Is it feasible to arrange an alternative examination date?

1. **Functional Expression**: Do you mind if...? (function: requesting permission)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite way to ask for permission, literally asking if it would bother the other person
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: Would it be okay if...?, Is it alright if...?, Can I...?, May I...?
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm going to..., I will... (without asking)
   
   **When to Use**: Asking permission for actions that might affect others; classroom or study group situations; showing consideration
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal situations (use "May I"); note that "No, I don't mind" = permission granted (can confuse learners)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Would you mind if...?
   - Do you mind me...? (less formal)
   - Mind if I...? (very casual)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative question form (asking about objection rather than permission) is very polite; shows consideration for other's comfort; response logic can be confusing
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - No, go ahead. (= permission)
   - Actually, I'd prefer if you didn't. (= refusal)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Do you mind if I borrow your notes?
   - Would you mind if I joined your study group?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Do you mind if I record this lecture?
   - Would you mind if I submitted the assignment electronically?

1. **Functional Expression**: I see your point, but... (function: disagreeing politely)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Acknowledges other's viewpoint before presenting disagreement
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: I understand what you're saying, however..., That's true, but..., I take your point, although..., While I agree...
   
   **Antonyms**: You're wrong, I disagree completely, That's not true
   
   **When to Use**: Academic discussions; group projects; presenting alternative viewpoints while maintaining respect
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When complete agreement is possible; in debates where strong disagreement is expected
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I see what you mean, but...
   - I understand your perspective, however...
   - I take your point, although...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Positive politeness strategy (showing understanding) before disagreement; "but" signals upcoming contrast; maintains relationship while disagreeing
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What do you think the issue is?
   - I hadn't considered that.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I see your point, but I think there's another way to interpret this.
   - I understand what you're saying, but the professor mentioned something different.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I appreciate your perspective, however, I would argue that the evidence suggests otherwise.
   - I acknowledge the validity of that argument, but alternative explanations merit consideration.
