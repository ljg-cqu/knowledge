1. **Adjective**: academic (gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Relating to education and scholarship; connected to schools, colleges, or universities
   - Scholarly or theoretical rather than practical or applied
   
   **Synonyms**: scholarly, educational, scholastic, intellectual, collegiate
   
   **Antonyms**: practical, vocational, non-academic, unscholarly
   
   **When to Use**: Educational contexts; describing scholarly work, achievement, or environments; formal and informal settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For vocational or purely practical training
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - academic achievement
   - academic year
   - academic performance
   
   **Root Analysis**: Greek akademeia (Plato's school) + -ic
   
   **Etymology**: Greek Akademeia (Plato's school) → Latin academicus → French académique → English academic (16c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How's your academic life going?
   - She's really academically focused.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution maintains rigorous academic standards.
   - Academic excellence is the primary institutional objective.
