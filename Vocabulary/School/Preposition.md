1. **Preposition**: in (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Within the boundaries or confines of (a place, building, or room)
   - *(Temporal)* During (a period of time); at the end of (a period of time)
   - *(Abstract)* Engaged in or involved with (an activity, field, or subject)
   
   **Synonyms**: within (spatial), during (temporal), engaged in (abstract)
   
   **Antonyms**: out of, outside, beyond
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating location within spaces, time periods, or engagement in activities/subjects; universal usage
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For surfaces (use "on"), specific points in time (use "at"), or direction toward (use "into")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in school, in class, in college, in the library
   - **Verb + prep**: enroll in, major in, specialize in, engage in
   - **Adjective + prep**: interested in, involved in, skilled in
   - **Noun + prep**: interest in, involvement in, expertise in
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English inn (inside)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English inn → in (unchanged through history)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm in the library studying.
   - What are you majoring in?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Students enrolled in the program demonstrate exceptional ability.
   - Research conducted in educational psychology reveals significant patterns.
