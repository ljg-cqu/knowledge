1. **Original Expression**: I need help with this assignment  
   **Context**: Student requesting assistance from instructor or peer
   
   **Core Intent**: To seek support or guidance in completing academic work
   
   **Casual**: Can you help me with this?  
   *Characteristics*: Direct, simple vocabulary, contracted verb forms, question format  
   *Use when*: Speaking with classmates, friends, peers in study groups, relaxed environments
   
   **Semi-Formal**: I was wondering if you could help me with this assignment.  
   *Characteristics*: Indirect request using "wondering," polite modal "could," specific reference to "assignment"  
   *Use when*: Email to instructor, office hours, formal peer interaction, professional academic settings
   
   **Formal**: I would appreciate guidance on this assignment.  
   *Characteristics*: Formal vocabulary ("guidance," "appreciate"), conditional mood, no contractions  
   *Use when*: Written communication to professors, department heads, formal academic requests
   
   **Humorous**: I'm drowning in this assignment—throw me a lifeline?  
   *Characteristics*: Metaphorical language, exaggeration, playful tone  
   *Use when*: Close friends, study buddies, when relationship allows humor  
   *Avoid when*: Communicating with instructors, formal settings, first interactions
   
   **Lively**: I'm really stuck on this and could use some help!  
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiastic tone, exclamation mark, emphasis with "really"  
   *Use when*: Engaging peers, creating collaborative atmosphere, group study sessions
   
   **Empathetic**: I'm finding this assignment challenging and would appreciate any support you could offer.  
   *Characteristics*: Vulnerability ("finding challenging"), acknowledgment of difficulty, gratitude expression  
   *Use when*: Seeking understanding from instructor, building rapport, showing genuine struggle
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Higher power (professor) requires semi-formal/formal; peers allow casual
   - *Social Distance*: New relationships require formal; established relationships allow casual
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Major assignments or failing grades require more formal approaches
   - *Cultural Context*: Some cultures expect greater formality in student-teacher interactions
   - *Medium*: Written communication generally more formal than spoken
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using casual language with new instructors (undermines professionalism)
   - Being too formal with study partners (creates social distance)
   - Using humor before establishing rapport (may be misunderstood)
   
   **Cultural Notes**: American academic culture allows more informality than many Asian or European contexts; email formality varies by institution
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: First email to professor → **Appropriate Variant**: Semi-formal/Formal (power distance + social distance)
   - **Situation 2**: Texting study group member → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual (low power distance + familiarity)
   - **Situation 3**: Office hours with approachable instructor → **Appropriate Variant**: Semi-formal (moderate formality with slight casualness)

   - Being vague about confusion (prevents effective help)
   - Only asking about practical/test-related content (misses intellectual depth)

   - **Situation 1**: Seminar discussion of complex theoretical text → **Appropriate Variant**: Lively/Empathetic (intellectual engagement + collaborative exploration)

   - **Situation 3**: Study group puzzling over reading → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual (peers + shared confusion)
