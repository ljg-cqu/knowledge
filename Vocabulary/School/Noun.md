1. **Noun**: curriculum (C/U, pl: curricula or curriculums)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The subjects comprising a course of study in a school or college
   - *(Sense 2)* The complete set of courses, learning experiences, and educational content provided by an educational institution
   
   **Synonyms**: syllabus, program, course of study, educational plan, study program
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (educational concept)
   
   **When to Use**: Academic and educational contexts; formal discussions about educational planning, policy, and structure
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to individual lessons or single courses (use "course" or "class" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - national curriculum
   - curriculum design
   - follow the curriculum
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin curriculum (racing chariot, course)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin curriculum (course, racing chariot) → metaphorical use for "course of study" (1824)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The curriculum at my school includes art and music.
   - What's in your curriculum this semester?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution revised its curriculum to incorporate digital literacy.
   - Curriculum development requires extensive stakeholder consultation.

1. **Noun**: semester (C, pl: semesters)
   **Meaning**:

   **Antonyms**: break, vacation, holiday
