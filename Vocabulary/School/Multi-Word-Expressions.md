1. **Expression**: pull an all-nighter (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To stay awake all night, especially to study for an exam or complete an assignment
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed idiom, non-literal)
   
   **Synonyms**: stay up all night, study all night, cram all night, burn the midnight oil
   
   **Antonyms**: get a good night's sleep, rest well, sleep early
   
   **When to Use**: Informal student contexts; describing intense study sessions before exams; casual conversations about academic stress
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal academic writing or professional contexts; when discussing healthy study habits (negative connotation)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pull an all-nighter studying
   - pulled an all-nighter for the exam
   - pulling all-nighters
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "All-nighter" emerged from student culture in the 1960s, combining "all night" with the "-er" suffix to create a noun describing the activity of staying awake throughout the night. "Pull" is used metaphorically to mean "accomplish" or "manage to do."
   
   **Root Analysis**: pull (Old English) + all-night + -er (agent suffix)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I had to pull an all-nighter to finish my essay.
   - She's pulling an all-nighter for her chemistry final.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *"Typically informal; avoid in formal contexts"*
