1. **Adverb**: academically (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that relates to education and scholarship
   - With respect to academic performance or achievement
   
   **Synonyms**: scholastically, educationally, intellectually
   
   **Antonyms**: practically, vocationally, non-academically
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing educational performance, scholarly approaches, or academic contexts; formal and informal settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For practical or hands-on skill discussions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - academically successful
   - perform academically
   - academically challenging
   
   **Root Analysis**: academic + -ally
   
   **Etymology**: Greek akademeia → academic + -ally → academically (19c.)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's doing really well academically.
   - I'm struggling academically this semester.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Students who excel academically receive recognition.
   - The program is academically rigorous and demanding.
