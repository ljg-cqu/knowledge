1. **Noun**: consensus (U/C, pl: consensuses)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A general agreement among a group of people; a collective opinion reached by most or all members of a group.
   - The process of decision-making that seeks the consent and agreement of participants.
   
   **Synonyms**: agreement, accord, harmony, concord, unity
   
   **Antonyms**: disagreement, discord, dissent, conflict, division
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, organizational decision-making, political discussions, academic discourse
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversations (use "agreement" instead); avoid when referring to majority vote only
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reach consensus
   - build consensus
   - consensus decision
   - broad consensus
   - emerging consensus
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + sens (feel, think) + -us (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → consensus from consentire (agree) from con- (together) + sentire (feel) → English consensus (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to reach consensus on this before moving forward.
   - There's a general consensus that we should change the schedule.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee sought to build consensus through iterative consultation.
   - A broad consensus emerged regarding the strategic priorities.

1. **Noun**: agreement (C/U, pl: agreements)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A situation in which people have the same opinion or have made the same decision about something.
   - A formal arrangement or contract between parties.
   
   **Synonyms**: accord, understanding, arrangement, settlement, pact
   
   **Antonyms**: disagreement, dispute, conflict, discord
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts, business negotiations, legal matters, everyday discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When specifically referring to unanimous consent (use "consensus" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reach an agreement
   - mutual agreement
   - binding agreement
   - come to an agreement
   - formal agreement
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (to) + gree (pleasing) + -ment (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French → agreer (to please) + -ment → English agreement (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We finally reached an agreement on where to go for dinner.
   - There's an agreement between us about how to split the work.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The parties executed a binding agreement with specified deliverables.
   - A mutual agreement was reached through diplomatic negotiation.

1. **Noun**: compromise (C/U, pl: compromises)

   **Synonyms**: settlement, concession, middle ground, accommodation, trade-off
   
   **Antonyms**: deadlock, stalemate, impasse, intransigence

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reach a compromise
   - acceptable compromise
   - make a compromise
   - compromise solution
   - negotiate a compromise
   
   **Root Analysis**: com- (together) + promise (agreement)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → compromissum (mutual promise) from com- (together) + promittere (promise) → English compromise (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We reached a compromise that works for everyone.
   - Sometimes you have to make a compromise to move forward.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - A mutually acceptable compromise was negotiated through facilitated dialogue.
   - The compromise solution balanced competing interests effectively.
