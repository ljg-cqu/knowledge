1. **Preposition**: on (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Concerning; about; regarding a particular subject or topic.
   - *(Agreement)* Used to indicate agreement or consensus about something.
   
   **Synonyms**: about (in context), regarding (in context), concerning (in context)
   
   **Antonyms**: off (in some contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts, indicating topics of agreement, subjects of discussion
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When spatial or temporal meaning is required; avoid confusion with other senses
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: on this issue, on the matter, on the topic
   - **Verb + prep**: agree on, decide on, vote on, focus on
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not common)
   - **Noun + prep**: consensus on, agreement on, position on
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English → on (simple preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English → on (in, on, at) → English on (Anglo-Saxon period)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We all agreed on the best approach.
   - Let's reach consensus on this issue before moving forward.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee reached consensus on the strategic priorities.
   - Stakeholders aligned on fundamental principles despite divergent perspectives.

   **Synonyms**: alongside (in context), in agreement with (in context)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, without, in opposition to

   - **Verb + prep**: agree with, align with, collaborate with, consult with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent with, in line with

1. **Preposition**: among (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Within a group; in the midst of; surrounded by.
   - Used to indicate distribution or presence within a group of people or things.
   
   **Synonyms**: amongst, within, between (for more than two)
   
   **Antonyms**: outside, apart from, separate from
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts, describing group dynamics, distribution, shared characteristics
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to only two entities (use "between"); avoid with singular nouns
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: among stakeholders, among members, among parties
   - **Verb + prep**: distribute among, share among, divide among
   - **Adjective + prep**: common among, widespread among
   - **Noun + prep**: consensus among, agreement among, division among
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English → on (in) + gemang (crowd, mixture)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English → ongemang (in the crowd) → among (12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's general agreement among the team members.
   - We need consensus among all stakeholders.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Broad consensus emerged among diverse stakeholder groups.
   - Significant divergence existed among constituent perspectives.

1. **Preposition**: between (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Indicating a connection or relationship involving two or more parties.
   - In the space separating two or more things, places, or people.
   
   **Synonyms**: amongst (for more than two), among (for more than two)
   
   **Antonyms**: separate from, apart from, outside
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts, describing relationships, negotiations, agreements involving parties
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Traditionally for two entities, though modern usage accepts more; avoid when referring to many entities
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: between parties, between stakeholders, between groups
   - **Verb + prep**: negotiate between, mediate between, choose between, decide between
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not common)
   - **Noun + prep**: agreement between, compromise between, relationship between
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English → be (by) + tweonum (two)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English → betweonum (by two) → between (Anglo-Saxon period)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need an agreement between both parties.
   - There's a difference of opinion between us.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - A mutually beneficial agreement was negotiated between the contracting parties.
   - The mediator facilitated dialogue between conflicting stakeholder groups.

1. **Preposition**: over (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Topic)* Concerning; about; regarding (especially in discussions or disagreements).
   - *(Duration)* Throughout the period of; during.
   
   **Synonyms**: about (in context), concerning (in context), regarding (in context)
   
   **Antonyms**: (not applicable)
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts, indicating topics of discussion, disagreement, or duration
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid ambiguity with spatial meaning; prefer "about" or "regarding" for clarity in formal writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: over the issue, over the decision, over time
   - **Verb + prep**: disagree over, argue over, deliberate over, debate over
   - **Adjective + prep**: concerned over, divided over
   - **Noun + prep**: disagreement over, conflict over, consensus over (less common)

   **Etymology**: Old English → ofer → over (Anglo-Saxon period, abstract meaning developed later)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We disagreed over the best approach.
   - The team debated over several options.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Stakeholders expressed divergent views over implementation timelines.
   - Extensive deliberation occurred over resource allocation priorities.

1. **Preposition**: regarding (type: compound/phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Topic)* About; concerning; with respect to.
   - In relation to; as regards.
   
   **Synonyms**: concerning, about, with regard to, with respect to, as to
   
   **Antonyms**: (not applicable)
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, business communication, official documents, academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversations (use "about" instead)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: regarding the proposal, regarding the issue, regarding implementation
   - **Verb + prep**: (less common, used at sentence start: "Regarding your question...")
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not used)

   **Root Analysis**: re- (again, back) + gard (look, observe) + -ing
   
   **Etymology**: French → regarder (to look at) + -ing → regarding (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to make a decision regarding the schedule.
   - What's your opinion regarding this issue?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Stakeholders reached consensus regarding strategic priorities and implementation frameworks.
   - Divergent perspectives persisted regarding appropriate governance mechanisms.

1. **Preposition**: pursuant to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Formal/Legal)* In accordance with; following; as authorized by.
   - In carrying out or in conformity with.
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, according to, as per, under, in conformity with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, despite
   
   **When to Use**: Very formal contexts, legal documents, official communications, contractual language
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Informal conversations; avoid in everyday business communication (use "according to" instead)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pursuant to the agreement, pursuant to regulations, pursuant to authority
   - **Verb + prep**: act pursuant to, proceed pursuant to
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not used)
   - **Noun + prep**: action pursuant to, decision pursuant to
   
   **Root Analysis**: pursue (follow) + -ant (adjective/adverb suffix) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Anglo-French → pursuiant (following) → pursuant to (15th century, legal usage)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Not used in casual contexts*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee acted pursuant to the authority granted by the governing resolution.
   - Decisions were implemented pursuant to the consensus agreement reached by stakeholders.
