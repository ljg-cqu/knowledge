1. **Adjective**: unanimous (non-gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - When a group of people are unanimous, they all agree about something completely and there is no disagreement at all.
   - *Attributive and predicative use; non-gradable*
   
   **Synonyms**: united, in agreement, of one mind, in accord, in harmony
   
   **Antonyms**: divided, split, disagreeing, conflicting
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, decision-making processes, voting outcomes, organizational agreements, diplomatic discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversations where "everyone agreed" is more natural; avoid when there is partial agreement
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - unanimous decision
   - unanimous vote
   - unanimous consent
   - reach unanimous agreement
   - unanimous support
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (one) + anim (mind, spirit) + -ous (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → unanimus ("of one mind") from un- (one) + animus (mind, spirit) → English unanimous (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone on the team agreed with the plan—it was unanimous.
   - We all voted the same way, so it was a unanimous yes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee reached a unanimous decision to approve the proposal.
   - The board expressed unanimous support for the strategic initiative.
