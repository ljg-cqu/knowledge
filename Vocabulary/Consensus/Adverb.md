1. **Adverb**: unanimously (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a manner showing complete agreement among all people involved; with everyone agreeing.
   - *Position*: mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: collectively, jointly, as one, in unison, in agreement
   
   **Antonyms**: divisively, contentiously, separately, individually
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, describing voting outcomes, decision announcements, organizational resolutions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversations (use "everyone agreed" instead); avoid when there was disagreement
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - voted unanimously
   - agreed unanimously
   - unanimously approved
   - unanimously rejected
   - unanimously decided
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (one) + anim (mind) + -ous + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → unanimus (of one mind) + -ly → English unanimously (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone voted unanimously to go with the new plan.
   - The team unanimously agreed it was the best option.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board unanimously approved the strategic acquisition proposal.
   - The resolution was unanimously adopted by all member states.
