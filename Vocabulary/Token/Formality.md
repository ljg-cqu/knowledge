# Token - Formality Variants

1. **Original Expression**: "Token prices are going crazy right now!"  
   **Context**: Discussing volatile token market movements

   **Core Intent**: Express that token values are experiencing extreme volatility
   
   **Casual**: "Token prices are going absolutely nuts—up 50% one day, down 30% the next!"  
   *Characteristics*: Informal vocabulary ("nuts," "crazy"), exclamation marks, conversational tone  
   *Use when*: Chatting with crypto friends, social media posts, casual trading discussions
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "Token prices are experiencing significant volatility currently."  
   *Characteristics*: Professional vocabulary ("experiencing," "significant"), neutral tone, complete sentences  
   *Use when*: Work discussions, general business contexts, professional crypto communities
   
   **Formal**: "Token valuations are exhibiting substantial price volatility during the present market cycle."  
   *Characteristics*: Academic vocabulary ("exhibiting," "substantial," "present market cycle"), impersonal tone  
   *Use when*: Research papers, investor reports, regulatory filings, academic presentations
   
   **Humorous**: "Token prices have lost their minds—they're on a rollercoaster that forgot how brakes work!"  
   *Characteristics*: Exaggeration, metaphorical imagery, playful language  
   *Use when*: Engaging community posts, building rapport in crypto forums, light-hearted market commentary  
   *Avoid when*: Professional investor communications, during serious market crashes, formal documentation
   
   **Lively**: "Token prices are absolutely exploding with volatility—we're seeing wild swings that have traders glued to their screens!"  
   *Characteristics*: Energetic vocabulary ("exploding," "wild"), vivid imagery, enthusiasm  
   *Use when*: Marketing content, community engagement, exciting market updates, storytelling
   
   **Empathetic**: "I know token price swings can be stressful—these volatile movements affect real portfolios and real people."  
   *Characteristics*: Acknowledgment of emotions, personal impact recognition, supportive tone  
   *Use when*: Supporting community members during volatility, addressing investor concerns, discussing losses
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Use formal with regulators/executives, casual with crypto peers
   - *Social Distance*: Use formal with strangers/investors, casual with community members
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Serious volatility requires formal/empathetic; normal trading allows casual/lively
   - *Cultural Context*: Crypto culture tends casual; traditional finance expects formal
   - *Medium*: Written reports require formal; Discord/Twitter allow casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "crazy" or "nuts" in investor presentations—unprofessional and reduces credibility
   - Being too formal in community spaces—appears disconnected from crypto culture
   - Remaining casual when discussing significant losses—shows insensitivity to financial impact
   
   **Cultural Notes**: Crypto culture embraces casualness more than traditional finance, but regulatory and institutional contexts require formal communication regardless of industry norms.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: SEC filing discussing token volatility → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (regulatory requirement)
   - **Situation 2**: Twitter update to community → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual or Lively (engaging audience)
   - **Situation 3**: Responding to someone's loss → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (supporting person)

1. **Original Expression**: "You need to DYOR before buying any token"  
   **Context**: Advising someone to research before investing

   **Core Intent**: Recommend conducting thorough personal research before token investment
   
   **Casual**: "Dude, seriously DYOR before throwing money at any token—don't trust random Twitter shills."  
   *Characteristics*: Slang ("dude"), acronyms (DYOR), informal warnings, direct address  
   *Use when*: Friendly advice, crypto community interactions, peer-to-peer conversations
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "I recommend conducting your own research before purchasing any tokens."  
   *Characteristics*: Complete phrases (no acronyms), professional recommendations, polite suggestions  
   *Use when*: General investment discussions, workplace conversations, professional advice platforms
   
   **Formal**: "Investors should perform comprehensive due diligence before allocating capital to token assets."  
   *Characteristics*: Technical vocabulary ("due diligence," "allocating capital"), impersonal construction  
   *Use when*: Investment advisories, legal disclaimers, institutional communications, educational content
   
   **Humorous**: "Don't just ape into tokens because some guy with a laser-eyed avatar tweeted about them—DYOR or prepare for rekt!"  
   *Characteristics*: Crypto memes ("ape," "laser eyes," "rekt"), self-aware humor, warning embedded in jokes  
   *Use when*: Engaging crypto-native audiences, community education with entertainment, relatable warnings  
   *Avoid when*: Speaking to traditional investors, serious investment guidance, professional contexts
   
   **Lively**: "Before diving into any token, take the time to really dig deep—check the tokenomics, read the whitepaper, scope out the team!"  
   *Characteristics*: Action-oriented language ("diving," "dig deep"), enthusiastic tone, specific guidance  
   *Use when*: Educational content, community workshops, motivational investment education
   
   **Empathetic**: "I understand the excitement around new tokens, but protecting your investment starts with thorough research—take your time to understand what you're buying."  
   *Characteristics*: Validation of feelings, protective framing, patient guidance, concern for wellbeing  
   *Use when*: Mentoring new investors, preventing FOMO decisions, supporting anxious community members
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal with clients/investors; casual with peers
   - *Social Distance*: Formal with strangers needing credibility; casual with established community
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Life savings at risk requires empathetic/formal; small amounts allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Crypto natives expect DYOR acronym; traditional investors need spelled-out advice
   - *Medium*: Legal disclaimers require formal; community posts allow casual/humorous
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "DYOR" acronym with non-crypto audiences—creates confusion and appears exclusionary
   - Being too casual when providing fiduciary advice—may have legal implications
   - Omitting empathy when advising newcomers—increases anxiety and mistakes
   
   **Cultural Notes**: "DYOR" (Do Your Own Research) is crypto-cultural shorthand widely understood in communities but potentially alienating to outsiders. Traditional finance equivalents include "due diligence" or "thorough research."
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Investment disclaimer in app → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (legal protection)
   - **Situation 2**: Responding to "Should I buy X?" on Reddit → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual (community norms)
   - **Situation 3**: Newcomer asking for help → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (supporting learning)

1. **Original Expression**: "The tokenomics are terrible—massive inflation with no utility"  
   **Context**: Criticizing a project's token economic model

   **Core Intent**: Express that the token's economic design is fundamentally flawed
   
   **Casual**: "The tokenomics are trash—they're just printing tokens like crazy with zero actual use case."  
   *Characteristics*: Strong negative language ("trash"), informal expressions ("like crazy"), direct criticism  
   *Use when*: Warning friends, community discussions, informal analysis, social media commentary
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "The token economics present significant concerns, particularly regarding high inflation without clear utility drivers."  
   *Characteristics*: Professional criticism, specific issues identified, measured tone, technical vocabulary  
   *Use when*: Professional analysis, investment committees, business reviews, educated audiences
   
   **Formal**: "The token economic model demonstrates structural deficiencies, including unsustainable emission schedules without corresponding utility value propositions."  
   *Characteristics*: Academic framing, precise terminology, comprehensive analysis, impersonal tone  
   *Use when*: Research publications, regulatory submissions, institutional reports, academic analysis
   
   **Humorous**: "These tokenomics are a masterclass in how to absolutely destroy value—infinite supply, zero use case, maximum rug potential!"  
   *Characteristics*: Sarcasm ("masterclass"), exaggeration ("infinite," "destroy"), crypto slang ("rug")  
   *Use when*: Entertainment-focused analysis, building engagement through humor, obvious scam warnings  
   *Avoid when*: Project might improve, dealing with people who lost money, professional credibility important
   
   **Lively**: "Red flags everywhere with these tokenomics—we're talking runaway inflation paired with virtually no real-world utility!"  
   *Characteristics*: Warning signals ("red flags"), vivid language ("runaway"), emphatic tone  
   *Use when*: Educational warnings, engaging content, urgent community alerts
   
   **Empathetic**: "I'm concerned about this project's tokenomics—the inflation rate may significantly impact investor value, and I'd hate to see people affected."  
   *Characteristics*: Personal concern expressed, impact on people prioritized, gentle warning, care for community  
   *Use when*: Warning friends or followers, expressing genuine concern, balancing criticism with care
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal with institutional clients; casual with community peers
   - *Social Distance*: Formal in professional analysis; casual in community warnings
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Potential scam warrants empathetic/lively; debatable design allows casual/formal
   - *Cultural Context*: Crypto communities accept blunt criticism; professional contexts require measured tone
   - *Medium*: Public research reports require formal; community forums allow casual/humorous
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Being too harsh when projects might improve—burns bridges and reduces credibility
   - Using "trash" or extreme language in professional analysis—appears unprofessional and biased
   - Lacking empathy when people are invested—alienates those who need the warning most
   
   **Cultural Notes**: Crypto communities often embrace direct, harsh criticism of bad tokenomics as protective behavior. Traditional finance requires more measured, evidence-based critique regardless of severity.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Due diligence report for VC firm → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (professional standards)
   - **Situation 2**: Warning Twitter followers → **Appropriate Variant**: Lively or Casual (engaging protection)
   - **Situation 3**: Friend already invested → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (support while informing)

1. **Original Expression**: "When will the tokens vest?"  
   **Context**: Asking about token release schedule

   **Core Intent**: Inquire about the timeline for locked tokens becoming transferable
   
   **Casual**: "When do the tokens unlock?"  
   *Characteristics*: Simple vocabulary, direct question, common terminology  
   *Use when*: Quick questions to team members, informal status checks, community inquiries
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "Could you clarify the vesting schedule for the allocated tokens?"  
   *Characteristics*: Polite request, technical terms ("vesting schedule"), professional courtesy  
   *Use when*: Employee communications, investor relations, professional inquiries
   
   **Formal**: "I would appreciate clarification regarding the vesting timeline and release schedule for token allocations."  
   *Characteristics*: Formal request structure, precise terminology, complete formality  
   *Use when*: Legal communications, contract negotiations, formal investor inquiries
   
   **Humorous**: "When does the token jail sentence end—what's the unlock schedule?"  
   *Characteristics*: Playful metaphor ("jail sentence"), light-hearted framing  
   *Use when*: Friendly team discussions, light-hearted community engagement  
   *Avoid when*: Serious investor communications, legal discussions, contract negotiations
   
   **Lively**: "Really curious about the vesting timeline—when will we see those tokens freed up?"  
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiastic tone, conversational language, genuine interest expressed  
   *Use when*: Engaged community discussions, curious inquiries, casual professional contexts
   
   **Empathetic**: "I know waiting for vesting can be frustrating—could you share when tokens will become available?"  
   *Characteristics*: Acknowledges emotional aspect, supportive tone, patient inquiry  
   *Use when*: Team members expressing frustration, long vesting periods, community concerns
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal to executives/legal; semi-formal to HR; casual to peers
   - *Social Distance*: Formal in contract contexts; casual with familiar colleagues
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Large allocations require formal; small amounts allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Startups tend casual; traditional corps expect semi-formal
   - *Medium*: Legal/contract emails require formal; Slack messages allow casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "unlock" in legal documents—imprecise and potentially ambiguous
   - Being too formal with startup teams—creates unnecessary distance
   - Ignoring emotional aspects with long vesting—misses opportunity for empathy
   
   **Cultural Notes**: "Vesting" is standard across tech/crypto. "Unlock" is crypto-casual shorthand. Traditional employment contexts always use "vesting schedule" formally.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Reviewing employment contract → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (legal precision)
   - **Situation 2**: Asking team lead in Slack → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual (internal communication)
   - **Situation 3**: Advisor concerned about timeline → **Appropriate Variant**: Semi-Formal (professional courtesy)

1. **Original Expression**: "We're going to airdrop tokens to early users"  
   **Context**: Announcing free token distribution plan

   **Core Intent**: Inform that tokens will be distributed freely to early community members
   
   **Casual**: "We're dropping free tokens for everyone who used the platform early!"  
   *Characteristics*: Abbreviated verb ("dropping"), emphasis on "free," enthusiastic tone  
   *Use when*: Community announcements, social media posts, informal updates
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "We will distribute tokens to early platform users as a reward for their participation."  
   *Characteristics*: Professional vocabulary ("distribute," "reward"), clear structure, respectful tone  
   *Use when*: Official blog posts, email announcements, general communications
   
   **Formal**: "The protocol will allocate token distributions to qualifying early participants according to predetermined eligibility criteria."  
   *Characteristics*: Precise legal language, formal structure, comprehensive description  
   *Use when*: Legal announcements, investor communications, regulatory filings, official documentation
   
   **Humorous**: "Surprise! Free tokens incoming for our OG users—because you believed in us before it was cool!"  
   *Characteristics*: Exclamation, informal slang ("OG"), playful appreciation, casual reward framing  
   *Use when*: Community-building announcements, engaging loyal users, social media excitement  
   *Avoid when*: Large financial amounts involved, securities regulations apply, formal documentation needed
   
   **Lively**: "Exciting news—we're rewarding our amazing early adopters with token airdrops!"  
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiasm expressed, positive adjectives, energetic tone, appreciation shown  
   *Use when*: Marketing announcements, community celebrations, engagement-focused communications
   
   **Empathetic**: "We deeply appreciate our early supporters, and we want to recognize your trust by sharing tokens with those who were here from the beginning."  
   *Characteristics*: Gratitude expressed, relationship acknowledged, inclusive language, genuine appreciation  
   *Use when*: Sincere community appreciation, building loyalty, acknowledging early supporter sacrifice
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal for investor announcements; casual for community
   - *Social Distance*: Formal for public/legal; casual for engaged community
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Large value/regulatory scrutiny requires formal; small community rewards allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Crypto communities expect casual excitement; traditional investors expect formal clarity
   - *Medium*: Legal docs require formal; Twitter/Discord allow casual/lively
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Being too casual when securities regulations might apply—creates legal exposure
   - Using overly formal language with engaged community—feels cold and corporate
   - Lacking gratitude in empathetic contexts—misses relationship-building opportunity
   
   **Cultural Notes**: "Airdrop" is crypto-specific terminology. Traditional equivalents include "token distribution" or "bonus allocation." Regulatory contexts may require avoiding "free" to prevent securities classification issues.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Legal announcement with regulatory review → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (compliance)
   - **Situation 2**: Twitter community announcement → **Appropriate Variant**: Lively or Casual (engagement)
   - **Situation 3**: Email to dedicated early users → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (appreciation)

1. **Original Expression**: "I got liquidated"  
   **Context**: Informing others about forced position closure in DeFi

   **Core Intent**: Communicate that collateralized position was forcibly sold due to insufficient collateral ratio
   
   **Casual**: "I got rekt—my position got liquidated when the price dumped."  
   *Characteristics*: Crypto slang ("rekt," "dumped"), conversational tone, self-deprecating  
   *Use when*: Sharing experiences with crypto friends, community discussions, learning from mistakes
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "My leveraged position was liquidated due to the recent price decline."  
   *Characteristics*: Technical accuracy, neutral tone, clear cause explanation  
   *Use when*: Explaining situations in professional contexts, educational discussions, general audiences
   
   **Formal**: "The collateralized debt position was liquidated following insufficient collateralization ratios triggered by market volatility."  
   *Characteristics*: Precise technical language, passive voice, comprehensive explanation, impersonal tone  
   *Use when*: Post-mortem reports, risk management documentation, institutional analysis
   
   **Humorous**: "Welp, I just got absolutely destroyed—liquidation bots swooped in faster than I could blink!"  
   *Characteristics*: Self-deprecating humor, exaggeration, lighthearted acknowledgment of loss  
   *Use when*: Coping with loss through humor, building rapport through shared experiences, not-too-serious amounts  
   *Avoid when*: Significant financial losses, others are suffering, professional credibility important
   
   **Lively**: "Brutal—I just watched my position get liquidated in real-time as the market crashed!"  
   *Characteristics*: Vivid description, emotional engagement, dramatic retelling  
   *Use when*: Storytelling, sharing market experiences, engaging discussions
   
   **Empathetic**: "I experienced a liquidation today—it's a tough reminder about the risks of leveraged positions, and I understand others might be going through similar challenges."  
   *Characteristics*: Vulnerability shared, learning acknowledged, consideration for others in same situation  
   *Use when*: Supporting others who lost positions, sharing lessons learned, building supportive community
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal in risk reports to management; casual with trading peers
   - *Social Distance*: Formal with investors/employers; casual with community
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Life-changing losses require empathetic/formal; small losses allow casual/humorous
   - *Cultural Context*: DeFi traders accept liquidations as normal risk; traditional contexts need careful explanation
   - *Medium*: Risk reports require formal; trading forums allow casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using humor when person lost significant money—shows insensitivity and poor judgment
   - Being too casual when reporting to employers/investors—minimizes serious risk management failure
   - Lacking empathy when others are also liquidated—misses community support opportunity
   
   **Cultural Notes**: "Liquidated" is standard across finance. "Rekt" is crypto slang from "wrecked," widely understood in DeFi communities but inappropriate in professional contexts.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Risk report explaining portfolio loss → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (accountability)
   - **Situation 2**: Sharing experience in DeFi Discord → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual (community norms)
   - **Situation 3**: Market-wide liquidation event → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (supporting community)
