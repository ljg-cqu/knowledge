# Token - Prepositions

1. **Preposition**: on (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Indicating position upon a surface
   - *(Abstract - Blockchain)* Indicating the blockchain network where a token exists or operates (e.g., "tokens on Ethereum")
   - *(Abstract - Protocol)* Indicating platform or protocol context (e.g., "staking on Uniswap")
   
   **Synonyms**: upon (formal), within (context-specific), via (mechanism)
   
   **Antonyms**: off, outside, away from
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying which blockchain network hosts a token, which platform provides services, or which protocol executes operations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing token ownership rather than location, or when "in" would be more appropriate for contained relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: tokens on Ethereum, staking on the platform, built on blockchain
   - **Verb + prep**: trade on exchanges, launch on mainnet, deploy on network
   - **Adjective + prep**: available on multiple chains, listed on exchanges
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens on the chain, contracts on Ethereum, assets on protocol
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "on" (indicating position)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "on" → unchanged through Middle English → modern usage expanded to abstract technological contexts
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - These tokens are on Ethereum, so you need ETH for gas fees.
   - You can stake your tokens on their platform to earn rewards.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The protocol deploys smart contracts on multiple EVM-compatible blockchain networks to maximize accessibility.
   - Token standards implemented on Ethereum enable seamless integration across decentralized application ecosystems.

1. **Preposition**: to (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Direction)* Indicating direction or destination of token transfers
   - *(Purpose)* Indicating intended use or function of tokens
   - *(Recipient)* Indicating the receiving address or party in transactions
   
   **Synonyms**: toward (direction), for (purpose), into (transformation)
   
   **Antonyms**: from, away from
   
   **When to Use**: When describing token transfers between addresses, purpose of token allocation, or directed actions in blockchain contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When the relationship is static rather than directional, or when other prepositions better capture the relationship
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: transfer to address, send to wallet, allocate to treasury
   - **Verb + prep**: send to, transfer to, distribute to, allocate to, mint to
   - **Adjective + prep**: subject to vesting, exposed to risk, limited to holders
   - **Noun + prep**: transfer to address, allocation to team, distribution to users
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "to" (indicating direction)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "to" → Middle English → modern usage consistent across contexts
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Send the tokens to this address and I'll receive them in a few minutes.
   - The team allocation goes to founders and early employees.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Token distributions will be allocated to eligible participants according to predetermined criteria and vesting schedules.
   - Smart contracts automatically transfer tokens to designated addresses upon condition fulfillment.

1. **Preposition**: from (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Source)* Indicating origin of token transfers or source blockchain
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating starting point of time periods (vesting, staking)
   - *(Derivation)* Indicating source of token value, rewards, or fees
   
   **Synonyms**: out of (source), away from (direction), originating in
   
   **Antonyms**: to, toward, into
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying token source addresses, origin blockchains in bridges, or sources of rewards and fees
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When direction is toward rather than away from, or when "to" better captures the relationship
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: tokens from address, rewards from staking, revenue from fees
   - **Verb + prep**: transfer from, bridge from, derive from, earn from, receive from
   - **Adjective + prep**: separate from, different from, distinct from
   - **Noun + prep**: transfer from wallet, tokens from airdrop, income from farming
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "fram" (forward, from)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "fram" → Middle English "from" → modern usage indicating source or origin
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I received these tokens from an airdrop last month.
   - Bridge your tokens from Ethereum to Polygon to save on gas fees.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Protocol revenue derives from transaction fees, with distribution allocated proportionally to governance token holders.
   - Cross-chain token transfers from source blockchains require validation mechanisms ensuring security and preventing double-spending.

1. **Preposition**: with (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Accompaniment)* Indicating tokens used together or in combination
   - *(Instrument)* Indicating tokens as means of payment or interaction
   - *(Characteristic)* Describing token features or properties
   
   **Synonyms**: using, via (instrument), having (characteristic), alongside (accompaniment)
   
   **Antonyms**: without, lacking, absent
   
   **When to Use**: When describing token characteristics, payment methods using tokens, or combinations of tokens in transactions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other prepositions more precisely capture relationships (like "by" for agency or "through" for means)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: tokens with utility, pay with crypto, trade with leverage
   - **Verb + prep**: pay with tokens, interact with protocol, swap with slippage
   - **Adjective + prep**: compatible with standards, integrated with platforms
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens with governance rights, protocol with security
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "mid" (with, together)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "mid" → Middle English "with" → modern usage for accompaniment and instrument
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can pay for services with their native token instead of using credit cards.
   - Tokens with strong utility tend to hold value better than pure speculation coins.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The protocol implements tokens with programmable functionality enabling complex financial instrument creation.
   - Governance systems with token-weighted voting mechanisms may concentrate power among large holders.

1. **Preposition**: for (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Purpose)* Indicating intended token use or function
   - *(Exchange)* Indicating what tokens are traded or swapped for
   - *(Duration)* Indicating time period of token locks or stakes
   - *(Beneficiary)* Indicating who tokens are allocated to or benefit
   
   **Synonyms**: to (purpose), in exchange for (trade), during (time), benefiting (recipient)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, from (opposite direction)
   
   **When to Use**: When describing token purposes, trading pairs, staking/vesting durations, or allocation beneficiaries
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other prepositions more precisely capture the relationship, or when "for" creates ambiguity
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: tokens for governance, swap for ETH, locked for 30 days
   - **Verb + prep**: use for payments, stake for rewards, trade for profit, lock for period
   - **Adjective + prep**: eligible for airdrop, suitable for investment, responsible for fees
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens for access, rewards for staking, allocation for team
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "for" (before, in place of, because of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "for" → Middle English → modern usage with multiple related meanings
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I swapped my USDC for ETH right before the price pump.
   - You need to lock tokens for 90 days to get the highest staking APY.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Utility tokens serve as medium of exchange for platform services and computational resources.
   - Token allocations for development teams typically include vesting schedules extending for multiple years to ensure long-term alignment.

1. **Preposition**: in (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Location/Containment)* Indicating tokens held within wallets, protocols, or pools
   - *(State/Condition)* Indicating token status (in circulation, in lockup)
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating time periods or timeframes
   - *(Medium)* Indicating denomination or measurement unit
   
   **Synonyms**: within, inside, among (containment), during (time)
   
   **Antonyms**: out, outside, beyond
   
   **When to Use**: When describing where tokens are stored, token states or conditions, timeframes for events, or measurement denominations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "on" is more appropriate for blockchain/platform context, or when other prepositions better capture relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: tokens in wallet, in circulation, in lockup, denominated in USD
   - **Verb + prep**: hold in wallet, participate in governance, invest in tokens, measured in ETH
   - **Adjective + prep**: available in market, stored in contract, locked in protocol
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens in reserve, increase in value, participation in voting
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "in" (indicating position within)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "in" → unchanged through Middle English → modern usage for containment and state
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Keep your tokens in a hardware wallet for maximum security.
   - Token price is measured in USD but you're actually trading in ETH pairs.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Tokens held in smart contract escrow remain non-transferable pending condition satisfaction.
   - Market capitalization measured in USD equivalent provides standardized valuation metrics across heterogeneous token assets.

1. **Preposition**: by (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Agency)* Indicating who performs token actions or creates mechanisms
   - *(Means)* Indicating method or mechanism of token operations
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating deadline or time limit
   - *(Measurement)* Indicating basis of calculation or proportion
   
   **Synonyms**: through (means), via (method), before (time deadline), according to (measurement)
   
   **Antonyms**: without, despite (contradiction)
   
   **When to Use**: When describing who executes token actions, mechanisms of operation, deadlines for events, or calculation bases
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other prepositions more clearly express the relationship, or when "by" creates passive voice unnecessarily
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: created by team, secured by cryptography, determined by market
   - **Verb + prep**: governed by holders, protected by encryption, validated by nodes
   - **Adjective + prep**: controlled by DAO, backed by collateral, supported by community
   - **Noun + prep**: governance by token holders, security by design, distribution by algorithm
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "bi" (near, at, by)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "bi" → Middle English "by" → modern usage for agency, means, and proximity
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The token supply is controlled by smart contracts, not by any central authority.
   - You can reduce slippage by splitting large trades into smaller chunks.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Token emissions are governed by algorithmically determined schedules enforced by immutable smart contract logic.
   - Price determination occurs by decentralized market mechanisms through automated market maker protocols.

1. **Preposition**: through (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Means/Method)* Indicating mechanism or process by which token operations occur
   - *(Medium)* Indicating platform, protocol, or channel for token interactions
   - *(Completion)* Indicating duration from start to finish
   
   **Synonyms**: via, by means of, using, across (span)
   
   **Antonyms**: around (avoidance), without (absence)
   
   **When to Use**: When describing mechanisms enabling token operations, platforms facilitating transactions, or processes from beginning to end
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simpler prepositions like "by" or "with" suffice, or when relationship is static rather than processual
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: through smart contracts, through exchanges, through protocols
   - **Verb + prep**: trade through DEX, transfer through bridge, earn through staking
   - **Adjective + prep**: available through platforms, accessible through wallets
   - **Noun + prep**: transactions through blockchain, distribution through airdrops
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "thurh" (through, by means of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "thurh" → Middle English "through" → modern usage for means and passage
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can swap tokens through Uniswap without creating an account.
   - I earned passive income through staking and liquidity provision.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Token transfers execute through cryptographically secured blockchain networks ensuring immutable transaction records.
   - Governance participation occurs through on-chain voting mechanisms weighted by token holdings and delegation relationships.

1. **Preposition**: at (type: simple)

   - *(Location/Point)* Indicating specific price points, addresses, or positions

   - *(Rate)* Indicating exchange rates, APY rates, or valuation levels
   
   **Synonyms**: located at (position), occurring at (time), priced at (rate)
   
   **Antonyms**: away from, far from
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying exact prices, addresses, time points, or rates in token contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing ranges rather than specific points, or when other prepositions better capture relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: trading at price, valued at amount, available at rate
   - **Verb + prep**: trade at price, arrive at consensus, buy at level, sell at peak
   - **Adjective + prep**: priced at, available at, trading at
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens at address, price at time, rate at 5%
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "æt" (at, near, by)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "æt" → Middle English "at" → modern usage for specific points
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I bought tokens at $1.50 and sold at $3.00, doubling my investment.
   - The token is trading at all-time highs right now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Preposition**: of (type: simple)

   - *(Characteristic)* Describing token features or properties
   - *(Composition)* Indicating what tokens consist of or contain
   
   **Synonyms**: belonging to (possession), comprising (composition), characterized by (feature)
   
   **Antonyms**: without, lacking, devoid of
   
   **When to Use**: When expressing token ownership, supply portions, token characteristics, or compositional relationships
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other prepositions more precisely capture relationships, or when "of" creates unnecessarily complex phrases
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: holder of tokens, portion of supply, value of token
   - **Verb + prep**: consist of, comprised of, dispose of
   - **Adjective + prep**: typical of protocol, characteristic of model, independent of price
   - **Noun + prep**: owner of tokens, percentage of supply, amount of ETH, type of token
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "of" (away, from)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "of" → Middle English → modern usage shifted to indicate relationship and possession
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I own a small percentage of the total token supply, maybe 0.1%.
   - The value of governance tokens comes from voting rights, not just speculation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Token holders possess fractional ownership of protocol governance rights proportional to their percentage of total supply.
   - The utility value of platform tokens derives from their necessity for transaction fee payment and service access provisioning.

1. **Preposition**: into (type: compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Direction/Transformation)* Indicating movement toward interior or conversion between token types
   - *(Investment)* Indicating capital allocation into tokens or protocols
   - *(State Change)* Indicating transformation of assets into tokenized form
   
   **Synonyms**: to (direction), toward (movement), converting to (transformation)
   
   **Antonyms**: out of, from, away from
   
   **When to Use**: When describing token conversions, capital investments, wrapping/unwrapping, or transformations from one state to another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When movement is surface-level rather than interior/transformative, or when "to" suffices
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: convert into tokens, put money into crypto, transform into NFT
   - **Verb + prep**: convert into, swap into, transform into, invest into, lock into
   - **Adjective + prep**: converted into, transformed into, locked into
   - **Noun + prep**: conversion into tokens, investment into protocol, entry into market
   
   **Root Analysis**: "in" + "to" (compound preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "in" + "to" → Middle English "into" → emphasizes movement to interior or transformation
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I converted all my USDC into ETH before the price went up.
   - You can wrap your Bitcoin into WBTC to use it on Ethereum DeFi.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Asset tokenization converts traditional financial instruments into blockchain-based digital representations with programmable functionality.
   - Capital inflows into DeFi protocols accelerated substantially following institutional infrastructure development and regulatory clarity improvements.

1. **Preposition**: across (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Distribution)* Indicating distribution spanning multiple blockchains, platforms, or wallets
   - *(Comparison)* Indicating comparison or consistency across different contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: throughout, over, among, spanning
   
   **Antonyms**: within, confined to, limited to
   
   **When to Use**: When describing multi-chain token presence, distribution across holders, or consistency across platforms
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When tokens exist on single chain only, or when "on" or "through" better capture relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: across blockchains, across platforms, across wallets
   - **Verb + prep**: distribute across, spread across, bridge across, operate across
   - **Adjective + prep**: available across, consistent across, distributed across
   - **Noun + prep**: distribution across holders, presence across chains, adoption across markets
   
   **Root Analysis**: "a-" (on) + "cross" (crossing structure)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English "on cross" → "across" → indicating spanning or crossing
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This token is available across five different blockchains using bridge protocols.
   - They're spreading the airdrop across multiple snapshots to prevent gaming.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Interoperability protocols enable token functionality across heterogeneous blockchain architectures through standardized cross-chain messaging.
   - Token distribution across diverse holder categories reduces centralization risks and improves governance participation resilience.

1. **Preposition**: between (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Connection)* Indicating relationship connecting two blockchains, addresses, or tokens
   - *(Choice/Comparison)* Indicating selection or comparison among token options
   - *(Distribution)* Indicating sharing or allocation among parties
   
   **Synonyms**: among (more than two), connecting, linking
   
   **Antonyms**: within (interior), outside (exterior)
   
   **When to Use**: When describing cross-chain bridges, token swaps, distribution among parties, or comparisons of two options
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing relationships among more than two items (use "among"), or when other prepositions better capture relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: between chains, between addresses, between protocols
   - **Verb + prep**: bridge between, swap between, transfer between, choose between, distribute between
   - **Adjective + prep**: split between, divided between, shared between
   - **Noun + prep**: bridge between chains, connection between wallets, swap between tokens
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "be-" (by) + "tweonum" (two)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "betweonum" → Middle English "bitwene" → "between" (indicating position or relationship involving two)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can bridge tokens between Ethereum and Polygon in just a few minutes.
   - I'm trying to decide between staking for safe returns or farming for higher risk/reward.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Cross-chain bridges facilitate token transfers between heterogeneous blockchain networks through lock-and-mint or burn-and-mint mechanisms.
   - Token allocation distribution between team members, investors, and community requires careful balance to optimize long-term incentive alignment.

1. **Preposition**: about (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Topic)* Indicating subject matter concerning tokens
   - *(Approximation)* Indicating approximate quantity, price, or value
   - *(Concern)* Indicating what token information relates to
   
   **Synonyms**: concerning, regarding (topic), approximately (quantity), around (approximation)
   
   **Antonyms**: exactly (precision vs. approximation)
   
   **When to Use**: When discussing token topics, providing approximate values, or indicating subject matter focus
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When precision is required for prices or quantities, or when other prepositions more accurately express relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: about tokens, about tokenomics, worth about $100
   - **Verb + prep**: talk about, learn about, care about, know about, worry about
   - **Adjective + prep**: concerned about, informed about, curious about
   - **Noun + prep**: information about, questions about, concerns about, details about
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "onbutan" (on + by + utan = on the outside)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "onbutan" → Middle English "aboute" → "about" (concerning or surrounding)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Do you know anything about this new token that just launched?
   - The token is trading at about $2.50, give or take a few cents.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Investor inquiries about token vesting schedules and unlock timelines increased substantially following initial distribution events.
   - Market capitalization approximates about $500 million based on circulating supply estimates and current trading prices.

1. **Preposition**: within (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Containment)* Indicating location inside protocols, ecosystems, or systems
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating timeframe or deadline for token events
   - *(Limitation)* Indicating boundaries or constraints
   
   **Synonyms**: inside, in, during (time), not exceeding (limitation)
   
   **Antonyms**: outside, beyond, exceeding
   
   **When to Use**: When describing tokens contained in ecosystems, operations completing within timeframes, or staying within limits
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "in" suffices and sounds more natural, or when describing external relationships
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: within ecosystem, within timeframe, within limits
   - **Verb + prep**: operate within, stay within, complete within, remain within
   - **Adjective + prep**: contained within, available within, accessible within
   - **Noun + prep**: tokens within protocol, activity within period, values within range
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "with" (against) + "innan" (inside)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "withinnan" → Middle English "withinne" → "within" (interior position or time limit)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - All token operations stay within the smart contract—nothing happens off-chain.
   - You'll receive the airdrop within 7 days of the snapshot.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Token utility functions operate exclusively within protocol boundaries, creating closed-loop economic systems.
   - Vesting schedules ensure token liquidity introduction occurs within predetermined timeframes, preventing sudden supply shocks.
