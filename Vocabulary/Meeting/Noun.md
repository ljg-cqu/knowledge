1. **Noun**: agenda (C, pl: agendas)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A list of items to be discussed at a meeting; the topics or activities planned for a meeting or event.
   
   **Synonyms**: schedule, program, plan, outline, itinerary
   
   **Antonyms**: impromptu discussion, unplanned session
   
   **When to Use**: In professional settings when organizing meetings; essential for structured business meetings and formal discussions.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for casual, unstructured conversations; inappropriate for spontaneous discussions without planning.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - meeting agenda
   - set the agenda
   - on the agenda
   
   **Root Analysis**: agend (things to be done) + -a
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → agenda (things to be done) → agenda
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's on the agenda for today's meeting?
   - Let me send you the agenda before the call.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please review the attached agenda prior to the quarterly meeting.
   - The agenda includes three strategic initiatives for board consideration.

   - brainstorming session
   - have a brainstorm

   **Examples [Casual]**:

   - The brainstorm yesterday was really productive—tons of good ideas.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The brainstorming session will employ structured ideation techniques.
   - Participants engaged in a collaborative brainstorm to identify solutions.

   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee seeks to achieve consensus on the policy recommendations.
