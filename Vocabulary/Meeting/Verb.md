1. **Verb**: convene
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive/Intransitive) To arrange or call people together for a formal meeting; to come together for a meeting.
   
   **Synonyms**: assemble, gather, summon, call together, meet
   
   **Antonyms**: adjourn, dismiss, disperse, dissolve
   
   **When to Use**: In formal meeting contexts; appropriate for official gatherings, board meetings, and formal assemblies.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for casual get-togethers; inappropriate when informality is preferred.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - convene a meeting
   - convene the board
   - committee convenes
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + ven (come) + -e
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → convenire (to come together) → convene
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's convene next Tuesday to discuss this further.
   - The team convenes every Monday morning for standup.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board will convene an emergency session to address the matter.
   - The committee convenes quarterly to review progress and strategic alignment.

1. **Verb**: adjourn
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive/Intransitive) To suspend or conclude a meeting temporarily or permanently; to stop proceedings until a later time.
   
   **Synonyms**: suspend, postpone, recess, discontinue, close
   
   **Antonyms**: convene, open, begin, start, continue
   
   **When to Use**: In formal meeting settings; appropriate when officially ending or pausing proceedings.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for casual conversation endings; inappropriate for informal "wrapping up."
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - adjourn the meeting
   - adjourn until
   - motion to adjourn
   
   **Root Analysis**: ad- (to) + journ (day)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French → ajourner (to postpone to another day) → adjourn
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Should we adjourn for today and pick this up tomorrow?
   - Let's adjourn and grab lunch—we can continue after.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The meeting is adjourned until further notice.
   - I move that we adjourn the session and reconvene next Tuesday.

1. **Verb**: reschedule
