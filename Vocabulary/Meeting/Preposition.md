1. **Preposition**: during (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Throughout the course or existence of a time period; at some point in the time when something is happening.
   
   **Synonyms**: throughout (in some contexts), in the course of, while, amid
   
   **Antonyms**: before, after, outside of
   
   **When to Use**: When indicating something happens within a time period; essential for meeting context and timing references.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when referring to specific points in time (use "at"); don't confuse with "while" for simultaneous actions.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: during the meeting, during discussion, during Q&A
   - **Verb + prep**: occur during, happen during, arise during
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: questions during, decisions during
   
   **Root Analysis**: dur (last) + -ing
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → durare (to last) → during
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can we discuss this during the meeting tomorrow?
   - She raised a good point during the brainstorming session.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Critical issues emerged during the strategic review process.
   - Participants may submit questions during the presentation.

1. **Preposition**: regarding (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* About or concerning a particular subject; with reference to.
   
   **Synonyms**: concerning, about, with regard to, relating to, as to
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated to, apart from
   
   **When to Use**: In formal communication about meeting topics; appropriate for agenda items and discussion points.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual contexts (use "about"); can sound overly formal in everyday conversation.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: regarding the proposal, regarding next steps, regarding concerns
   - **Verb + prep**: discuss regarding, inquire regarding, write regarding
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: questions regarding, decision regarding, meeting regarding
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again) + gard (look) + -ing
   
   **Etymology**: Old French → regarder (to look at) → regarding
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What did she say regarding the schedule change?
   - I have some questions regarding tomorrow's meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee convened regarding the proposed policy amendments.
   - Please contact the secretary regarding agenda item submissions.

1. **Preposition**: between (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial/Temporal/Abstract)* In the space, time, or gap separating two or more things; involving choice or relationship among multiple parties.
   
   **Synonyms**: among (for more than two), amid, within
   
   **Antonyms**: outside of, beyond
   
   **When to Use**: When indicating relationships or scheduling among two parties; common for one-on-one meetings and negotiations.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Use "among" when referring to more than two parties in formal writing; avoid ambiguity in time references.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: between meetings, between sessions, between departments
   - **Verb + prep**: schedule between, coordinate between, negotiate between
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: meeting between, discussion between, agreement between
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by) + twain (two)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English → betweonum (by two) → between
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can we schedule a meeting between you and me next week?
   - There's a 15-minute break between sessions.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The negotiation meeting between stakeholders is scheduled for Tuesday.
   - Coordination between departments is essential for project success.
