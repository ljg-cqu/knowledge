1. **Adverb**: promptly (type: manner/time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without delay; immediately or at the scheduled time. Used to describe punctual meeting attendance or quick responses.
   - *Position*: Usually mid-position (after auxiliary verbs) or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: immediately, quickly, punctually, swiftly, without delay
   
   **Antonyms**: late, belatedly, tardily, slowly
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing timeliness and efficiency; appropriate in professional contexts where punctuality is valued.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when delay was necessary or justified; inappropriate when criticizing someone's timing harshly.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond promptly
   - arrive promptly
   - begin promptly
   
   **Root Analysis**: prompt + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → promptus (brought forth) → promptly
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please reply promptly so we can finalize the schedule.
   - She always shows up promptly—never a minute late.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants are expected to join the meeting promptly at 9 AM.
   - Action items must be addressed promptly to maintain project momentum.
