# Adverbs: Pending

1. **Adverb**: currently (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At the present time; now; at this moment; during the current period
   - *Position*: Typically mid-sentence (before main verb or after auxiliary) or sentence-initial
   
   **Synonyms**: presently, now, at present, at the moment, right now
   
   **Antonyms**: previously, formerly, eventually, later, in the past
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and professional contexts describing current states; status updates; emphasizing present situation vs. past or future
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation (prefer "now" or "right now"); historical narratives
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - currently pending
   - currently under review
   - currently being processed
   - is currently
   - currently available
   
   **Root Analysis**: current (flowing, present) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin currere (to run) → current → currently (17th century), meaning "at the running/flowing moment"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm currently waiting to hear back about my application.
   - The issue is currently being looked at by the team.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter is currently pending review by the regulatory committee.
   - Applications are currently being processed and decisions will be communicated shortly.

1. **Adverb**: temporarily (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For a limited period of time; not permanently; for the time being; in a way that lasts only for a short time
   - *Position*: Mid-sentence (before main verb) or sentence-initial
   
   **Synonyms**: provisionally, for now, for the time being, momentarily, briefly
   
   **Antonyms**: permanently, indefinitely, forever, continuously, always
   
   **When to Use**: Describing interim states, provisional arrangements, short-term situations, changes expected to reverse
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Situations requiring permanent solutions; avoid when duration is actually indefinite despite claims
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - temporarily suspended
   - temporarily unavailable
   - temporarily delayed
   - temporarily closed
   - stored temporarily
   
   **Root Analysis**: temporary (limited time) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin tempus (time) → temporary → temporarily (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The service is temporarily down - should be back up soon.
   - I'm temporarily staying with my parents while I look for an apartment.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project has been temporarily suspended pending budget approval.
   - Access to the system is temporarily restricted for maintenance purposes.

1. **Adverb**: indefinitely (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For an unlimited or unspecified period of time; without a fixed end date; continuing without clearly defined limits
   - *Position*: Typically end position or mid-sentence
   
   **Synonyms**: endlessly, continuously, perpetually, for an unspecified time, without limit
   
   **Antonyms**: briefly, temporarily, momentarily, for a specific time, limitedly
   
   **When to Use**: Describing situations without clear end dates; postponements without new dates; open-ended arrangements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When a timeframe exists (even if approximate); contexts requiring optimism about resolution
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - postponed indefinitely
   - delayed indefinitely
   - suspended indefinitely
   - continue indefinitely
   - wait indefinitely
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + definite (clearly defined) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin definire (to limit) → indefinite → indefinitely (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The concert has been postponed indefinitely - no new date yet.
   - I can't wait indefinitely; I need an answer soon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed legislation has been delayed indefinitely due to parliamentary procedures.
   - The contract renewal remains suspended indefinitely pending legal review.

1. **Adverb**: eventually (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At some point in the future; in the end; after a period of time, difficulty, or uncertainty; sooner or later
   - *Position*: Sentence-initial, mid-sentence, or end position
   
   **Synonyms**: ultimately, finally, in the end, sooner or later, at length
   
   **Antonyms**: immediately, instantly, never, currently, at once
   
   **When to Use**: Describing outcomes that will occur but timing is uncertain; expressing patient expectation; narrative conclusions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Situations requiring immediate action; contexts where timing commitment is needed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - will eventually
   - must eventually
   - should eventually
   - eventually lead to
   - eventually result in
   
   **Root Analysis**: eventual (final, ultimate) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin eventus (outcome) → eventual → eventually (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They'll eventually make a decision - just give them time.
   - The pending items will eventually get resolved.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The pending applications will eventually be processed in order of submission.
   - All outstanding matters must eventually be addressed through proper channels.

1. **Adverb**: imminently (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In the very near future; about to happen at any moment; with imminent timing
   - *Position*: Mid-sentence or end position
   
   **Synonyms**: soon, shortly, momentarily, presently, any moment now
   
   **Antonyms**: distantly, remotely, eventually, in the long term, never
   
   **When to Use**: Situations requiring urgency; warnings of immediate events; emphasizing near-term occurrence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Distant future events; routine scheduling; when precision timing matters more than urgency
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - imminently expected
   - due imminently
   - arriving imminently
   - imminently pending
   - will imminently
   
   **Root Analysis**: imminent (about to happen) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin imminere (to overhang) → imminent → imminently (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The decision is expected imminently - probably by end of day.
   - She's arriving imminently; her flight just landed.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - A resolution is anticipated imminently following the final deliberations.
   - The regulatory announcement is expected imminently, pending executive approval.

1. **Adverb**: provisionally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a temporary or conditional manner; subject to change or confirmation; as an interim arrangement
   - *Position*: Mid-sentence (before main verb) or sentence-initial
   
   **Synonyms**: tentatively, conditionally, temporarily, for now, subject to confirmation
   
   **Antonyms**: definitively, finally, permanently, unconditionally, conclusively
   
   **When to Use**: Describing conditional arrangements, preliminary decisions, subject-to-change plans
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Final decisions; binding commitments; situations requiring certainty
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - provisionally approved
   - provisionally scheduled
   - provisionally accepted
   - agreed provisionally
   - provisionally allocated
   
   **Root Analysis**: provision (temporary arrangement) + -al (adjective) + -ly (adverb)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin provisio (foresight) → provisional → provisionally (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We've provisionally booked the venue, but we can still change it.
   - I've been provisionally accepted, pending final transcript review.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The candidate has been provisionally selected, subject to background verification.
   - Funding has been provisionally allocated pending budget committee approval.

1. **Adverb**: subsequently (type: time/sequence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Afterwards; following in time or order; occurring at a later time; as a consequence or result
   - *Position*: Sentence-initial or mid-sentence
   
   **Synonyms**: afterwards, later, thereafter, then, following that
   
   **Antonyms**: previously, before, earlier, prior to, beforehand
   
   **When to Use**: Formal narrative sequences; describing events in chronological order; academic and professional writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (prefer "then" or "later"); contexts requiring immediate timeframe
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - subsequently decided
   - subsequently approved
   - was subsequently
   - subsequently determined
   - subsequently resolved
   
   **Root Analysis**: subsequent (following) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin subsequi (to follow after) → subsequent → subsequently (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The issue was pending for weeks, but subsequently got resolved.
   - I applied in March and was subsequently accepted in April.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application remained pending until it was subsequently approved by the board.
   - The matter was initially tabled and subsequently addressed in the following session.

1. **Adverb**: promptly (type: manner/time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without delay; immediately or very soon; quickly and without hesitation; at the expected or proper time
   - *Position*: Mid-sentence (before main verb or after auxiliary) or end position
   
   **Synonyms**: immediately, quickly, swiftly, without delay, expeditiously
   
   **Antonyms**: slowly, belatedly, eventually, tardily, delayed
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing timely response; professional expectations for speed; service quality contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Situations where speed wasn't actually fast; contexts where gradual process is appropriate
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond promptly
   - addressed promptly
   - resolved promptly
   - act promptly
   - completed promptly
   
   **Root Analysis**: prompt (quick, ready) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin promptus (brought forth) → prompt → promptly (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please reply promptly so we can finalize the plans.
   - The pending items were promptly addressed after the meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All pending matters will be addressed promptly upon receipt of required documentation.
   - The committee will respond promptly to all outstanding inquiries within 48 hours.
