# Multi-Word Expressions: Pending

1. **Expression**: pending approval (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Waiting for official authorization or consent; in a state where permission has been requested but not yet granted
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed partnership; "pending" naturally collocates with "approval")
   
   **Synonyms**: awaiting approval, subject to approval, under consideration for approval, requiring authorization
   
   **Antonyms**: approved, authorized, rejected, denied
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts discussing proposals, requests, or applications requiring authorization; project management; formal business communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "waiting for the okay" suffices; when approval is already granted or definitively denied
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pending final approval
   - pending management approval
   - pending regulatory approval
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Pending" semantically indicates suspension/waiting state; "approval" is one of the most common events requiring waiting in professional contexts; the combination is conventional in business/administrative language
   
   **Root Analysis**: pending (Latin pendere, to hang) + approval (Latin approbare, to approve)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The project is pending approval from the boss.
   - Everything's ready, just pending approval now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract implementation remains pending approval from the executive committee.
   - Funding allocations are pending approval by the board of directors.

1. **Expression**: remain pending (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Continue to be unresolved or undecided; stay in a state of waiting for action or decision
   - *Fixedness*: medium (preferred partnership; "remain" commonly collocates with status adjectives including "pending")
   
   **Synonyms**: stay unresolved, continue to be outstanding, be still undecided, stay in limbo
   
   **Antonyms**: be resolved, get finalized, be completed, be settled
   
   **When to Use**: Formal status updates, professional communication emphasizing ongoing unresolved state, documentation of continuous pending status
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual updates (use "still pending"), when simpler phrasing is clearer, repetitive use in short text
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - remains pending
   - will remain pending
   - continued to remain pending
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Remain" expresses continuation of state; "pending" is a state adjective; together they emphasize ongoing suspension, which is semantically coherent and conventionally used
   
   **Root Analysis**: remain (Latin remanere, to stay behind) + pending (Latin pendere, to hang)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The issue remains pending until they decide what to do.
   - Several tasks remain pending on my list.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application remains pending review by the regulatory authority.
   - Multiple compliance matters remain pending resolution despite repeated follow-up attempts.

1. **Expression**: pending further notice (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Until additional information or instruction is provided; temporary status continuing until new announcement is made
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed; standard administrative/legal phrase)
   
   **Synonyms**: until further notice, until otherwise notified, until we hear otherwise, for the time being
   
   **Antonyms**: effective immediately, as of now, finalized, permanently established
   
   **When to Use**: Official announcements of temporary measures, administrative communications, policy statements, formal notices
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual communication, when specific timeline exists (use that instead), informal team updates
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - until further notice (more common variant)
   - pending further instruction
   - pending further communication
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: Standard bureaucratic/administrative language; historically used in formal notices; "pending" + "further" + "notice" forms conventional phrase pattern in institutional communication
   
   **Root Analysis**: pending + further (Old English furthor) + notice (Latin notitia)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The office is closed pending further notice.
   - Just keep doing what you're doing, pending further notice.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Operations shall continue under current protocols pending further notice from executive management.
   - The policy remains in effect pending further notice to all stakeholders.

1. **Expression**: keep [someone] posted (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Continue to inform someone about developments; provide regular updates about progress or changes regarding pending matters
   - *Fixedness*: strong (idiomatic; fixed form)
   
   **Synonyms**: keep [someone] updated, keep [someone] informed, provide ongoing updates, keep [someone] in the loop
   
   **Antonyms**: keep [someone] in the dark, withhold information from, fail to inform
   
   **When to Use**: Informal and semi-formal contexts about ongoing situations; expressing commitment to provide updates; managing expectations about pending matters
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal written communication (use "provide updates"), when no ongoing communication is intended
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'll keep you posted
   - keep me posted
   - keeping everyone posted
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "Posted" originally refers to posting letters/notices; idiomatically evolved to mean "informed"; conventional phrase in English since early 20th century
   
   **Root Analysis**: keep + posted (from "post" - deliver mail/notices)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll keep you posted on the approval status.
   - Keep me posted when you hear anything!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I will keep you posted regarding any developments on this pending matter.
   - *"Typically semi-formal; in very formal contexts, prefer 'provide ongoing updates'"*

1. **Expression**: in limbo (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal meaning: In an intermediate or transitional state
   - Figurative/Idiomatic meaning: In uncertain or suspended state waiting for decision; stuck between two states without resolution
   - *Fixedness*: strong (idiomatic expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: in suspension, in an uncertain state, stuck waiting, in a holding pattern, unresolved
   
   **Antonyms**: resolved, settled, decided, finalized, moving forward
   
   **When to Use**: Informal and semi-formal discussions of frustrating pending situations; expressing emotional aspect of waiting; narrative descriptions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal documentation (use "pending" or "unresolved"), legal documents, when neutral tone is required
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - left in limbo
   - stuck in limbo
   - hanging in limbo
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: From Catholic theology "limbus" (edge/border); metaphorically represents state between heaven and hell, adapted to mean suspended/uncertain state; culturally embedded expression since medieval period
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + limbo (Latin limbus, edge/border/hem)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My application is in limbo - no idea when I'll hear back.
   - We've been in limbo for weeks waiting for approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project has been in limbo pending executive decision for an extended period.
   - *"Use cautiously in formal contexts; prefer 'pending' or 'suspended' for maximum formality"*

1. **Expression**: up in the air (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal meaning: Physically suspended above ground
   - Figurative/Idiomatic meaning: Uncertain, undecided, or unresolved; not yet determined or settled
   - *Fixedness*: strong (idiomatic)
   
   **Synonyms**: uncertain, undecided, unresolved, pending, yet to be determined, in question
   
   **Antonyms**: settled, decided, resolved, confirmed, definite, nailed down
   
   **When to Use**: Informal and semi-formal contexts discussing uncertain plans or pending decisions; conversational tone about undecided matters
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal reports or legal documents, when expressing absolute precision is required, serious/grave situations
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - still up in the air
   - everything's up in the air
   - leave things up in the air
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Visual metaphor of something floating without landing represents lack of settlement; common English idiom since 19th century expressing uncertainty
   
   **Root Analysis**: up + in + the + air (metaphorical expression)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The whole project is up in the air right now - waiting on funding.
   - Whether we go ahead is still up in the air.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The implementation timeline remains up in the air pending budget approval.
   - *"Acceptable in semi-formal business contexts but avoid in highly formal documentation"*

1. **Expression**: on hold (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Temporarily stopped or suspended; paused pending further action or decision; in a state of postponement
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed collocation, especially in business contexts)
   
   **Synonyms**: paused, suspended, postponed, deferred, put on ice, shelved temporarily
   
   **Antonyms**: active, proceeding, ongoing, resumed, greenlit, moving forward
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts about suspended projects/activities; business communication about delayed matters; customer service about waiting
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When something is permanently cancelled (use "cancelled" instead), very formal legal writing preferring "suspended"
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - put on hold
   - placed on hold
   - currently on hold
   - remains on hold
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Originally from telephone terminology (caller waiting); extended metaphorically to any suspended activity; "on" + "hold" creates strong conventional partnership in modern business English
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (preposition of state) + hold (grasp, keep)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The project's on hold until we get the green light.
   - Everything's on hold pending approval from above.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The initiative has been placed on hold pending resolution of outstanding compliance issues.
   - Operations remain on hold until regulatory approval is secured.

1. **Expression**: pending review (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Waiting for examination or assessment; awaiting evaluation or inspection before decision can be made
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed; "pending" naturally collocates with "review" in administrative/professional contexts)
   
   **Synonyms**: awaiting review, under review, awaiting examination, subject to review, requiring assessment
   
   **Antonyms**: reviewed, assessed, evaluated, approved post-review, rejected after review
   
   **When to Use**: Professional and academic contexts about documents/applications awaiting assessment; administrative communication; formal status updates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "waiting to be checked"), when review is complete
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pending further review
   - pending final review
   - pending management review
   - pending peer review (academic)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Review" is common administrative/professional activity requiring time; "pending" naturally describes waiting state before review completion; conventional bureaucratic language
   
   **Root Analysis**: pending + review (Latin re + videre, to see again)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My paper is pending review by the professor.
   - The request is pending review - should hear back soon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application remains pending review by the credentials committee.
   - All submissions are pending review by the editorial board prior to publication consideration.

1. **Expression**: await a decision (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Wait for a determination or judgment to be made; be in a state of expectation for an authoritative choice
   - *Fixedness*: medium (strong preferred partnership; "await" commonly used with "decision")
   
   **Synonyms**: wait for a decision, expect a ruling, anticipate a determination, await judgment
   
   **Antonyms**: receive a decision, decision made, decision rendered, decision delivered
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and professional contexts about pending judgments; academic and legal situations; professional status communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual contexts (use "waiting to hear"), when decision has been made, informal conversation
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - awaiting a decision
   - await final decision
   - await the decision
   - await your decision
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Await" is formal verb for waiting; "decision" is common object requiring waiting in professional/administrative contexts; semantic compatibility makes natural partnership
   
   **Root Analysis**: await (Old French a- + wait) + decision (Latin decisio, cutting off)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're awaiting a decision from the committee.
   - Still awaiting a decision on the proposal.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The petitioners await a decision from the appellate court.
   - Stakeholders await a decision regarding the proposed merger pending regulatory review.

1. **Expression**: hang in the balance (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal meaning: Be suspended or poised
   - Figurative/Idiomatic meaning: Be in uncertain state where outcome could go either way; be at critical point where decision will determine future
   - *Fixedness*: strong (idiomatic expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: be undecided, be uncertain, be at a critical juncture, rest on a knife-edge, be in question
   
   **Antonyms**: be settled, be decided, be certain, be guaranteed, be assured
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing high-stakes nature of pending decisions; dramatic or narrative contexts; expressing gravity of uncertain situation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Routine pending matters without significant stakes, overly dramatic for minor issues, when neutral tone is required
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - hangs in the balance
   - hanging in the balance
   - hung in the balance
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Ancient metaphor from weighing scales; balance beam determining outcome; suggests delicate equilibrium where small change tips result; historical idiom dating to medieval period
   
   **Root Analysis**: hang + in + the + balance (metaphor from scales/weighing)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My whole future hangs in the balance waiting for this decision.
   - The project's success hangs in the balance pending approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization's accreditation hangs in the balance pending resolution of outstanding compliance issues.
   - The fate of the proposed legislation hangs in the balance as deliberations continue.

1. **Expression**: under consideration (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Being thought about or examined; receiving attention and deliberation; in process of being evaluated for decision
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed; standard administrative phrase)
   
   **Synonyms**: being considered, under review, under evaluation, being deliberated, receiving attention
   
   **Antonyms**: decided upon, rejected, approved, dismissed from consideration, finalized
   
   **When to Use**: Professional and administrative contexts about matters being actively deliberated; polite way to indicate pending status; formal communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (use "thinking about it"), when no actual consideration is happening, when decision is already made
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - currently under consideration
   - under active consideration
   - under serious consideration
   - under careful consideration
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Under" indicates state of being subject to something; "consideration" is formal noun for thinking/deliberation; conventional bureaucratic/administrative language pattern
   
   **Root Analysis**: under + consideration (Latin considerare, to examine carefully)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your proposal is under consideration by the team.
   - The idea is under consideration - we'll decide soon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application remains under consideration by the selection committee.
   - Multiple strategic options are currently under consideration pending further analysis.

1. **Expression**: in abeyance (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a state of temporary suspension or inactivity; held in reserve awaiting future action; pending but not actively progressing
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed; formal/legal term)
   
   **Synonyms**: suspended, dormant, in suspension, on hold, temporarily inactive, pending
   
   **Antonyms**: active, operational, proceeding, in force, activated
   
   **When to Use**: Legal contexts, very formal professional communication, technical discussions of suspended rights or processes
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (too formal/archaic), when simpler terms like "on hold" are clearer, general audience communication
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - held in abeyance
   - remain in abeyance
   - kept in abeyance
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: Legal/formal term from Old French "abeance" (expectation, gaping); historically used in law to describe suspended rights or claims; maintains specialized formal usage
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + abeyance (Old French abeance, from "beer," to gape/expect)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The plan is in abeyance until further notice.
   - *"Too formal for casual use; better: 'The plan is on hold'"*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract provisions shall remain in abeyance pending resolution of the disputed terms.
   - Certain regulatory requirements have been held in abeyance during the transition period.

1. **Expression**: wait and see (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Postpone action or judgment until more information becomes available; be patient while situation develops; await outcome before deciding
   - *Fixedness*: strong (idiomatic binomial expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: hold off and observe, be patient, await developments, take a wait-and-see approach, reserve judgment
   
   **Antonyms**: act immediately, decide now, take action, move forward, commit now
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing cautious approach to pending decisions, expressing patience about uncertain outcomes, informal strategy discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Situations requiring immediate action, when procrastination is inappropriate, very formal documentation
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - wait-and-see approach
   - let's wait and see
   - wait and see what happens
   - adopt a wait-and-see attitude
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: Binomial expression (two words joined by "and"); "wait" (passive patience) + "see" (observe outcome) form complementary pair; conventional English idiom expressing cautious deferral
   
   **Root Analysis**: wait (Old English w√¶tan) + and + see (Old English seon)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's just wait and see what the boss decides.
   - I'm taking a wait-and-see approach with this pending approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee has adopted a wait-and-see posture pending further developments.
   - Given the pending regulatory changes, a wait-and-see approach is advisable.
