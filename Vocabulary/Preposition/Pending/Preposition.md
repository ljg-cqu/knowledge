# Prepositions: Pending

1. **Preposition**: pending (type: simple preposition/compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Until something happens; during the time before an event; while awaiting
   - *(Abstract)* Subject to; awaiting; conditional upon
   
   **Synonyms**: until (in temporal contexts), awaiting (in formal contexts), while waiting for, subject to
   
   **Antonyms**: following, after, subsequent to, upon completion of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing and legal documents, professional communication about temporary states, expressing conditions that must be met
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "until" or "while waiting"), when simpler prepositions like "until" are equally clear and more natural
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pending approval, pending review, pending further notice, pending investigation
   - **Verb + prep**: remain pending, be pending, stay pending
   - **Adjective + prep**: (pending functions as adjective more than with adjective combinations)
   - **Noun + prep**: matter pending, decision pending, status pending
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin pendere (to hang, be suspended)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin pendere (to hang) → Old French pendre → English "pending" (15th century), originally meaning "hanging" or "suspended"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everything's on hold pending the boss's approval.
   - Pending further instructions, just keep doing what you're doing.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract shall remain in effect pending completion of the due diligence process.
   - Pending resolution of outstanding compliance issues, all operations will continue under current protocols.

2. **Preposition**: until (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Up to the point in time when; up to the time of; continuing up to a specified time or event
   - *(Spatial)* Up to a specified place or point (less common)
   
   **Synonyms**: till, up to, pending, before, up until
   
   **Antonyms**: since, from, after, following
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing temporal boundaries, indicating duration of pending states, everyday communication about time limits
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "pending" better captures formal suspended state; in spatial contexts where "to" is clearer
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: until now, until then, until further notice, until completion
   - **Verb + prep**: wait until, remain until, continue until, stay until
   - **Adjective + prep**: pending until, valid until, effective until
   - **Noun + prep**: delay until, postponement until
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English und (up to) + til (to)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "und" + "til" → Middle English "until" (12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The project's on hold until we get the green light.
   - Just wait until they make a decision.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application will remain pending until all required documentation has been submitted.
   - Operations shall continue under existing guidelines until such time as new protocols are approved.

3. **Preposition**: for (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal - Duration)* During the continuance of; throughout the period of; for the length of time
   - *(Purpose)* With the purpose of; intended for
   - *(Cause)* Because of; on account of
   
   **Synonyms**: during (temporal), throughout (temporal), awaiting (in pending contexts)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, despite (in some contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing duration of pending states, indicating purpose of waiting, explaining reasons for delays
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When duration is not the focus; when other prepositions are more precise
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pending for weeks, waiting for approval, for review, for consideration
   - **Verb + prep**: wait for, pending for, look for, hope for
   - **Adjective + prep**: eligible for, ready for, due for
   - **Noun + prep**: request for, need for, reason for, time for
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "for" (before, in front of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "for" → Middle English (unchanged usage continues)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It's been pending for three weeks now.
   - We've been waiting for approval for way too long.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter has been pending review for an extended period exceeding standard processing times.
   - The application has remained under consideration for approximately six weeks pending additional verification.

4. **Preposition**: under (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - State)* In a state or condition of; subject to; undergoing
   - *(Hierarchical)* Subject to the authority or control of
   
   **Synonyms**: subject to, undergoing, in the process of, within
   
   **Antonyms**: over, above, beyond, outside of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts describing states of review or consideration, professional language about processes, administrative descriptions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When literal physical position is meant; casual contexts preferring simpler constructions
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: under review, under consideration, under investigation, under examination, under deliberation
   - **Verb + prep**: be under, remain under, come under, fall under
   - **Adjective + prep**: (under typically follows verb rather than adjective)
   - **Noun + prep**: matter under review, case under investigation
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "under" (beneath, below)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "under" → extended metaphorically to mean "subject to" (process/authority)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The proposal is under review by the team.
   - Your application is still under consideration.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter remains under consideration by the oversight committee pending receipt of additional documentation.
   - All applications are currently under review by the credentials evaluation board.

5. **Preposition**: in (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(State)* Existing within a particular state or condition; characterized by
   - *(Temporal)* During; within the course of; at the end of a period
   
   **Synonyms**: within, during, inside (depending on context)
   
   **Antonyms**: out of, beyond, outside
   
   **When to Use**: Describing states of suspension or uncertainty, temporal contexts about timing, general state descriptions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When more specific prepositions better capture the relationship
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in limbo, in abeyance, in suspension, in progress, in the meantime, in consideration
   - **Verb + prep**: be in, remain in, stay in, result in
   - **Adjective + prep**: stuck in, caught in, held in
   - **Noun + prep**: state in, delay in, matter in
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "in" (inside, within)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "in" (within) → extended to abstract states
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The decision is in limbo right now.
   - Everything's in a holding pattern pending approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposal remains in abeyance pending executive committee deliberation.
   - The contract is currently in suspension pending resolution of disputed terms.

6. **Preposition**: on (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(State)* In a state or condition of; engaged in; continuing
   - *(Temporal)* At the time of; during
   
   **Synonyms**: upon (more formal), in (in some contexts)
   
   **Antonyms**: off, beyond
   
   **When to Use**: Describing suspended or paused states, indicating conditions, professional contexts about temporary statuses
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other prepositions more precisely capture the relationship
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: on hold, on pause, on standby, on ice (informal), on the back burner (informal)
   - **Verb + prep**: put on, place on, be on, remain on
   - **Adjective + prep**: pending on (less common; "depending on" is more typical)
   - **Noun + prep**: project on hold, status on record
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "on" (in position upon)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "on" → extended to abstract states and conditions
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The project's on hold until we get funding.
   - Everything's on pause pending the decision.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The initiative has been placed on hold pending resolution of outstanding compliance matters.
   - Operations remain on standby pending final authorization from regulatory authorities.

7. **Preposition**: to (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Direction/Purpose)* Expressing direction or purpose; indicating intended recipient or target
   - *(Extent)* Up to; as far as; indicating limit or boundary
   
   **Synonyms**: toward, until (in temporal contexts), for (purpose)
   
   **Antonyms**: from, away from
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating goals of pending processes, expressing purposes of waiting, showing directional relationships
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When static state rather than direction/purpose is emphasized
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: subject to approval, according to schedule, prior to decision, subsequent to review
   - **Verb + prep**: wait to hear, need to decide, expect to receive, plan to implement
   - **Adjective + prep**: subject to, due to, according to, prior to
   - **Noun + prep**: response to inquiry, answer to question, reply to request
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "to" (in the direction of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "to" → continues with similar usage
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting to hear back about the application.
   - It's subject to approval from management.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter is subject to final determination by the appellate authority.
   - Implementation is contingent upon and subject to regulatory approval.

8. **Preposition**: from (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Source)* Indicating the source, origin, or starting point
   - *(Temporal)* Starting at a particular time; since
   - *(Cause)* Because of; as a result of
   
   **Synonyms**: since (temporal), because of (causal), originating from
   
   **Antonyms**: to, toward, until
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating sources of decisions or information, expressing origins of delays, showing temporal starting points of pending states
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When direction or destination is focus rather than origin
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: from the committee, from management, from the start, from last week
   - **Verb + prep**: hear from, receive from, await from, expect from, result from
   - **Adjective + prep**: pending from, awaiting from (less common combinations)
   - **Noun + prep**: response from, approval from, decision from, word from
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "from" (forward, away)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "from" → Middle English (similar usage continues)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Still waiting to hear from the hiring manager.
   - We haven't received approval from headquarters yet.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application remains pending review and determination from the credentials committee.
   - Authorization from the executive board is required before implementation may commence.

9. **Preposition**: by (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal - Deadline)* Not later than; on or before a specified time
   - *(Agency)* Through the action or means of; indicating agent
   
   **Synonyms**: before (temporal), no later than (temporal), through the action of (agency)
   
   **Antonyms**: after (temporal), until (continues up to rather than deadline)
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing deadlines for pending matters, indicating agents of decisions, showing time limits
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "until" better expresses continuing state; when deadline is not the focus
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: by Friday, by the end of the month, by next week, approved by management
   - **Verb + prep**: decide by, approve by, review by, complete by
   - **Adjective + prep**: pending by (uncommon), due by, required by
   - **Noun + prep**: deadline by, decision by, approval by, determination by
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "bi" (near, by)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "bi" → Middle English "by" (near, through means of)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We should hear back by Friday.
   - The decision needs to be made by next week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All pending applications must be resolved by the close of the fiscal quarter.
   - Determination by the regulatory authority is anticipated by the end of the review period.

10. **Preposition**: upon (type: compound preposition)
    **Meaning**: 
    - *(Temporal)* Immediately after; at the time of; when
    - *(Conditional)* Contingent on; dependent on; subject to
    
    **Synonyms**: on (less formal), at the time of, when, immediately after
    
    **Antonyms**: before, prior to, in advance of
    
    **When to Use**: Formal writing expressing conditions or timing, legal documents, professional contexts requiring formal tone
    
    **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "when" or "after"), when simpler alternatives are clearer
    
    **Common Patterns**: 
    - **Prepositional phrases**: upon approval, upon completion, upon receipt, upon determination, upon resolution
    - **Verb + prep**: depend upon, contingent upon, pending upon (less common), act upon
    - **Adjective + prep**: conditional upon, dependent upon, based upon
    - **Noun + prep**: decision upon, action upon
    
    **Root Analysis**: up + on (combining to mean "on top of" or "at the time of")
    
    **Etymology**: Old English "uppan" (on) → Middle English "upon" (more formal variant of "on")
    
    **Examples [Casual]**: 
    - We'll start upon getting approval.
    - Upon hearing the decision, we'll move forward.
    
    **Examples [Formal]**: 
    - Implementation shall commence upon receipt of final regulatory approval.
    - The contract becomes effective upon resolution of all pending contingencies and execution by authorized signatories.
