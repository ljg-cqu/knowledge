# Nouns: Pending

1. **Noun**: approval (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Official permission or consent for something to happen or be done; the act of approving something; positive acceptance or agreement
   - *(Sense 2)* Favorable opinion or attitude toward something
   
   **Synonyms**: authorization, permission, consent, sanction, endorsement
   
   **Antonyms**: rejection, denial, disapproval, refusal, veto
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts discussing authorization processes, project management, formal requests requiring permission, administrative procedures
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual contexts where "okay" or "go-ahead" suffices; when discussing personal opinions (use "opinion" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending approval
   - seek approval
   - grant approval
   - approval process
   - final approval
   
   **Root Analysis**: ap- (to) + prove (test, demonstrate) + -al (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin approbare (to approve) → Old French aprover → English "approval" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting for approval from the boss before starting.
   - The plan needs approval before we can move forward.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposal remains pending approval from the executive committee.
   - Regulatory approval must be secured before implementation can commence.

1. **Noun**: decision (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A conclusion or resolution reached after consideration; the act of making up one's mind about something; an authoritative judgment or determination
   
   **Synonyms**: determination, resolution, judgment, ruling, verdict
   
   **Antonyms**: indecision, uncertainty, ambiguity, hesitation
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts involving choices or judgments; professional decision-making, legal contexts, everyday situations requiring choices
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to ongoing process rather than conclusion (use "deliberation" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - await a decision
   - make a decision
   - pending decision
   - final decision
   - decision-making process
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (off, away) + cis (cut) + -ion (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin decidere (to cut off, determine) → Old French → English "decision" (15th century), literally "cutting off" uncertainty
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting on a decision about the project.
   - No decision has been made yet.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter remains pending a decision from the appellate court.
   - Stakeholders await the committee's decision regarding the proposed amendments.

1. **Noun**: review (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal assessment or examination of something with the possibility or intention of instituting change if necessary; the act of reviewing or reconsidering
   - *(Sense 2)* A critical evaluation or report on something
   
   **Synonyms**: examination, assessment, evaluation, analysis, inspection
   
   **Antonyms**: approval (without examination), neglect, oversight
   
   **When to Use**: Professional and academic contexts about evaluation processes, quality assurance, performance assessment, formal examination procedures
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "check" suffices; when no formal evaluation is involved
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending review
   - under review
   - conduct a review
   - review process
   - subject to review
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again) + view (see)
   
   **Etymology**: French revoir (to see again) → English "review" (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The application is pending review by the team.
   - Your paper is still under review.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy remains pending comprehensive review by the oversight committee.
   - All submissions undergo rigorous peer review prior to publication consideration.

1. **Noun**: resolution (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action of solving a problem or contentious matter; a firm decision to do or not to do something; the conclusion or settlement of an issue
   - *(Sense 2)* A formal expression of opinion or intention agreed upon by an assembly
   
   **Synonyms**: settlement, solution, conclusion, determination, outcome
   
   **Antonyms**: continuation, perpetuation, unresolved state, impasse
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts about problem-solving, conflict resolution, professional discussions of outcomes, legal settlements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual everyday problems (use "solution" or "fix"), when referring to screen resolution (different meaning)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending resolution
   - seek resolution
   - reach a resolution
   - conflict resolution
   - await resolution
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again, thoroughly) + solv (loosen) + -tion (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin resolvere (to loosen, solve) → resolution (14th century), meaning "dissolving" or "solving"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting for resolution of the dispute.
   - The issue needs quick resolution.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter remains pending resolution through arbitration procedures.
   - Pending resolution of outstanding compliance issues, operations will continue under interim protocols.

1. **Noun**: outcome (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The result or consequence of an action, process, or situation; the way something turns out; the final product or end result
   
   **Synonyms**: result, consequence, conclusion, upshot, end result
   
   **Antonyms**: beginning, initiation, cause, input
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing results of pending processes, expressing uncertainty about future results, professional analysis of consequences
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "result" is simpler and equally clear; when discussing immediate effects vs. final outcomes
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending outcome
   - await the outcome
   - final outcome
   - uncertain outcome
   - outcome of the decision
   
   **Root Analysis**: out + come
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English "out" + "come" → "outcome" (17th century), literally "that which comes out"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting to see the outcome of the vote.
   - The outcome is still pending.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The investigation continues pending determination of the final outcome.
   - Strategic planning must account for multiple potential outcomes of the pending negotiations.

1. **Noun**: delay (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A period of time by which something is late or postponed; the act of postponing or slowing something; an instance of being made to wait
   
   **Synonyms**: postponement, holdup, setback, deferment, lag
   
   **Antonyms**: acceleration, advancement, expedition, promptness
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing timeline problems, explaining why things are pending longer than expected, logistics and scheduling contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When postponement is intentional and strategic (use "postponement" or "deferral"); when describing permanent cancellation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - cause a delay
   - experience delays
   - without delay
   - lengthy delay
   - due to delays
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (away, from) + lay (Old French laier, to leave)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French delaier (to put off) → English "delay" (13th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's been a delay in processing your request.
   - Sorry for the delay - it's still pending approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The application has experienced significant delays pending additional documentation.
   - Unforeseen delays have resulted in extended pending status for multiple initiatives.

1. **Noun**: status (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The current state or condition of something; the relative position or standing of something; the situation at a particular time
   - *(Sense 2)* High rank or social standing (different context)
   
   **Synonyms**: state, condition, position, situation, standing
   
   **Antonyms**: (no direct antonyms; contextual opposites like "change" vs. static status)
   
   **When to Use**: Professional communication about current states, project management, inquiries about progress, administrative tracking
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "state" or "condition" is simpler; when referring to social status (different meaning)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending status
   - status update
   - check the status
   - current status
   - status report
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin status (position, condition, state)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin status (state of standing) → English "status" (18th century, popularized 19th-20th centuries)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the status of that approval?
   - The status is still pending - no update yet.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The current status of the application is pending review by the credentials committee.
   - A comprehensive status report will be provided once all pending matters are resolved.

1. **Noun**: determination (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The process of establishing something exactly by calculation or research; an official decision or settlement; a firm or fixed intention
   - *(Sense 2)* Firmness of purpose or resolve (different connotation)
   
   **Synonyms**: decision, resolution, conclusion, judgment, settlement
   
   **Antonyms**: indecision, uncertainty, ambiguity, irresolution
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and legal contexts about official decisions, technical contexts requiring precise establishment, administrative final judgments
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "decision"); when referring to personal resolve rather than official decision
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending determination
   - final determination
   - make a determination
   - await determination
   - reach a determination
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (completely) + termin (boundary, limit) + -ation (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin determinare (to limit, fix boundaries) → determination (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're waiting for a determination on the appeal.
   - No determination has been made yet.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The case remains pending final determination by the administrative law judge.
   - Pending determination of eligibility, applicants should maintain current documentation.

1. **Noun**: limbo (U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An uncertain period of awaiting a decision or resolution; a state of neglect or oblivion; an intermediate or transitional state
   - *(Sense 2)* In Catholic theology, the supposed abode of unbaptized infants and righteous people who died before Christ
   
   **Synonyms**: uncertainty, suspended state, purgatory (metaphorical), pending state, indeterminate condition
   
   **Antonyms**: resolution, certainty, settled state, conclusion
   
   **When to Use**: Informal and semi-formal discussions emphasizing frustration with pending situations, narrative descriptions, expressing emotional aspect of waiting
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal documentation (use "pending" or "suspended state"), legal contexts, when neutral terminology is required
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in limbo
   - left in limbo
   - stuck in limbo
   - languish in limbo
   - escape limbo
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin limbus (edge, border, hem)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin limbus → Medieval Latin "limbus patrum" (limbo of the fathers) → English "limbo" (14th century), extended metaphorically to mean uncertain state
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My application is stuck in limbo - no idea when I'll hear back.
   - We've been in limbo for weeks waiting for approval.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project has remained in a state of limbo pending executive decision.
   - *"Use cautiously in formal contexts; prefer 'pending' or 'suspended state'"*

1. **Noun**: deliberation (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Long and careful consideration or discussion; the process of carefully considering or discussing something; thoughtful reflection before decision
   
   **Synonyms**: consideration, reflection, contemplation, discussion, thought
   
   **Antonyms**: hastiness, impulsiveness, rashness, snap decision
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts about decision-making processes, legal proceedings, professional discussions emphasizing careful thought, committee work
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "thinking about it"), when quick decisions are made, when minimal consideration occurred
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending deliberation
   - under deliberation
   - careful deliberation
   - after much deliberation
   - deliberation process
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (thoroughly) + liber (weigh, balance) + -ation (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin deliberare (to weigh carefully) → deliberation (14th century), from weighing scales metaphor
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The committee is still in deliberation about the proposal.
   - After long deliberation, they still haven't decided.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The matter remains pending deliberation by the full board at the next quarterly meeting.
   - Following extensive deliberation, the committee will issue a final determination.

1. **Noun**: suspension (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The temporary prevention or interruption of something; the act of stopping something temporarily; a state of being suspended or held in abeyance
   - *(Sense 2)* Temporary removal from duty, position, or privilege
   
   **Synonyms**: postponement, deferment, halt, stoppage, abeyance
   
   **Antonyms**: continuation, resumption, activation, commencement
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts about temporary halts to activities, administrative actions, technical discussions of interrupted processes
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Permanent cancellations (use "termination" or "cancellation"); casual contexts (use "on hold")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending suspension
   - temporary suspension
   - suspension of operations
   - lift the suspension
   - under suspension
   
   **Root Analysis**: sus- (from below) + pend (hang) + -sion (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin suspendere (to hang up) → suspension (16th century), literally "hanging state"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's a temporary suspension of the program pending review.
   - The suspension will be lifted once issues are resolved.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Operations have been placed in suspension pending resolution of outstanding compliance matters.
   - The suspension of new enrollments shall remain in effect until program review is completed.

1. **Noun**: backlog (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An accumulation of uncompleted work, tasks, or matters awaiting action; a reserve or accumulation of unfulfilled orders; pending items that have accumulated
   
   **Synonyms**: accumulation, buildup, arrears, queue, pile-up
   
   **Antonyms**: clearance, completion, current state, caught up
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts about work management, discussing accumulated pending items, explaining delays due to volume
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to single pending item (use "pending item"); when backlog has been cleared
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending backlog
   - clear the backlog
   - work through the backlog
   - backlog of cases
   - reduce backlog
   
   **Root Analysis**: back + log (originally referring to large log at back of fire)
   
   **Etymology**: "Back" + "log" → originally referred to large log at back of fireplace; metaphorically extended to accumulated work (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We have a huge backlog of pending approvals to work through.
   - The backlog keeps growing while we wait for resources.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The department faces a substantial backlog of pending applications requiring expedited processing.
   - Efforts to reduce the backlog of unresolved cases have been intensified through additional staffing allocations.

1. **Noun**: deferral (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action of putting something off to a later time; postponement of an action, event, or obligation; the state of being deferred
   
   **Synonyms**: postponement, delay, suspension, adjournment, putting off
   
   **Antonyms**: advancement, acceleration, immediate action, prompt handling
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts about scheduled postponements, financial contexts (deferred payment), administrative decisions to delay
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (use "putting it off"); when referring to permanent cancellation rather than postponement
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending deferral
   - request a deferral
   - deferral period
   - deferral of payment
   - grant a deferral
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (away) + fer (carry, bear) + -al (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin differre (to postpone) → deferral (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I requested a deferral of my loan payments.
   - The project is pending deferral until next quarter.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee has granted a deferral of the decision pending receipt of additional documentation.
   - Implementation is pending deferral until adequate funding can be secured.
