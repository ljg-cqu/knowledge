1. **Expression**: break down (type: phrasal verb/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Phrasal verb) To stop functioning; to fail mechanically or systematically
   - *(Sense 2)* To divide something into smaller parts for analysis or understanding
   - *(Sense 3)* To lose emotional control; to cry or become upset
   - *(Sense 4)* To decompose or cause to decompose chemically
   - *Fixedness*: strong (core meaning), medium (metaphorical extensions)
   
   **Synonyms**: fail, malfunction (sense 1), analyze, divide, decompose (sense 2), collapse emotionally (sense 3)
   
   **Antonyms**: function properly, work (sense 1), build up, synthesize (sense 2), maintain composure (sense 3)
   
   **When to Use**: Mechanical failures, analytical processes, emotional states, chemical processes; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Multiple meanings require clear context; specify which sense you intend
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - breakdown (noun form)
   - breaking down
   - broken down
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocation*: "Break" implies disruption of wholeness; "down" intensifies complete failure or reduction to components
   - Highly productive phrasal verb with multiple metaphorical extensions from physical breaking
   
   **Root Analysis**: break (separate, fail) + down (to lower state, into parts)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My car broke down on the highway.
   - She just broke down and cried.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The system experienced a catastrophic breakdown during peak operations.
   - Researchers break down complex phenomena into analyzable components.
