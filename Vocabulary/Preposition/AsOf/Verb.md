1. **Verb**: stand (at)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To be at a specified level or amount at a particular time
   - To remain in a specified condition or position
   - *(With "as of")* To be officially recorded or recognized at a certain figure or status at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: be, amount to, total, reach, equal
   
   **Antonyms**: fall (to), drop (to), decline (to)
   
   **When to Use**: In formal reports, financial statements, status updates when stating current figures or positions; commonly used with "as of" to indicate point-in-time values; formal and business registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual conversation where simpler verbs like "is" suffice; when dynamic change is being emphasized rather than static position
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - stand at [amount] as of [date]
   - stands as of [date]
   - currently stands as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "standan" (be in an upright position)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "standan" → Proto-Germanic "*standaną" → developed figurative sense "remain at a certain level" (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The total stands at 50 people as of now.
   - Where do things stand as of today?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Total assets stand at $15.7 million as of December 31, 2023.
   - The membership count stands at 3,247 as of the reporting date.

1. **Verb**: remain
   **Meaning**: 
   - To continue to exist or stay in the same state without change
   - To be left or still present at a specified time
   - *(With "as of")* To continue to be valid, applicable, or outstanding at a particular date
   
   **Synonyms**: stay, continue, persist, endure, be left
   
   **Antonyms**: change, cease, end, conclude, disappear
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing continuity or persistence of a state; commonly with "as of" to indicate ongoing status at a specific time; appropriate across formal and semi-formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing new states or changes; when "is" or "are" would be clearer and simpler
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - remain outstanding as of
   - remain valid as of
   - remain unchanged as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back, again) + main (stay)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "remanere" (stay behind, remain) → Old French "remaindre" → English "remain" (continue in place or condition) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Three items remain as of this morning.
   - The issue remains unresolved as of now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Outstanding obligations remain unpaid as of the statement date.
   - The contract remains in full force and effect as of December 31, 2023.

1. **Verb**: total
   **Meaning**: 
   - To amount to a specified figure when added together
   - To reach a particular sum or aggregate
   - *(With "as of")* To amount to a specific sum at a particular point in time
   
   **Synonyms**: amount to, sum to, come to, add up to, reach
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (calculation result has no direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In financial reports, summaries, calculations; when stating aggregate amounts; commonly with "as of" for temporal precision; business and formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When individual components rather than sums are being discussed; in very casual contexts where simpler language works
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - total [amount] as of [date]
   - totaling as of
   - totals stand at as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: tot (all, whole) + -al (verb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "totus" (all, entire) → Old French "total" → English verb use "amount to" (15th-17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The donations total $5,000 as of today.
   - Expenses totaled $200 as of last Friday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Accounts receivable total $127,450 as of December 31, 2023.
   - Outstanding liabilities total $2.3 million as of the balance sheet date.

1. **Verb**: amount (to)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To be equivalent to or add up to a particular total or quantity
   - To reach a certain sum or value when calculated
   - *(With "as of")* To equal a specific value at a particular time
   
   **Synonyms**: total, come to, equal, reach, sum to
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (quantitative result has no direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In financial and quantitative contexts; when stating what something equals or sums to; with "as of" for temporal precision; formal and business registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When the verb "total" or "equal" would be more direct; in casual contexts where simpler verbs suffice
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - amount to [figure] as of [date]
   - amounts as of
   - amounting to as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (to) + mount (go up, rise)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "amonter" (go up, amount to) → English "amount" (reach a total) (13th-14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The total amounts to around $500 as of now.
   - What does it amount to as of today?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Total compensation amounts to $147,000 as of the effective date.
   - Accumulated depreciation amounts to $3.2 million as of December 31, 2023.

1. **Verb**: report
   **Meaning**: 
   - To give a formal account or statement of facts, information, or events
   - To present information officially or formally
   - *(With "as of")* To formally state information that was accurate at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: state, announce, disclose, present, communicate
   
   **Antonyms**: conceal, hide, suppress, withhold
   
   **When to Use**: In business reports, financial statements, official communications; when presenting formal information; with "as of" to indicate when reported information was current; formal register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual contexts; when informal communication is appropriate; when "say" or "tell" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - report as of [date]
   - as reported as of
   - reporting as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + port (carry)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "reportare" (carry back, relate) → Old French "reporter" → English "report" (give an account) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They reported 50 attendees as of yesterday.
   - What was reported as of last week?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The company reports net income of $2.4 million as of fiscal year-end 2023.
   - Earnings per share as reported as of Q3 2024 were $1.85.

1. **Verb**: update
   **Meaning**: 
   - To make something more modern or current by adding new information
   - To bring information up to date; to revise
   - *(With "as of")* To revise information to be current as of a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: revise, refresh, modify, amend, renew
   
   **Antonyms**: leave unchanged, preserve, maintain (original state)
   
   **When to Use**: When modifying information to be current; in contexts involving data maintenance, record-keeping, documentation; with "as of" to indicate the new currency date; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When information has never existed before (use "create" or "establish"); when no change has been made
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - update as of [date]
   - updated as of
   - last updated as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: up- (to higher/current state) + date (point in time)
   
   **Etymology**: "up" (to bring current) + "date" → compound verb "update" (bring up to date) (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I updated my profile as of yesterday.
   - When was this last updated as of?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Records have been updated as of the date indicated in the document footer.
   - The database was last updated as of 11:59 PM on December 31, 2023.

1. **Verb**: reflect
   **Meaning**: 
   - To show, express, or represent accurately
   - To give evidence or an indication of something
   - *(With "as of")* To accurately represent information or conditions that existed at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: show, demonstrate, indicate, display, represent
   
   **Antonyms**: misrepresent, distort, conceal, obscure
   
   **When to Use**: In reports, financial statements, documentation when stating what information represents; with "as of" to indicate temporal accuracy; formal and business registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to physical reflection (mirrors, light); in casual contexts where simpler verbs work
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reflect as of [date]
   - reflects the status as of
   - accurately reflects as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + flect (bend)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "reflectere" (bend back) → extended to mean "show, express" (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This reflects what we had as of last month.
   - The numbers reflect the situation as of Friday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The financial statements accurately reflect the company's position as of year-end.
   - These figures reflect actual performance as of the close of the reporting period.

1. **Verb**: become (effective/valid/applicable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To begin to be; to start to have a particular quality or status
   - To come into existence or operation
   - *(With "as of")* To take effect or start to apply from a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: take effect, come into force, commence, begin, start
   
   **Antonyms**: cease, end, terminate, expire, become invalid
   
   **When to Use**: When describing transitions to new states; particularly with "effective," "valid," or "applicable"; with "as of" to indicate when the transition occurs; formal and semi-formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing ongoing states rather than transitions; in very casual contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - become effective as of
   - becomes valid as of
   - become applicable as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (about, around) + come (move toward)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "becuman" (come to, happen) → developed sense "come to be" (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The new rules become effective as of Monday.
   - When does this become valid as of?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The amendment becomes effective as of January 1, 2024.
   - This agreement becomes binding as of the date of execution by both parties.

1. **Verb**: indicate
   **Meaning**: 
   - To point out, show, or make known
   - To be a sign or symptom of; to suggest
   - *(With "as of")* To show or state what was true at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: show, reveal, demonstrate, suggest, point to
   
   **Antonyms**: conceal, hide, obscure, suppress
   
   **When to Use**: In reports, analysis, documentation when pointing to evidence or information; with "as of" for temporal precision; formal and academic registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual conversation; when simpler verbs like "show" or "mean" are clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - indicate as of [date]
   - as indicated as of
   - indicators as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (toward) + dic (point out, say) + -ate (verb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "indicare" (point out, show) → English "indicate" (show, point to) (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The report indicates 50 people as of yesterday.
   - What do the numbers indicate as of now?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Financial indicators as of Q3 2024 indicate strong growth momentum.
   - Records indicate that, as of the inspection date, all systems were compliant.

1. **Verb**: confirm
   **Meaning**: 
   - To establish the truth or correctness of something
   - To verify or validate information
   - *(With "as of")* To verify that information was accurate at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: verify, validate, substantiate, corroborate, affirm
   
   **Antonyms**: deny, refute, contradict, dispute, invalidate
   
   **When to Use**: When verifying or validating information; in formal communications requiring confirmation; with "as of" when timing of confirmation matters; formal and semi-formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual contexts where "check" is more natural; when information is being presented for first time rather than confirmed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - confirm as of [date]
   - can confirm that as of
   - confirmed as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together, completely) + firm (make strong, establish)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "confirmare" (make firm, establish) → Old French "confermer" → English "confirm" (verify, establish truth) (13th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you confirm the headcount as of today?
   - I can confirm we had 50 as of yesterday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We confirm that, as of December 31, 2023, all obligations were fulfilled.
   - Please confirm whether the status remains unchanged as of the date of this inquiry.

1. **Verb**: equal
   **Meaning**: 
   - To be the same in amount, number, or value
   - To amount to; to reach a total of
   - *(With "as of")* To have a value or amount that is the same as a specified figure at a particular date
   
   **Synonyms**: amount to, total, come to, match, reach
   
   **Antonyms**: differ from, vary from, exceed, fall short of
   
   **When to Use**: In mathematical, financial, and quantitative contexts; when stating equivalence; with "as of" for temporal precision; neutral to formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When discussing non-quantitative equality; when "total" or "amount to" would be more specific
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - equal [amount] as of [date]
   - equals as of
   - equaling as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: equ (level, even) + -al (verb/adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "aequalis" (equal, uniform) → Latin "aequare" (make equal) → English "equal" (be the same as) (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The total equals about $500 as of now.
   - What does it equal as of today?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Total liabilities equal $2.3 million as of the balance sheet date.
   - Net assets equal $15.7 million as of December 31, 2023.

1. **Verb**: comprise
   **Meaning**: 
   - To consist of; to be made up of
   - To include or contain as parts of a whole
   - *(With "as of")* To be composed of specified elements at a particular date
   
   **Synonyms**: consist of, include, contain, be made up of, incorporate
   
   **Antonyms**: exclude, omit, lack
   
   **When to Use**: In formal descriptions of composition or makeup; when listing components; with "as of" to indicate temporal reference; formal register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual contexts where "include" or "made up of" is more natural; when "include" (part of whole) vs "comprise" (whole contains parts) distinction matters
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - comprise [components] as of [date]
   - comprised as of
   - is comprised of as of (note: traditionally considered incorrect, but increasingly accepted)
   
   **Root Analysis**: com- (together) + prehend (seize, take)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "comprehendere" (grasp, seize) → French "compris" (included) → English "comprise" (contain, consist of) (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The group comprises 50 people as of now.
   - What does the list comprise as of today?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The portfolio comprises 237 individual holdings as of December 31, 2023.
   - As of the reporting date, the board comprises nine independent directors and three executive directors.
