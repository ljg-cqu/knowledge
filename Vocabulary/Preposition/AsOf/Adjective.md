1. **Adjective**: current (gradable: no)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Belonging to the present time; existing or happening now
   - Up to date; in use at this particular time
   - (Financial/Business) Valid, accurate, or applicable at a specified date when used with "as of"
   
   **Synonyms**: present, existing, contemporary, up-to-date, prevailing
   
   **Antonyms**: past, former, outdated, obsolete
   
   **When to Use**: When describing information, data, statistics, or status that applies to a specific point in time; commonly used in business reports, financial statements, and official documents with "as of" phrases
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in historical contexts where "former" or "previous" is more appropriate; don't use for future states
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - current status as of [date]
   - current balance as of [date]
   - current information as of today
   
   **Root Analysis**: curr (run, flow) + -ent (adjective suffix indicating state)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "currere" (to run) → "current" (running, flowing) → evolved to mean "belonging to the present time" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's your current address as of now?
   - My current balance as of today is pretty low.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The current membership count as of December 31, 2023, stands at 5,247 members.
   - Please provide your current employment status as of the application date.

1. **Adjective**: effective (gradable: no)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (When used with dates) In operation or in force from a specified date
   - Actually in effect or operative at a particular time
   - Valid and applicable starting from a specific point in time
   
   **Synonyms**: operative, in force, valid, active, applicable
   
   **Antonyms**: ineffective, expired, void, inactive
   
   **When to Use**: Particularly common with "as of" in legal, business, and policy contexts to indicate when rules, contracts, prices, or changes take effect; emphasizes the beginning of validity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing end dates (use "expires" or "terminates"); not appropriate for casual, informal settings when discussing time
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - effective as of [date]
   - effective immediately
   - becomes effective as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: ef- (ex-, out) + fect (make, do) + -ive (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "effectivus" (productive, effective) → "ex-" (out) + "facere" (to make) → meaning "producing an effect" extended to "in operation from a date"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The new rules are effective as of Monday.
   - Your discount is effective as of today.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This policy amendment is effective as of January 1, 2024.
   - The revised fee structure is effective as of the date of this notice.

1. **Adjective**: accurate (gradable: yes)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Correct and exact in all details; free from errors
   - Conforming precisely to truth or to a standard
   - (With "as of") Reflecting the true state of affairs at a specified point in time
   
   **Synonyms**: precise, exact, correct, true, faithful
   
   **Antonyms**: inaccurate, incorrect, wrong, false, erroneous
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing the correctness and reliability of information at a specific date; commonly used in disclaimers, data reports, and financial statements with "as of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when approximate or estimated information is sufficient; not needed in contexts where precision isn't critical
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - accurate as of [date]
   - accurate information as of
   - accurate to the best of our knowledge as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: ac- (to, toward) + cur (care) + -ate (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "accuratus" (prepared with care) → "ad-" (to) + "cura" (care) → developed meaning of "correct in all details" (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This info is accurate as of yesterday.
   - The numbers are accurate as of last Friday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data presented herein is accurate as of the publication date.
   - Financial figures are accurate as of the close of business on March 31, 2024.

1. **Adjective**: valid (gradable: no)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Legally or officially acceptable; having legal force
   - (With "as of") Still in effect or applicable at a particular date or time
   - Not expired or superseded; continuing to have authority
   
   **Synonyms**: legitimate, legal, binding, authentic, in effect
   
   **Antonyms**: invalid, expired, void, null, illegitimate
   
   **When to Use**: When discussing documents, agreements, credentials, or permissions that remain in force at a specific time; commonly used with "as of" in legal and administrative contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for past documents or agreements that have expired; not appropriate for future validations not yet in effect
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - valid as of [date]
   - remains valid as of
   - no longer valid as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: val (be strong, be worth) + -id (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "validus" (strong, effective) → French "valide" → English "valid" (legally binding) (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is your passport still valid as of next month?
   - My driver's license is valid as of the renewal date.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract remains valid as of the date of this correspondence.
   - All previously issued certificates are valid as of July 1, 2024.

1. **Adjective**: updated (gradable: no)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Made current by including the latest information
   - Revised or modified to reflect new data or changes
   - (With "as of") Reflecting the most recent information available at a specified date
   
   **Synonyms**: revised, current, refreshed, amended, latest
   
   **Antonyms**: outdated, obsolete, stale, old, unchanged
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing that information has been refreshed or modified to be current at a specific point in time; common in reports, databases, and documentation with "as of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when information has never been changed (use "current" instead); not appropriate when emphasizing original or initial state
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - updated as of [date]
   - last updated as of
   - recently updated as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: up- (to higher position) + date + -ed (past participle)
   
   **Etymology**: "up" (to bring current) + "date" (point in time) → compound verb "update" → past participle "updated" (20th century usage)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This list was updated as of this morning.
   - My profile is updated as of yesterday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The following statistics are updated as of the end of the fiscal quarter.
   - Records have been updated as of the date indicated in the footer.

1. **Adjective**: outstanding (gradable: yes)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Financial/Business sense) Not yet paid, settled, or resolved
   - Remaining to be dealt with or completed
   - (With "as of") Indicating amounts owed or issues pending at a specific date
   
   **Synonyms**: unpaid, unsettled, pending, owing, unresolved
   
   **Antonyms**: settled, paid, resolved, completed, finalized
   
   **When to Use**: In financial statements, invoices, and business communications to indicate debts, obligations, or tasks that remain incomplete at a specific date; commonly paired with "as of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid the positive sense ("excellent") when discussing financial obligations; context must be clear to prevent confusion
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - outstanding balance as of [date]
   - outstanding obligations as of
   - outstanding invoices as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: out- (beyond) + stand (to remain upright) + -ing (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: "out" + "standing" (standing out) → originally "projecting, prominent" → developed financial sense "unpaid, unsettled" (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You have an outstanding balance as of today.
   - What's my outstanding amount as of now?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The outstanding receivables as of December 31, 2023, total $45,000.
   - Please remit all outstanding payments as of the statement date.

1. **Adjective**: available (gradable: yes)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Able to be used or obtained; at someone's disposal
   - Present and ready for use at a particular time
   - (With "as of") Accessible or in existence at a specified date
   
   **Synonyms**: accessible, obtainable, at hand, on hand, ready
   
   **Antonyms**: unavailable, inaccessible, depleted, exhausted, gone
   
   **When to Use**: When indicating resources, data, products, or services that exist or can be accessed at a specific point in time; common with "as of" in inventory, financial, and informational contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing past availability that no longer applies; be clear about the reference date
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - available as of [date]
   - funds available as of
   - information available as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (to) + vail (be of value, avail) + -able (capable of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "valere" (be strong, be worth) → Old French "availer" → Middle English "available" (profitable, beneficial) → modern sense "at hand, obtainable" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How much cash is available as of today?
   - The app is available as of this week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Funds available as of the closing date amount to $125,000.
   - The following datasets are available as of the publication date.

1. **Adjective**: reported (gradable: no)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Made known or presented in an official account or statement
   - Documented or recorded in a formal manner
   - (With "as of") Indicating information that has been officially stated or recorded by a certain date
   
   **Synonyms**: stated, documented, recorded, disclosed, announced
   
   **Antonyms**: unreported, undisclosed, concealed, hidden, secret
   
   **When to Use**: In business and financial contexts to indicate officially communicated information at a specific date; emphasizes formal documentation; commonly used with "as of" in reports and statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing informal or unverified information; not appropriate for casual communication
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reported as of [date]
   - as reported as of
   - figures reported as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + port (carry) + -ed (past participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "reportare" (carry back) → Old French "reporter" → English "report" (to give an account) → "reported" as adjective (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The numbers reported as of last month look good.
   - What was reported as of yesterday's meeting?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Earnings per share as reported as of Q4 2023 were $2.15.
   - The incident count reported as of December 31 has decreased by 15%.

1. **Adjective**: applicable (gradable: yes)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Relevant or appropriate to a particular situation or person
   - Able to be applied; suitable for use
   - (With "as of") In effect or relevant at a specified date
   
   **Synonyms**: relevant, appropriate, pertinent, suitable, fitting
   
   **Antonyms**: inapplicable, irrelevant, unsuitable, inappropriate, unrelated
   
   **When to Use**: When indicating rules, regulations, rates, or conditions that are in force or relevant at a particular date; common in legal, regulatory, and policy contexts with "as of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing universal or timeless principles; not needed when time-specificity isn't important
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - applicable as of [date]
   - rates applicable as of
   - terms applicable as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: ap- (ad-, to) + plic (fold, apply) + -able (capable of)

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What rates are applicable as of next month?
   - The old rules are still applicable as of now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Tax rates applicable as of January 1, 2024, are outlined in Schedule A.

1. **Adjective**: pending (gradable: no)

   - Awaiting decision or settlement; not yet decided or dealt with

   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when matters have been concluded; not appropriate for completed actions or decided issues
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pending as of [date]
   - matters pending as of
   - cases pending as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: pend (hang) + -ing (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "pendere" (to hang) → French "pendant" → English "pending" (hanging, undecided) (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How many requests are still pending as of today?
   - The case is pending as of last week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Litigation pending as of the balance sheet date is disclosed in Note 15.
   - Applications pending as of March 31, 2024, will be processed in Q2.
