1. **Noun**: date (C, pl: dates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A specific day of a month or year, especially as given in documents or used as a reference point
   - *(Sense 2)* The particular time at which an event occurs or is scheduled
   
   **Synonyms**: day, time, point in time, specific time, moment
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (time concepts don't have direct opposites)
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying temporal reference points; essential in formal documentation, reports, contracts, and any context requiring temporal precision; used across all registers but particularly important with "as of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When approximate timing is sufficient; when context already provides clear temporal reference
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - as of [date]
   - effective date
   - as of the date hereof
   
   **Root Analysis**: dat (give) + -e (from past participle ending)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "data" (given) → originally referred to the date given/written on letters → Middle English "date" (statement of time) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the date on that receipt?
   - As of what date does this apply?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please specify the exact date as of which this information is accurate.
   - The effective date as of which these terms apply is January 1, 2024.

1. **Noun**: balance (C/U, pl: balances)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The amount of money remaining in an account at a particular time
   - *(Sense 2)* The difference between credits and debits in an account
   - *(Sense 3)* An amount still owed or remaining to be paid
   
   **Synonyms**: total, sum, amount, remainder, funds
   
   **Antonyms**: deficit, shortage, debt (in some contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: In banking, accounting, financial statements; when discussing money remaining in accounts; particularly common with "as of" for temporal specificity; business and personal finance contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to physical balance/equilibrium; in contexts where "total" would be clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - account balance as of
   - outstanding balance as of
   - current balance as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: bi- (two) + lanc (plate, scale)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "bilanx" (having two scale pans) → Old French "balance" → extended to financial sense "equality of debits and credits" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My balance as of today is $500.
   - What's your account balance as of now?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The account balance as of December 31, 2023, was $127,450.32.
   - Please remit the outstanding balance as of the statement date.

1. **Noun**: status (U, pl: statuses in some contexts)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The position or state of affairs at a particular time
   - *(Sense 2)* The current condition or standing of something
   - Often used with "as of" to indicate when the status was determined
   
   **Synonyms**: state, condition, position, situation, standing
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (descriptive term without direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In reports, updates, tracking systems, and communications about current state; business, project management, and administrative contexts; formal and semi-formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When discussing social status or rank; in contexts where more specific terms are available
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - current status as of
   - employment status as of
   - status as of [date]
   
   **Root Analysis**: stat (stand) + -us (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "status" (state, position, condition) from "stare" (to stand) (17th century in English)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the status as of this morning?
   - My status as of now is "waiting."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project status as of the end of Q3 is detailed in the attached report.
   - Please provide your employment status as of the application date.

1. **Noun**: amount (C, pl: amounts)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A quantity of something, especially money, that is measured or counted
   - The total sum or aggregate
   
   **Synonyms**: sum, total, quantity, figure, volume
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (quantitative term without direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In financial, business, and quantitative contexts; when specifying totals or sums; commonly used with "as of" to indicate when the amount was current; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When more specific terms (count, volume, weight) are appropriate; for non-quantifiable concepts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - total amount as of
   - outstanding amount as of
   - amount owed as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (to) + mont (mountain, quantity)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "amonter" (to go up, amount to) → "amount" (quantity reached) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the amount you owe as of today?
   - The total amount as of yesterday was around $2,000.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The total amount outstanding as of December 31, 2023, is $47,250.
   - Please specify the amount payable as of the due date.

1. **Noun**: total (C, pl: totals)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The complete sum or aggregate amount
   - The final number reached after addition or calculation
   
   **Synonyms**: sum, aggregate, grand total, full amount, entirety
   
   **Antonyms**: part, portion, subtotal, component
   
   **When to Use**: In accounting, financial reports, summaries, calculations; when emphasizing completeness of a sum; commonly with "as of" for temporal reference; business and formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to partial sums or components; when "amount" would be more appropriate
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - grand total as of
   - total amount as of
   - total balance as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: tot (all, whole) + -al (adjective/noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "totus" (all, entire) → Old French "total" → English noun use (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The total as of now is 50 people.
   - What's the total cost as of today?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The grand total as of the closing date amounts to $3.7 million.
   - Cumulative totals as of September 30, 2024, are presented in Table 3.

1. **Noun**: figure (C, pl: figures)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A number, especially one that is part of official statistics or data
   - *(Sense 2)* A numerical value in a calculation or financial statement
   
   **Synonyms**: number, statistic, value, datum, amount
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (numerical term without opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In reports, statistics, data presentations; when referring to numerical data; commonly with "as of" to specify when figures were current; formal and semi-formal business contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual conversation where "number" is simpler; when precision about type of data is needed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - figures as of
   - sales figures as of
   - latest figures as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: fig (form, shape) + -ure (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "figura" (shape, form) → extended to mean "numerical symbol" → "numerical value" (13th-17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What are the figures as of last month?
   - The sales figures as of today look good.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Revenue figures as of Q3 2024 show a 15% increase year-over-year.
   - The financial figures reported as of December 31, 2023, are audited.

1. **Noun**: membership (C/U, pl: memberships)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The state of being a member of an organization or group
   - *(Sense 2)* The total number of members in an organization (uncountable)
   
   **Synonyms**: enrollment, participation, affiliation, association, subscription
   
   **Antonyms**: non-membership, expulsion, exclusion
   
   **When to Use**: In organizational contexts, clubs, professional associations, subscription services; often with "as of" to indicate when membership status applies; formal and semi-formal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to individual members rather than membership status; in casual contexts where simpler terms work
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - membership as of
   - membership count as of
   - valid membership as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: member (limb, part) + -ship (state, condition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "membrum" (limb, part of body) → "member" (part of group) + "-ship" (condition) (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is your gym membership still good as of next month?
   - Total membership as of today is 200 people.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Membership privileges are valid as of the renewal date.
   - Total organizational membership as of December 31, 2023, stands at 5,247 active members.

1. **Noun**: count (C, pl: counts)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The total number obtained by counting; an enumeration
   - The act or result of counting items in a group
   
   **Synonyms**: tally, number, total, enumeration, sum
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (counting result has no opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In inventory, headcount, statistics, data reporting; when referring to enumerated totals; commonly with "as of" for temporal reference; neutral register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to non-countable quantities; when "amount" or "total" is more appropriate for monetary values
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - headcount as of
   - current count as of
   - final count as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old French "conter" (to count, tell)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "conter" (to count, enumerate) → Latin "computare" (to calculate) → English "count" (enumeration) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The count as of this morning is 47 people.
   - What's the total count as of now?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The employee headcount as of September 30, 2024, is 3,247 full-time equivalents.
   - Inventory counts as of the fiscal year-end are reconciled in Schedule B.

1. **Noun**: position (C, pl: positions)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A situation or set of circumstances, especially financial circumstances
   - *(Sense 2)* The state or condition at a particular time
   - *(Sense 3)* In finance, the amount of securities or assets held
   
   **Synonyms**: situation, standing, status, condition, state
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A (situational term without direct opposite)
   
   **When to Use**: In financial contexts (financial position), business reports, status updates; often with "as of" to specify timing; formal register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to physical location or job position (unless clear from context); in casual contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - financial position as of
   - cash position as of
   - net position as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: posit (place, put) + -ion (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "positio" (a placing, position) from "ponere" (to place) → extended to financial/situational meanings (14th-17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's our cash position as of today?
   - The company's position as of now is pretty strong.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The company's financial position as of December 31, 2023, is detailed in the consolidated statements.
   - The net asset position as of the reporting date reflects unrealized gains.

1. **Noun**: value (C/U, pl: values)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The monetary worth of something
   - *(Sense 2)* The numerical quantity assigned to or calculated for a variable
   
   **Synonyms**: worth, price, amount, valuation, figure
   
   **Antonyms**: worthlessness (in value sense)
   
   **When to Use**: In financial, accounting, assessment, and data contexts; when referring to monetary worth or numerical quantities; with "as of" to specify when value was determined; formal and technical registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to moral/ethical values; when more specific terms (price, cost) are clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - market value as of
   - fair value as of
   - book value as of
   
   **Root Analysis**: val (be strong, be worth) + -ue (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "valere" (be strong, be worth) → Old French "value" → English "value" (monetary worth) (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the value of your car as of today?
   - The total value as of now is about $10,000.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The fair market value as of the appraisal date is $450,000.
   - Asset values as of December 31, 2023, are reported at historical cost.
