1. **Preposition**: in line with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Conformity/Agreement)* In agreement with; consistent with; following the same direction as (used to show alignment with policies, trends, expectations, or standards)
   - *(Harmony)* In harmony with; matching; compatible with (indicates that something fits well with established patterns or principles)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, consistent with, in keeping with, aligned with
   
   **Antonyms**: at odds with, contrary to, inconsistent with, out of line with, in opposition to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in line with" to show that something matches or agrees with expectations, policies, trends, standards, or objectives; more flexible and less legalistic than "in accordance with"; suitable for both formal and semi-formal contexts; common in business, policy, and analytical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common for direct source attribution (prefer "according to"); avoid when strict legal compliance is emphasized (use "in accordance with" or "pursuant to"); not typically used in very informal conversation; avoid overuse in consecutive sentences
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in line with expectations, in line with policy, in line with trends, in line with our objectives, in line with industry standards, in line with best practices
   - **Verb + prep**: bring in line with, keep in line with, move in line with, fall in line with, remain in line with, stay in line with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent in line with (redundant, avoid)
   - **Noun + prep**: decision in line with, strategy in line with, approach in line with
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + line (direction, alignment, row) + with (preposition indicating accompaniment)
   
   **Etymology**: line (from Latin linea "cord, string, line") → metaphorical sense of "alignment, conformity" developed in Middle English → phrasal preposition "in line with" emerged in 20th century business/management discourse to express alignment with policies, standards, and expectations
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My plans are totally in line with what you suggested yesterday.
   - The new design is in line with current fashion trends, so it should sell well.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed budget is in line with the organization's strategic priorities for the fiscal year.
   - These findings are in line with previous research on cognitive development in early childhood.

   **Examples [Casual]**:
