1. **Adverb**: independently (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is free from outside control or support; without relying on others; autonomously
   
   **Synonyms**: autonomously, separately, alone, by oneself, self-sufficiently
   
   **Antonyms**: dependently, jointly, collectively, together, cooperatively
   
   **When to Use**: Use to describe actions done without assistance or external influence; appropriate in formal and neutral contexts; emphasizes self-reliance and autonomy
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May sound overly formal in very casual conversation; avoid when collaboration or teamwork is being emphasized positively
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - work independently
   - think independently
   - operate independently
   - function independently
   - act independently
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + dependent (relying on) + -ly (adverb suffix) → in a not-relying manner
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "in-" (not) + "dependere" (to hang from) → French "indépendant" → English "independently"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My kid can finally get dressed independently now.
   - I prefer to work independently rather than in groups.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Researchers must independently verify all experimental results before publication.
   - The committee operates independently without governmental interference.

2. **Adverb**: freely (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without being controlled or limited; without restrictions or constraints; in an unrestricted manner
   - *(Sense 2)* Willingly and readily; without reluctance or reservation
   
   **Synonyms**: openly, without restriction, unreservedly, willingly, readily
   
   **Antonyms**: restrictively, reluctantly, hesitantly, under constraint
   
   **When to Use**: Use to emphasize absence of restrictions, constraints, or reluctance; appropriate in all registers; can describe physical or abstract freedom
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Be aware that "freely" can mean both "without cost" and "without restriction" - context must clarify meaning
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - speak freely
   - move freely
   - flow freely
   - give freely
   - admit freely
   
   **Root Analysis**: free (Old English "frēo" = not in bondage) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "frēo" (free) + "-līce" (adverb suffix) → Middle English "frely" → Modern English "freely"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Feel free to ask me anything - you can speak freely here.
   - The kids were running around freely in the park.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants were encouraged to express their opinions freely without fear of reprisal.
   - Capital should flow freely across borders to optimize economic efficiency.

3. **Adverb**: involuntarily (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is not done by choice or willingly; without intention or conscious control; automatically or reflexively
   
   **Synonyms**: unintentionally, automatically, reflexively, unwillingly, against one's will
   
   **Antonyms**: voluntarily, willingly, intentionally, deliberately, consciously
   
   **When to Use**: Use to describe actions or reactions that occur without conscious choice or control; common in medical, legal, and psychological contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "unintentionally" (which may still involve some control); be precise about whether lack of will or lack of control is meant
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - react involuntarily
   - move involuntarily
   - shudder involuntarily
   - respond involuntarily
   - occur involuntarily
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + voluntary (done by choice) + -ly (adverb suffix) → not by choice
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "in-" (not) + "voluntarius" (willing) → Medieval Latin "involuntarius" → English "involuntarily"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I involuntarily jumped when I heard the loud noise.
   - She involuntarily gasped at the surprise.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The patient's muscles contracted involuntarily during the neurological examination.
   - Participants were involuntarily enrolled in the program without adequate informed consent.

4. **Adverb**: unconsciously (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without being aware of what you are doing; without conscious thought or intention
   - *(Sense 2)* While not conscious or awake (medical/literal sense)
   
   **Synonyms**: unintentionally, inadvertently, automatically, subconsciously, unknowingly
   
   **Antonyms**: consciously, deliberately, intentionally, knowingly, purposefully
   
   **When to Use**: Use to describe actions or mental processes that occur without awareness; common in psychological and everyday contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "subconsciously" (which is specifically psychological); clarify whether you mean literally unconscious or simply unaware
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - unconsciously influenced
   - unconsciously repeat
   - unconsciously imitate
   - unconsciously assume
   - act unconsciously
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (not) + conscious (aware) + -ly (adverb suffix) → without awareness
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "un-" (not) + "conscire" (to be aware) → English "unconsciously"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I unconsciously started copying his accent after spending time with him.
   - She unconsciously touched her necklace whenever she got nervous.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Researchers found that participants unconsciously favored candidates with familiar names.
   - Cultural biases may unconsciously influence judicial decision-making processes.

5. **Adverb**: unintentionally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not on purpose; without meaning to; accidentally or by mistake
   
   **Synonyms**: accidentally, inadvertently, unknowingly, by mistake, without meaning to
   
   **Antonyms**: intentionally, deliberately, purposely, on purpose, knowingly
   
   **When to Use**: Use to indicate that an action or outcome was not planned or desired; appropriate in all registers; useful for clarifications and apologies
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound like an excuse in some contexts; consider whether "accidentally" might be clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - unintentionally offend
   - unintentionally delete
   - unintentionally hurt
   - unintentionally mislead
   - unintentionally cause
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (not) + intentional (done on purpose) + -ly → not on purpose
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "un-" (not) + "intentio" (purpose, aim) → English "unintentionally"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Sorry, I unintentionally forgot to include you in the email.
   - I think I unintentionally offended her with my comment.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy may unintentionally disadvantage minority populations.
   - Data was unintentionally disclosed due to a technical error in the system.

6. **Adverb**: solely (type: degree/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Only; exclusively; without others; entirely and exclusively
   
   **Synonyms**: only, exclusively, entirely, alone, purely, merely
   
   **Antonyms**: partly, partially, jointly, collectively, additionally
   
   **When to Use**: Use to emphasize that something applies to only one person, thing, or factor; common in formal and neutral registers; emphasizes exclusivity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid overuse in casual speech where "only" or "just" would sound more natural; can sound emphatic or legalistic
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - solely responsible
   - rely solely
   - based solely
   - depend solely
   - focus solely
   
   **Root Analysis**: sole (Latin "solus" = alone) + -ly (adverb suffix) → in an alone manner
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "solus" (alone) → Old French "soul" → Middle English "sole" + "-ly" → "solely"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm not solely to blame for this mess.
   - The decision was based solely on price, nothing else.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization is funded solely through private donations without governmental support.
   - Success cannot be attributed solely to individual effort without acknowledging systemic factors.
