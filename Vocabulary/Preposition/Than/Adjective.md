1. **Adjective**: better (comp: better, sup: best)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Of higher quality, more suitable, or more desirable when compared to something else; superior
   - In improved health or recovered from illness
   
   **Synonyms**: superior, preferable, finer, greater, improved
   
   **Antonyms**: worse, inferior, poorer, lesser
   
   **When to Use**: Making comparisons of quality, suitability, or desirability; expressing preference between alternatives; discussing improvement or recovery
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When things are equal in quality (use "as good as"); when making non-comparative statements about quality
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - better than expected
   - better than nothing
   - better than average
   - feel better than yesterday
   - better than ever
   
   **Root Analysis**: bet- (good, Old English "bet") + -ter (comparative suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "betera" → comparative form of "good" (Germanic origins, 9th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This pizza is way better than the one we had last week.
   - I'm feeling better than I was this morning.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed solution is demonstrably better than previous alternatives.
   - Participants performed significantly better than the control group.

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - worse than before
   - worse than anticipated
   - getting worse than ever
   - worse than I thought
   - could be worse than this
   
   **Root Analysis**: wors- (bad, Old English "wiersa") + -e (comparative marker)

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The traffic today is worse than it was yesterday.
   - This movie is even worse than the reviews said.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The economic conditions are worse than analysts predicted.
   - Patient outcomes were statistically worse than baseline measurements.
