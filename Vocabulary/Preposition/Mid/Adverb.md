1. **Adverb**: abruptly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Suddenly and unexpectedly, often in a way that seems rude or surprising.
   
   **Synonyms**: suddenly, sharply, brusquely, unexpectedly, all of a sudden
   
   **Antonyms**: gradually, gently, slowly, smoothly
   
   **When to Use**: Use when an action stops or changes in a sharp, unexpected way. Fits mid‑contexts like "the call ended abruptly mid-sentence" or "the meeting ended abruptly mid-discussion".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when the change is gentle or planned ("gradually" or "slowly" may be better); can sound judgmental in personal feedback.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - ended abruptly mid-sentence
   - stopped abruptly mid-game
   - left abruptly mid-conversation
   
   **Root Analysis**: abrupt (from Latin "abruptus" meaning broken off) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "abruptus" → "abrupt" → "abruptly" (manner of being sudden or broken off).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The call ended abruptly mid-sentence.
   - He walked out abruptly mid-conversation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The presentation concluded abruptly mid-slide due to a system crash.
   - Negotiations ended abruptly mid-session when a key stakeholder withdrew.

1. **Adverb**: halfway (type: degree/place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At or to a point that is half the distance or half the time between two things; part of the way through something.
   
   **Synonyms**: midway, partway, in the middle, to some extent
   
   **Antonyms**: completely, fully, barely (in some contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasising being in the middle of a journey, task, or time period: "halfway through the book", "halfway into the season". It often substitutes for or combines naturally with "mid".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very formal writing when specific fractions or percentages are needed ("at 50% completion"); avoid when something is nearly finished ("almost" may be better).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - stop halfway through
   - turn back halfway
   - halfway into the season
   
   **Root Analysis**: half (one of two equal parts) + way (distance, path)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "healf" (half) + "weg" (way) → "halfway".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We turned back halfway through the hike.
   - I realised halfway through the meeting that we needed to pivot.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study was redesigned halfway through the data collection phase.
   - Recruitment targets were only halfway met mid-quarter.

1. **Adverb**: briefly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For a short time; in a concise way.
   
   **Synonyms**: shortly, momentarily, for a moment, concisely
   
   **Antonyms**: at length, extensively, for a long time
   
   **When to Use**: Use when something happens or stops for a short period, such as "briefly pausing mid-sentence" or "briefly disconnecting mid-call".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when the duration is long; overuse in writing can be vague about actual time.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - briefly pause mid-sentence
   - briefly disconnect mid-meeting
   - briefly stop mid-journey
   
   **Root Analysis**: brief (short) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "brevis" (short) → Old French "brief" → English "brief" → "briefly".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She briefly stopped mid-sentence to think.
   - Our internet briefly dropped mid-game.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Service was briefly interrupted mid-session due to maintenance.
   - The presenter briefly paused mid-lecture to check the equipment.

1. **Adverb**: unexpectedly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is not expected or planned; surprisingly.
   
   **Synonyms**: suddenly, surprisingly, out of the blue, unpredictably
   
   **Antonyms**: predictably, expectedly, unsurprisingly
   
   **When to Use**: Use when events in the middle of something come as a surprise, such as "unexpectedly cancelled mid-tour" or "unexpectedly promoted mid-year".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid where outcome was clearly predictable; overuse weakens the sense of surprise.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - unexpectedly end mid-season
   - unexpectedly leave mid-meeting
   - unexpectedly change course mid-journey
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (not) + expected (from Latin "expectare" to look out for) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "expectare" → English "expect" → "expected" → "unexpected" → "unexpectedly".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The show unexpectedly ended mid-season.
   - He unexpectedly left mid-conversation without saying goodbye.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Funding was unexpectedly withdrawn mid-project, forcing a redesign of the study.
   - The contract was unexpectedly terminated mid-term due to compliance issues.

1. **Adverb**: smoothly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an even, regular way, without sudden changes, problems, or difficulty.
   
   **Synonyms**: seamlessly, easily, steadily, effortlessly
   
   **Antonyms**: roughly, unevenly, awkwardly, jerkily
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing actions that continue through the middle of a process without difficulty, such as "the release went smoothly mid-cycle" or "the handover ran smoothly mid-shift".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when there are real problems or interruptions; it misrepresents reality.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - went smoothly mid-launch
   - flowed smoothly mid-conversation
   - transitioned smoothly mid-season
   
   **Root Analysis**: smooth (even, without bumps) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "smōth" (smooth) → English "smooth" → "smoothly".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everything ran surprisingly smoothly mid-move.
   - The conversation shifted smoothly mid-topic.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The new process was introduced smoothly mid-quarter with minimal disruption.
   - Responsibilities transitioned smoothly mid-project due to careful planning.
