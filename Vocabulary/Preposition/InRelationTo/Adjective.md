1. **Adjective**: relative (comparative: more relative, superlative: most relative)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Depending on, or considered in relation or proportion to, something else rather than in itself
   - *(relative to)* Existing or measured by comparing with something similar or with a reference point
   
   **Synonyms**: comparative, proportional, relational, contextual
   
   **Antonyms**: absolute, fixed, independent
   
   **When to Use**: When you want to stress that a value, position, or importance only makes sense compared with something else (baseline, standard, peer group).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you need an absolute statement (use `absolute`) or when you mean a family member (use `relative` as a noun instead).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - relative to X
   - relative importance
   - relative position
   - relative advantage

1. **Adjective**: related (comparative: more related, superlative: most related)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Connected in some way; having a relationship with something else
   - *(related to)* Belonging to the same family, group, or conceptual domain
   
   **Synonyms**: connected, linked, associated, relevant, allied
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated, separate, independent, disconnected

1. **Adjective**: proportional (comparative: more proportional, superlative: most proportional)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Corresponding in size, amount, or degree to something else; in the correct or expected ratio
   - *(proportional to)* Increasing or decreasing in relation to another quantity
   
   **Synonyms**: corresponding, commensurate, relative, in-scale
   
   **Antonyms**: disproportionate, excessive, inadequate
   
   **When to Use**: When you describe outcomes, responses, or penalties that match the size, risk, or input in a fair or mathematically consistent way.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When the relationship is clearly unbalanced (use `disproportionate`).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - proportional to X
   - proportional response
   - proportional increase
   - proportional representation
   
   **Root Analysis**: pro- (for, in relation to) + portion (part, share) + -al (adjective suffix)

1. **Adjective**: comparable (comparative: more comparable, superlative: most comparable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Similar enough to be compared in a meaningful way; of a kind that invites or allows comparison
   
   **Synonyms**: similar, analogous, parallel, equivalent (in some respects)
   
   **Antonyms**: incomparable, unique, unmatched
   
   **When to Use**: When you judge whether two things are close enough in type, scale, or context to make a fair comparison between them.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When items differ so much that comparison would mislead (use `incomparable` or say they are "not really comparable").
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - comparable to X
   - broadly comparable
   - directly comparable
   - comparable in size/scale/value

   - Only organisations of comparable size were included in the analysis.
