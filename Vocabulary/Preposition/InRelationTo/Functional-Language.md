1. **Functional Expression**: How does X compare to Y? (function: comparing/evaluating)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Asks someone to evaluate similarities and differences between two things, usually in terms of quality, performance, or importance.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: How does X stack up against Y?, In relation to Y, how is X?, How does X measure up to Y?
   
   **Antonyms**: X is better/worse than Y because... (giving an evaluation instead of asking for one)
   
   **When to Use**: When you want another person[200~'s comparative judgement between options, time periods, or people.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When a simple factual question is needed ("What is X?") rather than a comparison.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - How does this compare to last year?
   - How does our product compare to competitors?
   - How do your results compare with the benchmark?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Prompts analytical thinking; implies that a reference point (Y) is already known and accepted.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - It[200~'s much better/worse in relation to...
   - They[200~'re pretty similar, except that...
   - Compared with Y, X is...
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How does this phone compare to your old one?
   - How does your new job compare to the last one?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - How does our current performance compare to the targets we set?
   - How do these findings compare to results reported in previous studies?

1. **Functional Expression**: Where do we stand in relation to X? (function: benchmarking/status-checking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Requests an update on current position compared with a target, standard, or competitor.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect but clear
   
   **Synonyms**: How close are we to X?, How are we doing relative to X?, What is our position compared to X?
   
   **Antonyms**: We have already reached/exceeded X. (stating status without asking)
   
   **When to Use**: In reviews, planning meetings, or progress checks where targets, KPIs, or competitors are the reference.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When no clear benchmark exists, or when you only need a raw number.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Where do we stand in relation to our goals?
   - Where do you stand in relation to the deadline?
   - Where does this proposal stand in relation to alternatives?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals interest in relative, not absolute, progress; often used by managers and stakeholders.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - We[200~'re slightly behind in relation to the original plan.
   - We[200~'re ahead of schedule relative to last quarter.
   - We[200~'re roughly on track in relation to our targets.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Where do you stand in relation to finishing that assignment?
   - Where do we stand in relation to planning the trip?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Where do we stand in relation to the regulatory deadline?
   - Where does the project stand in relation to the approved budget?

1. **Functional Expression**: Can you put this in context (for me)? (function: requesting contextualisation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Asks someone to explain how a fact, number, or event fits into a broader picture, often by comparison with known references.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect, polite request
   
   **Synonyms**: Can you explain what this means in relation to the bigger picture?, How should I understand this relative to X?, Can you contextualise this?
   
   **Antonyms**: Just give me the raw number. (rejecting context)
   
   **When to Use**: When a bare figure or statement feels unclear or potentially misleading without background or comparison.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you already know the context well and only need precise details.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could you put these results in context?
   - Can you put that in context for the team?
   - Put this in context in relation to previous years.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals that context and relationships (to history, benchmarks, or constraints) matter more than isolated facts.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Compared with last year, this is actually an improvement.
   - In the broader context of the market, this change is small.
   - Relative to our size, this is a significant shift.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Ten percent doesn[200~'t mean much to me. Can you put that in context?
   - Those numbers look huge[200~â€”can you put them in context?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you put these variance figures in context for the board?
   - The report puts the incident rate in context in relation to industry averages.

1. **Functional Expression**: How is X related to Y? (function: asking about relationships/links)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Requests an explanation of the connection, dependency, or association between two elements.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct question
   
   **Synonyms**: What is the relationship between X and Y?, In what way does X affect Y?, How does X connect to Y?
   
   **Antonyms**: X is not related to Y. (explicitly denying a relationship)
   
   **When to Use**: In analysis, learning, or problem-solving when the relationship between factors is not obvious.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you seek a simple description of X or Y in isolation.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - How is this metric related to customer satisfaction?
   - How is this decision related to our long-term strategy?
   - How are these two issues related in practice?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Encourages explanation of causal, correlational, or structural links; fits academic and professional discussions.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - X is directly/indirectly related to Y through...
   - They[200~'re loosely related in that...
   - They[200~'re not really related in any meaningful way.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How is this related to what you said earlier?
   - How is your job stress related to your sleep?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - How is income inequality related to health outcomes in this model?
   - How is the new policy related to existing legislation in this area?
