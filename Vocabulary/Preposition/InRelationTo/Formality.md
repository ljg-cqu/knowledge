1. **Original Expression**: How does this compare to last year?  
   **Context**: Manager asking for a performance update in relation to a previous period
   
   **Core Intent**: To request a comparative evaluation between current results and a past benchmark.
   
   **Casual**: So, how are we doing compared to last year?  
   *Characteristics*: Discourse marker "so", conversational "how are we doing", informal tone  
   *Use when*: Talking with close colleagues, informal team catch-ups, relaxed performance chats
   
   **Semi-Formal**: How does this year[200~'s performance compare to last year?  
   *Characteristics*: Clear reference to "this year[200~'s performance", neutral verb "compare", straightforward question  
   *Use when*: Standard team meetings, written status reports, professional but not overly formal settings
   
   **Formal**: Could you provide a comparison between this year[200~'s results and last year[200~'s performance?  
   *Characteristics*: Polite modal "could you", noun "comparison", explicit reference to "results" and "performance"  
   *Use when*: Presenting to senior management, board reviews, formal documentation
   
   **Humorous**: Are we crushing it this year, or just surviving compared to last year?  
   *Characteristics*: Exaggerated verb "crushing", contrastive pair "crushing / surviving", playful framing  
   *Use when*: Positive context, strong rapport, low-stakes retrospectives  
   *Avoid when*: Serious underperformance, high-stakes reviews, tense situations
   
   **Lively**: I[200~'m really curious how this year stacks up against last year!  
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiastic "really curious", idiom "stacks up", exclamation mark  
   *Use when*: Energising discussion, brainstorming improvements, celebrating progress
   
   **Empathetic**: I[200~'d like to understand how this year compares to last year, especially given all the challenges you[200~'ve faced.  
   *Characteristics*: Acknowledgement of difficulties, supportive framing, focus on understanding  
   *Use when*: Reviewing performance after a tough period, building trust, avoiding blame
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: More formal when speaking upward; casual variants mainly sideways or downward.
   - *Social Distance*: Semi-formal for new relationships; more casual as trust builds.
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Formal and empathetic for serious shortfalls; casual/humorous only for safe topics.
   - *Cultural Context*: Some cultures expect careful, face-saving phrasing for negative comparisons.
   - *Medium*: Email and written reports lean formal; spoken meetings allow more flexibility.
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using humorous wording when results are clearly poor.
   - Being too casual with senior stakeholders.
   - Asking only for comparison without later acknowledging context.
   
   **Cultural Notes**: Direct performance comparisons are normal in Anglo business cultures but may need softer wording in high-context or face-sensitive cultures.
