1. **Verb**: relate (to)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To show or describe how two or more things are connected
   - *(relate to)* To have a connection with; to be about something
   
   **Synonyms**: connect, link, associate, pertain, correspond
   
   **Antonyms**: separate, disconnect, ignore
   
   **When to Use**: When explaining how ideas, data, or events are connected, especially in analysis and discussion.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you simply mean "tell a story" (use `relate` without `to`) or when there is clearly no connection.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - relate A to B
   - relate to X
   - as it relates to
   - cannot relate to

1. **Verb**: compare (to/with)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To examine two or more things in order to see how they are similar or different
   - *(compare A to/with B)* To judge the quality, value, or performance of one thing in relation to another
   
   **Synonyms**: contrast, set side by side, evaluate against, benchmark
   
   **Antonyms**: isolate, ignore, consider alone
   
   **When to Use**: When you explicitly line up items, periods, or groups to assess similarities, differences, or changes.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When no reference point is involved or when comparison would be misleading.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - compare A to/with B
   - compared to last year

1. **Verb**: correspond (to)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To have a close similarity; to match or agree
   - *(correspond to)* To be equivalent in character, function, or position
   
   **Synonyms**: match, align, agree, equate, parallel
   
   **Antonyms**: differ, diverge, conflict
   
   **When to Use**: When describing how one set of items lines up with another in position, value, or meaning.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you simply mean "communicate" (use `correspond with` for letter-writing sense).
   
   **Common Phrases**:

1. **Verb**: correlate (with)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To have a mutual relationship or connection, where one thing changes as another changes
   - *(correlate with)* To show a statistical relationship with another variable
   
   **Synonyms**: be associated with, relate to, connect with, co-vary with
   
   **Antonyms**: be independent of, show no relation to
   
   **When to Use**: In analytical or statistical contexts when you discuss how variables move together without claiming causation.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you want to clearly state cause-and-effect (use `cause`, `lead to`, or `bring about`).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - strongly/weakly correlate with
   - positively/negatively correlate with

   - Income level correlates strongly with educational attainment in this dataset.
