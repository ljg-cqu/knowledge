1. **Functional Expression**: We're almost there / We're nearly there (function: encouraging/informing about progress)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that completion or arrival is imminent; encouragement that the goal is close
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: We're getting close, We're close to finishing, We're nearing the end, Almost done, Nearly finished
   
   **Antonyms**: We've got a long way to go, We've barely started, We're nowhere near done
   
   **When to Use**: Use when encouraging others during difficult tasks, indicating proximity to destination or goal completion; in collaborative work, travel, or project contexts; builds morale and motivation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid if completion is not actually near (damages credibility); don't use when detailed status report is needed; can minimize remaining work inappropriately
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We're almost done
   - We're getting there
   - Almost there!
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Positive politeness strategy; builds solidarity and encouragement; acknowledges difficulty while emphasizing progress; reduces face threat of continued effort
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Finally! / Thank goodness! / Can't wait!
   - How much longer? / Really? / Are you sure?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Keep going! We're almost there—just one more mile to the top.
   - Don't give up now—we're nearly there with this project!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The team is nearly there in terms of meeting project deliverables.
   - We're approaching completion of the preliminary phase.

2. **Functional Expression**: That was close! / That was a close one! (function: expressing relief after near-miss)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to express relief after narrowly avoiding danger, failure, or negative outcome
   - *Formality*: informal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: That was a near miss, That was too close for comfort, We just barely made it, Phew, that was close
   
   **Antonyms**: We missed by a mile, That wasn't even close, Easy victory
   
   **When to Use**: Use after narrowly avoiding accidents, missing deadlines by small margins, barely succeeding; expresses relief and acknowledgment of risk; common in informal conversation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too casual for formal reports (use "near-miss incident"); can trivialize serious situations; avoid in serious safety contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Whew, that was close!
   - Too close for comfort!
   - That was cutting it close!
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Expresses shared relief; acknowledges danger without dwelling on it; solidarity-building in group contexts; downplays anxiety through light treatment
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I know, right! / Tell me about it!
   - Way too close! / Let's not do that again!
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That was close! We almost missed our flight completely.
   - Whew! That was a close one—the ball just missed the window!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The incident represented a close call requiring immediate policy review.
   - (More formal) The situation nearly resulted in significant complications.

3. **Functional Expression**: Is there a [place] nearby? / Is there a [place] near here? (function: requesting location information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask for information about the location of services or places in the immediate vicinity
   - *Formality*: neutral to informal
   - *Directness*: indirect (question form softens request)
   
   **Synonyms**: Where's the nearest [place]?, Do you know where I can find a [place]?, Is there a [place] close by?, Where can I find a [place] around here?
   
   **Antonyms**: (no direct opposite in function)
   
   **When to Use**: Use when seeking local services or facilities; asking strangers or service staff for directions; travel and navigation contexts; polite inquiry
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less effective if asking locals about well-known landmarks (too basic); avoid in emergency situations (be more direct)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Where's the nearest [place]?
   - Do you know of a [place] nearby?
   - Is there a [place] around here?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Indirect request minimizes imposition; question form shows deference; "nearby/near here" indicates hope for convenient location; negative politeness strategy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, there's one just around the corner / down the street
   - I'm not sure, sorry / I'm not from around here
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Excuse me, is there a coffee shop nearby? I'm dying for caffeine.
   - Do you know if there's an ATM near here?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you direct me to nearby dining facilities?
   - I'm seeking information regarding proximate lodging accommodations.

4. **Functional Expression**: We're getting close to [goal/deadline] (function: status update/warning)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to inform others that a deadline or goal is approaching; can serve as gentle reminder or warning
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (states fact rather than direct command)
   
   **Synonyms**: We're approaching [deadline], [Deadline] is coming up, We're nearing [goal], The deadline is getting close
   
   **Antonyms**: We have plenty of time, The deadline is far off, We're nowhere near [goal]
   
   **When to Use**: Use in project management, team coordination, or collaborative contexts to raise awareness of approaching deadlines; can motivate action without direct demands
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too indirect if urgent action needed; avoid if clear directives are required; can sound like nagging if overused
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We're getting close to the deadline
   - We're approaching [goal/deadline]
   - [Deadline] is getting near
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Indirect speech act—states fact to imply action needed; face-saving as doesn't directly command; maintains collegial relationships while prompting action
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I know, I'm working on it / Thanks for the reminder
   - Don't worry, we'll make it / How much time do we have?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Hey team, just a heads up—we're getting close to the submission deadline.
   - We're getting pretty close to our fundraising goal!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As we approach the project deadline, please ensure all deliverables are finalized.
   - The organization is nearing its quarterly targets and requires continued effort.

5. **Functional Expression**: I'm not even close to [done/ready/finished] (function: managing expectations/refusing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that something is far from completion; manages expectations about readiness or progress
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (clear statement of status)
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm nowhere near done, I'm far from ready, I've barely started, I'm not nearly finished, I still have a long way to go
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm almost done, I'm nearly ready, I'm just about finished, I'm close to completion
   
   **When to Use**: Use when others have unrealistic expectations about your progress; setting boundaries about readiness; explaining why you can't do something yet; honest status reporting
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound defensive or excuse-making; avoid if you should have made more progress; not suitable for formal status reports (use specific percentages)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm nowhere near ready
   - I'm not close to being done
   - I'm far from finished
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Manages expectations; asserts boundaries; provides honest assessment; can be face-threatening if implies failure to meet obligations; emphasis on "not even" increases distance from goal
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - How much longer do you need? / What can I do to help?
   - That's fine, just let me know when you're ready
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you present tomorrow? — No way, I'm not even close to done with the slides.
   - I'm nowhere near ready for this exam next week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project is not yet approaching completion status.
   - We are currently far from achieving the specified benchmarks.

6. **Functional Expression**: You're close! / You're getting closer! (function: encouraging/giving feedback)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to encourage someone who is attempting something; indicates they are approaching the correct answer or solution
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (but encouraging, not commanding)
   
   **Synonyms**: You're almost there, You're on the right track, Keep going, You're getting warmer (in guessing games), Almost, Nearly there
   
   **Antonyms**: You're way off, That's not even close, You're going in the wrong direction, Cold (in guessing games)
   
   **When to Use**: Use when teaching, coaching, or playing games; providing encouragement during problem-solving; positive feedback that acknowledges effort while indicating more is needed
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be patronizing if tone is wrong; avoid if person is frustrated and needs direct answer; don't use if they're actually far from correct
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - You're getting there!
   - That's closer!
   - You're warm! (guessing games)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Positive politeness strategy; builds confidence; maintains engagement in challenging tasks; acknowledges progress without giving away answer; pedagogical function
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Really? Let me try again / Give me a hint!
   - How close? / What am I missing?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is it Tuesday? — You're close! Try the next day.
   - You're getting closer! Think about what happens in the morning.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Your analysis is approaching the correct interpretation.
   - The proposed solution is nearing optimal configuration.

7. **Functional Expression**: Can we meet somewhere near [location]? (function: suggesting/negotiating meeting place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to suggest meeting at a convenient location close to a specified place; negotiating mutually accessible venue
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (question form softens suggestion)
   
   **Synonyms**: Could we meet close to [location]?, Is there somewhere near [location] we could meet?, How about meeting near [location]?, Let's meet somewhere around [location]
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's meet at [exact location], I need you to come to [specific place]
   
   **When to Use**: Use when arranging meetings, considering others' convenience; when exact location is flexible; showing consideration for others' travel needs; collaborative planning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too vague if specific location is essential; avoid if you have strong preference (state it directly); not suitable for formal scheduled meetings
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could we meet close to [location]?
   - How about somewhere near [location]?
   - Let's find a place near [location]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy—minimizes imposition by offering flexibility; shows consideration for other's convenience; opens negotiation rather than dictating terms
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Sure, how about [specific place]? / That works for me
   - Actually, could we meet closer to [other location]?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can we meet somewhere near your office? I have appointments in that area.
   - Let's grab coffee somewhere near campus—any suggestions?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Would it be possible to convene at a location proximate to the conference venue?
   - I propose meeting at a mutually accessible location near the city center.

8. **Functional Expression**: That's not quite right, but you're on the right track (function: correcting/encouraging)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to provide corrective feedback while maintaining encouragement; indicates partial correctness
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (softens correction with encouragement)
   
   **Synonyms**: You're close, but not exactly, You're getting there, That's almost it, You're heading in the right direction, Nearly, but...
   
   **Antonyms**: That's completely wrong, You're way off, That's not it at all, You're not even close
   
   **When to Use**: Use in teaching, coaching, or collaborative problem-solving; when someone's answer is partially correct; balancing correction with encouragement; pedagogical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be vague if specific correction is needed; avoid if time-sensitive and direct answer required; not suitable for high-stakes assessments
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - You're on the right track, but...
   - That's close, but not quite
   - You're almost there, just...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Hedging strategy—softens face-threatening act of correction; maintains positive face by acknowledging partial success; encourages continued effort; common in educational and mentoring contexts
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What should I change? / Can you give me a hint?
   - Let me try again / What am I missing?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's not quite right, but you're on the right track—think about the first step.
   - You're close! Just double-check your calculations.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The approach is sound, though minor adjustments would enhance accuracy.
   - Your analysis approximates the correct interpretation, requiring only minor refinement.
