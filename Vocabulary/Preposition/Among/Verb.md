1. **Verb**: divide among
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To separate something into parts and distribute those parts to three or more people, groups, or categories
   
   **Synonyms**: split among, distribute among, share among, apportion among, allocate among
   
   **Antonyms**: consolidate, gather, collect, unite, combine
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing the distribution of resources, responsibilities, or items among three or more entities; appropriate in all registers; essential for discussions of allocation and fair distribution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Use "divide between" for only two parties; avoid when describing unequal distribution without qualification; common error: using "divide among" for two items
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - divide equally among
   - divide fairly among
   - divide something among
   - divide resources among
   - divide responsibilities among
   
   **Root Analysis**: divide (from Latin "dividere" meaning "to separate") + among (in the company of multiple)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "dividere" (to force apart, separate) → Old French "divider" → Middle English "divide" (combined with "among" for multi-way distribution)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's divide the pizza equally among the five of us.
   - We should divide the work among all team members.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Verb**: distribute among
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To give shares of something to members of a group; to spread or supply something to three or more recipients
   
   **Synonyms**: allocate among, apportion among, share among, dispense among, spread among
   
   **Antonyms**: collect from, gather from, consolidate, centralize, monopolize
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing systematic allocation or sharing; appropriate in formal and neutral contexts; common in business, logistics, and organizational planning; slightly more formal than "divide"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual contexts (use "hand out" or "give"); not appropriate for uncontrolled spreading (use "scatter"); requires intentional agency
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - distribute equally among
   - distribute resources among
   - distribute responsibilities among
   - widely distributed among
   - evenly distributed among
   
   **Root Analysis**: dis- (apart) + tribut- (assign, from Latin "tribuere" meaning "to allot") + -e (verb ending)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "distribuere" (to divide up, distribute) → Old French "distribuer" → Middle English "distribute"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The teacher distributed the handouts among the students.
   - They distribute the profits among all the partners.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization distributes humanitarian aid among affected populations.
   - Survey questionnaires were distributed among participants prior to the intervention.

1. **Verb**: share among
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To use, occupy, or experience something jointly with others; to divide and distribute something among multiple people
   
   **Synonyms**: divide among, split among, apportion among, distribute among, allocate among
   
   **Antonyms**: keep to oneself, monopolize, hoard, withhold, retain exclusively
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing joint use or collaborative distribution; appropriate in all registers; common in discussions of resources, responsibilities, and collaborative work
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when distribution is mandatory rather than voluntary; less appropriate in formal legal contexts (use "apportion" or "allocate")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - share equally among
   - share resources among
   - share responsibilities among
   - share the cost among
   - share information among
   
   **Root Analysis**: share (from Old English "scearu" meaning "a cutting, division")
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "scearu" (portion, division) → Middle English "sharen" (to divide, distribute) → Modern English "share"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We'll just share the dessert among the three of us.
   - Let's share the driving responsibilities among everyone on the road trip.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Best practices should be shared among all departments to ensure consistency.
   - The research findings were shared among collaborative institutions.

   - (Transitive) To include someone or something as a member of a particular group or category; to regard or classify as one of a group
   
   **Synonyms**: include among, number among, rank among, consider one of, place among
   
   **Antonyms**: exclude from, separate from, distinguish from, remove from
   
   **When to Use**: Use when expressing membership, inclusion, or classification in a valued group; appropriate in formal and neutral contexts; common in expressions of pride, honor, or achievement

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - count someone among
   - count yourself among
   - proud to count among
   - count among the best
   - count among one's friends
   
   **Root Analysis**: count (from Latin "computare" meaning "to calculate") + among
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "computare" (to sum up, reckon) → Old French "conter" → Middle English "counten" (evolved to include sense of "include in a reckoning")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I count you among my closest friends.
   - She counts among the best players on the team.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The university counts among its alumni three Nobel Prize recipients.
   - We are honored to count your organization among our strategic partners.

   **Common Phrases**:

   **Root Analysis**: rank (from Old French "renc" meaning "row, line") + among

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That movie ranks among my all-time favorites.
   - She ranks among the smartest people I know.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This institution ranks among the world's leading research universities.

1. **Verb**: circulate among
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Intransitive) To move or pass from one person or place to another within a group; to be distributed or shared within a population
   
   **Synonyms**: spread among, pass among, move among, disseminate among, go around among
   
   **Antonyms**: stagnate, remain confined, stay put, be withheld, be restricted
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing movement or distribution within a closed group; appropriate in formal and neutral contexts; common when discussing information, documents, or social mingling
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual contexts (use "go around"); less appropriate for uncontrolled spreading (use "spread"); implies some degree of organization or containment within group
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - circulate widely among
   - circulate freely among
   - circulate information among
   - circulate documents among
   - circulate among members
   
   **Root Analysis**: circul- (circle, from Latin "circulus") + -ate (verb suffix indicating action)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "circulare" (to form a circle, move in a circle) → Middle English "circulaten" → English "circulate" (extended to mean "move through a group")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The petition is circulating among employees right now.
   - That story has been circulating among my friends for weeks.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The draft proposal will circulate among committee members for review.
   - Confidential documents were found to have circulated among unauthorized personnel.
