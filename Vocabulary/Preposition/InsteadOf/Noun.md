1. **Noun**: alternative (C, pl: alternatives)
   **Meaning**: 
   - One of two or more available possibilities; something that can be chosen instead of something else (C)
   - A choice limited to one of two or more possibilities (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: option, choice, substitute, replacement, other possibility
   
   **Antonyms**: only option, necessity, obligation, requirement
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; discussing choices and options; when presenting what can be selected instead of the original or default option
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When there is no choice available; when referring to additions rather than substitutions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - viable alternative
   - alternative option
   - alternative to X
   - consider alternatives
   - explore alternatives
   
   **Root Analysis**: alter (other) + -native (suffix indicating tendency)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin alternativus (offering choice between two) → French alternatif → English "alternative" (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - If you don't like that restaurant, we have other alternatives instead.
   - What are the alternatives to paying full price?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee evaluated several alternatives instead of accepting the initial proposal.
   - Policy alternatives must be thoroughly assessed instead of pursuing a single approach.

1. **Noun**: substitute (C, pl: substitutes)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A person or thing acting or serving in place of another (C)
   - Something used instead of something else, often temporarily (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: replacement, stand-in, proxy, surrogate, backup
   
   **Antonyms**: original, primary, authentic item, the real thing
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; when describing temporary or permanent replacements; sports, education, workplace contexts; emphasizes what is used instead
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to the original item; when both items are used simultaneously
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - substitute teacher
   - substitute player
   - poor substitute
   - find a substitute
   - serve as a substitute
   
   **Root Analysis**: sub- (under, in place of) + -stitute (to place)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin substitutus (put in place of another) → English "substitute" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Honey is a good substitute for sugar in most recipes.
   - We need to find a substitute instead of canceling the class.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Synthetic materials serve as substitutes for natural resources.
   - A substitute methodology was implemented instead of the original protocol.

1. **Noun**: replacement (C/U, pl: replacements)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action or process of replacing someone or something (U)
   - A person or thing that replaces another (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: substitute, successor, stand-in, alternative, new version
   
   **Antonyms**: original, predecessor, discontinued item
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; discussing things that take the place of others; emphasizes the new item that comes instead of the old
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to additions; when the original continues alongside the new item
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - replacement part
   - replacement cost
   - find a replacement
   - replacement worker
   - hip replacement
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again, back) + place + -ment (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: French replacer (to put back) + -ment → English "replacement" (18th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I need a replacement phone instead of this broken one.
   - They hired a replacement instead of promoting from within.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The replacement of traditional systems instead of upgrading them proved cost-effective.
   - Replacement personnel were recruited instead of retaining existing staff.

1. **Noun**: choice (C/U, pl: choices)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An act of selecting or making a decision when faced with two or more possibilities (C/U)
   - A range of possibilities from which one can be chosen (C, usually singular)
   
   **Synonyms**: option, alternative, selection, pick, decision
   
   **Antonyms**: compulsion, obligation, no choice, necessity
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; discussing decisions and selections; when emphasizing the act of choosing one thing instead of another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When there is no alternative; when decision is forced
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - make a choice
   - have a choice
   - choice between X and Y
   - good choice
   - first choice
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old French chois (choice, selection)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French chois → Middle English chois → Modern English "choice" (13th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That was a good choice instead of what I suggested.
   - You have a choice: coffee or tea instead of soda.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The choice to pursue alternative strategies instead of traditional approaches reflects changing priorities.
   - Institutional choices regarding resource allocation instead of expansion were carefully considered.

1. **Noun**: preference (C/U, pl: preferences)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A greater liking for one alternative over another or others (C/U)
   - A thing preferred; what is chosen instead of alternatives (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: choice, liking, predilection, inclination, favoritism
   
   **Antonyms**: aversion, dislike, rejection, antipathy
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; discussing what is favored or liked better; when explaining why one thing is chosen instead of another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When there is no basis for preference; when choices are equivalent
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - preference for X over Y
   - show preference
   - in preference to
   - state a preference
   - personal preference
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefer (choose before) + -ence (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praeferre (to set before) + -ence → French préférence → English "preference" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I have a preference for tea instead of coffee.
   - What's your preference instead of the usual choice?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Organizational preferences for centralized control instead of distributed authority were evident.
   - Consumer preferences shifted toward sustainable options instead of conventional products.

1. **Noun**: swap (C, pl: swaps)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An act of exchanging one thing for another (C)
   - A thing that has been given in exchange for something else (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: exchange, trade, switch, substitution, replacement
   
   **Antonyms**: retention, keeping, maintaining
   
   **When to Use**: Informal to neutral contexts; when describing exchanges or substitutions; emphasizes mutual or reciprocal replacement
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal contexts (use "exchange" or "substitution"); when replacement is not reciprocal
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - make a swap
   - do a swap
   - swap for something
   - fair swap
   - schedule swap
   
   **Root Analysis**: Middle English swappe (to strike, likely imitative)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English swappen (to strike a bargain) → "swap" (meaning exchange, 14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's do a swap: I'll take your Tuesday shift instead of Thursday.
   - The swap worked out well: we got a better car instead of the old one.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The schedule swap allowed for more efficient resource allocation instead of rigid adherence to original plans.
   - Currency swaps provide hedging mechanisms instead of direct exposure to exchange risk.

1. **Noun**: exchange (C/U, pl: exchanges)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An act of giving one thing and receiving another in return (C/U)
   - Something that is given or received in place of something else (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: swap, trade, switch, substitution, replacement
   
   **Antonyms**: retention, keeping, maintaining original
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; when describing reciprocal replacements; business, diplomatic, and academic contexts; emphasizes mutual substitution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When replacement is one-directional; when items are not equivalent
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in exchange for
   - make an exchange
   - exchange of goods
   - fair exchange
   - exchange program
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + change (French changer, to change)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French eschange → English "exchange" (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I made an exchange: I returned the blue one and got the red one instead.
   - It was a fair exchange: my bike for his skateboard instead.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The exchange of traditional practices for modern methodologies instead represents paradigm shifts.
   - Academic exchanges facilitate knowledge transfer instead of isolated research development.

1. **Noun**: proxy (C, pl: proxies)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A person authorized to act on behalf of another; a substitute (C)
   - The authority to represent someone else (U)
   
   **Synonyms**: substitute, representative, agent, stand-in, surrogate
   
   **Antonyms**: principal, original, primary person
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and technical contexts; legal, business, and academic settings; when someone/something acts instead of the original
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (use "substitute"); when describing permanent replacement
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - by proxy
   - proxy vote
   - act as proxy
   - proxy server
   - proxy for
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin procuratio (management, agency) → shortened to "proxy"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin procuratio → Middle English prokecie → Modern English "proxy" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She attended the meeting as my proxy instead of me.
   - He voted by proxy instead of showing up in person.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Board members may designate proxies to attend instead of participating personally.
   - Proxy indicators serve as measurements instead of direct observation.

1. **Noun**: surrogate (C, pl: surrogates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A substitute, especially a person deputizing for another in a specific role or office (C)
   - A thing that acts as a replacement or substitute (C)
   
   **Synonyms**: substitute, proxy, replacement, stand-in, representative
   
   **Antonyms**: original, primary, actual person/thing
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts; medical, legal, and technical fields; when describing someone/something acting instead of another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts; when simpler "substitute" or "replacement" is clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - surrogate mother
   - act as surrogate
   - surrogate for
   - surrogate marker
   - surrogate decision-maker
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin surrogatus (substituted)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin surrogare (to substitute) → English "surrogate" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She acted as a surrogate parent instead of his biological mother.
   - The mascot serves as a surrogate for the team instead of individual players.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Surrogate endpoints are utilized instead of long-term clinical outcomes in preliminary trials.
   - Surrogate decision-makers must act instead of incapacitated patients.
