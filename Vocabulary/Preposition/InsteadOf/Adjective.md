1. **Adjective**: alternative (comp: more alternative, sup: most alternative)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Available as another possibility or choice; one of two or more things from which to choose (gradable)
   - Different from the usual or conventional; not mainstream or traditional (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: substitute, replacement, other, different, optional
   
   **Antonyms**: primary, original, conventional, standard, mainstream
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing options, choices, or replacements; appropriate for formal and informal contexts; emphasizes availability of other possibilities instead of the original
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when referring to something that must be used rather than something optional; not appropriate when no other choices exist
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - alternative solution
   - alternative approach
   - alternative option
   - viable alternative
   - alternative method
   
   **Root Analysis**: alter (other) + -native (suffix indicating tendency)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin alternativus (offering a choice) → French alternatif → English "alternative" (16th century, meaning "offering a choice between two things")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - If you can't make it Tuesday, we can pick an alternative day.
   - She chose an alternative route instead of the highway.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee must consider alternative strategies instead of the current approach.
   - Alternative methodologies were explored instead of traditional techniques.

1. **Adjective**: substitute (non-gradable, attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Acting or serving in place of another; replacing something else (non-gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: replacement, surrogate, proxy, stand-in, backup
   
   **Antonyms**: original, primary, main, principal
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing something temporarily or permanently replacing another thing; common in education, sports, and professional contexts; indicates one thing used instead of another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when referring to the original item itself; not used when describing permanent or intended first choices
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - substitute teacher
   - substitute player
   - substitute product
   - substitute ingredient
   - substitute material
   
   **Root Analysis**: sub- (under, in place of) + -stitute (to place)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin substitutus (put in place of another) → English "substitute" (15th century, meaning "person appointed to act for another")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We used honey as a substitute sweetener instead of sugar.
   - The substitute player did great instead of the injured starter.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - A substitute methodology was implemented instead of the original protocol.
   - Substitute materials were procured instead of the specified components.

1. **Adjective**: different (comp: more different, sup: most different)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not the same as another or each other; unlike in nature, form, or quality (gradable)
   - Separate; distinct; not identical (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: dissimilar, distinct, unlike, contrasting, varied
   
   **Antonyms**: same, identical, similar, alike, equivalent
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing contrast or distinction; appropriate in all registers; commonly used when discussing choosing one thing instead of another
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid overuse when more specific adjectives (better, worse, larger) would be clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - different approach
   - different perspective
   - completely different
   - entirely different
   - different choice
   
   **Root Analysis**: differ (carry apart) + -ent (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin differentem (carrying apart) → Old French different → English "different" (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's try a different restaurant instead of the usual place.
   - I chose a different color instead of blue this time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research team adopted a different methodology instead of replicating previous studies.
   - A different analytical framework was employed instead of conventional models.

1. **Adjective**: preferable (comp: more preferable, sup: most preferable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - More desirable or suitable; better or more appropriate as a choice (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: superior, better, more desirable, more suitable, advantageous
   
   **Antonyms**: inferior, worse, undesirable, unsuitable
   
   **When to Use**: Use when indicating one option is better than another; often used with "to" to show comparison; formal and semi-formal contexts; implies selection instead of alternatives
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation where "better" is simpler; not appropriate when all options are equally good or bad
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - preferable option
   - preferable alternative
   - clearly preferable
   - far preferable
   - preferable choice
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefer (choose before) + -able (able to be)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praeferre (to set before) + -able → English "preferable" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Taking the train is preferable to driving instead of dealing with traffic.
   - A quiet evening is preferable instead of going to loud bars.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Digital documentation is preferable to paper records instead of traditional filing systems.
   - Preventive measures are preferable instead of reactive interventions.

1. **Adjective**: contrary (non-gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Opposite in nature, direction, or meaning; completely different (non-gradable)
   - Opposed; conflicting; incompatible (non-gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: opposite, opposed, contradictory, conflicting, antithetical
   
   **Antonyms**: similar, agreeing, compatible, consistent, harmonious
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts; emphasizes opposition or conflict; often used in academic, legal, or philosophical discourse; indicates something chosen instead of what was expected
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common in casual conversation; can sound overly formal in everyday contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - contrary opinion
   - contrary evidence
   - contrary to expectations
   - quite contrary
   - contrary position
   
   **Root Analysis**: contra- (against) + -ary (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin contrarius (opposite, opposed) → English "contrary" (13th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He did the contrary thing instead of following advice.
   - She took a contrary view instead of agreeing with everyone.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The findings present contrary evidence instead of supporting the hypothesis.
   - Contrary methodologies were adopted instead of conventional approaches.

1. **Adjective**: replacement (attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Serving to replace something; intended as a substitute for the original (non-gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: substitute, alternative, backup, spare, surrogate
   
   **Antonyms**: original, primary, first, initial
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing something that takes the place of something else; common in technical, medical, and everyday contexts; indicates something used instead of what was there before
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when referring to additions rather than substitutions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - replacement part
   - replacement value
   - replacement cost
   - replacement worker
   - replacement item
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again) + place + -ment (noun/adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: French replacer (to put back) + -ment → English "replacement" (18th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I need a replacement phone instead of this broken one.
   - They hired a replacement manager instead of promoting internally.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Replacement components were installed instead of repairing existing systems.
   - A replacement protocol was implemented instead of the outdated procedure.

1. **Adjective**: alternate (comp: more alternate, sup: most alternate)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Available as another choice; substitute (gradable)
   - Occurring in turns; every other one in a series (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: alternative, substitute, backup, other, secondary
   
   **Antonyms**: primary, main, original, principal
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing backup options or things occurring in sequence; more common in American English for "alternative"; indicates something available instead of the primary choice
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In British English, prefer "alternative" for choices; avoid confusion with "alternating"
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - alternate route
   - alternate plan
   - alternate solution
   - alternate method
   - alternate version
   
   **Root Analysis**: alter (other) + -nate (suffix indicating action)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin alternatus (done by turns) → English "alternate" (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Take the alternate road instead of the main highway.
   - I have an alternate idea instead of your original plan.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Alternate procedures should be considered instead of standard protocols.
   - An alternate hypothesis was tested instead of the primary theory.

1. **Adjective**: supplementary (comp: more supplementary, sup: most supplementary)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Additional; added to complete or enhance something (gradable)
   - Serving as a supplement; extra but related (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: additional, extra, complementary, auxiliary, ancillary
   
   **Antonyms**: primary, main, essential, core, fundamental
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and academic contexts; indicates something added to rather than used instead of; often used when new material doesn't replace but adds to existing content
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when something completely replaces rather than adds to original; not appropriate for exclusive alternatives
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - supplementary material
   - supplementary information
   - supplementary evidence
   - supplementary resources
   - supplementary reading
   
   **Root Analysis**: supplement (add to) + -ary (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin supplementum (something added to complete) + -ary → English "supplementary" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Check the supplementary notes instead of just the main text.
   - I took supplementary vitamins instead of relying on diet alone.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Supplementary analyses were conducted instead of relying solely on primary data.
   - Supplementary documentation was provided instead of verbal explanations.

1. **Adjective**: other (non-gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to refer to a person or thing that is different or distinct from one already mentioned (non-gradable)
   - Additional; further; more (non-gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: different, alternative, additional, further, else
   
   **Antonyms**: same, identical, this
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts and registers; extremely common; indicates choosing something different instead of what was mentioned; one of the most versatile adjectives
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Rarely inappropriate; ensure clarity about which "other" thing is meant
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - other option
   - other choice
   - other way
   - other alternative
   - other possibility
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English root "other" (second of two)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English ōþer (second of two) → Middle English other → Modern English "other"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's try the other restaurant instead of this one.
   - I chose the other option instead of what you suggested.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Other methodologies were considered instead of the proposed approach.
   - The research examined other variables instead of conventional factors.

1. **Adjective**: preferred (non-gradable, attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Liked better or best; chosen as more desirable (non-gradable)
   - Given priority or advantage over others (non-gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: favored, chosen, selected, desired, favoured
   
   **Antonyms**: rejected, undesired, avoided, disliked
   
   **When to Use**: Use when indicating something selected as better; common in business and formal contexts; shows active choice of one thing instead of alternatives
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when no selection has been made; not appropriate when all options are treated equally
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - preferred method
   - preferred option
   - preferred choice
   - preferred approach
   - preferred alternative
   
   **Root Analysis**: pre- (before) + fer (carry) + -red (past participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praeferre (to carry before, to prefer) → English "preferred" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's my preferred way instead of doing it your way.
   - I use the preferred brand instead of cheaper alternatives.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The preferred methodology was adopted instead of conventional techniques.
   - Preferred protocols were implemented instead of outdated procedures.
