1. **Preposition**: given (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Taking into account; considering; if you accept that something is true or exists
   - Used to introduce a fact or situation that must be considered when making a judgment or decision
   
   **Synonyms**: considering (in context of evaluation), in view of (in formal context), taking into account (in analytical context), bearing in mind (in reflective context), in light of (in context of new information)
   
   **Antonyms**: disregarding, ignoring, without regard to, irrespective of
   
   **When to Use**: When presenting conditions, assumptions, or facts that form the basis for reasoning or conclusions; commonly used in academic writing, formal argumentation, mathematical proofs, and analytical discussions; appropriate when establishing premises before drawing conclusions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation where simpler words like "if" or "considering" would be more natural; not appropriate when the condition is hypothetical rather than accepted as fact; learners often confuse with "giving" (present participle)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: given the circumstances, given the situation, given the evidence, given that [clause]
   - **Verb + prep**: N/A (given functions independently as preposition)
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   - **At sentence start**: Given [noun phrase], [main clause]
   - **Before a clause**: given that + [subject + verb]
   
   **Root Analysis**: give (Old English giefan) + -en (past participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English giefan → Middle English given → Modern English "given" as preposition evolved from the past participle of "give" to mean "granted as a premise" (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Given how tired you are, maybe you should skip the party tonight.
   - Given that it's raining, I think we should just stay home.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Given the complexity of the issue, further investigation is warranted.
   - Given that previous studies have shown mixed results, this research adopts a more rigorous methodology.

1. **Preposition**: given to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Having a tendency or inclination toward something; prone to; in the habit of doing something
   - Used to describe someone's characteristic behavior or habitual actions, often with slightly negative connotation
   
   **Synonyms**: prone to, inclined to, apt to, liable to, disposed to
   
   **Antonyms**: averse to, disinclined to, resistant to, opposed to
   
   **When to Use**: When describing habitual tendencies or characteristic behaviors; appropriate in both formal and informal contexts; often used in character descriptions, psychological assessments, or behavioral observations; works well in literary and academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when describing one-time actions rather than habitual behaviors; not appropriate for positive habits in formal recommendations (use "committed to" or "dedicated to" instead); learners may incorrectly use with positive qualities where "dedicated to" would be better
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: given to exaggeration, given to fits of anger, given to daydreaming, given to introspection
   - **Verb + prep**: be given to [noun/gerund]
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: someone/person given to [behavior]
   
   **Root Analysis**: give (Old English giefan) + to (preposition indicating direction)
   
   **Etymology**: From Old English giefan meaning "to bestow" → Middle English development of "given to" meaning "devoted to" or "inclined toward" (14th century) → modern usage emphasizing habitual tendency
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He's given to complaining about everything, so don't take it personally.
   - My aunt is given to telling long stories about the old days.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The author was given to philosophical musings on the nature of consciousness.
   - Individuals given to perfectionism may experience heightened levels of anxiety.
