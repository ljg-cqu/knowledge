1. **Preposition**: against (type: simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In physical contact with and supported by; touching or leaning on a surface for support
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable as primary temporal preposition
   - *(Abstract - Opposition)* In opposition to; contrary to; expressing disagreement or resistance
   - *(Abstract - Protection)* As defense or protection from something harmful
   - *(Abstract - Comparison)* As a contrasting background; in comparison or contrast with
   - *(Abstract - Competition)* In competition or rivalry with; versus
   
   **Synonyms**: opposed to (opposition), contrary to (opposition), versus (competition), touching (spatial), in contact with (spatial)
   
   **Antonyms**: in favor of, for, supporting, with, alongside
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing opposition, resistance, physical contact, protection, competition, or contrasting comparison; versatile preposition with multiple distinct meanings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "toward" (opposite direction) is meant; when neutral positioning without opposition/contact is intended; context must clarify which meaning is intended
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the wall, against the rules, against the odds, against time, against one's will
   - **Verb + prep**: fight against, compete against, protect against, guard against, argue against, vote against, rebel against, lean against
   - **Adjective + prep**: defensive against, resistant against (less common than "resistant to"), effective against
   - **Noun + prep**: opposition against (less common than "opposition to"), protection against, defense against, prejudice against, case against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin ad- (to/toward) + Germanic gēn (toward) → Old English ongēan (toward, opposite to) → Middle English "ayenst/againes" → Modern English "against"
   
   **Etymology**: Complex etymology combining Latin and Germanic elements; Old English ongegn (toward, in opposition to) → Middle English againes/ayenst → Modern English "against" (13th century); meaning evolved from "in the direction of" to "in opposition to"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She leaned against the wall.
   - I'm totally against that idea.
   - We're playing against the champions tonight.
   - You should protect yourself against identity theft.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The evidence weighs heavily against the defendant.
   - Organizations must guard against cybersecurity threats.
   - The structure is positioned against the northern boundary.
   - Arguments against the proposal were systematically addressed.

1. **Preposition**: against (opposition sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Opposition)* In opposition to; contrary to; in disagreement with policies, rules, beliefs, or desires
   - Expressing active resistance, disagreement, or contrary position
   
   **Synonyms**: in opposition to, contrary to, opposed to, counter to, in conflict with
   
   **Antonyms**: in favor of, in support of, for, aligned with, in agreement with
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing disagreement with ideas, policies, rules; voting contexts; describing positions contrary to norms or expectations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Physical positioning (different sense); when neutral comparison rather than opposition is meant
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the law, against policy, against one's wishes, against nature, against the rules
   - **Verb + prep**: vote against, argue against, speak against, work against, go against, rebel against, protest against
   - **Adjective + prep**: (adjectives typically use "opposed to" rather than "against")
   - **Noun + prep**: argument against, case against, opposition against (less common), vote against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Same as main entry - opposition meaning developed from spatial "facing" to abstract "opposing"
   
   **Etymology**: Opposition sense developed in Middle English (14th century) from earlier spatial sense of "facing toward" → "in confrontation with"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm against working on weekends.
   - That goes against everything I believe in.
   - Why are you always going against the plan?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee voted against the proposed amendments.
   - This action contravenes regulations and operates against established protocols.
   - Stakeholders argued against implementation without adequate consultation.

1. **Preposition**: against (physical contact sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In physical contact with; touching or pressing upon a surface or object
   - Often implies support, pressure, or resting position
   
   **Synonyms**: on, touching, in contact with, pressed to, resting on
   
   **Antonyms**: away from, separate from, distant from, apart from
   
   **When to Use**: Describing physical positioning where something touches or rests on another surface; emphasizes contact or support
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When objects are near but not touching; abstract opposition (different sense)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the wall, against the door, against the window, against one's chest, against the table
   - **Verb + prep**: lean against, press against, push against, hold against, place against, rest against, stand against
   - **Adjective + prep**: flat against, pressed against, tight against
   - **Noun + prep**: pressure against, position against (when physical), force against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Spatial sense preserved from Old English ongēan (toward, facing)
   
   **Etymology**: Original spatial meaning from Old English (pre-12th century); retained throughout language evolution
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Put the ladder against the wall.
   - He was leaning against the car.
   - Press your back flat against the floor.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The specimen should be positioned against the measurement grid.
   - Subjects were instructed to place their foreheads against the apparatus.
   - Structural elements rest against load-bearing surfaces.

1. **Preposition**: against (protection/defense sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Protection)* As protection or defense from harm, danger, or negative effects
   - Expressing preventive or defensive measures
   
   **Synonyms**: from (in protection contexts), as defense from, as protection from, to prevent, to guard from
   
   **Antonyms**: exposed to, vulnerable to, susceptible to, unprotected from
   
   **When to Use**: Describing protective measures, defensive strategies, preventive actions; common in medical, security, and safety contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing actual harm (use "from" or "by"); active opposition (different sense)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against disease, against harm, against threats, against attack, against infection, against loss
   - **Verb + prep**: protect against, guard against, defend against, insure against, vaccinate against, shield against, safeguard against
   - **Adjective + prep**: immune against (less common than "immune to"), secure against, safe against, resistant against (less common than "resistant to")
   - **Noun + prep**: protection against, defense against, insurance against, safeguard against, precaution against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Protection sense extended from "in confrontation with" → "in defense from"
   
   **Etymology**: Defense/protection meaning developed in Middle English (15th century) as metaphorical extension of oppositional stance
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You should get vaccinated against the flu.
   - This insurance protects against theft.
   - Wear sunscreen to protect against sunburn.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Systems must incorporate safeguards against unauthorized access.
   - Prophylactic measures guard against nosocomial infection.
   - Diversification provides insurance against market volatility.

1. **Preposition**: against (competition sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Competition)* In competition, rivalry, or contest with; opposing in a game, match, or competitive situation
   - Equivalent to "versus" in competitive contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: versus, in competition with, competing with, opposed to, facing
   
   **Antonyms**: with, alongside, partnered with, in cooperation with
   
   **When to Use**: Sports contexts, competitive situations, contests, or any scenario involving rivals or opponents
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Cooperative activities; when "with" (partnership) is meant
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the champions, against tough competition, against the best
   - **Verb + prep**: play against, compete against, race against, fight against (literal combat), match against, pit against
   - **Adjective + prep**: competitive against (less common)
   - **Noun + prep**: match against, game against, competition against, contest against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Competitive sense from oppositional meaning - "in opposition to" → "competing with"
   
   **Etymology**: Competitive usage emerged naturally from opposition sense (16th-17th century) as organized competitions became common
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're playing against the top team tonight.
   - She's competing against the best in the country.
   - It's you against me in the final round.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization competes against established market leaders.
   - Teams were matched against opponents of comparable skill levels.
   - Performance was evaluated against international standards.

1. **Preposition**: against (contrast/background sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Comparison)* With as a contrasting background; in comparison or contrast to; viewed in relation to
   - Emphasizes how something appears, stands out, or compares when viewed with contrasting element
   
   **Synonyms**: compared to, in contrast with, in comparison to, relative to, viewed against
   
   **Antonyms**: merged with, indistinguishable from, similar to
   
   **When to Use**: Describing visual contrast, analytical comparison, or evaluating something in context; emphasizes difference or visibility
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Simple equal comparison (use "with"); when similarity rather than contrast is emphasized
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the background, against the sky, against a dark backdrop, against expectations, against benchmarks
   - **Verb + prep**: stand out against, measure against, evaluate against, compare against, set against, judge against, assess against
   - **Adjective + prep**: visible against, clear against, stark against, evident against
   - **Noun + prep**: contrast against, comparison against, silhouette against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Comparison sense from spatial "positioned before/in front of" → "viewed in relation to"
   
   **Etymology**: Comparative usage developed in Modern English (17th-18th century) from visual contrast metaphor
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The bird stood out against the blue sky.
   - When you measure it against the competition, we're doing pretty well.
   - His behavior looks bad against what he promised.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Performance must be evaluated against established criteria.
   - Results are analyzed against baseline measurements.
   - The silhouette appears prominently against the illuminated background.

1. **Preposition**: against (resistance to force sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Force opposition)* In resistance to physical force, pressure, or movement; opposing force or motion
   - Emphasizes resistance to push, pull, or directional force
   
   **Synonyms**: resisting, opposing, counter to, in opposition to (force)
   
   **Antonyms**: with (force), in the direction of, following, yielding to
   
   **When to Use**: Physics, mechanics, describing physical resistance to forces; metaphorically for resistance to pressure or influence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Simple physical contact without resistance; when direction rather than resistance is meant
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the current, against the wind, against the tide, against the flow, against gravity, against resistance
   - **Verb + prep**: push against, pull against, swim against, row against, struggle against, fight against (metaphorical force), work against (force)
   - **Adjective + prep**: resistant against (less common)
   - **Noun + prep**: resistance against, force against, pressure against, struggle against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Force-resistance sense from opposition meaning applied to physical forces
   
   **Etymology**: Force-opposition usage established in Middle English (14th century) for both literal and metaphorical forces
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We were swimming against the current.
   - You're pushing against a locked door.
   - It's hard rowing against the wind.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The mechanism operates against gravitational forces.
   - Organisms must swim against significant hydrodynamic resistance.
   - Pressure against the valve exceeds design specifications.

1. **Preposition**: against (disadvantageous to sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Disadvantage)* To the disadvantage of; harmful or unfavorable to someone's interests or position
   - Indicating negative impact or working to someone's detriment
   
   **Synonyms**: to the disadvantage of, unfavorable to, harmful to, detrimental to, prejudicial to
   
   **Antonyms**: in favor of, to the benefit of, advantageous to, helpful to
   
   **When to Use**: Legal contexts (evidence against), describing unfavorable circumstances or factors working against success
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Neutral comparison; when positive or neutral impact is discussed
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against one's interests, against the odds, against all chances, against probability
   - **Verb + prep**: count against, work against, weigh against, testify against, tell against (British), go against
   - **Adjective + prep**: prejudicial against, detrimental against (less common)
   - **Noun + prep**: evidence against, testimony against, odds against, case against, ruling against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Disadvantage sense from opposition meaning → "positioned in opposition to one's benefit"
   
   **Etymology**: Disadvantage usage well-established by Early Modern English (16th century), particularly in legal contexts
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everything seems to be going against me today.
   - The odds are really against us.
   - That mistake will count against you.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The evidence weighs heavily against the defendant's claims.
   - Multiple factors worked against successful implementation.
   - Testimony provided against the accused proved decisive.

1. **Preposition**: against (contact with force sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Into contact with force or collision; striking or hitting a surface
   - Emphasizes impact or forceful contact rather than gentle resting
   
   **Synonyms**: into, onto (with force), upon (with impact), hitting
   
   **Antonyms**: away from, bouncing off, missing
   
   **When to Use**: Describing collisions, impacts, forceful contact; emphasizes energy or force in the contact
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Gentle resting or leaning (different sense of "against"); when near but not touching
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against the rocks, against the wall (with impact), against the ground
   - **Verb + prep**: crash against, bang against, slam against, throw against, beat against, pound against, dash against, strike against
   - **Adjective + prep**: (typically dynamic verbs rather than adjectives)
   - **Noun + prep**: impact against, collision against, crash against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Impact sense from basic spatial meaning with added force component
   
   **Etymology**: Impact meaning present from Middle English (13th century), natural extension of contact sense with force element
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The waves crashed against the shore.
   - He accidentally banged his head against the door.
   - Rain was beating against the window.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Projectiles struck against the protective barrier.
   - Hydraulic forces pound against structural components.
   - The vehicle collided against the barrier at high velocity.

1. **Preposition**: against (in exchange for sense)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Exchange)* In exchange or return for; as payment or security for something
   - Used in financial/commercial contexts for transactions and security
   
   **Synonyms**: in exchange for, in return for, as security for, for
   
   **Antonyms**: without payment, freely, gratis
   
   **When to Use**: Formal financial, banking, or commercial contexts; describing secured transactions or exchanges
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual transactions (use "for"); informal contexts; this is the least common sense
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against payment, against receipt, against collateral, against delivery, against invoice
   - **Verb + prep**: borrow against, lend against, secure against, issue against, provide against (as security)
   - **Adjective + prep**: (rare in this sense)
   - **Noun + prep**: loan against, advance against, credit against, payment against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Exchange sense from "in opposition/balance to" → "in return for"
   
   **Etymology**: Commercial/financial usage developed in Early Modern English (16th-17th century) in banking and trade contexts
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Rarely used casually; more natural: "in exchange for" or "for")
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Funds were advanced against future receivables.
   - Loans may be secured against property assets.
   - Payment is made against delivery of goods.
   - Credit was issued against verified collateral.
