1. **Adjective**: pro-democracy (comparative: more pro-democracy, superlative: most pro-democracy)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Supporting democratic systems of government and democratic reforms; favouring free elections, rule of law, and civil liberties.
   - *(Non-gradable in core political sense; used attributively before nouns)*
   
   **Synonyms**: democratic, pro-reform, liberal (in political sense), pro-freedom, pro-pluralist
   
   **Antonyms**: anti-democratic, authoritarian, autocratic, undemocratic, pro-authoritarian
   
   **When to Use**: Describe people, movements, parties, or policies that clearly support democratic institutions and processes ("pro-democracy activists", "a pro-democracy movement"). Common in news, analysis, and political discussion.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in contexts where "democratic" alone is clearer and sufficient, especially in neutral institutional descriptions. Do not use as a neutral label for all groups in an existing democracy—reserve it for actors specifically pushing to strengthen or restore democracy.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-democracy protesters
   - pro-democracy movement
   - pro-democracy reforms
   - pro-democracy agenda
   - pro-democracy coalition
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for, in favour of) + noun `democracy`
   
   **Etymology**: Formed in modern political English by combining Latin-derived prefix `pro-` (for) with `democracy`. Became common in reporting on democratic transitions and protest movements in the late 20th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - A lot of young people are strongly pro-democracy and want fair elections.
   - She joined a pro-democracy group at university.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - International observers expressed support for pro-democracy organisations operating under restrictive conditions.
   - The study compares outcomes in countries with strong pro-democracy civil society networks and those without.

1. **Adjective**: pro-environment / pro-environmental (comparative: more pro-environmental, superlative: most pro-environmental)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Supporting environmental protection, sustainability, and conservation; designed to reduce harm to the natural environment.
   - *(Gradable; used both attributively and predicatively)*
   
   **Synonyms**: environmentally friendly, eco-friendly, green, sustainability-focused, climate-conscious
   
   **Antonyms**: anti-environmental, polluting, environmentally harmful, carbon-intensive
   
   **When to Use**: Describe policies, behaviours, technologies, or attitudes that aim to protect the environment ("pro-environment policies", "pro-environment behaviour").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for measures with only minimal or symbolic environmental impact; in those cases "limited environmental impact" or "mixed impact" may be more accurate.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-environment policies
   - pro-environment behaviour
   - pro-environmental regulations
   - pro-environment business strategy
   - strongly pro-environment stance
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + noun `environment` / adjective-forming suffix `-al`
   
   **Etymology**: Emerged in late 20th-century English alongside environmental movements; `pro-environmental` is common in academic and policy writing.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Our city has become more pro-environment lately, with new bike lanes and tree planting.
   - He's pretty pro-environment—he cycles to work and rarely flies.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pro-environmental attitudes were a significant predictor of support for carbon pricing.
   - The firm adopted a pro-environment strategy centred on energy efficiency and waste reduction.

1. **Adjective**: pro-business (comparative: more pro-business, superlative: most pro-business)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Favouring business interests, lower regulation, or policies that make it easier for companies to operate and grow.
   - *(Gradable; typically attributive)*
   
   **Synonyms**: business-friendly, market-friendly, pro-market, pro-investment, pro-growth
   
   **Antonyms**: anti-business, highly interventionist, hostile to business, over-regulatory
   
   **When to Use**: Describe governments, policies, or environments that are supportive of commercial activity ("pro-business reforms", "a pro-business climate").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using as a neutral synonym for "effective"—some audiences may see strongly pro-business policies as unbalanced if worker or consumer interests are ignored.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-business government
   - pro-business reforms
   - pro-business agenda
   - strongly pro-business stance
   - pro-business tax policy
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + noun `business`
   
   **Etymology**: Widespread in late 20th-century economic and political commentary, especially when contrasting "pro-business" with "pro-labour" or "pro-consumer" positions.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This mayor is really pro-business, so startups get a lot of support.
   - The new rules are super pro-business but not great for workers.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The administration implemented a package of pro-business tax incentives to attract foreign investment.
   - Survey data suggest that pro-business rhetoric does not always translate into measurable improvements in the investment climate.

1. **Adjective**: pro-labour / pro-labor (US spelling) (comparative: more pro-labour, superlative: most pro-labour)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Supporting workers' rights, unions, and employee protections; tending to prioritise labour interests over purely corporate ones.
   - *(Gradable; mainly attributive)*
   
   **Synonyms**: worker-friendly, union-friendly, pro-union, labour-oriented, employee-centred
   
   **Antonyms**: anti-union, union-busting, strongly pro-business (in a conflicting sense), employer-dominated
   
   **When to Use**: Describe policies, parties, or leaders that support trade unions, collective bargaining, minimum-wage increases, or stronger safety standards ("pro-labour legislation").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for measures that simply comply with basic legal minimums; reserve it for clearly worker-supportive positions.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-labour party
   - pro-labour stance
   - pro-labour reforms
   - pro-labour agenda
   - pro-union / pro-labour coalition
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + noun `labour`
   
   **Etymology**: Common in political journalism when contrasting parties or coalitions aligned with unions versus those aligned with business.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The new government seems a lot more pro-labour than the last one.
   - She's very pro-labor and always talks about workers' rights.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pro-labour governments are more likely to expand collective bargaining coverage.
   - The study finds that pro-labour reforms correlate with modest reductions in income inequality.

1. **Adjective**: pro-market (comparative: more pro-market, superlative: most pro-market)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Supporting free-market mechanisms, competition, and limited state intervention in the economy.
   - *(Gradable; attributive and predicative)*
   
   **Synonyms**: market-oriented, market-friendly, liberal (economic sense), deregulatory, pro-competition
   
   **Antonyms**: anti-market, command-economy-oriented, highly interventionist, protectionist (in some contexts)

   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in non-economic contexts where "supportive" or "favourable" is clearer; do not use as a synonym for "pro-business" when policies also strengthen competition against incumbents.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-market reforms
   - pro-market agenda
   - pro-market stance
   - strongly pro-market approach
   - pro-market think tank
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + noun `market`
   
   **Etymology**: Became prominent in late 20th-century economics and policy debates, especially around deregulation and privatisation.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He's pretty pro-market and doesn't like heavy regulation.
   - The reforms are very pro-market—lots of privatisation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pro-market reforms were introduced to enhance competition in the telecommunications sector.
   - The party's manifesto sets out a distinctly pro-market economic programme.

   **Meaning**:

   **When to Use**: Describe individuals, organisations, or policies that prioritise scientific evidence in areas such as health, climate, and technology ("pro-science administration", "pro-science messaging").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using it merely as praise; keep it descriptive (based on actual support for research, funding, and evidence-informed policy).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-science government
   - pro-science stance
   - pro-science messaging
   - strongly pro-science community
   - pro-science education policy
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + noun `science`
   
   **Etymology**: Informal but widely used in 21st-century debates about vaccines, climate change, and public trust in expertise.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Our city is really pro-science—they invest a lot in research centres.
   - She's very pro-science and always checks the data first.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pro-science communication strategies emphasise transparency and methodological limitations.
   - The coalition positioned itself as pro-science, pro-innovation, and pro-evidence-based policy.

1. **Adjective**: pro-social (comparative: more pro-social, superlative: most pro-social)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Describing behaviour or attitudes that are helpful, cooperative, and intended to benefit others or society as a whole.
   - *(Gradable; attributive and predicative)*
   
   **Synonyms**: socially helpful, altruistic, cooperative, community-minded, public-spirited
   
   **Antonyms**: antisocial, selfish, self-centred, harmful, exploitative
   
   **When to Use**: Psychology, education, and social-policy contexts to describe positive social behaviours and attitudes ("pro-social behaviour", "pro-social norms").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in purely technical contexts where "cooperative" or "collaborative" is clearer; do not confuse with "social" meaning "outgoing".
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-social behaviour
   - pro-social norms
   - pro-social values
   - pro-social campaigns
   - encourage pro-social actions
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for) + adjective `social`
   
   **Etymology**: Entered psychological and sociological vocabulary in the late 20th century as the opposite of "antisocial".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Volunteering is a really pro-social thing to do.
   - The app rewards pro-social behaviour like sharing accurate information.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Interventions focused on strengthening pro-social norms reduced antisocial incidents among adolescents.
   - Pro-social messaging emphasised community benefit rather than individual gain.

1. **Adjective**: pro-innovation (comparative: more pro-innovation, superlative: most pro-innovation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Actively encouraging new ideas, experimentation, and change; supportive of trying novel approaches even at some risk.
   - *(Gradable; attributive)*
   
   **Synonyms**: innovation-friendly, forward-looking, progressive, experimental, change-positive
   
   **Antonyms**: risk-averse, conservative (in change sense), innovation-hostile, status-quo-oriented
   
   **When to Use**: Describe policies, leadership styles, or cultures that create space and incentives for experimentation ("pro-innovation regulation", "pro-innovation leadership").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when change is only cosmetic or when innovation is tightly constrained; use more precise terms like "limited pilot".
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pro-innovation policy
   - pro-innovation ecosystem
   - pro-innovation leadership
   - strongly pro-innovation stance
   - pro-innovation regulatory framework
   
   **Root Analysis**: prefix `pro-` (for, forward) + noun `innovation`
   
   **Etymology**: Modern policy and business term aligning `pro-` (for) with "innovation" as a positive goal in economic strategy.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Our manager is really pro-innovation—she lets us test new ideas.
   - The country has built a very pro-innovation startup scene.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pro-innovation regulatory sandboxes allow controlled experimentation with new financial services.
   - A pro-innovation stance must be balanced with safeguards for safety and fairness.
