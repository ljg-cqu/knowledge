1. **Functional Expression**: I'm speaking qua [role] (function: specifying standpoint/role)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to make explicit which role or capacity the speaker is adopting for the current statement (e.g., "I'm speaking qua teacher")
   - *Formality*: semi‑formal to formal (academic, professional discussions)
   - *Directness*: direct but metadiscursive (talking about how you're talking)
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm speaking as [role], From my position as [role], Speaking in my capacity as [role]
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm speaking personally, I'm not speaking as [role], Off the record
   
   **When to Use**: In meetings, panels, or discussions where one person has multiple identities (researcher, manager, parent); when you need to clarify that a remark reflects an official or expert role rather than a private opinion.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual conversation ("as your friend" is enough); don't use if the role distinction is obvious or unimportant.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm speaking qua [professional role]
   - Speaking qua member of [committee]
   - Not speaking qua [role] here
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Marks stance explicitly; a face‑saving device when distancing private views from institutional positions; also used to claim expert authority.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "That's helpful—thanks for speaking qua [role]."
   - Clarifying: "Are you speaking qua manager or qua engineer here?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm speaking qua friend now, not qua boss.
   - Just to be clear, I'm speaking qua teacher, thinking about your progress.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I'm speaking qua member of the ethics committee, so I must flag these concerns.
   - Here I am speaking qua researcher, not qua representative of the agency.

1. **Functional Expression**: In my capacity qua [role], ... (function: framing official statement)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Introduces a statement or decision made in an official or clearly defined role, often with legal or organizational consequences
   - *Formality*: formal (legal letters, policy documents, official emails)
   - *Directness*: indirect opening softening a firm decision
   
   **Synonyms**: In my capacity as [role], Acting as [role], In my official role as [role]
   
   **Antonyms**: In a purely personal capacity, Speaking informally, Not in my role as [role]
   
   **When to Use**: When signing decisions, issuing formal opinions, handling complaints, or communicating decisions that derive from institutional authority rather than personal preference.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in everyday talk; don't use if the message is purely personal or social; may sound pompous in casual contexts.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - acting in my capacity qua [role]
   - solely in my capacity qua [role]
   - in his/her/their capacity qua [role]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals institutional backing and delimits responsibility; helps manage power distance by clarifying that the speaker is constrained by a role.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Acknowledging: "We understand this is in your capacity qua [role]."
   - Challenging: "Are you speaking only in that capacity, or also personally?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In my capacity qua team lead, I have to say no to that request.
   - In my capacity qua parent, I can sign this form; qua friend, I can't.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In my capacity qua department chair, I hereby approve your research proposal.
   - The ombudsperson, in her capacity qua independent officer, will review the appeal.

1. **Functional Expression**: I'm not speaking qua [role], just as a [other role] (function: disclaiming official capacity)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to distance a comment from one's official or expert role and reframe it as a personal or alternative‑role opinion
   - *Formality*: neutral to semi‑formal
   - *Directness*: indirect softening of potential authority/power
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm speaking personally, This is just my personal view, Not in my role as [role]
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm speaking entirely qua [role], This is the official position
   
   **When to Use**: In mentoring, supervision, or advisory contexts where you want to share a personal perspective without binding your institution or formal role.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when you *are* obliged to speak officially; don't use to evade responsibility for formal decisions.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm not speaking qua manager, just as a colleague.
   - I'm not speaking qua regulator, just as a researcher.
   - This is me qua friend, not qua supervisor.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Protects both the speaker and institution from misunderstanding; reduces perceived pressure on the listener; an important boundary‑setting tool.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Appreciating: "Thanks, it's helpful to hear you just as a friend."
   - Clarifying: "At some point, could you also respond qua manager?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm not speaking qua boss, just as someone who cares about you.
   - This is me not speaking qua teacher, just as another person who struggled with exams.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - At this point, I am not speaking qua examiner, but as a colleague who has faced similar difficulties.
   - Let me step aside from my role: I'm not speaking qua regulator here, but as a policy researcher.
