1. **Verb**: regard (as)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To think of or consider someone or something in a particular way
   - Often followed by "as" or implicitly by a role/aspect phrase (regard X qua Y)
   
   **Synonyms**: consider, view, see, treat, judge
   
   **Antonyms**: disregard, overlook, ignore, misjudge
   
   **When to Use**: Use when you consciously choose a standpoint—"regard her qua scientist, not qua manager". Common in philosophy, ethics, and legal theory.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual speech where "see" or "think of" is more natural; don't use when you simply mean "respect" (different verb sense).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - regard X as Y
   - regard the agent qua [role]
   - be regarded qua member of the group
   - widely regarded as
   - properly regarded qua subject of rights
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again) + gard (look) → "look at again/with care"
   
   **Etymology**: Old French *regarder* (to look back at, heed) → Middle English "regarden" → "regard".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I try to regard him more as a colleague than as a rival.
   - In this chat I'm regarding you qua learner, not qua customer.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The theory asks us to regard persons qua rational agents.
   - Courts increasingly regard the corporation qua separate legal person.

1. **Verb**: consider
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To think about carefully, especially in order to make a decision
   - To think of someone or something in a particular way (consider X qua Y)
   
   **Synonyms**: contemplate, reflect on, regard, view, treat
   
   **Antonyms**: ignore, dismiss, overlook, disregard
   
   **When to Use**: Use when shifting frame—"consider the worker qua citizen" vs "qua employee". Frequent in analytic writing when moving between aspects of the same entity.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid as a filler where no real thought is implied; don't pair with "about" when you need an object clause ("consider that...", not *"consider about"*).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - consider X qua Y
   - consider the agent qua chooser
   - consider A qua member of B
   - consider separately/as distinct
   - consider only in so far as
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + sider (star) → "consult the stars together" → ponder carefully
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *considerare* (to examine closely) → Old French → Middle English "consideren".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Try to consider me qua friend, not qua boss, for this question.
   - We should consider the school qua community, not just qua building.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We shall consider the firm qua nexus of contracts.
   - The argument fails once we consider citizens qua equals in political power.

1. **Verb**: treat (as)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To behave toward or deal with someone or something in a particular way
   - To regard something as having a particular quality or status (treat X qua Y)
   
   **Synonyms**: handle, deal with, regard, view, behave toward
   
   **Antonyms**: neglect, ignore, mistreat, overlook
   
   **When to Use**: Use when specifying how something is handled from a role/aspect viewpoint—"treat data qua confidential", "treat patients qua persons". Common in ethics, law, and professional codes.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when describing medical treatment (different sense); don't use vaguely without indicating "as what".
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - treat X as Y
   - treat individuals qua ends, not merely as means
   - treat the case qua exception
   - treat both sides equally qua parties to the contract
   
   **Root Analysis**: treat (from Latin *tractare* = to handle, manage)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *tractare* (handle, manage) → Old French *traiter* → Middle English "treaten" / "treat".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please treat this message qua private, not for the whole group.
   - They treated him qua guest, not qua employee, so everything was free.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy requires us to treat users qua data subjects with specific rights.
   - Kant insists we treat persons qua rational agents, never merely as means.

1. **Verb**: view (as)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To see or judge something in a particular way; to regard from a certain perspective
   
   **Synonyms**: see, regard, consider, interpret, perceive
   
   **Antonyms**: misperceive, misinterpret, overlook, disregard
   
   **When to Use**: Use in phrases like "view X qua Y" when highlighting interpretive standpoint—"view the practice qua ritual" vs "qua entertainment". Common in humanities and social sciences.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when literally looking at something (use "look at"); don't overuse as vague academic filler.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - view X as Y
   - view the agent qua [role]
   - can be viewed qua institution
   - traditionally viewed as
   - alternatively viewed qua symbol
   
   **Root Analysis**: view (look at, see) from Latin *videre* (to see)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French *veue* (a look, sight) → "view"; verb sense "to look at as" developed later.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I tend to view you qua mentor more than qua colleague.
   - Try to view the exam qua challenge, not qua punishment.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The firm can be viewed qua social institution, not merely qua profit machine.
   - Practices once viewed qua religious rites are now viewed qua cultural heritage.

1. **Verb**: construe (as)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To interpret the meaning of words or actions in a particular way
   - Especially in law and philosophy: to understand something *as* having a certain role or significance
   
   **Synonyms**: interpret, read, understand, parse, take (as)
   
   **Antonyms**: misinterpret, misunderstand, distort, misconstrue
   
   **When to Use**: Use in careful analysis where you choose one aspect or capacity—"construe the state qua guarantor of rights". Common in legal interpretation and theoretical argument.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very informal speech; don't use if you simply mean "translate" or "explain" for beginners.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - construe X as Y
   - construe the agent qua [role]
   - properly construed qua
   - can be construed either qua A or qua B
   - narrowly/broadly construed
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + struere (build) → "build together" meaning from parts
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *construere* (to pile together, arrange) → "construe" in English (15th c.); from grammar (construing sentences) to general "interpret".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I wouldn't construe that joke qua insult.
   - You could construe my question qua curiosity, not criticism.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract is best construed as imposing duties on the firm qua employer.
   - Political authority can be construed qua service to citizens rather than qua mere domination.
