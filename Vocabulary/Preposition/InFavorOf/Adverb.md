1. **Adverb**: favorably (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that shows approval or support; in a positive or advantageous manner (modifies verbs, typically in mid or end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: positively, approvingly, advantageously, beneficially, well
   
   **Antonyms**: unfavorably, negatively, adversely, disapprovingly
   
   **When to Use**: In formal or semi-formal contexts when describing how someone views, responds to, or evaluates something; common in business and academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very casual conversation where simpler adverbs like "well" or "positively" sound more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - view favorably
   - respond favorably
   - look favorably upon
   - compare favorably
   - favorably disposed
   
   **Root Analysis**: favor (Latin favor) + -able + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin favor → Old French favour → English favor + -ably (14th century with adverb form emerging later)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The boss looked favorably on my request for time off.
   - People are responding favorably to the new policy so far.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposal was favorably received by the review committee.
   - Stakeholders have responded favorably to the company's sustainability initiatives.

1. **Adverb**: supportively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that gives encouragement, approval, or help; showing support (modifies verbs, usually end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: encouragingly, approvingly, helpfully, cooperatively
   
   **Antonyms**: unsupportively, critically, hostilely, obstructively
   
   **When to Use**: When describing how actions or behaviors demonstrate support or encouragement; appropriate in both personal and professional contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing the nature of support itself rather than the manner of action; can sound awkward in some constructions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond supportively
   - act supportively
   - speak supportively
   - nod supportively
   - listen supportively
   
   **Root Analysis**: support (Latin supportare) + -ive + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin supportare → Old French supporter → English support + -ively
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She nodded supportively when I shared my concerns about the project.
   - My teammates reacted supportively to my unusual idea.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The department head responded supportively to the innovation proposal.
   - Colleagues engaged supportively throughout the consultation process.

1. **Adverb**: affirmatively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that expresses agreement or approval; saying "yes" or indicating positive response (modifies verbs, end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: positively, approvingly, in agreement, yes, agreeably
   
   **Antonyms**: negatively, disapprovingly, in opposition, no
   
   **When to Use**: In formal, legal, or official contexts when describing responses or votes that indicate agreement; more formal than simply saying "yes"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual conversation where it sounds overly formal or stilted; use simpler alternatives
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - answer affirmatively
   - respond affirmatively
   - vote affirmatively
   - nod affirmatively
   - reply affirmatively
   
   **Root Analysis**: affirm (Latin affirmare) + -ative + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin affirmare → Old French affermer → English affirm + -atively
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - When asked if I wanted dessert, I nodded affirmatively.
   - He replied affirmatively to the invitation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee voted affirmatively on all three proposed amendments.
   - Shareholders responded affirmatively to the merger recommendation by a margin of 73%.

1. **Adverb**: wholeheartedly (type: manner/degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a completely enthusiastic and committed way; with full sincerity and without reservation (modifies verbs, typically end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: completely, unreservedly, enthusiastically, fully, unconditionally
   
   **Antonyms**: halfheartedly, reluctantly, reservedly, lukewarmly, hesitantly
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing complete and enthusiastic support or agreement; suitable for both formal and informal contexts when expressing strong commitment
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing partial or qualified support; wholeheartedly implies no reservations
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - support wholeheartedly
   - agree wholeheartedly
   - endorse wholeheartedly
   - embrace wholeheartedly
   - recommend wholeheartedly
   
   **Root Analysis**: whole + heart + -ed + -ly (combining full with emotion)
   
   **Etymology**: Whole (Old English hal) + heart (Old English heorte) + -ed + -ly (compound formed in English, 19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I wholeheartedly agree with your decision to take that job.
   - We're wholeheartedly behind you on this.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board wholeheartedly endorses the strategic direction proposed by management.
   - This institution wholeheartedly supports initiatives that promote academic excellence.

1. **Adverb**: enthusiastically (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval (modifies verbs, usually end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: eagerly, keenly, excitedly, passionately, zealously
   
   **Antonyms**: unenthusiastically, reluctantly, halfheartedly, apathetically, indifferently
   
   **When to Use**: When describing actions or responses that show strong positive emotion and commitment; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In contexts requiring professional restraint or when enthusiasm might seem inappropriate or excessive
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - support enthusiastically
   - endorse enthusiastically
   - embrace enthusiastically
   - respond enthusiastically
   - participate enthusiastically
   
   **Root Analysis**: enthuse (from Greek entheos "inspired") + -iastic + -ally
   
   **Etymology**: Greek entheos → enthusiasm → enthusiastic + -ally (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone clapped enthusiastically when she announced the good news.
   - He enthusiastically agreed to help with the event.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Committee members enthusiastically endorsed the comprehensive reform package.
   - Stakeholders have enthusiastically embraced the digital transformation initiative.

1. **Adverb**: decidedly (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is definite and clear; unquestionably or unmistakably (modifies adjectives and verbs, emphasizes degree)
   
   **Synonyms**: definitely, clearly, unquestionably, unmistakably, distinctly
   
   **Antonyms**: arguably, questionably, ambiguously, uncertainly
   
   **When to Use**: To emphasize that a position or attitude is clear and definite; works in formal and informal contexts to stress lack of ambiguity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When neutrality or diplomatic ambiguity is preferred; decidedly can sound too strong in some situations
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - decidedly in favor
   - decidedly supportive
   - decidedly positive
   - decidedly pro
   - decidedly favorable
   
   **Root Analysis**: decide (Latin decidere "to cut off") + -ed + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin decidere → French décider → English decide + -edly (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's decidedly in favor of the new work-from-home policy.
   - I'm decidedly on your side in this argument.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The electorate has shifted decidedly toward progressive candidates.
   - Economic indicators remain decidedly positive for continued expansion.

1. **Adverb**: actively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that involves energetic participation, direct action, or deliberate effort (modifies verbs, emphasizes agency)
   
   **Synonyms**: energetically, vigorously, dynamically, purposefully, deliberately
   
   **Antonyms**: passively, inactively, idly, indifferently
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing that support involves action and engagement rather than passive agreement; suitable across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing passive support or agreement; actively implies effortful engagement
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - actively support
   - actively promote
   - actively advocate
   - actively champion
   - actively endorse
   
   **Root Analysis**: act (Latin actus "doing") + -ive + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin actus → activus → English active + -ly (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm actively supporting her campaign by volunteering on weekends.
   - They're actively looking for ways to help the cause.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization actively promotes policies that advance social equity.
   - Research institutions actively collaborate to accelerate innovation.

1. **Adverb**: unreservedly (type: manner/degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is complete and without any doubts or conditions; without holding anything back (modifies verbs, end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: completely, fully, unconditionally, without reservation, wholeheartedly
   
   **Antonyms**: reservedly, conditionally, hesitantly, cautiously, partially
   
   **When to Use**: In formal contexts when expressing complete support or approval without qualifications; emphasizes totality of commitment
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When support is partial or conditional; unreservedly implies no limitations or doubts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - support unreservedly
   - endorse unreservedly
   - recommend unreservedly
   - approve unreservedly
   - back unreservedly
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (not) + reserve (keep back) + -ed + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin reservare → French réserver → English reserve with un- prefix + -edly
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I unreservedly apologize for my mistake.
   - She backs the plan unreservedly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The commission unreservedly endorses the recommendations contained in this report.
   - We unreservedly support legislative measures that strengthen consumer protection.
