1. **Preposition**: for (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In support of; in favor of; on the side of (expressing support or preference)
   - *(Sense 2)* Indicating the beneficiary or recipient
   - *(Sense 3)* Indicating purpose or reason
   
   **Synonyms**: in favor of (in context of support), supporting, pro (informal), on behalf of (in some contexts)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, versus, anti
   
   **When to Use**: In all registers when expressing support, preference, or being on a particular side; versatile preposition appropriate from casual to formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When more formal expression "in favor of" would add necessary formality; "for" can be ambiguous as it has many meanings
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: vote for, argue for, speak for, fight for
   - **Verb + prep**: be for, stand for, declare for, come out for
   - **Adjective + prep**: keen for, eager for (when meaning supporting)
   - **Noun + prep**: support for, argument for, case for, vote for
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English for/fore (cognate with Greek pro "before, for")
   
   **Etymology**: Old English for → Middle English for (unchanged basic form, one of oldest English prepositions)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm definitely for the new schedule—it works much better.
   - Are you for or against this plan?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee voted 8-2 for the proposed amendments.
   - Arguments for comprehensive reform have gained traction among policymakers.

1. **Preposition**: in favor of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Supporting or preferring something; on the side of; approving of
   - *(Sense 2)* To the advantage of; benefiting
   
   **Synonyms**: supporting, for, pro, on the side of, in support of
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, in opposition to, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: In formal and semi-formal contexts when expressing support, preference, or position; more formal than simple "for"; appropriate in debates, voting, official statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very casual conversation where "for" is more natural; avoid redundancy with other formal expressions
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: be in favor of, come out in favor of, vote in favor of
   - **Verb + prep**: decide in favor of, rule in favor of, argue in favor of
   - **Adjective + prep**: strongly in favor of, very much in favor of
   - **Noun + prep**: those in favor of, majority in favor of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (preposition of state) + favor (Latin favor "goodwill") + of (connecting preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin favor → French faveur → English "in favor of" (phrase established by 14th-15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm in favor of taking a longer lunch break today.
   - Most people seem to be in favor of the new policy.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The court ruled in favor of the plaintiff's constitutional challenge.
   - Senators voted 67-33 in favor of the comprehensive infrastructure package.

1. **Preposition**: behind (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Supporting or backing someone or something; in a position of support
   - *(Spatial)* At or toward the back of; following
   
   **Synonyms**: supporting, backing, in support of (in support context)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, in front of (spatial)
   
   **When to Use**: When expressing support with metaphorical positioning; implies active backing; appropriate across all registers; commonly used with verbs of support
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When spatial meaning would create ambiguity; in very formal contexts where more explicit language is preferred
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: stand behind, get behind, throw weight behind, rally behind
   - **Verb + prep**: be behind, come behind, fall behind (different sense)
   - **Adjective + prep**: united behind, solidly behind
   - **Noun + prep**: support behind, those behind
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English behindan (be- "by" + hindan "from behind")
   
   **Etymology**: Old English behindan → Middle English behinde → modern behind; metaphorical support sense developed from spatial positioning
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're all behind you on this decision.
   - The whole team is behind the new approach.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The administration stands firmly behind its commitment to transparency.
   - Stakeholders have aligned behind the strategic restructuring plan.

1. **Preposition**: with (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* On the same side as; in support of; in agreement with
   - *(Sense 2)* Accompanying; in the company of
   - *(Sense 3)* Having; possessing
   
   **Synonyms**: alongside, on the side of, supporting (in support context)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, versus
   
   **When to Use**: When expressing alignment, agreement, or being on the same side; versatile preposition appropriate across all contexts; often used with "side"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When more specific support language is needed; "with" is ambiguous and has many meanings
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: side with, agree with, align with, go with
   - **Verb + prep**: side with, stand with, cast lot with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent with, aligned with
   - **Noun + prep**: agreement with, alignment with
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English mid/with "against, toward, with"
   
   **Etymology**: Old English wiþ → Middle English with; meaning evolved from "against" to "in company of" to "in support of"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm with you on this—let's do it.
   - Who are you with in this debate?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The judiciary sided with the appellant on procedural grounds.
   - The organization stands with communities affected by systemic inequality.

1. **Preposition**: on the side of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Supporting or favoring a particular person, group, or position; aligned with
   
   **Synonyms**: in favor of, supporting, backing, for, with
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, on the opposite side of
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing taking a position in a dispute or choice; implies choosing between alternatives; appropriate in formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When no clear alternatives or opposition exists; when simpler prepositions like "for" suffice
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: be on the side of, come down on the side of, err on the side of
   - **Verb + prep**: stand on the side of, fight on the side of
   - **Adjective + prep**: firmly on the side of
   - **Noun + prep**: those on the side of
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (preposition of position) + the side (position in conflict) + of (linking)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound prepositional phrase using "side" metaphor (choosing which side in conflict/choice); established in English by 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In this argument, I'm on the side of caution.
   - Which side are you on—his or hers?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The court came down decisively on the side of consumer protection.
   - Historical evidence suggests the administration was on the side of reform despite public opposition.

1. **Preposition**: in support of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In order to support, help, or promote; showing backing for
   
   **Synonyms**: supporting, in favor of, for, backing, endorsing
   
   **Antonyms**: against, in opposition to, opposing
   
   **When to Use**: In formal contexts when indicating purpose of support or describing supportive actions; common in descriptions of rallies, statements, actions taken to show backing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simple "for" is sufficient in casual contexts; when describing passive agreement rather than active support
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: rally in support of, speak in support of, act in support of
   - **Verb + prep**: come out in support of, organize in support of
   - **Adjective + prep**: vocal in support of
   - **Noun + prep**: actions in support of, measures in support of, statement in support of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (preposition of manner) + support (Latin supportare) + of (linking)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound phrase combining "support" with directional prepositions; established usage by 17th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're organizing an event in support of the local food bank.
   - She spoke passionately in support of the proposal.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Thousands rallied in support of comprehensive immigration reform.
   - The resolution was adopted in support of strengthening international cooperation.

1. **Preposition**: pro (type: prefix/simple preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In favor of; supporting; on the affirmative side
   - Usually used as prefix (pro-democracy) or in "pro and con" constructions
   
   **Synonyms**: for, in favor of, supporting
   
   **Antonyms**: anti, against, con, contra
   
   **When to Use**: In formal debates, discussions, or when categorizing positions; often used in academic or analytical contexts; common as prefix before causes (pro-environment)
   
   **When NOT to Use**: As standalone preposition in general writing; mainly used in specific constructions or as prefix
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pro and con, pro arguments, go pro (different meaning)
   - **Prefix usage**: pro-democracy, pro-choice, pro-business, pro-reform
   - **Noun + prep**: arguments pro and con
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin pro "for, forward, on behalf of"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin pro → directly borrowed into English as prefix and in formal constructions (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's list the pro and con arguments before deciding.
   - I'm definitely more pro than con on this issue.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee reviewed arguments both pro and contra before rendering judgment.
   - Pro-reform advocates have gained significant legislative momentum.

1. **Preposition**: on behalf of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* As the representative of; in the interest of; speaking for or acting for
   - *(Sense 2)* In support of; for the benefit of
   
   **Synonyms**: for, representing, in the name of, as representative of, in support of
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, counter to
   
   **When to Use**: In formal contexts when speaking or acting as representative of others or group; emphasizes agency and representation; common in official statements and formal proceedings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When speaking only for oneself; in casual contexts where simpler language suffices
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: speak on behalf of, act on behalf of, vote on behalf of
   - **Verb + prep**: represent on behalf of, appear on behalf of (legal)
   - **Noun + prep**: statement on behalf of, action on behalf of, representative on behalf of
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (preposition) + behalf (Middle English "advantage, side") + of (linking)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English behalve "side, benefit" → on behalf of (phrasal construction by 14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm speaking on behalf of the whole team when I say we support this.
   - Can you accept the award on behalf of the group?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The attorney appeared on behalf of the defendant corporation.
   - On behalf of the committee, I wish to express our full support for this initiative.
