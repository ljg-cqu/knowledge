1. **Functional Expression**: I'm aware of... (function: acknowledging/informing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expressing knowledge or consciousness of a situation; acknowledging information
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: I know about..., I'm conscious of..., I understand that..., I realize...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm unaware of..., I don't know about..., I'm oblivious to...
   
   **When to Use**: To acknowledge knowledge of a situation; useful in professional and personal contexts; shows attentiveness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when you actually don't know; don't use to deflect responsibility
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm fully aware of...
   - I'm well aware of...
   - I wasn't aware of...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Acknowledges information while maintaining neutral stance; can precede explanation or justification; useful for showing receptiveness
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Good, so we're on the same page."
   - "Then you understand the situation."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm aware of the problem, and I'm working on it.
   - I'm aware of what happened yesterday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I'm aware of the concerns raised by stakeholders.
   - The committee is aware of the regulatory requirements.

   **Meaning**: 
   - Expressing satisfaction or pleasure with achievements or qualities
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm pleased with..., I'm satisfied with..., I'm happy about...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm disappointed in..., I'm ashamed of..., I'm unhappy with...
   
   **When to Use**: To express positive feelings about accomplishments or qualities; common in encouragement and recognition
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when false praise would be inappropriate; be genuine

   - I'm so proud of you

   - We're proud of...

   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Thank you, that means a lot."
   - "I appreciate your support."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm so proud of what you've accomplished!
   - I'm proud of you for trying.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution is proud of its distinguished history.
   - We are proud of these significant achievements.

   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Do you know about...?, Have you heard about...?, Did you realize...?
   
   **Antonyms**: None directly applicable

   **Common Variations**:

   - Our staff is fully capable of handling complex cases.

   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: It's about..., It depends on..., It involves..., It's a question of...
