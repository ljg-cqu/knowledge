1. **Adverb**: completely (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In every way or as much as possible; totally and absolutely; to the fullest extent
   - *Position*: Can appear in mid-position (before main verb, after auxiliary) or end position
   
   **Synonyms**: totally, entirely, fully, wholly, absolutely
   
   **Antonyms**: partially, partly, incompletely, somewhat
   
   **When to Use**: Use to emphasize total depletion, exhaustion, or absence when describing running out of something. Intensifies the meaning of "out of". Common in both casual and formal contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when partial depletion is meant. Don't overuse as an intensifier; can sound exaggerated in some contexts.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - completely out of
   - completely run out
   - completely exhausted
   - completely empty
   - completely drained
   
   **Root Analysis**: complete (entire, whole) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin completus (filled up, complete) → complete + -ly → completely (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're completely out of milk, need to grab some.
   - I'm completely out of ideas for what to do tonight.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization is completely out of funding for this fiscal year.
   - Resources have been completely exhausted, requiring immediate replenishment.
