1. **Preposition**: in front of (type: phrasal/complex)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* At a position ahead of or before something in space; at the forward part relative to something
   - *(Abstract)* In the presence of someone; facing someone
   - *(Priority)* Ahead of in importance or consideration
   
   **Synonyms**: before (spatial/temporal), ahead of (spatial), facing (spatial), opposite (spatial)
   
   **Antonyms**: behind, at the back of, in back of, after
   
   **When to Use**: Describing spatial position ahead of something; indicating someone's presence; showing priority; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Temporal contexts (use "before" for time); avoid splitting the phrase (incorrect: "in the front of" when meaning spatial position ahead)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in front of the house, in front of everyone, in front of the line
   - **Verb + prep**: stand in front of, park in front of, wait in front of, appear in front of
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned in front of, located in front of
   - **Noun + prep**: position in front of, area in front of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (location) + front (forward part) + of (relationship marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English combination of prepositions to express forward spatial relationship
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Park in front of my house and I'll meet you there.
   - Don't stand in front of the TV!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The defendant appeared in front of the judge.
   - Participants were positioned in front of individual workstations.

1. **Preposition**: before (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Earlier than; at an earlier time than; preceding in time
   - *(Spatial)* In front of in position; ahead of spatially
   - *(Abstract)* In the presence of, especially in formal or official contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: prior to (temporal), ahead of (spatial/temporal), in front of (spatial), preceding
   
   **Antonyms**: after, following, behind, subsequent to
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating time sequence; showing spatial position ahead; expressing presence in formal contexts; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simultaneity is meant (use "during"); avoid when "by" indicates deadline better
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: before noon, before the meeting, before long, before the deadline
   - **Verb + prep**: appear before, come before, go before, stand before, bring before
   - **Adjective + prep**: scheduled before, mentioned before, discussed before
   - **Noun + prep**: time before, day before, moment before, period before
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by) + fore (front, earlier)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English beforan (be "by" + foran "in front") → temporal and spatial meanings
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Call me before you leave.
   - The store is right before the intersection.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All applications must be submitted before the specified deadline.
   - The case will be heard before the Supreme Court.

1. **Preposition**: ahead of (type: complex)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In front of in position; at a point further forward than
   - *(Temporal)* Before in time; earlier than scheduled
   - *(Abstract)* In advance of; more progressed or advanced than
   
   **Synonyms**: in front of (spatial), before (temporal), in advance of, preceding
   
   **Antonyms**: behind, after, following, later than, trailing
   
   **When to Use**: Describing position in front; indicating temporal or competitive advantage; showing progress or superiority; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal contexts may prefer single-word alternatives; avoid when specific measurements are needed
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: ahead of schedule, ahead of time, ahead of the curve, ahead of the competition
   - **Verb + prep**: get ahead of, stay ahead of, move ahead of, think ahead of
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned ahead of, located ahead of
   - **Noun + prep**: position ahead of, step ahead of
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (on) + head (front part) + of (relationship)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English "on hed" + "of" → ahead of (in front position)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I got here ahead of everyone else.
   - We're way ahead of schedule.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project is proceeding ahead of initial projections.
   - The institution remains ahead of its peers in research output.

1. **Preposition**: opposite (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In a position facing someone or something; on the other side from
   - *(Spatial)* Directly in front of but on the other side
   
   **Synonyms**: across from, facing, in front of, facing toward
   
   **Antonyms**: beside, next to, adjacent to, alongside
   
   **When to Use**: Describing facing positions; indicating position across from something; showing direct spatial relationship; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Abstract opposition (use "opposed to"); when "beside" or "next to" is more accurate
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: opposite the park, opposite the entrance, opposite each other
   - **Verb + prep**: sit opposite, stand opposite, face opposite, park opposite
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned opposite, located opposite
   - **Noun + prep**: house opposite, building opposite, seat opposite
   
   **Root Analysis**: op- (against) + posite (placed)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin oppositus (placed against) → Old French → English (spatial and abstract meanings, 14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll meet you at the café opposite the library.
   - Sit opposite me so we can talk.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The property is located opposite the municipal building.
   - Participants were seated opposite one another for the exercise.

1. **Preposition**: facing (type: simple/participial)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Positioned to look toward; with the front directed at; oriented toward
   - Looking out on or overlooking
   
   **Synonyms**: opposite, looking toward, overlooking, fronting, directed at
   
   **Antonyms**: backing onto, facing away from, turned from
   
   **When to Use**: Describing orientation or direction something faces; indicating what something looks out on; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Abstract confrontation (use "confronting" or "facing up to"); when "opposite" is clearer
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: facing the street, facing south, facing the audience, facing the ocean
   - **Verb + prep**: position facing, stand facing, sit facing
   - **Adjective + prep**: room facing, window facing, seat facing
   - **Noun + prep**: house facing, apartment facing, position facing
   
   **Root Analysis**: face (front part, to be directed toward) + -ing (participial form used as preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: From noun "face" (Old French face → Latin facies) → verb "to face" → prepositional use
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I want a room facing the beach.
   - Turn your chair facing the board.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The property features south-facing windows for optimal natural light.
   - Participants should be seated facing the presentation screen.

1. **Preposition**: at the front of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* At the forward part or section of something; in the front area
   - Often indicates position within or attached to the front of an enclosed space
   
   **Synonyms**: in the front of, at the fore of, in the front part of
   
   **Antonyms**: at the back of, at the rear of, in the back of
   
   **When to Use**: Describing position in the front section of something; indicating location in front area; appropriate across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When position is ahead of rather than within (use "in front of"); avoid redundancy with other spatial markers
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: at the front of the room, at the front of the line, at the front of the bus
   - **Verb + prep**: stand at the front of, sit at the front of, wait at the front of
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned at the front of, located at the front of
   - **Noun + prep**: seat at the front of, position at the front of
   
   **Root Analysis**: at (location) + the + front (forward part) + of (relationship)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase combining position marker with spatial noun
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can I sit at the front of the class?
   - Meet me at the front of the store.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The presenter stood at the front of the auditorium.
   - Control panels are located at the front of the equipment.

1. **Preposition**: up front (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* At or toward the front; in the front position
   - *(Temporal/Financial)* In advance; at the beginning; as advance payment
   - *(Manner)* Honestly and openly; directly
   
   **Synonyms**: at the front, in advance, beforehand, openly, honestly
   
   **Antonyms**: at the back, later, afterward, evasively, indirectly
   
   **When to Use**: Describing front position; indicating advance payment or timing; showing honesty; informal to neutral register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal contexts (use "in advance" or "at the front"); note: "upfront" (one word) is adjective/adverb
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: up front in the car, up front about costs, pay up front
   - **Verb + prep**: sit up front, be up front, pay up front, tell up front
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned up front
   - **Noun + prep**: seats up front, payment up front
   
   **Root Analysis**: up (forward, to the front) + front (forward position)
   
   **Etymology**: American English (20th century) from spatial phrase → honest/advance meanings
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll sit up front with the driver.
   - Let me be up front—this will cost extra.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Payment is required up front prior to service delivery.
   - Participants were informed up front about all procedures.

1. **Preposition**: in advance of (type: complex)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Before something happens; prior to; preceding with preparation
   - *(Spatial, less common)* Ahead of in position
   
   **Synonyms**: before, prior to, ahead of, in preparation for, preceding
   
   **Antonyms**: after, following, subsequent to, in the wake of
   
   **When to Use**: Describing preparation or anticipatory actions; indicating temporal priority; formal register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Simple temporal sequences where "before" suffices; casual contexts (use "before" or "ahead of time")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in advance of the meeting, in advance of the deadline, in advance of the event
   - **Verb + prep**: prepare in advance of, notify in advance of, complete in advance of
   - **Adjective + prep**: done in advance of, scheduled in advance of
   - **Noun + prep**: preparation in advance of, notification in advance of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + advance (forward movement) + of (relationship)
   
   **Etymology**: From "advance" (move forward) + "of" → temporal/positional precedence (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Buy your tickets in advance of the show.
   - We got there in advance of everyone else.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Policy adjustments were implemented in advance of regulatory changes.
   - Stakeholders were consulted in advance of the decision-making process.

1. **Preposition**: at the forefront of (type: phrasal/complex)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In the leading or most important position; at the cutting edge or vanguard
   - At the most advanced or prominent position in a field or movement
   
   **Synonyms**: leading, at the vanguard of, pioneering, in the lead in, at the cutting edge of
   
   **Antonyms**: at the rear of, lagging behind in, following in, behind in, trailing in
   
   **When to Use**: Describing leadership positions; emphasizing innovation or priority; showing prominence; formal to neutral register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Literal spatial front (use "at the front of"); casual contexts (use simpler expressions)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: at the forefront of research, at the forefront of innovation, at the forefront of change
   - **Verb + prep**: remain at the forefront of, position at the forefront of, be at the forefront of
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned at the forefront of
   - **Noun + prep**: position at the forefront of, role at the forefront of
   
   **Root Analysis**: at (location) + the + fore (before/in front) + front (forward position) + of
   
   **Etymology**: Military terminology → metaphorical use for leadership (18th-19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's at the forefront of the environmental movement.
   - Our company is at the forefront of tech innovation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution remains at the forefront of cancer research.
   - These developments place the field at the forefront of scientific inquiry.

1. **Preposition**: in the face of (type: complex/idiomatic)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* When confronted with; despite the presence of; in the presence of challenges or adversity
   - Indicating confrontation with difficulty while maintaining position or action
   
   **Synonyms**: despite, confronted with, when faced with, in spite of, notwithstanding
   
   **Antonyms**: with the support of, aided by, helped by, thanks to, because of (positive)
   
   **When to Use**: Showing resilience despite challenges; indicating confrontation with difficulty; formal to neutral register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Literal face-to-face meaning (use "face to face with"); trivial obstacles (overly dramatic)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in the face of adversity, in the face of danger, in the face of criticism
   - **Verb + prep**: persevere in the face of, continue in the face of, maintain in the face of
   - **Adjective + prep**: resilient in the face of, steadfast in the face of
   - **Noun + prep**: courage in the face of, determination in the face of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + the + face (front/confrontation) + of
   
   **Etymology**: Metaphorical extension from physical "face" to confrontation (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They stayed calm in the face of total chaos.
   - We kept going in the face of all that criticism.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization maintained operations in the face of significant financial challenges.
   - In the face of mounting evidence, the hypothesis was revised.
