1. **Original Expression**: The cost is around $500  
   **Context**: Giving price estimates in various business contexts
   
   **Core Intent**: Communicate approximate cost without committing to exact figure
   
   **Casual**: It's like 500 bucks, maybe a bit more  
   *Characteristics*: Informal "like," colloquial "bucks," vague qualifier "maybe a bit more"  
   *Use when*: Friends, casual shopping, informal negotiations
   
   **Semi-Formal**: The cost is around $500  
   *Characteristics*: Standard "around," professional currency notation, clear structure  
   *Use when*: Standard business communications, client emails, general workplace
   
   **Formal**: The estimated cost is approximately $500  
   *Characteristics*: Professional "estimated," formal "approximately," precise  
   *Use when*: Business proposals, formal quotes, executive communications
   
   **Humorous**: We're talking ballpark five hundred bucks—give or take a pizza  
   *Characteristics*: Playful "ballpark," casual "bucks," humorous comparison  
   *Use when*: Friendly business relationships, creative industries, rapport-building  
   *Avoid when*: Serious financial discussions, formal proposals, first-time clients
   
   **Lively**: We're looking at around 500 dollars—exciting, right?!  
   *Characteristics*: Engaging "we're looking at," exclamation, enthusiasm marker  
   *Use when*: Sales presentations, motivational contexts, positive announcements
   
   **Empathetic**: I understand budget is a concern—it's around $500, and we can discuss payment options  
   *Characteristics*: Acknowledgment of concern, soft delivery, solution-oriented  
   *Use when*: Price-sensitive clients, discussing affordability, customer service
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Higher status clients deserve formal; equals allow semi-formal
   - *Social Distance*: Unknown clients require formal; established relationships allow casual
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Large purchases need formal; small items allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Some cultures expect more formal pricing discussions
   - *Medium*: Written proposals require formal; in-person allows more casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "around" in formal contracts (too vague—use exact figures or "estimated")
   - Saying "like" in business contexts (unprofessional—use "approximately")
   - Being too vague when precision matters (specify range or conditions)
   
   **Cultural Notes**: American business culture accepts approximations more readily than some Asian or European cultures that expect precise figures
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Formal RFP response → **Appropriate Variant**: "The estimated cost is approximately $500" (Formal)
   - **Situation 2**: Coffee shop chat with friend about purchase → **Appropriate Variant**: "It's like 500 bucks" (Casual)
   - **Situation 3**: Email to existing client about project → **Appropriate Variant**: "The cost is around $500" (Semi-Formal)
