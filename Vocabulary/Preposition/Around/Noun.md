1. **Noun**: turnaround (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A complete change from a bad situation to a good one; a reversal
   - The time taken to complete a process or task
   - A space where vehicles can turn around
   
   **Synonyms**: reversal, recovery, transformation, comeback; (time) processing time, completion time; (space) turning area
   
   **Antonyms**: decline, deterioration, downturn
   
   **When to Use**: Business, organizational contexts for improvements; logistics for timing; transportation for physical spaces
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "turn around" (verb phrase); note single word vs. two words
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - quick turnaround
   - turnaround time
   - impressive turnaround
   
   **Root Analysis**: turn + around → complete reversal or completion
   
   **Etymology**: 1930s business terminology from phrasal verb "turn around" → compounded as noun
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The team's turnaround this season has been amazing!
   - How long is the turnaround on those shirts? I need them by Friday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The company's financial turnaround resulted from strategic restructuring initiatives.
   - The standard turnaround time for processing applications is five business days.

1. **Noun**: runaround (C, informal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Evasive or deceptive treatment; being sent from one person or place to another without getting help
   
   **Synonyms**: evasion, avoidance, buck-passing, deflection
   
   **Antonyms**: direct answer, straightforward help, immediate assistance
   
   **When to Use**: Informal complaints about unhelpful bureaucracy or customer service
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too informal for formal writing; primarily used in phrase "give someone the runaround"
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - give someone the runaround
   - get the runaround
   - stop giving me the runaround
   
   **Root Analysis**: run + around → being sent in circles without resolution
   
   **Etymology**: Early 20th century American slang from "run around" (evade)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I called customer service three times and just got the runaround.
   - Stop giving me the runaround and tell me what's really going on!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *Typically informal; avoid in formal contexts* Customers complained about receiving the runaround when attempting to resolve billing issues.

1. **Noun**: surroundings (plural, C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The things and conditions around a person or place; the environment or area around something
   
   **Synonyms**: environment, setting, environs, area, vicinity, atmosphere
   
   **Antonyms**: (no direct antonym)
   
   **When to Use**: Describing physical environment or context; appropriate in formal and informal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Usually used in plural form; singular "surrounding" is less common
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - natural surroundings
   - unfamiliar surroundings
   - pleasant surroundings
   
   **Root Analysis**: sur- (over, beyond) + round (circular) + -ings (plural noun) → things around
   
   **Etymology**: From verb "surround" (14th c., French "suronder") → noun form 18th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Take a minute to get used to your new surroundings.
   - The cabin's surroundings were absolutely beautiful—mountains everywhere.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study examined how individuals adapt to novel surroundings.
   - Historical buildings should be preserved in harmony with their surroundings.

1. **Noun**: vicinity (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The area near or around a particular place; proximity
   
   **Synonyms**: neighborhood, area, locality, proximity, region, surroundings
   
   **Antonyms**: distance, remote area
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts to indicate nearness or surrounding area
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation (use "area" or "around here" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in the vicinity of
   - immediate vicinity
   - general vicinity
   
   **Root Analysis**: vicin- (neighbor, near) + -ity (state/quality) → nearness
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "vicinitas" (neighborhood) → French → English 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is there a gas station in the vicinity? We're running low.
   - Several restaurants in the vicinity offer good lunch options.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The suspect was last seen in the vicinity of Fifth Avenue and Main Street.
   - All residents in the vicinity were evacuated as a precautionary measure.

1. **Noun**: perimeter (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The continuous line forming the boundary of a closed geometric figure
   - The outer boundary or edge of an area
   
   **Synonyms**: boundary, border, edge, circumference, periphery, outline
   
   **Antonyms**: center, interior, middle, core
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical contexts, security/military terminology, or describing outer boundaries; formal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too technical for casual conversation about everyday boundaries
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - secure the perimeter
   - perimeter fence
   - around the perimeter
   
   **Root Analysis**: peri- (around) + -meter (measure) → measurement around
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "perimetros" via Greek "perimetron" (measure around) → English 15th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They put a fence around the perimeter of the property.
   - We jogged around the perimeter of the park.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Security personnel patrol the perimeter of the facility continuously.
   - Calculate the perimeter by summing the lengths of all sides.

1. **Noun**: circumference (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The distance around a circle or other curved geometric figure
   - The boundary line of a circular area
   
   **Synonyms**: perimeter (of circle), girth, periphery
   
   **Antonyms**: diameter, radius, center
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, scientific, or precise measurement contexts; formal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use for non-circular shapes (use "perimeter"); too technical for casual contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - measure the circumference
   - circumference of a circle
   - around the circumference
   
   **Root Analysis**: circum- (around) + fer (carry) + -ence (state) → carrying around
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "circumferentia" (carrying around) → English 14th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The tree's circumference is about six feet—you can't wrap your arms around it.
   - We walked around the entire circumference of the lake.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The circumference is calculated using the formula C = 2πr.
   - The Earth's circumference at the equator measures approximately 40,075 kilometers.

1. **Noun**: rotation (C/U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action of rotating around an axis or center
   - A complete turn around a fixed point
   - *(Sense 2)* A system of taking turns or operating in sequence
   
   **Synonyms**: revolution, spin, turn, turning; (sequence) cycle, shift, alternation
   
   **Antonyms**: stillness, stationary position; (sequence) permanence, fixed assignment
   
   **When to Use**: Physics, astronomy, mechanics, or organizational scheduling; formal and technical
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing irregular movement (use "spinning" or "moving around" casually)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - rotation of the Earth
   - job rotation
   - in rotation
   
   **Root Analysis**: rotat- (turn, wheel) + -ion (action/process) → turning action
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "rotare" (turn like a wheel) → rotation (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The Earth completes one rotation every 24 hours.
   - We take turns in rotation—you're up next.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The rotation of crops prevents soil depletion and maintains agricultural productivity.
   - Staff assignments follow a systematic rotation schedule to ensure equitable distribution.

1. **Noun**: orbit (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon
   - *(Sense 2)* A sphere of activity or influence
   
   **Synonyms**: path, trajectory, revolution, circuit; (influence) sphere, realm, domain
   
   **Antonyms**: straight path, direct line; (influence) exclusion
   
   **When to Use**: Astronomy, space science, or metaphorically for spheres of influence; formal to informal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "rotation" (spinning on axis vs. revolving around object)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in orbit around
   - orbital path
   - enter orbit
   
   **Root Analysis**: orbit- (circle, ring) → circular path
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "orbita" (wheel track, course) → English 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The satellite is in orbit around Earth.
   - She's moved into his orbit now—they work together.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The International Space Station maintains a low Earth orbit at approximately 400 kilometers altitude.
   - The research examines policy development within the orbit of international relations.

1. **Noun**: circle (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from the center
   - A group of people with shared interests, professions, or acquaintances
   - *(Sense 3)* A curved or circular movement or path
   
   **Synonyms**: ring, round; (group) clique, set, group; (movement) loop, circuit
   
   **Antonyms**: straight line, square; (group) outsider status; (movement) straight path
   
   **When to Use**: Geometry, social groups, or describing circular paths; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse mathematical circle with casual "around" when precision matters
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - form a circle around
   - social circle
   - go around in circles
   
   **Root Analysis**: circ- (ring, around) + -le (diminutive) → small ring
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "circulus" (small ring) → Old English "circul" → modern "circle"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone sit in a circle around the campfire.
   - Most of my social circle works in tech.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Calculate the area of a circle using the formula A = πr².
   - The policy debate occurs primarily within academic circles.

1. **Noun**: loop (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A shape produced by a curve that bends around and crosses itself
   - A structure, path, or route that comes back to its starting point
   - *(Computing)* A sequence of instructions that repeats
   
   **Synonyms**: circle, ring, coil, curve; (route) circuit, round trip
   
   **Antonyms**: straight line, direct path
   
   **When to Use**: Describing circular paths, repeated processes, or physical curved shapes; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Be specific about which type of loop when context is technical
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - loop around
   - in the loop (idiom: included in information sharing)
   - feedback loop
   
   **Root Analysis**: Origin uncertain, possibly Germanic or Celtic root for "bend"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English "loupe" (noose) → extended to any curved closed path
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The trail makes a loop around the lake.
   - Keep me in the loop about the wedding plans!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The software executes a loop to process each data element sequentially.
   - The feedback loop ensures continuous quality improvement.
