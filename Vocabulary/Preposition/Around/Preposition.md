1. **Preposition**: around (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* on all sides of something; encircling or surrounding a place or object
   - *(Spatial)* in various parts of a place; here and there within an area
   - *(Temporal)* approximately at a particular time
   - *(Abstract)* concerning or relating to a particular subject
   
   **Synonyms**: about (in context of approximation), round (British English, spatial), surrounding (spatial context), concerning (abstract)
   
   **Antonyms**: away from, far from, distant from
   
   **When to Use**: Use for describing circular movement, approximate locations, estimated times, or when discussing topics/subjects; appropriate in all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when precise measurements are needed (use "at exactly" instead of "around"); don't confuse with "round" in American English formal writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: around the corner, around the world, around the clock, around noon
   - **Verb + prep**: walk around, look around, turn around, gather around, revolve around
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not commonly used)
   - **Noun + prep**: area around, space around, people around
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (on) + round (circular) → moving in a circular path or position
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → from "a-" (on) + "round" (circular), evolved from Old English "onbūtan"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll be there around 3 o'clock, maybe a bit later.
   - There's a great coffee shop just around the corner from here.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The conference will commence around midday, with registration opening at 11:30.
   - The research focuses on issues around sustainable development in urban areas.

1. **Preposition**: around (with motion verbs) (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* moving in a circular direction or path
   - *(Spatial)* from one place to another within an area without specific direction
   
   **Synonyms**: round (British), about (archaic), through (context-dependent)
   
   **Antonyms**: toward, straight to, directly at
   
   **When to Use**: With verbs of motion to indicate circular movement or aimless wandering; common in both formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when indicating direct, purposeful movement to a destination
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: around the building, around the park, around town
   - **Verb + prep**: spin around, move around, drive around, wander around, show around
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: trip around, tour around, journey around
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (on) + round (circular)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → developed from combining "a-" with "round" to indicate circular motion
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We drove around the city for hours looking for parking.
   - Stop spinning around—you're making me dizzy!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The tour guide led visitors around the museum, highlighting key exhibits.
   - The delegation traveled around the region to assess development needs.

1. **Preposition**: around (approximation) (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* approximately; used with times, dates, or numbers
   - *(Abstract)* used to indicate an estimated quantity or measurement
   
   **Synonyms**: about, approximately, roughly, circa (formal, with dates)
   
   **Antonyms**: exactly, precisely, at exactly
   
   **When to Use**: When giving estimates or approximations; acceptable in all registers though "approximately" is more formal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in contexts requiring precision (scientific measurements, legal documents, schedules); don't use in formal academic writing where "approximately" is preferred
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: around midnight, around $100, around 50 people, around the same time
   - **Verb + prep**: cost around, take around, last around
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: price around, time around, number around
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- + round → suggesting "round numbers" or estimates
   
   **Etymology**: Extended use from spatial "around" to temporal/quantitative approximation in Modern English
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The movie starts around 7, so let's meet at 6:30.
   - It'll cost you around fifty bucks, give or take.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project will require around 200 hours of labor to complete.
   - Data collection occurred around the period of March to May 2023.

1. **Preposition**: around (concerning) (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* concerning or relating to a particular subject or issue
   - *(Abstract)* centered on or focused on a particular theme
   
   **Synonyms**: about, concerning, regarding, on the subject of
   
   **Antonyms**: excluding, apart from, unrelated to
   
   **When to Use**: In discussions, debates, or written work to indicate topic or focus; appropriate in semi-formal to formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common in very formal academic writing where "concerning" or "regarding" are preferred
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: debate around, discussion around, issues around, questions around
   - **Verb + prep**: center around, revolve around, focus around, build around
   - **Adjective + prep**: concerns around (noun use)
   - **Noun + prep**: conversation around, debate around, controversy around
   
   **Root Analysis**: Extended metaphorical use from spatial "around" to abstract topics
   
   **Etymology**: Metaphorical extension from physical surrounding to conceptual focus, 20th century usage
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's been a lot of talk around the new policy at work.
   - The whole conversation revolved around what we should do next summer.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research addresses concerns around data privacy in digital healthcare.
   - The symposium centered around contemporary challenges in environmental policy.

1. **Preposition**: around (availability/presence) (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* present in a place; available or existing
   - *(Temporal)* existing or popular during a particular time
   
   **Synonyms**: about (British informal), available, present, here
   
   **Antonyms**: absent, gone, unavailable
   
   **When to Use**: Informal to semi-formal contexts to indicate presence or availability
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too informal for academic writing; avoid in formal reports
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: (not usually in phrases)
   - **Verb + prep**: be around, stick around, hang around, stay around
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: people around, anyone around, nobody around
   
   **Root Analysis**: Extended from spatial presence to general availability
   
   **Etymology**: 20th century colloquial extension from spatial meaning
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is Jake around? I need to ask him something.
   - I'll be around all weekend if you need help moving.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The technology has been around for several decades but only recently gained widespread adoption.
   - Dr. Martinez will be around during office hours to answer questions.
