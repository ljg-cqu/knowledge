1. **Functional Expression**: What's next? / What comes next? (function: inquiring about sequence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask about the following action, event, or step in a sequence
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: What happens next?, What should we do next?, What follows?, What's the next step?
   
   **Antonyms**: What came before?, What did we just do?, What was previous?
   
   **When to Use**: Use when seeking information about subsequent steps or events; common in planning, instructions, and narratives; suitable for most contexts except very formal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound impatient in some contexts; soften with "So" or "Then" at beginning
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - So what's next?
   - What happens next?
   - What do we do next?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Direct question; neutral politeness; can show engagement or impatience depending on tone
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Next, we'll..." (providing information)
   - "I'm not sure yet" (uncertainty)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We finished step one. What's next?
   - So what comes next in this recipe?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The preliminary phase is complete. What comes next?
   - Having addressed initial concerns, what's next on the agenda?

1. **Functional Expression**: Next! (function: calling/summoning)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to call the next person in a queue or sequence to come forward
   - *Formality*: neutral to informal
   - *Directness*: very direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Who's next?, Next person please, Next in line, You're next
   
   **Antonyms**: Wait, Hold on, Not yet
   
   **When to Use**: Use in service contexts, medical offices, customer service, or any queuing system; standard professional shorthand
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound abrupt if not in established service context; add "please" for politeness in less formal settings
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Next, please!
   - Next person!
   - You're next!
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Institutional language; accepted brevity in service contexts; tone matters significantly
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - (Person steps forward without verbal response)
   - "That's me!" (acknowledgment)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (at a deli counter) Next!
   - Next, please! (more polite)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - (in medical office) Next patient, please.
   - The committee will see the next candidate now.

1. **Functional Expression**: Let's move on to the next [topic/item/question] (function: transitioning)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to suggest or direct transition from current topic to the following one
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (inclusive "let's")
   
   **Synonyms**: Let's proceed to the next..., Shall we move to the next..., Let's continue with the next..., On to the next...
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's stay on this topic, Let's go back, Let's revisit this
   
   **When to Use**: Use in meetings, presentations, classes, or discussions to signal topic transition; inclusive language reduces directness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be dismissive if current topic needs more attention; ensure closure before transitioning
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let's proceed to the next
   - Moving on to the next
   - On to the next item
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Inclusive imperative ("let's") softens directive; signals shared activity; collaborative tone
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - (Group proceeds without comment)
   - "Wait, I have a question about this first" (requesting pause)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Okay, let's move on to the next question!
   - Let's go to the next item on the list.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Let's move on to the next agenda item.
   - We shall proceed to the next phase of the analysis.

1. **Functional Expression**: Who's next? (function: inquiring about turn/order)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask which person has the next turn or position in a sequence
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Whose turn is it?, Who goes next?, Who's up?, Who's after this?
   
   **Antonyms**: Who was last?, Who just went?, Who went before?
   
   **When to Use**: Use in informal queues, games, turn-taking situations, or informal scheduling
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too informal for formal proceedings; use "Who is next in line?" or "The next speaker is..." instead
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Who's up next?
   - Who goes next?
   - Whose turn is next?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Assumes shared understanding of turn-taking system; informal register; democratic tone
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "I am" / "Me" (claiming turn)
   - "I think it's Sarah" (identifying person)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Who's next for karaoke?
   - Okay, who's next to present?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Who is next on the speaker list?
   - The chair inquires: who is next to provide testimony?

1. **Functional Expression**: What should I do next? (function: requesting instruction/guidance)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask for direction about the following action or step
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct question
   
   **Synonyms**: What's the next step?, What do I do after this?, What comes next?, How should I proceed?
   
   **Antonyms**: What did I just do?, What should I have done?, What was I supposed to do?
   
   **When to Use**: Use when seeking instruction, guidance, or clarification about procedure; shows willingness to follow directions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can show lack of initiative if overused; try to anticipate next steps when possible
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - What do I do next?
   - What's my next step?
   - How do I proceed next?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Shows deference to authority/expertise; acknowledges need for guidance; positive politeness strategy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Next, you should..." (providing instruction)
   - "Try [action] next" (suggesting)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I finished this part. What should I do next?
   - What do I do next with this recipe?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Having completed the initial assessment, what should I do next?
   - What should be my next course of action?

1. **Functional Expression**: You're up next / You're next (function: notifying turn)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to inform someone that their turn is coming or has arrived
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: It's your turn next, You're after this, Your turn is coming, You follow this person
   
   **Antonyms**: You already went, You're not next, Someone else is before you
   
   **When to Use**: Use to notify someone of upcoming turn; can be advance notice or immediate call; casual to semi-formal settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too casual for very formal proceedings; use full sentences in formal contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - You're up
   - You're on next
   - Your turn is next
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Alert function; can be warning or summons; allows preparation time; casual register
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Okay, thanks!" (acknowledgment)
   - "Already? I'm not ready!" (expressing unpreparedness)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Get ready—you're up next!
   - Sarah, you're next!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Dr. Smith, you are next to present.
   - The next presenter is scheduled to begin momentarily.

1. **Functional Expression**: We'll discuss this next time (function: postponing/deferring)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to defer discussion or action to a future occasion
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (polite deferral)
   
   **Synonyms**: Let's talk about this later, We'll address this at our next meeting, Let's revisit this, We'll come back to this
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's discuss this now, We need to address this immediately, Let's finish this today
   
   **When to Use**: Use to politely defer topics due to time constraints, lack of information, or prioritization; maintains commitment to address later
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be perceived as avoidance tactic if overused; ensure genuine intention to return to topic
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let's save this for next time
   - We'll pick this up next time
   - Let's table this until next time
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Polite postponement strategy; acknowledges importance while deferring; maintains social harmony; requires follow-through
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Okay, sounds good" (acceptance)
   - "Can we at least decide on..." (requesting partial resolution)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're running out of time—let's discuss this next time.
   - This is complicated. Can we tackle it next time?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Due to time constraints, we shall defer this matter to the next session.
   - This agenda item will be carried forward to the next meeting.

1. **Functional Expression**: See you next [time period] (function: farewell with future reference)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to say goodbye with indication of next expected meeting time
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct but friendly
   
   **Synonyms**: Until next [time], See you at the next [event], Talk to you next [time], Catch you next [time]
   
   **Antonyms**: This is goodbye (permanent), We won't meet again, This is our last meeting
   
   **When to Use**: Use as friendly farewell when future meeting is expected; establishes continuity and ongoing relationship
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use if future meeting is uncertain; can create false expectations
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - See you next week/month/year
   - Until next time
   - Catch you next time
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Relationship maintenance strategy; projects continuity; friendly register; assumes ongoing connection
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "See you then!" (reciprocal)
   - "Looking forward to it!" (enthusiastic agreement)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Great class! See you next week!
   - Okay, see you next time!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Thank you for your time. I look forward to our next meeting.
   - We shall reconvene at the next scheduled session.

1. **Functional Expression**: What do you want to do next? (function: soliciting preference/decision)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask someone about their preference for the following activity or step
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct question
   
   **Synonyms**: What would you like to do next?, What's next on your agenda?, What should we do next?, Where to next?
   
   **Antonyms**: This is what we're doing next, I've decided what's next, We have no choice about what's next
   
   **When to Use**: Use to give someone agency in decision-making; collaborative and respectful; social or planning contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you should make the decision yourself; can seem indecisive if overused
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - What would you like to do next?
   - Where do you want to go next?
   - What's your next move?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy (giving options); collaborative decision-making; shows respect for preferences
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "How about we..." (suggesting)
   - "I'm not sure, what do you think?" (deferring back)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We finished the movie. What do you want to do next?
   - What do you want to tackle next on the list?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Having completed this phase, what direction would you like to pursue next?
   - What would you like to address next in our discussion?

1. **Functional Expression**: I have no idea what comes next (function: expressing uncertainty)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to express lack of knowledge or clarity about subsequent events or steps
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (honest admission)
   
   **Synonyms**: I don't know what happens next, I'm not sure what's next, I'm uncertain about the next step, I'm lost about what follows
   
   **Antonyms**: I know exactly what's next, It's clear what comes next, I have a plan for what's next
   
   **When to Use**: Use to honestly admit uncertainty; can request help or express anxiety; shows vulnerability
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can undermine confidence if you're expected to know; consider whether admission is appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I don't know what comes next
   - I'm not sure what's next
   - I'm unclear about what happens next
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Vulnerability display; can be request for help; honesty strategy; may invite support or direction
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Let me help you figure it out" (offering assistance)
   - "Neither do I" (shared uncertainty)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This is so confusing—I have no idea what comes next!
   - I have no clue what we do next.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Given the current circumstances, I am uncertain about what comes next.
   - The situation is ambiguous, and I am unclear about the next appropriate step.
