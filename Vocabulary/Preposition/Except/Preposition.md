1. **Preposition**: except (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Exclusion)* Not including; other than; apart from; with the exception of
   - *(Conditional)* Used before stating the only thing that prevents or prevents something from being completely true
   
   **Synonyms**: apart from, excluding, but, other than, save (formal)
   
   **Antonyms**: including, plus, with
   
   **When to Use**: Use "except" when excluding specific items or people from a general statement; common in formal and informal contexts; indicates exception to a rule or pattern
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "accept" (to receive); avoid in contexts requiring "besides" or "in addition to"; not used for adding items
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except for, except when, except in cases, except under circumstances
   - **Verb + prep**: do nothing except, say nothing except, accept all except
   - **Adjective + prep**: everyone except, everything except, all except
   - **Noun + prep**: days except, times except, people except
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + cept (taken, from Latin capere "to take")
   
   **Etymology**: Latin exceptus (past participle of excipere "to take out, exclude") → Old French excepter → Middle English excepten → modern "except" (exclusion meaning preserved)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone came to the party except John.
   - I like all vegetables except broccoli.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All participants except those previously exempted must complete the survey.
   - The policy applies to all employees except senior management personnel.

1. **Preposition**: except for (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Exclusion with emphasis)* Apart from; not including; with the single exception of
   - *(Contrast)* Used to introduce the only thing that prevents a situation from being completely true or perfect
   
   **Synonyms**: apart from, aside from, but for, excluding, save for
   
   **Antonyms**: including, along with, together with
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing an exception or contrast; often introduces the only flaw or difference; can appear at sentence beginning for emphasis
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid redundancy when simple "except" suffices; not for additions or inclusions
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except for one thing, except for the fact that, except for this issue
   - **Verb + prep**: would be perfect except for, could work except for
   - **Sentence-initial**: Except for [exception], [main statement]
   - **Noun + prep**: everything except for, everyone except for
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + for (indicating the specific exception)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound prepositional phrase emphasizing the exception (16th century usage)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The movie was great, except for the ending.
   - Except for Sarah, nobody showed up.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The results were consistent except for minor variations in data set B.
   - Except for documented medical circumstances, attendance is mandatory.

1. **Preposition**: except in (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Conditional exclusion)* Not applying or not true in specific circumstances, situations, or conditions
   
   **Synonyms**: unless in, only not in, apart from in, save in
   
   **Antonyms**: including in, especially in, particularly in
   
   **When to Use**: Specifies exceptional circumstances or contexts where a rule doesn't apply; common in legal, technical, and formal writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not for general exclusions without specific context or condition
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except in cases where, except in situations, except in circumstances, except in emergencies
   - **Legal/formal contexts**: except in accordance with, except in compliance with
   - **Conditional statements**: except in the event that, except in instances of
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + in (within/during specific context)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase specifying conditional exceptions (legal usage from 17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can't park here except in emergencies.
   - We're open daily except in really bad weather.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Personal data shall not be disclosed except in circumstances prescribed by law.
   - The protocol applies universally except in specifically designated research contexts.

1. **Preposition**: except when (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal/conditional exclusion)* At all times other than; only not during the times that; with the exception of occasions when
   
   **Synonyms**: unless, only when not, apart from when, save when
   
   **Antonyms**: especially when, particularly when, including when
   
   **When to Use**: Introduces temporal or conditional exceptions; indicates times when a general rule doesn't apply; bridges into subordinate clauses
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not purely prepositional (often functions as conjunction); avoid when simple "except" with noun suffices
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except when necessary, except when required, except when specified
   - **Temporal contexts**: except when [time condition], except when [situation occurs]
   - **Rule exceptions**: always except when, never except when, only except when
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + when (time/condition marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound form extending "except" into temporal/conditional clauses (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I never eat dessert except when it's chocolate cake.
   - She's usually quiet except when she's around her friends.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Confidentiality must be maintained except when disclosure is legally mandated.
   - Standard procedures apply except when emergency protocols supersede them.

1. **Preposition**: except under (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Conditional/circumstantial exclusion)* Only not when subjected to specific conditions, circumstances, or authority
   
   **Synonyms**: unless under, only not under, save under, apart from under
   
   **Antonyms**: including under, even under, especially under
   
   **When to Use**: Specifies conditions or circumstances creating exceptions; common in legal, regulatory, and technical documents; emphasizes contextual conditions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not for general exclusions; requires specific conditional or circumstantial context
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except under circumstances, except under conditions, except under authorization, except under duress
   - **Legal/regulatory**: except under provisions of, except under terms of, except under jurisdiction
   - **Conditional**: except under no circumstances, except under specific conditions
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + under (subject to conditions)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase for conditional exceptions (legal usage from 18th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I won't tell anyone except under extreme circumstances.
   - You can't enter except under special permission.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Disclosure is prohibited except under court order or statutory requirement.
   - Access is restricted except under authorized supervision and documented protocols.

1. **Preposition**: except as (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Qualification/limitation)* Other than in the manner, capacity, or way specified; with the exception of how something is specified
   
   **Synonyms**: unless as, only not as, save as, apart from being
   
   **Antonyms**: including as, also as, even as
   
   **When to Use**: Introduces qualifications or limitations on roles, capacities, or manners; very common in legal and formal documents; specifies how something applies differently
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not for simple exclusions of objects or people; requires specification of manner or capacity
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except as provided, except as otherwise stated, except as noted, except as specified
   - **Legal/formal**: except as defined, except as permitted, except as authorized, except as required
   - **Qualification**: except as necessary, except as applicable, except as appropriate
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + as (in the manner/capacity of)
   
   **Etymology**: Legal and formal usage for qualified exceptions (19th century standardization)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can use my car except as a taxi service!
   - Share anything except as personal secrets.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Terms remain valid except as modified by subsequent written amendments.
   - Data may not be reproduced except as expressly authorized in Section 4.2.

1. **Preposition**: except to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Purpose/limitation)* Other than for the purpose of; with no other aim than; only for the reason of
   
   **Synonyms**: only to, solely to, purely to, but to
   
   **Antonyms**: including to, also to, additionally to
   
   **When to Use**: Limits purpose or intention to single specified goal; emphasizes exclusive purpose; common in formal restrictions and prohibitions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not for general exclusions; requires purpose or intention context
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except to say, except to add, except to note, except to clarify
   - **Purpose limitation**: except to [verb phrase], used except to [purpose]
   - **Restriction**: disclosed except to, shared except to, available except to
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + to (purpose/direction marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase limiting purpose or direction (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I won't call him except to apologize.
   - Don't open it except to read the instructions.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Information shall not be utilized except to fulfill contractually specified obligations.
   - Access is denied except to authorized personnel for maintenance purposes.

1. **Preposition**: except from (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Origin exclusion)* Excluding things originating from or coming from a specific source
   
   **Synonyms**: excluding from, apart from, other than from, save from
   
   **Antonyms**: including from, especially from, also from
   
   **When to Use**: Excludes items or information from specific sources; emphasizes origin or source of exclusion; formal and technical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common than other "except" forms; often "except" alone or "except for" suffices
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except from sources, except from locations, except from departments
   - **Source exclusion**: data except from, information except from, materials except from
   - **Origin-based**: accept all except from, receive all except from
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + from (origin/source marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound prepositional form for source-based exclusions
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll eat food from anywhere except from that restaurant.
   - Accept gifts from everyone except from him.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All submissions are considered except from previously disqualified entities.
   - Data from all sources will be analyzed except from compromised collection sites.

1. **Preposition**: except on (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal/surface exclusion)* Not applying on specific days, dates, or occasions; not valid on particular surfaces or platforms
   
   **Synonyms**: other than on, excluding on, apart from on, save on
   
   **Antonyms**: including on, especially on, also on
   
   **When to Use**: Specifies temporal exceptions (days/dates) or surface/platform exceptions; common in schedules, rules, and technical specifications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Context must clearly support temporal or surface interpretation
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except on weekends, except on holidays, except on Sundays, except on specific dates
   - **Schedule contexts**: open except on, available except on, operating except on
   - **Surface/platform**: works except on, valid except on, supported except on
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + on (temporal/surface position)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase for temporal and positional exceptions
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're open every day except on Mondays.
   - You can park anywhere except on the grass.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The facility operates continuously except on designated maintenance days.
   - This application functions on all platforms except on legacy operating systems.

1. **Preposition**: except by (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Means/agency exclusion)* Only possible through; achievable only by means of; not able to occur unless through specific method or agent
   
   **Synonyms**: only by, solely by, exclusively by, but by
   
   **Antonyms**: including by, also by, even by
   
   **When to Use**: Specifies the exclusive means, method, or agent by which something can occur; emphasizes singular pathway; common in formal procedures and restrictions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not for general exclusions; requires method or agency context
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: except by permission, except by authorization, except by appointment, except by force
   - **Method specification**: accessible except by, achievable except by, obtainable except by
   - **Agency**: done except by, performed except by, executed except by
   
   **Root Analysis**: except (exclude) + by (means/agency marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Prepositional phrase specifying exclusive means or agency (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can't get in except by invitation.
   - This jar won't open except by force!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Access to restricted areas is prohibited except by explicit written authorization.
   - Modifications may not be implemented except by qualified technical personnel.
