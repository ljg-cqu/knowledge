1. **Original Expression**: "As per your request, I've attached the document."  
   **Context**: Responding to a formal request in professional communication
   
   **Core Intent**: Acknowledge fulfillment of a request while indicating the requested item is provided
   
   **Casual**: "Here's that document you asked for."  
   *Characteristics*: Direct, simple vocabulary, conversational tone, drops formal "as per"  
   *Use when*: Emailing colleagues you work with regularly, informal workplace settings, close professional relationships
   
   **Semi-Formal**: "As requested, please find the document attached."  
   *Characteristics*: Professional but approachable, standard business language, maintains politeness  
   *Use when*: General business correspondence, emails to managers or clients, standard professional communication
   
   **Formal**: "In accordance with your request, the requisite documentation has been attached for your review."  
   *Characteristics*: Elevated vocabulary ("requisite," "in accordance with"), passive construction, ceremonious tone

   **Lively**: "Great newsâ€”I've got that document ready for you! It's attached and ready to go!"  
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiastic tone, energetic vocabulary ("great news," "ready to go"), exclamation points  
   *Use when*: Sales contexts, energetic workplace culture, building enthusiasm, positive relationship
   
   **Empathetic**: "I understand you needed this document. I've attached it for youâ€”please let me know if you need anything else."  
   *Characteristics*: Acknowledges need, offers additional support, caring tone  
   *Use when*: Responding to urgent requests, supporting stressed colleagues, customer service contexts
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Higher status (CEO, board members) requires formal; peers allow semi-formal to casual
   - *Social Distance*: First-time contacts need semi-formal/formal; established relationships allow casual
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Legal matters require formal; routine requests allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: International business often prefers semi-formal; startup culture may prefer casual
   - *Medium*: External client emails need semi-formal/formal; internal Slack messages can be casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "as per" in casual contexts sounds bureaucratic and stilted
   - Overly casual tone with new clients can seem unprofessional
   - Using humorous tone in serious or time-sensitive matters undermines urgency
   
   **Cultural Notes**: "As per" is more common in Indian English business communication; American English tends to prefer "as requested" or simply "attached is..."
