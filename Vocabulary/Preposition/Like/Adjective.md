1. **Adjective**: like-minded
   **Meaning**: 
   - Having similar opinions, interests, or attitudes; sharing the same views or goals
   - *Gradable: can use "very", non-gradable when used to describe group membership*
   - *Both attributive and predicative use*
   
   **Synonyms**: similar-thinking, kindred, compatible, sympathetic, of one mind
   
   **Antonyms**: different, opposing, conflicting, divergent
   
   **When to Use**: When describing people or groups who share common values, beliefs, or goals; appropriate in formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when emphasizing superficial similarities rather than fundamental values; not suitable when discussing opposing viewpoints
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - like-minded individuals
   - like-minded people
   - like-minded community
   - gathering of like-minded souls
   - attract like-minded friends
   
   **Root Analysis**: like (similar) + minded (having a particular way of thinking)
   
   **Etymology**: English compound → formed from "like" (OE gelīc) + "minded" (OE gemynd). First recorded use mid-16th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm looking for like-minded people who love hiking as much as I do.
   - We're all pretty like-minded when it comes to work-life balance.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The conference aims to connect like-minded professionals committed to sustainable development.
   - Organizations often seek like-minded partners to collaborate on strategic initiatives.

1. **Adjective**: lifelike
   **Meaning**: 
   - Closely resembling real life; appearing very realistic or natural
   - *Non-gradable: something either looks real or it doesn't*
   - *Primarily attributive use*
   
   **Synonyms**: realistic, natural, true-to-life, authentic, convincing
   
   **Antonyms**: artificial, fake, unrealistic, stylized
   
   **When to Use**: When describing art, sculptures, dolls, simulations, or representations that closely resemble reality; suitable for describing visual or physical accuracy
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not appropriate for abstract concepts or intangible qualities; avoid when discussing superficial similarity without depth
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - lifelike representation
   - lifelike detail
   - lifelike quality
   - remarkably lifelike
   - almost lifelike appearance
   
   **Root Analysis**: life (living existence) + like (similar to)
   
   **Etymology**: English compound → from "life" (OE līf) + "like" (OE gelīc). First recorded use 16th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That robot looks so lifelike, it's kind of creepy!
   - The wax figures at Madame Tussauds are incredibly lifelike.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The artist's lifelike portrayal captures the subject's essence with remarkable precision.
   - Advanced computer graphics now produce lifelike simulations for medical training.

1. **Adjective**: childlike
   **Meaning**: 
   - Having the positive qualities of a child, such as innocence, wonder, trust, or simplicity
   - *(Note: differs from "childish" which has negative connotations)*
   - *Non-gradable: describes quality rather than degree*
   - *Both attributive and predicative use*
   
   **Synonyms**: innocent, naive, trusting, pure, guileless
   
   **Antonyms**: cynical, jaded, sophisticated, worldly
   
   **When to Use**: When describing positive child-like qualities in adults such as wonder, enthusiasm, innocence; appropriate when praising simplicity or authenticity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Do not confuse with "childish" (immature, petulant); avoid when describing negative behaviors or immaturity
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - childlike wonder
   - childlike innocence
   - childlike enthusiasm
   - childlike trust
   - maintain childlike curiosity
   
   **Root Analysis**: child (young person) + like (similar to)
   
   **Etymology**: English compound → from "child" (OE cild) + "like" (OE gelīc). First recorded use 14th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Her childlike excitement about the trip was so infectious!
   - He approaches life with a childlike sense of wonder.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The philosopher advocated for maintaining a childlike curiosity throughout one's intellectual pursuits.
   - Artists often cultivate a childlike perspective to perceive the world with fresh eyes.

1. **Adjective**: businesslike
   **Meaning**: 
   - Efficient, practical, and professional in manner; focused on getting work done without unnecessary socializing
   - *Non-gradable: describes a quality of behavior or approach*
   - *Both attributive and predicative use*
   
   **Synonyms**: professional, efficient, practical, matter-of-fact, no-nonsense
   
   **Antonyms**: casual, informal, disorganized, leisurely
   
   **When to Use**: When describing professional behavior, efficient meetings, or practical approaches; appropriate in work contexts when emphasizing professionalism
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when warmth and personal connection are valued; not suitable for describing social or personal relationships
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - businesslike manner
   - businesslike approach
   - businesslike tone
   - keep things businesslike
   - adopt a businesslike attitude
   
   **Root Analysis**: business (commercial activity) + like (similar to)
   
   **Etymology**: English compound → from "business" (OE bisignis) + "like" (OE gelīc). First recorded use mid-19th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She was totally businesslike during the negotiation—no small talk at all.
   - Let's keep this meeting businesslike so we can finish on time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee conducted proceedings in a businesslike manner, adhering strictly to the agenda.
   - Management expects all external communications to maintain a businesslike tone.

1. **Adjective**: warlike
   **Meaning**: 
   - Aggressive, hostile, or inclined to engage in warfare; characteristic of war or warriors
   - *Gradable: can be more or less warlike*
   - *Both attributive and predicative use*
   
   **Synonyms**: aggressive, bellicose, militant, hostile, combative
   
   **Antonyms**: peaceful, pacifist, conciliatory, dovish
   
   **When to Use**: When describing nations, leaders, rhetoric, or behavior that shows aggression or readiness for conflict; appropriate in historical, political, or military contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in contexts requiring diplomatic language; not suitable when discussing peaceful negotiations or cooperation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - warlike behavior
   - warlike posture
   - warlike rhetoric
   - warlike nation
   - increasingly warlike stance
   
   **Root Analysis**: war (armed conflict) + like (similar to)
   
   **Etymology**: English compound → from "war" (OE wyrre) + "like" (OE gelīc). First recorded use 15th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The boss's warlike attitude made everyone nervous at the meeting.
   - That country's been getting pretty warlike lately with all the military drills.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The empire's warlike expansion threatened neighboring territories throughout the century.
   - Historians debate whether the civilization was inherently warlike or merely defensive.
