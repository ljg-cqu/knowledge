# Onto - Related Nouns

1. **Noun**: transition (C)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The process or period of changing from one state, condition, form, or activity to another
   - A passage connecting two sections or themes in music, writing, or speech
   
   **Synonyms**: change, shift, transformation, passage, conversion, progression
   
   **Antonyms**: stasis, stability, permanence, constancy
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing change processes between states, especially in formal or technical contexts; emphasizes the in-between period
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For sudden, complete changes, use "transformation"; transition emphasizes the process itself
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - transition onto a new system
   - smooth transition
   - transition period
   - make a transition
   - ease the transition
   
   **Root Analysis**: trans- (across) + -ition (action/state)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "trans-" (across) + "ire" (to go) + "-ition" → English "transition" meaning passage from one state to another
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The transition onto remote work was harder than I expected.
   - She's making a big transition from city life to country living.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The transition onto digital recordkeeping will be implemented over six months.
   - Adolescence represents a critical transition onto adulthood.
