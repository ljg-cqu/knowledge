1. **Preposition**: to (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Indicating direction or movement toward a point, place, or position
   - *(Temporal)* Not primary meaning
   - *(Abstract)* Indicating the recipient or target of an action, feeling, or response; expressing a relationship of orientation or purpose
   
   **Synonyms**: toward (in context of direction), for (in context of recipient)
   
   **Antonyms**: from, away from
   
   **When to Use**: Universal usage expressing direction, recipient, or target of responses; used with "in response to", "reply to", "react to"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: With verbs that take different prepositions (e.g., "answer" takes no preposition before the object)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in response to, reply to, respond to, according to
   - **Verb + prep**: respond to, react to, reply to, listen to, speak to
   - **Adjective + prep**: similar to, identical to, equal to, responsive to
   - **Noun + prep**: response to, reaction to, answer to, reply to
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "tō" (toward, in direction of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "tō" → Middle English "to" - one of the most ancient English prepositions
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I need to respond to her message before tomorrow.
   - What was his reaction to the news?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee's response to the inquiry was detailed and comprehensive.
   - Organizations must be responsive to changing regulatory requirements.

1. **Preposition**: in response to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* As a reaction to something; as an answer to something; following and caused by something
   
   **Synonyms**: in answer to, in reaction to, following, as a result of
   
   **Antonyms**: regardless of, independent of, without regard to
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts showing causal relationship between stimulus and response; official communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use simpler forms like "because of" or "after"); when no causal relationship exists
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in response to criticism, in response to demands, in response to requests
   - **Verb + prep**: issued in response to, written in response to, created in response to
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: action in response to, measures in response to, statement in response to
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + response (re- + spons) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Compound prepositional phrase - formed in Modern English from combination of prepositions and noun
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They changed the policy in response to all the complaints.
   - In response to your question, yes, I'll be there.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The regulation was enacted in response to identified systemic failures.
   - In response to stakeholder feedback, the framework has been substantially revised.

1. **Preposition**: following (type: simple/participial)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not primary meaning
   - *(Temporal)* After in time; subsequent to
   - *(Abstract)* As a result of; in response to; because of
   
   **Synonyms**: after, subsequent to, as a result of, in the wake of
   
   **Antonyms**: before, prior to, preceding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts indicating temporal sequence or causal relationship
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simultaneous action is described; casual speech (use "after" instead)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: following the announcement, following criticism, following requests
   - **Verb + prep**: Not standard pattern (following is itself a preposition here)
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: response following, action following, changes following
   
   **Root Analysis**: follow (Old English "folgian") + -ing (present participle used as preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "folgian" + "-ing" → Middle English - participial form grammaticalized as preposition
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Following your email, I checked the files and found the issue.
   - They made changes following all the feedback.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Following the review, several recommendations were implemented.
   - The policy was revised following extensive consultation with stakeholders.

1. **Preposition**: regarding (type: participial)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* Concerning; about; in relation to; with reference to
   
   **Synonyms**: concerning, about, with regard to, in relation to, as regards
   
   **Antonyms**: None specific
   
   **When to Use**: Formal business and academic contexts to introduce topics or subjects being addressed
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "about" instead); narrative descriptions
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: regarding your inquiry, regarding the matter, regarding concerns
   - **Verb + prep**: write regarding, respond regarding, inquire regarding
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: questions regarding, response regarding, information regarding
   
   **Root Analysis**: regard (re- + guard = look at) + -ing (present participle)
   
   **Etymology**: French "regarder" (to look at) + "-ing" → English - participial form used as preposition (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm writing regarding the job posting I saw online.
   - Do you have any questions regarding the assignment?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We are responding regarding your inquiry dated March 15, 2024.
   - The committee addressed several concerns regarding implementation procedures.

1. **Preposition**: concerning (type: participial)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* About; regarding; relating to; in connection with
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, about, relating to, with respect to, as to
   
   **Antonyms**: None specific
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts to introduce subject matter being discussed or addressed
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Informal conversation (use "about"); can sound bureaucratic if overused
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: concerning the issue, concerning your request, concerning allegations
   - **Verb + prep**: respond concerning, write concerning, inquire concerning
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: questions concerning, response concerning, information concerning
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern (con- + cern = sift together) + -ing (present participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "concernere" (to relate to) + "-ing" → English participial preposition (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I have a few questions concerning the schedule.
   - Thanks for your email concerning the meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The administration issued a statement concerning the allegations.
   - We are writing concerning the discrepancies identified in the audit report.

1. **Preposition**: with regard to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* Concerning; about; in connection with; as far as something is concerned
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, with respect to, in relation to, as regards
   
   **Antonyms**: None specific
   
   **When to Use**: Very formal written communication; official correspondence; academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual speech; when simpler alternatives would suffice; can sound overly formal
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: with regard to your inquiry, with regard to the matter, with regard to concerns
   - **Verb + prep**: respond with regard to, write with regard to
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: questions with regard to, comments with regard to
   
   **Root Analysis**: with + regard (re- + guard) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Complex phrasal preposition formed in Modern English - highly formal register marker
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - With regard to your question about the deadline, it's next Friday.
   - I wanted to follow up with regard to our conversation yesterday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - With regard to your inquiry of April 3rd, we have completed our review.
   - The committee's recommendations with regard to governance structures have been accepted.

1. **Preposition**: in light of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* Considering; taking into account; because of; given the circumstances of
   
   **Synonyms**: considering, given, in view of, taking into account, because of
   
   **Antonyms**: regardless of, despite, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts showing that a response or action is influenced by particular circumstances or information
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Simple causal relationships (use "because of"); casual conversation
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in light of recent events, in light of evidence, in light of findings
   - **Verb + prep**: respond in light of, reconsider in light of, revise in light of
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: decision in light of, changes in light of, response in light of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + light (illumination, understanding) + of
   
   **Etymology**: Metaphorical use of "light" (understanding) - phrasal preposition formed in Modern English
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In light of what happened, I think we should postpone the meeting.
   - They changed their minds in light of the new information.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In light of the audit findings, the board has initiated a comprehensive review.
   - The recommendation was reconsidered in light of stakeholder feedback.

1. **Preposition**: pursuant to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; following; as required by; in response to (especially legal/official requirements)
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, according to, following, as required by, under
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, despite
   
   **When to Use**: Legal, regulatory, and highly formal bureaucratic contexts; official compliance statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: General business writing (use "according to"); casual or semi-formal contexts; sounds legalistic
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pursuant to regulations, pursuant to statute, pursuant to agreement
   - **Verb + prep**: issued pursuant to, conducted pursuant to, submitted pursuant to
   - **Adjective + prep**: Not standard pattern
   - **Noun + prep**: action pursuant to, requirement pursuant to
   
   **Root Analysis**: pursue (pur- = thoroughly + sue = follow) + -ant (suffix) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "poursuir" (to follow after) → "pursuant" (15th century) - specialized legal usage
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Rarely used in casual contexts - would sound inappropriate)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This response is submitted pursuant to Section 12(b) of the Administrative Procedures Act.
   - The investigation was conducted pursuant to the terms of the settlement agreement.

1. **Preposition**: about (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Around; in the area of
   - *(Temporal)* Approximately
   - *(Abstract)* Concerning; on the subject of; in connection with
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, on (in context of topics)
   
   **Antonyms**: None specific for abstract meaning
   
   **When to Use**: Universal - casual to formal; most natural way to introduce topics in everyday speech
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Highly formal legal or official documents (prefer "regarding" or "concerning")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: about the issue, about your question, about concerns
   - **Verb + prep**: talk about, write about, worry about, ask about, complain about
   - **Adjective + prep**: concerned about, worried about, curious about, serious about
   - **Noun + prep**: question about, concern about, information about, response about
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "onbūtan" (on + by + outside)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "onbūtan" → Middle English "aboute" → Modern English "about"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I have some questions about your email.
   - What did she say about the proposal?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The report addresses concerns about data security and privacy.
   - We received numerous inquiries about the implementation timeline.

1. **Preposition**: against (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In contact with; touching; pressing on
   - *(Temporal)* Not primary meaning
   - *(Abstract)* In opposition to; contrary to; as a defense or counter to
   
   **Synonyms**: in opposition to, counter to, contrary to, versus
   
   **Antonyms**: for, in favor of, in support of
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing opposition, defense, or counter-responses; debates and arguments
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Neutral or supportive responses; agreement contexts
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: against allegations, against criticism, against claims
   - **Verb + prep**: argue against, defend against, respond against, protest against
   - **Adjective + prep**: defensive against, protected against
   - **Noun + prep**: defense against, response against, protection against, argument against
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "ongegn" (toward, opposite)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "ongegn" → Middle English "ayein" → Modern English "against"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He argued against every point I made.
   - This is a response against the accusations they made.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The defendant's counsel presented evidence against the prosecution's claims.
   - The organization issued a vigorous response against the allegations of misconduct.
