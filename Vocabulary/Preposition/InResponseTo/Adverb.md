1. **Adverb**: promptly (type: manner/time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a prompt manner; without delay; immediately or quickly
   - At the expected or proper time; punctually
   
   **Synonyms**: quickly, immediately, swiftly, without delay, at once
   
   **Antonyms**: slowly, eventually, belatedly, tardily
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts emphasizing speed and efficiency; business communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing careful, deliberate processes where speed is not the priority
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond promptly
   - reply promptly
   - act promptly
   - arrive promptly
   - address promptly
   
   **Root Analysis**: prompt (Latin "promptus") + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: "Prompt" + "-ly" → English adverb form (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please reply promptly so we can finalize the plans.
   - She answered my question promptly without hesitation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All inquiries will be addressed promptly within 24 hours of receipt.
   - The committee responded promptly to the regulatory agency's requests.

1. **Adverb**: immediately (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At once; without any delay or lapse of time
   - Directly, with nothing in between
   
   **Synonyms**: instantly, at once, right away, straightaway, without delay
   
   **Antonyms**: eventually, later, gradually, after a while
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing zero delay; urgent situations; cause-and-effect with no time lag
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When some delay is expected or acceptable
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond immediately
   - react immediately
   - take effect immediately
   - available immediately
   - notify immediately
   
   **Root Analysis**: immediate (im- + mediate) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Late Latin "immediatus" + "-ly" → English (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I need you to respond immediately - this is urgent!
   - She immediately understood what I was trying to say.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The system immediately triggers an alert when anomalies are detected.
   - Staff must immediately report any safety incidents to their supervisor.

1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: manner/sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances
   - As a result; consequently; therefore
   
   **Synonyms**: consequently, therefore, appropriately, correspondingly, thus
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, regardless
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts showing logical consequence or appropriate adaptation; often sentence-initial or mid-position
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (sounds overly formal); when simple cause-effect markers suffice
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond accordingly
   - act accordingly
   - adjust accordingly
   - proceed accordingly
   - plan accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: according (ac- + cord = heart, agreement) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: "Accord" (agreement) + "-ing" + "-ly" → Middle English (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather forecast says rain, so plan accordingly.
   - He was warned about the consequences and acted accordingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data revealed significant trends; accordingly, the methodology was revised.
   - Regulations have changed; institutions must adjust their procedures accordingly.

1. **Adverb**: thoroughly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**:

   **Antonyms**: superficially, partially, incompletely, hastily
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing completeness and attention to detail; academic and professional contexts

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond thoroughly
   - examine thoroughly
   - investigate thoroughly
   - review thoroughly
   - address thoroughly

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Adverb**: formally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a formal manner; according to official or conventional rules and procedures
   - With proper ceremony, protocol, or official recognition
   
   **Synonyms**: officially, ceremoniously, conventionally, properly
   
   **Antonyms**: informally, casually, unofficially
   
   **When to Use**: Official communications, legal contexts, ceremonies requiring proper protocol
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual personal communications; relaxed social settings
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond formally
   - formally acknowledge
   - formally request
   - formally announce
   - formally submit
   
   **Root Analysis**: formal (Latin "formalis") + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "forma" (form, shape) → "formalis" + "-ly" → English (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I haven't formally responded yet, but I plan to accept.
   - He hasn't formally asked me, but I know he wants my help.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution formally responded to the accreditation committee's inquiry.
   - The agreement must be formally ratified by both parties before implementation.
