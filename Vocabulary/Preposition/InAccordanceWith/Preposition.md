1. **Preposition**: with (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* In the company of; accompanied by; alongside
   - *(Temporal)* At the same time as; during
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; following; agreeing with; having as a characteristic or feature; by means of
   
   **Synonyms**: alongside (spatial), during (temporal), according to (abstract - rules), by means of (instrumental)
   
   **Antonyms**: without, against, contrary to, despite
   
   **When to Use**: Universal preposition with multiple functions; expressing agreement, accompaniment, manner, means, or characteristics
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Common errors - using "with" instead of required dependent prepositions (e.g., *comply with standards is correct, but *agree with proposal should be "agree to proposal" in formal contexts)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in accordance with, in line with, consistent with, comply with
   - **Verb + prep**: agree with, comply with, conform with, deal with, work with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent with, compatible with, satisfied with, pleased with
   - **Noun + prep**: agreement with, compliance with, accordance with, relationship with
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English wið/wiþ (against, toward, with)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English wiþ (originally "against") → Middle English with (meanings expanded to include "alongside, by means of") (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm going to the store with Sarah.
   - You need to deal with this problem today.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The methodology is consistent with established research protocols.
   - All procedures must be conducted in strict accordance with regulatory requirements.

1. **Preposition**: to (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Expressing direction toward a place, person, or thing
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating the end point of a period or range
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; expressing conformity, agreement, or relation; indicating the recipient of an action
   
   **Synonyms**: toward (direction), until (temporal), according to (abstract - rules)
   
   **Antonyms**: from, away from
   
   **When to Use**: Universal preposition expressing direction, relation, or conformity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Common errors - confusing "to" with "too" or "two"; using after certain verbs that don't take "to"
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: according to, pursuant to, due to, relative to
   - **Verb + prep**: adhere to, conform to, refer to, relate to, respond to, listen to
   - **Adjective + prep**: relative to, subject to, relevant to, opposed to, similar to
   - **Noun + prep**: response to, adherence to, reference to, solution to, approach to
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English tō
   
   **Etymology**: Old English tō → Middle English to (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm going to the gym later.
   - You need to stick to the plan we agreed on.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All personnel must adhere to established safety protocols.
   - The findings are relative to the specific conditions of the experimental design.

1. **Preposition**: according to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* As stated or indicated by; in a way that agrees with or is in accordance with; following or based on
   
   **Synonyms**: as stated by, per, in accordance with, based on, as reported by, in line with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Universal expression for citing sources, referencing policies, or indicating conformity with rules or information
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Rarely inappropriate - most versatile attribution/conformity expression across all registers
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: according to [source], according to [policy], according to plan
   - **Verb + prep phrase**: act according to, proceed according to, operate according to
   - **Adjective + prep phrase**: done according to, arranged according to
   - **Noun + prep phrase**: [action] according to [standard]
   
   **Root Analysis**: ac- (to) + cord (heart) + -ing + to
   
   **Etymology**: Latin accordāre → Old French acorder → Middle English accord + -ing + to (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to Mike, the meeting got moved to Friday.
   - Everything's going according to plan so far.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to recent epidemiological studies, prevalence rates have increased substantially.
   - The procedure must be executed according to standardized protocols.

1. **Preposition**: in accordance with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Following or agreeing with rules, standards, wishes, or decisions; in conformity with; as required by
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, in conformity with, pursuant to, consistent with, according to, per
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, contrary to, in breach of, at odds with
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and legal contexts, official communications, compliance statements, contracts, regulations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (overly formal/bureaucratic), informal emails, creative writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in strict accordance with, in full accordance with, in close accordance with
   - **Verb + prep phrase**: act in accordance with, operate in accordance with, proceed in accordance with
   - **Adjective + prep phrase**: done in accordance with, made in accordance with
   - **Noun + prep phrase**: [action] in accordance with [rule/standard]
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + ac- (to) + cord (heart) + -ance + with
   
   **Etymology**: Latin accordāre → accordance → phrasal preposition "in accordance with" (17th century legal usage)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Rarely used casually - too formal] You need to do this in accordance with company policy.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All transactions must be executed in accordance with applicable securities regulations.
   - The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki ethical principles.

1. **Preposition**: pursuant to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; following or conforming to (legal and official usage); as authorized or required by
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, under, by virtue of, as required by, in compliance with
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, contrary to, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents, contracts, regulatory filings, official government communications, formal compliance statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (extremely formal), general business communication (overly legalistic), informal contexts
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pursuant to Section [X], pursuant to the terms of, pursuant to authority
   - **Verb + prep phrase**: act pursuant to, issued pursuant to, filed pursuant to
   - **Adjective + prep phrase**: done pursuant to, made pursuant to
   - **Noun + prep phrase**: [action] pursuant to [legal authority]
   
   **Root Analysis**: pursue (follow) + -ant + to
   
   **Etymology**: French poursuir → pursuant + to → legal formula (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Not used in casual contexts - exclusively legal/formal]
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pursuant to Section 12(a) of the Securities Exchange Act, the company hereby files this quarterly report.
   - The investigation was conducted pursuant to authority granted under federal statute.

1. **Preposition**: in line with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; consistent with; matching or conforming to expectations, policies, or standards
   
   **Synonyms**: consistent with, in accordance with, in keeping with, aligned with, conforming to
   
   **Antonyms**: out of line with, inconsistent with, contrary to, at odds with
   
   **When to Use**: Business contexts, policy discussions, showing consistency with standards or expectations, professional communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal legal documents (prefer "in accordance with"), highly casual contexts
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in line with expectations, in line with policy, in line with standards
   - **Verb + prep phrase**: operate in line with, act in line with, remain in line with
   - **Adjective + prep phrase**: [be] in line with
   - **Noun + prep phrase**: [action/result] in line with [standard/expectation]
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + line + with (metaphor of alignment)
   
   **Etymology**: Metaphorical extension from physical alignment → business language (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is this budget in line with what we spent last year?
   - Your performance has been in line with our expectations.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed initiative is in line with strategic organizational objectives.
   - Compensation adjustments are in line with prevailing market conditions.

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Rarely used casually - too formal]
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Preposition**: per (type: simple - Latin borrowing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* According to; in accordance with; as specified by; for each; by means of
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, as per, in accordance with, by, via (for transmission)
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite
   
   **When to Use**: Business communication, instructions, specifications, rates and measurements, referencing documents
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal legal documents (use "pursuant to" or "in accordance with"), academic writing (prefer "according to")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: per [document], per [person], per [unit], as per
   - **Verb + prep**: act per, proceed per, charge per
   - **Common collocations**: per annum, per capita, per diem, as per usual, per your request
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (through, by means of, according to)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per → English per (direct borrowing, 15th century, expanded usage in business English 20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you send me that document per the email thread?
   - The rate is $50 per hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Reimbursement will be calculated at $150 per diem for travel expenses.
   - Per the terms specified in Appendix C, deliverables are due quarterly.

1. **Preposition**: under (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Below or beneath; covered by
   - *(Temporal)* During the period of
   - *(Abstract)* Subject to; governed by; in accordance with (authority, rules, conditions); experiencing or undergoing
   
   **Synonyms**: below (spatial), during (temporal), subject to (abstract - rules), governed by, pursuant to
   
   **Antonyms**: over, above, beyond
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing subjection to authority, rules, or conditions; indicating governance or applicability
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be ambiguous - ensure context clarifies whether spatial or abstract meaning intended
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: under the law, under regulations, under authority of, under circumstances
   - **Verb + prep**: operate under, fall under, work under, act under
   - **Adjective + prep**: subject under (rare - usually "subject to")
   - **Noun + prep**: authority under, provisions under, rights under
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English under
   
   **Etymology**: Old English under → Middle English (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The book is under the pile of papers on my desk.
   - We're operating under a tight deadline here.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Under the provisions of Section 501(c)(3), the organization qualifies for tax-exempt status.
   - The matter falls under the jurisdiction of the federal regulatory authority.

1. **Preposition**: by (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Near to; beside; past
   - *(Temporal)* Not later than; before
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; according to; through the agency or means of; using as a standard or measure
   
   **Synonyms**: beside (spatial), before (temporal), according to (abstract - rules), through (agency), via (means)
   
   **Antonyms**: away from (spatial), after (temporal), contrary to (abstract)
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing agency, means, method, conformity to standards, deadlines
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Common errors - confusing "by" with "buy" or "bye"; misusing in passive constructions
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: by [means], by [standard], by [deadline], by [agent]
   - **Verb + prep**: abide by, go by, judge by, measured by, governed by
   - **Adjective + prep**: bound by, constrained by, determined by
   - **Noun + prep**: compliance by, adherence by
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English bī
   
   **Etymology**: Old English bī (near) → Middle English by (meanings expanded) (before 12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll be there by 5 PM.
   - We need to go by the rules they set up.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Performance is measured by established key performance indicators.
   - All parties are bound by the terms stipulated in the contractual agreement.
