1. **Functional Expression**: in accordance with [rules/regulations/policy] (function: stating compliance)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that an action or decision follows or agrees with established rules, standards, or decisions
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, in conformity with, pursuant to, consistent with, according to
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, contrary to, in breach of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents, official communications, policy statements, compliance contexts, formal business writing, regulatory discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (sounds bureaucratic), creative writing (too stiff), informal emails to colleagues (overly formal)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in strict accordance with (more emphatic)
   - in full accordance with (emphasizes completeness)
   - in accordance with the terms of (legal/contractual)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals formal compliance and adherence to authority; used to establish legitimacy and proper procedure; face-saving strategy that shields speaker from personal responsibility by attributing action to rules
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Understood. We'll ensure compliance."
   - Questioning: "Could you clarify which specific regulation this refers to?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Rarely used in casual contexts]
   - "We need to do this in accordance with what the boss said." (sounds overly formal)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All personnel decisions must be made in accordance with applicable employment legislation.
   - The project will be executed in accordance with the specifications outlined in the contract.

1. **Functional Expression**: As per [agreement/policy/discussion] (function: referencing prior agreement)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to refer back to previously established agreements, discussions, or documents; indicates following what was decided or stated
   - *Formality*: semi-formal to formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in line with, following, per, as agreed, as discussed
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Business emails, meeting follow-ups, referencing previous agreements, professional correspondence, project management communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal legal documents (prefer "in accordance with"), highly casual settings (too businesslike), academic writing (prefer "according to")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as per our discussion (meeting follow-ups)
   - as per your request (responding to requests)
   - as per usual (casual, means "as always")
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Efficiently references shared context without repeating details; establishes accountability by citing sources; face-saving device that shows respect for prior agreements
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Yes, that matches my understanding."
   - Correcting: "Actually, my recollection of our discussion was slightly different..."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As per usual, he showed up late to the meeting.
   - Can you send me the report as per what we talked about?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As per our agreement dated March 15, payment will be remitted within 30 days.
   - The deliverables have been completed as per the project specifications.

1. **Functional Expression**: According to [source/policy/person] (function: citing authority or source)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate information comes from a specific source or should follow certain rules; showing alignment with stated information
   - *Formality*: neutral (works in all registers)
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: as stated by, per, in line with, based on, as reported by
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of
   
   **When to Use**: Universal expression for citing sources, referencing policies, attributing information, all academic and professional contexts, news reporting
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you want to emphasize strict legal compliance (use "in accordance with" instead)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - according to [person] (attributing statements)
   - according to plan (matching expectations)
   - according to the rules (following regulations)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Neutral citation form that attributes information or requirements to external source; reduces personal responsibility; used in evidential contexts to support claims
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "That aligns with what I've heard as well."
   - Questioning: "Where did that information come from?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to Sarah, the meeting got moved to Thursday.
   - Everything went according to plan today!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to the latest research findings, climate patterns have shifted significantly.
   - Procedures must be followed according to established safety protocols.

1. **Functional Expression**: In line with [expectations/policy/standards] (function: showing conformity)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that something matches or conforms to expectations, policies, or standards; shows alignment
   - *Formality*: semi-formal to formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: consistent with, in keeping with, in accordance with, aligned with, conforming to
   
   **Antonyms**: out of line with, inconsistent with, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Business strategy discussions, performance reviews, policy implementation, showing alignment with goals or standards
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversations (sounds businesslike), highly technical legal documents (prefer "in accordance with")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in line with expectations (performance contexts)
   - in line with industry standards (benchmarking)
   - in line with our strategy (strategic planning)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Emphasizes harmony and alignment; positive politeness strategy that shows cooperation; used to demonstrate compliance without sounding overly bureaucratic
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Good to hear we're aligned."
   - Questioning: "How does this compare to industry benchmarks?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your proposal is totally in line with what we've been thinking.
   - Is this in line with what you expected?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed budget is in line with organizational strategic priorities.
   - Performance metrics are in line with industry standards for this sector.

1. **Functional Expression**: Pursuant to [law/regulation/agreement] (function: citing legal authority)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used in legal and official contexts to indicate actions taken in accordance with specific laws, regulations, or formal agreements
   - *Formality*: very formal (legal/official)
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, under, by virtue of, in compliance with, as required by
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, notwithstanding, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents, contracts, official notices, regulatory filings, government communications, formal compliance statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (sounds extremely formal), general business communication (overly legalistic), informal writing
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pursuant to Section [X] (legal citations)
   - pursuant to the terms of (contracts)
   - pursuant to our agreement (formal references)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Strongest form of citing authority; establishes legal basis for action; face-saving through institutional authority; signals non-negotiable compliance requirement
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Acknowledged. We will comply accordingly."
   - Questioning: "Could you provide the specific statutory reference?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Not used in casual contexts]
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pursuant to Section 12(a) of the Securities Act, the company must file quarterly reports.
   - This notice is issued pursuant to the terms of our service agreement dated January 1, 2024.

   **Response Patterns**:

   - Questioning: "Which specific standards are being referenced?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Rarely used in casual contexts]
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Functional Expression**: Consistent with [policy/findings/principles] (function: showing alignment)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that something aligns with or does not contradict established policies, findings, or principles
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, in accordance with, aligned with, compatible with, agreeing with
   
   **Antonyms**: inconsistent with, contrary to, at odds with, contradicting
   
   **When to Use**: Academic writing, research contexts, policy analysis, showing logical coherence, professional reports
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When strict legal compliance language is needed (use "in accordance with"), overly casual contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - consistent with our understanding (qualifying statements)
   - consistent with previous research (academic contexts)
   - consistent with best practices (professional standards)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Emphasizes logical coherence and non-contradiction rather than strict rule-following; softer than "in accordance with"; allows for interpretation
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "That makes sense given the context."
   - Questioning: "How confident are we in this alignment?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your story is consistent with what everyone else said.
   - That's consistent with my experience too.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The experimental results are consistent with theoretical predictions.
   - The proposed approach is consistent with established organizational principles.

1. **Functional Expression**: In keeping with [tradition/values/approach] (function: maintaining continuity)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that something continues or maintains alignment with established traditions, values, or approaches
   - *Formality*: neutral to semi-formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (softer than "in accordance with")
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, consistent with, true to, following, maintaining
   
   **Antonyms**: breaking with, departing from, contrary to, at odds with
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing traditions, organizational culture, brand values, maintaining continuity, cultural contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Legal compliance contexts (not strong enough), when strict adherence is required, technical specifications
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in keeping with tradition (cultural contexts)
   - in keeping with our values (organizational culture)
   - in keeping with the spirit of (emphasizes intent over letter)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Emphasizes cultural and value alignment rather than rule compliance; positive politeness strategy showing respect for tradition; softer and more relationship-oriented
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "That honors our heritage nicely."
   - Questioning: "Is this the direction we want to continue?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In keeping with tradition, we always have pizza at the end-of-year party.
   - The design is in keeping with the company's usual style.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The initiative is in keeping with the organization's commitment to sustainability.
   - This decision is in keeping with our long-standing policy of stakeholder engagement.
