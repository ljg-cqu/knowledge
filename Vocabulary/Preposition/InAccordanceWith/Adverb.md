1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; in accordance with what has been stated or decided
   - *(Sense 2)* As a result; therefore; consequently (used to introduce a logical conclusion)
   
   **Synonyms**: correspondingly, appropriately, suitably, consequently, therefore, thus
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, contrary to, regardless
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing and professional communication; introducing logical consequences; showing compliance with previous statements; academic and business contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation; can sound stiff in informal emails; avoid overuse in creative writing
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act accordingly
   - plan accordingly
   - adjust accordingly
   - proceed accordingly
   - respond accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (verb) + -ing + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English acorden → accord + -ing + -ly (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The meeting is at 3 PM, so plan accordingly.
   - She didn't like the food, and accordingly, she didn't eat much.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The regulatory framework has been revised; organizations must adjust their compliance protocols accordingly.
   - Research findings indicate significant risk factors; accordingly, preventive measures should be implemented immediately.

1. **Adverb**: precisely (type: degree/manner)
   **Meaning**:

   **Synonyms**: exactly, accurately, correctly, specifically, literally, just
   
   **Antonyms**: approximately, roughly, inexactly, vaguely

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - precisely defined
   - more precisely
   - precisely because
   - precisely what
   - precisely the point
   
   **Root Analysis**: precise (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praecīsus (cut short) → precise + -ly (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's precisely what I was thinking!
   - The meeting starts at 2 PM precisely, so don't be late.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Measurements must be precisely calibrated in accordance with international standards.
   - The statute addresses precisely those situations wherein regulatory ambiguity has previously existed.
