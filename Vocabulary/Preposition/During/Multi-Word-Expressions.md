1. **Expression**: during business hours (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Within the time period when a business, office, or organization is officially open and operating
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: within operating hours, during office hours, during working hours, when open
   
   **Antonyms**: after hours, outside business hours, when closed
   
   **When to Use**: Common in customer service, professional communications, appointment scheduling, and business policies. Indicates when services are available or when contact is possible. Standard business terminology across all English-speaking regions.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not applicable to contexts without formal operating hours. Avoid when specific times would be clearer (e.g., "between 9 AM and 5 PM" vs. "during business hours" if precision matters).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during regular business hours
   - during normal business hours
   - during standard business hours
   - within business hours
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Business hours" is a fixed term for operating times, and "during" naturally pairs with time periods. This combination is conventional in commercial and professional contexts. High frequency of co-occurrence in business communication.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" (Latin *durare*) + "business" (Old English *bisignes*) + "hours" (Greek *hōra* via Latin)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Give them a call during business hours—they're usually there until 6.
   - Sorry, I can only handle that during business hours. Can it wait until Monday?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Customer inquiries received after hours will be addressed during business hours on the next business day.
   - All appointments must be scheduled during business hours by contacting our administrative office.

2. **Expression**: during the course of (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At some point within a process, event, or time period; emphasizes progression through a sequence
   - More formal equivalent of "during"; adds sense of progression or development
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: during, in the course of, throughout, over the course of, in the process of
   
   **Antonyms**: before the start of, after the completion of, at the outset of, at the conclusion of
   
   **When to Use**: Preferred in formal, academic, and legal contexts. Useful when emphasizing the progressive nature of events or when a more weighty, formal tone is appropriate. Common in official reports, scholarly writing, and legal documents.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation; will sound stilted in everyday speech. Unnecessarily wordy when simple "during" suffices. Avoid overuse in writing as it can seem pretentious.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during the course of the investigation
   - during the course of the study
   - during the course of events
   - in the course of [same usage]
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: "Course" implies progression and duration, making it naturally compatible with "during." The phrase became fixed in formal registers, particularly legal and academic English, where it adds gravitas and precision to temporal references.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" + "course" (Latin *cursus*, "a running, course") + "of"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - During the course of dinner, we talked about old times.
   - I realized during the course of the day that I'd forgotten my keys.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - During the course of the investigation, several inconsistencies were discovered.
   - The research team collected extensive data during the course of the five-year study.

3. **Expression**: during rush hour (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Within the peak traffic times when roads are most congested, typically morning and evening commutes
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: during peak hours, during peak traffic, in peak time, at rush time
   
   **Antonyms**: during off-peak hours, outside rush hour, during quiet times
   
   **When to Use**: Common in discussions of transportation, commuting, traffic, and urban planning. Useful for scheduling, explaining delays, or warning about travel times. Universally understood in urban contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May not be relevant in rural areas or small towns without significant traffic patterns. Not appropriate when discussing other types of "rush" (retail rush, emotional rush, etc.).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during rush hours (plural)
   - during the rush hour
   - during morning/evening rush hour
   - in rush hour traffic
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Rush hour" is a fixed compound noun for peak traffic times. "During" naturally pairs with time periods and events. This collocation is standard in transportation and urban contexts worldwide.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" + "rush" (Middle English *russhen*) + "hour"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Don't even try to drive downtown during rush hour—you'll be stuck forever.
   - I leave early to avoid being on the road during rush hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The incident occurred during rush hour, causing significant delays for commuters.
   - Public transportation capacity is increased during rush hour to accommodate higher demand.

4. **Expression**: during one's time (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Within the period when someone held a position, lived in a place, or was involved in something
   - References a person's tenure or period of involvement
   - *Fixedness*: medium
   
   **Synonyms**: while [person] was [role/place], in [person's] tenure, when [person] was [role]
   
   **Antonyms**: before [person's] time, after [person] left, outside [person's] period
   
   **When to Use**: Common in biographical, historical, and professional contexts. Useful for attributing events or changes to specific periods of leadership or involvement. Helps establish temporal context related to individuals.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound vague if the actual time period is important. May seem evasive if avoiding specific dates intentionally. Not appropriate for short-term or trivial involvements.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during his/her/their time
   - during my/your/our time
   - during [name's] time
   - during one's tenure
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "One's time" is a conventional way to refer to someone's period of involvement or tenure. "During" naturally combines with this to create temporal references tied to individuals. Common in professional and historical contexts.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" + possessive + "time"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - During my time at the company, I never saw anything like that happen.
   - Things were different during his time—nobody worried about that stuff back then.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - During her tenure as director, the organization underwent significant restructuring.
   - Several important reforms were implemented during President Lincoln's time in office.

5. **Expression**: during the war (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Within the time period when a war was taking place
   - Often refers to World War II when capitalized: "During the War"
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: in wartime, throughout the war, in the war years, while the war was on
   
   **Antonyms**: before the war, after the war, in peacetime, post-war
   
   **When to Use**: Common in historical discussions, memoirs, family stories, and historical analysis. Serves as temporal reference point for major historical periods. Often carries emotional or cultural weight.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Requires shared understanding of which war is referenced. May need clarification if multiple wars are relevant to the context. Can be unclear without additional context.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during the war years
   - during wartime
   - during World War II / the Vietnam War / etc.
   - during the First/Second World War
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Wars are major temporal landmarks that divide history into periods. "During the war" has become a fixed expression, particularly in cultures deeply affected by major conflicts. Very high frequency in historical narratives and personal histories.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" + "the" + "war" (Old English *werre*)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My grandparents met during the war—he was stationed near her hometown.
   - During the war, everyone had to use ration coupons for food.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Economic policies during the war required significant civilian sacrifice.
   - The study examines social changes that occurred during the war and their lasting impact.

6. **Expression**: during the day/night (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Within daylight hours (during the day) or nighttime hours (during the night)
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: by day/by night, in daytime/nighttime, in the day/night hours
   
   **Antonyms**: during the day ↔ during the night (each other's antonyms)
   
   **When to Use**: Universal temporal reference for distinguishing between daytime and nighttime activities, schedules, or events. Essential for describing routines, schedules, animal behavior, work shifts, etc.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May need to be more specific with actual times if precision matters. Not appropriate when discussing a specific day (use "on that day" instead).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - during the daytime/nighttime
   - during day/night
   - during daylight hours / during the night hours
   - by day / by night
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Day" and "night" are fundamental temporal divisions. "During" naturally pairs with these time periods. This collocation is among the most basic and frequent temporal expressions in English.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "during" + "the" + "day"/"night" (both from Old English)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I sleep during the day because I work the night shift.
   - The temperature drops a lot during the night in the desert.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Nocturnal animals are active during the night and rest during the day.
   - Security patrols are conducted during the night to ensure facility protection.
