1. **Original Expression**: during the meeting  
   **Context**: Referring to when something happened within a meeting timeframe
   
   **Core Intent**: To indicate that an event occurred at some point while a meeting was taking place
   
   **Casual**: while we were meeting  
   *Characteristics*: conversational tone, active voice, personal pronouns  
   *Use when*: talking to colleagues, friends, informal updates
   
   **Semi-Formal**: during the meeting  
   *Characteristics*: standard preposition, neutral tone, professional but not stiff  
   *Use when*: workplace emails, regular business communication, team updates
   
   **Formal**: in the course of the meeting  
   *Characteristics*: longer phrase, more abstract, official tone  
   *Use when*: formal reports, official documentation, executive communication
   
   **Humorous**: in the middle of our thrilling meeting  
   *Characteristics*: ironic tone, sarcastic descriptor  
   *Use when*: bonding with coworkers, casual team chat, venting about boring meetings  
   *Avoid when*: talking to superiors, formal settings, meeting minutes
   
   **Lively**: right there in the meeting!  
   *Characteristics*: emphasis words, exclamation, immediate tone  
   *Use when*: storytelling, sharing exciting news, emphasizing timing
   
   **Empathetic**: during what must have been a difficult meeting  
   *Characteristics*: acknowledges emotional context, shows understanding  
   *Use when*: discussing challenging situations, showing support, sensitive topics
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Use "in the course of" with executives; "during" with peers; "while we were" with subordinates
   - *Social Distance*: "in the course of" for strangers/clients; "during" for regular contacts; "while" for close colleagues
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Serious matters require "during" or "in the course of"; light topics allow "while"
   - *Cultural Context*: Anglo cultures accept casual forms; hierarchical cultures prefer formal
   - *Medium*: Written reports favor formal; emails accept semi-formal; chat allows casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "while" in formal writing: "The decision was made while the meeting" sounds too casual for reports
   - Overusing "in the course of" in everyday speech: sounds pompous and unnatural
   - Mixing registers: "The issue was raised right there in the meeting" mixes formal subject with casual expression
   
   **Cultural Notes**: In British English, formal expressions are more common in business; in American English, semi-formal is standard for most workplace contexts.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Email to your boss about a decision → **Appropriate Variant**: "during the meeting" (semi-formal, respects hierarchy)
   - **Situation 2**: Legal deposition transcript → **Appropriate Variant**: "in the course of the meeting" (formal, official record)
   - **Situation 3**: Texting a coworker about what happened → **Appropriate Variant**: "while we were meeting" (casual, peer relationship)

2. **Original Expression**: during this time  
   **Context**: Referring to events within a previously mentioned period
   
   **Core Intent**: To indicate simultaneity with a referenced time period
   
   **Casual**: back then, at that time, when that was happening  
   *Characteristics*: colloquial, time deixis, shorter phrases  
   *Use when*: storytelling, personal narratives, informal conversation
   
   **Semi-Formal**: during this time, during this period  
   *Characteristics*: standard temporal reference, clear and precise  
   *Use when*: professional presentations, business reports, standard communication
   
   **Formal**: throughout this period, in the course of this interval, over the duration of this timeframe  
   *Characteristics*: extended phrases, technical vocabulary, precise specification  
   *Use when*: academic writing, legal documents, formal presentations
   
   **Humorous**: in those wild times, back in the day  
   *Characteristics*: exaggeration, nostalgic humor, informal idioms  
   *Use when*: entertaining presentations, team building, light-hearted retrospectives  
   *Avoid when*: serious historical discussion, formal analysis, sensitive topics
   
   **Lively**: right around then!, in those crazy days  
   *Characteristics*: excitement markers, vivid descriptors, emphasis  
   *Use when*: engaging storytelling, motivational talks, creating interest
   
   **Empathetic**: during those challenging times, throughout that difficult period  
   *Characteristics*: emotional acknowledgment, supportive descriptors  
   *Use when*: discussing hardships, historical tragedies, personal struggles
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal phrases with authority figures; standard with equals; casual with close relationships
   - *Social Distance*: More formal with distant relationships; more casual with close ones
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Serious topics (war, disaster) require formal; everyday events allow casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Academic contexts worldwide prefer formal variants
   - *Medium*: Academic papers require formal; blog posts accept casual; professional emails use semi-formal
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "back then" in academic writing: too colloquial for scholarly discourse
   - Overusing "throughout this period" in conversation: sounds pedantic
   - Inconsistent register: "The economic crisis occurred back then and affected millions" mixes casual with serious
   
   **Cultural Notes**: Historical references in formal education globally require formal temporal expressions. Casual variants more accepted in American than British academic contexts.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Academic paper on historical events → **Appropriate Variant**: "throughout this period" (formal, scholarly standard)
   - **Situation 2**: Company blog post about startup years → **Appropriate Variant**: "during this time" or "back then" (semi-formal to casual, engaging)
   - **Situation 3**: Keynote speech at memorial event → **Appropriate Variant**: "during those challenging times" (empathetic, respectful)

3. **Original Expression**: during the pandemic  
   **Context**: Referring to events within the COVID-19 pandemic period
   
   **Core Intent**: To situate events within the pandemic timeframe
   
   **Casual**: when COVID hit, during COVID, in COVID times  
   *Characteristics*: abbreviated, personal reference, conversational  
   *Use when*: everyday conversation, personal sharing, informal reflection
   
   **Semi-Formal**: during the pandemic, during the COVID-19 pandemic  
   *Characteristics*: standard reference, clear temporal marker, professional  
   *Use when*: workplace discussion, news articles, business communication
   
   **Formal**: during the COVID-19 pandemic period, throughout the pandemic era, in the course of the global health crisis  
   *Characteristics*: full terminology, precise designation, official tone  
   *Use when*: research papers, official reports, policy documents
   
   **Humorous**: in the great indoors, during the lock-down times, in the zoom era  
   *Characteristics*: playful references, shared cultural experience, ironic tone  
   *Use when*: relating to shared experiences, building rapport, light-hearted discussion  
   *Avoid when*: discussing deaths or serious impacts, formal settings, sensitive audiences
   
   **Lively**: during those wild pandemic days!, in the thick of COVID!  
   *Characteristics*: energetic description, exclamatory tone, dramatic flair  
   *Use when*: storytelling about adaptations, sharing survival stories, creating engagement
   
   **Empathetic**: during those difficult pandemic times, throughout the challenging period of COVID-19  
   *Characteristics*: acknowledges hardship, compassionate language, sensitive framing  
   *Use when*: discussing losses, acknowledging struggles, supporting others
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Official communications require full formal terms; peer discussions allow casual
   - *Social Distance*: Professional audiences need "pandemic"; friends accept "COVID times"
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Discussing deaths/illness requires formal/empathetic; discussing remote work allows casual
   - *Cultural Context*: Global scientific community requires formal designation
   - *Medium*: Published research uses formal; social media accepts casual; news uses semi-formal
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using casual "COVID times" in academic papers: inappropriate informality
   - Using overly formal "pandemic period" in personal conversation: sounds distant and cold
   - Making jokes about "lock-down times" when discussing serious impacts: insensitive
   
   **Cultural Notes**: Different regions experienced pandemic differently; some may find light references insensitive. Global health organizations require formal terminology in all communications.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Medical research paper → **Appropriate Variant**: "during the COVID-19 pandemic period" (formal, precise)
   - **Situation 2**: Casual conversation with friends → **Appropriate Variant**: "during COVID" or "when COVID hit" (casual, relatable)
   - **Situation 3**: Memorial or support group → **Appropriate Variant**: "during those difficult pandemic times" (empathetic, respectful)
