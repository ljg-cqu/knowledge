1. **Adjective**: separate (comparative: more separate, superlative: most separate)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Existing or occurring independently; not joined or connected to something else
   - *(apart from)* Being distinct or isolated from other things or people
   
   **Synonyms**: distinct, independent, detached, isolated, disconnected
   
   **Antonyms**: connected, joined, together, unified, attached
   
   **When to Use**: Describing things or concepts that exist independently; formal and neutral contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing temporary separation (use "separated" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - separate from something
   - keep separate from
   - entirely separate from
   - separate from the main group
   - separate from others
   
   **Root Analysis**: se- (apart) + par (prepare, arrange) + -ate
   
   **Etymology**: Latin separatus → past participle of separare (to divide) → English separate
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My work life is totally separate from my personal life.
   - Keep the whites separate from the colors when washing.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The judicial branch remains separate from legislative authority.
   - These phenomena are entirely separate from previously documented cases.

1. **Adjective**: different (comparative: more different, superlative: most different)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not the same as another or each other; unlike in nature, form, or quality
   - *(apart from)* Distinct or distinguishable from something else
   
   **Synonyms**: distinct, dissimilar, unlike, divergent, contrasting
   
   **Antonyms**: similar, same, identical, alike, equivalent
   
   **When to Use**: Describing distinctions or contrasts between entities; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When emphasizing complete opposition (use "opposite" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - different from something
   - quite different from
   - completely different from
   - different from what was expected
   - different from before
   
   **Root Analysis**: dif- (apart) + fer (carry) + -ent
   
   **Etymology**: Latin differre (to scatter, differ) → Old French different → English different
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This version is different from the one I saw yesterday.
   - Your approach is really different from mine.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The methodology employed was markedly different from conventional approaches.
   - Current findings are substantially different from earlier research.

1. **Adjective**: exceptional (comparative: more exceptional, superlative: most exceptional)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Unusual or outstanding; not typical; forming an exception
   - *(apart from)* Different from the general rule; standing out as an exception
   
   **Synonyms**: extraordinary, outstanding, unusual, remarkable, unique
   
   **Antonyms**: typical, ordinary, common, average, normal
   
   **When to Use**: Describing something that stands out from the norm; positive or neutral connotation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When standard or typical is meant
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - exceptional from the norm
   - quite exceptional
   - truly exceptional
   - exceptional circumstances
   - exceptional case
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + cept (take) + -ion + -al
   
   **Etymology**: Latin exceptionalis → from exceptio (exception) → English exceptional
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This is exceptional—totally apart from what we usually see.
   - Her talent is exceptional compared to others.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The results were exceptional, standing apart from previous findings.
   - This case represents exceptional circumstances apart from standard protocols.
