1. **Verb**: separate
   **Meaning**: 
   - To cause to move or be apart; to divide into constituent or distinct elements
   - To stop living together as a couple; to distinguish or consider differently
   
   **Synonyms**: divide, split, disconnect, detach, isolate
   
   **Antonyms**: join, unite, connect, merge, combine
   
   **When to Use**: Describing the action of creating distance or division; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When unification is being described
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - separate from something
   - separate into parts
   - separate out
   - clearly separate
   - help separate
   
   **Root Analysis**: se- (apart) + par (prepare) + -ate
   
   **Etymology**: Latin separare (to divide) → English separate
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's separate the good ones from the bad, apart from sorting later.
   - They separated last year, apart from occasional contact.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The methodology separates variables systematically, apart from confounding factors.
   - It is essential to separate fact from interpretation, apart from analytical frameworks.

1. **Verb**: exclude

   - To deny access to or bar from a place, group, or privilege
   - To remove from consideration; to prevent the occurrence of
   
   **Synonyms**: bar, omit, eliminate, reject, leave out
   
   **Antonyms**: include, admit, accept, incorporate, welcome
   
   **When to Use**: Describing intentional omission or denial of access; neutral to formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When inclusion is being emphasized
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - exclude from something
   - exclude someone/something
   - exclude the possibility
   - deliberately exclude
   - cannot exclude
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + clud (close)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin excludere (to shut out) → English exclude
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They excluded him from the party, apart from the main group invite.
   - Apart from Sarah, they excluded everyone who was late.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study excluded participants with prior conditions, apart from the control variables.
   - We cannot exclude alternative explanations, apart from our primary hypothesis.

1. **Verb**: distinguish

   - To recognize or treat someone or something as different
   - To perceive or point out a difference; to make oneself noticeably different

   **Antonyms**: confuse, mix up, blend, combine, equate

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - distinguish from something
   - distinguish between
   - clearly distinguish
   - difficult to distinguish
   - distinguish oneself
   
   **Root Analysis**: dis- (apart) + stingu (prick, mark)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin distinguere (to separate by pricking) → English distinguish
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you distinguish this one from that one, apart from the color?
   - It's hard to distinguish them apart from their voices.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The analysis distinguishes primary from secondary effects, apart from confounding influences.
   - It is crucial to distinguish correlation from causation, apart from statistical significance.
