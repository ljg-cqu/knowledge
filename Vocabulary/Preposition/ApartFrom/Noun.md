1. **Noun**: exception (C, pl: exceptions)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A person or thing that is excluded from a general statement or does not follow the rule
   - An instance that does not conform to a general pattern
   
   **Synonyms**: anomaly, deviation, exclusion, special case, irregularity
   
   **Antonyms**: rule, norm, standard, regularity, conformity
   
   **When to Use**: Describing items or cases that differ from the general pattern; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing the general rule itself
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - make an exception
   - with the exception of
   - notable exception
   - without exception
   - exception to the rule
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + cept (take) + -ion
   
   **Etymology**: Latin exceptio (a taking out) → Old French → English exception
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone came to the party, apart from John—he's the exception.
   - Apart from a few exceptions, the rule applies to everyone.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Apart from isolated exceptions, the pattern holds consistently.
   - This case represents a clear exception to established principles.

1. **Noun**: exclusion (U/C, pl: exclusions)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The process or state of excluding or being excluded
   - The action of leaving someone or something out
   
   **Synonyms**: omission, rejection, elimination, barring, prohibition
   
   **Antonyms**: inclusion, admission, incorporation, acceptance, integration
   
   **When to Use**: Describing the act or state of keeping something or someone apart; formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When inclusion is being discussed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - exclusion from something
   - social exclusion
   - policy of exclusion
   - exclusion criteria
   - to the exclusion of
   
   **Root Analysis**: ex- (out) + clus (close) + -ion
   
   **Etymology**: Latin exclusio → Old French exclusion → English exclusion
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - His exclusion from the team hurt, apart from his pride.
   - Apart from one exclusion, everyone was invited.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The exclusion of certain variables was necessary for methodological rigor.
   - Social exclusion, apart from economic factors, affects well-being significantly.

   - significant divergence
   - divergence in opinion
   - divergence between

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's a big divergence in their views, apart from agreeing on basics.
   - Apart from this divergence, they think alike.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The divergence from established methodology requires justification, apart from innovation benefits.
   - Significant divergence between groups was observed, apart from demographic similarities.
