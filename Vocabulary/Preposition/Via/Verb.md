1. **Verb**: transmit
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To send or convey something from one person, place, or thing to another; to broadcast or pass on information, signals, or diseases
   
   **Synonyms**: send, convey, transfer, relay, broadcast
   
   **Antonyms**: receive, retain, block, obstruct
   
   **When to Use**: Use in formal or technical contexts to describe sending signals, data, diseases, or information. Common in telecommunications, medical, and technical writing. Formal register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation where "send" would be more natural. Not appropriate for physical movement of large objects (use "transport" instead).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - transmit data via
   - transmit signals via
   - transmit information via
   - transmit electronically
   - transmit securely
   
   **Root Analysis**: trans- (across) + mit (send) from Latin "mittere"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "trans-" (across) + "mittere" (to send) → "transmittere" → Old French "transmettre" → English "transmit" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The disease can be transmitted via close contact with infected people.
   - Can you transmit the file via email?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Data is transmitted via encrypted channels to ensure confidentiality and integrity.
   - The signal is transmitted via satellite to remote receiving stations worldwide.

1. **Verb**: send
   **Meaning**: 
   - (transitive) To cause to go or be taken to a destination; to dispatch or transmit something
   
   **Synonyms**: dispatch, mail, transmit, deliver, forward
   
   **Antonyms**: receive, keep, retain, hold
   
   **When to Use**: Use in general contexts for transferring items, messages, or information. Universal and appropriate for all registers. Neutral register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when highly technical or formal terminology is required. Not appropriate when emphasizing the receiving end.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - send via email
   - send via post
   - send documents via
   - send a message via
   - send information via
   
   **Root Analysis**: from Old English "sendan" meaning "to send, dispatch"
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "sendan" (to send, dispatch) → Middle English "senden" → Modern English "send" (Germanic origin)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Just send me the details via text when you have them.
   - I'll send the package via courier so it arrives faster.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Applications must be sent via the online portal by the specified deadline.
   - Documents are sent via secure channels in compliance with data protection regulations.

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Verb**: broadcast

   **Antonyms**: conceal, withhold, suppress, restrict

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - broadcast via satellite
   - broadcast via radio
   - broadcast live via
   - broadcast information via
   - broadcast simultaneously via
   
   **Root Analysis**: broad (wide) + cast (throw)
   
   **Etymology**: "broad" (Old English "brād," wide) + "cast" (Old Norse "kasta," to throw) → "broadcast" (18th century agricultural term, 20th century radio/TV meaning)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The game will be broadcast via streaming services and traditional TV.
   - They broadcast emergency alerts via all available channels.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Educational content is broadcast via multiple media platforms to maximize accessibility.
   - The conference proceedings are broadcast via satellite to international participating institutions.

1. **Verb**: relay
   **Meaning**:

   **Synonyms**: pass on, transmit, forward, convey, communicate
   
   **Antonyms**: withhold, retain, block, suppress
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing intermediary transmission or passing along information. Common in communication and electrical contexts. Neutral to formal register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for direct, non-intermediated transmission. Not appropriate when information originates rather than passes through.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - relay via
   - relay information via
   - relay messages via
   - relay signals via
   - relay through via
   
   **Root Analysis**: from French "relayer" meaning "to change horses, to relieve"
   
   **Etymology**: French "relayer" (to change horses at a relay station) → English "relay" (15th century), extended to information transmission (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you relay this message via text to the group?
   - The information was relayed via several intermediaries before reaching us.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Intelligence is relayed via secure communication networks to relevant operational units.
   - Signals are relayed via repeater stations to extend transmission range and coverage.

   **Common Phrases**:

   - distribute via email
   - distribute content via
   - distribute information via
   
   **Root Analysis**: dis- (apart) + tribute (assign) from Latin "tribuere"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "dis-" (apart) + "tribuere" (to assign, allot) → "distribuere" → English "distribute" (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:
