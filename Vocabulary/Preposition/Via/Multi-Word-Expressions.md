1. **Expression**: send via email (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To transmit documents, messages, or information using electronic mail as the delivery method
   - *Fixedness*: strong (highly conventional partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: email, send by email, transmit electronically, send electronically
   
   **Antonyms**: send by post, mail physically, deliver in person
   
   **When to Use**: Use in professional, business, and everyday contexts when specifying email as the transmission medium. Extremely common in modern communication. Neutral register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid redundancy if email is already contextually obvious. Not appropriate for physical item transmission.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - submit via email
   - transmit via email
   - receive via email
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Via" emphasizes the channel/medium aspect of transmission, making it natural with "email" as a transmission method. High frequency of co-occurrence in professional communication contexts.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "send" (Old English) + "via" (Latin "way") + "email" (electronic mail, 20th century coinage)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you send me the photos via email? My phone's not getting messages.
   - I'll send you the directions via email later tonight.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Applications must be sent via email to the designated address by the closing date.
   - Supporting documentation should be sent via email as PDF attachments to ensure compatibility.

1. **Expression**: by means of (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Using a particular method, instrument, or agency to accomplish something; through the use of
   - *Fixedness*: strong (formal fixed expression, functionally equivalent to "via")
   
   **Synonyms**: via, through, by way of, using, through the use of
   
   **Antonyms**: without, lacking, absent
   
   **When to Use**: Use in formal or academic contexts to specify method or instrument. More formal than "via" or "using." Formal register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation where "via" or "using" would sound more natural. Can sound overly formal or stilted in everyday speech.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - through the means of
   - by way of means
   - via means of
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Historical formal expression for specifying instrumentality. Predates "via" in English formal writing. Maintains strong presence in legal, academic, and formal documentation.
   
   **Root Analysis**: "by" (Old English) + "means" (Old English "mǣne," common, shared) + "of" (Old English)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Typically used in formal contexts; sounds unnatural in casual speech*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Quality assurance is achieved by means of systematic testing protocols and peer review.
   - Data collection was conducted by means of structured interviews and survey instruments.
