1. **Adjective**: indirect (comp: more indirect, sup: most indirect)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not going straight to the destination; taking a route that passes through other places or involves intermediate steps (gradable, both attributive and predicative)
   - *(Sense 2)* Not straightforward or explicit in manner or expression; involving intermediate stages or agents
   
   **Synonyms**: roundabout, circuitous, meandering, oblique, mediated
   
   **Antonyms**: direct, straight, immediate, straightforward
   
   **When to Use**: Use to describe routes, methods, or communication that involves intermediate points, stages, or agents. Appropriate in navigation, communication, and procedural contexts. Neutral to formal register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when emphasizing efficiency or directness as positive qualities. Can carry negative connotations of inefficiency or evasiveness in some contexts.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - indirect route
   - indirect flight
   - indirect method
   - indirect approach
   - indirect communication
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + direct (from Latin "directus," straight, guided)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "in-" (not) + "directus" (straight, guided) → Middle French "indirect" → English (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We took an indirect route through the countryside to avoid the highway traffic.
   - It's an indirect flight with a layover in Dubai, but the price was much better.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study examined both direct and indirect effects of the policy intervention.
   - Communication via intermediary channels may be less efficient due to indirect transmission pathways.
