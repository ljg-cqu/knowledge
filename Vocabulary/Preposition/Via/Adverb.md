1. **Adverb**: directly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a straight line or by the shortest route; without stopping or changing direction; without intermediate stages or agents
   - *Position*: Can appear in front, mid, or end position
   
   **Synonyms**: straight, immediately, straightforwardly, right, promptly
   
   **Antonyms**: indirectly, circuitously, roundabout
   
   **When to Use**: Use to emphasize the absence of intermediate points, stages, or agents. Common in navigation, communication, and process descriptions. Neutral register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when describing routes or methods that necessarily involve intermediate steps. Not appropriate when indirect approach is preferred or required.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - travel directly
   - send directly
   - communicate directly
   - go directly
   - connect directly
   
   **Root Analysis**: direct (from Latin "directus," straight) + -ly (adverbial suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "directus" (straight, guided) → "direct" + "-ly" → English (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you send it directly to my email instead of via the group chat?
   - We flew directly to Tokyo—no layovers this time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Data packets are routed directly to the destination server without intermediate processing.
   - Participants may communicate directly with the research team rather than via institutional intermediaries.
