1. **Original Expression**: I remember when we used to hang out all the time  
   **Context**: Casual conversation with old friend about past relationship
   
   **Core Intent**: Express nostalgia for frequent past social interaction that no longer occurs
   
   **Casual**: Remember when we used to hang out all the time? Those were the days!  
   *Characteristics*: Contraction "we'd," informal "hang out," exclamatory tone, nostalgic idiom  
   *Use when*: Close friends, relaxed settings, reminiscing together
   
   **Semi-Formal**: I remember when we used to spend a lot of time together.  
   *Characteristics*: Complete sentence structure, neutral verb "spend time," measured tone  
   *Use when*: Acquaintances reconnecting, professional-turned-personal contexts, polite nostalgia
   
   **Formal**: I recall the period when we maintained frequent social contact.  
   *Characteristics*: Formal verb "recall," precise phrase "maintained frequent social contact," detached tone  
   *Use when*: Very formal correspondence, professional distance required, legal/official contexts
   
   **Humorous**: Back in the golden age when we were practically joined at the hip!  
   *Characteristics*: Hyperbole "golden age," idiom "joined at the hip," exaggerated tone  
   *Use when*: Light-hearted reminiscing, close friends, want to add levity  
   *Avoid when*: Serious or emotional conversations, formal settings
   
   **Lively**: Man, we were inseparable back then! Every single day we'd be together!  
   *Characteristics*: Interjection "Man," emphatic "inseparable," repetition "every single"  
   *Use when*: Enthusiastic reminiscing, energetic conversation, reinforcing bond
   
   **Empathetic**: I miss those days when we spent so much time together. Our friendship meant a lot to me.  
   *Characteristics*: Explicit emotion "I miss," validation "meant a lot," emotional openness  
   *Use when*: Emotionally significant conversation, re-establishing connection, showing vulnerability
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Equal status allows casual; if now different statuses, use semi-formal
   - *Social Distance*: Close friends use casual; drifted apart or reconnecting after years may warrant semi-formal
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Light reminiscing allows casual/humorous; discussing drift in friendship requires empathetic
   - *Cultural Context*: Some cultures maintain formality even among old friends
   - *Medium*: Face-to-face allows more casual; written message may be more formal
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "hang out" in formal contexts sounds jarring and unprofessional
   - Being too formal with close friends creates uncomfortable distance
   - Using humorous register when friend may have hurt feelings about lost connection
   
   **Cultural Notes**: American culture generally allows casual register among old friends; some Asian and European cultures maintain more formality
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Running into old college roommate at café → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual or Lively (close past relationship, spontaneous reunion)
   - **Situation 2**: LinkedIn message to former colleague after 10 years → **Appropriate Variant**: Semi-Formal (professional context, time has passed, written medium)
   - **Situation 3**: Heart-to-heart about why you drifted apart → **Appropriate Variant**: Empathetic (emotional topic, repairing relationship, vulnerability appropriate)
