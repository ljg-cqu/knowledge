1. **Functional Expression**: Do you remember (when/how/what)...? (function: asking about shared memories)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Question form to elicit shared memories or confirm recollection of past events
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Can you recall...?, Do you recall...?, Can you remember...?, Does it ring a bell?
   
   **Antonyms**: Have you forgotten...?, You've forgotten...? (implies negative)
   
   **When to Use**: Use to engage someone in reminiscing or to confirm shared memories; builds rapport; appropriate for personal conversations; works in most social contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May sound accusatory if memory should be obvious; avoid in formal reports or academic writing; can be insensitive regarding traumatic memories
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Do you remember the time when...?
   - Remember when...? (more casual, assumes shared knowledge)
   - You remember..., right?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Opens conversational exchange about past; positive politeness strategy (assumes shared experience); creates bonding through nostalgia
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, I remember (that)! / Of course I remember!
   - Vaguely / Sort of / Not really / I have no memory of that
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Do you remember when we went to that crazy party in college?
   - Do you remember what she said that made everyone laugh?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Do you remember the circumstances surrounding the policy change?
   - Could you recall what transpired during that meeting?

1. **Functional Expression**: I used to... (function: describing past habits/states)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expressing past habitual actions or states that no longer continue; contrasts past with present
   - *Formality*: neutral (works in all registers)
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: In the past, I..., Formerly, I..., I would... (for repeated actions), Back then, I...
   
   **Antonyms**: I now..., These days I..., Currently I...
   
   **When to Use**: Use to describe discontinued habits or past states; emphasizes change between past and present; appropriate for all contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Do not confuse with "I am used to" (be accustomed to) or "I'm getting used to" (becoming accustomed)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I used to [verb] all the time
   - I never used to [verb]
   - I didn't use to [verb]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Establishes contrast between past and present self; can express nostalgia, regret, or personal growth; indirect way to explain current behavior
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Really? What made you stop?
   - Me too! / I never did that
   - Why did you used to...?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I used to skateboard every day after school, but I got too old for it.
   - I never used to like coffee, but now I can't live without it!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization used to maintain significantly different operational procedures.
   - Researchers used to rely exclusively on manual data collection methodologies.

1. **Functional Expression**: As I recall,... / If I recall correctly,... (function: hedging memory claims)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Introducing a statement based on memory while acknowledging possibility of inaccuracy
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedged)
   
   **Synonyms**: As far as I remember..., If memory serves..., To the best of my recollection..., As I remember it...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm certain that..., I know for sure that..., Definitely...
   
   **When to Use**: Use when sharing memories while acknowledging uncertainty; shows epistemic modesty; appropriate for all contexts; particularly useful when accuracy matters
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Weakens authority; avoid if certainty is needed; don't overuse or speaker sounds perpetually uncertain
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - If I recall correctly
   - As I seem to recall
   - If my memory serves me right
   - To the best of my recollection
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Hedging device; protects face if memory proves incorrect; negative politeness (doesn't impose claims); shows appropriate epistemic caution
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, that's right / No, actually it was...
   - Are you sure? I remember it differently
   - Let me check / I think you're right
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As I recall, we agreed to meet at seven, not six.
   - If I recall correctly, you said you'd handle that part of the project.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As I recall, the committee reached consensus on this matter during the previous session.
   - If I recall correctly, the policy was implemented in fiscal year 2018.

1. **Functional Expression**: If I remember rightly/correctly, ... (function: hedged assertion about past)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Making a claim about the past while acknowledging possibility of memory error; epistemic hedge
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedged)
   
   **Synonyms**: If I recall correctly..., As I remember it..., If memory serves..., Unless I'm mistaken...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm certain that..., I definitely remember..., Without doubt...
   
   **When to Use**: Use when offering information from memory while protecting against error; shows appropriate epistemic caution; appropriate for all contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Weakens assertiveness; avoid in situations requiring confident authority; overuse suggests poor memory or lack of confidence
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - If I remember right
   - If I'm remembering this correctly
   - Unless I'm misremembering
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Hedging device protecting face if wrong; negative politeness (doesn't impose claim); invites correction; shows epistemic humility
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, that's correct / Actually, I think it was...
   - Are you sure? / Let me verify that
   - That sounds right to me
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - If I remember rightly, you said you'd pay me back this week.
   - If I remember correctly, that restaurant has closed down.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - If I remember correctly, the regulation was implemented in 2017.
   - If I recall rightly, the committee rejected similar proposals previously.

1. **Functional Expression**: I vaguely remember... / I dimly recall... (function: expressing uncertain memory)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Indicating incomplete or unclear memory of past events; strong epistemic hedge
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (strongly hedged)
   
   **Synonyms**: I sort of remember..., I have a fuzzy memory of..., I barely remember..., It's coming back to me...
   
   **Antonyms**: I clearly remember..., I distinctly recall..., I remember perfectly...
   
   **When to Use**: Use when memory is genuinely unclear but some recollection exists; shows honesty about limitations; appropriate for all contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Suggests very weak memory; avoid when strong recollection is expected or needed; can sound evasive in legal/formal contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I vaguely recall...
   - I have a vague memory of...
   - I dimly remember...
   - My memory is hazy, but...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Strong hedging; manages expectations about accuracy; protects face if wrong; shows epistemic honesty; may invite others to fill in details
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Let me help you remember... / Do you remember when...?
   - Don't worry about it / It's okay if you don't remember
   - That's all right; maybe someone else remembers
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I vaguely remember meeting her at some party, but I'm not sure when.
   - I dimly recall something about a deadline, but I can't remember the details.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I vaguely recall that issue being discussed during previous sessions.
   - The witness reported dimly recalling the vehicle's color but provided no other details.

1. **Functional Expression**: Whatever happened to...? (function: inquiring about past people/things)

   **Common Variations**: 
   - Whatever became of...?

   **Pragmatic Notes**: Opens conversation about past; can express nostalgia or curiosity; assumes shared knowledge of subject; may lead to story-sharing
   
   **Response Patterns**:

   **Examples [Casual]**:

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Functional Expression**: If only I had/hadn't... (function: expressing regret about past actions)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expressing wish that past had been different; counterfactual regret
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (about emotion), indirect (about request for change)
   
   **Synonyms**: I wish I had/hadn't..., I regret that I..., If I could do it over..., I should have/shouldn't have...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm glad I..., I don't regret..., I'd do it again the same way
   
   **When to Use**: Use to express regret or wish past had been different; counterfactual construction; appropriate for personal reflection in all contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound like dwelling on unchangeable past; avoid excessive use (sounds defeatist); inappropriate if regret isn't genuine
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - If only I'd...
   - If I'd only...
   - I wish I had/hadn't...
   - Grammatically: past perfect tense required
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Expresses regret using counterfactual; acknowledges past cannot change; can seek sympathy or share lesson; vulnerable self-disclosure
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - You couldn't have known / Don't be so hard on yourself
   - Learn from it and move on / What's done is done
   - At least now you know for next time
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - If only I hadn't eaten that last piece of pizza—I feel so sick now!
   - If only I'd studied harder in school, my life would be different.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - If only the organization had implemented these measures sooner, substantial difficulties might have been avoided.
   - If only we had had access to this data earlier, our conclusions would have been more robust.
