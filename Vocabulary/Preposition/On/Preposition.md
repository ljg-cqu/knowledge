1. **Preposition**: on (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Positioned above and supported by the surface of something, making physical contact with it
   - *(Temporal)* Used to indicate a specific day or date
   - *(Abstract)* Connected with or about a particular subject or topic; in a state or condition; dependent on; showing relationship or association
   
   **Synonyms**: upon (formal, spatial), about (topic), concerning (topic), regarding (topic), atop (spatial)
   
   **Antonyms**: off (spatial, state), under (position), beneath (position)
   
   **When to Use**: Use "on" for surface contact (book on table), specific days (on Monday, on Christmas), topics/subjects (book on history), states/conditions (on fire, on duty), and dependency relationships (rely on)
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use "on" for enclosed spaces (use "in": in the room, not on the room), general time periods (use "in": in March, not on March), or point locations without surface contact (use "at": at the corner)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: on the table, on Monday, on time, on purpose, on vacation, on the phone
   - **Verb + prep**: rely on, depend on, focus on, work on, concentrate on, insist on, comment on, decide on
   - **Adjective + prep**: keen on, based on, focused on, dependent on, intent on
   - **Noun + prep**: impact on, effect on, influence on, attack on, comment on, focus on
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "on" (in, into, upon, onto)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "on, an" (in, into, upon) → Middle English unchanged → Modern English "on". Related to German "an" and Dutch "aan". One of the oldest and most fundamental prepositions in English.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I left my keys on the kitchen counter this morning.
   - We're meeting on Friday at the coffee shop downtown.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee will convene on the 15th of December to discuss the proposal.
   - This research focuses on the environmental impact of renewable energy technologies.
