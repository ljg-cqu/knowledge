# Unto - Related Adverbs

1. **Adverb**: forever (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For all time in the future; endlessly; without end.
   - Position: usually end position or after the verb; can also appear mid‑clause for emphasis.
   
   **Synonyms**: eternally, always, endlessly, perpetually, for evermore, without end
   
   **Antonyms**: temporarily, briefly, for a while, for now, for the moment
   
   **When to Use**: Commonly used with "unto" in hymns and prayers ("glory be unto Him forever", "praise unto His name forever") to emphasise unending duration of praise, mercy, or rule.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In precise time statements, use specific limits instead ("for ten years", "until 2030"). "Forever" with "unto" sounds very religious or poetic.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - glory be unto Him forever
   - praise unto Your name forever
   - mercy unto you forever
   - His truth endureth unto all generations forever
   
   **Root Analysis**: for (preposition of duration) + ever (at any time) → fused into one adverb
   
   **Etymology**: Old English *fore ǣfre* (for ever, through all time) → contracted spellings → modern "forever/for ever"; deeply embedded in English hymn and psalm language.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The chorus keeps repeating, "All glory be unto You forever." 
   - They promised to stay grateful unto those who helped them forever.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The dedication closes with the words, "Praise be unto His name forever." 
   - In the creed, the kingdom is said to endure unto all ages, world without end, forever.

1. **Adverb**: evermore (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For all future time; from now on and always; poetic or literary form.
   - Position: typically clause‑final or after a verb of being.
   
   **Synonyms**: forever, always, eternally, from this time forth
   
   **Antonyms**: never again, no longer, temporarily, for a season
   
   **When to Use**: Strongly associated with religious songs and archaic style ("blessed be He unto ages evermore"). Use when you want a hymnic, elevated tone around commitments or praise directed unto someone.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in neutral reports, emails, or academic prose; it sounds deliberately old‑fashioned. Prefer "always" or "forever" there.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - unto Him be glory evermore
   - peace be unto you evermore
   - mercy unto you and peace and love be multiplied evermore
   - remembered unto all generations evermore
   
   **Root Analysis**: ever (at any time) + more (further, in addition) → lexicalised as a single adverb
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English *evyrmore* → used heavily in religious verse; often appears together with "unto" to reinforce sense of endless duration.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The poem ends, "May kindness be unto you evermore." 
   - They joked that her promise would bind him unto that task evermore.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The blessing invokes peace and good will unto the people, now and evermore.
   - In older charters, duty is pledged unto the crown for evermore.

1. **Adverb**: continually (type: frequency)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Very often, repeatedly, or without interruption over a period of time.
   - Position: typically mid‑position (before main verb) or end position.
   
   **Synonyms**: constantly, repeatedly, repeatedly, without ceasing, again and again, perpetually
   
   **Antonyms**: occasionally, rarely, intermittently, sporadically
   
   **When to Use**: With "unto" in devotional formulas ("pray unto Him continually", "offer thanks unto God continually") to emphasise repeated or ongoing direction of an act toward a recipient.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: If action is genuinely periodic, "regularly" or "from time to time" may be more accurate. "Continually" suggests sustained or frequent repetition.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pray unto God continually
   - give thanks unto Him continually
   - offer praise unto His name continually
   - look unto Him continually
   
   **Root Analysis**: continual (from Latin *continuus* - unbroken) + ‑ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: From Latin *continuare* (to join together) via Old French; used in many older English translations: "offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks unto His name".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They kept lifting their worries unto God continually, instead of carrying them alone.
   - The song talks about sending praise unto heaven continually.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The rule prescribes that gratitude be expressed unto the Giver continually, in word and deed.
   - The narrative portrays a people who cry unto the Lord continually in their distress.

1. **Adverb**: diligently (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that shows care, effort, and persistence; with close attention and hard work.
   - Position: usually mid‑position (before main verb) or end position.
   
   **Synonyms**: carefully, earnestly, assiduously, conscientiously, industriously
   
   **Antonyms**: carelessly, lazily, negligently, casually
   
   **When to Use**: Particularly in moral or spiritual instructions ("seek Him diligently", "teach these things diligently unto your children") where the action is directed unto someone.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For simple quick tasks where extra effort is not expected, "quickly" or "briefly" may be more natural. "Diligently unto" belongs in serious, effort‑focused contexts.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - teach them diligently unto your children
   - seek the truth diligently unto understanding
   - serve diligently unto your calling
   - attend diligently unto this work
   
   **Root Analysis**: diligent (from Latin *diligens* – careful, attentive) + ‑ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *diligere* (to value highly, love) → *diligens* (careful) → Old French *diligent* → English; often paired with instructions directed unto someone.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The story reminds parents to teach good habits diligently unto their kids.
   - He tried to pass on his skills diligently unto the younger team members.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The statute requires officials to attend diligently unto the welfare of those in their charge.
   - The manual urges leaders to communicate expectations diligently unto all who serve under them.

1. **Adverb**: earnestly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a serious, sincere, and intense way; with deep feeling and honest purpose.
   - Position: often mid‑position before the main verb or at the end of the clause.
   
   **Synonyms**: seriously, sincerely, fervently, intently, wholeheartedly
   
   **Antonyms**: casually, lightly, half‑heartedly, superficially
   
   **When to Use**: Typical with verbs that take "unto" in prayers or appeals ("cry unto Him earnestly", "entreat mercy earnestly unto"), highlighting deep sincerity directed toward a listener.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For playful or lighthearted requests; "kindly" or "politely" may be more fitting. "Earnestly" suggests weight and intensity.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - cry earnestly unto the Lord
   - pray earnestly unto God
   - entreat favour earnestly unto the ruler
   - call earnestly unto those in power
   
   **Root Analysis**: earnest (serious, intense) + ‑ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English *eornost* (zeal, seriousness) → Middle English *ernest* → adjective/adverb forms; retained especially in prayer language ("pray most earnestly unto God").
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They spoke earnestly unto their friend, hoping he would finally listen.
   - In the scene the child cries earnestly unto her mother for help.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The letter urges the leaders to consider the plea set forth unto them most earnestly.
   - The congregation was called to seek wisdom earnestly unto the One who gives generously.

1. **Adverb**: humbly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a modest way, without pride; recognising one’s limits and dependence.
   - Position: usually mid‑position (before main verb) or end position.
   
   **Synonyms**: modestly, meekly, lowly, respectfully
   
   **Antonyms**: proudly, arrogantly, boastfully, haughtily
   
   **When to Use**: Often used with "unto" in formulas of address ("I come humbly unto You", "we confess humbly unto our neighbours") to highlight attitude toward the person addressed.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In contexts where confidence or authority is the focus, "frankly" or "clearly" may be more suitable.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - come humbly unto God
   - confess humbly unto one another
   - bow humbly unto the king
   - submit humbly unto the decision
   
   **Root Analysis**: humble (from Latin *humilis* – lowly) + ‑ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: From Latin *humilis* (on the ground) via Old French *humble*; adverb "humbly" appears frequently in prayers and public confessions directed unto someone.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He came humbly unto his parents and admitted he was wrong.
   - The apology was written humbly unto everyone who had been hurt.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The petitioners came humbly unto the council, seeking redress for longstanding grievances.
   - In the liturgy the people confess humbly unto God and ask for pardon.
