# Unto - Multi-Word Expressions

1. **Expression**: glory be unto God (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A set formula giving praise and honour to God; often used in doxologies and hymns.
   - *For idioms*: Literal meaning vs. Figurative/Idiomatic meaning
     - Literal: May glory (honour, praise) belong to God.
     - Figurative/Idiomatic: We openly and completely ascribe all credit and praise to God.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very stable liturgical formula)
   
   **Synonyms**: praise be to God, all glory to God, blessed be God, thanks be to God
   
   **Antonyms**: (conceptual) curse upon, blame laid on, reproach toward
   
   **When to Use**: In religious songs, prayers, or testimonies when expressing worship; strongly marked as Christian hymn language.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In secular settings like business reports or academic writing; sounds explicitly religious and stylistically out of place.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - glory be unto the Father, and unto the Son, and unto the Holy Ghost
   - all glory be unto God alone
   - to God be the glory, great things He hath done
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "Glory" as honour/praise is verbally directed "unto" God as recipient; centuries of liturgical use have frozen the wording into a recognisable formula.
   
   **Root Analysis**: glory (Latin *gloria* – praise, fame) + be (copular verb) + unto (to, toward) + God
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The old choir piece ends with everyone singing, "Glory be unto God in the highest!" 
   - After everything worked out, she whispered, "Glory be unto God for this rescue." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The doxology concludes, "Glory be unto God, world without end. Amen." 
   - In his sermon he repeated the line, glory be unto God, to underline that success was not their own.

1. **Expression**: peace be unto you (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal blessing or greeting wishing inner and outer peace to the person addressed.
   - *For idioms*: Literal meaning vs. Figurative/Idiomatic meaning
     - Literal: May peace exist toward you.
     - Figurative/Idiomatic: I sincerely wish you well‑being, safety, and wholeness.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (classic biblical greeting)
   
   **Synonyms**: peace be with you, may you be at peace, shalom, rest well, blessings of peace to you
   
   **Antonyms**: (conceptual) woe be unto you, trouble upon you, unrest for you
   
   **When to Use**: In religious gatherings, letters, or reflective writing when offering a reverent blessing, particularly in Christian or Jewish‑influenced contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In everyday casual greetings where "Hi" or "Good morning" is sufficient; "peace be unto you" can sound theatrical if used without context.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - peace be unto you and your house
   - peace be unto all who are in Christ Jesus
   - grace and peace be unto you
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: English translators rendered Semitic blessing formulas using "unto" to mark the direction of peace toward the hearer.
   
   **Root Analysis**: peace (Latin *pax* – peace) + be (copula) + unto (to, toward) + you
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He signed the card, "Peace be unto you, my friend," half‑serious and half‑playful.
   - After the tense meeting she texted, "Good night—peace be unto you and your family." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The benediction closed with, "Peace be unto you all, now and evermore." 
   - The letter opened in scriptural style: "Grace and peace be unto you from God our Father." 

1. **Expression**: woe be unto [someone] (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal: May trouble or misery come upon that person.
   - Figurative/Idiomatic: A strong warning or denunciation that serious consequences will fall on someone who acts wrongly.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (classic warning formula)
   
   **Synonyms**: shame on, disaster to, there will be serious trouble for, watch out for
   
   **Antonyms**: blessed be, blessed are, peace be unto
   
   **When to Use**: Only in highly rhetorical, biblical, or deliberately dramatic language when warning about moral or practical consequences of an action.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In ordinary disagreements, it will sound exaggerated or mocking; avoid in serious professional communication.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - woe be unto him that…
   - woe be unto them which…
   - woe be unto the one who trusts in violence
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "Woe" personifies misfortune; "unto" directs that misfortune toward a target; the phrase crystallised as a prophetic warning formula.
   
   **Root Analysis**: woe (exclamation of sorrow) + be (copula) + unto (to, toward)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He laughed and said, "Woe be unto whoever touches my snacks without asking!" 
   - The narrator jokes, "Woe be unto the one who forgets their anniversary." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The pamphlet thunders, "Woe be unto those who call evil good and good evil." 
   - In the drama, the prophet declares, "Woe be unto the city that has forgotten justice." 

1. **Expression**: faithful unto death (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Remaining loyal and obedient right up to the point of dying; never turning back.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (well‑known biblical collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: loyal to the end, unwavering unto death, steadfast even in death
   
   **Antonyms**: faithless in the trial, turning back in the end, giving up under pressure
   
   **When to Use**: In storytelling, memorials, or spiritual exhortation where extreme commitment or martyrdom is in view.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For minor commitments (study plans, diets); would sound absurdly intense.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - be faithful unto death
   - remained faithful unto death
   - faithful even unto death
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Faithful" + "unto" + the extreme limit "death" creates a strong image of loyalty that reaches its ultimate boundary.
   
   **Root Analysis**: faithful (full of faith) + unto (up to) + death
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The movie paints the sidekick as faithful unto death, sticking with the hero no matter what.
   - Their grandparents had a quiet, faithful‑unto‑death kind of love.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The epitaph honours those who were faithful unto death in defence of their homeland.
   - The text promises a crown of life to those who remain faithful unto death.

1. **Expression**: unto the ends of the earth (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To the farthest possible places; to every corner of the world.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (poetic formula)
   
   **Synonyms**: everywhere, to the very ends of the earth, to the farthest reaches, throughout the whole world
   
   **Antonyms**: in one place only, locally, narrowly, within limited bounds
   
   **When to Use**: In speeches, literature, or hymns when describing global reach or total scope ("carry this message unto the ends of the earth").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For literal travel plans; say "around the world" or "to many countries" instead.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - go unto the ends of the earth
   - send light unto the ends of the earth
   - carry the word unto the ends of the earth
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Ends of the earth" is an established metaphor for the world’s limits; "unto" stresses motion or outreach toward those farthest points.
   
   **Root Analysis**: unto (up to) + the ends (limits) + of the earth (world)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She swore she would chase that dream unto the ends of the earth if she had to.
   - The song talks about love that reaches unto the ends of the earth.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The mission is described as carrying hope unto the ends of the earth.
   - In the charter they pledge to take education and care unto the ends of the earth.

1. **Expression**: unto this day (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Up to the present time; still true or still continuing.
   - *Fixedness*: strong in historical narrative
   
   **Synonyms**: to this day, up to now, even now, still
   
   **Antonyms**: no longer, not anymore, only in the past, formerly
   
   **When to Use**: In storytelling or historical reflection when showing that an effect, custom, or memory continues into the present.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very informal speech, "to this day" is more common; "unto this day" feels biblical or archaic.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - remaineth unto this day
   - standeth unto this day
   - is called by that name unto this day
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Older translators used "unto" to mark temporal range from past event up to the narrative present.
   
   **Root Analysis**: unto (up to) + this (demonstrative) + day (period of time)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The story says the old stone still stands there unto this day.
   - That habit has stayed with him unto this day.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The inscription is reported to remain legible unto this day.
   - Many customs described in the chronicle continue unto this day.

1. **Expression**: a blessing unto [someone/something] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Something that brings help, benefit, or joy to a particular person, group, or place.
   - *Fixedness*: medium (pattern "X is a blessing unto Y")
   
   **Synonyms**: a gift to, a boon for, a great help to, a grace for, a benefit to
   
   **Antonyms**: a curse upon, a burden to, a blight on, a harm to
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasising how strongly good effects are directed toward a recipient, especially in reflective or spiritual tone ("be a blessing unto your neighbours").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For mundane advantages (discounts, small perks), the phrase can feel overblown.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - be a blessing unto others
   - become a blessing unto the nations
   - prove a blessing unto many
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Blessing" implies a good thing given; "unto" highlights the direction toward those who receive it.
   
   **Root Analysis**: blessing (favour, good gift) + unto (to, toward)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That old teacher was a blessing unto generations of students.
   - She wants her work to be a blessing unto people who feel unseen.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The fund is intended to be a lasting blessing unto the communities it serves.
   - Their efforts became a blessing unto all who depended on that river for life.

1. **Expression**: grace be unto you (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal greeting or blessing wishing unearned favour, kindness, and spiritual help to the hearer.
   - *For idioms*: Literal meaning vs. Figurative/Idiomatic meaning
     - Literal: May grace (favour) exist toward you.
     - Figurative/Idiomatic: I sincerely wish you divine kindness and help.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (epistolary formula)
   
   **Synonyms**: may you receive grace, may favour rest on you, blessings be upon you
   
   **Antonyms**: (conceptual) condemnation be upon you, judgment upon you
   
   **When to Use**: In letters, sermons, or prayers that echo New Testament epistles ("Grace be unto you, and peace").
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In secular professional correspondence; "Best regards" or "Kind wishes" is more appropriate there.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - grace be unto you, and peace
   - grace and peace be multiplied unto you
   - grace be unto all them that love our Lord
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Grace" is treated as something that can rest or move toward a person; "unto" marks them as the recipient.
   
   **Root Analysis**: grace (Latin *gratia* – favour) + be (copula) + unto (to, toward) + you
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He sometimes signs his notes, "Grace be unto you and your family today." 
   - At the end of the retreat they said, "Grace be unto you as you go back to everyday life." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The circular letter opens, "Grace be unto you, and peace, from God our Father." 
   - The benediction extends grace be unto all who labour unseen for the common good.
