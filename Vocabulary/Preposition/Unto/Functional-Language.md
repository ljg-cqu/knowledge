# Unto - Functional Language

1. **Functional Expression**: Peace be unto you (function: blessing / greeting)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal or spiritual way to wish someone inner calm, safety, and well‑being.
   - *Formality*: formal/ceremonial
   - *Directness*: direct (explicit blessing)
   
   **Synonyms**: Peace be with you, May you be at peace, I wish you peace, Shalom, Rest well
   
   **Antonyms**: Woe be unto you, Trouble upon you, May unrest follow you (opposite‑effect expressions)
   
   **When to Use**: Appropriate in prayers, religious services, condolence messages, or very reflective writing when you want a solemn blessing directed unto the listener.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In everyday quick greetings where "Hi" or "Morning" is natural; in casual business emails it sounds out of place or overly religious.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Peace be unto you and your house.
   - Peace be unto all who hear these words.
   - May peace be unto your heart and mind.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals care and goodwill, often at the close of a meeting or letter. Because it is formulaic, it carries cultural weight beyond the literal words.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - And also with you.
   - Thank you, and peace be unto you too.
   - Amen.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - After the long, heavy talk he simply said, "Peace be unto you, my friend," and left.
   - She signed her message, "Peace be unto you tonight," knowing her friend was anxious.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The presider closed the gathering with, "Peace be unto you all, now and evermore." 
   - In the liturgy the congregation hears, "Peace be unto this house, and to all who dwell therein." 

1. **Functional Expression**: Grace be unto you (function: epistolary blessing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal opening that wishes undeserved favour, kindness, and spiritual help to the recipients.
   - *Formality*: highly formal, scriptural
   - *Directness*: direct blessing
   
   **Synonyms**: May grace rest on you, May you receive favour, Blessings be upon you
   
   **Antonyms**: Judgment be upon you, Condemnation upon you (opposite‑effect expressions)
   
   **When to Use**: In letters or speeches written in a biblical or devotional register, especially to faith communities.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In secular professional contexts (e.g., audit reports, project updates); the religious tone may be inappropriate.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Grace be unto you, and peace.
   - Grace and peace be multiplied unto you.
   - Grace be unto all those who love the truth.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Establishes an atmosphere of goodwill and shared faith; echoes New Testament epistles and signals that what follows is pastoral or spiritual.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - We receive that.
   - Thank you; grace be unto you as well.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He ended his note to the small group, "Grace be unto you all as you travel." 
   - On the card she wrote, "Grace be unto your family in this season." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The circular begins, "Grace be unto you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ." 
   - The closing line reads, "Grace be unto all who labour in hidden places for the sake of others." 

1. **Functional Expression**: Woe be unto [someone] (function: warning / denunciation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A strong prophetic‑style warning that serious trouble or punishment will fall on someone who acts wrongly.
   - *Formality*: highly rhetorical, archaic
   - *Directness*: very direct and forceful
   
   **Synonyms**: Shame on, Beware, Disaster will come on, There will be serious trouble for
   
   **Antonyms**: Blessed are, Peace be unto, Congratulations to (opposite‑effect)
   
   **When to Use**: In stylised rhetoric, literature, or religious preaching when you intentionally adopt a prophetic tone.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In everyday feedback or disagreement; it will sound theatrical or sarcastic and can damage trust.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Woe be unto him that…
   - Woe be unto them which…
   - Woe be unto the one who trusts in violence.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Carries moral judgment and threat of consequences; easily perceived as harsh or condemning, so use with caution even in sermons.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [Typically none; listeners may respond with silence or discomfort.]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He laughed and said, "Woe be unto whoever eats the last slice of cake without asking!" 
   - The comic uses "woe be unto you" to parody over‑serious warnings.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The pamphlet thunders, "Woe be unto those who profit from the suffering of the poor." 
   - In the play a prophet cries, "Woe be unto this city if it will not turn from injustice." 

1. **Functional Expression**: I dedicate this work unto [recipient] (function: dedicating / offering)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal statement that a piece of work, effort, or life is offered in honour of someone.
   - *Formality*: formal, ceremonial
   - *Directness*: direct declaration
   
   **Synonyms**: I dedicate this to, I offer this in honour of, This is for, I present this to
   
   **Antonyms**: I withhold this from, I refuse to honour, I deny recognition to
   
   **When to Use**: In book forewords, speeches, memorials, or creative works where you wish to explicitly honour a person or group.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For routine tasks (status reports, code commits); "dedicate unto" would sound inflated.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - This is dedicated unto all who…
   - We dedicate this work unto our teachers.
   - I dedicate this piece unto the memory of my parents.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals gratitude, respect, and sometimes grief; frames the work as a gift reaching toward the named recipient.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That means a lot, thank you.
   - We are honoured by this dedication.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - At the small concert she said, "I dedicate this song unto my grandmother." 
   - He wrote, "This project is dedicated unto everyone who refused to give up on me." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The preface opens, "I dedicate this work unto my mentors, whose patience shaped my craft." 
   - The plaque reads, "Dedicated unto the memory of those who gave their lives in service." 

1. **Functional Expression**: I commend you unto [God/your care] (function: entrusting / commending)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To formally place someone in the care, protection, or guidance of another person or of God.
   - *Formality*: formal, pastoral
   - *Directness*: direct but gentle
   
   **Synonyms**: I entrust you to, I place you in the care of, I leave you in the hands of
   
   **Antonyms**: I withdraw my support from you, I abandon you to yourself (opposite‑effect)
   
   **When to Use**: At farewells, blessings, or formal send‑offs, especially in religious settings.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual goodbyes; "Take care" or "All the best" is more natural there.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I commend you unto God and to the word of His grace.
   - We commend this child unto your keeping.
   - I commend this case unto your wise judgment.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Expresses both trust in the recipient (or in God) and continuing concern for the person being commended.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you; that means a lot.
   - We receive that blessing.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As they parted he said softly, "I commend you unto His care," and hugged him.
   - She wrote, "For now I commend you unto the capable hands of your new mentor." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The elder concluded, "Brethren, I commend you unto God and to the word of His grace." 
   - In the letter he writes, "I commend this matter unto your careful and impartial consideration." 

1. **Functional Expression**: I commit this matter unto your judgment (function: transferring responsibility / seeking decision)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To hand a decision or case over to someone else’s authority, signalling trust in their evaluation.
   - *Formality*: formal/official
   - *Directness*: direct but respectful
   
   **Synonyms**: I leave this to your judgment, I place this in your hands, I ask you to decide, I submit this to your ruling
   
   **Antonyms**: I overrule you, I will decide this myself, I refuse your judgment
   
   **When to Use**: In formal letters, hearings, or mediated conversations where you want another party to decide the outcome.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In casual disagreements between friends; "You decide" is usually enough.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We commit this cause unto your judgment.
   - I commit the choice unto your wisdom.
   - Let us commit the dispute unto an impartial arbitrator.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Shows deference to another’s authority while still framing the issue clearly; often used to close a presentation of facts.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I accept this responsibility.
   - We will consider the matter carefully.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - After explaining everything, he shrugged and said, "I commit this matter unto your judgment—do what you think is best." 
   - They told the mediator, "We commit the final decision unto your experience." 
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The brief concludes, "We respectfully commit this matter unto the court’s judgment." 
   - After reviewing the evidence, counsel stated, "We now commit the case unto the jury."
