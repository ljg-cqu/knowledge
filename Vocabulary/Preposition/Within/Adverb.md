1. **Adverb**: strictly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an exact, precise, or rigorous manner; with firm adherence to rules, limits, or standards; without exception or deviation
   
   **Synonyms**: precisely, exactly, rigorously, firmly, rigidly
   
   **Antonyms**: loosely, flexibly, approximately, roughly, leniently
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing rigid adherence to limits, rules, or boundaries; appropriate in formal, legal, and regulatory contexts; common when discussing enforcement or compliance; often precedes "within" to emphasize precision
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual contexts where it might sound overly formal or rigid; not appropriate when flexibility is valued; can sound harsh when discussing human behavior
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - strictly within limits
   - strictly within bounds
   - strictly within guidelines
   - strictly adhere to
   - strictly enforce
   
   **Root Analysis**: strict (from Latin "strictus" meaning "drawn tight") + -ly (adverb suffix) → "in a tight, exact manner"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "strictus" (tight, close, strait) from "stringere" (to draw tight) → Old French "strict" → English "strict" + "-ly" → "strictly"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to stay strictly within budget this time.
   - Keep your comments strictly within professional boundaries.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All experimental procedures must remain strictly within the parameters approved by the ethics committee.
   - The organization operates strictly within the legal framework established by regulatory authorities.

1. **Adverb**: comfortably (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that provides ease, relaxation, or freedom from difficulty; with margin to spare; without strain or excessive effort
   - With sufficient space, resources, or time; not at the extreme limit
   
   **Synonyms**: easily, adequately, sufficiently, conveniently, without difficulty
   
   **Antonyms**: uncomfortably, barely, narrowly, with difficulty, tightly
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing that something is within limits with room to spare; appropriate in both casual and formal contexts; common when discussing budgets, space, or capabilities; implies ease rather than strain
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing situations that are barely within limits; not appropriate when emphasizing tightness of constraints
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - comfortably within limits
   - comfortably within budget
   - comfortably within range
   - comfortably fit within
   - comfortably afford
   
   **Root Analysis**: comfort (from Latin "confortare" meaning "to strengthen") + -able + -ly → "in a manner that provides ease"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin "confortare" (to strengthen much) from "com-" (intensive) + "fortis" (strong) → Old French "conforter" → English "comfort" → "comfortable" → "comfortably"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We can comfortably stay within budget with this plan.
   - The car fits comfortably within the garage.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed expenditures fall comfortably within the allocated budgetary constraints.
   - All measurements remained comfortably within acceptable tolerances throughout testing.

1. **Adverb**: barely (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Only just; almost not; by the smallest margin; with very little to spare

   **Synonyms**: scarcely, hardly, just, narrowly, only just
   
   **Antonyms**: easily, comfortably, amply, clearly, well
   
   **When to Use**: Use when emphasizing that something is only just within limits with no room to spare; appropriate when highlighting tightness of constraints or near-violation of boundaries; common in both casual and formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when something is clearly within limits; not appropriate when emphasizing comfort or ease
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - barely within limits
   - barely within budget
   - barely within range
   - barely visible
   - barely audible
   
   **Root Analysis**: bare (from Old English "bær" meaning "naked, uncovered") + -ly (adverb suffix) → "in an exposed, minimal manner"
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "bær" (naked, bare) → Middle English "bare" → "barely" (by small margin, only just), sense developed from "openly, barely concealed" to "scarcely, only just"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're barely within budget on this project.
   - He barely stayed within the legal speed limit.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The experimental results barely remained within acceptable confidence intervals.
   - The proposal's scope barely fits within the committee's designated authority.

   **Etymology**: Latin "salvus" (uninjured, safe) → Old French "sauf" → Middle English "safe" → "safely" (in a safe manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Keep your speed safely within the limit.
   - Make sure everything stays safely within the designated area.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All radiation exposure levels remained safely within regulatory guidelines throughout the procedure.

   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when something is clearly within limits with room to spare; not appropriate when emphasizing comfort or ease
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - narrowly within limits
   - narrowly within range
   - narrowly avoid
   - narrowly escape
   - narrowly defined within
   
   **Root Analysis**: narrow (from Old English "nearu" meaning "narrow, confined") + -ly (adverb suffix) → "in a confined, restricted manner"
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "nearu" (narrow, confined, constricted) → Middle English "narwe" → "narrow" + "-ly" → "narrowly" (by small margin, barely)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We narrowly stayed within budget—barely made it!
   - He narrowly kept within the speed limit.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The experimental values remained narrowly within acceptable confidence intervals.
   - The proposal narrowly fits within the scope of the committee's jurisdiction.
