1. **Functional Expression**: Upon [your/our/the] [noun], [action will occur] (function: stating procedural timing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal way to indicate when an action will be taken; specifies the trigger or condition
   - *Formality*: formal to very formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (uses procedural language to soften directness)
   
   **Synonyms**: When [noun occurs], As soon as [noun occurs], After [noun], Following [noun]
   
   **Antonyms**: Before [noun], Prior to [noun], Without [noun]
   
   **When to Use**: Official communications, procedural instructions, formal business correspondence, legal documents
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "when" or "after" are more natural; overly informal contexts where it would sound pretentious
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Upon receipt of [document], we will [action]
   - Upon completion of [task], [next step]
   - Upon approval, [implementation will begin]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Creates professional distance and formality; signals established procedures rather than personal discretion; face-saving through institutional language
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Acknowledgment: "Understood. I will proceed accordingly."
   - Confirmation: "I confirm receipt and will act upon your instructions."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Upon arrival, just text me and I'll come get you.
   - Upon finishing the test, you can leave.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Upon receipt of your application, we will commence the review process.
   - Upon completion of the investigation, findings will be reported to the board.

1. **Functional Expression**: I hate to impose upon [you/your time], but... (function: polite requesting with apology)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Apologetic preface to a request; acknowledges potential burden while asking for help
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedged request with apology)
   
   **Synonyms**: I don't want to bother you, but..., I'm sorry to trouble you, but..., I apologize for asking, but..., May I impose on you to...
   
   **Antonyms**: I need you to..., You must..., Do this (direct command without apology)
   
   **When to Use**: When making requests that might inconvenience someone; showing awareness of imposing; maintaining politeness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: With close friends in casual settings (too formal); when direct requests are expected; emergency situations requiring immediate action
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I don't want to impose upon you, but...
   - Sorry to impose upon your time, but...
   - I hope I'm not imposing upon you by asking...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy (recognizing imposition); face-saving for both speaker and hearer; signals respect for hearer's autonomy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Not at all, I'm happy to help."
   - Declining politely: "I'd love to help, but I'm afraid I can't right now."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I hate to impose upon you, but could you watch my dog this weekend?
   - Don't want to impose upon your evening, but I could really use some advice.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I apologize for imposing upon your valuable time, but I require clarification on several matters.
   - I hope not to impose upon your schedule, but your expertise would be invaluable to our project.

   **Response Patterns**:

   **Examples [Casual]**:

   - We call upon all member states to honor their treaty obligations.
