1. **Verb**: depend
   **Meaning**: 
   - To be controlled or determined by; to be contingent on
   - To rely on someone or something for support or help
   - (Transitive/Intransitive) Usually followed by "on" or "upon"
   
   **Synonyms**: rely, count on, hinge on, rest on, be contingent on
   
   **Antonyms**: control, determine, be independent of, dictate
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and informal contexts; expressing conditionality or reliance; "depend upon" is more formal than "depend on"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing independent or self-sufficient situations
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - depend upon circumstances
   - depend upon factors
   - depend upon conditions
   - can depend upon
   
   **Root Analysis**: de- (down, from) + pend (hang)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin dependere (to hang from) → metaphorical extension to reliance or conditionality
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Whether we go depends upon the weather.
   - You can always depend upon me.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The outcome depends upon multiple interrelated variables.
   - Success depends upon sustained commitment and adequate resources.

1. **Verb**: rely
   **Meaning**: 
   - To depend on someone or something with confidence; to trust
   - To be dependent on something for support or help
   - (Intransitive) Always followed by "on" or "upon"
   
   **Synonyms**: depend on, count on, bank on, trust, lean on
   
   **Antonyms**: distrust, doubt, be independent of
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing trust or confidence in dependence; slightly more formal with "upon"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing distrust or independence
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - rely upon experience
   - rely upon expertise
   - heavily rely upon
   - can't rely upon
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + ligare (bind) → "bind back"
   
   **Etymology**: Latin religare (to bind fast) → Old French relier → English rely (trust based on binding connection)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I rely upon my friends for support.
   - You can't rely upon the bus being on time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The methodology relies upon established theoretical frameworks.
   - Institutional effectiveness relies upon continuous assessment and improvement.
