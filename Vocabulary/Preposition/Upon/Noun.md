1. **Noun**: arrival (C, pl: arrivals)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action or process of arriving; reaching a destination
   - The time at which someone or something arrives
   - A person or thing that has arrived
   
   **Synonyms**: coming, appearance, advent, entrance, landing
   
   **Antonyms**: departure, leaving, exit, going
   
   **When to Use**: Common in travel contexts, formal instructions, procedural language; frequently paired with "upon"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing departures or ongoing presence
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - upon arrival
   - arrival time
   - safe arrival
   - shortly after arrival
   
   **Root Analysis**: arrive (ar-: to + rive: shore) + -al (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French arriver (to reach shore) → Latin ad ripam (to the shore) → act of reaching destination
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Call me upon arrival so I know you're safe.
   - His arrival was a pleasant surprise.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Passengers must present documentation upon arrival at the terminal.
   - Upon arrival, all participants shall register at the designated location.
