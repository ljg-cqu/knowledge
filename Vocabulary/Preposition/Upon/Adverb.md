1. **Adverb**: immediately (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At once; without any delay; instantly
   - Directly; with nothing in between
   - *Position*: Can appear in front, mid, or end position; commonly precedes "upon" constructions
   
   **Synonyms**: instantly, at once, right away, promptly, straightaway
   
   **Antonyms**: eventually, gradually, later, slowly, belatedly
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing the absence of delay; formal and informal contexts; particularly common with "upon" to stress immediacy
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing gradual or delayed actions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - immediately upon arrival
   - act immediately
   - respond immediately
   - immediately following
   
   **Root Analysis**: immediate (im-: not + medi: middle + -ate) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin immediatus + English -ly → "without anything in between" + adverbial marker
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Call me immediately upon landing.
   - I knew immediately that something was wrong.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Authorities must be notified immediately upon discovery of any irregularities.
   - Implementation shall commence immediately upon receipt of authorization.

1. **Adverb**: subsequently (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - After a particular event or time; at a later time; afterwards

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - subsequently discovered
   - subsequently determined
   - subsequently built upon
   - act subsequently
   
   **Root Analysis**: subsequent (sub-: after + sequ: follow + -ent) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin subsequens + -ly → "following after" + adverbial marker
