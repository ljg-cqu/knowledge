1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: sentence/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; in agreement with something; therefore or consequently
   - *(Sentence adverb or manner adverb)* Can modify entire sentences or describe manner of action
   
   **Synonyms**: consequently, therefore, thus, appropriately, correspondingly
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, regardless, contrarily, inconsistently
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing, logical reasoning, describing appropriate responses; sentence-initial or mid-position
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where simpler connectives suffice; creative writing seeking variety
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - plan accordingly
   - act accordingly
   - adjust accordingly
   - respond accordingly
   - and accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (agreement) + -ing + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French acorder → English accord + -ingly (14th century), meaning "in agreement with"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather's bad, so dress accordingly.
   - You know the rules; act accordingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data were analyzed and results interpreted accordingly as per statistical guidelines.
   - Policies should be revised accordingly to reflect current standards.

1. **Adverb**: proportionally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that corresponds in size or amount to something else; maintaining a constant ratio
   - *(Manner adverb)* Typically mid or end position, modifies how distribution or relationship occurs
   
   **Synonyms**: proportionately, commensurately, correspondingly, relatively, rationally
   
   **Antonyms**: disproportionally, unevenly, inequitably, arbitrarily
   
   **When to Use**: Technical or mathematical contexts, formal descriptions of distributions or relationships
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual everyday conversation; when absolute rather than relative measures are discussed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - distributed proportionally
   - increase proportionally
   - allocated proportionally
   - vary proportionally
   - scale proportionally
   
   **Root Analysis**: pro- (forward) + port (carry) + -ion + -al + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin proportionalis + -ly → English (16th century), meaning "in due proportion"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone's paid proportionally to their hours.
   - The servings should be divided proportionally.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Resources were allocated proportionally as per institutional equity guidelines.
   - Voting rights increase proportionally with share ownership.

1. **Adverb**: respectively (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In the order given; separately in the sequence mentioned; used to show individual correspondence in a series
   - *(Sentence adverb)* End position, relates multiple items to multiple other items in order
   
   **Synonyms**: individually, separately, severally, in order, correspondingly
   
   **Antonyms**: collectively, jointly, together, commonly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal or academic writing when listing multiple items that correspond to other lists
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual speech; when items don't have clear one-to-one correspondence
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - A and B, respectively
   - first and second, respectively
   - respectively assigned
   - respectively measured
   - respectively defined
   
   **Root Analysis**: respective (re- + spect) + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin respective + -ly → English (16th century), meaning "with respect to each"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - John and Mary are 25 and 23, respectively.
   - They scored 85 and 90, respectively.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Temperatures were 20°C and 25°C, respectively, as per measurement protocols.
   - The institutions received $5M and $3M, respectively.

   **Synonyms**: similarly, likewise, proportionally, accordingly, analogously

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - correspondingly high
   - vary correspondingly
   - increase correspondingly
   - correspondingly low
   - adjust correspondingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: correspond (cor- [together] + respond) + -ing + -ly

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Prices went up, and sales dropped correspondingly.
   - If you work harder, you'll be paid correspondingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As temperature increased, reaction rates rose correspondingly as per chemical kinetics.
   - Investment returns varied correspondingly with market conditions.
