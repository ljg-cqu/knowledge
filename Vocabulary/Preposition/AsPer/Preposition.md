1. **Preposition**: per (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* For each; through; by means of; according to
   - *(Distribution)* For each unit or instance (per person, per day)
   - *(Authority)* According to; as stated by; in accordance with
   
   **Synonyms**: according to (authority sense), for each (distribution), through, by means of
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to (authority sense), regardless of, total (vs. unit distribution)
   
   **When to Use**: Business writing, formal contexts, rates and distributions, citing authority
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual speech (sounds stiff); creative writing; when simpler "for each" or "according to" is clearer
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: per person, per day, per capita, per annum, per se
   - **Verb + prep**: calculated per, charged per, paid per
   - **Adjective + prep**: (less common with adjectives)
   - **Noun + prep**: cost per unit, rate per hour, price per item
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (through, by means of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per (through) → English (direct Latin borrowing, 16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The cost is $10 per person.
   - We're paid per hour, not salary.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Reimbursement rates are calculated per established guidelines.
   - Performance metrics are evaluated per quarterly review cycles as per policy.

1. **Preposition**: according to (type: compound/phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Authority)* As stated or indicated by someone or something
   - *(Conformity)* In a manner corresponding with or proportionate to
   
   **Synonyms**: as per, as stated by, in accordance with, per, by, following
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, in opposition to
   
   **When to Use**: Citing sources, referencing authorities, indicating conformity with standards
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you want to distance yourself from the source (use "allegedly" or "reportedly"); extremely formal legal writing (prefer "pursuant to")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: according to the report, according to experts, according to law
   - **Verb + prep**: varies according to, changes according to, decided according to
   - **Adjective + prep**: different according to, arranged according to
   - **Noun + prep**: classification according to, distribution according to
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (agree) + -ing + to
   
   **Etymology**: Old French acorder → according to (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to Jane, the meeting's cancelled.
   - According to the weather app, it'll rain.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to established protocols, approval must be obtained.
   - The data were analyzed according to statistical guidelines as per methodological standards.

1. **Preposition**: pursuant to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Legal)* In accordance with; by virtue of; under the authority of
   - Used primarily in legal contexts to cite authority for actions
   
   **Synonyms**: under, according to, as per, by virtue of, in accordance with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, outside the authority of, unauthorized by
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents, regulatory filings, formal legal correspondence, official citations of authority
   
   **When NOT to Use**: General business writing (too formal); casual contexts; when simpler language works
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pursuant to section, pursuant to statute, pursuant to authority
   - **Verb + prep**: acting pursuant to, issued pursuant to, filed pursuant to
   - **Adjective + prep**: valid pursuant to, binding pursuant to
   - **Noun + prep**: action pursuant to, authority pursuant to, power pursuant to
   
   **Root Analysis**: pursue (follow) + -ant (doing) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Anglo-French pursuir → pursuant (16th century), legal terminology
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Avoid in casual contexts: inappropriately legalistic)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The investigation proceeds pursuant to statutory authority.
   - Notice is provided pursuant to Section 12.3 as per contractual obligations.

1. **Preposition**: under (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Below or beneath something; lower than
   - *(Abstract - Authority)* Subject to the authority, control, or influence of
   - *(Abstract - Conditions)* Subject to; in accordance with; governed by
   
   **Synonyms**: below (spatial), subject to (authority), governed by (rules), pursuant to (legal)
   
   **Antonyms**: above (spatial), free from (authority), exempt from (rules)
   
   **When to Use**: Describing authority relationships, legal governance, conditions; spatial relationships
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "below" specifically means physically lower; when "according to" is clearer for citation
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: under the law, under regulations, under contract, under authority
   - **Verb + prep**: operate under, function under, work under, fall under
   - **Adjective + prep**: subject under, binding under, valid under
   - **Noun + prep**: rights under, obligations under, provisions under
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English under (beneath, among)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English under → Middle English → Modern English
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The keys are under the mat.
   - We work under a new manager now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All parties are bound under the terms of the agreement as per Section 5.
   - The organization operates under federal regulations.

1. **Preposition**: within (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Inside; enclosed or contained by
   - *(Temporal)* Before the end of a period; not exceeding a time limit
   - *(Abstract - Limits)* Not exceeding the bounds or limits of; in conformity with
   
   **Synonyms**: inside (spatial), before (temporal), in accordance with (conformity), not exceeding
   
   **Antonyms**: outside, beyond, exceeding, after (temporal)
   
   **When to Use**: Specifying limits, deadlines, boundaries; conformity with parameters
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "inside" is more natural for physical location; when "by" is clearer for deadlines
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: within limits, within regulations, within timeframe, within bounds
   - **Verb + prep**: remain within, operate within, fall within, stay within
   - **Adjective + prep**: compliant within, valid within, acceptable within
   - **Noun + prep**: activities within, actions within, operations within
   
   **Root Analysis**: with (alongside) + in (inside)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English withinnan (from with + innan) → within
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Finish within two hours.
   - Keep it within budget.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All expenditures must remain within approved budgets as per fiscal policies.
   - Responses must be submitted within the stipulated timeframe.

1. **Preposition**: by (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Agency)* Identifying the agent performing an action (passive voice)
   - *(Temporal)* Not later than; before or at
   - *(Abstract - Means)* Through the means or agency of; using
   - *(Abstract - Authority)* According to; as authorized by
   
   **Synonyms**: through (means), before (time), according to (authority), via (means)
   
   **Antonyms**: after (time), without (means), contrary to (authority)
   
   **When to Use**: Passive constructions, deadlines, means/methods, citing authority
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When active voice is clearer; when "with" better indicates instrument
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: by law, by regulation, by authority, by deadline, by means of
   - **Verb + prep**: required by, mandated by, governed by, authorized by
   - **Adjective + prep**: bound by, constrained by, determined by
   - **Noun + prep**: compliance by, adherence by, approval by
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English bī (near, by, about)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English bī → Middle English → Modern English by
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The report was written by Sarah.
   - Finish by Friday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As mandated by federal regulations, annual audits are required.
   - Submissions must be received by the specified deadline as per program guidelines.

1. **Preposition**: in accordance with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Conformity)* In agreement with; conforming to; following
   - Used to show that something conforms to rules, wishes, or standards
   
   **Synonyms**: as per, per, according to, pursuant to, in conformity with, consistent with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, in breach of, inconsistent with
   
   **When to Use**: Formal documents, compliance statements, official procedures, contracts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual communication; when simpler "following" or "per" works; creative writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in accordance with policy, in accordance with regulations, in accordance with wishes
   - **Verb + prep**: operate in accordance with, act in accordance with, proceed in accordance with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent in accordance with, compliant in accordance with
   - **Noun + prep**: procedures in accordance with, actions in accordance with
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + accord (agreement) + -ance (state) + with
   
   **Etymology**: French accord → in accordance with (17th century), formal phrase
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Use simpler alternatives in casual contexts)
   - We followed what the instructions said.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All procedures are conducted in accordance with ISO standards.
   - The investigation proceeded in accordance with established protocols as per institutional requirements.

1. **Preposition**: subject to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Condition)* Dependent on; conditional upon; requiring
   - *(Abstract - Authority)* Under the control or authority of; governed by
   - *(Abstract - Liability)* Likely to be affected by; exposed to
   
   **Synonyms**: contingent on, dependent on, conditional upon, governed by, under, bound by
   
   **Antonyms**: independent of, regardless of, unconditional, free from, exempt from
   
   **When to Use**: Contracts, agreements, policies with conditions, legal documents
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When expressing certainty; simple conditions (use "if"); contexts emphasizing freedom
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: subject to approval, subject to conditions, subject to law, subject to change
   - **Verb + prep**: remain subject to, become subject to, are subject to
   - **Adjective + prep**: (typically predicative): The offer is subject to...
   - **Noun + prep**: agreement subject to, terms subject to, rights subject to
   
   **Root Analysis**: sub- (under) + ject (throw) + to
   
   **Etymology**: Latin subjectus → subject to (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Plans are subject to change.
   - The price is subject to availability.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This agreement is subject to approval by the board as per governance procedures.
   - All rights are subject to applicable laws and regulations.

1. **Preposition**: in conformity with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract - Compliance)* In agreement or harmony with; matching; conforming to
   - Technical/formal phrase indicating compliance with standards
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, consistent with, as per, conforming to, in compliance with
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, inconsistent with, contrary to, nonconforming to
   
   **When to Use**: Technical documentation, quality assurance, compliance reports, formal specifications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts; when simpler alternatives work; general business writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in conformity with standards, in conformity with requirements, in conformity with specifications
   - **Verb + prep**: operate in conformity with, manufactured in conformity with, designed in conformity with
   - **Adjective + prep**: (less common with adjectives in this pattern)
   - **Noun + prep**: production in conformity with, processes in conformity with
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + conform (con- [with] + form [shape]) + -ity + with
   
   **Etymology**: Latin conformare → conformity → in conformity with (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Use simpler alternatives)
   - The product meets all standards.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Manufacturing processes are maintained in conformity with ISO 9001 standards.
   - All designs must be in conformity with regulatory specifications as per certification requirements.

1. **Preposition**: for (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Purpose)* With the purpose of; intended to benefit or be used by
   - *(Duration)* Throughout the duration of; during
   - *(Exchange)* In exchange for; as payment for
   - *(Support)* In favor of; in support of
   
   **Synonyms**: in favor of (support), during (duration), in exchange for, intended for
   
   **Antonyms**: against (opposition), despite, from (source rather than destination)
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating purpose, duration, beneficiary, exchange, support
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Causal relationships (use "because of"); spatial relationships (use "to" or "toward")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: for the purpose of, for compliance, for approval, for consideration
   - **Verb + prep**: prepare for, provide for, account for, apply for
   - **Adjective + prep**: responsible for, necessary for, sufficient for, eligible for
   - **Noun + prep**: need for, requirement for, basis for, reason for
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English for (before, because of, for the sake of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English for → Middle English → Modern English for
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This is for you.
   - We waited for two hours.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy provides for periodic reviews as per regulatory requirements.
   - Applications for approval must include all required documentation.
