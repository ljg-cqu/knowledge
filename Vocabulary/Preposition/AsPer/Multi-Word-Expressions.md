1. **Expression**: as per usual (type: idiom/fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In the normal or expected way; as always happens; following the typical pattern
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed idiom, though "as usual" is more common)
   
   **Synonyms**: as usual, as always, as expected, typically, in the normal way
   
   **Antonyms**: unusually, exceptionally, surprisingly, unexpectedly, atypically
   
   **When to Use**: Informal contexts describing predictable behavior or events; can carry ironic or resigned tone
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal documents (use "as usual" or "typically"); positive contexts where routine might sound boring
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as usual (much more common, less marked)
   - as per normal (British informal)
   - business as usual
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "As per" adds mock-formal emphasis to "usual," often with humorous or ironic effect; popular in British and Irish English informal speech
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in the manner of) + per (according to) + usual (customary)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He showed up late, as per usual.
   - Everything went wrong, as per usual with our plans.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *"Typically informal; avoid in formal contexts"* (Use "as usual" or "typically" instead)

1. **Expression**: in accordance with [noun] (type: collocation/fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Following or conforming to rules, standards, wishes, or agreements
   - *Fixedness*: strong (formal collocation, standard legal/business phrase)
   
   **Synonyms**: as per, per, according to, pursuant to, in conformity with, consistent with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, in breach of, despite, against
   
   **When to Use**: Formal documents, compliance statements, legal writing, official procedures
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; creative writing; when simpler language suffices
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in line with (less formal alternative)
   - in compliance with (emphasizes regulatory aspect)
   - in conformity with (more technical/formal)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Accordance" naturally pairs with "in...with" to express conformity; legal and business convention; formal register marker
   
   **Root Analysis**: in + accord (agreement) + -ance + with
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Better to use simpler alternatives in casual contexts)
   - We followed what the manual said.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All procedures must be conducted in accordance with ISO 9001 standards.
   - The investigation proceeded in accordance with established protocols as per institutional policy.

1. **Expression**: as per [one's] instructions/request/agreement (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Following what someone has instructed, requested, or agreed to
   - *Fixedness*: medium (strong collocation with these particular nouns)
   
   **Synonyms**: following your instructions, as you requested, as instructed, per your request, as agreed
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to instructions, ignoring the request, in violation of agreement
   
   **When to Use**: Formal business correspondence, professional emails, official communications showing compliance
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (sounds overly formal); when simpler "as requested" works better
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - per your request (more concise, semi-formal)
   - as requested (simplest, neutral)
   - following your instructions (formal without "as per")
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "As per" strongly collocates with nouns indicating authority or prior communication (instructions, request, agreement, discussion, etc.); business English convention
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in manner of) + per (according to) + [authoritative noun]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I did what you asked.
   - Following what we talked about...
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The documents are attached as per your request dated April 15th.
   - Changes have been implemented as per our agreement.

1. **Expression**: subject to [approval/review/conditions] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Dependent upon or conditional on approval, review, or specified conditions
   - *Fixedness*: strong (standard legal/business collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: contingent on, dependent on, conditional upon, pending, requiring
   
   **Antonyms**: independent of, final, unconditional, approved, confirmed
   
   **When to Use**: Contracts, proposals, offers, formal agreements indicating contingency
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When expressing certainty; informal arrangements; after conditions are met
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - subject to change (common warning phrase)
   - pending approval (similar meaning, different construction)
   - conditional upon (more formal)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Subject to" naturally pairs with nouns indicating oversight, conditions, or authority (approval, review, verification, conditions, terms); legal convention
   
   **Root Analysis**: sub- (under) + ject (throw) + to + [governing noun]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This offer is subject to approval.
   - Prices subject to change without notice.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The agreement is subject to board approval as per corporate governance procedures.
   - All claims are subject to verification in accordance with audit standards.

1. **Expression**: adhere to [guidelines/protocols/principles] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Follow closely or stick to rules, guidelines, or principles
   - *Fixedness*: strong (verb "adhere" collocates strongly with these objects)
   
   **Synonyms**: follow, comply with, observe, conform to, stick to, abide by
   
   **Antonyms**: deviate from, violate, disregard, ignore, depart from
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts discussing rule-following, professional standards, ethical principles
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts where "follow" is simpler; when literal sticking (physical adhesion) might cause confusion
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - adherence to (noun form)
   - strictly adhere to (emphatic)
   - failure to adhere to (negative construction)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Adhere" metaphorically extends physical sticking to following rules; naturally pairs with nouns representing systematic guidance (guidelines, protocols, standards, principles)
   
   **Root Analysis**: ad- (to) + here (stick) + to + [guidance noun]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to adhere to the safety guidelines.
   - Everyone should adhere to basic principles.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All research must adhere to ethical protocols as per institutional review board requirements.
   - Organizations are expected to adhere to industry best practices.

1. **Expression**: stipulated in [contract/agreement/document] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Specified or demanded as a condition in a formal document or agreement
   - *Fixedness*: strong (legal/formal collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: specified in, stated in, required by, set forth in, prescribed in
   
   **Antonyms**: omitted from, excluded from, unstated in, not covered by
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents, formal contracts, official agreements referencing conditions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual arrangements; informal discussions; when "stated" or "specified" is clearer
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as stipulated (shortened form)
   - stipulated requirements (noun + adjective)
   - stipulated conditions (noun + adjective)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Stipulated" (demanded as condition) naturally pairs with formal documents that contain binding terms (contract, agreement, policy, terms); legal vocabulary
   
   **Root Analysis**: stipul(ate) (bargain) + -ed + in + [formal document]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Follow what the contract says.
   - Do what's required in the agreement.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All obligations stipulated in the contract must be fulfilled as per agreed timelines.
   - Penalties apply as stipulated in Section 8.2 of the Service Agreement.

1. **Expression**: prescribed by [authority/regulation/doctor] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Ordered, recommended, or required by an authority or professional
   - *Fixedness*: strong (medical and regulatory contexts)
   
   **Synonyms**: ordered by, required by, mandated by, directed by, recommended by
   
   **Antonyms**: optional under, discouraged by, prohibited by, contraindicated by
   
   **When to Use**: Medical contexts (prescriptions), regulatory requirements, official directives
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual suggestions; contexts without clear authority; when "recommended" is more accurate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as prescribed (shortened form)
   - prescribed medication/treatment (medical)
   - prescribed method/procedure (technical)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Prescribed" (authoritatively directed) pairs with authority figures or governing documents (doctor, regulation, law, protocol); medical and legal vocabulary
   
   **Root Analysis**: pre- (before) + scribe (write) + -ed + by + [authority]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Take the medicine prescribed by your doctor.
   - Follow the steps prescribed by the manual.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Treatment must follow protocols prescribed by regulatory authorities as per clinical guidelines.
   - Documentation follows the format prescribed by industry standards.
