1. **Functional Expression**: "As per [noun/agreement/policy]..." (function: citing authority)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to reference an authoritative source as the basis for a statement or action
   - *Formality*: formal/very formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, per, pursuant to, following
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal written communications, legal documents, contracts, compliance contexts, official correspondence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation, creative writing, contexts where less formal language is more appropriate; overuse makes writing stilted
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - per [noun] (more concise, semi-formal)
   - in accordance with [noun] (equally formal)
   - pursuant to [noun] (very formal, legal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "As per" emphasizes adherence to authority or prior agreement; signals compliance-oriented mindset; common in Indian English business contexts
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Acknowledged, we will proceed as specified
   - Understood, I will ensure compliance
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We'll do it as per the boss's instructions (better: "like the boss said")
   - As per our chat earlier... (better: "like we talked about")
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Payment shall be made as per the contractual terms outlined in Section 4.2.
   - All submissions must be formatted as per institutional guidelines.

1. **Functional Expression**: "I would like to confirm that [statement] as per [source]" (function: verifying/confirming)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to formally verify information while citing the authoritative source
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: I can verify that according to, I confirm that per, This is confirmed in accordance with
   
   **Antonyms**: I question whether, I dispute that, This contradicts
   
   **When to Use**: Business correspondence, audit responses, compliance verifications, formal confirmations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual check-ins, friendly communications, when informal tone is more appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I can confirm that per... (slightly less formal)
   - This is confirmed as per... (passive construction)
   - We verify compliance with... (alternative without "as per")
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Demonstrates due diligence by citing source; provides audit trail; protects speaker by referencing authority
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you for the confirmation
   - Noted, this aligns with our records
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Just confirming what we discussed (avoid "as per" here)
   - Yeah, that matches what the email said
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I would like to confirm that all requirements have been met as per the compliance checklist.
   - We verify that delivery occurred as per the tracking documentation.

1. **Functional Expression**: "Please proceed as per [instructions/plan]" (function: requesting/directing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite directive to follow specified instructions or plans
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: somewhat indirect (softened by "please")
   
   **Synonyms**: Please follow, Kindly proceed according to, Please implement as outlined in
   
   **Antonyms**: Please disregard, Please deviate from, Feel free to improvise
   
   **When to Use**: Professional emails, project management, formal requests, superior-to-subordinate communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Peer-to-peer casual contexts, when collaborative rather than directive tone is needed
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Kindly proceed per... (more formal)
   - Please follow the... (less formal, without "as per")
   - Proceed in accordance with... (equally formal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "Please" softens directive force; "as per" shifts authority to referenced document rather than speaker; useful for face-saving
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Understood, I will proceed accordingly
   - Confirmed, moving forward as specified
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Just follow the steps we talked about (avoid "as per")
   - Go ahead with the plan we discussed
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please proceed as per the implementation plan distributed yesterday.
   - Kindly submit all documentation as per the attached requirements.

1. **Functional Expression**: "This is in compliance with [standard/regulation] as per [authority]" (function: asserting compliance)
   **Meaning**: 
   - States that something meets required standards while citing the governing authority
   - *Formality*: very formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: This conforms to... according to, This meets... per, This adheres to... in accordance with
   
   **Antonyms**: This violates, This deviates from, This is non-compliant with
   
   **When to Use**: Audit responses, regulatory submissions, compliance reports, certification applications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Informal status updates, casual compliance discussions, preliminary assessments
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Compliant with... per (more concise)
   - In conformity with... as per (very formal)
   - Meets requirements of... according to (semi-formal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Double citation (standard + authority) provides extra credibility; demonstrates thoroughness; protects organization legally
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Acknowledged, documentation received
   - Compliance verified and accepted
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're following all the rules (avoid formal construction)
   - This meets what they asked for
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This implementation is in compliance with ISO 27001 standards as per the certification body's requirements.
   - All procedures conform to FDA regulations as per 21 CFR Part 11.

1. **Functional Expression**: "As per our discussion/conversation/meeting" (function: referencing prior communication)
   **Meaning**: 
   - References previous verbal communication as basis for current statement or action
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Following our discussion, As we discussed, Per our conversation, As agreed in our meeting
   
   **Antonyms**: Contrary to our discussion, Changing from what we discussed
   
   **When to Use**: Follow-up emails, meeting minutes, action confirmations, formal correspondence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: First contact, casual chat follow-ups (use simpler language), creative communication
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Per our discussion (more concise, semi-formal)
   - As discussed (simplest, neutral)
   - Following our conversation (formal without "as per")
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Creates paper trail of verbal agreements; establishes shared understanding; can be used to hold others accountable; some find "as per" stilted for routine follow-ups
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, that aligns with my understanding
   - Confirmed, proceeding as discussed
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Like we talked about earlier... (better for casual)
   - Following up on our chat...
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As per our discussion yesterday, I am attaching the revised proposal.
   - Following our meeting on March 15th, please find the action items as per our agreement.

1. **Functional Expression**: "Please find attached [document] as per your request" (function: providing requested information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Formally presents requested documents while acknowledging the prior request
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Attached is... as requested, Please see enclosed... per your request, As requested, attached is
   
   **Antonyms**: I cannot provide, This was not requested, Unsolicited attachment
   
   **When to Use**: Business emails, formal correspondence, professional document delivery, client communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual emails to colleagues, friendly communications, when simpler language suffices
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Attached per your request (more concise)
   - As requested, please find attached (without "as per")
   - Enclosed as per your inquiry (more formal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "Please find" is formal holdover from postal mail era; "as per your request" clarifies this is responsive, not proactive; demonstrates attentiveness
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you, received
   - Acknowledged, reviewing now
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Here's the file you asked for (simpler, better for casual)
   - Attaching what we discussed
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please find attached the quarterly report as per your request dated April 3rd.
   - Enclosed please find the executed agreement as per your instructions.

1. **Functional Expression**: "Kindly note that [information] as per [source]" (function: informing/notifying)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Politely directs attention to important information while citing authority
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: somewhat indirect (softened by "kindly")
   
   **Synonyms**: Please be advised that... per, Please note that... according to, Be aware that... as per
   
   **Antonyms**: You may disregard, This is unimportant, Ignore the fact that
   
   **When to Use**: Formal notifications, policy announcements, important updates, professional reminders
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Urgent emergencies (too polite), casual team updates, friendly reminders
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Please note that... per (equally formal)
   - Be advised that... as per (very formal)
   - Note that... according to (semi-formal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "Kindly" is common in Indian English business correspondence; "note that" creates obligation to pay attention; citing source adds weight
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Duly noted, thank you
   - Acknowledged with thanks
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Just so you know... (much better for casual)
   - FYI, the policy says...
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Kindly note that the deadline has been extended as per management directive.
   - Please be advised that access will be restricted as per the security protocol.

1. **Functional Expression**: "We would like to clarify that [statement] as per [standard/policy]" (function: clarifying)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Formally explains or corrects understanding while referencing authoritative source
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: somewhat indirect (softened by "would like to")
   
   **Synonyms**: We wish to clarify that... according to, To clarify,... per, For clarification,... as per
   
   **Antonyms**: This remains unclear, We dispute that, This contradicts
   
   **When to Use**: Correcting misunderstandings, responding to questions, formal explanations, official clarifications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual Q&A, friendly explanations, when simple direct answer is better
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - To clarify,... per (more concise)
   - For clarification,... according to (formal)
   - Just to be clear,... (informal, avoid with "as per")
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Diplomatic way to correct errors; "we would like" softens potential face-threat; citing authority deflects blame from speaker
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you for the clarification
   - Understood, this clears up the confusion
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let me explain what I meant (simpler)
   - To clear things up...
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We would like to clarify that reimbursement is limited to approved expenses as per the travel policy.
   - For clarification, the submission deadline is firm as per program guidelines.

1. **Functional Expression**: "This shall be executed as per the terms specified in [document]" (function: committing to action)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Formal commitment to perform actions according to documented specifications
   - *Formality*: very formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: This will be implemented per, This shall proceed according to, Execution will follow
   
   **Antonyms**: This will not proceed, This deviates from, We cannot execute
   
   **When to Use**: Contracts, formal agreements, legal commitments, official project plans
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual plans, informal agreements, friendly coordination
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Will be executed per... (slightly less formal, "will" vs "shall")
   - Shall proceed as per... (equally formal)
   - Implementation follows... (formal without "as per")
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "Shall" creates legal obligation stronger than "will"; double reference (action + source document) establishes clear accountability
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Agreed and accepted
   - Acknowledged, parties bound accordingly
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We'll do what the plan says (much simpler)
   - Going ahead with what we agreed
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project shall be executed as per the terms specified in Appendix A.
   - All deliverables shall conform to specifications as per the Statement of Work.

1. **Functional Expression**: "In reference to your inquiry, [response] as per [source]" (function: responding formally)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Formal opening for responding to questions or requests while citing authoritative sources
   - *Formality*: formal/very formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (elaborate opening)
   
   **Synonyms**: Regarding your question,... according to, In response to your inquiry,... per, With respect to your request
   
   **Antonyms**: Ignoring your inquiry, We cannot address, This is irrelevant to
   
   **When to Use**: Formal correspondence, customer service responses, official inquiries, regulatory responses
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Quick replies, casual Q&A, friendly exchanges, when brevity is valued
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Regarding your inquiry,... per (slightly less formal)
   - In response to... as per (equally formal)
   - Re: your question,... (semi-formal shorthand)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Formal opening establishes context; acknowledges inquirer; citing source demonstrates due diligence and accuracy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you for the comprehensive response
   - This addresses my concerns, appreciated
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - About your question... (much simpler)
   - You asked about...
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In reference to your inquiry dated May 10th, the eligibility criteria are as per Section 3.2 of the guidelines.
   - Regarding your request, approval is granted as per the authorization matrix.
