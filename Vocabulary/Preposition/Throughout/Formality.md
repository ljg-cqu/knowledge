1. **Original Expression**: throughout the entire process  
   **Context**: Describing continuous presence or consistency during a procedure or workflow
   
   **Core Intent**: Communicate that something was present, maintained, or occurred consistently from start to finish
   
   **Casual**: the whole time  
   *Characteristics*: Simple vocabulary, time-focused, conversational tone  
   *Use when*: Talking with friends, colleagues in relaxed settings, informal updates
   
   **Semi-Formal**: throughout the process  
   *Characteristics*: Standard professional vocabulary, clear and direct, neutral tone  
   *Use when*: Regular business communication, team meetings, standard reports
   
   **Formal**: throughout the entire process / over the course of the entire procedure  
   *Characteristics*: Complete prepositional phrases, precise vocabulary, structured expression  
   *Use when*: Academic writing, formal reports, presentations to senior management, official documentation
   
   **Humorous**: from soup to nuts / from start to finish (with dramatic emphasis)  
   *Characteristics*: Idiomatic expression, playful tone, exaggeration  
   *Use when*: Building rapport with colleagues, lightening mood in meetings, storytelling  
   *Avoid when*: Formal presentations, serious project reviews, communications with unfamiliar stakeholders
   
   **Lively**: every single step of the way / all the way through  
   *Characteristics*: Emphatic language, energetic phrasing, strong emphasis  
   *Use when*: Motivational contexts, expressing enthusiasm, engaging audiences
   
   **Empathetic**: throughout the journey / all along the way  
   *Characteristics*: Journey metaphor, supportive language, process-oriented  
   *Use when*: Supporting team members, acknowledging challenges, reflecting on experiences
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Senior management requires formal ("throughout the entire process"), peers allow casual ("the whole time")
   - *Social Distance*: Strangers/clients require semi-formal or formal, close colleagues allow casual
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Critical reports require formal, routine updates allow semi-formal
   - *Cultural Context*: Anglo-American business culture accepts moderate informality; some cultures expect higher formality
   - *Medium*: Written reports favor formal, face-to-face meetings allow more flexibility
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "the whole time" in written reports to executives (too casual; should use "throughout the process")
   - Using "throughout the entire process" in casual team chat (overly formal; should use "the whole time" or "all along")
   - Overusing "from soup to nuts" with non-native speakers or unfamiliar audiences (idiom may cause confusion)
   
   **Cultural Notes**: British English may prefer "all the way through" over "throughout" in semi-formal speech. American business culture generally accepts more casual language than European or Asian business contexts.
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Email to CEO about quality assurance → **Appropriate Variant**: "throughout the entire process" (formal due to high power distance)
   - **Situation 2**: Team Slack message about yesterday's meeting → **Appropriate Variant**: "the whole time" (casual due to equal status and informal medium)
   - **Situation 3**: Project status presentation to client → **Appropriate Variant**: "throughout the process" (semi-formal balancing professionalism with accessibility)
   - **Situation 4**: Debriefing with team after difficult project → **Appropriate Variant**: "throughout the journey" (empathetic to acknowledge shared experience)
