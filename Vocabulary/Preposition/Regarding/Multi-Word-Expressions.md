1. **Expression**: with regard to (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; about; on the subject of; regarding
   - Used to introduce the topic or subject being discussed, particularly in formal contexts
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, with respect to, in relation to, as regards
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Formal written communication, business correspondence, academic writing, official documents
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "about" would be more natural; avoid when brevity is preferred
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in regard to (alternative form, less preferred by some style guides)
   - as regards (British English preference)
   - with respect to (more technical/academic)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Fixed formal expression that evolved from legal and administrative language; "regard" (consideration) combines with "to" to create prepositional phrase indicating what deserves consideration
   
   **Root Analysis**: with (preposition) + regard (noun from French regarder) + to (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - With regard to what you asked, I'm not sure yet.
   - I have some concerns with regard to the timeline.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - With regard to your inquiry dated March 15, we are pleased to provide the requested information.
   - The committee has several recommendations with regard to budget allocation.

   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; about; regarding (alternative form of "with regard to")
   - Used to introduce topics in formal contexts
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, with regard to, with respect to
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, though some style guides prefer "with regard to" over this form
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When style guide specifically prohibits; casual conversation
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - with regard to (more common variant)
   - as regards
   - regarding (single-word alternative)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Variant construction of "with regard to"; "in" suggests containment within the scope of consideration

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Expression**: with respect to (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; regarding; in relation to
   - Particularly common in academic, scientific, and technical contexts
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, with regard to, in relation to
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Formal academic and technical writing, especially when discussing specific parameters or aspects
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; when simpler alternatives would suffice
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - with regard to (similar but less technical)
   - in respect of (British legal English)
   - regarding (simpler alternative)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Latin-derived formal construction; "respect" (consideration, regard) creates elevated register; particularly favored in mathematical and scientific contexts ("differentiate with respect to x")
   
   **Root Analysis**: with (preposition) + respect (noun from Latin respectus) + to (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - With respect to your concerns, I understand completely.
   - What's the status with respect to the budget?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The function was differentiated with respect to the independent variable.
   - With respect to the experimental methodology, several improvements have been implemented.

1. **Expression**: in connection with (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; related to; associated with; as a result of
   - Emphasizes relationship or causal connection
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, in relation to, associated with, related to
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated to, separate from, independent of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when emphasizing relationships, associations, or causal connections
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When no clear connection exists; casual settings where "about" is clearer
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in relation to (similar but more neutral)
   - regarding (simpler alternative)
   - associated with (emphasizes connection)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Emphasizes the linking aspect between topics; "connection" explicitly signals relationship, making it useful when causation or association needs emphasis
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (preposition) + connection (noun from Latin connexio) + with (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm calling in connection with your order from yesterday.
   - There are concerns in connection with the new policy.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The audit was conducted in connection with the annual review process.
   - Several arrests were made in connection with the ongoing investigation.

1. **Expression**: as regards (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; regarding; with respect to
   - British English preference for formal topic introduction
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, with regard to, as for
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Formal British English contexts; business and academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual American English; may sound archaic or overly formal to some audiences
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - with regard to (American preference)
   - regarding (simpler alternative)
   - concerning (neutral alternative)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Combines conjunction "as" with verb "regards" (3rd person) to create formal prepositional function; British legal and administrative tradition
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (conjunction/adverb) + regards (verb, 3rd person present)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As regards your question, I'll need to check.
   - As regards tonight, I'm free.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As regards the financial projections, we anticipate moderate growth.
   - The committee has reached consensus as regards the implementation strategy.

1. **Expression**: on the subject of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; about; regarding
   - Used to introduce or shift to a different topic
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, about, as for, speaking of
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Semi-formal contexts when introducing or changing topics; conversational formal writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When brevity is important (use "regarding" instead); very formal academic writing
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - regarding (more concise)
   - concerning (formal alternative)
   - about (casual alternative)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: More explicit than "regarding" in signaling topic shift; "subject" makes the topical nature very clear; slightly less formal than pure prepositional alternatives
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (preposition) + the (article) + subject (noun) + of (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - On the subject of dinner, what should we have?
   - On the subject of vacations, where are you going this year?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - On the subject of resource allocation, the committee proposes several alternatives.
   - On the subject of compliance, all departments must submit quarterly reports.

1. **Expression**: as to (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Concerning; regarding; about
   - Also used to express uncertainty or indicate subject matter
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: regarding, concerning, about, as for, with respect to
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts for introducing topics or expressing uncertainty; common in legal writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Some style guides discourage overuse; casual conversation where "about" is clearer
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - regarding (more standard)
   - concerning (formal alternative)
   - about (casual alternative)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Compact formal construction; "as" + "to" creates directional sense toward topic; particularly common when expressing uncertainty ("as to whether")
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (conjunction/adverb) + to (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm not sure as to whether we should go.
   - As to your question, let me think about it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As to the implementation timeline, several factors require consideration.
   - Uncertainty remains as to whether the proposed changes will achieve intended outcomes.
