1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; in a manner that is consistent with what has just been said
   - As a result; consequently; therefore
   - *Can appear in initial, medial, or final position*
   
   **Synonyms**: consequently, therefore, thus, hence, as a result
   
   **Antonyms**: regardless, nevertheless, contrarily
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing when showing logical consequence or indicating appropriate action based on circumstances
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "so" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - plan accordingly, adjust accordingly, act accordingly
   - proceed accordingly, respond accordingly
   - should be adjusted accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (agree, harmony) + -ing + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English accorden → "accord" + -ing + -ly → "accordingly" (in agreement with, consequently)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather's bad, so plan accordingly.
   - We know there are risks, and we'll proceed accordingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Regarding the budget constraints, departments should adjust their plans accordingly.
   - The policy has been revised; all procedures must be modified accordingly.

1. **Adverb**: specifically (type: degree/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is exact and clear; explicitly and in detail
   - For a particular purpose; especially; particularly
   - *Typically in medial or initial position*
   
   **Synonyms**: particularly, especially, explicitly, precisely, exactly
   
   **Antonyms**: generally, vaguely, broadly, approximately
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when emphasizing precision, particularity, or explicit purpose
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When general statements are more appropriate
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - more specifically, specifically regarding, specifically designed
   - specifically mention, specifically state, specifically request
   - designed specifically for
   
   **Root Analysis**: specific (precise) + -ally (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin specificus → specific + -ally → "specifically" (in a precise manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm talking specifically about what you said regarding the deadline.
   - Can you be more specific? Specifically, what do you need?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The guidelines specifically address requirements regarding documentation.
   - This section deals specifically with matters regarding data protection.

1. **Adverb**: particularly (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To a higher degree than is usual or average; especially
   - Used to single out one person or thing over others; specifically
   - *Typically in medial position, can be initial*
   
   **Synonyms**: especially, notably, specifically, unusually, markedly
   
   **Antonyms**: generally, ordinarily, commonly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts when emphasizing special importance or singling out specific items
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "especially" would be clearer or more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - particularly important, particularly regarding, particularly concerned
   - not particularly, particularly relevant, particularly interested
   - particularly true, particularly useful
   
   **Root Analysis**: particular (specific) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin particularis → particular + -ly → "particularly" (especially, in particular)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm particularly interested in what you said regarding the schedule.
   - This is particularly important to remember.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee is particularly concerned regarding implementation timelines.
   - These findings are particularly relevant regarding policy development.

1. **Adverb**: directly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a direct manner; without intervening factors or intermediaries
   - Exactly; precisely; immediately
   - Frankly and openly; without evasion
   - *Can appear in various positions depending on meaning*
   
   **Synonyms**: immediately, straight, exactly, precisely, frankly
   
   **Antonyms**: indirectly, obliquely, eventually, vaguely
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when emphasizing immediacy, straightforwardness, or lack of intermediaries
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When indirect approaches are more appropriate or diplomatic
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - directly related, directly regarding, speak directly
   - directly involved, directly affect, directly address
   - report directly to
   
   **Root Analysis**: direct (straight) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin directus (straight, direct) → direct + -ly → "directly" (in a straight manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll talk to her directly about this.
   - This directly relates to what we discussed regarding the project.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy directly addresses concerns regarding employee safety.
   - Issues directly relating to implementation should be escalated immediately.

1. **Adverb**: concerning (type: manner - rarely used as adverb)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *Note: "Concerning" is primarily used as a preposition (meaning "regarding") rather than as an adverb*
   - When used adverbially (rare): In a way that causes concern or worry
   
   **Synonyms**: worryingly, disturbingly, alarmingly (when used as adverb)
   
   **Antonyms**: reassuringly, encouragingly
   
   **When to Use**: Rarely used as adverb; primarily use as preposition meaning "regarding"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using as adverb in formal writing; use "worryingly" instead
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - concerning this matter (prepositional use)
   - concerningly (adverbial form gaining usage)
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern + -ing (participial form)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere → concern + -ing → primarily prepositional use
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The results are concerning. (adjective use)
   - Concerning your question, I'll need more time. (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data reveal concerning trends regarding student performance.
   - Concerning the implementation schedule, further review is needed. (preposition)

1. **Adverb**: respectively (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In the order given; in the same sequence as previously mentioned items
   - Separately or individually in the order stated
   - *Typically appears in final position*
   
   **Synonyms**: in that order, correspondingly, in sequence, individually
   
   **Antonyms**: collectively, jointly, together
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when matching items in two lists in corresponding order
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When items don't need to be matched in order; casual conversation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - are respectively, were respectively, represent respectively
   - increase respectively, regarding their respective
   
   **Root Analysis**: respective (individual) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin respectivus → respective + -ly → "respectively" (in the order corresponding)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - John and Mary are 25 and 30 years old, respectively.
   - The teams scored 3 and 5 goals, respectively.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The departments reported increases of 15% and 23%, respectively, regarding their annual targets.
   - Chapters 3 and 4 address implementation and evaluation, respectively.

1. **Adverb**: relevantly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate or pertinent to what is being discussed or considered
   - In a manner that has bearing on the matter at hand
   - *Can appear in medial or final position*
   
   **Synonyms**: pertinently, appropriately, aptly, applicably
   
   **Antonyms**: irrelevantly, inappropriately, pointlessly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when discussing how something relates to the topic
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; somewhat uncommon even in formal writing
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - most relevantly, more relevantly
   - relevantly discuss, relevantly address
   
   **Root Analysis**: relevant (pertinent) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin relevare → relevant + -ly → "relevantly" (in a pertinent manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - More relevantly, what are we going to do about it?
   - He spoke relevantly about the issues.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The speaker addressed, most relevantly, concerns regarding resource allocation.
   - The analysis relevantly explores questions regarding implementation feasibility.

1. **Adverb**: explicitly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a clear and detailed manner, leaving no room for confusion or doubt
   - Stated clearly and in detail; not implied
   - *Typically in medial position*
   
   **Synonyms**: clearly, specifically, definitely, unambiguously, plainly
   
   **Antonyms**: implicitly, vaguely, ambiguously, indirectly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when emphasizing that something is stated clearly and directly
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "clearly" would be simpler and equally effective
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - explicitly state, explicitly mention, explicitly regarding
   - explicitly addressed, explicitly defined, explicitly required
   - make explicitly clear
   
   **Root Analysis**: explicit (clearly expressed) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin explicitus (unfolded, set forth) → explicit + -ly → "explicitly" (in a clear manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I explicitly told you not to do that.
   - She explicitly mentioned concerns regarding the timeline.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy explicitly addresses requirements regarding data retention.
   - Guidelines explicitly state procedures regarding conflict resolution.

1. **Adverb**: precisely (type: degree/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In exact terms; exactly or accurately
   - Used to emphasize complete accuracy or agreement
   - *Can appear in various positions*
   
   **Synonyms**: exactly, accurately, specifically, just, correctly
   
   **Antonyms**: approximately, roughly, vaguely, imprecisely
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when emphasizing exactness or complete agreement
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When approximate language is more appropriate
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - more precisely, precisely because, precisely what/when/how
   - precisely this, precisely the reason
   - state precisely
   
   **Root Analysis**: precise (exact) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praecisus (cut short, exact) → precise + -ly → "precisely" (exactly)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's precisely what I was thinking!
   - Precisely! That's the problem.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The guidelines precisely define requirements regarding documentation.
   - This is precisely the issue regarding implementation that requires resolution.

1. **Adverb**: duly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In accordance with what is required or appropriate; properly
   - At the expected or proper time; punctually
   - *Typically in medial or final position*
   
   **Synonyms**: properly, appropriately, correctly, punctually, accordingly
   
   **Antonyms**: improperly, inappropriately, late, wrongly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, especially legal and administrative writing, when indicating proper procedure
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "properly" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - duly noted, duly authorized, duly appointed
   - duly recorded, duly received, duly considered
   - duly signed
   
   **Root Analysis**: due (owed, proper) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French deu (owed) → Middle English due → due + -ly → "duly" (properly, in due manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your concerns have been duly noted.
   - The message was duly received.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All matters regarding the proposal have been duly considered.
   - The committee was duly authorized regarding the investigation.
