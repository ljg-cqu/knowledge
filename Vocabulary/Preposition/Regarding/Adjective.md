1. **Adjective**: relevant
   **Meaning**: 
   - Closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered; pertinent to the matter at hand
   - *Gradable adjective; can be used attributively (relevant information) or predicatively (this is relevant)*
   
   **Synonyms**: pertinent, applicable, appropriate, germane, related
   
   **Antonyms**: irrelevant, unrelated, inapplicable, immaterial
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and academic contexts when discussing what matters or applies to a particular subject; extremely common in professional and scholarly writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simpler words like "related" would be clearer in casual conversation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - relevant information, relevant to, highly relevant
   - directly relevant, particularly relevant, most relevant
   - relevant experience, relevant data
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + lev (lift, raise) + -ant (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin relevare (to raise up, relieve) → Medieval Latin relevantem (bearing upon) → French relevant → English "relevant" (pertinent, applicable)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is this relevant to what we're talking about?
   - I don't think that's relevant right now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All relevant information regarding the proposal has been provided.
   - The committee will review documents relevant to the investigation.

1. **Adjective**: pertinent
   **Meaning**: 
   - Relevant or applicable to a particular matter; appropriate to the situation
   - *Gradable adjective; primarily used attributively*
   
   **Synonyms**: relevant, applicable, appropriate, germane, apt
   
   **Antonyms**: irrelevant, impertinent, inapplicable, immaterial
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, especially in legal, academic, and professional writing; more formal than "relevant"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "relevant" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pertinent information, pertinent to, pertinent questions
   - pertinent details, pertinent facts, most pertinent
   - particularly pertinent
   
   **Root Analysis**: per- (through, thoroughly) + tin (hold, keep) + -ent (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin pertinere (to relate to, concern) → Old French pertinent → English "pertinent" (relevant, appropriate)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's a pertinent question regarding the schedule.
   - I'll only mention the most pertinent details.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please provide all pertinent documentation regarding the transaction.
   - The witness offered pertinent testimony regarding the events in question.

1. **Adjective**: related
   **Meaning**: 
   - Connected or associated with something; having a connection or relationship
   - *Gradable adjective; used attributively and predicatively*
   
   **Synonyms**: connected, associated, linked, allied, relevant
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated, separate, disconnected, independent
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when indicating connections or associations between things
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "similar" or "connected" would be more precise
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - related to, closely related, directly related
   - related issues, related matters, related questions
   - work-related, health-related
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + lat (carry, bring) + -ed (past participle/adjective)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin relatus (carried back) → English "relate" + -ed → "related" (connected, associated)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - These two problems are closely related.
   - I have some related questions regarding the assignment.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The document addresses related concerns regarding implementation.
   - Researchers examined issues related to data privacy.

1. **Adjective**: concerned
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Sense 1)* Worried or anxious about something
   - *(Sense 2)* Involved in or affected by something; relating to
   - *Gradable for sense 1; non-gradable for sense 2*
   
   **Synonyms**: worried, anxious, troubled (sense 1); involved, related, regarding (sense 2)
   
   **Antonyms**: unconcerned, unworried, indifferent
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts; sense 1 for worry, sense 2 in formal writing to mean "relating to"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When confusion might arise between the two meanings
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - concerned about, concerned with, deeply concerned
   - as far as... is concerned, concerned parties
   - all concerned, those concerned
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + cern (sift, separate) + -ed (past participle/adjective)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere → Middle French concerner → English "concern" + -ed → "concerned"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm concerned about what you said regarding the test.
   - Everyone concerned should attend the meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Stakeholders concerned are invited to submit feedback regarding the proposal.
   - The audit revealed issues concerning all departments concerned.

1. **Adjective**: respective
   **Meaning**: 
   - Belonging or relating separately to each of two or more people or things previously mentioned
   - *Non-gradable adjective; used attributively*
   
   **Synonyms**: individual, separate, particular, own
   
   **Antonyms**: collective, shared, common, joint
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when distinguishing items that belong to different people or groups
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where simpler terms like "their own" would work
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - their respective, in their respective, respective roles
   - respective positions, respective responsibilities
   - respective fields, respective areas
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + spect (look, see) + -ive (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin respectivus (having regard to) → Medieval Latin respectivus → English "respective" (relating separately to each)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They went back to their respective homes.
   - We each have our respective responsibilities regarding the project.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The departments submitted reports regarding their respective operational objectives.
   - Participants returned to their respective institutions following the conference.

1. **Adjective**: specific
   **Meaning**: 
   - Clearly defined or identified; precise and exact
   - Relating to a particular thing or category rather than general
   - *Gradable adjective; used attributively and predicatively*
   
   **Synonyms**: particular, precise, exact, definite, explicit
   
   **Antonyms**: general, vague, unspecific, generic, broad
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when emphasizing precision or particularity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "particular" or "exact" would be clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - specific to, more specific, be specific
   - specific information, specific details, specific requirements
   - specific regarding, specific about
   
   **Root Analysis**: spec (look, see) + -ific (making, creating)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin specificus (constituting a species) → French spécifique → English "specific" (precise, particular)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you be more specific regarding what you need?
   - I have some specific questions about the assignment.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The guidelines provide specific requirements regarding documentation.
   - Participants received specific instructions regarding the protocol.

1. **Adjective**: particular
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Sense 1)* Used to single out an individual member or thing from a group; specific
   - *(Sense 2)* Especially great or intense; notable
   - *(Sense 3)* Fastidious; hard to please
   - *Gradable adjective*
   
   **Synonyms**: specific, certain, individual, special, precise
   
   **Antonyms**: general, universal, common, unspecific
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when singling out specific items or emphasizing individual cases
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "specific" alone would be clearer and less formal
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in particular, particular attention, particular regarding
   - this particular, particular case, particular interest
   - nothing particular
   
   **Root Analysis**: part (part, piece) + -ic + -ular (adjective suffixes)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin particularis (of a part) → Old French particuler → Middle English particuler → "particular" (relating to a single instance)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is there anything particular you wanted to talk about?
   - I'm not looking for anything in particular.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This study focuses on particular aspects regarding implementation challenges.
   - The committee expressed particular concern regarding budget constraints.

1. **Adjective**: appropriate
   **Meaning**: 
   - Suitable or proper in the circumstances; fitting for a particular purpose or situation
   - *Gradable adjective; used attributively and predicatively*
   
   **Synonyms**: suitable, fitting, proper, apt, relevant
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriate, unsuitable, improper, unfit
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when discussing suitability or correctness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "right" or "suitable" would be simpler in casual speech
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - appropriate for, appropriate to, as appropriate
   - most appropriate, more appropriate, entirely appropriate
   - appropriate action, appropriate response
   
   **Root Analysis**: ap- (to, toward) + propri (own) + -ate (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin appropriatus (made one's own, fitting) → Late Latin appropriare → English "appropriate" (suitable, proper)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is this appropriate regarding what we discussed?
   - That doesn't seem appropriate for the situation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee will determine appropriate measures regarding compliance.
   - All staff must follow appropriate procedures regarding data handling.

1. **Adjective**: applicable
   **Meaning**: 
   - Relevant or appropriate in a particular situation; able to be applied
   - *Gradable adjective; primarily predicative*
   
   **Synonyms**: relevant, appropriate, pertinent, suitable, germane
   
   **Antonyms**: inapplicable, irrelevant, unsuitable, inappropriate
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, especially legal and technical writing, when discussing what rules or principles apply
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "relevant" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - applicable to, where applicable, not applicable
   - applicable laws, applicable regulations, applicable standards
   - directly applicable, equally applicable
   
   **Root Analysis**: ap- (to, toward) + plic (fold, apply) + -able (able to be)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin applicare (to apply, attach) → Medieval Latin applicabilis → English "applicable" (capable of being applied)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - These rules aren't applicable to our situation.
   - Is this applicable regarding what we're discussing?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All applicable regulations regarding safety must be observed.
   - The policy is applicable to all matters regarding employee conduct.

1. **Adjective**: corresponding
   **Meaning**: 
   - Similar in character, form, or function; matching or analogous
   - Having a relationship or connection with something else
   - *Gradable adjective; primarily attributive*
   
   **Synonyms**: matching, equivalent, analogous, related, parallel
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated, different, dissimilar, contrasting
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when indicating relationships or equivalences between things
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When simpler terms like "matching" or "related" would be clearer
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - corresponding to, corresponding with, corresponding changes
   - corresponding period, corresponding increase/decrease
   - corresponding section, corresponding data
   
   **Root Analysis**: cor- (together, with) + respond (answer, match) + -ing (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin correspondere (to answer together) → French correspondre → English "correspond" + -ing → "corresponding"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Check the corresponding section regarding instructions.
   - Each question has a corresponding answer key.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The report includes corresponding data regarding the previous fiscal year.
   - Corresponding adjustments must be made regarding the budget allocation.
