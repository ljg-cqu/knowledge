1. **Functional Expression**: Before we begin, let me... (function: preliminary statement/framing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to introduce preliminary information, context, or housekeeping matters before starting the main topic
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (softens transition to main content)
   
   **Synonyms**: First, let me..., To start with..., Let me begin by..., Initially, I'd like to...
   
   **Antonyms**: In conclusion..., To wrap up..., Finally...
   
   **When to Use**: Presentations, meetings, classes, formal discussions when providing context or preliminaries
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversations among friends; when immediate action is needed; after already starting
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Before we start, I'd like to...
   - Before we get into that, let me...
   - Before we proceed, I should mention...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Manages discourse flow; signals meta-commentary about upcoming content; face-saving (prepares audience)
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Certainly./Of course./Go ahead.
   - Please do./We're listening.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Before we begin, does anyone need a bathroom break?
   - Before we start, let me grab my notes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Before we begin today's discussion, let me outline the agenda.
   - Before we proceed, I must clarify several key assumptions.

1. **Functional Expression**: I should mention before... (function: providing advance information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to introduce important information that should be known in advance; provides context or warnings
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedged with "should")
   
   **Synonyms**: I need to tell you beforehand that..., Let me say upfront that..., Fair warning..., You should know that...
   
   **Antonyms**: I forgot to mention..., By the way..., Oh, I should have said...
   
   **When to Use**: Providing advance warnings, setting expectations, giving context before proceeding
   
   **When NOT to Use**: After the fact; when information isn't actually important; as excuse-making
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I should say before we continue...
   - I should warn you before...
   - I should clarify before we go further...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy (respects autonomy by providing information); manages expectations; prevents misunderstandings
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thanks for letting me know./I appreciate the heads up.
   - Good to know./That's helpful.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I should mention before we go—the place is pretty messy.
   - I should say before you agree—it's a lot of work.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I should mention before we proceed that several constraints apply.
   - I should clarify before continuing that this remains preliminary.

1. **Functional Expression**: Before you say anything... (function: preempting objections/responses)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to interrupt or prevent someone from speaking to provide explanation or justification first
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (controls conversation flow)
   
   **Synonyms**: Let me explain first..., Hear me out..., Wait, before you respond..., Hold on...
   
   **Antonyms**: Go ahead, what do you think?..., What's your reaction?..., Tell me what you think...
   
   **When to Use**: Anticipating negative reactions, preventing misunderstandings, controlling conversation flow
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When other person hasn't indicated they'll speak; formal presentations; when it sounds defensive
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Before you say no...
   - Before you judge...
   - Before you get upset...
   - Before you respond...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Manages turn-taking; preempts objections; can sound defensive if overused; controls dialogue
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Okay, I'm listening./Go ahead./Alright, explain.
   - Fine, but then it's my turn./This better be good.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Before you say anything, I can explain what happened.
   - Before you judge, hear the whole story.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Before you respond to the proposal, please review the supporting documentation.
   - Before you make a decision, consider the following factors.

1. **Functional Expression**: Can/Could I just say something before...? (function: requesting turn/permission)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Requests permission to speak or interject before something happens or continues
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal ("could" more polite)
   - *Directness*: indirect (asks permission)
   
   **Synonyms**: May I add something before...?, Can I interrupt before...?, If I could just mention..., Excuse me, but...
   
   **Antonyms**: (Direct statements without permission-seeking)
   
   **When to Use**: Formal meetings, interrupting politely, requesting speaking turns, showing deference
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Emergencies; when you have clear speaking rights; very casual among friends
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could I just add something before we move on?
   - Can I say one thing before we continue?
   - May I interject before we proceed?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness (asks permission); manages turn-taking; shows respect for current speaker; face-saving
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Of course./Certainly./Go ahead.
   - Actually, let me finish first./Can it wait?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can I just say something before we leave?
   - Could I add one thing before you decide?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could I just raise a concern before we proceed to voting?
   - May I clarify a point before we continue the discussion?

1. **Functional Expression**: I've never... before (function: expressing novelty/first experience)
   **Meaning**: 
   - States that something is a first-time experience; emphasizes novelty or lack of prior experience
   - *Formality*: neutral (works in all registers)
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: This is my first time..., I have no experience with..., This is new to me..., It's a first for me...
   
   **Antonyms**: I've done this many times..., I'm experienced with..., I'm familiar with...
   
   **When to Use**: Admitting inexperience, expressing excitement about new experience, setting expectations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When experience is required/expected and admission would be problematic; when exaggeration is clear
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I've never done this before
   - I've never been here before
   - I've never seen anything like this before
   - I've never experienced this before
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Sets expectations; admits limitations; can express humility or excitement; manages face
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - No worries, it's easy./Don't worry, I'll help you.
   - Really? You'll love it!/First time for everything!
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I've never tried sushi before.
   - I've never been to New York before.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I've never presented to such a large audience before.
   - I've never encountered this particular issue before in my practice.

1. **Functional Expression**: Before I forget... (function: interjecting remembered information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Introduces information that speaker suddenly remembers and wants to mention before forgetting
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (interrupts flow)
   
   **Synonyms**: Oh, by the way..., While I remember..., That reminds me..., Oh, I meant to tell you...
   
   **Antonyms**: (Planned, sequential information delivery)
   
   **When to Use**: Interjecting important information that just came to mind; casual conversations, informal meetings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal presentations; as excuse for poor planning; repeatedly (suggests disorganization)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Oh, before I forget to mention...
   - Before it slips my mind...
   - Remind me before I forget...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Explains interruption/topic shift; implies information importance; casual register marker
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What is it?/Yes?/Go ahead.
   - Thanks for remembering!/Good thing you mentioned it.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Before I forget, can you pick up milk?
   - Oh, before I forget—Sarah called earlier.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Before I forget to mention, the deadline has been extended.
   - Allow me to note, before I forget, that approvals are required.

1. **Functional Expression**: Let me check before... (function: delaying commitment/verifying)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expresses need to verify information or circumstances before proceeding or committing
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (delays commitment)
   
   **Synonyms**: I need to verify before..., Let me confirm before..., I should make sure before..., I'll need to check first...
   
   **Antonyms**: I can confirm that..., Definitely..., Yes, absolutely...
   
   **When to Use**: Avoiding premature commitments, showing responsibility, managing expectations, being careful
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When immediate answer is expected and possible; when it sounds like avoidance; as stalling tactic
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let me check my calendar before committing
   - Let me verify before confirming
   - I need to check before I agree
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness (avoids imposing); shows responsibility; manages expectations; prevents over-commitment
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Sure, no problem./Of course, let me know.
   - When can you tell me?/Take your time.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let me check before I say yes.
   - I need to check with my partner before deciding.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Let me verify availability before confirming the meeting.
   - I should check budget constraints before approving this expenditure.

1. **Functional Expression**: Before we go any further... (function: pausing for clarification/address issues)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Signals need to pause progress to address something important before continuing
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (stops progress)
   
   **Synonyms**: Before we continue..., Before we proceed..., Let's pause here for a moment..., Hold on...
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's continue..., Moving on..., Let's proceed...
   
   **When to Use**: Addressing problems, seeking clarification, raising concerns, ensuring understanding before proceeding
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When trivial issues arise; repeatedly (slows progress); as avoidance tactic
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Before we go further, we need to...
   - Before we continue, I have a question...
   - Before we move forward, let's address...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Controls discourse flow; signals importance; ensures alignment; can indicate concerns
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What is it?/Yes?/What's the issue?
   - Okay, let's address that./Good point.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Before we go any further, are you sure about this?
   - Hold on, before we go any further—what's the budget?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Before we go any further, we must address the legal implications.
   - Before we proceed, several concerns require resolution.

1. **Functional Expression**: I'd like to... before we... (function: stating preference/making request)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expresses desire or preference to do something before proceeding with something else
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedged with "would like")
   
   **Synonyms**: I want to... before..., I prefer to... first..., Could we... before...?, I think we should... before...
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's skip that and..., We can do that later..., There's no need to...
   
   **When to Use**: Making polite requests, expressing preferences, organizing sequence, managing process
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Emergencies; when direct commands are appropriate; when preference is trivial
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'd prefer to... before we...
   - I'd like to check/verify/discuss... before...
   - I think we should... before we...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness (hedges request); expresses preference without demanding; facilitates negotiation
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That makes sense./Good idea./Okay, let's do that.
   - Is that really necessary?/Can we do that later?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'd like to eat something before we go out.
   - I'd like to think about it before I decide.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I'd like to review the documentation before we finalize the agreement.
   - I'd like to consult with colleagues before committing to this approach.

1. **Functional Expression**: This has never happened before (function: expressing surprise/emphasizing abnormality)
   **Meaning**: 
   - States that current situation is unprecedented or highly unusual; emphasizes novelty or abnormality
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: This is unprecedented..., We've never seen this before..., This is a first..., This is unusual...
   
   **Antonyms**: This happens all the time..., This is normal..., We've seen this before...
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing surprise, emphasizing seriousness, explaining lack of precedent, justifying uncertainty
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When exaggerating; when precedents exist; as excuse for poor performance
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I've never seen this happen before
   - Nothing like this has happened before
   - We've never experienced this before
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Emphasizes abnormality; can excuse lack of preparation; expresses surprise or concern
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Really?/That's concerning./What should we do?
   - There's a first time for everything./Let's figure it out.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:

   **Examples [Formal]**:

   - Such volatility has never been observed before in this market.

1. **Functional Expression**: Make sure you... before... (function: giving instructions/warnings)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Instructs or advises someone to complete an action before proceeding with something else
   - *Formality*: neutral (can be informal or formal depending on context)
   - *Directness*: direct (imperative form)
   
   **Synonyms**: Be sure to... before..., Don't forget to... before..., Remember to... before..., Ensure you... before...
   
   **Antonyms**: You don't need to... before..., Skip that and..., Don't bother...
   
   **When to Use**: Giving advice, issuing instructions, providing warnings, ensuring proper sequence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When sequence doesn't matter; to people of higher status without softening; when advice is unsolicited
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Make sure to... before you...
   - Be sure you... before...
   - Ensure you... before... (more formal)
   - Remember to... before...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Can be directive or advisory; relationship context matters; "make sure" softer than "must"
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Okay, I will./Got it./Thanks for the reminder.
   - I know./Already did./Don't worry.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Make sure you lock the door before you leave.
   - Make sure you eat something before the exam.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Make sure you obtain approval before initiating the project.
   - Ensure all documentation is complete before submission.

1. **Functional Expression**: We need to... before... (function: stating requirement/necessity)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expresses necessity or requirement to complete something before proceeding
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct (states necessity)
   
   **Synonyms**: We must... before..., It's necessary to... before..., We have to... before..., We should... before...
   
   **Antonyms**: We don't need to... before..., We can skip..., That's unnecessary...
   
   **When to Use**: Stating requirements, organizing tasks, ensuring proper sequence, planning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "need" is too strong (use "should"); when requirement is personal preference only
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We'll need to... before we can...
   - We must... before...
   - It's essential to... before...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Expresses group necessity; implies collaboration; can be persuasive or directive depending on context
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Agreed./You're right./Let's do that.
   - Do we really need to?/Why is that necessary?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to stop for gas before we hit the highway.
   - We need to talk before you leave.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We need to secure funding before we can proceed with implementation.
   - We need to establish clear protocols before beginning data collection.

1. **Functional Expression**: As I mentioned before... (function: referring back/emphasizing repetition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Refers to previously stated information; can emphasize repetition or reminder
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (can be perceived as slightly impatient)
   
   **Synonyms**: As I said earlier..., Like I mentioned..., As previously stated..., To reiterate...
   
   **Antonyms**: Let me introduce a new point..., Here's something I haven't mentioned...
   
   **When to Use**: Reinforcing earlier points, reminding listeners, emphasizing importance, showing continuity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: First-time information; when it sounds condescending; repeatedly (implies listeners aren't paying attention)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - As I said before...
   - As previously mentioned...
   - Like I told you before...
   - As stated earlier...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Can signal frustration if overused; useful for continuity; may imply listener wasn't attentive
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Right, sorry./Yes, I remember./Go on.
   - I heard you the first time./Okay, okay.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As I mentioned before, I can't make it on Friday.
   - Like I said before, it's not a big deal.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As I mentioned before, several concerns require attention.
   - As previously stated, the methodology requires modification.
