1. **Expression**: before long (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Soon; in a short time from now; after a brief period; not long from now
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed idiomatic expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: soon, shortly, in a little while, in the near future, presently, ere long
   
   **Antonyms**: much later, eventually, in the distant future, never
   
   **When to Use**: Predictions, expectations, temporal references to near future; neutral to formal register
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When immediate action is meant (use "right away"); very distant future
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - it won't be long before
   - not before long
   - before too long
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *Idiomatic*: "Long" in this phrase paradoxically means "a long time," so "before long" = "before a long time passes" = "soon"
   - Established temporal idiom in English since Middle English period
   
   **Root Analysis**: before (earlier than) + long (extended period)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Before long, everyone will know about this.
   - He'll be back before long.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Before long, the technology became obsolete.
   - The situation will resolve itself before long.

   **Meaning**: 
   - Think carefully about consequences before taking action; consider risks before acting

   - *Fixedness*: strong (proverbial expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: think before acting, be cautious, consider the consequences, proceed carefully, measure twice cut once
   
   **Antonyms**: act impulsively, leap before you look, jump in headfirst, act rashly
   
   **When to Use**: Advising caution, warning against impulsiveness, recommending deliberation; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When encouraging boldness or quick action; when overthinking is the problem
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - you should look before you leap
   - looking before leaping
   - leap before you look (opposite meaning)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *Idiom*: Proverbial wisdom dating to medieval times; rhyming structure aids memorability
   - Physical metaphor (checking before jumping) extended to decision-making
   
   **Root Analysis**: Proverbial construction with rhyme (leap/before)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You should look before you leap with this investment.
   - Remember to look before you leap!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *"Typically informal; avoid in formal contexts"* - The principle of careful deliberation applies in strategic planning.

1. **Expression**: prior to (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Before; preceding in time; earlier than
   - More formal alternative to "before"
   - *Fixedness*: strong (formal temporal preposition)
   
   **Synonyms**: before, in advance of, ahead of, preceding, earlier than
   
   **Antonyms**: after, following, subsequent to, later than
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing, academic papers, professional communication, technical documentation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (prefer "before"); when simpler language is preferred
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - prior to [event]
   - prior to [time]
   - immediately prior to
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *Phrasal preposition*: "Prior" (adjective/adverb) + "to" creates formal prepositional phrase
   - Latin-influenced formal alternative to Anglo-Saxon "before"
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin prior (earlier) + to (directional preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Rare in casual use; prefer "before")
   - We met prior to the conference.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Prior to implementation, comprehensive testing must be conducted.
   - Informed consent was obtained prior to data collection.
