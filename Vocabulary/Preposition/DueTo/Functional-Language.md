1. **Functional Expression**: This is due to [noun/noun phrase] (function: explaining/attributing cause)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to attribute or explain the cause of something previously mentioned
   - *Formality*: formal/neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: This results from, This stems from, This is because of, This is caused by
   
   **Antonyms**: This occurs despite, This happens regardless of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal presentations, academic writing, business reports when explaining causes explicitly; technical contexts requiring precise causation statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation where "It's because..." is more natural; when cause is uncertain (use "may be due to" instead)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - This is largely due to...
   - This is primarily due to...
   - This may be due to...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Direct attribution statement; establishes clear cause-effect relationship; appropriate for objective, analytical discourse; avoids hedging unless uncertainty exists
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I see, so [restatement of cause]
   - That makes sense because...
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The delay? This is due to heavy traffic.
   - This is due to a system error.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The decline in enrollment is due to demographic changes.
   - This phenomenon is due to thermal expansion.

1. **Functional Expression**: The reason for [X] is that... (function: explaining/giving reasons)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to provide explicit explanation or justification for something
   - *Formality*: neutral/formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: X is because..., X results from..., The cause of X is...
   
   **Antonyms**: Despite X, Regardless of...
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when providing clear, explicit reasons; appropriate for explanations, justifications, and analytical statements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When multiple complex causes exist (use "One reason is..." or "Reasons include...")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - The main reason for X is...
   - One reason for X is...
   - The primary reason is...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Clear, explicit explanation structure; signals forthcoming justification; useful for organizing logical explanations; appropriate for both spoken and written discourse
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I understand the reasoning
   - That explains it
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The reason for the change is that we need more space.
   - The reason I'm late is that my car broke down.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The reason for policy modification is that outcomes were suboptimal.
   - The reason for increased costs is that materials prices rose.

1. **Functional Expression**: Owing to [circumstances/reasons]... (function: introducing causal context)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to introduce circumstances or reasons as context for an outcome or decision
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Due to, Because of, As a result of, On account of
   
   **Antonyms**: Despite, In spite of, Regardless of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing and announcements; business communications explaining decisions or changes; more common in British English
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where "because of" is more natural; American English where "due to" is more common
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Owing to circumstances beyond our control
   - Owing to unforeseen events
   - Owing to the fact that
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Formal announcement or explanation structure; often used for explanations in official communications; maintains professional distance; appropriate for written notices and formal statements
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Understood, thank you for the explanation
   - We appreciate the notice
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Owing to the weather, we're cancelling.
   - Owing to technical issues, there's a delay.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Owing to unforeseen circumstances, the event is postponed.
   - Owing to budgetary constraints, the program is suspended.
