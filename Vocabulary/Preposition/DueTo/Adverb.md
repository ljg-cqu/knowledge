1. **Adverb**: consequently (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As a result; therefore; for this reason
   - Used to show that something is the result of something else
   
   **Synonyms**: therefore, thus, accordingly, hence, as a result
   
   **Antonyms**: nevertheless, however, despite this
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal writing to signal cause-effect relationships; common in academic and professional contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation where "so" is more natural; avoid overuse in single passage
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - consequently resulted in
   - and consequently
   - consequently became
   - consequently led to
   - consequently changed
   
   **Root Analysis**: consequent + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin consequent- (following) + -ly → meaning "as a logical result"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The roads were icy; consequently, we stayed home.
   - He missed the deadline and consequently lost the contract.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The hypothesis was disproven; consequently, the framework required revision.
   - Resources were limited; consequently, priorities had to be established.

1. **Adverb**: therefore (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For that reason; as a result of that; consequently
   - Used to introduce a logical conclusion or result
   
   **Synonyms**: thus, hence, consequently, accordingly, so
   
   **Antonyms**: however, nevertheless, nonetheless
   
   **When to Use**: All registers to signal logical conclusions; particularly common in argumentative and analytical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When no clear causal or logical relationship exists between statements
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - therefore must
   - therefore is
   - and therefore
   - therefore should
   - therefore became
   
   **Root Analysis**: there (that) + fore (before, in front)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English ther (there) + fore (before) → meaning "for that reason"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm busy; therefore, I can't come.
   - He agreed, therefore we proceeded.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The correlation is significant; therefore, the hypothesis is supported.
   - Markets collapsed; therefore, intervention was necessary.

1. **Adverb**: thus (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As a result or consequence of this; in this way; therefore
   - In the manner now being indicated or exemplified
   
   **Synonyms**: therefore, consequently, hence, accordingly, so
   
   **Antonyms**: however, nevertheless, conversely
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing to signal cause-effect or to introduce examples; common in academic contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation where it sounds overly formal
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - thus creating
   - thus resulting in
   - thus leading to
   - and thus
   - thus making
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English thus (in this way)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English þus (in this way) → meaning "in this manner, as a result"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We missed the bus, thus arriving late.
   - He saved money, thus affording the trip.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The sample was contaminated, thus invalidating the results.
   - Demand exceeded supply, thus driving prices upward.

1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; correspondingly
   - As a result; consequently; therefore
   
   **Synonyms**: consequently, therefore, thus, hence, correspondingly
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, regardless, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing to signal appropriate response or consequence; common in business and academic contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where simpler connectors are more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - adjusted accordingly
   - proceeded accordingly
   - acted accordingly
   - responded accordingly
   - planned accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: according + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English accord (agreement) + -ing + -ly → meaning "in accordance, consequently"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather changed, so we planned accordingly.
   - Prices rose and we adjusted accordingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Risk levels increased; resources were allocated accordingly.
   - The evidence suggested fraud; we proceeded accordingly.

1. **Adverb**: hence (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As a consequence; for this reason; therefore
   - From this time; from now
   
   **Synonyms**: therefore, thus, consequently, accordingly
   
   **Antonyms**: nevertheless, however, despite this
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and academic writing to signal logical conclusions or consequences
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where it sounds archaic or overly formal
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - hence the need
   - hence the importance
   - and hence
   - hence resulting
   - hence explaining
   
   **Root Analysis**: Middle English hennes (from here)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English hennes (from here) → meaning "from this point, as a result"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He's allergic to nuts, hence the careful menu planning.
   - The road is closed, hence the detour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Data were incomplete; hence, conclusions remained tentative.
   - Constraints were significant, hence the need for prioritization.

1. **Adverb**: directly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(In causation)* In a direct manner; immediately; without intervening factors
   - With nothing or no one in between
   
   **Synonyms**: immediately, straightforwardly, proximately
   
   **Antonyms**: indirectly, mediately, remotely
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when emphasizing immediate or unmediated causation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing complex or mediated relationships
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - directly caused by
   - directly related to
   - directly attributable to
   - directly resulting from
   - directly linked to
   
   **Root Analysis**: direct + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin directus (straight) + -ly → meaning "in a straight manner, immediately"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The accident was directly caused by speeding.
   - Success directly resulted from hard work.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Outcomes were directly attributable to the intervention.
   - The effect is directly proportional to the dose.

1. **Adverb**: indirectly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(In causation)* In an indirect manner; through intermediate stages or factors
   - Not in a direct way or by direct connection
   
   **Synonyms**: obliquely, circuitously, mediately
   
   **Antonyms**: directly, immediately, straightforwardly
   
   **When to Use**: Analytical contexts when describing mediated or complex causation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing clear, immediate causation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - indirectly caused by
   - indirectly related to
   - indirectly affected by
   - indirectly contributing to
   - indirectly linked to
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + direct + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin in- (not) + directus (straight) + -ly → meaning "not in a direct manner"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The problem was indirectly caused by budget cuts.
   - They were indirectly affected by the decision.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Benefits accrued indirectly through improved infrastructure.
   - Variables were indirectly associated through mediating factors.

1. **Adverb**: largely (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To a great extent; mostly; mainly; predominantly
   - For the most part; in large measure
   
   **Synonyms**: mostly, mainly, chiefly, primarily, predominantly
   
   **Antonyms**: slightly, minimally, marginally, barely
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when indicating primary but not exclusive causation; appropriate for formal and informal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing complete or sole causation (use "entirely" or "solely")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - largely due to
   - largely because of
   - largely attributable to
   - largely responsible for
   - largely the result of
   
   **Root Analysis**: large + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French large (generous, broad) + -ly → meaning "to a great extent"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The success was largely due to teamwork.
   - Problems were largely caused by miscommunication.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The decline is largely attributable to demographic shifts.
   - Outcomes were largely determined by initial conditions.

1. **Adverb**: inadvertently (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without intention; accidentally; unintentionally
   - By accident rather than design
   
   **Synonyms**: accidentally, unintentionally, unwittingly, by mistake
   
   **Antonyms**: deliberately, intentionally, purposely, consciously
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts when describing unintended causation; appropriate for formal and informal registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing deliberate or intentional actions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - inadvertently caused
   - inadvertently created
   - inadvertently led to
   - inadvertently contributed to
   - inadvertently triggered
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + ad- (to) + vert (turn) + -ent + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin in- (not) + advertere (to turn attention to) + -ly → meaning "without attention, accidentally"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I inadvertently caused the problem.
   - She inadvertently revealed the secret.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy inadvertently created perverse incentives.
   - Changes inadvertently undermined program effectiveness.
