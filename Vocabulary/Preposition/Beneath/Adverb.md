1. **Adverb**: underneath (type: place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In or to a position below or beneath something; on the underside or lower surface
   - *(Figurative)* At a deeper or more fundamental level; beneath the surface appearance
   
   **Synonyms**: beneath, below, under, at the bottom, lower down
   
   **Antonyms**: above, overhead, on top, over
   
   **When to Use**: When describing location or position beneath something, particularly when emphasizing the underside or what's concealed below
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing levels in hierarchy without physical position (use "below" in rank), or in formal writing where "beneath" might be more appropriate
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - look underneath
   - hidden underneath
   - buried underneath
   - lies underneath
   - placed underneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: under (beneath) + neath (below, from Old English neoþan)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English underneothan (beneath) → Middle English undernethe → modern "underneath" emphasizing beneath-position
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Check underneath the couch—your phone might have fallen there.
   - Underneath all that attitude, she's actually pretty insecure.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The structural supports are positioned underneath to provide maximum stability.
   - Underneath the superficial similarities lie fundamental theoretical differences.

2. **Adverb**: below (type: place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At a lower level or position; beneath something in position or rank
   - In or to a place at a lower level; further down
   
   **Synonyms**: beneath, underneath, lower down, downward, under
   
   **Antonyms**: above, overhead, higher, upward, over
   
   **When to Use**: When indicating position at a lower level, or referring to something mentioned later in text; more neutral than "beneath"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When direct contact is emphasized (use "under"), or when emphasizing unworthiness (use "beneath")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - mentioned below
   - listed below
   - explained below
   - fall below
   - sink below
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by, about) + low (not high)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English bilooghe (at a lower position) → modern "below" indicating beneath-position
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The answer key is printed below on the next page.
   - Your test scores fell way below what we were expecting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The methodology is described in detail below.
   - Performance indicators consistently remained below established benchmarks.

3. **Adverb**: down (type: place/direction)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Toward or in a lower place or position; moving from a higher to a lower level
   - To or at a position beneath the surface
   
   **Synonyms**: downward, beneath, lower, to the bottom, below
   
   **Antonyms**: up, upward, higher, above, overhead
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing downward movement or direction toward a beneath-position, or when describing position at a lower level
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing static position without movement (use "beneath" or "below"), or when upward direction is meant
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - go down beneath
   - dig down beneath
   - look down beneath
   - push down beneath
   - settle down beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English dūne (downward, from the hill)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English dūne → Middle English doun → modern "down" indicating downward or beneath-direction
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You'll need to dig down beneath the frost line to plant those posts.
   - The submarine went down beneath the surface and disappeared.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Drilling operations extend down beneath the sedimentary layers to reach bedrock.
   - The analysis drills down beneath superficial data patterns to reveal underlying trends.

4. **Adverb**: deep (type: place/degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Far down from the surface; extending a long way beneath
   - *(Figurative)* Profoundly; at a fundamental or hidden level beneath the surface
   
   **Synonyms**: deeply, far beneath, far down, profoundly, way below
   
   **Antonyms**: shallowly, superficially, near the surface, slightly
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing significant distance beneath a surface, or metaphorically for profound or fundamental levels
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing shallow or surface-level positions, or when precision rather than depth is the focus
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - buried deep beneath
   - hidden deep beneath
   - lies deep beneath
   - runs deep beneath
   - extends deep beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English dēop (extending far down)
   
   **Etymology**: Proto-Germanic *deupaz (deep) → Old English dēop → used adverbially to indicate far beneath the surface
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The treasure is supposed to be buried deep beneath that old oak tree.
   - Deep beneath her cheerful exterior, she was struggling with depression.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Mineral deposits lie deep beneath the Earth's crust, beyond current extraction capabilities.
   - The psychological trauma remained buried deep beneath conscious awareness.

5. **Adverb**: secretly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a secret manner; hidden from view or knowledge beneath surface appearances
   - Without others knowing; covertly
   
   **Synonyms**: covertly, privately, surreptitiously, hidden beneath notice, concealed
   
   **Antonyms**: openly, publicly, obviously, overtly, visibly
   
   **When to Use**: When describing actions or states that are deliberately concealed beneath outward appearances or kept hidden from others
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing open or obvious actions, or when there's no element of concealment
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - secretly hidden beneath
   - secretly kept beneath
   - secretly stored beneath
   - operating secretly beneath
   - secretly lying beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: secret (from Latin secretus "hidden") + -ly (manner)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin secretus (set apart, hidden) → French secret → English "secretly" (in a hidden manner, beneath notice)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He was secretly storing money beneath the floorboards for years.
   - She secretly thinks his jokes are funny, beneath all her eye-rolling.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization was secretly operating beneath a legitimate business facade.
   - Data were secretly encoded beneath seemingly innocuous communications.

6. **Adverb**: invisibly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that cannot be seen; hidden from view beneath or behind something
   - In a manner that escapes notice
   
   **Synonyms**: unseen, imperceptibly, hidden, concealed, beneath detection
   
   **Antonyms**: visibly, obviously, apparently, clearly, noticeably
   
   **When to Use**: When describing something that operates or exists beneath the level of visibility or perception
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing clearly visible phenomena, or when emphasizing visibility rather than concealment
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - operating invisibly beneath
   - flowing invisibly beneath
   - working invisibly beneath
   - existing invisibly beneath
   - moving invisibly beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + visible (able to be seen) + -ly (manner)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin invisibilis (not visible) → English "invisibly" (in a manner beneath visibility)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Water was flowing invisibly beneath the surface, causing the erosion.
   - The real power structure operates invisibly beneath the official hierarchy.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The mechanisms operate invisibly beneath the observable phenomena.
   - Cultural assumptions function invisibly beneath conscious reasoning processes.

7. **Adverb**: fundamentally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In central or primary respects; at the deepest or most basic level beneath surface manifestations
   - In a way that affects the foundation or essence
   
   **Synonyms**: basically, essentially, at the core, at root, beneath it all
   
   **Antonyms**: superficially, trivially, peripherally, marginally
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing the basic, underlying, or essential nature of something beneath surface features
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When discussing surface-level or minor aspects, or when emphasizing variety rather than basic nature
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - fundamentally different beneath
   - fundamentally similar beneath
   - fundamentally unchanged beneath
   - fundamentally flawed beneath
   - fundamentally sound beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: fundament (Latin: foundation, base) + -al + -ly (manner)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin fundamentum (foundation) → "fundamentally" meaning at the foundation or beneath surface levels
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Beneath all the changes, the game is fundamentally the same.
   - They're fundamentally different people beneath those superficial similarities.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The theories are fundamentally incompatible beneath superficial terminological similarities.
   - The proposal is fundamentally sound despite minor procedural concerns.

8. **Adverb**: inwardly (type: manner/place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In one's mind or spirit rather than outwardly; beneath the external appearance
   - Toward the inside or interior; beneath the surface
   
   **Synonyms**: internally, privately, beneath the surface, deep down, inside
   
   **Antonyms**: outwardly, externally, on the surface, visibly, openly
   
   **When to Use**: When contrasting inner feelings or reality with outer appearance; when describing what's beneath surface presentation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing external or visible characteristics, or when there's no inner/outer contrast
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - inwardly beneath the surface
   - inwardly different
   - feel inwardly
   - think inwardly
   - suffer inwardly beneath
   
   **Root Analysis**: inward (toward the inside) + -ly (manner)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English inweard (toward the interior) → "inwardly" meaning beneath the exterior or surface
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He was smiling, but inwardly he was furious beneath that calm exterior.
   - Beneath her confident talk, she was inwardly panicking about the exam.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The patient remained inwardly distressed beneath an outward appearance of composure.
   - Beneath diplomatic pleasantries, both parties were inwardly calculating strategic advantages.
