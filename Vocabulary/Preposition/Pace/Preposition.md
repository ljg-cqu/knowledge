1. **Preposition**: pace (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* With all due respect to (used before a person's name, group, or widely accepted view when politely indicating disagreement or exception).
   
   **Synonyms**: with all due respect to (in contexts of polite disagreement), despite (the opinion of), contrary to (the view of), notwithstanding (the authority of)
   
   **Antonyms**: in accordance with, in line with, according to, in support of
   
   **When to Use**: Use mainly in formal written English—academic articles, essays, legal or intellectual debates—when you want to acknowledge another person's authority or reputation while clearly signaling that your own position differs from theirs.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in everyday conversation or simple emails, where it can sound pretentious or confusing. Do not use before ordinary objects or events (it almost always precedes a person, school of thought, or established belief). Do not use merely to mean "according to"—it normally implies respectful disagreement, not neutral citation.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pace Aristotle, pace my distinguished colleague, pace conventional wisdom, pace the critics
   - **Verb + prep**: argue, pace Smith, that..., suggest, pace earlier studies, that...
   - **Adjective + prep**: controversial, pace some reviewers, for...
   - **Noun + prep**: consensus, pace a minority of scholars, that...
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin *pace* (ablative singular of *pax* "peace") meaning "with the peace/permission of" → used formulaically as "with all due respect to" someone whose view you are about to question.
   
   **Etymology**: Learned borrowing from Latin in legal and scholarly writing. Originally part of phrases like *pace X* "with X's permission," it developed into a conventional preposition in English indicating polite disagreement with a respected authority or commonly held view.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Pace my friends who love working at breakneck speed, I still think a steadier pace is healthier.
   - Pace my parents, I chose a different career path and I'm happy with the slower pace of life it offers.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pace earlier commentators, this paper argues that the rapid pace of reform was a feature, not a flaw, of the policy design.
   - Pace Newton, modern physics treats space and time as a single continuum rather than entirely separate entities.
