1. **Functional Expression**: Let's pick up the pace (function: suggesting / motivating)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to encourage others to move, work, or decide faster.
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct but usually friendly
   
   **Synonyms**: Let's speed things up, Let's move a bit faster, Let's step up the pace
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's slow down, Let's take it easy, We can afford to relax the pace
   
   **When to Use**: Team work, meetings, sports, or study sessions where the current speed feels too slow for the goal or deadline.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when people are already stressed or exhausted; can sound demanding or insensitive if overused.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We need to pick up the pace.
   - Can we pick up the pace a little?
   - It's time to pick up the pace.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Positions the speaker as task-focused and time-aware; tone can be motivating or pressuring depending on voice and context.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Sure, let's do it.
   - Okay, I'll try to move faster.
   - I am already at my limit; can we keep this pace?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The deadline is tomorrow; let's pick up the pace and finish this.
   - We're walking too slowly; let's pick up the pace a bit.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Given the revised deadline, we may need to pick up the pace in the next sprint.
   - The chair suggested that the committee pick up the pace to cover all agenda items.

1. **Functional Expression**: Can we slow down the pace a bit? (function: requesting / negotiating)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite request to reduce the speed or intensity of work, speech, or change.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect-softened request
   
   **Synonyms**: Could we take things more slowly?, Can we ease up a little?, Is it possible to slow the pace down?
   
   **Antonyms**: Can we move faster?, Let's speed up, Let's push harder
   
   **When to Use**: When you feel overloaded, lost, or worried about quality at the current pace; suitable in meetings, classes, and collaborative work.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Emergencies or crises where speed is non-negotiable; highly hierarchical cultures where juniors rarely question pace.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could we slow down the pace during this part?
   - Can we slow the pace a little so everyone can follow?
   - Is there any way to slow the pace next week?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals vulnerability and care for quality or wellbeing; appeals to fairness and shared responsibility.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Yes, let's slow down here.
   - We can slow the pace for this section.
   - I understand, but the deadline makes it hard to slow down.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can we slow down the pace a bit? I'm getting lost.
   - This workout is too intense. Can we slow the pace for a while?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Given the error rate, may we slow down the pace of deployment?
   - Several participants asked whether the instructor could slow down the pace of the lecture.

1. **Functional Expression**: I'm struggling to keep up with the pace (function: expressing difficulty / raising risk)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Signals that the current speed of work or learning is too fast for the speaker.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct but framed as personal experience
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm finding this pace hard to sustain, I'm having trouble keeping up, This is moving too fast for me
   
   **Antonyms**: This pace is fine for me, I can easily keep up, We could even go faster
   
   **When to Use**: One-on-ones, team meetings, or classes where you need to warn others that the current pace is unsustainable.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In highly competitive cultures where admitting difficulty may be punished; in those cases reframe as concern about quality.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm having trouble keeping up with the pace.
   - I'm not able to keep up with this pace.
   - I find it hard to keep up with the current pace.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Honest self-disclosure; can prevent burnout and serious mistakes if leaders respond constructively.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thanks for telling me; let's review the workload.
   - What would be a more sustainable pace for you?
   - I hear you, but we have limited flexibility until this deadline.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm struggling to keep up with the pace of messages in this group.
   - I'm struggling to keep up with the pace at this gym class.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Several team members reported that they were struggling to keep up with the pace of change.
   - New hires often struggle to keep up with the initial pace of training.

1. **Functional Expression**: Let's go at your own pace (function: offering support / reducing pressure)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Offers permission to choose a comfortable speed, often to reduce anxiety or protect wellbeing.
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct, supportive
   
   **Synonyms**: Take your time, We can move as slowly as you need, We will follow your pace
   
   **Antonyms**: You have to keep up, We must move faster, There is no time to slow down
   
   **When to Use**: Coaching, teaching, mentoring, or health contexts where the other person's pace should lead (for example, rehab, language learning).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: High-stakes situations with fixed timing (exams, flights, emergency procedures).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We can go at your own pace.
   - You can go at your own pace.
   - Let's move at a pace that works for you.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Communicates safety and flexibility; lowers performance anxiety and encourages honest feedback.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thanks, that helps a lot.
   - I appreciate being able to go at my own pace.
   - Great, I'll tell you if it feels too fast.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Don't worry, we can go at your own pace while you learn this.
   - Let's go at your own pace until you feel more confident.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The instructor emphasized that participants could go at their own pace during the workshop.
   - Therapists often invite clients to go at their own pace when discussing difficult topics.

1. **Functional Expression**: We need to find a sustainable pace (function: negotiating workload / preventing burnout)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Proposes adjusting speed and workload so that people can continue over the long term.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct but collaborative
   
   **Synonyms**: We should slow down to a manageable level, We need a more realistic tempo, We must balance speed and wellbeing
   
   **Antonyms**: We have to push as hard as possible, We cannot ease off, We must keep this breakneck pace
   
   **When to Use**: Strategic discussions about work intensity, project planning, or organizational change, especially after periods of overwork.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very short emergency sprints where everyone already understands the temporary nature of the pace.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let's find a sustainable pace.
   - We have to move at a more sustainable pace.
   - This is not a sustainable pace.
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Frames pace as a shared design choice, not just an individual weakness; invites collaborative problem-solving.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I agree; let's look at our commitments.
   - What would a sustainable pace look like for this team?
   - The constraints are tight, but we can negotiate some changes.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to find a sustainable pace or we will all burn out.
   - This study plan is not a sustainable pace for you.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Leadership acknowledged the need to find a sustainable pace after several quarters of intense growth.
   - The report concludes that reforms must proceed at a sustainable pace to avoid implementation fatigue.
