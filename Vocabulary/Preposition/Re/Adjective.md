1. **Adjective**: related (comparative: more related, superlative: most related)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Connected in some way; having a relationship with something else
   
   **Synonyms**: connected, linked, associated, relevant, corresponding
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated, separate, independent, distinct
   
   **When to Use**: Describing topics, issues, or documents that are re (about) the same subject: "related documents", "topic-related questions".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you need to emphasise strong importance rather than simple connection (use "critical" or "key").
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - closely related to
   - related documents
   - related issues re [topic]
   - topic-related questions
   - work-related email
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + lat (carry) + -ed (having) → "having a carried-back connection"
   
   **Etymology**: From verb "relate" plus -ed; meaning "connected".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's not really related to what we're talking about.
   - I have a few related questions re your trip.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - This section summarises related work re neural networks.
   - Please attach all related documents re your claim.

1. **Adjective**: relevant (comparative: more relevant, superlative: most relevant)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Directly connected with and important to what is being discussed or considered
   
   **Synonyms**: pertinent, applicable, germane, related, connected
   
   **Antonyms**: irrelevant, unrelated, beside the point
   
   **When to Use**: Judging whether information is worth including in an email, report, or discussion re (about) a specific issue.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For personal value judgements unrelated to the topic ("nice", "interesting" may be better).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - relevant information
   - relevant experience
   - relevant to
   - not relevant re this matter
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again/back) + lev (raise) → "raise again" (bring up appropriately)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *relevare* (to raise again) → French → English "relevant".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Is that really relevant to what we're deciding?
   - Only share the relevant details re your trip.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please include only relevant documents re your application.
   - The study focuses on variables relevant to policy design.

1. **Adjective**: applicable (comparative: more applicable, superlative: most applicable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - That can be applied or is suitable in a particular situation or to a particular subject
   
   **Synonyms**: relevant, appropriate, suitable, pertinent, fitting
   
   **Antonyms**: inapplicable, irrelevant, inappropriate
   
   **When to Use**: In policies, guidelines, and emails re rules or procedures: "where applicable", "if applicable".
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual situations where "fits" or "works" would be more natural.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - where applicable
   - if applicable
   - not applicable (N/A)
   - applicable to
   
   **Root Analysis**: ap- (to) + plic (fold) + -able (capable of) → "capable of being folded onto" (applied)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *applicare* (to attach to) → French → English "applicable".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Fill in the sections that are applicable to you.
   - Some of these rules aren't really applicable here.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please complete all applicable fields re your employment history.
   - The policy is applicable to all staff re use of company devices.

1. **Adjective**: pertinent (comparative: more pertinent, superlative: most pertinent)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Directly related to and important for the topic being considered
   
   **Synonyms**: relevant, applicable, germane, appropriate
   
   **Antonyms**: irrelevant, tangential, extraneous
   
   **When to Use**: In formal or academic writing when emphasising that a comment or question is clearly re (about) the matter at hand.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual speech ("relevant" is more common); when you want to sound neutral and simple.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - pertinent to
   - highly pertinent
   - not pertinent
   - questions pertinent to this case
   
   **Root Analysis**: per- (thoroughly) + ten (hold) → "holding thoroughly" (clearly connected)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *pertinere* (to pertain) → French → English "pertinent".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's not really pertinent to what we're re discussing.
   - Do you have any pertinent questions re the trip?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Only issues pertinent to the claim re damages will be heard.
   - The committee welcomed several pertinent comments re transparency.

1. **Adjective**: corresponding (comparative: more corresponding, superlative: most corresponding)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Matching or consistent with something; having a direct relationship with
   
   **Synonyms**: matching, equivalent, parallel, analogous, aligned
   
   **Antonyms**: mismatched, inconsistent, conflicting
   
   **When to Use**: Describing items, figures, or sections that are re (about) each other—used especially in tables, contracts, and technical documentation.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For personal relationships ("close", "similar" may be better), or where simple "same" is sufficient.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - corresponding section
   - corresponding figure
   - corresponding item re each category
   - corresponding data
   
   **Root Analysis**: co- (together) + respond (answer) + -ing (having)
   
   **Etymology**: From verb "correspond"; adjective "corresponding" means "matching".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Each point has a corresponding note re what to do.
   - The colours have corresponding labels.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - See the corresponding annex re implementation details.
   - The corresponding figures re Q2 are shown in Table 3.

1. **Adjective**: topic-related (comparative: more topic-related, superlative: most topic-related)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Directly connected to the subject under discussion
   
   **Synonyms**: subject-related, on-topic, relevant, related
   
   **Antonyms**: off-topic, unrelated, irrelevant
   
   **When to Use**: In teaching or meeting contexts where you want to distinguish material that is re the current topic from distractions.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very formal academic writing ("relevant" or "pertinent" are preferred); in casual talk where "about this" is easier.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - topic-related questions
   - topic-related vocabulary
   - topic-related emails re the project
   
   **Root Analysis**: topic (subject) + related (connected)
   
   **Etymology**: Modern compound of "topic" + "related".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please keep your comments topic-related.
   - I made a list of topic-related phrases re job interviews.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Only topic-related questions re the proposed policy will be considered today.
   - Students should focus on topic-related sources when writing their essays.

1. **Adjective**: document-specific (comparative: more document-specific, superlative: most document-specific)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Restricted to, or designed for, a particular document or text
   
   **Synonyms**: text-specific, case-specific, context-specific, tailored
   
   **Antonyms**: general, generic, broad, universal
   
   **When to Use**: When talking about references, `Re:` lines, or guidance that only apply to one particular document or case.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For broad rules or general principles.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - document-specific reference
   - document-specific guidance re
   - document-specific notes re this case
   
   **Root Analysis**: document (written text) + specific (particular)
   
   **Etymology**: Modern compound; "specific" from Latin *species* (kind).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Those comments are very document-specific—they're just re that report.
   - These notes are document-specific, not general advice.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please include document-specific references re each annex.
   - The following recommendations are document-specific and should not be generalised.
