1. **Expression**: Re: your application (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A subject-line or heading formula introducing the topic of a letter or email related to someone's application
   - *Fixedness*: strong (conventional heading pattern in business and academic correspondence)
   
   **Synonyms**: Regarding your application, Concerning your application, About your application
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: In email or letter subject lines and headings when you want to make the topic immediately clear (for example, job applications, university places, grants).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Inside normal sentences (use "regarding your application" instead); in very informal texts where a plain subject like "Your job application" is enough.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Re: your recent application for the [Role]
   - Re: your application dated [Date]
   - Re: your scholarship application
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: Conventional business-letter pattern using `Re:` plus a concise noun phrase to label the document's subject.
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin *re* (about) + colon + noun phrase "your application"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Subject: Re: your application—quick question
   - Just a note Re: your application; can you confirm your start date?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Re: your application for the position of Senior Analyst.
   - Re: your application to the MSc in Data Science.

1. **Expression**: questions re your order (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A compact way to say "questions regarding your order" in emails, chats, or notes
   - *Fixedness*: medium (pattern `questions re + noun` is common, but noun varies)
   
   **Synonyms**: questions about your order, queries regarding your order, inquiries about your order
   
   **Antonyms**: answers about your order, confirmation of your order
   
   **When to Use**: In customer-service or internal emails when raising or answering questions about a specific order, invoice, or booking; semi-formal to neutral.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal legal letters (prefer "questions regarding"); very casual chat ("questions about" may sound more natural).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - questions re your invoice
   - questions re your booking
   - follow-up questions re yesterday's delivery
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Business English favours short prepositional markers like `re` after abstract nouns such as "questions," "concerns," and "decisions" to keep subject lines and notes concise.
   
   **Root Analysis**: questions + re (about) + noun phrase
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I have a couple of questions re your order—can I call you?
   - Any questions re the new system, just ping me.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Should you have any questions re your order, please contact Customer Support.
   - We are happy to answer any questions re your recent purchase.

1. **Expression**: decision re [matter] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A formal choice or ruling about a specific matter
   - *Fixedness*: medium (decision re + noun phrase is a standard legal/business pattern)
   
   **Synonyms**: decision regarding, ruling on, determination about, outcome for
   
   **Antonyms**: indecision about, pending question re
   
   **When to Use**: In minutes, memos, and official letters when stating what has been decided about a case, proposal, or application.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very informal chat ("decision about" is clearer for learners); creative writing where legal/business tone is not desired.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - decision re your application
   - decision re budget allocation
   - final decision re the merger
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Legal and corporate writing compresses "decision regarding" to "decision re" in headings and short summaries.
   
   **Root Analysis**: decision + re (about) + noun phrase
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Have they made a decision re the new schedule yet?
   - We'll let you know our decision re your proposal soon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board reached a decision re the restructuring plan on 12 May.
   - This letter confirms our decision re your tenancy renewal.

1. **Expression**: memo re [topic] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A short written message about a particular topic within an organization
   - *Fixedness*: medium ("memo re" commonly appears in internal headings)
   
   **Synonyms**: memo regarding, memo on, note about
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: For internal communication headings or titles in offices, especially when labelling the subject clearly and briefly.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: External customer-facing communication (often uses "letter" or "email" instead of "memo"); very informal chat tools.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - memo re office relocation
   - memo re updated security procedures
   - memo re Friday's town hall
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Short noun + `re` + noun phrase is a standard headline structure in business English.
   
   **Root Analysis**: memo (memorandum) + re (regarding) + topic noun phrase
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - See the memo re Friday's event on the intranet.
   - Did you read the memo re the new dress code?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please refer to the attached memo re compliance training requirements.
   - The HR department issued a memo re annual leave policy changes.

1. **Expression**: in re [case] (type: fixed legal expression)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A legal case-naming formula meaning "in the matter of" a particular person, estate, or issue
   - *Fixedness*: strong (conventional in case names and legal headings)
   
   **Synonyms**: in the matter of, regarding, concerning
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: In legal writing, judgments, and case citations; almost never in everyday non-legal English.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: General business emails, casual speech, and learner writing outside a legal context.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - In re Smith (a minor)
   - In re the estate of Johnson
   - In re application for judicial review
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase preserved as a fixed legal formula; learners should recognise it as "about this case" rather than try to translate literally each time.
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin *in* (in) + *re* (thing, matter)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The case is titled *In re Brown*, which basically means "about Brown".
   - Lawyers love phrases like *in re*—they just mean "in the matter of".
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In re the application for leave to appeal, the court grants an extension of time.
   - Costs are reserved in re the outstanding issues.

1. **Expression**: email re [topic] (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An email concerning a particular topic or issue
   - *Fixedness*: medium (the pattern is common but flexible)
   
   **Synonyms**: email about, email regarding, message about
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated email, off-topic email
   
   **When to Use**: Describing or labelling email communication on a specific subject; neutral register.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal document titles (prefer "email regarding" or explicit description); extremely casual speech ("email about" is slightly more natural).
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - email re your booking
   - email re last week's meeting
   - follow-up email re the outstanding invoice
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: `re` keeps headings and descriptions short while marking the relationship "about/concern".
   
   **Root Analysis**: email + re (about) + noun phrase
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I got an email re the concert tickets.
   - I'll send you a quick email re the changes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please refer to my earlier email re the proposed schedule.
   - This email re your recent enquiry summarises our recommendations.

   **Why This Partnership**:

   **Examples [Casual]**:
