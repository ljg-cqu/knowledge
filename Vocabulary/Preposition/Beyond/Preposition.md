1. **Preposition**: beyond (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* At or to the further side of; on the other side of; past in position or location
   - *(Temporal)* Later than; past a particular time or point in time
   - *(Abstract)* Outside the limits, range, or scope of something; surpassing or exceeding in degree, amount, or extent; not within the reach or understanding of
   
   **Synonyms**: past (in spatial/temporal contexts), further than (spatial), outside (abstract scope), exceeding (abstract degree), above (abstract level)
   
   **Antonyms**: within, inside, below, beneath
   
   **When to Use**: Use "beyond" to indicate spatial position on the far side of something, to express time after a deadline or limit, to describe something outside normal limits or expectations, or to indicate something that surpasses understanding or ability
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using "beyond" when "within" would be more accurate; don't confuse with "beside" (meaning next to); avoid in contexts requiring precise measurements without additional specification
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: beyond the horizon, beyond belief, beyond doubt, beyond measure, beyond repair, beyond recognition, beyond one's control, beyond expectations, beyond compare, beyond the pale
   - **Verb + prep**: go beyond, extend beyond, reach beyond, look beyond, think beyond, move beyond
   - **Adjective + prep**: (rarely used)
   - **Noun + prep**: (rarely used)
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by, near) + yond (Old English geond, meaning "yonder, over there")
   
   **Etymology**: Old English begeondan, from be- (by) + geondan (yonder, on the other side) → Middle English biyonde → Modern English beyond
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The store is just beyond that hill on the left.
   - This math problem is beyond me—I need help!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research findings extend beyond the scope of our initial hypothesis.
   - The committee must consider factors beyond immediate financial considerations.
