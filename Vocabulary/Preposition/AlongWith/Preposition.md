1. **Preposition**: along with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In addition to; together with; as well as
   - *(Abstract)* In the company of; accompanying; in conjunction with
   
   **Synonyms**: together with, in addition to, as well as, alongside, coupled with
   
   **Antonyms**: without, excluding, apart from, separate from
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts when adding supplementary information while maintaining focus on the primary subject; written and professional communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When both elements should be equally emphasized as subjects (use "and"); very casual conversation (less formal)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: [Subject] along with [additional element], along with [supplementary information]
   - **Verb + prep**: comes along with, brings along with, sent along with
   - **Adjective + prep**: included along with, provided along with
   - **Noun + prep**: [main item] along with [additional items]
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (on) + long (beside) + with (accompanied by)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English andlang (alongside) + with → Middle English along with → phrasal preposition (1700s)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The manager, along with his team, decided to extend the deadline.
   - I brought the reports along with some extra copies for everyone.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposal, along with supporting documentation, was submitted to the committee.
   - The study examines primary outcomes along with secondary measures of effectiveness.

1. **Preposition**: together with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In combination with; in association with; accompanied by
   - *(Abstract)* As well as; in addition to; jointly with
   
   **Synonyms**: along with, in conjunction with, coupled with, as well as, combined with
   
   **Antonyms**: apart from, separate from, without, excluding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, emphasizing combination or joint action; written communication, academic and professional settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation (overly formal); when elements should be treated as equal subjects (use "and")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: [Subject] together with [additional element]
   - **Verb + prep**: works together with, combines together with, operates together with
   - **Adjective + prep**: associated together with, linked together with
   - **Noun + prep**: [main noun] together with [supplementary noun]
   
   **Root Analysis**: to- (toward) + gather (assemble) + with (accompanied by)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English tōgædere (together) + with → phrasal preposition together with
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The director, together with the cast, celebrated the premiere.
   - My experience, together with my qualifications, makes me a strong candidate.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research findings, together with methodological considerations, inform policy recommendations.
   - The organization, together with its partners, implements comprehensive programs.

1. **Preposition**: alongside (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Next to; by the side of; beside
   - *(Abstract)* In cooperation with; together with; in addition to; at the same time as
   
   **Synonyms**: beside, next to (spatial); together with, in conjunction with, along with (abstract)
   
   **Antonyms**: opposite, across from (spatial); against, in competition with (abstract)
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating parallel action, cooperation, or simultaneous occurrence; neutral to formal registers; both spatial and abstract contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When clear hierarchy rather than equality should be emphasized; when separation or opposition is meant
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: alongside [person/group], work alongside, operate alongside
   - **Verb + prep**: function alongside, serve alongside, develop alongside, placed alongside
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned alongside, situated alongside
   - **Noun + prep**: partnership alongside, collaboration alongside, position alongside
   
   **Root Analysis**: a- (on) + long (beside) + side
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English along + side → alongside (compound preposition, 1700s)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll be working alongside you on this project.
   - The new features will be released alongside the main update.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The program operates alongside existing initiatives to maximize impact.
   - Traditional methods are employed alongside innovative approaches in the curriculum.

1. **Preposition**: in addition to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* As well as; besides; over and above; supplementing
   - *(Abstract)* Added to what already exists or has been mentioned
   
   **Synonyms**: besides, as well as, along with, on top of, beyond
   
   **Antonyms**: excluding, without, apart from, except for
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when emphasizing supplementary or extra elements; written communication, academic and professional settings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (too formal); when the additional element is not truly extra or supplementary
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in addition to [existing element], in addition to [mentioned items]
   - **Verb + prep**: provides in addition to, offers in addition to
   - **Adjective + prep**: available in addition to, included in addition to
   - **Noun + prep**: [extra element] in addition to [primary element]
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + addition (adding) + to (toward)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin additio (adding) → French addition → English phrase in addition to
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In addition to my regular job, I freelance on weekends.
   - They need to submit references in addition to their resume.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In addition to quantitative data, qualitative interviews provide contextual insights.
   - The program offers mentorship in addition to financial support for participants.

1. **Preposition**: as well as (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In addition to; and also; together with
   - *(Abstract)* To the same degree or extent as; equally
   
   **Synonyms**: in addition to, and also, along with, together with, plus
   
   **Antonyms**: excluding, without, instead of, rather than
   
   **When to Use**: All registers; connecting related elements while adding supplementary information; both spoken and written communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When strict grammatical parallelism is required (can create agreement issues); when only "and" is needed
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: [element] as well as [additional element]
   - **Verb + prep**: serves as well as, functions as well as
   - **Adjective + prep**: important as well as, necessary as well as
   - **Noun + prep**: [item] as well as [other items]
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (equally) + well (in a good manner) + as (comparison)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English phrase as well as → Modern English conjunction/preposition
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She handles marketing as well as sales for the company.
   - I need to finish this report as well as prepare for tomorrow's meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The framework addresses theoretical considerations as well as practical applications.
   - The study examines primary variables as well as potential confounding factors.

1. **Preposition**: coupled with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Combined with; together with; in conjunction with
   - *(Abstract)* When considered along with; in combination with
   
   **Synonyms**: combined with, together with, along with, in conjunction with, paired with
   
   **Antonyms**: separated from, divorced from, independent of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts emphasizing combination or joint effect; academic writing, professional reports, analysis
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (overly formal); when simple "and" or "with" suffices
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: [factor] coupled with [additional factor]
   - **Verb + prep**: when coupled with, if coupled with
   - **Adjective + prep**: effective coupled with, powerful coupled with
   - **Noun + prep**: [element] coupled with [complementary element]
   
   **Root Analysis**: couple (join/pair) + -ed (past participle) + with
   
   **Etymology**: Latin copula (bond) → French coupler → English coupled with (phrasal preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - His experience, coupled with his attitude, makes him perfect for the role.
   - The bad weather, coupled with equipment failure, caused major delays.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Strong leadership, coupled with organizational commitment, drives transformation.
   - The methodology, coupled with rigorous analysis, ensures validity of findings.

1. **Preposition**: besides (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In addition to; apart from; as well as
   - *(Abstract)* Other than; except for
   
   **Synonyms**: in addition to, as well as, apart from, aside from, beyond
   
   **Antonyms**: excluding, without, only, merely
   
   **When to Use**: Both formal and informal contexts when adding information; can mean "in addition to" or "except for" depending on context
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When meaning is ambiguous between "in addition to" and "except for"; when greater precision is needed
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: besides [item/person], besides [mentioned element]
   - **Verb + prep**: exists besides, included besides
   - **Adjective + prep**: available besides
   - **Noun + prep**: options besides, alternatives besides
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by) + side + -s (adverbial suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English be sīdan (by the side) → Middle English biside → besides
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Besides the main course, we should order appetizers too.
   - Who else is coming besides Tom and Lisa?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Besides quantitative measures, the study incorporates qualitative assessments.
   - The framework considers multiple factors besides cost efficiency.

   - **Noun + prep**: [amount/item] plus [extra amount/item]

1. **Preposition**: including (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Having as part of the whole; containing; comprising
   - *(Abstract)* Along with; together with; as well as
   
   **Synonyms**: along with, together with, comprising, containing, encompassing
   
   **Antonyms**: excluding, without, except for, not including
   
   **When to Use**: All registers; specifying what is contained or part of a group; clarifying scope
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When exclusion should be emphasized; when complete list is needed (use "consisting of")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: including [items], including [examples]
   - **Verb + prep**: is including, was including
   - **Adjective + prep**: listed including
   - **Noun + prep**: [group] including [specific members]
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (in) + clude (close) + -ing (present participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin includere (to enclose) → English include + -ing
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone's invited, including you!
   - The price covers everything, including taxes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study surveyed multiple demographics, including age and socioeconomic status.
   - Various methodologies were employed, including qualitative and quantitative approaches.
