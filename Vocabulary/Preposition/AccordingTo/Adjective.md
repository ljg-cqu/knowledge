1. **Adjective**: conforming
   **Meaning**: 
   - Acting in agreement with established rules, standards, or expectations; complying with norms or requirements (gradable, both attributive and predicative)
   
   **Synonyms**: compliant, obedient, complying, adherent, observant
   
   **Antonyms**: nonconforming, rebellious, deviant, defiant
   
   **When to Use**: Use "conforming" when describing behavior, products, or actions that follow rules, standards, or norms; suitable for formal, technical, and neutral contexts; emphasizes alignment with established requirements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can have negative connotations (lack of individuality, excessive compliance); avoid when celebrating independence or creativity; less suitable for casual contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - conforming behavior
   - conforming standards
   - conforming to regulations
   - conforming society
   - gender-conforming
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together, with) + form (shape) + -ing (present participle/adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin conformare (to shape, fashion) → Old French conformer → Middle English "conform" → present participle "conforming" as adjective (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He's always been the conforming type, never breaking any rules.
   - They're looking for conforming behavior in all employees.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The product must meet conforming standards before market release.
   - Conforming documentation is required for regulatory approval.

1. **Adjective**: compliant
   **Meaning**: 
   - Meeting or agreeing to comply with rules, standards, laws, or requests; disposed to agree or consent (gradable, both attributive and predicative)
   
   **Synonyms**: obedient, conforming, cooperative, acquiescent, submissive
   
   **Antonyms**: noncompliant, defiant, resistant, disobedient
   
   **When to Use**: Use "compliant" in formal, technical, legal, or business contexts when describing adherence to regulations, standards, or requirements; emphasizes meeting specific requirements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can imply excessive passivity or lack of critical thinking; avoid when celebrating independent thinking; less common in casual conversation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - compliant behavior
   - regulatory compliant
   - fully compliant
   - tax compliant
   - compliant with standards
   
   **Root Analysis**: com- (with) + pli (fold) + -ant (adjective suffix meaning "characterized by")
   
   **Etymology**: Latin complere (to fill up, complete) → complanare (to level, make even) → compliance → "compliant" as adjective (17th century), emphasizing agreement and conformity
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's always been pretty compliant with whatever the teacher asks.
   - I need to be more compliant with my doctor's recommendations.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All systems must be compliant with international safety regulations.
   - The organization maintains a strictly compliant approach to data protection laws.

1. **Adjective**: consistent

   **Common Phrases**:

   **Examples [Casual]**:

1. **Adjective**: attributed
   **Meaning**: 
   - Regarded as belonging to, caused by, or created by a particular person or thing; ascribed to a source (non-gradable, typically predicative or in past participle constructions)
   
   **Synonyms**: ascribed, credited, assigned, imputed, associated
   
   **Antonyms**: unattributed, unascribed, anonymous
   
   **When to Use**: Use "attributed" when describing something that is assigned or credited to a particular source, cause, or person; common in academic, journalistic, and analytical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when ownership or authorship is certain and direct (use "created by" or "written by" instead); less appropriate in casual conversation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - attributed to
   - commonly attributed
   - traditionally attributed
   - attributed source
   - attributed quotation
   
   **Root Analysis**: ad- (to) + tribut (assign, allot) + -ed (past participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin attribuere (to assign, allot) → Old French attribuer → English "attribute" as verb → "attributed" as adjective (15th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This quote is often attributed to Einstein, but I'm not sure he said it.
   - The success was attributed to the whole team's hard work.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The painting, long attributed to Rembrandt, has been reattributed to one of his students.
   - The decline in performance is attributed to multiple contributing factors.

1. **Adjective**: corresponding
   **Meaning**: 
   - Matching; similar or equivalent to something; having a close similarity or connection (gradable, both attributive and predicative)
   
   **Synonyms**: matching, equivalent, analogous, comparable, parallel
   
   **Antonyms**: unrelated, dissimilar, disparate, nonequivalent
   
   **When to Use**: Use "corresponding" when describing something that matches, relates to, or is equivalent to something else; suitable for formal, academic, and technical contexts; emphasizes correlation or equivalence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound formal in casual contexts (use "matching" or "related" instead); avoid when relationship is unclear
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - corresponding increase
   - corresponding section
   - corresponding value
   - corresponding period
   - corresponding angles
   
   **Root Analysis**: cor- (together, with) + respond (answer) + -ing (present participle/adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin correspondere (to answer together, agree with) → Medieval Latin correspondere → French correspondre → English "correspond" → "corresponding" as adjective (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - For each question, pick the corresponding answer from the list.
   - There was a corresponding drop in sales when prices went up.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The corresponding values are displayed in Table 3.
   - Each variable showed a corresponding change in the experimental condition.

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - N/A (Too technical for casual use)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The participant's verbal reports were congruent with their observed behaviors.
   - The model's predictions are congruent with empirical observations.

   **Examples [Casual]**:

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Attendance at the training is mandated by company policy.

   **Examples [Formal]**:
