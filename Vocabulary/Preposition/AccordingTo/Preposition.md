1. **Preposition**: according to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Attribution)* As stated by; as reported by; in the words of (used to cite sources or attribute information)
   - *(Conformity)* In accordance with; in agreement with; following (used to show conformity with rules, standards, or plans)
   
   **Synonyms**: as per (formal), in accordance with (formal), based on (attribution), in line with (conformity), as stated by (attribution)
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, in opposition to, regardless of
   
   **When to Use**: Use "according to" when citing sources, attributing statements to others, or indicating that something follows a particular standard, rule, or plan; extremely common in academic writing, journalism, and formal communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using "according to" to cite your own opinion (use "in my opinion" instead); don't use when you need to express disagreement or contrast; avoid overuse in consecutive sentences
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: according to the report, according to experts, according to plan, according to the rules
   - **Verb + prep**: varies according to, differ according to, change according to, act according to
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent according to (rare, prefer "consistent with")
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A (typically introduces attributive clause)
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (agreement, harmony) + -ing (gerund form) + to (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin accordare (to bring into agreement) → Old French acorder → Middle English "accord" + "-ing to" → phrasal preposition meaning "in agreement with" evolved to include attribution sense in 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to my mom, I need to be home by ten.
   - Everything went according to plan, so we're all good!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to recent studies, climate change poses significant risks to coastal communities.
   - The project was executed according to the specifications outlined in the contract.

1. **Preposition**: as per (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Formal Conformity)* In accordance with; following (used in business and formal contexts to reference documents, instructions, or agreements)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, per, following, as specified by
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, despite
   
   **When to Use**: Use "as per" in formal business communication, legal documents, or professional correspondence when referencing specific documents, instructions, or prior agreements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation (sounds overly formal); don't use for general attribution of opinions or statements (use "according to" instead); considered bureaucratic by some style guides
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: as per your request, as per the agreement, as per usual, as per our discussion
   - **Verb + prep**: processed as per, executed as per, delivered as per
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in the manner of) + per (Latin: through, by means of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per (through, by means of) → entered English through business Latin → combined with "as" to form phrasal preposition in 19th century business correspondence
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As per usual, he showed up late to the meeting.
   - I'll send you the files as per your request.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As per the terms outlined in Section 3.2 of the contract, payment is due within thirty days.
   - The shipment will be delivered as per the specifications provided in your purchase order.

1. **Preposition**: in accordance with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Formal Conformity)* Following; in compliance with; in agreement with (used to show strict adherence to rules, laws, standards, or guidelines)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in line with, in compliance with, pursuant to (legal), in conformity with
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, contrary to, in defiance of, in breach of
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in accordance with" in formal, legal, academic, or technical contexts when emphasizing compliance with rules, regulations, standards, or formal agreements; common in legal documents and official communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation; don't use for simple attribution of statements (use "according to"); avoid in informal writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in accordance with regulations, in accordance with policy, in accordance with the law, in accordance with protocol
   - **Verb + prep**: act in accordance with, proceed in accordance with, operate in accordance with
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A (use "in accordance with" as standalone phrase)
   - **Noun + prep**: compliance in accordance with, action in accordance with
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + accord (agreement) + -ance (noun suffix) + with (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin accordare (to bring into agreement) → Old French acordance → Middle English "accordaunce" → phrasal preposition "in accordance with" emerged in legal/formal English in 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I did everything in accordance with what you told me.
   - The party will be planned in accordance with her wishes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All experiments were conducted in accordance with institutional ethical guidelines.
   - The company operates in accordance with international safety standards and local regulations.

1. **Preposition**: in line with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Conformity/Agreement)* In agreement with; consistent with; following the same direction as (used to show alignment with policies, trends, expectations, or standards)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, consistent with, in keeping with, aligned with
   
   **Antonyms**: at odds with, contrary to, inconsistent with, out of line with
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in line with" to show that something matches or agrees with expectations, policies, trends, or standards; more flexible and less legalistic than "in accordance with"; suitable for both formal and semi-formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common for direct source attribution (prefer "according to"); avoid when strict legal compliance is emphasized (use "in accordance with" or "pursuant to")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in line with expectations, in line with policy, in line with trends, in line with our objectives
   - **Verb + prep**: bring in line with, keep in line with, move in line with, fall in line with
   - **Adjective + prep**: consistent in line with (redundant, avoid)
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + line (direction, alignment) + with (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: line (from Latin linea "cord, string") → metaphorical sense of "alignment" → phrasal preposition "in line with" emerged in 20th century business/management discourse
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My plans are totally in line with what you suggested.
   - The new design is in line with current fashion trends.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposed budget is in line with the organization's strategic priorities.
   - These findings are in line with previous research on cognitive development.

1. **Preposition**: based on (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Foundation)* Using as a foundation; derived from; using as evidence or reasoning (indicates the source or foundation for a conclusion, decision, or action)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, founded on, grounded in, derived from, stemming from
   
   **Antonyms**: regardless of, ignoring, without regard to, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "based on" when showing the foundation, evidence, or reasoning for a decision, conclusion, or creation; emphasizes the source material or reasoning rather than simple attribution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when you simply want to cite what someone said (use "according to"); avoid when emphasizing compliance with rules (use "in accordance with")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: based on evidence, based on research, based on experience, based on the fact that
   - **Verb + prep**: decide based on, conclude based on, judge based on, act based on
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A (base is a verb in this construction)
   - **Noun + prep**: decision based on, conclusion based on, assessment based on
   
   **Root Analysis**: base (foundation) + -ed (past participle) + on (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin basis (foundation) → Old French base → English "base" as verb meaning "to establish" → "based on" as phrasal preposition emphasizing foundational relationship (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I chose this restaurant based on the reviews online.
   - She made her decision based on what felt right.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee's recommendations were based on extensive analysis of the available data.
   - This model is based on well-established principles of cognitive psychology.

1. **Preposition**: pursuant to (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Legal Conformity)* In accordance with; following; as required by (formal legal term used to indicate compliance with laws, regulations, or formal agreements)
   
   **Synonyms**: in accordance with, according to, under (legal), per, in compliance with
   
   **Antonyms**: in violation of, in breach of, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "pursuant to" exclusively in legal documents, formal regulations, official government communications, or highly formal business contexts; signals strict legal compliance
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Never use in casual conversation or informal writing (extremely formal); don't use in general academic writing (use "according to" or "in accordance with"); avoid in business communication unless citing legal requirements
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pursuant to statute, pursuant to the agreement, pursuant to Section X, pursuant to authority
   - **Verb + prep**: act pursuant to, proceed pursuant to, execute pursuant to
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: action pursuant to, authority pursuant to
   
   **Root Analysis**: pursu(e) (to follow) + -ant (present participle suffix) + to (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin prosequi (to follow after) → Old French poursuir → Middle English "pursuen" → legal term "pursuant" (following, in accordance with) + "to" → established in legal language by 17th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - N/A (Not appropriate for casual register)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Pursuant to Section 5(a) of the Securities Act, the company must file annual reports.
   - The action was taken pursuant to the authority granted under the Emergency Management Act.

1. **Preposition**: in keeping with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Harmony/Consistency)* In agreement with; consistent with; in harmony with (used to show that something matches or harmonizes with traditions, character, style, or expectations)
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, consistent with, in accordance with, in harmony with, true to
   
   **Antonyms**: at odds with, inconsistent with, contrary to, out of character with
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in keeping with" to show that something matches established traditions, character, style, or values; emphasizes harmony and consistency rather than strict compliance; suitable for formal and semi-formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common for legal compliance (use "in accordance with"); not used for direct attribution of statements (use "according to"); avoid in very informal contexts
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in keeping with tradition, in keeping with character, in keeping with style, in keeping with values
   - **Verb + prep**: N/A (typically used as adverbial phrase)
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + keep (maintain, preserve) + -ing (gerund) + with (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English cēpan (to care for, maintain) → "keep" + "-ing with" → phrasal preposition emphasizing maintenance of harmony or consistency (emerged in literary English, 18th-19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The decorations are totally in keeping with the holiday spirit.
   - His behavior is in keeping with what I'd expect from him.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The architectural design is in keeping with the historical character of the neighborhood.
   - This policy decision is in keeping with the institution's commitment to sustainability.

1. **Preposition**: following (type: simple/compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* After; subsequent to (indicating time sequence)
   - *(Conformity)* In accordance with; according to (indicating adherence to rules, instructions, or guidelines)
   
   **Synonyms**: after (temporal), according to (conformity), in accordance with (conformity), subsequent to (temporal), as per (conformity)
   
   **Antonyms**: before, preceding, prior to (temporal); contrary to, ignoring (conformity)
   
   **When to Use**: Use "following" to indicate sequence in time or to show adherence to instructions, guidelines, or procedures; suitable for both formal and semi-formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can be ambiguous between temporal and conformity meanings; in casual contexts, "after" is clearer for temporal meaning; avoid overuse in academic writing (alternate with "after" or "subsequent to")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: following the meeting, following instructions, following the guidelines, following the incident
   - **Verb + prep**: act following, proceed following, occur following
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   
   **Root Analysis**: follow (to go after) + -ing (present participle/gerund)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English folgian (to go after, pursue) → Middle English "followen" → gerund form "following" grammaticalized as preposition by 16th century, developing both temporal and conformity senses
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Following the party, we all went out for pizza.
   - I did the project following the instructions you gave me.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Following the board meeting, several policy changes were implemented.
   - The study was conducted following established ethical protocols.

1. **Preposition**: per (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Rate/Distribution)* For each; by means of each (expressing rate, frequency, or distribution)
   - *(Conformity)* According to; in accordance with (formal, especially in business contexts)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to (conformity), as per (conformity), for each (rate), by (rate)
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to (conformity)
   
   **When to Use**: Use "per" for expressing rates, frequencies, or distributions (miles per hour, cost per unit); use for conformity in formal business or technical contexts; very common in business, technical, and scientific writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid "per" for conformity in casual conversation (sounds overly formal); don't overuse in general writing (prefer "according to" or "for each"); some style guides discourage "per" in non-technical writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: per hour, per capita, per unit, per annum, per your request, per the agreement
   - **Verb + prep**: calculated per, charged per, distributed per
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: cost per, rate per, price per
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (through, by means of, for each)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per (through, by means of) → entered English directly from Latin in technical, scientific, and commercial contexts → developed both rate and conformity meanings by 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The speed limit is sixty miles per hour.
   - Per your request, I'm sending the files now.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The productivity rate increased to 150 units per worker per day.
   - Per the terms of the contract, payment is due upon completion of deliverables.

1. **Preposition**: consistent with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Compatibility)* In agreement with; compatible with; not contradicting (indicates that something aligns with or doesn't contradict other information, principles, or patterns)
   
   **Synonyms**: in line with, in accordance with, compatible with, in agreement with, aligned with
   
   **Antonyms**: inconsistent with, contrary to, at odds with, contradictory to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "consistent with" to show that findings, behaviors, or statements align with theories, expectations, or previous information; common in academic, scientific, and analytical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common for direct attribution of statements (use "according to"); not typically used for legal compliance (use "in accordance with"); avoid redundancy with "in line"
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: consistent with findings, consistent with theory, consistent with expectations, consistent with evidence
   - **Verb + prep**: remain consistent with, prove consistent with, appear consistent with
   - **Adjective + prep**: fully consistent with, entirely consistent with, largely consistent with
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + sist (stand) + -ent (adjective suffix) + with (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin consistere (to stand together, be firm) → Middle English "consistent" → phrasal preposition "consistent with" developed in academic/scientific discourse (18th-19th century) to indicate alignment or compatibility
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What you're saying is consistent with what I heard yesterday.
   - Her story is consistent with the facts we know.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - These results are consistent with the hypothesis proposed in earlier research.
   - The observed patterns are consistent with theoretical predictions of the model.
