1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: sentence/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the circumstances; correspondingly; as a result; therefore (sentence adverb when meaning "therefore"; manner adverb when meaning "appropriately")
   
   **Synonyms**: consequently, therefore, thus, correspondingly, appropriately
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, regardless, nonetheless
   
   **When to Use**: Use "accordingly" to show that an action follows logically from previous information or to indicate that something is done in an appropriate manner; common in formal writing, business, and academic contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound formal in casual conversation (use "so" or "therefore" instead); avoid overuse in a single text; less appropriate for emotional or personal contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act accordingly
   - plan accordingly
   - adjust accordingly
   - respond accordingly
   - and accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: accord (agreement) + -ing (gerund) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin accordare (to bring into agreement) → Old French acorder → Middle English "accord" → "accordingly" as adverb (14th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather looks bad, so dress accordingly.
   - He was late, and they treated him accordingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data revealed significant discrepancies; accordingly, the methodology was revised.
   - Resources should be allocated accordingly to ensure optimal outcomes.

1. **Adverb**: consistently (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that does not vary or change; uniformly; in a manner compatible or in agreement with something
   
   **Synonyms**: uniformly, regularly, constantly, reliably, steadily
   
   **Antonyms**: inconsistently, erratically, variably, sporadically
   
   **When to Use**: Use "consistently" to describe actions, behaviors, or results that remain uniform over time or that align with expectations; suitable for all registers; emphasizes reliability and stability
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when describing deliberate variation or creativity; less appropriate when unpredictability is valued
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - perform consistently
   - consistently high
   - behave consistently
   - consistently with
   - consistently demonstrate
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (together) + sist (stand) + -ent (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin consistere (to stand together) → consistent → "consistently" as adverb (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's consistently been one of the best players on the team.
   - He consistently shows up late to everything.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization has consistently demonstrated commitment to ethical practices.
   - Results consistently indicate a positive correlation between the variables.

1. **Adverb**: correspondingly (type: manner/sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that corresponds or is similar to something; in a matching or equivalent manner; as a result or consequence
   
   **Synonyms**: similarly, proportionately, equivalently, accordingly, likewise
   
   **Antonyms**: oppositely, conversely, inversely, differently
   
   **When to Use**: Use "correspondingly" to show that something changes or responds in a parallel or proportionate way to something else; common in formal, academic, and analytical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound formal or technical in casual contexts (use "similarly" or "in the same way" instead); avoid overuse
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - increase correspondingly
   - adjust correspondingly
   - decrease correspondingly
   - vary correspondingly
   - change correspondingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: cor- (together, with) + respond (answer) + -ing (gerund) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin correspondere (to answer together) → correspond → "correspondingly" as adverb (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As the temperature went up, the ice cream sales increased correspondingly.
   - Prices dropped, and demand rose correspondingly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As investment increased, productivity improved correspondingly.
   - The complexity of the task increases, and the time required rises correspondingly.

1. **Adverb**: reportedly (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - According to what has been reported or stated; allegedly; as it is said (sentence adverb expressing epistemic stance about information source)
   
   **Synonyms**: allegedly, supposedly, purportedly, apparently, ostensibly
   
   **Antonyms**: certainly, definitely, confirmedly, verifiably
   
   **When to Use**: Use "reportedly" to indicate that information comes from reports or statements but may not be independently verified; common in journalism and academic writing when citing sources; signals epistemic caution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when you have direct knowledge or verification; don't use to hedge unnecessarily; can imply doubt about the information's accuracy
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - reportedly said
   - reportedly involved
   - reportedly planned
   - is reportedly
   - reportedly worth
   
   **Root Analysis**: report (relate, tell) + -ed (past participle) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin reportare (to carry back) → Old French reporter → Middle English "report" → "reportedly" as adverb (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He's reportedly dating someone new, but I haven't confirmed it.
   - The restaurant is reportedly closing next month.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The company reportedly plans to expand operations into Asian markets.
   - The policy change was reportedly motivated by budgetary constraints.

1. **Adverb**: allegedly (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to convey that something is claimed to be the case but is not proved; supposedly; according to allegations (sentence adverb expressing strong epistemic caution)
   
   **Synonyms**: supposedly, reportedly, purportedly, ostensibly, reputedly
   
   **Antonyms**: certainly, definitely, provenly, verifiably
   
   **When to Use**: Use "allegedly" to indicate that something is claimed but not verified, especially in legal or journalistic contexts; provides protection against defamation; signals skepticism or lack of proof
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when facts are established; don't use frivolously; can imply doubt or legal caution; inappropriate in casual conversation about mundane matters
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - allegedly involved
   - allegedly committed
   - was allegedly
   - allegedly responsible
   - allegedly stole
   
   **Root Analysis**: allege (claim, assert) + -ed (past participle) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin allegare (to send as a deputy, cite) → Old French alegier → Middle English "allege" → "allegedly" as adverb (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He allegedly took the money, but nobody saw him do it.
   - She's allegedly the one who spread the rumor.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The defendant allegedly violated the terms of the agreement.
   - The organization was allegedly involved in fraudulent activities.

1. **Adverb**: purportedly (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As appears or is stated to be true, though not necessarily so; allegedly; supposedly (sentence adverb expressing epistemic caution)
   
   **Synonyms**: allegedly, reportedly, supposedly, ostensibly, seemingly
   
   **Antonyms**: genuinely, authentically, verifiably, actually
   
   **When to Use**: Use "purportedly" to indicate that something is claimed or appears to be true but may not be; suggests more skepticism than "reportedly"; common in formal writing when questioning authenticity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation (use "supposedly" instead); avoid when facts are established; can sound skeptical or dismissive
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - purportedly authentic
   - purportedly written
   - is purportedly
   - purportedly contains
   - purportedly happened
   
   **Root Analysis**: purport (profess, claim) + -ed (past participle) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin proponere (to put forth) → Old French purporter (to mean, signify) → English "purport" → "purportedly" as adverb (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This is purportedly a genuine antique, but I have my doubts.
   - The video purportedly shows a UFO.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The manuscript is purportedly authored by a 15th-century scholar.
   - The document purportedly contains classified information.

1. **Adverb**: ostensibly (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As appears or is stated to be true, but not necessarily so; apparently; seemingly (sentence adverb suggesting discrepancy between appearance and reality)
   
   **Synonyms**: apparently, seemingly, supposedly, purportedly, outwardly
   
   **Antonyms**: genuinely, actually, truly, really
   
   **When to Use**: Use "ostensibly" to suggest that the stated reason or appearance may differ from the actual truth; implies hidden motives or alternative explanations; common in analytical and critical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual conversation; can sound cynical or suspicious; avoid when taking claims at face value; may offend if overused
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - ostensibly for
   - ostensibly to
   - is ostensibly
   - ostensibly designed
   - ostensibly independent
   
   **Root Analysis**: ostens(ible) (apparent, shown) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin ostendere (to show, display) → French ostensible → English "ostensible" → "ostensibly" as adverb (18th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He came over ostensibly to return my book, but I think he wanted to see my sister.
   - The meeting is ostensibly about the budget, but there's probably another agenda.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy was ostensibly introduced to improve efficiency, though critics suggest financial motivations.
   - The organization is ostensibly independent but receives substantial government funding.

1. **Adverb**: proportionately (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that corresponds in size, amount, or degree to something else; in proportion; correspondingly
   
   **Synonyms**: proportionally, correspondingly, commensurately, relatively, comparably
   
   **Antonyms**: disproportionately, unevenly, unequally
   
   **When to Use**: Use "proportionately" to indicate that something varies in direct or appropriate proportion to something else; common in mathematical, statistical, and analytical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound technical in casual contexts (use "in proportion" or "fairly" instead); avoid when exact mathematical proportion isn't intended
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - increase proportionately
   - distributed proportionately
   - grow proportionately
   - vary proportionately
   - proportionately more/less
   
   **Root Analysis**: pro- (forward, in favor of) + portion (part, share) + -ate (verb/adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin proportio (comparative relation) → proportion → proportionate → "proportionately" as adverb (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Everyone should contribute proportionately to their income.
   - The slices weren't divided proportionately at all.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Resources are allocated proportionately according to demonstrated need.
   - Risk increases proportionately with exposure duration.

1. **Adverb**: precisely (type: manner/degree)

   **Synonyms**: exactly, accurately, specifically, just so, to the letter
   
   **Antonyms**: approximately, roughly, vaguely, loosely, inaccurately
   
   **When to Use**: Use "precisely" to emphasize exactness, accuracy, or specificity; suitable for all registers; can function as intensifier or manner adverb
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when approximation is acceptable or when being pedantic; can sound overly fussy in casual contexts if overused
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - precisely defined
   - precisely because
   - precisely the same
   - more precisely
   - precisely when
   
   **Root Analysis**: pre- (before) + cis (cut) + -e (verb ending) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin praecisus (cut off, brief) → precise → "precisely" as adverb (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - That's precisely what I was thinking!
   - It happened at precisely 3:00 PM.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The measurements must be precisely calibrated to ensure accuracy.
   - The methodology is precisely described in the appendix.

1. **Adverb**: strictly (type: manner/degree)

   **Synonyms**: rigorously, exactly, precisely, stringently, absolutely
   
   **Antonyms**: loosely, flexibly, leniently, approximately

   **Common Phrases**: 
   - strictly speaking
   - strictly enforced
   - strictly prohibited
   - strictly confidential
   - strictly necessary
   
   **Root Analysis**: strict (precise, tight) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin strictus (drawn tight, rigid) → strict → "strictly" as adverb (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Strictly speaking, that's not really accurate.
   - The rules are strictly enforced around here.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participation is strictly voluntary and may be withdrawn at any time.
   - The procedure must be strictly followed to ensure validity.
