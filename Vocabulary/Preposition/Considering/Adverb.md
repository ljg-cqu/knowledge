1. **Adverb**: carefully (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that deliberately avoids harm or errors; with careful attention to detail
   - Used to describe how someone considers or evaluates something with attention and caution
   
   **Synonyms**: cautiously, attentively, meticulously, prudently, thoughtfully
   
   **Antonyms**: carelessly, recklessly, hastily, negligently, heedlessly
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing cautious approach; when attention to detail matters; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing quick, instinctive actions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - carefully consider
   - carefully evaluate
   - carefully assess
   - think carefully
   - plan carefully
   
   **Root Analysis**: careful + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English cearful + -ly (adverbial suffix) → Middle English → modern "carefully"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Think carefully before you decide.
   - Considering carefully, I think we should wait.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee carefully considered all submissions before making recommendations.
   - Proposals must be carefully evaluated against established criteria.
