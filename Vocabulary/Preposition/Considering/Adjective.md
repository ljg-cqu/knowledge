1. **Adjective**: thoughtful (comparative: more thoughtful, superlative: most thoughtful)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Showing careful consideration or attention; absorbed in or involving thought
   - *(considering context)* Taking time to think carefully about different factors before making decisions
   
   **Synonyms**: considerate, reflective, contemplative, deliberate, attentive
   
   **Antonyms**: thoughtless, careless, impulsive, rash, hasty
   
   **When to Use**: Describing people who carefully consider options; formal and informal contexts; expressing appreciation for careful consideration
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing quick, instinctive decisions (use "spontaneous" or "intuitive")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - thoughtful consideration
   - thoughtful approach
   - thoughtful decision
   - thoughtful analysis
   - deeply thoughtful
   
   **Root Analysis**: thought (noun) + -ful (full of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English þōht (thought) + -ful (full of) → Middle English → modern "thoughtful"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's really thoughtful when considering job offers.
   - That was a thoughtful way to look at the problem.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee adopted a thoughtful approach when considering the proposals.
   - A thoughtful analysis of the data reveals several important considerations.

   **Examples [Formal]**:
