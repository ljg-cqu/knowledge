1. **Noun**: consideration (U/C, pl: considerations)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (U) Careful thought about something, typically before making a decision
   - (C) A factor that is taken into account when making a decision or judgment
   
   **Synonyms**: thought, deliberation, reflection, contemplation, evaluation
   
   **Antonyms**: disregard, neglect, thoughtlessness, impulsiveness, haste
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts; decision-making discussions; expressing thorough evaluation; professional communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation (use "thought" or "thinking about it"); when simpler words suffice
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - take into consideration
   - give consideration to
   - under consideration
   - careful consideration
   - due consideration
   
   **Root Analysis**: consider (verb) + -ation (noun suffix denoting action/process)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin considerare (to observe carefully) → consideration (act of considering)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Thanks for your consideration of my request.
   - Budget is an important consideration.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The proposal is under active consideration by the executive committee.
   - Multiple considerations informed the board's decision, including financial viability and strategic alignment.

1. **Noun**: deliberation (U/C, pl: deliberations)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (U) Long and careful consideration or discussion
   - (C, usually plural) Formal discussions and considerations, especially by a group making a decision
   
   **Synonyms**: contemplation, reflection, thought, discussion, consultation
   
   **Antonyms**: haste, impulsiveness, rashness, spontaneity
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts; emphasizing careful, lengthy consideration; legal and official settings; group decision-making
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (too formal); quick decisions; informal discussions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - after careful deliberation
   - following deliberation
   - the deliberations of the committee
   - under deliberation
   - lengthy deliberations
   
   **Root Analysis**: deliberate (verb) + -tion (noun suffix denoting action/result)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin deliberatio (careful consideration) → Old French → English "deliberation"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - After some deliberation, we decided to go.
   - The jury's deliberations took three hours.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - After extensive deliberation, the board approved the merger proposal.
   - The committee's deliberations resulted in five key recommendations for policy reform.

1. **Noun**: implication (C, pl: implications)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated; a likely consequence of something
   
   **Synonyms**: consequence, ramification, effect, inference, meaning
   
   **Antonyms**: explicit statement, direct consequence, stated fact
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing consequences; analytical contexts; showing awareness of indirect effects; formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When directness is more appropriate; casual conversation about obvious effects
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - implications of
   - consider the implications
   - far-reaching implications
   - serious implications
   - practical implications
   
   **Root Analysis**: im- (in) + plic (fold) + -ation (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin implicare (to involve/entangle) → implication (something folded in/involved)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Have you thought about the implications?
   - What are the implications of this decision?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy change carries significant implications for operational procedures.
   - Careful consideration of long-term implications is essential for strategic planning.
