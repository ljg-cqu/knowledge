1. **Expression**: take into consideration (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To think carefully about something, typically before making a decision; to include as a factor in one's thinking
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: consider, take into account, bear in mind, factor in, think about
   
   **Antonyms**: disregard, ignore, overlook, dismiss, neglect
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts; decision-making discussions; showing comprehensive evaluation; professional communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation (use "think about"); when simpler "consider" is clearer; when overused creating wordiness
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - take [X] into consideration
   - taking [X] into consideration
   - taken into consideration
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Take into consideration" is the formal, complete form of expressing consideration. The phrase structure emphasizes the active incorporation of factors into thinking, stronger than simple "consider."
   
   **Root Analysis**: take (incorporate) + into (direction) + consideration (thought process)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to take everyone's schedule into consideration.
   - Let's take the weather into consideration before planning.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee will take all stakeholder feedback into consideration before finalizing the policy.
   - When evaluating proposals, we take multiple factors into consideration, including cost, timeline, and feasibility.

1. **Expression**: give consideration to (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To think about something seriously or carefully; to devote attention to evaluating something
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed formal collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: consider, think about, evaluate, examine, contemplate
   
   **Antonyms**: dismiss, disregard, ignore, overlook, reject without thought
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts; professional requests; diplomatic language; showing serious attention
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (too formal); when more direct language is appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - give serious consideration to
   - give due consideration to
   - give careful consideration to
   - give full consideration to
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: The verb "give" with "consideration" creates a formal expression that treats consideration as something valuable that can be bestowed. The formality makes it appropriate for professional and official contexts.
   
   **Root Analysis**: give (bestow) + consideration (careful thought)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll give it some consideration and let you know.
   - Please give my suggestion some consideration.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board will give due consideration to all proposed amendments.
   - We ask that you give serious consideration to this matter before the deadline.

   **Common Variations**:

   - worthy of further consideration

   - still under consideration
   - under active consideration
   - under careful consideration
   
   **Why This Partnership**:

   **Root Analysis**: under (beneath/subject to) + consideration (evaluation process)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your application is under consideration.
   - That idea is still under consideration.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:

   - Three candidates remain under consideration for the position.

1. **Expression**: careful consideration (type: collocation - adjective + noun)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Thorough and attentive thinking about something; evaluation with attention to detail and implications
   - *Fixedness*: medium (preferred collocation)
   
   **Synonyms**: thorough evaluation, serious thought, careful examination, detailed analysis
   
   **Antonyms**: hasty decision, cursory glance, superficial review, careless thinking
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing thoroughness; professional contexts; showing due diligence; important decisions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When speed is valued over thoroughness; casual decisions; when overused as empty phrase
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - after careful consideration
   - deserves careful consideration
   - requires careful consideration
   - give careful consideration
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Careful" naturally pairs with "consideration" because both emphasize attentiveness and thoroughness. This is one of the most common and natural adjective-noun pairs in formal decision-making discourse.
   
   **Root Analysis**: careful (full of care/attention) + consideration (thinking)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - After careful consideration, I've decided to accept.
   - This deserves careful consideration before we commit.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - After careful consideration of all proposals, the selection committee has identified three finalists.
   - This matter requires careful consideration of legal and ethical implications.

1. **Expression**: mull it over (type: phrasal verb/idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To think about something carefully and at length; to consider something thoroughly over time
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed phrasal verb)
   
   **Synonyms**: think it over, consider it, ponder it, reflect on it, contemplate it
   
   **Antonyms**: decide immediately, act impulsively, dismiss without thought
   
   **When to Use**: Requesting or indicating time for reflection; informal to semi-formal contexts; showing thoughtful approach
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal writing (use "deliberate" or "consider"); when immediate decision is required
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - mull it over for a while
   - mull things over
   - let me mull it over
   - mulling over the decision
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "Mull" originally meant to heat and spice wine, extended metaphorically to mean warming/working over thoughts. Combined with "over," it creates an idiom for prolonged, thorough consideration. The phrase suggests unhurried, deep thinking.
   
   **Root Analysis**: mull (ponder, from heating/mixing) + over (thoroughly)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let me mull it over and I'll get back to you.
   - I've been mulling it over all week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The executive team is mulling over several strategic alternatives.
   - *"Typically informal; in formal contexts prefer 'deliberating' or 'considering carefully'"*

1. **Expression**: sleep on it (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To postpone a decision until the next day after having time to think; to consider something overnight before deciding
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed idiom)
   
   **Synonyms**: think it over, take time to decide, defer decision, consider overnight
   
   **Antonyms**: decide immediately, act now, commit right away
   
   **When to Use**: Informal contexts; resisting pressure to decide immediately; buying thinking time; everyday decisions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal contexts; emergency situations; after excessive delays
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - let me sleep on it
   - sleep on the decision
   - sleep on your offer
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Based on the folk wisdom that rest and time provide perspective and clarity. The idiom combines "sleep" (rest/pause) with "on it" to create a natural-sounding phrase for postponing decision-making.
   
   **Root Analysis**: sleep (rest) + on (continuing attention to) + it (the decision)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can I sleep on it and let you know tomorrow?
   - I slept on it and I think you're right.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *"Typically informal; avoid in formal contexts. Use 'take time to consider' instead."*

1. **Expression**: on second thought / on reflection (type: fixed phrase/discourse marker)
   **Meaning**: 
   - After thinking about something again or more carefully; used to introduce a change of mind or revised opinion
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed transitional phrase)
   
   **Synonyms**: thinking about it again, reconsidering, after further thought, upon reconsideration
   
   **Antonyms**: sticking with first instinct, without reconsidering, maintaining original position
   
   **When to Use**: Changing one's mind; showing reconsideration; introducing revised judgment; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When consistency is important; when appearing indecisive is problematic; after multiple changes
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - on second thought (American)
   - on second thoughts (British)
   - on further reflection
   - upon reflection
   - after reflection
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: "Second thought" combines ordinal (second) with "thought" to indicate reconsideration. "Reflection" similarly indicates looking back at one's thinking. These phrases signal intellectual honesty and willingness to reconsider.
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (upon) + second (subsequent) + thought (consideration)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - On second thought, let's try the other restaurant.
   - I'll come with you. On second thought, I'll stay home.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - On further reflection, the committee has revised its recommendation.
   - Upon reflection, the initial assessment appears overly optimistic.
