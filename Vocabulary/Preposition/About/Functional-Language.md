1. **Functional Expression**: What do you think about...? (function: asking for opinion)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask someone for their opinion or view on a particular topic or matter
   - *Formality*: neutral (suitable for most contexts)
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: How do you feel about...?, What's your opinion on...?, What are your thoughts on...?
   
   **Antonyms**: I'll tell you what I think..., Here's what I believe... (giving opinion rather than asking)
   
   **When to Use**: Seeking opinions in conversations, meetings, discussions; appropriate for both personal and professional contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When opinion is not wanted; in very formal contexts (use "What is your view on...")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - What do you think about [X]?
   - What did you think about [X]?
   - What would you think about [X]?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Shows respect for other's opinions; invites participation; neutral politeness strategy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I think (that)...
   - In my opinion...
   - Honestly, I think...
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What do you think about the new restaurant downtown?
   - What do you think about going to the beach this weekend?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - What do you think about the proposed changes to the policy?
   - What do you think about implementing this approach?

1. **Functional Expression**: I was wondering about... (function: indirect inquiry)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite indirect way to ask questions or express curiosity about something
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (softened request)
   
   **Synonyms**: I wanted to ask about..., I'm curious about..., Could you tell me about...?
   
   **Antonyms**: Tell me about... (more direct)
   
   **When to Use**: Polite inquiries; professional emails; when softer approach is preferred
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Emergency situations requiring directness; very casual contexts (use "I want to know")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I was wondering about [topic]
   - I was wondering if you could tell me about...
   - I've been wondering about...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy (minimizing imposition); softer than direct questions; professional and considerate
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Sure, [information]
   - Of course, let me explain...
   - I'd be happy to clarify...
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I was wondering about your plans for the summer.
   - I was wondering about that new coffee shop.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I was wondering about the application deadline.
   - I was wondering about the requirements for this position.

1. **Functional Expression**: Could you tell me about...? (function: polite request for information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite way to request information or explanation about a topic
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (polite request form)
   
   **Synonyms**: Can you tell me about...?, Would you mind telling me about...?, I'd like to know about...
   
   **Antonyms**: Tell me about... (direct command)
   
   **When to Use**: Requesting information politely; professional and academic contexts; interviews
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very informal situations with close friends (overly polite)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could you tell me more about...?
   - Could you tell me a bit about...?
   - Could you possibly tell me about...?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Modal "could" softens request; shows respect; appropriate for power distance situations
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Certainly, [information]
   - Of course, [information]
   - I'd be happy to...
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Could you tell me about your trip?
   - Could you tell me about how this works?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you tell me about your experience in this field?
   - Could you tell me about the company's approach to innovation?

1. **Functional Expression**: Let me tell you about... (function: introducing topic)

   **Synonyms**: I'll tell you about..., I want to tell you about..., Listen to this about...
   
   **Antonyms**: I won't discuss..., I'd rather not talk about...
   
   **When to Use**: Storytelling; sharing information enthusiastically; presentations; casual and semi-formal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal presentations (use "I will discuss"); when listener hasn't shown interest
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let me tell you a bit about...
   - Let me tell you something about...
   - Let me quickly tell you about...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Seeks permission implicitly; engaging opener; conversational tone; attention-directing

   - Okay, go ahead
   - Sure, I'm listening
   - [Silent attention]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let me tell you about this crazy thing that happened!
   - Let me tell you about my new project.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Let me tell you about our company's approach.
   - Let me tell you about the key findings.

   - Actually, I hadn't thought about that
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What about pizza for dinner?

   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - What about the budget implications?
   - What about alternative approaches?

1. **Functional Expression**: Let's not talk about... (function: declining discussion)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite but firm way to avoid or postpone discussion of a topic
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: fairly direct
   
   **Synonyms**: I'd rather not discuss..., Can we avoid talking about...?, Let's change the subject
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's talk about..., We need to discuss...
   
   **When to Use**: Setting boundaries; avoiding uncomfortable topics; redirecting conversations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When topic must be discussed; very formal settings (use "Perhaps we should postpone discussion of...")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Let's not talk about that right now
   - Can we not talk about...?
   - I'd rather not talk about...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Boundary-setting; may indicate sensitivity or discomfort; relatively gentle refusal; suggests alternative needed
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Okay, no problem
   - Sure, what should we talk about instead?
   - Understood
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's not talk about work right now.
   - Let's not talk about that anymore, okay?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Perhaps we should not discuss that at this juncture.
   - Let's table that discussion for now.
