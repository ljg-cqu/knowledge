1. **Expression**: what about (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to make a suggestion or to ask about something that should be considered
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very common idiomatic expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: how about, what of, concerning
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Making suggestions, asking for opinions, or drawing attention to overlooked matters; casual to semi-formal
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal writing (use "regarding" or "concerning")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - What about if...?
   - But what about...?
   - So what about...?
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For fixed phrases*: Conventionalized interrogative form combining "what" with "about" to redirect attention or make suggestions; extremely high frequency
   
   **Root Analysis**: Interrogative what + preposition about
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What about going to the beach this weekend?
   - That's great, but what about John? Did anyone tell him?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We've addressed the budget. What about timeline considerations?
   - The proposal is sound. What about implementation risks?

1. **Expression**: talk about (type: collocation - phrasal verb)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To discuss or speak concerning a particular subject
   - *Fixedness*: strong (extremely common verb + preposition partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: discuss, speak about, converse about, address
   
   **Antonyms**: ignore, avoid discussing
   
   **When to Use**: General discussion contexts; slightly less formal than "discuss"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal academic writing (prefer "discuss")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - talk about something/someone
   - let's talk about
   - need to talk about
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Talk" naturally combines with "about" to indicate topic; preferred preposition (vs. "talk of")
   
   **Root Analysis**: talk + about (preposition)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's talk about your plans.
   - We were just talking about you!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The panel will talk about emerging technologies.
   - Participants talked about their experiences during interviews.
