1. **Adverb**: approximately (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to show that something is close to but not exactly a particular amount, time, or number
   
   **Synonyms**: about, roughly, around, nearly, close to
   
   **Antonyms**: exactly, precisely
   
   **When to Use**: When giving estimates or approximate figures; formal and technical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When exact precision is available and required
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - approximately equal to
   - approximately the same
   - lasting approximately
   - approximately how many
   - approximately when
   
   **Root Analysis**: approximat (brought near) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin proximus (nearest) → approximatus → approximately (near to)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It takes approximately two hours to get there.
   - There were approximately fifty people at the party.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The sample size consisted of approximately 300 participants.
   - The project will cost approximately $2 million.

1. **Adverb**: roughly (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an approximate or imprecise manner; without exactness
   
   **Synonyms**: approximately, about, around, nearly, more or less
   
   **Antonyms**: exactly, precisely, accurately
   
   **When to Use**: Giving informal estimates; emphasizing approximation rather than precision
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In highly technical or scientific contexts requiring precision
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - roughly equal
   - roughly the same
   - roughly how much
   - roughly speaking
   - roughly estimated
   
   **Root Analysis**: rough + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Old English ruh (coarse) + -ly → roughly (not precisely)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It costs roughly twenty bucks.
   - Roughly how long will it take?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The figures are roughly equivalent across all groups.
   - Response rates were roughly similar between conditions.
