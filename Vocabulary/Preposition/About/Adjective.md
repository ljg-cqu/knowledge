1. **Adjective**: concerned (comparative: more concerned, superlative: most concerned)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Worried, troubled, or anxious about something; interested in or affected by something
   - *(concerned about)* Feeling worry or anxiety regarding a particular matter
   
   **Synonyms**: worried, anxious, troubled, apprehensive, interested
   
   **Antonyms**: unconcerned, indifferent, carefree, unworried
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing worry or interest about topics; formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For positive emotions (use "excited" or "enthusiastic")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - concerned about something
   - deeply concerned about
   - increasingly concerned about
   - concerned about the future
   - concerned about safety
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (with) + cern (sift, separate)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere (relate to) → past participle concerned → adjective meaning "worried"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm really concerned about my exam results.
   - Parents are concerned about screen time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Stakeholders are concerned about regulatory compliance.
   - Researchers remain concerned about data integrity issues.
