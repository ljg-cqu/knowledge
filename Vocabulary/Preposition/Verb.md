# Verbs for Preposition Vocabulary Learning

1. **Verb**: indicate
   **Meaning**: 
   - To point out or show; to be a sign or symptom of something (transitive)
   
   **Synonyms**: show, demonstrate, point to, signal, denote
   
   **Antonyms**: hide, conceal, obscure, disguise
   
   **When to Use**: Formal or academic contexts, describing functions, showing relationships
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When a simpler word like "show" would be more appropriate in casual speech
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - indicate direction
   - indicate location
   - indicate a relationship
   - clearly indicate
   - indicate that
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- [in, toward] + dic [say, point] + -ate [verb suffix]
   
   **Etymology**: Latin indicare (to point out, show) → indicate
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The preposition indicates where the object is.
   - This sign indicates the direction you should go.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Spatial prepositions indicate the relative position of entities within a frame of reference.
   - The preposition indicates the semantic relationship between the verb and its complement.

1. **Verb**: express

   - express meaning

   - express an idea

   **Etymology**: Latin exprimere (to press out, express) → express
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can express time relationships using different prepositions.
   - How do you express this idea in English?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Prepositions express spatial, temporal, and abstract relationships between constituents.
   - The construction expresses a directional relationship from source to goal.
