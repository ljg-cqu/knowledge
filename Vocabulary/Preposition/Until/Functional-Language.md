# Functional Language Related to: Until

1. **Functional Expression**: "Wait until..." (function: requesting delay/patience)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A request or instruction to delay action until a specified time, event, or condition is met
   - *Formality*: neutral (can be adjusted with "please" or tone)
   - *Directness*: moderately direct
   
   **Synonyms**: Hold on until..., Don't [act] until..., Please be patient until..., Delay until...
   
   **Antonyms**: Go ahead now, Proceed immediately, Don't wait
   
   **When to Use**: Use when you need someone to delay their action until a specific condition is met; common in coordination, instructions, and managing timing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound commanding without softeners; add "please" or "could you" for politeness in formal/professional settings
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Please wait until... (more polite)
   - Could you wait until... (polite question form)
   - Just wait until... (casual, potentially threatening in some contexts)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: The bare imperative "wait until" can be softened with "please," question forms, or reasons. In power-asymmetric situations, softer forms are expected unless urgent.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Sure, no problem (accepting)
   - How long until...? (seeking clarification)
   - I can't wait that long (declining/negotiating)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Wait until you see what I got you!
   - Just wait until your dad gets home!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Please wait until you receive confirmation before proceeding.
   - We ask that you wait until the official announcement is made.

2. **Functional Expression**: "I can't wait until..." (function: expressing anticipation/excitement)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Express strong anticipation, excitement, or impatience for a future event; emphasizes eagerness
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct expression of emotion
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm looking forward to..., I'm so excited about..., I'm eager for...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm dreading..., I'm not looking forward to...
   
   **When to Use**: Use to express genuine enthusiasm about upcoming events; builds rapport and shows positive emotion
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May sound insincere if overused; inappropriate for negative or formal serious contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I can hardly wait until... (more emphatic)
   - I'm counting down until... (emphasizes duration)
   - Can't wait until... (casual, contracted)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This expression is primarily positive politeness strategy, showing enthusiasm and creating solidarity; genuine tone is important
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Me too! / Me neither! (sharing excitement)
   - It'll be here before you know it (reassuring)
   - I know, right? (affirming shared anticipation)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I can't wait until summer vacation!
   - Can't wait until we can hang out again!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I look forward to the conference next month. (more appropriate formal alternative)
   - We anticipate the launch with great enthusiasm. (formal alternative)

3. **Functional Expression**: "Not until..." (function: refusing/setting conditions)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Indicate that something will not happen before a specified condition is met; often used to set boundaries or conditions
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: quite direct; emphatic refusal or condition-setting
   
   **Synonyms**: Only when..., Only after..., Not before...
   
   **Antonyms**: Right now, Immediately, Before...
   
   **When to Use**: Use to establish clear conditions or boundaries; common in negotiations, parenting, and setting terms
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound stubborn or inflexible; soften with explanations when negotiating or in professional settings
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I won't [act] until... (clearer agent focus)
   - It won't happen until... (impersonal)
   - Not until you [condition]... (direct conditional)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This is face-threatening when setting boundaries; explain reasons to maintain relationships. In hierarchical contexts, superiors use this more freely than subordinates.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Okay, I understand (accepting)
   - But why not? (questioning/challenging)
   - Can we negotiate? (seeking compromise)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You're not going out until you finish your homework.
   - I'm not eating there again, not until they fix their service.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project will not commence until proper authorization is obtained.
   - We cannot proceed until all stakeholders have provided approval.

4. **Functional Expression**: "Until recently..." (function: introducing contrast/change)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Introduce information about a past state that has recently changed; sets up contrast with current situation
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (sets context before main point)
   
   **Synonyms**: Up until recently..., Until lately..., Before now...
   
   **Antonyms**: For a long time now..., Since long ago...
   
   **When to Use**: Use to frame recent changes or new information; common in reporting, storytelling, and explanations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use if the change is not recent; specify timing if it's important
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Until just recently... (emphasizes recency)
   - Until very recently... (emphasizes how recent)
   - Up until recently... (more emphatic about continuity)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This phrase manages information flow by establishing prior context before presenting new information; helps listener process change
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What changed? (seeking information)
   - Really? When? (expressing surprise)
   - I see (acknowledging)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Until recently, I thought he was married.
   - I didn't know about this place until recently.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Until recently, this approach was considered the standard methodology.
   - The correlation remained undetected until recently discovered archival data.

5. **Functional Expression**: "How long until...?" (function: inquiring about time/duration)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Ask about the remaining time before an event or completion; seeking temporal information
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct question
   
   **Synonyms**: When will...?, How much longer until...?, How much time until...?
   
   **Antonyms**: (no direct antonym; different question type)
   
   **When to Use**: Use to inquire about timing, deadlines, or remaining duration; common in planning and coordination
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound impatient if asked repeatedly; soften with context or reasons if checking on someone's work
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - How much longer until... (emphasizes duration)
   - Do you know when... (more tentative)
   - Any idea how long until... (casual, seeking estimate)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Repeated asking can imply impatience; if checking on others' work, frame as planning/coordination need rather than pressure
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - About [time] (providing estimate)
   - Not sure, but soon (uncertain response)
   - Why do you ask? (checking if there's urgency)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How long until we're there?
   - How long until dinner's ready?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you provide an estimate of how long until the report is completed?
   - How long until the system will be back online?

6. **Functional Expression**: "Let's wait until..." (function: suggesting delay)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Suggest postponing action until more favorable conditions; collaborative proposal for timing
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (suggestion rather than command)
   
   **Synonyms**: Why don't we wait until..., Perhaps we should wait until..., How about waiting until...
   
   **Antonyms**: Let's do it now, Let's not wait, We should start immediately
   
   **When to Use**: Use to propose delaying action for strategic reasons; softens disagreement or introduces caution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May sound indecisive if overused; provide clear reasoning for delay
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Maybe we should wait until... (more tentative)
   - I think we should wait until... (personal opinion)
   - Shouldn't we wait until... (question form, seeking agreement)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Using "let's" creates collaborative frame and reduces face-threat of disagreement; effective for polite opposition to immediate action
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Good idea (agreeing)
   - Why? / What's the benefit? (seeking clarification)
   - But we can't wait that long (disagreeing)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's wait until everyone gets here before we order.
   - Let's wait until it stops raining to go out.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I suggest we wait until we have complete data before making a decision.
   - Perhaps we should wait until legal review is complete before proceeding.

7. **Functional Expression**: "It won't be ready until..." (function: managing expectations)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Inform about a delay or set realistic timeline; manage expectations about availability or completion
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct information about delay
   
   **Synonyms**: It will only be ready at..., Don't expect it until..., It will take until...
   
   **Antonyms**: It's ready now, It will be ready soon/by...
   
   **When to Use**: Use to set clear expectations about timing; important for managing disappointment and planning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Provide reasoning for delay when possible to maintain trust and understanding
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - It won't be finished until... (emphasizes completion)
   - We won't have it until... (emphasizes possession/availability)
   - Don't count on it until... (warns against premature expectations)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Proactive expectation management prevents disappointment; provide reasoning to maintain face and trust
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Okay, thanks for letting me know (accepting)
   - Is there any way to speed it up? (negotiating)
   - That's too long (expressing dissatisfaction)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The food won't be ready until 7 PM.
   - My car won't be fixed until next week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The report will not be available until the end of the quarter.
   - System restoration will not be completed until maintenance windows conclude.

8. **Functional Expression**: "Until further notice" (function: indefinite suspension/continuation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Indicate that a situation will continue indefinitely until new information is provided; open-ended timeline
   - *Formality*: formal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct but non-specific about endpoint
   
   **Synonyms**: For the time being, For now, Indefinitely, Until we announce otherwise
   
   **Antonyms**: Until [specific date], Temporarily until [date]
   
   **When to Use**: Use in official announcements when specific end date is unknown; common in policies and public notices
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can frustrate people who need specific timelines; provide estimates or check-in points if possible
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Until we notify you otherwise (more personal)
   - Until you hear from us (more personal)
   - Until announced (briefer, formal)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This phrase protects against commitment to specific dates but can create anxiety; balance with check-in timelines when appropriate
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - How will we know? (seeking clarification)
   - Do you have any estimate? (seeking more info)
   - Understood (accepting)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The gym is closed until further notice.
   - We're working from home until further notice.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Operations are suspended until further notice pending investigation.
   - This policy remains in effect until further notice from administration.

9. **Functional Expression**: "I didn't realize until..." (function: explaining delayed awareness)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Explain that understanding or awareness came late; often used in apologies or clarifications
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (focuses on lack of awareness rather than direct accountability)
   
   **Synonyms**: I only found out when..., I wasn't aware until..., It didn't dawn on me until...
   
   **Antonyms**: I knew from the start, I was aware all along
   
   **When to Use**: Use to explain delays in action or response due to late awareness; softens potential criticism
   
   **When NOT to Use**: May sound like excuse if overused; take clear responsibility when appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I had no idea until... (emphasizes complete lack of awareness)
   - I only just realized... (emphasizes recency)
   - It never occurred to me until... (emphasizes lack of consideration)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This face-saving strategy explains inaction without full admission of fault; balance with responsibility acceptance
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That's understandable (accepting explanation)
   - You should have checked earlier (rejecting excuse)
   - No worries (forgiving)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I didn't realize until today that you were coming.
   - Sorry, I didn't realize until now that you needed this.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We were not made aware of this issue until the audit was conducted.
   - The discrepancy was not identified until the reconciliation process.

10. **Functional Expression**: "Save it until..." (function: advising postponement)
    **Meaning**: 
    - Advise or instruct to reserve something (information, action, resource) for a more appropriate later time
    - *Formality*: informal to neutral
    - *Directness*: moderately direct advice/instruction
    
    **Synonyms**: Hold it until..., Keep it for..., Reserve it for..., Wait to use it until...
    
    **Antonyms**: Use it now, Don't wait
    
    **When to Use**: Use to advise strategic timing or resource conservation; shows strategic thinking
    
    **When NOT to Use**: Can sound controlling if used imperatively; frame as suggestion when appropriate
    
    **Common Variations**: 
    - You might want to save it until... (softer suggestion)
    - I'd save it until... (personal recommendation)
    - Better to save it until... (comparative advice)
    
    **Pragmatic Notes**: This can be strategic advice or resource management; tone affects whether it's helpful or controlling
    
    **Response Patterns**: 
    - Good thinking (accepting advice)
    - Why? (seeking reasoning)
    - I think I'll use it now (politely declining)
    
    **Examples [Casual]**: 
    - Save your energy until the final round.
    - Save that story until everyone's here!
    
    **Examples [Formal]**: 
    - I recommend reserving those funds until the next fiscal quarter.
    - Consider postponing this announcement until stakeholder alignment is achieved.
