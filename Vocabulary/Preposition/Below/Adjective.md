1. **Adjective**: below-par
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not as good as usual or expected; below the normal or acceptable standard
   - *Gradable*; *Both attributive and predicative*
   
   **Synonyms**: substandard, inferior, inadequate, unsatisfactory, subpar
   
   **Antonyms**: excellent, superior, outstanding, above-par, exceptional
   
   **When to Use**: When describing performance, quality, or standards that fail to meet expectations; commonly used in professional assessments, sports commentary, and quality evaluations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to physical position (use "below" or "lower" instead), in highly formal academic writing where "inadequate" or "insufficient" is preferred
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - below-par performance
   - below-par results
   - below-par quality
   - feeling below-par (British English for feeling unwell)
   
   **Root Analysis**: below (lower position) + par (standard/expected level from golf)
   
   **Etymology**: From golf terminology where "par" is the standard number of strokes; "below par" originally meant better in golf but evolved to mean "worse than expected" in general usage
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm feeling a bit below-par today, might be coming down with something.
   - His work's been really below-par latelyâ€”not sure what's going on.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The quarterly financial performance was deemed below-par by industry analysts, necessitating strategic intervention.
   - Clinical assessments revealed below-par cognitive function in patients with prolonged exposure to the environmental factors.

1. **Adjective**: below-average
   **Meaning**: 
   - Less than the typical or mean level; lower than what is considered normal or standard
   - *Gradable*; *Primarily attributive*
   
   **Synonyms**: subaverage, mediocre, inferior, low-grade, second-rate
   
   **Antonyms**: above-average, superior, exceptional, excellent, outstanding
   
   **When to Use**: In educational contexts, statistical analysis, performance reviews, and when making comparative assessments against norms
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing individuals in sensitive contexts (can be offensive), when precision requires specific numerical data rather than relative terms
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - below-average intelligence
   - below-average income
   - below-average student
   - below-average temperature
   
   **Root Analysis**: below (lower than) + average (mean/typical value)
   
   **Etymology**: Mathematical/statistical term combining "below" with "average" (from French avarie "damage to ship"); used since late 19th century for statistical comparisons
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The restaurant had below-average reviews, but we gave it a shot anyway.
   - He's not a bad player, just below-average compared to the rest of the team.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Students with below-average reading comprehension scores were provided supplementary educational interventions.
   - The region experienced below-average precipitation levels for the third consecutive fiscal year.
