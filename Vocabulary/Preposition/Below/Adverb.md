1. **Adverb**: below (type: place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In or to a lower position or place; at a lower level or layer
   - Further down in a piece of writing or on a page; in a later section
   - On a lower floor or deck of a building or ship
   
   **Synonyms**: beneath, underneath, downstairs, underneath, lower down
   
   **Antonyms**: above, overhead, upstairs, higher up
   
   **When to Use**: When referring to physical position without specifying what something is lower than, when directing readers to information later in a document, or when referring to lower floors/levels
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When specific reference point is needed (use "below [noun]" instead), in contexts requiring directional movement verbs (use "down" instead), when referring to rank abstractly without spatial metaphor
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - see below (directing attention)
   - from below (origin point)
   - down below (emphatic form)
   - as mentioned below
   - illustrated below
   - listed below
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by, about) + low (not high) → adverbial form
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English bilooghe, used adverbially to indicate lower position or later position in text; nautical use for lower decks dates from 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I could hear voices from below, so I knew someone was in the basement.
   - Check out the recipe below—it's super easy to make!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The structural integrity of the foundation is compromised, as evidenced by the subsidence patterns observed below.
   - As demonstrated below, the methodology employed in this study adheres to established protocols.

1. **Adverb**: far below (type: degree/place)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At a position or level that is much lower than something; at a considerable distance beneath
   - Significantly less than a standard, expectation, or requirement
   
   **Synonyms**: well below, considerably lower, much beneath, significantly under
   
   **Antonyms**: far above, well above, considerably higher
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasizing substantial distance or difference; when highlighting significant inadequacy or deficiency in measurements, standards, or expectations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When minor differences need to be described (use "slightly below"), when exact measurements are more appropriate than relative descriptions
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - far below expectations
   - far below the surface
   - far below capacity
   - far below average
   
   **Root Analysis**: far (great distance) + below (lower position/level)
   
   **Etymology**: Combination of Old English feor (far, distant) with "below"; used to intensify the degree of separation or deficiency
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The shipwreck is far below the surface—you'd need special equipment to reach it.
   - Her test scores were far below what we expected given her usual performance.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Current enrollment figures remain far below the institutional targets established in the strategic plan.
   - The observed thermal conductivity values were far below those predicted by theoretical models.
