# Multi-Word Expressions for Preposition Vocabulary Learning

1. **Expression**: at the moment (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Right now; currently; at this particular point in time
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed temporal expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: right now, currently, at present, at this time, presently
   
   **Antonyms**: later, eventually, in the past, previously
   
   **When to Use**: Indicating current time, temporary situations, present circumstances
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Permanent states (use "always"), past or future (adjust time reference)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - at this moment
   - at that moment
   - for the moment
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "At" pairs with "moment" to create specific point-in-time reference; conventional pairing for current time reference
   
   **Root Analysis**: at [specific point] + the + moment [point in time]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm really busy at the moment—can I call you back?
   - At the moment, I'm living with my parents.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - At the moment, the research is in its preliminary stages.
   - The system is unavailable at the moment due to maintenance.

1. **Expression**: in advance (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Beforehand; ahead of time; before something happens
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed temporal-sequential expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: beforehand, ahead of time, previously, earlier, in anticipation
   
   **Antonyms**: afterward, later, in arrears, belatedly
   
   **When to Use**: Preparation, booking, planning, doing something before deadline
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For past actions (use "previously"), simultaneous actions (use "while")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - well in advance
   - far in advance
   - months in advance
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "In" combines with "advance" to indicate temporal precedence; conventional expression for preparation
   
   **Root Analysis**: in [within state] + advance [forward movement]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You need to book your tickets in advance.
   - Thanks for letting me know in advance!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants were informed in advance of the study procedures.
   - Payment must be received in advance of service delivery.

1. **Expression**: on behalf of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As the representative of; in the interest of; speaking for; acting for
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression for representation)
   
   **Synonyms**: for, representing, in the name of, as a representative of
   
   **Antonyms**: against, in opposition to
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts, representation, acting as agent, official communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Personal actions not representing others, informal contexts (use "for")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - on someone's behalf
   - on behalf of [organization]
   - acting on behalf of
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Medieval concept of representing another's interests; "behalf" (benefit/interest) with "on" indicates agency
   
   **Root Analysis**: on [position of] + behalf [benefit, interest]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm calling on behalf of my mom to cancel her appointment.
   - Can you thank them on behalf of all of us?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - On behalf of the committee, I welcome you to this conference.
   - The attorney acted on behalf of the plaintiff.

1. **Expression**: by accident (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Unintentionally; not on purpose; by chance; accidentally
   - Literal meaning vs. Figurative/Idiomatic meaning: Somewhat transparent but fixed expression
   - *Fixedness*: strong (cannot say "by an accident" or "through accident")
   
   **Synonyms**: accidentally, unintentionally, by chance, inadvertently
   
   **Antonyms**: on purpose, deliberately, intentionally, by design
   
   **When to Use**: Describing unintended actions or events, lack of planning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Intended actions (use "on purpose"), neutral descriptions (use "accidentally")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - purely by accident
   - quite by accident
   - almost by accident
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Historical pairing where "by" indicates manner and "accident" means chance occurrence; fixed through long usage
   
   **Root Analysis**: by [manner] + accident [chance occurrence]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I found out about the party by accident.
   - Did you break it on purpose or by accident?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The discovery was made by accident during routine testing.
   - The file was deleted by accident rather than intentionally.

1. **Expression**: in charge of (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Responsible for; managing; supervising; having authority over
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed collocation for responsibility)
   
   **Synonyms**: responsible for, managing, overseeing, supervising, leading
   
   **Antonyms**: under the supervision of, subordinate to, reporting to
   
   **When to Use**: Describing responsibility, authority, management roles
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Peer relationships (use "working with"), when not in authority position
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - put someone in charge of
   - be in charge of
   - person in charge
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "In" indicates state of responsibility, "charge" means duty/custody; semantic compatibility creates natural pairing
   
   **Root Analysis**: in [state of] + charge [responsibility, care]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Who's in charge of bringing the drinks?
   - My sister is in charge of the whole department.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Dr. Smith is in charge of the research program.
   - The manager in charge of operations will address your concerns.

1. **Expression**: out of order (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not functioning; broken; not working properly
   - *(Sense 2)* Not following proper procedure or rules (in meetings/debates)
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: broken, not working, malfunctioning, defective, inoperative
   
   **Antonyms**: working, functional, operational, in order
   
   **When to Use**: Describing broken equipment, improper procedure, wrong sequence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Working items (use "functional"), when order is correct
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - completely out of order
   - temporarily out of order
   - out of order (procedurally)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "Out of" indicates departure from state, "order" means proper arrangement/function; conventionalized for malfunction
   
   **Root Analysis**: out of [away from state] + order [proper arrangement]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Sorry, the elevator's out of order.
   - That comment was completely out of order!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The equipment was taken out of service as it was out of order.
   - The motion is out of order according to parliamentary procedure.

1. **Expression**: for the sake of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In order to help, benefit, or please; for the purpose of; because of concern for
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression for purpose/benefit)
   
   **Synonyms**: for the purpose of, for the benefit of, in order to, because of
   
   **Antonyms**: to the detriment of, against the interests of
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing purpose, sacrifice, motivation based on concern for someone/something
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Neutral purposes (use "for"), when no sacrifice/concern implied
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - for someone's sake
   - for God's sake (emphatic)
   - for old times' sake
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Medieval concept; "sake" means cause/benefit; "for the sake of" grammaticalized into purpose marker
   
   **Root Analysis**: for + the + sake [cause, benefit, purpose]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm doing this for the sake of our friendship.
   - For heaven's sake, please be quiet!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - For the sake of clarity, the terms will be defined precisely.
   - The policy was revised for the sake of consistency.

1. **Expression**: at risk (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In danger; in a situation where something bad might happen; vulnerable
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression for vulnerability)
   
   **Synonyms**: in danger, endangered, vulnerable, threatened, at stake
   
   **Antonyms**: safe, secure, protected, out of danger
   
   **When to Use**: Describing danger, vulnerability, potential negative outcomes
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Complete safety (use "safe"), guaranteed outcomes
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - at high risk
   - at risk of/for
   - put at risk
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "At" indicates position/state, "risk" means danger; conventional pairing for vulnerability state
   
   **Root Analysis**: at [in state of] + risk [danger, exposure]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your health is at risk if you don't exercise.
   - We're putting our friendship at risk here.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants at risk for cardiovascular disease were excluded.
   - The species is at risk of extinction due to habitat loss.

1. **Expression**: by means of (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Using; through; via; with the help of; by way of
   - *Fixedness*: medium-strong (formal expression for method)
   
   **Synonyms**: by, using, through, via, with, by way of
   
   **Antonyms**: without, in the absence of
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing, describing methods, instruments, or processes
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "by" or "using"), when simpler phrasing works
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - by this means
   - by various means
   - by what means
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "By" indicates agency/method, "means" indicates instrument/method; formal grammaticalized instrument marker
   
   **Root Analysis**: by [through agency] + means [method, instrument]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You can pay by means of credit card or cash.
   - She communicates by means of sign language.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The hypothesis was tested by means of controlled experimentation.
   - Communication occurs by means of chemical signaling.

1. **Expression**: on time (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Punctually; at the scheduled or expected time; not late
   - *Fixedness*: strong (cannot substitute preposition)
   
   **Synonyms**: punctually, promptly, as scheduled
   
   **Antonyms**: late, behind schedule, delayed, tardy
   
   **When to Use**: Describing punctuality, meeting deadlines, scheduled arrivals
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Early arrival (use "early"), general time sufficiency (use "in time")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - right on time
   - exactly on time
   - arrive on time
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "On" suggests contact with schedule point; conventionalized for punctuality (contrast "in time" = before deadline)
   
   **Root Analysis**: on [at the point of] + time [scheduled moment]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Make sure you're on time for the interview.
   - The train was actually on time today!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The project was completed on time and within budget.
   - Participants were required to arrive on time for each session.

   **Synonyms**: shared, mutual, jointly held
   
   **Antonyms**: different, distinct, unique, individual
   
   **When to Use**: Describing shared characteristics, mutual interests, commonalities
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Differences (use "different"), individual traits (use "unique to")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - have in common
   - in common with
   - nothing in common
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "In" indicates state, "common" means shared; conventional pairing for shared attributes
   
   **Root Analysis**: in [in state of] + common [shared, mutual]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We have a lot in common—we both love hiking.

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Expression**: at once (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Immediately; without delay; right away
   - *(Sense 2)* Simultaneously; at the same time
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed temporal expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: immediately, right away, instantly, simultaneously (sense 2)
   
   **Antonyms**: later, gradually, eventually, one at a time (sense 2)
   
   **When to Use**: Urgency, immediate action, simultaneous occurrence
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Delayed action (use "later"), sequential actions (use "one by one")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - all at once
   - right at once (rare)
   - do two things at once
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "At" indicates temporal point, "once" means single instance; conventionalized for immediacy/simultaneity
   
   **Root Analysis**: at [at point] + once [one time, immediately]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Come here at once!
   - I can't do everything at once.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The situation requires immediate attention; action must be taken at once.
   - The system can process multiple requests at once.

1. **Expression**: under control (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Managed; regulated; not out of hand; mastered
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression for management state)
   
   **Synonyms**: managed, contained, regulated, mastered, in hand
   
   **Antonyms**: out of control, uncontrolled, chaotic, wild
   
   **When to Use**: Describing managed situations, regulated conditions, mastered skills
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Unmanaged situations (use "out of control"), beginning stages (use "getting under control")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - bring under control
   - get under control
   - keep under control
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Under" indicates subordination/governance, "control" means regulation; conventional pairing for managed state
   
   **Root Analysis**: under [in position of subordination] + control [regulation, management]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Don't worry, I've got everything under control.
   - The fire is finally under control.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The epidemic was brought under control through vaccination programs.
   - Variables were carefully kept under control throughout the experiment.

1. **Expression**: in accordance with (type: phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Following; conforming to; as specified by; in agreement with
   - *Fixedness*: strong (formal expression for conformity)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in conformity with, in line with, following, as per
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, in violation of, in opposition to
   
   **When to Use**: Formal/legal writing, following rules/standards, compliance contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (use "following" or "according to"), violations
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in strict accordance with
   - in full accordance with
   - in accordance with regulations
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "In" indicates state, "accordance" means agreement/conformity; grammaticalized compliance marker
   
   **Root Analysis**: in [in state of] + accordance [agreement, conformity] + with
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Just do it in accordance with the instructions.
   - Everything was done in accordance with her wishes.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The study was conducted in accordance with ethical guidelines.
   - All procedures were performed in accordance with institutional protocols.

1. **Expression**: on purpose (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Intentionally; deliberately; not by accident; on intention
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed expression for intentionality)
   
   **Synonyms**: intentionally, deliberately, on intention, willfully, knowingly
   
   **Antonyms**: accidentally, by accident, unintentionally, inadvertently
   
   **When to Use**: Describing deliberate actions, intentional behavior, planned events
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Accidents (use "by accident"), neutral descriptions (use "intentionally")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - do something on purpose
   - did you do that on purpose?
   - (rarely: for purpose - archaic)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: "On" indicates basis/ground, "purpose" means intention; conventionalized intentionality marker (contrast "by accident")
   
   **Root Analysis**: on [on basis of] + purpose [intention, aim]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Did you spill that on purpose?
   - She ignored me on purpose, I know it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data were excluded on purpose to maintain sample homogeneity.
   - The design was simplified on purpose to enhance usability.
