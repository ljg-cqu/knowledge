1. **Preposition**: in view of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* considering or taking into account (a particular fact or circumstance); because of
   - Used to introduce a reason or justification for something, especially in formal contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: considering (in context of reasoning), given (in context of circumstances), in light of (in context of new information), taking into account (in context of deliberation), because of (in context of causation)
   
   **Antonyms**: disregarding, ignoring, despite, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal writing or speech when presenting reasons, justifications, or considerations; academic writing, business communications, official statements, policy discussions; when introducing causal relationships with sophistication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversations (sounds overly formal); when simpler alternatives like "because" would suffice in informal contexts; avoid overuse in speech as it can sound pompous; not appropriate when the relationship is straightforward and doesn't require formal reasoning
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in view of the circumstances, in view of recent events, in view of these findings, in view of the evidence, in view of this situation
   - **Sentence patterns**: In view of [noun phrase], [main clause] | [Main clause] in view of [noun phrase]
   - **With demonstratives**: in view of this/these/that/those
   - **With possessives**: in view of our/your/their [concerns/needs/position]
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (preposition: within, inside) + view (noun: sight, perspective, consideration) + of (preposition: concerning, about) = "within the consideration of"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English; compound phrasal preposition formed from "view" (from Old French veue, from Latin videre "to see") combined with prepositions "in" and "of"; evolved from literal "within sight of" to abstract "taking into consideration"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Look, in view of what happened last week, I think we should just postpone the meeting.
   - In view of how tired everyone is, maybe we should call it a day.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In view of the current economic climate, the board has decided to implement cost-reduction measures.
   - The policy requires revision in view of recent legislative changes and stakeholder feedback.
