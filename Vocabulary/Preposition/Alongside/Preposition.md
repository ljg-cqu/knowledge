1. **Preposition**: alongside (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Next to or at the side of something; positioned parallel to and close to something
   - *(Spatial)* Moving or positioned in parallel with something while maintaining proximity
   - *(Abstract)* Together with; in cooperation with; at the same time as
   - *(Abstract)* In comparison with; considered together with
   
   **Synonyms**: beside (spatial), next to, by the side of, together with (abstract), in conjunction with
   
   **Antonyms**: opposite, away from, apart from, separately from
   
   **When to Use**: Use "alongside" to indicate parallel positioning or cooperation; common for describing physical proximity, collaborative work, or simultaneous occurrence; slightly more formal than "beside"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use for perpendicular positioning (use "across from" or "opposite"); avoid when direct contact is emphasized (prefer "against")
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: alongside the road, alongside each other, work alongside, stand alongside
   - **Verb + prep**: work alongside, walk alongside, sit alongside, run alongside
   - **Adjective + prep**: positioned alongside, placed alongside
   - **Noun + prep**: colleagues alongside, partners alongside
   
   **Root Analysis**: along (parallel to) + side (beside)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English (15th century) → along + side → compound preposition emphasizing parallel positioning
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I parked my car alongside yours in the driveway.
   - She walked alongside me the whole way home.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The new regulations will be implemented alongside existing policies.
   - Research findings should be considered alongside historical precedents.
