1. **Expression**: work alongside (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To collaborate or cooperate with someone in a professional capacity; to work together as equals or partners
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very common partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: collaborate with, work together with, cooperate with, partner with, team up with
   
   **Antonyms**: work against, work independently from, oppose
   
   **When to Use**: In professional contexts to emphasize equal partnership and collaboration; suitable for describing teamwork
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When hierarchical relationships are emphasized (use "work under" or "work for")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - working alongside
   - worked alongside
   - works alongside
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: The verb "work" naturally combines with "alongside" to emphasize collaborative professional relationships and equal partnership, distinguishing it from hierarchical arrangements
   
   **Root Analysis**: work (labor, effort) + alongside (in parallel, together)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I get to work alongside some really talented designers.
   - She worked alongside her mentor for years.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - External consultants will work alongside internal teams to facilitate change.
   - Researchers work alongside industry partners to develop practical applications.

   **Meaning**:
