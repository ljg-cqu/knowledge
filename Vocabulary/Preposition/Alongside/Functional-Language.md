1. **Functional Expression**: I'd like to work alongside [person/team] (function: expressing desire for collaboration)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Expression of desire to collaborate or cooperate with someone as equals or partners
   - *Formality*: formal/neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect
   
   **Synonyms**: I'd like to collaborate with, I'd like to partner with, I'd like to team up with, I'd like to work together with
   
   **Antonyms**: I'd prefer to work independently, I'd rather work alone, I'd like to work separately
   
   **When to Use**: In professional contexts when expressing interest in collaborative work; emphasizes equal partnership and respect
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When hierarchical relationship is implied; avoid in very casual contexts where simpler language suffices
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I would love to work alongside...
   - I'm hoping to work alongside...
   - I look forward to working alongside...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Politeness strategy using conditional "would like" softens request; "alongside" emphasizes equal partnership rather than hierarchy
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That would be great! I'd enjoy working with you too.
   - I appreciate your interest. Let's discuss how we can collaborate.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'd really like to work alongside you on this project.
   - It would be cool to work alongside someone with your experience.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I would welcome the opportunity to work alongside your team on this initiative.
   - I'd like to work alongside industry leaders to advance this research.
