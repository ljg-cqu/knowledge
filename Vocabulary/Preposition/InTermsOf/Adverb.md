1. **Adverb**: quantitatively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that relates to quantity or can be measured numerically; by means of quantitative measurement or analysis
   
   **Synonyms**: numerically, measurably, statistically, mathematically, metrically
   
   **Antonyms**: qualitatively, descriptively, subjectively
   
   **When to Use**: Use "quantitatively" in academic, scientific, or business contexts when emphasizing numerical measurement or statistical analysis; suitable for formal writing and technical discourse
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation; overly technical for non-specialist audiences; inappropriate when discussing subjective qualities
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - quantitatively assessed
   - quantitatively measured
   - quantitatively analyzed
   - quantitatively significant
   - quantitatively expressed
   
   **Root Analysis**: quantitative (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin quantitas (quantity) → quantitative → "quantitatively" (19th century), emphasizing numerical measurement approach
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We need to look at this quantitatively, with actual numbers and data.
   - Can you measure it quantitatively rather than just describing it?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The hypothesis was tested quantitatively using regression analysis.
   - Performance improvements were quantitatively demonstrated across all metrics.

1. **Adverb**: comparatively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - By comparison with something else; in relative terms rather than absolutely; to a moderate degree when compared to others
   - *(Sense 2)* Using a method of comparison
   
   **Synonyms**: relatively, proportionally, in comparison, moderately, fairly
   
   **Antonyms**: absolutely, independently, extremely, categorically
   
   **When to Use**: Use "comparatively" when expressing relative evaluation or when something is considered moderate when compared to others; suitable for formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when discussing absolute values or when comparison is irrelevant; may weaken statements requiring definitiveness
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - comparatively speaking
   - comparatively small
   - comparatively easy
   - comparatively few
   - comparatively recent
   
   **Root Analysis**: comparative (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin comparare (to compare) → comparative → "comparatively" (16th century), emphasizing relational evaluation
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Comparatively speaking, this year's been much easier than last year.
   - The price is comparatively low for what you're getting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The intervention proved comparatively effective when assessed against alternative approaches.
   - Resource allocation was comparatively efficient in this implementation phase.
