1. **Adjective**: above-mentioned (non-gradable, attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Referred to or cited in a preceding part of a text or document; previously stated or described
   - Used in formal writing to reference something discussed earlier
   
   **Synonyms**: aforementioned, aforesaid, previously mentioned, above-stated, foregoing
   
   **Antonyms**: below-mentioned, following, subsequent, later-described
   
   **When to Use**: Formal written communication, legal documents, academic papers, business reports when referring back to previously discussed items
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation or informal writing; sounds overly formal in everyday contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - the above-mentioned individual
   - above-mentioned factors
   - above-mentioned conditions
   - as per the above-mentioned guidelines
   - above-mentioned requirements
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (earlier in text) + mentioned (past participle of "mention")
   
   **Etymology**: Compound adjective from documentary language (17th century) → Latin-influenced formal English
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - (Rarely used casually; more natural: "the thing I mentioned earlier" or "what I said before")
   - The above-mentioned restaurant was really good.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The above-mentioned criteria must be satisfied before approval.
   - Applicants meeting the above-mentioned qualifications should submit documentation.

1. **Adjective**: above-average (gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Higher in quality, performance, or measurement than the typical or mean level; exceeding normal standards
   - Better than most but not exceptional or outstanding
   
   **Synonyms**: better-than-average, superior, higher-than-normal, above-standard, better-than-typical
   
   **Antonyms**: below-average, substandard, inferior, worse-than-average, subpar
   
   **When to Use**: Describing performance, quality, intelligence, or measurements that exceed the median; used in evaluations, assessments, and comparisons
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing truly exceptional performance (use "excellent" or "outstanding"); when precision requires specific percentiles
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - above-average performance
   - above-average intelligence
   - above-average results
   - above-average student
   - above-average salary
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (exceeding) + average (mean/typical level)
   
   **Etymology**: Statistical terminology → general evaluative language (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's an above-average player, pretty good actually.
   - The food was above-average but nothing special.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The candidate demonstrated above-average analytical capabilities.
   - Students with above-average scores qualify for advanced placement.

1. **Adjective**: aboveboard (single word variant, gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Honest, legitimate, and transparent in dealings or conduct; operating without deception
   - Conforming to ethical and legal standards openly
   
   **Synonyms**: legitimate, honest, transparent, open, straightforward, legal
   
   **Antonyms**: dishonest, shady, underhanded, deceitful, under-the-table
   
   **When to Use**: Describing business transactions, dealings, or behavior that is ethical and transparent; positive connotation in professional and legal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Overly formal for casual contexts; may sound defensive when applied to oneself
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - completely aboveboard
   - entirely aboveboard
   - keep things aboveboard
   - aboveboard transaction
   - aboveboard business practices
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (visible) + board (gaming table) → honest play with hands visible
   
   **Etymology**: Card gaming terminology (17th century) → general ethical descriptor → single-word compound (19th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Don't worry, it's all aboveboard.
   - Is this company aboveboard?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All procurement processes must be conducted in an aboveboard manner.
   - The investigation found the transactions to be entirely aboveboard.

1. **Adjective**: above-normal (gradable)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Higher than the usual, expected, or standard level; exceeding typical or baseline measurements
   - Used particularly in medical, meteorological, and statistical contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: elevated, higher-than-normal, increased, raised, excessive
   
   **Antonyms**: below-normal, subnormal, reduced, decreased, lower-than-normal
   
   **When to Use**: Medical reports, weather forecasts, scientific measurements when describing readings exceeding normal ranges
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "abnormal" (deviation in either direction) is meant; "above-normal" specifically indicates positive deviation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - above-normal temperatures
   - above-normal levels
   - above-normal rainfall
   - above-normal blood pressure
   - above-normal readings
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (exceeding) + normal (standard/typical)
   
   **Etymology**: Medical and scientific terminology (19th century) → technical measurement language
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It's been above-normal temperatures all week.
   - His fever is above-normal but not dangerous.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The region experienced above-normal precipitation throughout the quarter.
   - Blood glucose levels were consistently above-normal during testing.

   - above-par quality
   - above-par results
   - trading above-par
   - above-par work
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (exceeding) + par (standard/expected level)

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your work has been above-par lately, great job!
   - It's an above-par restaurant for the price.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The team consistently delivers above-par results across all metrics.
   - Bonds trading above-par indicate investor confidence in the issuer.

1. **Adjective**: above-threshold (non-gradable, technical)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Exceeding a minimum required level or criterion for activation, detection, or qualification
   - Meeting or surpassing the critical point where an effect or response occurs
   
   **Synonyms**: exceeding-threshold, suprathreshold, above-cutoff, qualifying
   
   **Antonyms**: below-threshold, subthreshold, under-threshold, non-qualifying
   
   **When to Use**: Scientific, medical, technical contexts describing stimuli, measurements, or values exceeding critical levels
   
   **When NOT to Use**: General conversation; highly technical terminology requiring domain knowledge
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - above-threshold stimulus
   - above-threshold values
   - above-threshold exposure
   - above-threshold response
   - above-threshold levels
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (exceeding) + threshold (minimum level for effect)
   
   **Etymology**: Scientific measurement terminology (20th century) → neuroscience and physiology
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The noise was above-threshold—it definitely woke me up.
   - (More commonly: "The noise was loud enough to wake me")
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Above-threshold stimuli reliably elicit neural responses in test subjects.
   - Exposure levels remained consistently above-threshold throughout the experimental period.

1. **Adjective**: above-water (non-gradable, attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Literal)* Located or occurring at or above the water surface; not submerged
   - *(Metaphorical)* Financially solvent; managing to survive economically
   
   **Synonyms**: surface, emerged, floating (literal); solvent, surviving, managing (metaphorical)
   
   **Antonyms**: underwater, submerged, below-water (literal); insolvent, failing, drowning (metaphorical)
   
   **When to Use**: Marine/aquatic contexts for literal meaning; financial contexts for metaphorical meaning; describes visibility or survival status
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Context must make clear whether literal or metaphorical; potential ambiguity in mixed contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - above-water portion
   - above-water visibility
   - above-water assets
   - barely above-water financially
   - keep above-water
   
   **Root Analysis**: above (higher than) + water (liquid surface or financial metaphor)
   
   **Etymology**: Marine terminology (literal, 18th century) → financial metaphor (20th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Only the tip of the iceberg is above-water.
   - We're barely above-water with these bills.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The above-water structure exhibits significant weathering from marine exposure.
   - Organizations struggled to maintain above-water financial positions during the recession.
