1. **Expression**: be in a [strong/weak] position vis-à-vis [X]
   **Type**: Collocation / semi‑fixed pattern
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Describe how much leverage, power, or room to maneuver one party has relative to another.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - be in a strong / weak / better / worse position vis-à-vis [counterparty]
   - Subject is usually *we / they / the firm / the union / the platform*.
   
   **Register**: Neutral → formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: negotiations, strategy, labor relations, platform dependence.
   
   **Synonyms**: have the upper hand over, be in a good/bad spot with, have leverage over  
   **Antonyms**: be at the mercy of, be in a vulnerable position, be on the back foot
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - After the new deal, we’re in a much stronger position vis-à-vis the landlord.  
   - As a small client, we’re in a weak position vis-à-vis that vendor.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - The consortium is now in a stronger position vis-à-vis regulators.  
   - Smaller providers remain in a structurally weak position vis-à-vis dominant platforms.

1. **Expression**: strengthen / improve our position vis-à-vis [X]
   **Type**: Collocation (verb + noun phrase)
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Take deliberate actions to gain leverage, security, or credibility relative to another party or risk.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - strengthen / improve / reinforce / consolidate our position vis-à-vis [X]
   
   **Register**: Semi‑formal → formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: strategy offsites, compliance roadmaps, partnership plans.
   
   **Synonyms**: gain leverage over, shore up our standing with, move to a better place with  
   **Antonyms**: weaken / undermine our position vis-à-vis, erode our leverage with
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - We need to strengthen our position vis-à-vis the big players before we launch.  
   - This feature really improves our position vis-à-vis competitors.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - The initiative aims to strengthen our position vis-à-vis regulatory scrutiny.  
   - Diversifying suppliers will improve our position vis-à-vis cost shocks.

1. **Expression**: obligations / duties vis-à-vis [group]
   **Type**: Collocation
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - The responsibilities one party has toward another.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - legal / ethical / contractual obligations vis-à-vis [employees / users / clients / citizens]
   
   **Register**: Formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: legal texts, policies, ethics guidelines.
   
   **Synonyms**: obligations toward, responsibilities to, duties regarding  
   **Antonyms**: rights vis-à-vis [group]; no obligations toward [group]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - What exactly are our obligations vis-à-vis early beta users?  
   - We have clear duties vis-à-vis staff when it comes to safety.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - The policy clarifies obligations vis-à-vis data subjects under the new regulation.  
   - Management’s duties vis-à-vis employees are laid out in section 3.

1. **Expression**: rights vis-à-vis [X]
   **Type**: Collocation
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - The entitlements one party enjoys relative to another authority or counterpart.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - rights vis-à-vis the state / the employer / the platform / service providers
   
   **Register**: Formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: law, contracts, citizen guidance, employee handbooks.
   
   **Synonyms**: rights against, rights toward, entitlements in relation to  
   **Antonyms**: obligations vis-à-vis [X], lack of rights, waivers
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - Do users have any rights vis-à-vis the platform if it goes down?  
   - I’d like to know my rights vis-à-vis the landlord.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - Citizens’ rights vis-à-vis state surveillance are defined in statute.  
   - The document explains workers’ rights vis-à-vis algorithmic management.

1. **Expression**: from [party]’s standpoint vis-à-vis [issue]
   **Type**: Semi‑fixed frame
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Introduces a perspective from one side of a relationship on a particular issue.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - From [users’ / regulators’ / investors’ / employees’] standpoint vis-à-vis [issue], …
   
   **Register**: Neutral → formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: multi‑stakeholder analysis, design workshops, policy debates.
   
   **Synonyms**: from [party]’s perspective in relation to [issue], for [party], when it comes to [issue]  
   **Antonyms**: objectively speaking (implying no specific standpoint)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - From the team’s standpoint vis-à-vis workload, this timeline is brutal.  
   - From users’ standpoint vis-à-vis trust, dark patterns are a disaster.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - From regulators’ standpoint vis-à-vis systemic risk, the current safeguards are insufficient.  
   - From employees’ standpoint vis-à-vis job security, the change is worrying.

1. **Expression**: relative to / in relation to [X]
   **Type**: Multi‑word prepositions (family of expressions)
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Frame a statement so that it is interpreted against a named baseline or dimension.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - [adjective] relative to [baseline]  
   - [verb] in relation to [dimension]
   
   **Register**: Neutral (in relation to) → analytic (relative to).
   
   **Typical Contexts**: data reporting, strategic comparisons, evaluation.
   
   **Synonyms**: compared with, as against, vis-à-vis  
   **Antonyms**: in absolute terms, regardless of
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - The rent is high relative to my salary.  
   - We’re doing okay in relation to where we started.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - Returns are modest relative to sector benchmarks.  
   - The study interprets results in relation to prior literature.

1. **Expression**: X versus Y, vis-à-vis [dimension]
   **Type**: Comparative frame
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Explicitly contrasts two options and states the dimension of comparison.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - [Option A] versus [Option B], vis-à-vis [cost / risk / user impact]
   
   **Register**: Neutral → formal (in writing).
   
   **Typical Contexts**: decision memos, trade‑off discussions, evaluation tables.
   
   **Synonyms**: X vs Y in terms of [dimension], X compared with Y regarding [dimension]  
   **Antonyms**: treating X and Y as equivalent; no explicit comparison
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - Cloud versus on‑prem, vis-à-vis latency—what’s our best bet?  
   - A versus B, vis-à-vis risk, which would you pick?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - Centralized versus federated architectures, vis-à-vis governance, are assessed in Section 4.  
   - Manual versus automated review, vis-à-vis error rates, shows a clear advantage for automation.

1. **Expression**: have leverage vis-à-vis [X]
   **Type**: Collocation
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - Possess bargaining power or influence over another party.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - have / gain / lose leverage vis-à-vis [platform / supplier / client]
   
   **Register**: Business‑formal.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: negotiations, platform economics, labor relations.
   
   **Synonyms**: have bargaining power over, be able to push back on, have clout with  
   **Antonyms**: lack leverage vis-à-vis, be dependent on, be at the mercy of
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - Right now we don’t have much leverage vis-à-vis that platform.  
   - This data gives us real leverage vis-à-vis our suppliers.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - Consolidation has increased the platform’s leverage vis-à-vis smaller merchants.  
   - Collective action can boost workers’ leverage vis-à-vis employers.

1. **Expression**: asymmetry / imbalance vis-à-vis [X]
   **Type**: Noun‑based expression
   
   **Core Meaning**: 
   - A structural lack of equality in power, information, or risk between parties.
   
   **Structure**: 
   - information / power / risk asymmetry vis-à-vis [group]
   - structural imbalance vis-à-vis [counterparty]
   
   **Register**: Formal / analytical.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: economics, law, ethics, policy.
   
   **Synonyms**: unequal relationship with, tilted playing field against, lopsided situation with  
   **Antonyms**: symmetry vis-à-vis, parity with, balanced relationship with
   
   **Examples [Casual]**:  
   - There’s huge information asymmetry vis-à-vis users here.  
   - The power imbalance vis-à-vis big tech is obvious.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**:  
   - Information asymmetry vis-à-vis end users raises accountability concerns.  
   - The agreement entrenches a structural imbalance vis-à-vis smaller partners.
