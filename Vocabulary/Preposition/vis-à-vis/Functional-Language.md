1. **Expression**: Where do we stand vis-à-vis [X]?
   **Pragmatic Function**: Asking for a comparative status update
   
   **Meaning**: Requests a concise picture of our current position in relation to a specific reference point (competitor, regulator, target, risk).
   
   **Typical Contexts**: strategy meetings, leadership reviews, risk briefings, project retrospectives.
   
   **Register**: Neutral → semi‑formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - Where do we stand vis-à-vis [the competition / the regulator / our targets]?
   - Where do we stand vis-à-vis our original plan?
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You want a high‑level comparison rather than raw numbers.
   - The audience already knows who or what [X] is.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - With people unfamiliar with *vis-à-vis* → prefer *“in relation to”* or *“compared with”*.
   - When you need a very specific metric (ask about *revenue vis-à-vis last year*, etc.).
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Meeting]**:
   - A: Where do we stand vis-à-vis the main competitor after their latest launch?
   - B: We’re still ahead on features, but weaker on price.

1. **Expression**: How does this change our position vis-à-vis [stakeholders]?
   **Pragmatic Function**: Probing impact on relationships
   
   **Meaning**: Asks how a decision or event shifts our leverage, reputation, or risk compared with some group.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: decision reviews, risk assessments, design workshops.
   
   **Register**: Neutral → formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - How does this change our position vis-à-vis [customers / regulators / partners]?
   - How does this change our position vis-à-vis smaller competitors?
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You want people to think beyond internal trade‑offs and consider external effects.
   - Before confirming a major policy or product change.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - When talking about purely internal technical refactors with no external visibility.
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Planning]**:
   - A: If we drop the free tier, how does this change our position vis-à-vis new users?
   - B: It improves short‑term revenue but makes us less attractive than freemium rivals.

1. **Expression**: We’re in a [strong/weak] position vis-à-vis [X].
   **Pragmatic Function**: Summarizing leverage or vulnerability
   
   **Meaning**: Gives a clear qualitative judgment about our relative power or options.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: negotiation prep, risk summaries, board slides.
   
   **Register**: Neutral
   
   **Patterns**:
   - We’re in a strong position vis-à-vis our suppliers.
   - We’re in a weak position vis-à-vis the platform right now.
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You need a short, digestible summary of where you stand.
   - As a headline before sharing supporting data.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - When nuance matters greatly (use *“mixed position vis-à-vis…”* or describe both strengths and weaknesses).
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Negotiation]**:
   - A: Are we in a strong position vis-à-vis that vendor?
   - B: Not yet—we still depend on them for a critical feature.

1. **Expression**: We need to strengthen our position vis-à-vis [X].
   **Pragmatic Function**: Calling for protective or improvement action
   
   **Meaning**: Signals that the current relative stance is not good enough and that proactive steps are required.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: strategy offsites, risk mitigation planning, compliance discussions.
   
   **Register**: Semi‑formal → formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - strengthen our position vis-à-vis regulators
   - strengthen our position vis-à-vis key accounts
   - strengthen our position vis-à-vis future competitors
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You want to motivate investment in resilience, trust, or leverage.
   - You are planning medium‑ to long‑term positioning.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - When the issue is purely short‑term execution, not underlying position.
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Risk]**:
   - A: We need to strengthen our position vis-à-vis regulators before the new rules land.
   - B: Agreed. Let’s increase our engagement and evidence base this quarter.

1. **Expression**: From [party]’s standpoint vis-à-vis [issue], …
   **Pragmatic Function**: Framing perspective and managing conflict
   
   **Meaning**: Explicitly introduces a viewpoint from one side of a relationship, preventing it from sounding like an absolute truth.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: stakeholder mapping, design critiques, negotiation analysis.
   
   **Register**: Neutral → formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - From the user’s standpoint vis-à-vis privacy, this feels risky.
   - From regulators’ standpoint vis-à-vis systemic risk, this isn’t acceptable.
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You want to surface and respect multiple perspectives.
   - You’re explaining why another side might object.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - When you must state a firm organizational decision, not just a perspective.
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Design]**:
   - A: From our standpoint vis-à-vis cost, this is great.
   - B: From users’ standpoint vis-à-vis trust, it’s much less attractive.

1. **Expression**: This creates an [asymmetric/balanced] relationship vis-à-vis [X].
   **Pragmatic Function**: Evaluating fairness and structure
   
   **Meaning**: Comments on whether obligations, risks, or benefits are fairly distributed between sides.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: contract reviews, policy drafting, ethics committees.
   
   **Register**: Formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - creates an asymmetric relationship vis-à-vis smaller suppliers
   - maintains a balanced relationship vis-à-vis users
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You need to comment on fairness or power distribution.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - For minor, temporary inconveniences—reserve for structural patterns.
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Policy]**:
   - A: The clause creates an asymmetric relationship vis-à-vis creators.
   - B: Then we should rebalance obligations and rights.

1. **Expression**: We’ll reassess our stance vis-à-vis [X] after [event].
   **Pragmatic Function**: Deferring a decision while keeping orientation
   
   **Meaning**: Signals that the current position is provisional and will be revisited in light of a future trigger.
   
   **Typical Contexts**: roadmapping, regulatory strategy, partnership talks.
   
   **Register**: Semi‑formal → formal
   
   **Patterns**:
   - reassess our stance vis-à-vis the platform after the pilot
   - reassess our stance vis-à-vis investment once Q2 results arrive
   
   **When to Use**:
   - You need to avoid a premature hard commitment but still show direction.
   
   **When NOT to Use**:
   - When stakeholders require a clear yes/no decision now.
   
   **Mini‑Dialogue [Roadmap]**:
   - A: Are we committing long‑term to this vendor?
   - B: We’ll reassess our stance vis-à-vis them after the first year’s results.
