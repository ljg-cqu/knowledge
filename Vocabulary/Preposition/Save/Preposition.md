1. **Preposition**: for (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* Indicating the purpose, goal, or intended recipient of saving; expressing the beneficiary of an action
   - *(Temporal)* Indicating duration or future point in time related to savings
   
   **Synonyms**: toward (in context of purpose), to benefit
   
   **Antonyms**: against, from
   
   **When to Use**: Expressing purpose of saving, intended use of saved resources, or duration of savings
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When expressing source or origin (use "from"); avoid when spatial location is meant
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: for retirement, for emergencies, for the future
   - **Verb + prep**: save for, set aside for, prepare for
   - **Adjective + prep**: ready for, prepared for
   - **Noun + prep**: savings for, fund for, money for
   
   **Root Analysis**: for (Old English)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English for "before, in front of, instead of, on behalf of" (brief)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm saving for a new laptop.
   - We've been putting money aside for vacation.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants are encouraged to save for retirement through systematic contributions.
   - The organization allocates resources for future capital investments.
