1. **Expression**: save money (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To keep money instead of spending it; to reduce expenses
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: economize, reduce spending, cut costs, put money aside
   
   **Antonyms**: spend money, waste money, splurge
   
   **When to Use**: All contexts discussing financial management, budgeting, or cost reduction
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to earning money (use "make money"); avoid for investment contexts
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - save some money
   - save a lot of money
   - save your money
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Money" is the most natural direct object of "save" in financial contexts; high frequency pairing establishes strong collocation
   
   **Root Analysis**: save (Latin salvare) + money (Latin moneta)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm trying to save money for a new phone.
   - This app helps you save money on groceries.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Organizations implement efficiency measures to save money on operational costs.
   - Consumers can save money through strategic purchasing decisions.
