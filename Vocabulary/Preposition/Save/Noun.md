1. **Noun**: savings (C/U, pl: savings)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Uncountable) The money that you have saved, especially in a bank; the act of keeping money for future use
   - *(Sense 2)* (Countable) A reduction in cost, time, or resources; the amount saved
   
   **Synonyms**: reserves, funds, nest egg, capital, resources
   
   **Antonyms**: expenditures, spending, debts, outgoings
   
   **When to Use**: Financial contexts discussing money set aside; business contexts discussing cost reductions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Immediate spending or consumption contexts; avoid when discussing loans or debts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - personal savings
   - savings account
   - cost savings
   
   **Root Analysis**: save (from Latin salvare) + -ing (gerund) + -s (plural/mass noun marker)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin salvare → save + -ings → savings (brief)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm building up my savings for a vacation.
   - We made huge savings by shopping during the sale.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The institution reported substantial savings through operational efficiency improvements.
   - Personal savings rates have declined significantly over the past decade.

1. **Noun**: backup (C, pl: backups)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A copy of data or files stored separately to prevent loss; a reserve or substitute
   
   **Synonyms**: copy, duplicate, reserve, redundancy, safeguard
   
   **Antonyms**: original, primary, main version
   
   **When to Use**: Technology and data management contexts; emergency planning situations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Original or primary data contexts; avoid when referring to the main version
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - data backup
   - backup copy
   - backup system
   
   **Root Analysis**: back (adverb) + up (particle) → backup (compound noun)
   
   **Etymology**: English back + up → backup (20th century compound) (brief)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Do you have a backup of that file anywhere?
   - I keep backups of all my photos on an external drive.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Regular data backups are essential for business continuity.
   - The organization maintains redundant backups across multiple geographical locations.

1. **Noun**: nest egg (C, pl: nest eggs)
   **Meaning**:

   **Antonyms**: debt, liability
   
   **When to Use**: Informal to semi-formal contexts discussing long-term savings; retirement planning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal financial or legal documents; avoid in technical financial contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - retirement nest egg
   - build a nest egg
   - substantial nest egg
   
   **Root Analysis**: nest (bird's home) + egg (literal egg saved to encourage laying) → metaphorical compound
   
   **Etymology**: From farmers placing eggs in nests to encourage hens to lay more → savings metaphor (brief)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's been building her nest egg for retirement.
   - It's never too early to start your nest egg.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Financial advisors recommend establishing a substantial nest egg for retirement security.
   - The retirement planning seminar emphasized the importance of building an adequate nest egg.
