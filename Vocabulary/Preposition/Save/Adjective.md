1. **Adjective**: saved
   **Meaning**: 
   - (attributive/predicative, non-gradable) Kept from being spent, lost, or wasted; preserved for future use
   - *(Sense 2)* Rescued from danger, harm, or destruction
   
   **Synonyms**: preserved, stored, conserved, rescued, protected
   
   **Antonyms**: spent, wasted, lost, squandered
   
   **When to Use**: When describing resources (money, time, energy) that have been set aside or protected; when describing people or things that have been rescued from danger
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid using with uncountable abstract nouns where "preserved" would be more appropriate (e.g., dignity, honor)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - saved money
   - saved documents
   - saved data
   
   **Root Analysis**: save (base verb from Latin salvare) + -ed (past participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin salvare ("to save") → Old French sauver → Middle English saven (brief)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I've got some saved cash in case of emergencies.
   - Thanks to the saved files, we didn't lose anything.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The saved resources will be allocated to future projects.
   - The saved data demonstrates significant energy conservation.
