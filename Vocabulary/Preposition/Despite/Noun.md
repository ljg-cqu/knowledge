1. **Noun**: obstacle (C, pl: obstacles)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A thing that blocks one's way or prevents or hinders progress
   - Something that makes it difficult to achieve something
   
   **Synonyms**: barrier, impediment, hindrance, hurdle, obstruction
   
   **Antonyms**: advantage, aid, assistance, help, facilitator
   
   **When to Use**: Describing challenges or difficulties; all registers; problem-solving contexts; when emphasizing things to overcome
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to people blocking progress (use "opposition"); for minor inconveniences (use "difficulty")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - overcome obstacles
   - face obstacles
   - despite obstacles
   - insurmountable obstacle
   - major obstacle
   
   **Root Analysis**: ob- (against) + sta (stand) + -cle (diminutive)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin obstaculum (hindrance) → Old French → English "obstacle"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Despite many obstacles, she finished her degree.
   - We hit several obstacles along the way.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite significant regulatory obstacles, the organization achieved compliance within projected timelines.
   - The research team overcame substantial methodological obstacles to produce reliable findings.
