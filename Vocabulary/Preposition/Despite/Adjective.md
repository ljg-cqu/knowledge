1. **Adjective**: persistent (comparative: more persistent, superlative: most persistent)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Continuing firmly or obstinately despite difficulty, opposition, or warnings
   - *(despite context)* Maintaining effort or action even when facing obstacles or discouragement
   
   **Synonyms**: determined, tenacious, steadfast, relentless, unwavering
   
   **Antonyms**: yielding, irresolute, wavering, inconstant, fickle
   
   **When to Use**: Describing continued effort despite challenges; formal and informal contexts; expressing admiration or criticism for stubbornness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing flexible, adaptable behavior (use "flexible" or "adaptable"); when persistence is seen as negative obstinacy
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - persistent effort
   - persistent problem
   - persistent despite setbacks
   - persistent attempts
   - remain persistent
   
   **Root Analysis**: per- (through, thoroughly) + sist (stand) + -ent (adjective suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin persistere (to continue steadfastly) → Old French persister → English "persistent"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She's really persistent despite all the rejections.
   - He kept trying, persistent despite the odds.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The team remained persistent despite numerous technical challenges throughout the project.
   - Despite unfavorable market conditions, the company maintained persistent growth efforts.
