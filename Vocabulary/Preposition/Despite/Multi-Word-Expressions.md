1. **Expression**: against all odds (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In spite of very low probability of success; despite overwhelming difficulties
   - *Fixedness*: strong (highly fixed expression)
   
   **Synonyms**: despite the odds, against the odds, defying expectations, in the face of adversity
   
   **Antonyms**: as expected, predictably, with favorable odds, easily
   
   **When to Use**: Dramatic contexts; emphasizing improbability of success; storytelling; inspirational contexts; when contrast is striking
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When success wasn't particularly unlikely; understatement contexts; technical writing requiring precision
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - against the odds (less dramatic)
   - defying all odds (more active verb form)
   - in the face of overwhelming odds
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Gambling metaphor (odds = probability); emphasizes statistical improbability or extreme difficulty
   
   **Root Analysis**: against (in opposition to) + all (every) + odds (probability of success)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Against all odds, they made it to the finals.
   - She recovered against all odds.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Against all odds, the startup achieved profitability within its first year of operation.
   - The patient survived against all odds, defying medical expectations and statistical probabilities.

1. **Expression**: despite the fact that (type: complex conjunction phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Although; even though; in spite of the truth that
   - *Fixedness*: strong (standard phrase for introducing clauses with "despite")
   
   **Synonyms**: although, even though, in spite of the fact that, notwithstanding that
   
   **Antonyms**: because, since, given that, due to the fact that
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing; when "despite" needs to introduce a clause rather than noun phrase; academic writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When "although" is simpler and clearer; very casual contexts; when wordiness is a concern (prefer "although")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - in spite of the fact that (interchangeable)
   - despite the reality that (emphasizes truth)
   - despite the circumstance that (more formal)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Grammatical necessity; "despite" takes noun objects, so "the fact that" converts clause to noun phrase
   
   **Root Analysis**: despite + the fact (noun) + that (complementizer introducing clause)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Despite the fact that it's late, I'm not tired.
   - She succeeded despite the fact that nobody helped.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite the fact that sample sizes were modest, results demonstrate statistically robust patterns.
   - The proposal merits consideration despite the fact that implementation challenges are substantial.
