1. **Adverb**: nevertheless (type: sentence adverb/discourse marker)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In spite of that; notwithstanding; all the same
   - Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said
   
   **Synonyms**: nonetheless, however, still, yet, even so
   
   **Antonyms**: therefore, consequently, thus, accordingly, hence
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal writing; introducing contrasting statements; showing that something is true despite what was just mentioned
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation (use "but" or "still"); when causal relationship exists (use "therefore")
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - nevertheless, despite this
   - nevertheless continued
   - but nevertheless
   - nevertheless managed
   - nevertheless persisted
   
   **Root Analysis**: never + the + less (not the less for that)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English never the lesse → nevertheless (compound adverb meaning "not any the less")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It was tough, but I succeeded nevertheless.
   - She was tired; nevertheless, she kept going.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data contains significant limitations; nevertheless, the findings warrant serious consideration.
   - Despite methodological challenges, the research nevertheless provides valuable insights into the phenomenon.

1. **Adverb**: nonetheless (type: sentence adverb/discourse marker)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In spite of that; nevertheless; however
   - Used to emphasize that something remains true despite what has just been mentioned
   
   **Synonyms**: nevertheless, however, still, yet, all the same
   
   **Antonyms**: therefore, consequently, thus, accordingly, hence
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and semi-formal contexts; academic and professional writing; emphasizing contrast; slightly more emphatic than "nevertheless"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very casual conversation; when simple "but" suffices; when showing causation
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - nonetheless, despite
   - nonetheless succeeded
   - but nonetheless
   - nonetheless important
   - nonetheless continued
   
   **Root Analysis**: none + the + less (not any the less)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English none the lesse → nonetheless (not any the less for that)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The odds were against us, but we won nonetheless.
   - He was nervous but performed well nonetheless.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite these constraints, the project nonetheless achieved its primary objectives with remarkable efficiency.
   - The sample size was modest; nonetheless, the results demonstrate statistically significant patterns.

1. **Adverb**: still (type: sentence adverb/time adverb)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Up to and including the present or the time mentioned; nevertheless; even so
   - *(despite context)* Emphasizing continuation or persistence despite obstacles or expectations

   **Antonyms**: no longer, not anymore, ceased, stopped
   
   **When to Use**: All registers; very common; versatile usage; can emphasize time or contrast
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When ambiguity between temporal and concessive meaning could cause confusion
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - still, despite everything
   - still managed
   - still persisted
   - but still
   - still succeeded
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English word, related to stillness
   
   **Etymology**: Old English stille (motionless) → extended to mean "continuing despite" 
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Despite the rain, we still went out.
   - It was hard, but I still finished.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite significant methodological limitations, the study still contributes meaningfully to existing literature.
