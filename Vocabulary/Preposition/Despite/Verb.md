1. **Verb**: persist
   **Meaning**: 
   - To continue firmly or obstinately in an opinion or course of action despite difficulty, opposition, or failure (intransitive)
   - To continue to exist or endure over a prolonged period (intransitive)
   
   **Synonyms**: persevere, continue, endure, maintain, carry on
   
   **Antonyms**: quit, surrender, yield, abandon, desist
   
   **When to Use**: Describing continued action despite obstacles; formal and informal contexts; emphasizing determination or stubbornness
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing brief or temporary actions; when flexibility is valued (may sound stubborn)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - persist despite setbacks
   - persist in efforts
   - persist with attempts
   - continue to persist
   - persist against odds
   
   **Root Analysis**: per- (through, thoroughly) + sist (stand)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin persistere (to stand firm, continue steadfastly) → French → English "persist"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Despite everyone saying no, he persisted.
   - She persisted despite all the rejection.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite repeated setbacks, the research team persisted in pursuing empirical validation.
   - The organization persisted with implementation despite substantial stakeholder resistance.
