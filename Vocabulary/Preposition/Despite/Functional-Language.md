1. **Functional Expression**: Despite [X], I still think [Y] (function: expressing opinion with concession)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to maintain an opinion while acknowledging a contrasting factor or objection
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct with hedging
   
   **Synonyms**: Even though [X], I believe [Y]; Regardless of [X], in my view [Y]; [X] notwithstanding, I maintain [Y]
   
   **Antonyms**: Because of [X], I think [Y]; [X] proves that [Y]
   
   **When to Use**: Discussions where acknowledging opposing viewpoints; maintaining position while showing openness; professional and personal contexts; building credibility through balanced perspective
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When concession undermines your main point; when appearing uncertain damages credibility; in contexts requiring absolute positions
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Despite [X], I believe [Y] (more formal)
   - Even with [X], I still say [Y] (more casual)
   - [X] aside, I think [Y] (more dismissive)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Positive politeness strategy (showing consideration of others' views); face-saving (acknowledges validity of counterarguments); creates perception of balanced thinking
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That's fair / I see your point (accepting the balanced view)
   - But doesn't [X] change things? (challenging the dismissal of the concession)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Despite the bad reviews, I still think it's worth watching.
   - Even though it's expensive, I still think we should buy it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Despite the preliminary data suggesting otherwise, I still believe the hypothesis merits further investigation.
   - Notwithstanding the concerns raised by stakeholders, I maintain that this approach offers optimal outcomes.

1. **Functional Expression**: I understand [X], but despite that, [Y] (function: showing empathy while disagreeing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to acknowledge someone's concern or position before presenting a contrasting view
   - *Formality*: neutral to semi-formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (softened disagreement)
   
   **Synonyms**: I hear what you're saying about [X], but even so [Y]; I appreciate [X], however [Y]; I recognize [X], yet [Y]
   
   **Antonyms**: You're wrong about [X] because [Y]; [X] is irrelevant, [Y] is what matters
   
   **When to Use**: Disagreeing respectfully; maintaining relationships while challenging views; showing thoughtfulness; professional and diplomatic contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When empathy seems insincere or manipulative; when directness is more appropriate; in very casual contexts where simpler language works
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I get that [X], but despite that, [Y] (more casual)
   - I acknowledge [X], but despite that consideration, [Y] (more formal)
   - [X] makes sense, but despite that, [Y] (balanced middle ground)
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Negative politeness strategy (respecting others' face); minimizes imposition of disagreement; builds rapport through empathy before contrast
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - I appreciate you understanding that (acknowledging empathy)
   - So you're still going with [Y]? (seeking clarification of position)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I understand you're worried, but despite that, I think we should try.
   - I get that it's risky, but despite that, the potential payoff is worth it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - I understand stakeholders have concerns about timeline, but despite those concerns, I recommend proceeding as planned.
   - I acknowledge the budgetary constraints, but despite those limitations, this investment is critical for long-term success.
