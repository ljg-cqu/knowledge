1. **Noun**: collaboration (U/C, pl: collaborations)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The action of working with someone to produce or create something; cooperative effort
   - *(Sense 2)* A piece of work produced by two or more people or groups working together
   
   **Synonyms**: cooperation, partnership, teamwork, joint effort, alliance
   
   **Antonyms**: competition, independence, isolation, rivalry
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts, academic settings, project descriptions; discussing cooperative work arrangements; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing individual work, competitive situations, or when partnership is informal/temporary
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - in collaboration with
   - close collaboration
   - international collaboration
   - form a collaboration
   - successful collaboration
   
   **Root Analysis**: col- (together) + labor (work) + -ation (noun suffix indicating action)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin collaborare (to work together) → French collaboration → English collaboration (1860s)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Our collaboration on the project was smooth—we worked well together.
   - This song is a collaboration between two of my favorite artists.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research represents a collaboration among multiple international institutions.
   - Effective collaboration requires clear communication and shared objectives.

1. **Noun**: synergy (U/C, pl: synergies)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The interaction of two or more agents or forces producing a combined effect greater than the sum of their individual effects
   - Enhanced capability or performance resulting from combined action
   
   **Synonyms**: collaboration, cooperation, interaction, combined effect, teamwork
   
   **Antonyms**: conflict, antagonism, counterproductivity, independence
   
   **When to Use**: Business strategy, scientific contexts, emphasizing enhanced collaborative outcomes; formal/professional registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (technical term), when simple addition rather than multiplication of effects
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - create synergy
   - synergy between [elements]
   - potential synergies
   - synergistic effect
   - operational synergy
   
   **Root Analysis**: syn- (together) + erg (work) + -y (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Greek synergos (working together) → New Latin synergia → English synergy (1650s, popularized in business 1960s)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - There's real synergy between our teams—we accomplish way more together.
   - The synergy is obvious when they collaborate on projects.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The merger creates significant synergies through economies of scale.
   - Research collaborations generate synergies that accelerate innovation.
