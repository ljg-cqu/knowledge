1. **Adverb**: collaboratively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that involves two or more people or groups working together to achieve something
   - Through cooperative effort and shared work (typically mid or end position)
   
   **Synonyms**: cooperatively, jointly, collectively, together, in partnership
   
   **Antonyms**: independently, individually, separately, alone
   
   **When to Use**: Professional contexts describing how work is done, project descriptions, emphasizing teamwork and cooperative methods
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing individual work or competitive situations; avoid when the work was done separately despite shared goals
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - work collaboratively
   - developed collaboratively
   - collaboratively designed
   - approach collaboratively
   - solve problems collaboratively
   
   **Root Analysis**: col- (together) + labor (work) + -ative + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin collaborare → French collaboratif → English collaborative + -ly (1800s)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We worked collaboratively on the whole thing, bouncing ideas back and forth.
   - The project came together because everyone pitched in collaboratively.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The research teams worked collaboratively to develop innovative solutions.
   - Data was collected and analyzed collaboratively across multiple institutions.
