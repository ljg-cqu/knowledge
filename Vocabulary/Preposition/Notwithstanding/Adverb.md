1. **Adverb**: notwithstanding (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Nevertheless; in spite of that; all the same; used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
   - Functions as a sentence adverb modifying the entire clause, typically appearing at the beginning or end of a sentence
   
   **Synonyms**: nevertheless, nonetheless, however, still, even so, all the same, in any case
   
   **Antonyms**: therefore, consequently, accordingly, thus, hence
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing and speech to introduce a contrasting point; academic writing, legal documents, formal presentations; when emphasizing that despite previous information, something else is true; can appear sentence-initially, sentence-finally, or mid-sentence (with commas)
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "anyway," "still," or "but" instead); informal emails; when simpler alternatives would be clearer; avoid overuse as it can sound pretentious; don't use in informal storytelling
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - Notwithstanding, we must proceed
   - We decided to proceed, notwithstanding
   - The plan, notwithstanding, remains viable
   
   **Root Analysis**: not + with + stand + ing = functioning as adverbial form meaning "despite this circumstance"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → evolved from prepositional usage to adverbial function in formal discourse → maintains formal register throughout history
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The car is old and needs repairs. Notwithstanding, it still runs pretty well.
   - I know you're worried, but notwithstanding, we should give it a try.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The economic indicators suggest caution; notwithstanding, the board recommends moving forward with the investment.
   - Several methodological concerns have been raised. Notwithstanding, the study's findings remain robust and significant.
