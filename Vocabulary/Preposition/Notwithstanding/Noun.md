1. **Noun**: notwithstanding (U, rarely used)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The act or instance of not withstanding or opposing; non-resistance; acceptance despite objections
   - *(Archaic/Obsolete)* The state of proceeding without opposition or hindrance
   
   **Synonyms**: acceptance, acquiescence, non-opposition, tolerance, allowance
   
   **Antonyms**: opposition, resistance, objection, hindrance, obstruction
   
   **When to Use**: Extremely rare and archaic; virtually never used in modern English; historical texts or deliberate archaism; linguists discussing the word's evolution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: All contemporary contexts; modern writing of any register; use the prepositional or adverbial forms instead; this nominal usage is essentially obsolete
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - without notwithstanding (archaic)
   - in notwithstanding (obsolete)
   - the notwithstanding of objections (archaic)
   
   **Root Analysis**: not + with + stand + ing = nominalized form (gerund) meaning "the act of not opposing"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → gerund form of "withstand" with negative particle → fell out of use by Early Modern English → replaced by prepositional and adverbial forms
