1. **Preposition**: notwithstanding (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In spite of; despite; without being affected or prevented by
   
   **Synonyms**: despite, in spite of, regardless of, for all, in the face of
   
   **Antonyms**: because of, due to, owing to, thanks to
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when expressing that something happens or is true despite an obstacle or contrary circumstance; legal documents, academic writing, formal correspondence; emphasizes contrast between two facts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (too formal); use "despite" or "even with" instead; avoid in informal emails or everyday speech; don't confuse with "nevertheless" (adverb) or use redundantly with "but"
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: notwithstanding the difficulties, notwithstanding these concerns, notwithstanding the evidence, notwithstanding any provision
   - **Verb + prep**: continue notwithstanding, proceed notwithstanding, apply notwithstanding
   - **Adjective + prep**: valid notwithstanding, effective notwithstanding
   - **Noun + prep**: rights notwithstanding, provisions notwithstanding, objections notwithstanding
   
   **Root Analysis**: not + withstanding (with + stand + ing) = "not standing against" or "not opposing"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → from "not" + "withstanding" (present participle of "withstand") → Originally meant "not opposing or resisting"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're going to the beach tomorrow, notwithstanding the weather forecast.
   - She finished the project on time, notwithstanding all the setbacks.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Notwithstanding the objections raised by the committee, the proposal was approved unanimously.
   - The contract remains valid notwithstanding any subsequent changes to the regulations.
