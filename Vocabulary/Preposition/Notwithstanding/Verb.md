1. **Verb**: withstand (base form from which "notwithstanding" derives)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To resist or endure successfully; to remain undamaged or unaffected by; to stand up against
   - To oppose or resist with determination; to hold out against pressure, force, or attack
   
   **Synonyms**: resist, endure, survive, bear, tolerate, weather, stand up to, hold out against
   
   **Antonyms**: succumb, yield, surrender, give in, collapse, fall, submit
   
   **When to Use**: Describing physical durability, emotional resilience, or resistance to pressure; technical contexts (materials withstanding stress); describing people enduring hardship; formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing voluntary acceptance (use "accept"); when no opposition or force is involved; don't confuse with "understand" or "withhold"
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - withstand the test of time
   - withstand pressure/scrutiny/criticism
   - withstand extreme conditions/temperatures
   - built to withstand earthquakes
   - able to withstand anything
   
   **Root Analysis**: with + stand = "stand against" or "stand in opposition to"
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "wiþstandan" → "with" (against) + "standan" (to stand) → Germanic origin → maintained core meaning throughout history
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This phone case can withstand drops from pretty high up.
   - I don't think I can withstand another boring meeting today.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The infrastructure must withstand seismic activity of magnitude 7.0 or greater.
   - The defendant's testimony did not withstand cross-examination by the prosecution.

2. **Verb**: notwithstand (archaic/obsolete - base form)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Archaic) To fail to oppose or resist; to not stand against
   - (Obsolete) To permit or allow despite potential opposition
   
   **Synonyms**: permit despite, allow nevertheless, not oppose, not resist
   
   **Antonyms**: oppose, resist, withstand, block, prevent
   
   **When to Use**: Not used in modern English; found only in historical texts; linguistic or historical analysis
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Any contemporary context; use "not withstand" (two words) or prepositional/adverbial "notwithstanding" instead
