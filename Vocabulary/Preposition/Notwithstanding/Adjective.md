1. **Adjective**: notwithstanding (rare usage as postpositive adjective)
   **Meaning**: 
   - When used postpositively (after a noun), means "despite what has been stated or the circumstances mentioned"; indicates that something remains true or valid regardless of obstacles
   - *(Legal/Formal)* Used in legal contexts to indicate provisions that remain in effect despite other clauses
   
   **Synonyms**: regardless, nevertheless, nonetheless, still valid, unaffected
   
   **Antonyms**: contingent, dependent, conditional, subject to
   
   **When to Use**: Legal documents and formal contracts where a clause needs to remain effective despite other provisions; highly specialized formal writing; legislative texts; postpositive position after the noun it modifies
   
   **When NOT to Use**: General writing (extremely rare as adjective); casual conversation; modern English prefers prepositional use; avoid when simpler alternatives exist; don't use attributively (before nouns)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - anything herein notwithstanding
   - provisions notwithstanding
   - circumstances notwithstanding
   
   **Root Analysis**: not + with + stand + ing = "not standing in opposition"
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English → Latin "non obstante" (not standing in the way) → adopted into legal English as postpositive adjective in formal documents
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - His age notwithstanding, he's still the best player on the team.
   - The rain notwithstanding, we had a great time at the festival.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Any provisions to the contrary notwithstanding, this clause shall remain in full force and effect.
   - The previous agreements notwithstanding, the parties hereby establish new terms.
