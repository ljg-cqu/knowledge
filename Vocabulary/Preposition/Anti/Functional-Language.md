1. **Functional Expression**: I'm anti [thing] (function: expressing stance/opposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to state that you are opposed to something such as a policy, practice, or trend ("I'm anti war", "I'm anti smoking indoors").
   - *Formality*: informal to neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm against [thing], I don't support [thing], I'm opposed to [thing], I'm not in favour of [thing]
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm pro [thing], I'm in favour of [thing], I support [thing], I'm all for [thing]
   
   **When to Use**: Casual discussions about politics, habits, or preferences where you want a quick label for your stance (anti war, anti clutter, anti meetings on Fridays).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very formal contexts; when the issue is sensitive and requires precise language; when you need to explain reasons instead of just a label.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm very anti [thing]
   - I'm kind of anti [thing]
   - I'm pretty anti [trend/feature]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Convenient but vague; signals opposition without specifying why. Can sound ideological or dismissive if overused.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Why are you anti [thing]?
   - In what sense are you anti [thing]?
   - Are you totally anti it, or just cautious?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm pretty anti meetings after 5 p.m.
   - I'm anti tracking people without clear consent.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - (Prefer: "I am opposed to" / "I do not support" in formal writing.)

1. **Functional Expression**: take a stand against [something] (function: publicly opposing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To clearly and publicly declare your opposition to something you believe is wrong.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: speak out against, stand up against, oppose, resist, take a firm position against
   
   **Antonyms**: stay silent about, go along with, support, back, tolerate
   
   **When to Use**: Ethical, political, or organisational contexts where you want to talk about active resistance (take a stand against racism, harassment, corruption).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For minor preferences; the phrase usually implies moral weight.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - take a strong stand against
   - take a public stand against
   - refuse to take a stand against
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Suggests courage and responsibility; often used in calls to action or praise.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - We need more people willing to take a stand against this.
   - What would it look like to take a stand against it here?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm glad they took a stand against that kind of behaviour.
   - Sometimes you have to take a stand against unfair rules.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organisation must take a clear stand against discriminatory practices.
   - Leaders are expected to take a stand against corruption at every level.

1. **Functional Expression**: speak out against [something] (function: vocal criticism)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To publicly criticise or openly oppose something, especially when silence is the easier option.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: publicly criticise, voice opposition to, condemn, denounce, call out
   
   **Antonyms**: stay silent, keep quiet about, look the other way, endorse
   
   **When to Use**: When emphasising verbal action *against* injustice, abuse, or harmful policies (speak out against bullying, speak out against censorship).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For private feedback; when strong public language may create legal or personal risk without support.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - bravely speak out against
   - speak out strongly against
   - afraid to speak out against
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Often associated with risk and courage; can inspire others but may escalate conflict.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you for speaking out against that.
   - Many people are afraid to speak out against it.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - More students are speaking out against unfair grading.
   - She spoke out against the new policy on social media.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Employees were encouraged to speak out against unethical conduct.
   - Several experts spoke out against the proposed reforms.

1. **Functional Expression**: campaign against [something] (function: organised opposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To organise actions over time to try to stop or change something you oppose.
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: run an anti‑[issue] campaign, lobby against, organise against, mobilise against
   
   **Antonyms**: campaign for, promote, lobby for, push for, advocate
   
   **When to Use**: Describing collective efforts *against* policies, products, or behaviours (campaign against smoking, campaign against data misuse).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For spontaneous complaints without structure or continuity.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - launch a campaign against
   - actively campaign against
   - campaign hard against
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Implies planning, resources, and repetition; stronger than just "protest".
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Who is campaigning against it?
   - How long have they been campaigning against this practice?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They're campaigning against single‑use plastics in the office.
   - Local groups are campaigning against the new law.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Several organisations campaign against discriminatory legislation at the national level.
   - Civil society groups have long campaigned against corruption.

1. **Functional Expression**: warn against [something] (function: advising not to do something)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To advise someone not to do something because it may be harmful or unwise.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (advice wrapped in a warning)
   
   **Synonyms**: advise against, caution against, strongly discourage, recommend avoiding
   
   **Antonyms**: encourage, recommend, advise in favour of, endorse
   
   **When to Use**: Giving protective advice *against* risky actions (warn against investing, warn against anti‑science claims, warn against mixing drugs).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you have no clear reason for your warning; when a neutral description is more appropriate.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - strongly warn against
   - repeatedly warn against
   - officially warn against
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Soften strong "don't do it" with explanation; can sound paternalistic if overused.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Why are you warning against it?
   - Thanks for warning me against that.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'd really warn against clicking those random links.
   - They warned us against believing every anti‑vaccine post.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Health authorities warn against consuming unregulated products.
   - Experts warn against simplistic "anti‑science" narratives.

1. **Functional Expression**: frame [X] as anti‑[Y] (function: characterising position)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To present or describe something as being against a group, value, or principle.
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (about description, not necessarily truth)
   
   **Synonyms**: portray as anti‑, depict as hostile toward, label as anti‑, cast as being against
   
   **Antonyms**: frame as pro‑, portray as supportive of, describe as aligned with
   
   **When to Use**: Analysing media, rhetoric, or branding where something is presented as being anti‑something (framed as anti‑elite, framed as anti‑traditional, framed as anti‑democratic).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you want to state your own view rather than describe how others present it.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - framed as anti‑[group]
   - often framed as anti‑[value]
   - unfairly framed as anti‑[issue]
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: "Frame" highlights perspective and interpretation, useful for critical analysis.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Who is framing it as anti‑[Y]?
   - Is it really anti‑[Y], or just being framed that way?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The campaign got framed as anti‑science, even though that wasn't the goal.
   - Some people frame any criticism as anti‑their side.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy was widely framed as anti‑worker despite its stated objectives.
   - Media narratives often frame youth activism as anti‑establishment.
