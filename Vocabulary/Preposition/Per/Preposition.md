1. **Preposition**: per (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* For each unit of time (per day, per hour, per year)
   - *(Abstract)* For each unit; by means of; according to; in accordance with
   
   **Synonyms**: for each (in rate contexts), by means of (in method contexts), according to (in compliance contexts), a, every
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, combined, overall
   
   **When to Use**: Rates, measurements, means of communication, formal instructions, ratios, and specifications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts where "each" or "every" sounds more natural; avoid overuse in informal writing
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: per person, per unit, per capita, per annum, per day, per hour
   - **Verb + prep**: calculate per, charge per, measure per, allocate per
   - **Adjective + prep**: (rare—per is not typically used with adjectives)
   - **Noun + prep**: rate per, cost per, price per, fee per, allocation per
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (through, by means of)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per → English per (16th century), meaning "for each" or "by means of"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It costs $20 per person for the buffet.
   - We're driving 60 miles per hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The contract stipulates payment of $5,000 per month per consultant.
   - Per our discussion, the allocation is $1,000 per capita per annum.

1. **Preposition**: as per (type: compound/phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not typically used
   - *(Abstract)* In accordance with; according to; following the specifications of
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, following, pursuant to, as specified by
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal business writing, legal contexts, compliance statements; emphasizes adherence to rules or agreements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (sounds overly formal); academic writing (prefer "according to"); when not referencing specific documents or agreements
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: as per agreement, as per policy, as per instructions, as per usual, as per requirements
   - **Verb + prep**: comply as per, proceed as per, act as per, operate as per
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: allocation as per, payment as per, procedure as per
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in the manner of) + per (according to)
   
   **Etymology**: English as + Latin per → as per (19th century), business English compound
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll do it as per your request.
   - As per usual, he's late again. (slightly sarcastic)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Payment will be processed as per the terms outlined in Section 3.2.
   - Resources are allocated as per institutional guidelines at $500 per capita.

1. **Preposition**: per capita (type: Latin phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* For each person; by or for each individual in a population; per head
   
   **Synonyms**: per person, per head, per individual, apiece, for each person
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, collective, overall, combined
   
   **When to Use**: Economic, statistical, demographic, or formal contexts when expressing per-person measurements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per person" instead); when discussing totals rather than per-person figures
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: per capita income, per capita consumption, GDP per capita, per capita basis, per capita expenditure
   - **Verb + prep**: calculate per capita, measure per capita, allocate per capita, distribute per capita
   - **Adjective + prep**: (typically follows noun: "income per capita" not "per capita income" as adjective)
   - **Noun + prep**: income per capita, consumption per capita, production per capita, spending per capita
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (for each) + capita (heads, from caput = head)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per capita (by heads) → English economic term (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The income per capita here is pretty high.
   - We calculated the cost per capita for the trip.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Healthcare expenditure per capita increased from $8,500 to $9,200 per annum.
   - Energy consumption per capita averaged 500 kWh per month per household.

1. **Preposition**: per annum (type: Latin phrasal preposition)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* For each year; annually; yearly; every year
   - *(Abstract)* Used to express annual rates, especially financial rates
   
   **Synonyms**: per year, annually, yearly, each year, every year, p.a.
   
   **Antonyms**: per month, per quarter, per day, per week
   
   **When to Use**: Formal financial, legal, or business contexts; interest rates, salaries, fees, statistical annual rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per year" or "yearly"); when discussing non-annual time periods
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: percentage per annum, salary per annum, growth per annum, interest per annum, rate per annum
   - **Verb + prep**: earn per annum, grow per annum, increase per annum, compound per annum
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not typically used)
   - **Noun + prep**: interest per annum, income per annum, salary per annum, revenue per annum
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (for each) + annum (year, from annus = year)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per annum (by the year) → English financial term (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She makes $75,000 per annum.
   - The account earns 3% per annum.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The bond yields 4.25% per annum compounded quarterly.
   - Salary increments average 3.5% per annum per employee grade.

1. **Preposition**: per se (type: Latin phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* By or in itself; intrinsically; considered independently
   
   **Synonyms**: in itself, intrinsically, as such, in essence, inherently
   
   **Antonyms**: contextually, extrinsically, in relation to, comparatively
   
   **When to Use**: Formal or philosophical contexts when distinguishing inherent qualities from contextual ones
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; when simpler expressions like "itself" work; often misused—ensure you mean "intrinsically"
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: not X per se, problem per se, issue per se, illegal per se
   - **Verb + prep**: (not typically combined)
   - **Adjective + prep**: not bad per se, not illegal per se, not wrong per se
   - **Noun + prep**: the concept per se, the policy per se, technology per se
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (through) + se (itself)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per se (by itself) → English (16th century), philosophical term
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It's not wrong per se, just unusual.
   - I don't dislike him per se, just his attitude.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The technology per se is not problematic; implementation raises concerns.
   - The policy per se does not violate regulations, but its application may.

1. **Preposition**: as per usual (type: idiomatic compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* As normally happens; as is customary
   - *(Abstract)* According to the usual pattern; in the typical manner
   
   **Synonyms**: as usual, as always, typically, normally, as is customary
   
   **Antonyms**: unusually, exceptionally, atypically, surprisingly
   
   **When to Use**: Informal contexts, often with slight sarcasm or resignation; describing predictable patterns
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal writing (use "as usual" instead); positive contexts (can sound negative)
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: as per usual, he/she/it/they [verb] as per usual
   - **Verb + prep**: arrive as per usual, behave as per usual, proceed as per usual
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not typically used)
   - **Noun + prep**: (not typically used as modifier)
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in the manner of) + per (according to) + usual (customary)
   
   **Etymology**: Modern English compound (20th century), informal business/casual expression
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As per usual, the meeting started late.
   - He forgot his keys, as per usual.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *Avoid in formal contexts; use "as usual" or "typically" instead*
   - The committee convened at 10:00 AM, as is customary. (formal alternative)

1. **Preposition**: per stirpes (type: Latin legal phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* Legal term: by branch or stock; inheritance distributed by family branches rather than per capita
   
   **Synonyms**: by branch, by family line, by representation
   
   **Antonyms**: per capita (in legal inheritance contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: Legal contexts only, specifically wills, trusts, and inheritance distribution
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Any non-legal context; use only when discussing inheritance law
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: distributed per stirpes, inheritance per stirpes, division per stirpes
   - **Verb + prep**: distribute per stirpes, divide per stirpes, allocate per stirpes
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: distribution per stirpes, allocation per stirpes, inheritance per stirpes
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (by) + stirpes (roots, stocks, family branches, plural of stirps)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per stirpes (by roots/branches) → English legal term (17th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Not used in casual contexts*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The estate shall be distributed per stirpes among the deceased's descendants.
   - Under per stirpes distribution, each branch of the family receives an equal share.

1. **Preposition**: per pro / pp (type: Latin abbreviation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* On behalf of; by proxy; used when signing documents for someone else
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, for and on behalf of, by proxy, representing
   
   **Antonyms**: personally, directly, in person
   
   **When to Use**: Business correspondence, formal letters, contract signing when acting as authorized representative
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts; when signing for yourself; informal emails
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: pp [name], per pro [name], signed pp
   - **Verb + prep**: sign per pro, act per pro, authorize per pro
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: signature per pro, authorization per pro
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (by) + pro (on behalf of, from procurationem = agency)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per procurationem (by agency) → pp abbreviation (16th century), business term
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Not typically used in casual contexts*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Signed: pp John Smith, Chief Executive Officer
   - The document was executed per pro by the authorized signatory.

1. **Preposition**: per cent / percent (type: compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Spatial)* Not applicable
   - *(Temporal)* Not applicable
   - *(Abstract)* Out of each hundred; for every hundred units; rate per hundred
   
   **Synonyms**: %, percentage, per hundred, out of 100
   
   **Antonyms**: absolute number, total, whole
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, statistical, financial contexts expressing rates per hundred
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When absolute numbers are more appropriate than ratios
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: 5 per cent, 10 percent, X per cent of Y
   - **Verb + prep**: increase by X percent, decrease by Y percent, grow X percent per year
   - **Adjective + prep**: (not applicable)
   - **Noun + prep**: rate per cent, percentage per cent (redundant), growth per cent
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (for each) + cent (hundred, from centum)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per centum (by the hundred) → percent (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The discount is 20 percent per item.
   - Interest is 3 percent per year.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The population increased by 2.5 percent per annum per region.
   - Inflation averaged 3.2 percent per year over the period.
