1. **Verb**: calculate (transitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To determine the amount or number of something mathematically; to compute per-unit values
   - *(Transitive)* Often used when determining rates per unit, person, or period
   
   **Synonyms**: compute, figure, determine, work out, reckon
   
   **Antonyms**: estimate, guess, approximate
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, business, or technical contexts when determining per-unit rates; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When estimation or approximation is the method used
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - calculate per capita
   - calculate the rate per hour
   - calculate cost per unit
   - calculate per annum
   - calculate per person
   
   **Root Analysis**: calcul (stone, counter) + -ate (verb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin calculare (to count with pebbles) → calculate (16th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let me calculate the cost per person for dinner.
   - Can you calculate how much per hour that is?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Researchers calculated consumption rates at 2.5 liters per capita per day.
   - Costs were calculated at $450 per unit per quarter.

1. **Verb**: charge (transitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To demand payment of a specified price; to set a price per unit or service
   - *(Transitive)* Used extensively when setting per-unit, per-person, or per-service fees
   
   **Synonyms**: bill, invoice, ask, demand, levy
   
   **Antonyms**: pay, refund, reimburse, waive
   
   **When to Use**: Business and commercial contexts when setting per-unit prices; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When discussing payments made rather than prices demanded
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - charge per hour
   - charge per person
   - charge per unit
   - charge per service
   - charge per night
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old French charger (to load)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French charger → charge (13th century), extended to "demand payment"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We charge $50 per hour for tutoring.
   - They charge $20 per person for entry.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The facility charges $150 per consultation per licensed practitioner.
   - Vendors charge $25 per unit per wholesale transaction.

1. **Verb**: allocate (transitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose; to assign amounts per recipient
   - *(Transitive)* Used when distributing per-person, per-unit, or per-period amounts
   
   **Synonyms**: distribute, assign, allot, apportion, designate
   
   **Antonyms**: withhold, retain, centralize, hoard
   
   **When to Use**: Business, government, or resource management contexts for per-unit distributions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When resources are centralized rather than distributed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - allocate per capita
   - allocate per department
   - allocate per person
   - allocate resources per unit
   - allocate budget per project
   
   **Root Analysis**: al- (to) + loc (place) + -ate (verb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin allocare → allocate (17th century), meaning "assign to a place"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Let's allocate $100 per person for the trip.
   - They allocated 5 hours per task.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee allocated $50,000 per initiative per fiscal year.
   - Resources were allocated at 200 units per facility per month.

1. **Verb**: distribute (transitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To give shares of something; to spread or supply per recipient or location
   - *(Transitive)* Used when giving per-person or per-unit shares
   
   **Synonyms**: dispense, allocate, divide, share out, apportion
   
   **Antonyms**: collect, gather, centralize, accumulate, hoard
   
   **When to Use**: Logistics, business, or resource contexts when spreading per-unit amounts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When gathering or centralizing rather than spreading
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - distribute per capita
   - distribute per household
   - distribute evenly per person
   - distribute per location
   - distribute per unit
   
   **Root Analysis**: dis- (apart) + tribute (assign) + -e
   
   **Etymology**: Latin distribuere → distribute (15th century), meaning "divide and assign"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Distribute the supplies—one kit per person.
   - We'll distribute $50 per child.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Vaccines were distributed at 500 doses per clinic per week.
   - Funds are distributed proportionally at $1,000 per capita per region.

1. **Verb**: assess (transitive)
   **Meaning**:

   - *(Transitive)* Used in taxation and evaluation contexts for per-unit assessments
   
   **Synonyms**: evaluate, appraise, determine, calculate, estimate
   
   **Antonyms**: ignore, overlook, neglect
   
   **When to Use**: Taxation, evaluation, or formal contexts when determining per-unit values
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts where simpler verbs suffice
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - assess per capita
   - assess tax per property
   - assess fees per unit
   - assess per annum
   - assess per transaction
   
   **Root Analysis**: as- (to) + sess (sit, settle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin assidere → assess (15th century), meaning "to sit beside to judge value"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They assess property tax per year based on value.
   - Let's assess the cost per person first.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Taxes are assessed at $2,500 per property per annum.
   - Penalties are assessed at $100 per violation per occurrence.

1. **Verb**: average (transitive/intransitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To amount to or achieve as an average; to calculate the mean per unit or period
   - *(Transitive/Intransitive)* Used when calculating or expressing per-unit means
   
   **Synonyms**: mean, typically amount to, usually total, generally reach
   
   **Antonyms**: vary, fluctuate, deviate, differ
   
   **When to Use**: Statistical or summary contexts when expressing per-unit means
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When exact figures rather than averages are needed
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - average per capita
   - average per person
   - average per hour
   - average per unit
   - average per year
   
   **Root Analysis**: Arabic awar (damage) → Italian avaria → average (maritime insurance term)
   
   **Etymology**: French avarie → average (15th century), evolved to mean "arithmetic mean"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We average $200 per person for groceries.
   - Sales average 50 units per day.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Household income averaged $75,000 per capita per annum.
   - Production averaged 120 units per hour per facility.

1. **Verb**: consume (transitive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - To use up a resource; to eat or drink; to use per unit of time or per person
   - *(Transitive)* Used in resource, energy, or consumption contexts for per-unit usage
   
   **Synonyms**: use, utilize, expend, use up, go through
   
   **Antonyms**: conserve, save, preserve, produce, generate
   
   **When to Use**: Environmental, economic, or resource contexts for per-unit consumption rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When emphasizing conservation or production
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - consume per capita
   - consume per household
   - consume per day
   - consume per unit
   - consume per person
   
   **Root Analysis**: con- (completely) + sume (take up)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin consumere → consume (14th century), meaning "use up entirely"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We consume about 3 liters per person per day.
   - This car consumes 10 liters per 100 km.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Households consume an average of 900 kWh per month per residence.
   - The facility consumes 500 gallons per hour per production cycle.

   - produce per unit time
