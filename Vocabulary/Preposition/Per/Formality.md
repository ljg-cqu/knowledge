1. **Original Expression**: The cost is $50 per person
   **Context**: Discussing prices in service, hospitality, or event contexts
   
   **Core Intent**: Communicate the individual cost for each participant or customer
   
   **Casual**: It's $50 a head / It's $50 each / $50 per person
   *Characteristics*: Simple vocabulary, "a head" colloquial, contracted forms
   *Use when*: Friends, informal planning, casual business settings
   
   **Semi-Formal**: The cost is $50 per person / The price is $50 per individual
   *Characteristics*: Clear "per person" phrasing, complete sentences, professional but accessible
   *Use when*: General business communication, emails to clients, standard quotes
   
   **Formal**: The fee is $50 per capita / Individual charges amount to $50 per person
   *Characteristics*: "Per capita" for very formal contexts, precise terminology, fuller sentence structures
   *Use when*: Official documentation, contracts, formal proposals, academic writing
   
   **Humorous**: That'll be 50 bucks a pop / $50 per warm body
   *Characteristics*: Colloquial "a pop," playful "warm body" depersonalization
   *Use when*: Casual settings with friends, lighthearted business environments
   *Avoid when*: Formal contexts, first meetings, serious negotiations
   
   **Lively**: Just $50 each—what a deal! / Only $50 per person!
   *Characteristics*: Enthusiasm markers ("just," "only"), exclamations, positive framing
   *Use when*: Marketing, sales pitches, exciting announcements
   
   **Empathetic**: It works out to $50 per person, so everyone contributes equally
   *Characteristics*: Explanation of fairness, reassurance, consideration of concerns
   *Use when*: Group planning where cost sensitivity is a concern, budget discussions
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Higher formality for business clients; casual with friends
   - *Social Distance*: Formal with strangers/new clients; casual with established relationships
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Higher amounts or formal contexts require formal register
   - *Cultural Context*: Some cultures expect more formal pricing communication
   - *Medium*: Written quotes/contracts more formal; verbal discussions more flexible
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using "per capita" in casual conversation—sounds pretentious and overly academic
   - Using "a head" in formal contracts—too colloquial for legal documents
   - Mixing registers: "The fee per capita is $50 a pop"—inconsistent formality
   
   **Cultural Notes**: In some cultures, discussing individual costs is less common than presenting group totals; adjust phrasing accordingly
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Restaurant reservation for friends → **Casual**: "It's $50 a head for the tasting menu"
   - **Situation 2**: Corporate event planning email → **Semi-Formal**: "The cost is $50 per person for the conference"
   - **Situation 3**: Legal contract for services → **Formal**: "Fees are assessed at $50 per capita per event"
