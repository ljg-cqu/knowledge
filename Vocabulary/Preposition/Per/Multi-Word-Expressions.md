1. **Expression**: per capita (type: Latin phrase/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For each person; by or for each individual in a population; calculated on a per-person basis
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed Latin phrase; always "per capita," never variations like "per capitas")
   
   **Synonyms**: per person, per head, per individual, for each person, apiece
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, overall, combined, collective
   
   **When to Use**: Economic, statistical, demographic, or formal academic contexts when expressing per-person measurements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per person"); when discussing non-population-based measurements; totals rather than per-person figures
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - GDP per capita
   - income per capita
   - consumption per capita
   - on a per capita basis
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase meaning "by heads"; originated in Roman census-taking and legal contexts; entered English economic terminology in 17th century as precise demographic measurement term
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (for each) + capita (heads, plural of caput = head)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The income per capita here is pretty high.
   - That works out to $50 per capita.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Healthcare expenditure per capita increased from $8,500 to $9,200 per annum in the reporting period.
   - Energy consumption per capita averaged 500 kWh per month per household across all regions.

1. **Expression**: per annum (type: Latin phrase/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For each year; annually; yearly; every year; on a yearly basis
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed Latin phrase; often abbreviated as "p.a.")
   
   **Synonyms**: per year, annually, yearly, each year, every year, p.a.
   
   **Antonyms**: per month, per day, per quarter, one-time, lump sum
   
   **When to Use**: Formal financial, legal, or business contexts; interest rates, salaries, annual fees, growth rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per year" or "yearly"); informal contexts where Latin phrases sound pretentious
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - interest per annum
   - salary per annum
   - growth per annum
   - percentage per annum
   - compounded per annum
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase meaning "by the year"; standard financial terminology since 16th century; universally recognized in banking, investment, and legal documents
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (for each) + annum (year, accusative of annus)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She makes $75,000 per annum at her job.
   - The account earns 3% per annum interest.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The bond yields 4.25% per annum, compounded quarterly at a rate of 1.0625% per quarter.
   - Revenue growth averaged 7.3% per annum over the five-year period, representing $2M per annum per region.

1. **Expression**: per diem (type: Latin phrase/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - For each day; by the day; daily allowance for expenses, especially in business travel
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed Latin phrase; functions as both adverb and noun)
   
   **Synonyms**: per day, daily, daily allowance, day rate, daily rate
   
   **Antonyms**: per week, per month, per annum, lump sum, monthly stipend
   
   **When to Use**: Business travel, employment, expense reimbursement contexts; daily allowances, daily rates, temporary work
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per day"); permanent salary discussions (use "salary" not "per diem")
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - per diem allowance
   - per diem rate
   - receive per diem
   - per diem payment
   - per diem basis
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase meaning "by the day"; became standard business English term in 18th century for daily expense allowances; widely recognized in corporate travel and contracting
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (for each) + diem (day, accusative of dies)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You'll get a per diem of $100 for meals while traveling.

   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Employees receive a per diem of $150 per day per company travel policy, calculated at $50 per meal per employee.

1. **Expression**: as per (type: compound preposition/fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In accordance with; according to; following the specifications or requirements of
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed compound; always "as per," never "like per" or variations)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, following, pursuant to, in line with
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Formal business writing when referencing agreements, policies, instructions, or previous discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Academic writing (prefer "according to"); casual conversation (sounds bureaucratic); some style guides discourage it as redundant
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as per agreement
   - as per policy
   - as per instructions
   - as per usual (idiomatic, slightly sarcastic)
   - as per requirements
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Emerged in 19th-century business English combining "as" (in the manner of) with Latin "per" (according to); conventional in business correspondence despite stylistic criticism
   
   **Root Analysis**: English as (in the manner of) + Latin per (according to, by means of)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll send the files as per our chat yesterday.
   - As per usual, the meeting started late. (idiomatic, often sarcastic)
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Payment shall be remitted as per Section 4.2 of the agreement at $500 per unit per quarter.
   - As per the policy, expenses are reimbursed at $75 per diem per employee per business day.

1. **Expression**: per se (type: Latin phrase/fixed expression)
   **Meaning**: 
   - By or in itself; intrinsically; considered independently of context or relationships
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed Latin phrase; always together, never separated)
   
   **Synonyms**: in itself, intrinsically, as such, inherently, by itself, in essence
   
   **Antonyms**: contextually, in relation to, comparatively, extrinsically, relatively
   
   **When to Use**: Formal or philosophical contexts when distinguishing inherent qualities from contextual or relational ones
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "itself" or simpler terms); when simpler expressions work; frequently misused—verify you mean "intrinsically"
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - not X per se
   - the concept per se
   - illegal per se (legal term)
   - problem per se
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase meaning "through itself"; philosophical term dating to medieval Latin; distinguishes essential from accidental properties; entered English legal and philosophical usage in 16th century
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (through, by) + se (itself, reflexive pronoun)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It's not wrong per se, just unusual.
   - I don't dislike technology per se, just how some people use it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The technology per se is not problematic; however, its implementation raises significant ethical concerns.
   - While the policy per se complies with regulations at $500 per capita per annum, its application may violate equity principles.

1. **Expression**: per stirpes (type: Latin legal phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - By branch or stock; method of inheritance distribution by family branches rather than individuals
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed legal terminology; never altered)
   
   **Synonyms**: by branch, by family line, by representation (legal contexts)
   
   **Antonyms**: per capita (in legal inheritance distribution contexts)
   
   **When to Use**: Legal contexts only, specifically wills, trusts, and estate planning documentation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Any non-legal context; general conversation about inheritance (use descriptive language instead)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - distributed per stirpes
   - inheritance per stirpes
   - division per stirpes
   - (no casual variations—technical legal term only)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin legal phrase meaning "by roots/stocks"; distinguishes branch-based inheritance (children inherit parent's share if parent predeceased) from per capita (equal division among all heirs); Roman law origin, entered English common law 17th century
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (by) + stirpes (roots, stocks, branches; plural of stirps)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Not used in casual contexts—strictly legal terminology*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The estate shall be distributed per stirpes among the deceased's descendants, with each family branch receiving equal shares.
   - Under per stirpes distribution at $1M per branch, three branches each receive $333,333 regardless of the number of individuals per branch.

1. **Expression**: per pro / pp (type: Latin abbreviation/business formula)
   **Meaning**: 
   - On behalf of; by proxy; used when signing documents for someone else as their authorized representative
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed business formula; abbreviated consistently as "pp" or "p.p.")
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, for and on behalf of, by proxy, as agent for
   
   **Antonyms**: personally, in person, directly
   
   **When to Use**: Business correspondence, contracts, formal letters when signing as authorized representative
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts; when signing for yourself; informal emails where full explanation is better
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pp [name]
   - per pro [name]
   - p.p. [name]
   - signed per pro
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Latin phrase per procurationem (by agency/proxy); business convention since 16th century; indicates signatory has legal authority to act for named principal
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (by) + pro (on behalf of, short for procurationem = agency, power of attorney)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - *Not typically used in casual contexts*
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Signed: pp John Smith, Chief Executive Officer [signed by authorized representative]
   - The contract was executed per pro by the authorized signatory at $10,000 per month per the agreement.

1. **Expression**: per cent / percent (type: compound term/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Out of each hundred; for every hundred units; rate expressed per 100
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed mathematical term; "per cent" in British English, "percent" in American English)
   
   **Synonyms**: %, percentage, per hundred, out of 100, hundredths
   
   **Antonyms**: absolute number, total, whole number, actual amount
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, statistical, financial contexts expressing ratios or rates per hundred
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When absolute numbers are more meaningful; when audiences unfamiliar with percentage calculations
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - X per cent
   - percent increase
   - percentage per annum
   - percent per capita
   - basis points (financial: 1/100 of 1 percent)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Latin per centum (by the hundred); entered English mathematics and commerce in 16th century; universally recognized measurement convention
   
   **Root Analysis**: Latin per (for each) + centum (hundred)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The discount is 20 percent per item today.
   - Interest is 3 percent per year on savings.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The population increased by 2.5 percent per annum per region, or 250,000 persons per year per capita growth.
   - Inflation averaged 3.2 percent per year, with costs rising $32 per $1,000 per annum.

1. **Expression**: miles per hour (mph) / kilometers per hour (km/h) (type: measurement collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Speed measurement expressing distance traveled per unit of time (one hour)
   - *Fixedness*: strong (standard measurement unit; always in this order)
   
   **Synonyms**: speed of X, velocity of X, traveling at X
   
   **Antonyms**: stationary, at rest, not moving
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing speed, velocity, travel rates in transportation contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Scientific contexts preferring meters per second; when other time units are more appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - X miles per hour
   - mph (standard abbreviation)
   - km/h or kph (metric)
   - speed limit of X per hour
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Standard measurement convention combining distance unit with time unit; natural semantic partnership expressing speed; universally recognized in transportation
   
   **Root Analysis**: English miles (distance unit) + per (for each) + hour (time unit)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're going 60 miles per hour on the highway.
   - The speed limit is 30 mph per the signs.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Average velocity was 55 miles per hour per vehicle per the traffic monitoring system.
   - Wind speeds reached 120 kilometers per hour per meteorological measurements.

   - per unit cost
   - cost per unit
   - per unit basis

   - calculated per unit
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: Natural semantic partnership in economic analysis; "per unit" specifies the denominator for cost calculations; conventional in accounting and manufacturing

1. **Expression**: as per usual (type: idiomatic expression/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As normally happens; as is customary; in the typical manner (often with slight sarcasm or resignation)
   - *Fixedness*: strong (fixed idiomatic phrase; informal alternative to "as usual")
   
   **Synonyms**: as usual, as always, typically, as is customary, like always
   
   **Antonyms**: unusually, surprisingly, unexpectedly, atypically, for once
   
   **When to Use**: Informal contexts describing predictable patterns; often carries slight sarcasm or resignation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal writing (use "as usual" or "typically"); positive contexts (can sound negative); academic writing
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - as per usual, he/she/they...
   - as per usual (no alternatives—fixed phrase)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Modern informal English combining business formula "as per" with "usual"; emerged 20th century; adds formality irony to casual contexts; often used sarcastically about predictable negative behaviors
   
   **Root Analysis**: English as (in the manner of) + Latin per (according to) + English usual (customary)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As per usual, the meeting started 15 minutes late.
   - He forgot his wallet, as per usual.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - *Avoid in formal contexts; use "as usual" or "typically" instead*
   - The committee convened at 10:00 AM, as is customary. (formal alternative)
