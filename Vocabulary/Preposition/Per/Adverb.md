1. **Adverb**: individually (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - One at a time; separately rather than as a group; on a per-person basis
   - *(Manner adverb)* Mid or end position; describes per-individual distribution or calculation
   
   **Synonyms**: separately, singly, one by one, per person, independently
   
   **Antonyms**: collectively, together, jointly, as a group, en masse
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying per-person rates or individual treatment; formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When emphasizing group actions or collective rates
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - calculated individually
   - charged individually per person
   - measured individually
   - individually assessed per capita
   - priced individually
   
   **Root Analysis**: individual + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin individualis + -ly → individually (17th century), meaning "in a separate manner"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're paying individually—$20 per person.
   - Each item is sold individually at $5 per piece.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants were compensated individually at $50 per session per person.
   - Costs were calculated individually per household per annum.

1. **Adverb**: respectively (type: sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In the order given; separately in the sequence mentioned; used for per-item correspondence
   - *(Sentence adverb)* End position; relates multiple per-unit values to multiple items in order
   
   **Synonyms**: in order, separately, individually, severally, correspondingly
   
   **Antonyms**: collectively, jointly, together, commonly, uniformly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing when listing different per-unit values for different items
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual speech; when all items have identical per-unit values
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - $50 and $75 per hour, respectively
   - at rates of X and Y per unit, respectively
   - respectively priced per item
   - respectively allocated per capita
   - measured at X and Y per minute, respectively
   
   **Root Analysis**: respective + -ly (in the manner of)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin respective + -ly → respectively (16th century), meaning "with respect to each"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Tickets cost $20 and $30 per person, respectively.
   - They earn $15 and $18 per hour, respectively.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Allocations were $2,500 and $3,000 per capita for regions A and B, respectively.
   - Production rates were 50 and 75 units per hour, respectively.
