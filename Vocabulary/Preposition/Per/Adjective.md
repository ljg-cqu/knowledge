1. **Adjective**: individual (comparative: more individual, superlative: most individual)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Relating to or characteristic of a single person or thing; separate and distinct from others
   - *(Gradable adjective, both attributive and predicative)* Used when describing per-person or per-unit allocation
   
   **Synonyms**: separate, single, distinct, personal, respective
   
   **Antonyms**: collective, combined, group, aggregate, total
   
   **When to Use**: When specifying rates, allocations, or measurements per person/unit; both formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to collective or group totals rather than per-unit breakdowns
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - individual basis
   - individual rate
   - individual cost per person
   - individual allocation
   - on an individual level
   
   **Root Analysis**: in- (not) + divid (divide) + -ual (relating to)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin individualis → individual (15th century), meaning "single, indivisible"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The individual cost per ticket is $50.
   - Each person gets an individual serving.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Individual consumption rates were calculated per capita for the study.
   - The individual fee per participant is specified in the contract.

1. **Adjective**: daily (comparative: more daily, superlative: most daily)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Happening, done, or produced every day; relating to each day
   - *(Non-gradable when used in "per day" contexts)* Describes frequency or rate measured per day
   
   **Synonyms**: everyday, quotidian, diurnal, per diem, day-to-day
   
   **Antonyms**: weekly, monthly, yearly, occasional, sporadic
   
   **When to Use**: When describing rates, routines, or measurements per day; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For longer time periods (use "weekly," "monthly," "yearly" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - daily rate per person
   - daily allowance
   - daily intake per individual
   - daily limit
   - on a daily basis
   
   **Root Analysis**: day + -ly (characteristic of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English dæglic → daily (9th century), meaning "of or belonging to each day"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The daily cost per room is about $100.
   - I need my daily dose of coffee.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The daily consumption rate was measured at 2,000 calories per person.
   - Daily emissions per capita have increased significantly.

1. **Adjective**: respective (no comparative/superlative)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Belonging or relating separately to each of two or more people or things previously mentioned
   - *(Non-gradable, attributive only)* Used when assigning different rates or values per individual item
   
   **Synonyms**: individual, separate, particular, corresponding, own
   
   **Antonyms**: collective, common, shared, joint, combined
   
   **When to Use**: Formal writing when specifying different per-unit values for different items; academic and technical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; when values are identical for all items
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respective rates per unit
   - at their respective costs
   - respective fees per service
   - in their respective categories
   - respective allocations
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (back) + spect (look) + -ive (tending to)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin respectivus → respective (15th century), meaning "having regard to individual cases"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The hotels cost $100 and $150 per night, respectively.
   - They earn different amounts per hour in their respective jobs.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Samples were analyzed at respective rates of 50 and 75 per minute.
   - The respective costs per capita were $2,500 and $3,200 for regions A and B.

1. **Adjective**: proportional (comparative: more proportional, superlative: most proportional)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Corresponding in size or amount to something else; maintaining a constant ratio
   - *(Gradable)* Describes relationships where one quantity changes per unit change in another
   
   **Synonyms**: proportionate, commensurate, corresponding, relative, balanced
   
   **Antonyms**: disproportional, unequal, imbalanced, arbitrary, unrelated
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, scientific, or formal contexts describing per-unit relationships; technical writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; when relationships are not mathematically proportional
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - proportional to per capita income
   - proportional rate per unit
   - directly proportional
   - inversely proportional per
   - proportional distribution
   
   **Root Analysis**: pro- (forward) + port (carry) + -ion + -al (relating to)
   
   **Etymology**: Late Latin proportionalis → proportional (14th century), meaning "having proper proportion"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your share is proportional to your hours worked per week.
   - The cost per person is proportional to group size.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Tax rates are proportional to income earned per annum.
   - Growth rates were proportional to investment per capita.

1. **Adjective**: per-unit (no comparative/superlative)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Calculated or measured for each single item or individual unit
   - *(Non-gradable, attributive)* Technical term for rates measured per individual unit
   
   **Synonyms**: unit-based, individual, per-item, single-unit, unitary
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, combined, bulk, overall
   
   **When to Use**: Technical, business, or economic contexts when specifying rates per individual unit; formal writing
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; general contexts where "per item" is sufficient
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - per-unit cost
   - per-unit price
   - per-unit analysis
   - per-unit basis
   - per-unit contribution
   
   **Root Analysis**: per (for each) + unit (single quantity)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per + unit → per-unit (20th century), technical economic term
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The per-unit price drops if you buy in bulk.
   - Calculate the per-unit cost and compare.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Per-unit manufacturing costs decreased by 12% per quarter.
   - The per-unit contribution margin was analyzed per product line.
