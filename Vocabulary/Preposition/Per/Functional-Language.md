1. **Functional Expression**: As per [our/your/the] [agreement/discussion/policy/etc.] (function: referencing)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to reference and comply with previously established agreements, policies, or discussions
   - *Formality*: formal/neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (softens by referencing external authority)
   
   **Synonyms**: according to, in accordance with, following, in line with, per
   
   **Antonyms**: contrary to, despite, regardless of, notwithstanding
   
   **When to Use**: Business emails, formal reminders, compliance contexts when referencing specific agreements or policies
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (sounds bureaucratic); academic writing (prefer "according to"); when no specific reference exists
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - As per our previous discussion
   - As per the policy
   - As per your request
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Soften directives by attributing them to external authority; reduces personal confrontation; common in business English but considered wordy by some style guides
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Acknowledged / Understood
   - Thank you for the reminder / clarification
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - As per our chat yesterday, I'll send the files.
   - As per usual, he's running late!
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - As per our contractual agreement, payment is due within 30 days.
   - As per company policy, all expenses must be submitted per the attached guidelines.

1. **Functional Expression**: Could you please [verb] per [unit/time period]? (function: requesting with specification)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Polite request that includes specific per-unit or per-period specifications
   - *Formality*: neutral/semi-formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (polite request form)
   
   **Synonyms**: Would you mind [verb]-ing per...?, Can you please [verb] per...?, I'd appreciate if you could [verb] per...
   
   **Antonyms**: [Verb] per... (direct command), You must [verb] per...
   
   **When to Use**: Professional requests when specifying rates, frequencies, or per-unit requirements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal contexts (use "I request"); very casual contexts (use simpler forms); with superiors (may need more deference)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Could you calculate this per capita?
   - Would you mind breaking this down per unit?
   - Can you please submit reports per the schedule?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Combines negative politeness (minimizing imposition) with precision; "per" specifications reduce ambiguity
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Certainly, I'll [verb] per [specification]
   - Yes, no problem / Of course
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Could you figure out the cost per person for me?
   - Can you break this down per month?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you please provide the analysis per capita per the methodology outlined in Section 2?
   - Would you please submit invoices per the billing schedule per our agreement?

1. **Functional Expression**: The rate/cost/fee is [amount] per [unit] (function: stating rates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Declarative statement specifying per-unit rates, costs, or measurements
   - *Formality*: neutral (adaptable to context)
   - *Directness*: direct (factual statement)
   
   **Synonyms**: It costs [amount] per [unit], The price is [amount] per [unit], We charge [amount] per [unit]
   
   **Antonyms**: The total is [amount] (aggregate vs. per-unit)
   
   **When to Use**: Stating prices, rates, or measurements in business, service, or technical contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When totals are more appropriate than per-unit breakdowns
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - The hourly rate is $50
   - The cost per person is $25
   - The price per unit is $10
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Clear and unambiguous; establishes expectations; neutral tone avoids negotiation implications
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That sounds reasonable / That works
   - Could you offer a better rate?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The cost is $30 per person for the dinner.
   - It's $20 per hour for parking.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The consultation fee is $200 per hour per consultant.
   - The rate is established at $5,000 per annum per employee.

1. **Functional Expression**: We/I calculate/measure/assess this per [unit/period] (function: explaining methodology)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Explains the unit or period basis for calculations or measurements
   - *Formality*: neutral/semi-formal
   - *Directness*: direct (explanatory)
   
   **Synonyms**: We work it out per [unit], This is measured per [unit], We figure this per [unit]
   
   **Antonyms**: We calculate totals, We measure overall amounts
   
   **When to Use**: Explaining billing, measurement, or analytical methodologies; clarifying calculation bases
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When the calculation method is obvious or already understood
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We calculate this per capita
   - I measure productivity per hour
   - We assess performance per quarter
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Provides transparency; establishes clear methodology; preempts questions about calculation basis
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That makes sense / I understand
   - Why per [unit] rather than per [alternative]?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We figure out costs per person to keep it fair.
   - I track my progress per week.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - We calculate efficiency metrics per hour per production line per shift.
   - Performance is assessed per annum per the evaluation framework.

1. **Functional Expression**: Per your request/instruction/guidance (function: acknowledging directives)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Acknowledges and indicates compliance with requests or instructions
   - *Formality*: formal/semi-formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (attributes action to external request)
   
   **Synonyms**: As you requested, Following your instructions, As per your guidance, In response to your request
   
   **Antonyms**: On my own initiative, Independently, Without prompting
   
   **When to Use**: Professional responses to requests; showing compliance; formal business communication
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts (sounds overly formal); when taking independent initiative (contradicts meaning)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Per your request, I have attached...
   - Per your instructions, the team will...
   - Per your guidance, we proceeded with...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Shows responsiveness; deflects responsibility to requester; common in hierarchical workplace communication
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Thank you for following up
   - Excellent, thank you
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Per your request, here are the files.
   - Per what you asked, I contacted them.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Per your request of November 15th, please find attached the quarterly analysis.
   - Per your instructions, the allocation has been adjusted to $500 per capita.

1. **Functional Expression**: [X] per [Y] basis (function: specifying calculation method)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Specifies the fundamental unit or method used for calculations or distributions
   - *Formality*: formal/technical
   - *Directness*: direct (methodological specification)
   
   **Synonyms**: calculated per [Y], on a per-[Y] basis, measured per [Y]
   
   **Antonyms**: on a total basis, overall, in aggregate
   
   **When to Use**: Technical, financial, or formal contexts when clarifying calculation methodology
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (unnecessarily formal and wordy)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - On a per capita basis
   - Per unit basis
   - On a per-person basis
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Establishes clear methodological framework; common in technical and financial documentation; can sound bureaucratic if overused
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Understood, on a per-[Y] basis
   - Why that basis rather than...?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We split costs on a per-person basis.
   - Calculate it per unit, not as a total.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Taxes are assessed on a per-capita basis at $1,200 per household per annum.
   - Distribution is calculated on a per-unit basis per the allocation protocol.

1. **Functional Expression**: How much per [unit/period]? (function: inquiring about rates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Direct question about per-unit or per-period rates or costs
   - *Formality*: neutral (adaptable)
   - *Directness*: direct (information request)
   
   **Synonyms**: What's the rate per [unit]?, What do you charge per [unit]?, What's the cost per [unit]?
   
   **Antonyms**: What's the total?, How much altogether?
   
   **When to Use**: Inquiring about rates, prices, or measurements in business or service contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Very formal written communication (use fuller question structures)
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - How much per person?
   - What's the rate per hour?
   - How much per unit?
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Simple and efficient for rate inquiries; "per" specification prevents ambiguity; tone can be adjusted by adding "please" or "if you don't mind"
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - It's [amount] per [unit]
   - The rate is [amount] per [unit]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - How much per person for the group dinner?
   - What's the parking fee per hour?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Could you please clarify the consultation fee per hour per consultant?
   - What is the allocation per capita per annum per the budget proposal?

1. **Functional Expression**: We allocate/distribute/assign [amount] per [unit/person/capita] (function: explaining distribution)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Explains how resources, funds, or items are distributed on a per-unit basis
   - *Formality*: formal/semi-formal
   - *Directness*: direct (explanatory)
   
   **Synonyms**: We give [amount] per [unit], We divide [amount] per [unit], Each [unit] receives [amount]
   
   **Antonyms**: We distribute [amount] total, We allocate [amount] overall
   
   **When to Use**: Resource management, budget discussions, distribution planning; professional and organizational contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual contexts where simpler language suffices
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - We allocate $500 per employee
   - We distribute resources per capita
   - We assign budgets per department
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Formal verbs ("allocate," "distribute," "assign") signal organizational authority; "per" specification ensures clarity and equity perception
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - That seems fair / equitable
   - Why that amount per [unit]?
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're giving everyone $50 per person.
   - We split it equally per household.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee allocates $10,000 per department per fiscal year per the approved budget.
   - Resources are distributed at 250 units per facility per month per operational guidelines.

1. **Functional Expression**: According to [source], [amount/rate] per [unit] (function: citing sources for rates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Cites authoritative sources for per-unit rates or statistics
   - *Formality*: formal/academic
   - *Directness*: indirect (authority-based)
   
   **Synonyms**: Per [source], [source] indicates [amount] per [unit], As per [source]
   
   **Antonyms**: In my opinion, I estimate, Roughly
   
   **When to Use**: Academic writing, research reports, formal presentations when citing data sources
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation; when personal estimation is appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - According to the report, $50 per capita
   - Per the study, 100 units per hour
   - As stated in the policy, $200 per annum
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Establishes credibility by citing sources; "according to" more standard in academic writing than "as per"; source citation essential for data claims
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - What does [source] say about...?
   - That aligns with / differs from [other source]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to the website, it's $30 per person.
   - The email said $50 per hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to the World Bank (2023), GDP per capita averaged $45,000 per annum.
   - Per the regulatory framework, fees are capped at $100 per transaction per the guidelines in Appendix B.
