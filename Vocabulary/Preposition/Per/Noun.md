1. **Noun**: rate (C, pl: rates)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A measure, quantity, or frequency measured against another quantity or measure; the amount charged per unit
   - *(Countable)* Used extensively with "per" to express per-unit measurements or costs
   
   **Synonyms**: price, cost, charge, fee, tariff
   
   **Antonyms**: total, sum, aggregate, lump sum
   
   **When to Use**: Financial, business, or technical contexts when specifying per-unit measurements; all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to total amounts rather than per-unit calculations
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - rate per hour
   - rate per person
   - rate per unit
   - interest rate per annum
   - exchange rate per dollar
   
   **Root Analysis**: rat (reckoned) + -e
   
   **Etymology**: Old French rate → rate (15th century), meaning "estimated value"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What's the rate per night at that hotel?
   - The parking rate is $2 per hour.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The interest rate is established at 4.5% per annum.
   - Production rates averaged 150 units per hour per facility.

1. **Noun**: capita (U, from Latin "per capita")
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Used in phrase "per capita") For each person; by or for each individual person in a population
   - *(Uncountable, technical term)* Latin term used in economic and statistical contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: per person, per head, per individual, each person, apiece
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, collective, overall
   
   **When to Use**: Economic, statistical, or formal contexts when expressing per-person measurements
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per person" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - per capita income
   - per capita consumption
   - GDP per capita
   - per capita expenditure
   - per capita basis
   
   **Root Analysis**: capit (head) + -a (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin capita (heads) → per capita (by heads), used in English from 17th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The cost per capita is about $50 per person.
   - Income per capita has risen.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Healthcare spending per capita increased 5.2% per annum.
   - Energy consumption per capita averaged 500 kWh per month per household.

1. **Noun**: unit (C, pl: units)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An individual thing or person regarded as single and complete; a standard quantity used as measurement
   - *(Countable)* Fundamental term used with "per" to express per-item measurements
   
   **Synonyms**: item, piece, individual, element, component
   
   **Antonyms**: total, whole, aggregate, entirety, collection
   
   **When to Use**: Technical, business, or measurement contexts when specifying per-individual-item calculations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to combined or total quantities
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - cost per unit
   - price per unit
   - production per unit
   - per unit basis
   - per unit time
   
   **Root Analysis**: un (one) + -it (make)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin unitas → unit (16th century), meaning "oneness, single entity"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The cost per unit drops with bulk orders.
   - How much per unit are you charging?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Manufacturing costs decreased to $12.50 per unit per quarter.
   - Output averaged 75 units per hour per production line.

1. **Noun**: annum (U, Latin term)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Used in phrase "per annum") For each year; annually
   - *(Uncountable, formal Latin term)* Used in financial and formal contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: per year, annually, yearly, each year, every year
   
   **Antonyms**: per month, per day, per quarter
   
   **When to Use**: Formal, financial, or legal contexts when expressing per-year rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per year" or "annually" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - per annum interest
   - salary per annum
   - growth per annum
   - percentage per annum
   - compound per annum
   
   **Root Analysis**: ann (year) + -um (noun suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin annum (year) → per annum (by the year), used in English from 16th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She makes $60,000 per annum.
   - The account earns 3% per annum.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The bond yields 4.25% per annum compounded quarterly.
   - Revenue growth averaged 7.3% per annum over the five-year period.

1. **Noun**: head (C, pl: heads)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Used in phrase "per head") For each person; per individual
   - *(Countable)* Informal alternative to "per capita," often used in hospitality and event planning
   
   **Synonyms**: per person, per capita, per individual, apiece, each
   
   **Antonyms**: total, group rate, combined, overall
   
   **When to Use**: Hospitality, catering, or informal contexts when expressing per-person costs
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Formal statistical or academic writing (use "per capita" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - cost per head
   - price per head
   - charge per head
   - payment per head
   - $X per head
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English heafod (top of body)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English heafod → head, extended to mean "per person" in counting contexts (medieval period)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The dinner is $75 per head.
   - We're charging $25 per head for the event.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Catering costs are estimated at $50 per head per attendee.
   - The per-head allocation for the conference is $200 per participant.

1. **Noun**: diem (U, Latin term)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Used in phrase "per diem") For each day; a daily allowance
   - *(Uncountable)* Latin term used in business and travel contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: daily allowance, per day, daily rate, day rate
   
   **Antonyms**: weekly, monthly, lump sum, annual
   
   **When to Use**: Business travel, employment, or formal contexts for daily allowances or rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation (use "per day" instead)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - per diem allowance
   - per diem rate
   - receive per diem
   - per diem payment
   - per diem basis
   
   **Root Analysis**: di (day) + -em (accusative case)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin diem (day) → per diem (by the day), used in English from 18th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You'll get a per diem of $100 per day for meals.
   - The per diem covers food and incidentals.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Employees receive a per diem of $150 per day per business travel regulations.
   - The standard per diem rate is $75 per person per day.

1. **Noun**: percentage (C, pl: percentages)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A rate, number, or amount in each hundred; a proportion per hundred
   - *(Countable)* Mathematical term expressing rates per 100 units
   
   **Synonyms**: percent, proportion, ratio, fraction, share
   
   **Antonyms**: absolute number, total, whole amount
   
   **When to Use**: Mathematical, statistical, financial contexts expressing per-hundred rates
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When absolute numbers are more appropriate than ratios
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - percentage per annum
   - percentage per capita
   - high percentage per
   - percentage per unit
   - growth percentage per year
   
   **Root Analysis**: per + cent (hundred) + -age (condition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin per centum (by the hundred) → percentage (18th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - What percentage per year do you earn in interest?
   - A small percentage per person adds up.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The mortality rate declined by 2.3 percentage points per year per region.
   - Inflation averaged 3.5 percentage points per annum.

1. **Noun**: allocation (C, pl: allocations)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An amount of a resource assigned to a particular recipient; distribution per recipient or unit
   - *(Countable)* Amount distributed per person, unit, or category
   
   **Synonyms**: allotment, distribution, share, assignment, apportionment
   
   **Antonyms**: total, aggregate, whole, undivided amount
   
   **When to Use**: Business, government, or resource management contexts for per-unit distributions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to total available resources rather than distributed amounts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - allocation per capita
   - allocation per unit
   - budget allocation per department
   - resource allocation per person
   - allocation per annum
   
   **Root Analysis**: al- (to) + loc (place) + -ation (action)
   
   **Etymology**: Medieval Latin allocare → allocation (17th century), meaning "distribution to specific recipients"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Each person gets an allocation of $500 per year.
   - What's the water allocation per household?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Budget allocations were established at $10,000 per department per fiscal year.
   - Resource allocations averaged 250 units per facility per month.

1. **Noun**: basis (C, pl: bases)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The underlying principle or foundation; the method of calculation
   - *(Countable)* Used in "per X basis" to indicate the unit of measurement or calculation
   
   **Synonyms**: foundation, ground, principle, method, standard
   
   **Antonyms**: result, outcome, conclusion
   
   **When to Use**: Formal or technical contexts when specifying the per-unit method of calculation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where simpler expressions suffice
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - per capita basis
   - per unit basis
   - on a per-person basis
   - per annum basis
   - calculated on a per basis
   
   **Root Analysis**: Greek basis (foundation, base)
   
   **Etymology**: Greek basis → Latin → basis (16th century), meaning "foundation"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We calculate costs on a per-person basis—$30 each.
   - Prices are set on a per-item basis.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Taxes are assessed on a per-capita basis at $1,200 per household per annum.
   - Fees are calculated on a per-unit basis at $15 per transaction.

1. **Noun**: allowance (C, pl: allowances)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The amount of something permitted or allocated; a sum granted per period or person
   - *(Countable)* Amount granted per person or per time period
   
   **Synonyms**: allocation, stipend, grant, quota, ration
   
   **Antonyms**: total, unlimited, unrestricted amount
   
   **When to Use**: Employment, travel, or resource management contexts for per-person or per-period allocations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When referring to unlimited or unrestricted amounts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - daily allowance per person
   - allowance per child
   - expense allowance per trip
   - allowance per annum
   - per diem allowance
   
   **Root Analysis**: al- (to) + low (permit) + -ance (state of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French alouer → allowance (14th century), meaning "permitted amount"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Kids get an allowance of $10 per week.
   - The baggage allowance is 23kg per person.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Travel allowances are set at $200 per employee per business trip.
   - The housing allowance is $1,500 per month per qualifying individual.
