1. **Adverb**: specifically (type: degree/manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is exact and clear; particularly; with regard to a specific aspect or detail
   - *Position: typically mid-sentence or sentence-initial for emphasis*
   
   **Synonyms**: particularly, especially, precisely, exactly, explicitly
   
   **Antonyms**: generally, broadly, vaguely, approximately
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and informal contexts when emphasizing precision or particularity; when directing attention to specific details with regard to a topic
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When general statements are intended; when precision might seem pedantic
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - specifically designed, specifically with regard to, specifically mentioned
   - more specifically, specifically stated, specifically address
   
   **Root Analysis**: specific + -ally (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin species (kind, sort) → specificus → specifically (in a precise manner)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I was specifically talking about the budget with regard to next year.
   - What specifically do you need help with?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The policy specifically addresses concerns with regard to data protection.
   - Research specifically focused on variables with regard to performance outcomes.

1. **Adverb**: accordingly (type: manner/sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; with regard to what is fitting or suitable
   - *(Logical)* As a result; therefore; consequently
   - *Position: typically sentence-initial or sentence-final*
   
   **Synonyms**: appropriately, correspondingly, consequently, therefore, thus
   
   **Antonyms**: inappropriately, regardless, nevertheless
   
   **When to Use**: Formal contexts when indicating appropriate action or logical consequence; when showing regard to proper responses
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Casual conversation where simpler terms like "so" would be more natural
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act accordingly, plan accordingly, accordingly with regard to
   - adjust accordingly, respond accordingly
   
   **Root Analysis**: according + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French acorder (to agree) → according → accordingly (in agreement with, appropriately)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The weather forecast changed, so plan accordingly.
   - We need to adjust our approach accordingly with regard to the feedback.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board has reviewed the proposal and will act accordingly.
   - Policies must be revised accordingly with regard to regulatory changes.
