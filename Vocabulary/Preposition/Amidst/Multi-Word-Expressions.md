1. **Expression**: amidst the chaos (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - During or while surrounded by a situation of complete disorder and confusion
   - *Fixedness*: strong (highly frequent, natural word partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: in the chaos, during the confusion, surrounded by disorder, in the midst of chaos
   
   **Antonyms**: in calm conditions, in orderly circumstances, during peace
   
   **When to Use**: Describing actions or states that occur during extremely disorderly situations; journalism, narrative writing, formal and informal contexts; emphasizes functioning despite disorder; very common collocation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For mild disorganization (use "amidst the confusion"); when situation isn't genuinely chaotic; can be hyperbolic if overused; avoid when "during the chaos" is simpler and clearer
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - amid the chaos
   - in the midst of the chaos
   - amidst all the chaos
   - amidst the utter chaos
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Amidst" strongly collocates with "chaos" because both are formal/literary words often used in journalistic and narrative contexts to emphasize dramatic circumstances; "chaos" represents extreme disorder that "amidst" helps frame as an surrounding environment
   
   **Root Analysis**: amidst (Old English "on middan") + chaos (Greek "khaos")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She found her keys amidst the chaos of moving day.
   - He stayed calm amidst the chaos at the party.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization maintained operations amidst the chaos of institutional restructuring.
   - Decision-making processes functioned amidst the chaos of rapid environmental change.
