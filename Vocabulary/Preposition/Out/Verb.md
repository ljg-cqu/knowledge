1. **Verb**: come out
   **Meaning**: 
   - To move from inside to outside; to emerge or appear
   - To become publicly known or revealed
   - *(Sense 3)* To declare one's identity, especially regarding sexuality
   
   **Synonyms**: emerge, appear, exit, surface, be revealed
   
   **Antonyms**: go in, enter, hide, conceal
   
   **When to Use**: Use in physical contexts (emerging from spaces), information disclosure, or identity revelation. Appropriate in both casual and formal contexts depending on usage.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid overuse in formal academic writing where precise verbs like "emerge" or "appear" may be more appropriate.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - come out of the closet
   - come out in support of
   - come out on top
   - the truth comes out
   - come out swinging
   
   **Root Analysis**: come (Old English cuman) + out (Old English ūt)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English → phrasal verb combination expressing outward movement (12th century)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Come out here and see what I found!
   - The photos came out really well.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The findings came out in the latest research publication.
   - Several witnesses came out to testify in the proceedings.
