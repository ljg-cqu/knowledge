1. **Expression**: out of the blue (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Happening unexpectedly or without warning; suddenly and surprisingly
   - Literal meaning: From a clear sky (no clouds/warning) vs. Figurative: Completely unexpected
   - *Fixedness*: strong (very fixed partnership)
   
   **Synonyms**: unexpectedly, suddenly, without warning, out of nowhere, all of a sudden
   
   **Antonyms**: as expected, predictably, as planned
   
   **When to Use**: Use when something happens completely unexpectedly, especially pleasant or neutral surprises. Common in narrative and conversational contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can minimize serious negative events; use sensitively for tragedies or serious matters.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - out of nowhere
   - out of the clear blue sky (fuller form)
   - completely out of the blue
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Originates from lightning from a clear blue sky—an unexpected event with no warning signs. "Blue" refers to clear sky, symbol of calm before surprise.
   
   **Root Analysis**: Meteorological imagery—lightning from clear blue sky as metaphor for surprise
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She called me out of the blue after five years!
   - The job offer came completely out of the blue.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The announcement came out of the blue, surprising all stakeholders.
   - Market shifts occurred out of the blue, with no preceding indicators.

   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The situation spiraled out of control despite intervention efforts.

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I haven't thought about him in years—out of sight, out of mind, I guess.
   - Keep snacks visible or it's out of sight, out of mind.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The principle of "out of sight, out of mind" affects organizational memory.
