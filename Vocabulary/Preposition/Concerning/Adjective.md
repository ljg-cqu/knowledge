1. **Adjective**: concerned
   **Meaning**: 
   - Worried, anxious, or troubled about something
   - Involved with or interested in something
   - *(Sense 2)* Related to or connected with something (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: worried, anxious, troubled, apprehensive, uneasy
   
   **Antonyms**: unconcerned, indifferent, carefree, relaxed
   
   **When to Use**: When expressing worry, involvement, or interest in matters affecting oneself or others; formal and informal contexts; professional and personal discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in contexts requiring neutral observation without emotional involvement; inappropriate when discussing trivial matters with exaggerated emotion
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - deeply concerned about
   - concerned citizens
   - concerned parties
   - concerned with the issue
   - as far as X is concerned
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern (from Latin) + -ed (past participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → concernere "to relate to, involve" → Old French concerner → Middle English concern + -ed
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm really concerned about my friend's health lately.
   - She's not concerned with what other people think.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board is deeply concerned about the company's financial trajectory.
   - All concerned parties must be notified of the policy changes.

1. **Adjective**: concerning (attributive)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Causing worry or anxiety; troubling; alarming (non-gradable)
   - Relating to or about something (typically used attributively)
   
   **Synonyms**: worrying, troubling, alarming, disturbing, disquieting
   
   **Antonyms**: reassuring, comforting, encouraging, heartening
   
   **When to Use**: Formal and professional contexts when describing situations that cause worry; medical, legal, business reports; appropriate for serious matters requiring attention
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation (sounds overly formal); don't use for minor issues that don't warrant serious concern
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - concerning developments
   - concerning findings
   - concerning behavior
   - concerning trend
   - particularly concerning
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern + -ing (present participle suffix functioning as adjective)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere → Old French concerner → English concern + -ing (adjectival use)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - His grades have been pretty concerning this semester.
   - That's a concerning amount of caffeine you're drinking.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The audit revealed several concerning discrepancies in the financial records.
   - Recent studies show concerning trends in adolescent mental health.

1. **Adjective**: unconcerned
   **Meaning**: 
   - Not worried or anxious about something; showing no concern
   - Not interested in or involved with something (gradable)
   
   **Synonyms**: indifferent, unworried, untroubled, carefree, nonchalant
   
   **Antonyms**: concerned, worried, anxious, troubled, invested
   
   **When to Use**: When describing lack of worry or interest; neutral or slightly critical tone; both formal and informal contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when you need to show empathy or understanding; inappropriate when describing situations requiring attention
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - seemingly unconcerned
   - remain unconcerned
   - appear unconcerned
   - completely unconcerned about
   - unconcerned with details
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (negative prefix) + concern + -ed (past participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: un- (negation) + concerned (from Latin concernere)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He seems totally unconcerned about the deadline tomorrow.
   - She was unconcerned with what people thought of her outfit.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The management appeared unconcerned about employee retention issues.
   - Participants remained unconcerned despite the potential risks outlined in the study.
