1. **Adverb**: concernedly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a worried or anxious manner; with concern or apprehension
   - Position: typically mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: worriedly, anxiously, apprehensively, nervously, uneasily
   
   **Antonyms**: confidently, calmly, indifferently, carelessly
   
   **When to Use**: When describing how someone speaks or acts with worry or care; narrative writing; formal and informal contexts describing emotional states
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Overuse can make writing melodramatic; avoid in technical or objective reporting where neutral tone is required
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - looked concernedly at
   - asked concernedly
   - glanced concernedly
   - spoke concernedly about
   - watched concernedly
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern + -ed + -ly (manner adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere → concern + -ed + -ly (adverbial formation)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She looked at him concernedly when he mentioned the pain.
   - "Are you okay?" he asked concernedly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The physician examined the patient's symptoms concernedly before ordering additional tests.
   - Board members listened concernedly as the financial projections were presented.

1. **Adverb**: concerningly (type: sentence/degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a manner that causes concern or worry; to a degree that is troubling
   - Position: typically sentence-initial or pre-adjectival
   
   **Synonyms**: worryingly, alarmingly, disturbingly, troublingly
   
   **Antonyms**: reassuringly, encouragingly, comfortingly
   
   **When to Use**: Formal academic or professional writing; emphasizing the worrying nature of a situation; objective analysis of concerning trends
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in casual conversation (sounds formal); don't use for minor issues; inappropriate when downplaying concerns
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - concerningly high levels
   - concerningly low rates
   - more concerningly
   - concerningly similar to
   - perhaps most concerningly
   
   **Root Analysis**: concern + -ing + -ly (adverb from present participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin concernere → concern + -ing + -ly (modern formation)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The temperature is getting concerningly high in here.
   - He's been concerningly quiet about his plans.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data shows concerningly high rates of antibiotic resistance in this population.
   - More concerningly, these trends appear to be accelerating despite intervention efforts.

1. **Adverb**: unconcernedly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a manner showing no worry or interest; without concern; indifferently
   - Position: typically mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: indifferently, casually, nonchalantly, carelessly, coolly
   
   **Antonyms**: anxiously, worriedly, nervously, attentively
   
   **When to Use**: When describing actions performed without worry or interest; narrative writing; both positive (carefree) and negative (indifferent) connotations
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when describing situations requiring appropriate concern; can sound critical if misapplied
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - shrugged unconcernedly
   - continued unconcernedly
   - walked unconcernedly
   - responded unconcernedly
   - said unconcernedly
   
   **Root Analysis**: un- (negative prefix) + concern + -ed + -ly (manner adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: un- (negation) + concern + -ed + -ly
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He shrugged unconcernedly when I asked about the test.
   - She scrolled through her phone unconcernedly while we waited.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The defendant sat unconcernedly throughout the proceedings.
   - Despite the mounting evidence, the executive continued unconcernedly with business as usual.
