1. **Expression**: pick up (type: phrasal verb/collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Phrasal verb) To lift something from a surface
   - *(Sense 2)* To collect someone or something
   - *(Sense 3)* To learn or acquire a skill casually
   - *(Sense 4)* To improve or increase
   - *(Sense 5)* To resume after interruption
   - *(Sense 6)* To notice or detect something
   - *Fixedness*: strong (core meanings), medium (metaphorical extensions)
   
   **Synonyms**: lift (sense 1), collect, fetch (sense 2), learn, acquire (sense 3), improve (sense 4), resume, continue (sense 5), detect, notice (sense 6)
   
   **Antonyms**: put down (sense 1), drop off (sense 2), decline, worsen (sense 4), interrupt (sense 5)
   
   **When to Use**: Extremely versatile phrasal verb; appropriate across all registers; context determines specific meaning
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Multiple meanings require clear context; specify which sense you intend
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - pickup (noun: collection or improvement)
   - picking up
   - picked up
   - pick up on (notice/understand)
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocation*: "Pick" implies selection/grasping; "up" adds direction and completion; highly productive with multiple metaphorical extensions
   
   **Root Analysis**: pick (select/grasp) + up (upward/into possession)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you pick up some milk on your way home?
   - She picked up Spanish really quickly.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The economy is beginning to pick up after the recession.
   - Sensors picked up anomalous readings during the test.
