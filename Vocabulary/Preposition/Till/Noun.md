1. **Noun**: till (C, pl: tills)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A cash register or the drawer in a shop or business where money from sales is kept
   - *(Sense 2)* The money kept in such a drawer
   - *(Sense 3)* (Geology) Unstratified glacial sediment consisting of clay, sand, gravel, and boulders mixed together
   
   **Synonyms**: cash register, cash drawer, register, cashbox, money drawer
   
   **Antonyms**: N/A
   
   **When to Use**: Use "till" in retail, business, or commercial contexts when referring to the cash register or money drawer. Common in British English for cash registers. In American English, "cash register" or "register" is more common. In geological contexts, use for describing glacial deposits.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use in formal financial reports (use "cash register" or "point of sale"). Not appropriate for online/digital payment systems. Avoid confusing with "until" (the preposition). The geological sense is specialized - use only in scientific contexts.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - open the till
   - close the till
   - money in the till
   - rob the till
   - balance the till
   - empty the till
   - till receipt
   - at the till
   
   **Root Analysis**: Middle English "tylle" (drawer, compartment) - simple noun without modern affixes

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can you grab some change from the till?

   **Examples [Formal]**:

1. **Noun**: tillage (U)
   **Meaning**: 
   - The preparation of land for growing crops by plowing, harrowing, and fertilizing
   - *(Sense 2)* Land that has been tilled or cultivated
   
   **Synonyms**: cultivation, farming, plowing, agriculture, husbandry, soil preparation
   
   **Antonyms**: fallow land, wilderness, uncultivated land
   
   **When to Use**: Use "tillage" in agricultural, farming, or soil science contexts when discussing land preparation methods. Common in agricultural literature, farming manuals, and environmental studies. Appropriate for both traditional and modern farming discussions.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use in non-agricultural contexts. Not appropriate for urban gardening (use "soil preparation" instead). Avoid in casual conversation - "plowing" or "farming" are simpler alternatives.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - tillage practices
   - minimum tillage
   - no-till/no-tillage farming
   - conventional tillage
   - conservation tillage
   - tillage system
   - tillage depth
   - reduce tillage
   
   **Root Analysis**: "till" (verb root) + "-age" (noun suffix indicating action or result)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English, from "tillen" (to till) + "-age" (suffix) - appeared in agricultural texts by the 14th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Dad says we need to start tillage early this year if we want a good harvest.
   - The old fields show clear signs of years of tillage.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Conservation tillage methods have been shown to reduce soil erosion by up to 70 percent.
   - The adoption of no-tillage systems has significantly improved soil health in the region.
