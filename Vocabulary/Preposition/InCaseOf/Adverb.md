1. **Adverb**: immediately (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without delay; at once; at the very moment something happens.
   - *Position*: Usually mid-position (after auxiliary) or end-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: at once, right away, instantly, promptly, without delay
   
   **Antonyms**: gradually, eventually, later, slowly
   
   **When to Use**: Use in instructions that describe what to do in case of emergencies or failures when speed is critical (fire, medical issues, security breaches).
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid if some delay is acceptable or procedures require confirmation first—then "promptly" or "as soon as possible" may be better.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act immediately in case of fire
   - call emergency services immediately in case of accident
   - shut down immediately in case of overheating
   - report issues immediately in case of data loss
   
   **Root Analysis**: immediate (without anything in between) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *immediatus* (not in the middle, direct) → "immediate" → "immediately" meaning "at once".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In case of an emergency, call this number immediately.
   - Leave the building immediately in case of fire.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - In case of critical incident, the duty officer must be notified immediately.
   - All operations shall cease immediately in case of detected safety hazards.

1. **Adverb**: promptly (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without unnecessary delay; quickly and efficiently, but allowing for minimal practical steps.
   - *Position*: Usually end-position or mid-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: swiftly, quickly, without delay, in a timely manner
   
   **Antonyms**: slowly, belatedly, tardily, late
   
   **When to Use**: Use in policies and formal instructions where fast response is required in case of incident, but not necessarily "instant" response.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in highly urgent life‑or‑death cases where "immediately" is clearer and stronger.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond promptly in case of emergency
   - report promptly in case of incident
   - act promptly in case of system failure
   - notify security promptly in case of suspicious activity
   
   **Root Analysis**: prompt (quick) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *promptus* (ready, quick) → "prompt" → "promptly".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please call me promptly in case of any problems.
   - Let the teacher know promptly in case of illness.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Staff shall report promptly in case of safety incidents or near misses.
   - Customers must be informed promptly in case of data breaches.

1. **Adverb**: calmly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a quiet, controlled, and not panicked way.
   - *Position*: Usually end-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: steadily, without panic, composedly, with self-control
   
   **Antonyms**: nervously, frantically, anxiously, hysterically
   
   **When to Use**: Use when giving behavioural guidance for emergencies—how people should act in case of fire, accident, or crisis.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when emotional expression is appropriate (e.g., condolence contexts) or when urgency must be strongly emphasized over composure.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - respond calmly in case of emergency
   - follow instructions calmly in case of fire
   - act calmly in case of accident
   - remain calmly in place in case of lockdown
   
   **Root Analysis**: calm (quiet, peaceful) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: From Latin *cauma* (heat of the day, rest) → Old French → English "calm" → "calmly".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Try to react calmly in case of an emergency so you can think clearly.
   - Stay calmly in the safe area in case of earthquake.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Staff are trained to respond calmly in case of critical incidents.
   - Passengers must proceed calmly to the exits in case of evacuation.

1. **Adverb**: safely (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that avoids danger, harm, or risk.
   - *Position*: Usually end-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: securely, without danger, without harm, with care
   
   **Antonyms**: dangerously, recklessly, carelessly, unsafely
   
   **When to Use**: Use with verbs describing evacuation, shutdown, or switching in case of emergencies or failures.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when the main focus is speed at any cost; then "immediately" or "urgently" might be prioritized.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - evacuate safely in case of fire
   - shut down the system safely in case of malfunction
   - exit safely in case of earthquake
   - move to a safe area in case of explosion risk
   
   **Root Analysis**: safe (free from harm) + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Old French *sauf* (safe) → English "safe" → "safely".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In case of an emergency, follow the signs to get out safely.
   - Make sure you can turn off the gas safely in case of a leak.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Procedures describe how to safely isolate equipment in case of electrical fault.
   - All personnel must be evacuated safely in case of structural instability.

1. **Adverb**: automatically (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Without human intervention; by itself, as part of a programmed or built‑in response.
   - *Position*: Usually mid-position or end-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: by default, mechanically, by itself, without manual action
   
   **Antonyms**: manually, deliberately, by hand, intentionally
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing systems that trigger alarms, backups, or shutdowns in case of error, overload, or emergency conditions.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when people must take action themselves; then use "manually" or "personally" instead.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - alarms trigger automatically in case of fire
   - data is backed up automatically in case of device loss
   - doors lock automatically in case of security breach
   - system shuts down automatically in case of overheating
   
   **Root Analysis**: automatic (self‑acting) + -ally/-ly
   
   **Etymology**: Greek *automatos* (self‑moving) → "automatic" → "automatically".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The sprinklers come on automatically in case of fire.
   - My files back up automatically in case my laptop dies.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Critical alarms are triggered automatically in case of parameter deviations.
   - The failover cluster activates automatically in case of primary‑node failure.
