1. **Verb**: evacuate
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive/Intransitive) To move people from a dangerous place to a safer place, especially in an emergency.
   - (Transitive) To empty a building or area as part of safety procedures.
   
   **Synonyms**: clear, empty, move out, withdraw, relocate
   
   **Antonyms**: occupy, enter, remain, shelter (in place)
   
   **When to Use**: Use in safety instructions that specify what to do in case of fire, gas leak, earthquake, or other hazards.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use for routine leaving at the end of the day; "leave" or "go out" is enough. "Evacuate" implies danger or formal procedure.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - evacuate the building in case of fire
   - evacuate immediately in case of gas leak
   - evacuate to the assembly point in case of alarm
   - be prepared to evacuate in case of natural disaster
   
   **Root Analysis**: e- (out) + vacu- (empty) + -ate (verb)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *evacuare* (to empty out) → English "evacuate" (to empty, then to remove people from danger).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In case of fire, we have to evacuate the building right away.
   - They told us to evacuate to the school gym in case of a typhoon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All occupants must evacuate via the nearest safe exit in case of alarm activation.
   - Staff shall be trained to evacuate vulnerable persons in case of emergency.

1. **Verb**: notify
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To formally or officially inform someone about something.
   
   **Synonyms**: inform, alert, advise, apprise, report to
   
   **Antonyms**: ignore, conceal, withhold, fail to inform
   
   **When to Use**: Use in procedures that specify who should be told in case of incident, emergency, or system failure.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In very casual conversation, "tell" or "let someone know" may sound more natural.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - notify security in case of suspicious activity
   - notify your manager in case of incident
   - notify the authorities in case of serious accident
   - automatically notify users in case of data breach
   
   **Root Analysis**: notus (known) + -fy (make) → make known
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *notificare* (to make known) → Old French → English "notify".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Please notify me in case of any changes to the schedule.
   - Call this number to notify the building manager in case of emergency.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The incident response team must be notified in case of critical system failure.
   - Clients shall be notified without undue delay in case of personal‑data breaches.

1. **Verb**: activate
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To make a device, system, or process start working.
   
   **Synonyms**: switch on, trigger, enable, start, engage
   
   **Antonyms**: deactivate, shut down, disable, turn off
   
   **When to Use**: Use when describing alarms, backup systems, or emergency procedures that start in case of specific events.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for simple daily use of devices if no special state is implied—"turn on" is often enough.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - activate the alarm in case of intrusion
   - activate backup systems in case of primary failure
   - automatically activate sprinklers in case of fire
   - activate the emergency plan in case of disaster
   
   **Root Analysis**: active (working, in motion) + -ate (verb)
   
   **Etymology**: From "active" (Latin *activus*) → "activate" meaning "to make active".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The smoke will activate the alarm in case of fire.
   - In case of power cuts, a sensor activates the backup generator.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The business continuity plan shall be activated in case of prolonged service interruption.
   - Automatic containment procedures are activated in case of chemical release.

1. **Verb**: shut down
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive/Intransitive, phrasal) To stop a machine, system, or service from operating, especially in an orderly way.
   
   **Synonyms**: power down, turn off, stop, switch off, close
   
   **Antonyms**: start up, switch on, power up, bring online
   
   **When to Use**: Use in technical and safety contexts where systems must be stopped in case of overload, overheating, or other hazards.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for temporary pauses ("pause" or "suspend" may be better) or for ending human activities ("finish," "end").
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - shut down the system in case of overheating
   - shut down equipment in case of abnormal noise
   - automatically shut down in case of fault
   - safely shut down in case of power surge
   
   **Root Analysis**: shut (close) + down (direction/completion)
   
   **Etymology**: From phrasal verb "shut down" (to close completely), extended to machines and systems in the 20th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In case of a storm, we shut down the machines to avoid damage.
   - The server will shut down automatically in case of serious errors.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All machinery shall shut down automatically in case of emergency‑stop activation.
   - Operators must shut down the line in case of detected safety violations.

1. **Verb**: secure
   **Meaning**: 
   - (Transitive) To make something safe or protected so that it cannot be harmed, lost, or accessed without permission.
   - (Transitive) To fasten or fix firmly so it does not move in dangerous conditions.
   
   **Synonyms**: protect, safeguard, lock, fasten, stabilize
   
   **Antonyms**: expose, endanger, loosen, leave vulnerable
   
   **When to Use**: Use for physical safety (securing objects, areas) and information/security contexts (securing data, accounts) in case of risk.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Do not confuse with "ensure" (make certain) or "assure" (reassure someone).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - secure loose items in case of strong winds
   - secure doors and windows in case of evacuation
   - secure data in case of system compromise
   - secure backups in case of primary storage failure
   
   **Root Analysis**: se- (without) + cura (care) → free from care; then verb "secure" = make safe
   
   **Etymology**: Latin *securus* (safe) → Old French → English "secure" as verb meaning "to make safe or sure".
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Secure the shelves in case of earthquake so they don't fall over.
   - Make sure your account is secured in case of hacking attempts.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - All hazardous materials must be secured in case of seismic activity.
   - Critical records shall be securely stored in case of primary‑site loss.
