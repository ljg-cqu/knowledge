1. **Adverb**: even (type: degree)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to emphasize something surprising or extreme in a given context (non-gradable, typically mid-position before main verb or adjective)
   
   **Synonyms**: actually, in fact, indeed, surprisingly
   
   **Antonyms**: barely, hardly, scarcely
   
   **When to Use**: Use with "amid" to emphasize unexpected persistence or occurrence despite challenging circumstances; effective for highlighting surprising facts; appropriate in all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid overuse as it can weaken emphasis; not appropriate when situation is expected or normal; can sound dramatic if misapplied
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - even amid [challenges]
   - even managed/succeeded amid
   - even grew/increased amid
   
   **Root Analysis**: even (from Old English "efen" meaning level, equal)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "efen" (level, equal) → Middle English "even" → intensifying adverb use developed in Middle English period
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She even laughed amid all the stress at work.
   - They even expanded the business amid the economic downturn.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization even achieved growth targets amid unprecedented market disruption.
   - Participant retention remained high, even amid significant protocol modifications.

1. **Adverb**: still (type: time)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Up to and including the present or specified time; continuing to happen or be the case (non-gradable, typically mid-position)
   - *(Sense 2)* Nevertheless; in spite of that
   
   **Synonyms**: yet, nevertheless, nonetheless, however (concessive sense)
   
   **Antonyms**: no longer, not anymore
   
   **When to Use**: Use with "amid" to emphasize persistence despite challenges; effective for showing resilience or continuation; appropriate in all registers; adds emphasis to endurance
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when action has clearly ended; can sound contradictory if circumstances have resolved; ambiguity between temporal and concessive meanings
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - still persisted/continued amid
   - still managed/succeeded amid
   - still thriving/growing amid
   
   **Root Analysis**: still (from Old English "stille" meaning motionless, quiet)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "stille" (motionless) → Middle English "stille" → temporal/concessive adverb uses developed from 14th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They're still together amid all the problems they've had.
   - The store still made profit amid the pandemic shutdowns.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The protocol still maintained validity amid evolving methodological standards.
   - Performance metrics still exceeded benchmarks amid significant organizational restructuring.

1. **Adverb**: nevertheless (type: sentence/linking)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In spite of that; notwithstanding; however (non-gradable, typically sentence-initial with comma, or mid-position)
   
   **Synonyms**: nonetheless, however, still, yet, even so
   
   **Antonyms**: therefore, consequently, thus
   
   **When to Use**: Use with "amid" to introduce contrasting information or unexpected outcomes; effective for showing resilience; appropriate in formal writing; adds sophisticated transition
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid in very casual speech (use "but" or "still"); can sound overly formal; not appropriate when outcomes align with expectations
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - nevertheless succeeded/persisted amid
   - nevertheless maintained/continued amid
   - nevertheless achieved amid
   
   **Root Analysis**: never (not ever) + the (definite article) + less (to a smaller degree)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English "never the less" → "nevertheless" (single word by 14th century, meaning "in spite of that")
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They were tired but nevertheless pushed forward amid the difficulties.
   - She was scared but nevertheless stayed calm amid the emergency.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The organization nevertheless maintained strategic focus amid considerable environmental turbulence.
   - Implementation targets were nevertheless achieved amid substantial resource constraints.
