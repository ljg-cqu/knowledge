1. **Functional Expression**: I'm calling/writing on behalf of [person/organization] (function: identifying representative role)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to establish that the speaker/writer is acting as a representative for another party
   - *Formality*: semi-formal to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (clear statement of representative relationship)
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm calling/writing for [person], I represent [person], I'm [person]'s representative, I'm reaching out for [person]
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm calling for myself, I'm representing only my own views
   
   **When to Use**: Use when initiating contact as a representative to immediately establish your role and authority; essential in business, legal, and professional contexts to clarify who you represent
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when speaking for yourself; avoid in casual personal calls where context already makes relationship clear; unnecessary when identity as representative is already established
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm calling/writing to you on behalf of...
   - I'm contacting you on behalf of...
   - I'm reaching out on behalf of...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This expression serves as a crucial face-saving and clarity device—it immediately establishes that you're not acting in personal capacity and clarifies the authority relationship. Protects both you and the person you represent.
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Thank you for calling. How can I help [person/organization]?"
   - "I see. What can I do for [person] today?"
   - "Please go ahead with your message/request."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "Hi, I'm calling for my boss—she wanted me to check on the order status."
   - "I'm writing for the neighborhood association about the parking issue."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "Good morning. I'm calling on behalf of Dr. Martinez to schedule a consultation at your earliest convenience."
   - "I am writing on behalf of Thompson & Associates LLP regarding the matter of Johnson v. Smith."

1. **Functional Expression**: On behalf of [group], I would like to [thank/welcome/congratulate/express]... (function: expressing collective sentiment)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to express gratitude, welcome, congratulations, or other sentiments representing a group
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct (clear attribution to group)
   
   **Synonyms**: Speaking for [group], I want to..., [Group] asked me to..., Representing [group], I wish to...
   
   **Antonyms**: Personally, I..., Speaking only for myself...
   
   **When to Use**: Use in formal speeches, presentations, award ceremonies, official communications when expressing collective feelings; particularly important when acknowledgment needs to come from authority representing others
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use in casual personal contexts; avoid when you might not actually represent group consensus; skip in informal team settings where "we all want to..." works better
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - On behalf of everyone here, I'd like to...
   - On behalf of the team/organization/board, I want to...
   - On behalf of all of us, let me...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This expression manages face by distributing sentiment across a group rather than making it personal; reduces imposition and increases weight of sentiment through collective backing; shows respect by formalizing the expression
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Thank you—please extend my gratitude to everyone."
   - "I appreciate that, and please thank the team for me."
   - "It's an honor. Thank you all."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "On behalf of all of us, thanks so much for hosting this party!"
   - "Everyone wanted me to say congrats on your promotion!"
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "On behalf of the Board of Directors, I would like to express our sincere appreciation for your years of dedicated service."
   - "On behalf of the graduating class of 2024, I welcome you to this commencement ceremony."

1. **Functional Expression**: I'm authorized to act on behalf of [person/organization] (function: establishing legal authority)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to assert that one has official permission and power to represent and make decisions for another party
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct (explicit statement of authority)
   
   **Synonyms**: I have authority to represent..., I'm empowered to act for..., I'm the authorized representative of..., I have power of attorney for...
   
   **Antonyms**: I have no authority to..., I cannot speak for..., I'm not authorized to represent...
   
   **When to Use**: Use in legal, business, and official contexts where establishing your authority to represent is crucial; essential before signing documents, making commitments, or taking binding actions; protects all parties
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when you lack actual authorization (serious legal implications); avoid in casual contexts where formal authority isn't relevant; unnecessary when speaking/acting role doesn't involve commitments
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I'm duly authorized to represent...
   - I have been authorized by [person] to act on their behalf
   - I'm legally authorized to...
   - I hold authority to act for...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This is a high-stakes expression with legal implications; establishes agency relationship and scope of authority; protects principal from unauthorized acts and agent from ultra vires liability; requires evidence (power of attorney, retainer agreement, corporate resolution)
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "May I see your authorization/credentials?"
   - "Thank you for clarifying your authority."
   - "Please provide documentation of your authorization."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - [Not appropriate—authorization requires formal context]
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "I am authorized to act on behalf of my client to negotiate and execute this settlement agreement."
   - "As the duly authorized representative of XYZ Corporation, I am empowered to sign this contract on the company's behalf."

1. **Functional Expression**: Could you speak on behalf of [person/group] at [event/meeting]? (function: requesting representation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask someone to serve as a representative or spokesperson for another party
   - *Formality*: semi-formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (polite request using modal "could")
   
   **Synonyms**: Would you be willing to represent..., Can you speak for..., Would you mind representing..., Could you attend on behalf of...
   
   **Antonyms**: Could you attend personally, Will [person] speak for themselves
   
   **When to Use**: Use when asking someone to serve as a representative or delegate; appropriate in professional settings when principals cannot attend; shows respect by requesting rather than commanding
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when formal appointment rather than request is required; avoid if authorization procedures must be followed; skip when person lacks authority or knowledge to represent
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Would you be able to represent [person] at...
   - Could you attend on behalf of...
   - Would you mind speaking for [group] at...
   - Can you stand in for [person] at...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Requesting representation involves face work—you're asking for a favor and burdening the person with responsibility; modal "could/would" softens imposition; important to clarify scope and provide necessary information
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Accepting: "Yes, I'd be happy to represent [person/group]."
   - Declining: "I'm afraid I won't be able to—perhaps [alternative] could do it?"
   - Clarifying: "What specifically would I need to communicate on their behalf?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "Could you speak for me at the meeting tomorrow? I have a conflict."
   - "Can you represent our team at the presentation? You know the material best."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "Would you be willing to speak on behalf of the department at the university senate meeting?"
   - "Could you represent the organization at the conference? We believe you would be an excellent spokesperson."

1. **Functional Expression**: [Person] will be representing [party] in this matter (function: announcing representation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to formally announce or inform others that someone will serve as a representative
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct (clear statement of representation arrangement)
   
   **Synonyms**: [Person] will serve as representative for..., [Person] is authorized to act on behalf of..., [Person] will act as agent for...
   
   **Antonyms**: [Person] will be acting independently, [Person] will represent themselves
   
   **When to Use**: Use in legal proceedings, business negotiations, meetings, and formal contexts to establish who will serve as representative; important for clarity about authority and communication channels
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Unnecessary in casual contexts where representation is informal; avoid when representation status is already well-established; skip when temporary or situational representation doesn't need announcement
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - [Person] has been appointed to represent...
   - [Person] will serve on behalf of...
   - [Person] will be acting for [party] in...
   - [Person] is the designated representative for...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This announcement establishes communication protocol and authority structure; helps all parties know who to contact and who has decision-making power; face-neutral statement of organizational fact
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Understood. We'll direct all communications to [person]."
   - "Thank you for clarifying the representation."
   - "Will [person] have full authority to negotiate on [party]'s behalf?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "Sarah will be representing our department at the meeting since I can't make it."
   - "Just so you know, Mike will be speaking for the team at the presentation."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "Attorney Johnson will be representing the defendant in all proceedings related to this case."
   - "Ms. Chen has been appointed to represent the shareholders at the annual general meeting."

1. **Functional Expression**: I cannot speak on behalf of [person/organization] (function: declining to represent)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to clarify that one lacks authority or willingness to represent another party; establishes boundaries
   - *Formality*: semi-formal to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (clear boundary statement)
   
   **Synonyms**: I'm not authorized to speak for..., I can't represent [person]'s views, I'm not in a position to speak for..., I don't have authority to represent...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'm authorized to speak on behalf of..., I can represent [person]'s position
   
   **When to Use**: Use when asked to represent someone without authority, when views may not align with those you're being asked to represent, or when you need to clarify you're speaking only for yourself; crucial for avoiding misrepresentation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When you do have authority and should represent (avoiding responsibility); in casual contexts where formal representation isn't at stake; unnecessary when no one has asked you to represent others
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I can only speak for myself, not on behalf of...
   - I'm not authorized to represent [person/organization]
   - I cannot presume to speak on behalf of...
   - That's not my call to make on behalf of...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Important face-saving and legal protection expression; clarifies boundaries and prevents misunderstanding about whose views you're expressing; protects you from liability for unauthorized representation and protects principal from misrepresentation
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "I understand. Perhaps you could direct us to the appropriate representative?"
   - "That's fine—we'll contact [person/organization] directly."
   - "Thank you for clarifying. Who should we speak with?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "I can't really speak for the team on that—you should ask the manager."
   - "That's not my call to make on behalf of everyone. Let me check."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "I cannot speak on behalf of the board regarding that matter. I can only express my personal opinion."
   - "I am not authorized to represent the company's position on this issue. Please direct your inquiry to our legal department."

1. **Functional Expression**: Acting on behalf of [person/organization], I [action] (function: performing representative action)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to introduce an action taken in representative capacity; emphasizes active execution of duties
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct (clear statement of representative action)
   
   **Synonyms**: Representing [person], I..., As agent for [person], I..., In my capacity as representative, I..., On behalf of [person], I...
   
   **Antonyms**: Acting independently, On my own behalf, For myself
   
   **When to Use**: Use when taking action as a representative to clearly attribute the action to your representative capacity rather than personal capacity; common in legal documents, business transactions, official communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use for personal actions; avoid when representative status is already clear from context; unnecessary in casual contexts where formality isn't required
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - In my capacity as [role], acting on behalf of [person], I...
   - Acting as representative for [person], I hereby...
   - On behalf of and as agent for [person], I...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This expression provides legal clarity about capacity and attribution; distinguishes personal from representative actions; creates record of agency relationship; particularly important in binding commitments
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Acknowledged. We understand you're acting in representative capacity."
   - "Please confirm your authority to act on [person]'s behalf."
   - "Thank you. This will be attributed to [person/organization]."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "Acting for my parents, I'm accepting the delivery."
   - "I'm signing this on behalf of the homeowners association."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "Acting on behalf of the estate, I hereby accept service of process in this matter."
   - "As the duly authorized attorney-in-fact, acting on behalf of the principal, I execute this document."

1. **Functional Expression**: Please extend my [thanks/congratulations/condolences] to [person] on behalf of [group] (function: requesting transmission of sentiment)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to ask someone to convey collective sentiments to another party; creates representative transmission chain
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: indirect (polite request creates obligation on intermediary)
   
   **Synonyms**: Please convey [group]'s [sentiment] to..., Please tell [person] that [group]..., Please pass along [group]'s...
   
   **Antonyms**: I'll contact [person] directly, [Person] should hear from each of us individually
   
   **When to Use**: Use when a group's sentiments need to be conveyed through an intermediary; appropriate when direct contact isn't possible or when formal group acknowledgment is needed; shows respect through formal transmission
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When direct communication is better (more personal); avoid when intermediary may not convey message properly; skip when individual rather than collective sentiment is appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Please give [person] [group]'s regards/thanks
   - Please convey to [person] on behalf of [group]...
   - Please tell [person] that on behalf of [group], we...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Creates three-party communication structure; intermediary becomes temporary representative; face-saving through formal transmission; shows respect by using proper channels; collective attribution strengthens sentiment
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "I certainly will. I'll make sure [person] knows."
   - "Of course. I'll convey your message."
   - "I'll be happy to pass that along on your behalf."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "Please tell your mom thanks from all of us for the wonderful dinner!"
   - "Give Sarah our congratulations on the new baby when you see her!"
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "Please extend the board's sincere appreciation to Dr. Martinez on behalf of the entire organization."
   - "Kindly convey to the ambassador, on behalf of our government, our deepest condolences on this tragic loss."

1. **Functional Expression**: I speak for [everyone/the group/all of us] when I say... (function: asserting collective agreement)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to claim that one's statement represents group consensus; asserts representative authority for opinion expression
   - *Formality*: semi-formal
   - *Directness*: direct but presumptive (claims authority to represent group sentiment)
   
   **Synonyms**: Everyone would agree that..., We all feel that..., I think we can all agree that..., It's safe to say on behalf of everyone that...
   
   **Antonyms**: Speaking only for myself..., I can't speak for others but..., This is just my opinion...
   
   **When to Use**: Use when confident of genuine group consensus on a clear matter; appropriate when group has discussed and agreed; effective in building unity or expressing collective gratitude/concern
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when group consensus is unclear or disputed (can provoke "speak for yourself!" response); avoid when claiming authority you don't have; risky when presuming agreement that may not exist
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - I think I speak for everyone when I say...
   - I believe we all agree that...
   - On behalf of all of us, I think it's fair to say...
   - I'm sure everyone would agree that...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: This expression involves risk—you're claiming representative authority that may be contested; face-threatening if group members disagree; strengthens statement through collective backing but can backfire if presumptuous; hedge with "I think" softens claim
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - Agreement: "Absolutely—you speak for all of us."
   - Disagreement: "Well, you might speak for yourself, but I'm not sure everyone agrees."
   - Neutral: "I think most people would agree with that."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "I think I speak for everyone when I say this has been an awesome trip!"
   - "I'm pretty sure we all agree that we need a break right now."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "I believe I speak for the entire committee when I say that this proposal has our full support."
   - "I think it's fair to say, on behalf of all stakeholders, that transparency in this process is essential."

1. **Functional Expression**: In my capacity as [role], I represent [person/organization] (function: identifying official representative role)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to specify the official role or position that gives one authority to represent; clarifies basis of representation
   - *Formality*: formal
   - *Directness*: direct (explicit statement of role and authority)
   
   **Synonyms**: As [role], I act on behalf of..., In my official capacity as [role], I speak for..., Serving as [role], I represent...
   
   **Antonyms**: In my personal capacity, Speaking as an individual not as [role]
   
   **When to Use**: Use when it's important to clarify the official basis for your representative authority; essential in legal, governmental, and formal business contexts; distinguishes official from personal capacity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Unnecessary when role is already clear; avoid in casual contexts where formality isn't needed; skip when personal views rather than role-based representation is appropriate
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - In my official capacity as [role], I...
   - As the [role], I am authorized to represent...
   - Serving in my capacity as [role], I act for...
   - In my role as [role], I speak on behalf of...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Distinguishes role-based authority from personal action; protects individual from personal liability by attributing to role; clarifies scope and limitations of authority; particularly important when same person has multiple roles
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - "Thank you for clarifying your capacity."
   - "I understand you're speaking in your official capacity as [role]."
   - "Are you speaking as [role] or in a personal capacity?"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - "In my capacity as team captain, I'm here to represent the players' concerns."
   - "As class president, I'm speaking on behalf of the students."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - "In my capacity as executor of the estate, I represent the interests of all beneficiaries named in the will."
   - "As General Counsel, I am authorized to speak on behalf of the corporation regarding all legal matters."
