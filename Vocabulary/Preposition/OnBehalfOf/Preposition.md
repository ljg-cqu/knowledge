1. **Preposition**: on behalf of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Representation)* As the representative of; speaking or acting for someone; in the place of someone
   - *(Benefit)* For the benefit of; in support of; in the interest of someone or something
   
   **Synonyms**: for (simple), representing, in the name of, as a representative of, for the sake of (benefit sense)
   
   **Antonyms**: against, in opposition to, contrary to the interests of
   
   **When to Use**: Use "on behalf of" when speaking, acting, or making decisions as someone's representative, or when doing something for someone's benefit; extremely common in formal speeches, business correspondence, legal contexts, and official communications
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't confuse with "on the behalf of" (archaic, avoid); don't use when you mean "because of" (incorrect usage); avoid in casual contexts where "for" would suffice
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: on behalf of the company, on behalf of all employees, on behalf of my client, on behalf of the community
   - **Verb + prep**: speak on behalf of, act on behalf of, write on behalf of, accept on behalf of, thank on behalf of
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A (typically used with verbs)
   - **Noun + prep**: statement on behalf of, action on behalf of, petition on behalf of
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (preposition) + behalf (interest, benefit) + of (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "be" (by) + "healfe" (side) → Middle English "behalve" (by the side of, in the interest of) → Modern English "behalf" → phrasal preposition "on behalf of" emerged in 16th century meaning "representing" or "for the benefit of"
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm calling on behalf of my brother—he wanted me to ask you something.
   - Can you accept this award on behalf of the whole team?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - On behalf of the Board of Directors, I would like to extend our sincere gratitude for your contributions.
   - The attorney filed the lawsuit on behalf of her client yesterday afternoon.

1. **Preposition**: in the name of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Authority)* By the authority of; using someone's authority or power; as a representative with official authorization
   - *(Purpose)* For the sake of; in support of a cause, principle, or belief
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, representing, under the authority of, for the sake of, in support of
   
   **Antonyms**: against, in defiance of, without authority from
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in the name of" when invoking someone's authority or when acting for a cause or principle; common in legal, religious, ceremonial, and formal contexts; can emphasize official authorization more than "on behalf of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Can sound overly dramatic in casual contexts; avoid when simple representation without special authority is meant (use "on behalf of" or "for" instead); be cautious as it can imply abuse of authority in negative contexts
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in the name of the law, in the name of justice, in the name of the king, in the name of progress
   - **Verb + prep**: act in the name of, speak in the name of, arrest in the name of, commit crimes in the name of
   - **Adjective + prep**: done in the name of, justified in the name of
   - **Noun + prep**: actions in the name of, atrocities in the name of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + the (definite article) + name (authority, identity) + of (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "nama" (name, reputation) → Middle English "in the name of" as legal/religious formula invoking authority → Modern usage extends to both official representation and ideological justification
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Stop right there in the name of the law! (playful)
   - They're doing all this in the name of tradition.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The warrant was issued in the name of the Commonwealth of Virginia.
   - Many historical injustices were committed in the name of progress and civilization.

1. **Preposition**: representing (type: simple/gerund)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Agency)* Acting as an authorized agent or spokesperson for; speaking or acting with authority on behalf of someone or an organization
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, for, as a representative of, as an agent of, acting for
   
   **Antonyms**: opposing, against, unaffiliated with
   
   **When to Use**: Use "representing" to clearly indicate official agency or spokesperson status; common in legal, business, and political contexts; emphasizes active agency more than "on behalf of"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common in expressions of gratitude or ceremonial contexts (prefer "on behalf of"); avoid when the representation is informal or unofficial
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: representing the company, representing clients, representing interests, representing constituents
   - **Verb + prep**: speak representing, act representing, appear representing (often as participle: "speaking while representing")
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: attorney representing, delegate representing, spokesperson representing
   
   **Root Analysis**: re- (again, back) + present (bring before) + -ing (gerund/present participle)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin repraesentare (to show, present) → Old French representer → English "represent" → gerund form "representing" used prepositionally in legal and formal contexts by 18th century
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm representing my school at the competition tomorrow.
   - She's representing the neighborhood at the city council meeting.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The law firm is representing multiple plaintiffs in the class action lawsuit.
   - Ambassador Chen is representing China at the international climate summit.

1. **Preposition**: for (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Benefit/Representation)* In support of; in favor of; intended to benefit; as a representative of (among many other meanings)
   - *(Purpose)* With the purpose of helping or benefiting someone
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of (representation), in support of, in favor of, to benefit
   
   **Antonyms**: against, opposed to, contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "for" as a simpler, more versatile alternative to "on behalf of" in less formal contexts; appropriate across all registers when representation or benefit is clear from context
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Ambiguous without context (has many meanings); in highly formal or ceremonial situations, "on behalf of" is more explicitly respectful; in legal contexts, more specific terms like "representing" may be required
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: for the company, for my client, for the team, for the organization
   - **Verb + prep**: speak for, act for, work for, fight for, vote for
   - **Adjective + prep**: good for, responsible for, suitable for
   - **Noun + prep**: representative for, spokesman for, advocate for
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "for" (before, in front of, instead of, because of, for the purpose of)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English/Proto-Germanic "for" → one of the most versatile prepositions in English, retaining multiple ancient meanings including representation and benefit
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm speaking for everyone when I say we appreciate your help.
   - Can you sign for me? I can't make it to the office today.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Dr. Martinez will be speaking for the research team at tomorrow's conference.
   - The attorney appeared for the defendant at the preliminary hearing.

1. **Preposition**: as a representative of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Official Agency)* In the capacity of an authorized agent; acting with official status for an organization or person
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, representing, for, as an agent of, in the capacity of representative for
   
   **Antonyms**: as an opponent of, in personal capacity (not representing)
   
   **When to Use**: Use "as a representative of" when you need to explicitly clarify your official capacity or agency relationship; more verbose but clearer than "on behalf of" when status needs emphasis
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too wordy for most contexts (prefer "on behalf of" or "representing"); avoid in casual conversation; unnecessarily formal for routine communication
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: as a representative of the company, as a representative of the government, as a representative of the people
   - **Verb + prep**: serve as a representative of, act as a representative of, appear as a representative of
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: N/A (the phrase itself describes a role)
   
   **Root Analysis**: as (in the capacity of) + a (article) + representative (re- + present + -ative) + of (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Compound phrase using "representative" (Latin repraesentativus) → formal English construction emphasizing official capacity
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm here as a representative of the student council.
   - She attended the meeting as a representative of our neighborhood.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The ambassador spoke as a representative of the United Nations Security Council.
   - Dr. Thompson serves as a representative of the American Medical Association on this advisory board.

1. **Preposition**: in support of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Advocacy)* In favor of; backing; promoting; endorsing someone or something
   - *(Benefit)* For the benefit of; to help or assist
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of (benefit sense), for, in favor of, backing, promoting, endorsing
   
   **Antonyms**: against, in opposition to, opposing, undermining
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in support of" when emphasizing advocacy, endorsement, or active backing; stronger than simple representation; common in political, activism, and advocacy contexts
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not appropriate when neutral representation is meant (use "on behalf of" instead); avoid when you need to emphasize official agency (use "representing"); less common in business correspondence
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in support of the motion, in support of the candidate, in support of the cause, in support of reform
   - **Verb + prep**: speak in support of, vote in support of, campaign in support of, rally in support of
   - **Adjective + prep**: N/A
   - **Noun + prep**: testimony in support of, evidence in support of, demonstration in support of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + support (uphold, sustain) + of (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin supportare (to carry, sustain) → Old French supporter → English "support" → phrasal preposition "in support of" emphasizing active backing
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We're organizing a fundraiser in support of the local animal shelter.
   - She spoke in support of her friend's proposal.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The senator delivered a speech in support of the proposed healthcare legislation.
   - Several witnesses testified in support of the plaintiff's claims.

1. **Preposition**: acting for (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Agency)* Performing actions as someone's agent; doing something in someone else's place; serving as a substitute or representative
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of, representing, as a representative of, in place of, substituting for
   
   **Antonyms**: acting against, opposing, working contrary to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "acting for" when emphasizing the performance of actions as someone's agent; common in legal and business contexts; implies active execution of duties rather than just speaking
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common in ceremonial or gratitude expressions (prefer "on behalf of"); can be ambiguous without context; in very formal legal documents, "representing" may be preferred
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: acting for the client, acting for the principal, acting for the company, acting for the estate
   - **Verb + prep**: serve acting for, appear acting for, negotiate acting for
   - **Adjective + prep**: authorized acting for
   - **Noun + prep**: attorney acting for, agent acting for, trustee acting for
   
   **Root Analysis**: act (do, perform) + -ing (gerund) + for (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin actus (doing, performance) → Old French "acte" → English "act" → gerund phrase "acting for" developed in legal/business English emphasizing agency relationship
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm acting for my parents while they're out of town.
   - The manager is acting for the director during her absence.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The executor is acting for the deceased in settling the estate.
   - Counsel acting for the respondent has filed a motion to dismiss.

1. **Preposition**: in the interest(s) of (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Benefit)* For the benefit of; for the advantage of; to serve the welfare or advantage of someone or something
   - *(Purpose)* For the sake of; with the aim of promoting or protecting
   
   **Synonyms**: on behalf of (benefit sense), for the benefit of, for the sake of, to benefit, serving
   
   **Antonyms**: against the interests of, to the detriment of, harmful to
   
   **When to Use**: Use "in the interest(s) of" when emphasizing benefit, advantage, or welfare; can be singular ("interest") or plural ("interests"); common in legal, political, and ethical discussions
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Not used for simple representation or agency (use "on behalf of" instead); avoid when active advocacy is meant (prefer "in support of"); can sound bureaucratic if overused
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: in the interests of justice, in the interest of clarity, in the interests of all parties, in the public interest
   - **Verb + prep**: act in the interests of, work in the interest of, decide in the interests of
   - **Adjective + prep**: done in the interests of, necessary in the interest of
   - **Noun + prep**: action in the interests of, decision in the interest of
   
   **Root Analysis**: in (within) + the (article) + interest/interests (advantage, benefit) + of (preposition)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin interesse (to be between, make a difference) → Old French "interesse" → English "interest" → phrasal preposition "in the interest(s) of" emphasizing benefit or welfare
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - In the interests of fairness, let's split the cost evenly.
   - They decided to postpone the event in the interest of everyone's safety.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The court ruled in the interests of the minor child.
   - In the interest of transparency, all financial records will be made publicly available.
