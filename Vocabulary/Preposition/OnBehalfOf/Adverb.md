1. **Adverb**: representatively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that serves as a typical example of a group or class; in a manner that acts or speaks for others
   - Position: Usually mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: typically, characteristically, as a representative, exemplarily
   
   **Antonyms**: atypically, uncharacteristically, unusually
   
   **When to Use**: Use "representatively" when describing how someone acts as a representative or how something typifies a category; formal register; emphasizes manner of representation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Rare in casual speech (use "as a representative" or "typically" instead); can sound awkward; often clearer to rephrase
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act representatively
   - speak representatively
   - representatively selected
   - function representatively
   - serve representatively
   
   **Root Analysis**: represent (re- + present) + -ative (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin repraesentare → English "representative" → adverb form "representatively" (formal, somewhat rare)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - She spoke representatively for the whole group at the meeting.
   - The sample was chosen representatively from all regions.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The committee members were selected representatively to ensure all constituencies had voice in deliberations.
   - Data must be collected representatively to avoid sampling bias in research findings.

1. **Adverb**: officially (type: manner/sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a formal or authorized manner; with official authority or approval; as an official representative
   - *(Sentence adverb)* According to official sources or statements (used to qualify entire sentence)
   - Position: Front-position (sentence adverb), mid-position, or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: formally, authoritatively, in an official capacity, legitimately
   
   **Antonyms**: unofficially, informally, privately
   
   **When to Use**: Use "officially" when emphasizing formal authorization, public announcement, or acting in official capacity; suitable across registers when contrasting with unofficial status
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when informal or personal action is meant; avoid overuse as hedge (sounds bureaucratic)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - officially announced
   - officially represent
   - officially authorized
   - officially appointed
   - act officially on behalf of
   
   **Root Analysis**: official (offic[e] + -ial) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin officialis → English "official" + "-ly" → emphasizes formal, authorized manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Has it been officially announced yet, or is it still just a rumor?
   - She's officially representing the company at the conference.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The ambassador was officially accredited to represent her nation at the United Nations.
   - Officially, the policy has been implemented, though informal practices may persist.

1. **Adverb**: formally (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an official or ceremonious manner; according to established protocol or rules; in one's official capacity
   - Position: Usually mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: officially, ceremoniously, according to protocol, in due form
   
   **Antonyms**: informally, casually, unofficially
   
   **When to Use**: Use "formally" when emphasizing adherence to official procedures, protocol, or ceremonial manner; suitable across registers when contrasting with informal approach
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when casual or relaxed manner is meant; can imply stiffness or excessive ceremony if overused
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - formally represent
   - formally appointed
   - formally authorized
   - formally request
   - act formally on behalf of
   
   **Root Analysis**: formal (form + -al) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin formalis (relating to form) → English "formal" + "-ly" → emphasizes adherence to established form or protocol
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Do we need to formally request this, or can we just ask?
   - He formally represents the organization, but he's pretty laid-back about it.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The attorney was formally retained to represent the defendant in all legal proceedings.
   - The proposal must be formally submitted on behalf of the department by the deadline.

1. **Adverb**: duly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In accordance with what is required or appropriate; properly; in the correct or expected manner
   - Position: Usually mid-position before past participle (duly authorized, duly appointed)
   
   **Synonyms**: properly, appropriately, correctly, rightfully, legitimately
   
   **Antonyms**: improperly, incorrectly, inappropriately
   
   **When to Use**: Use "duly" in formal contexts when emphasizing that something was done properly or according to requirements; common before past participles; signals compliance with proper procedures
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for casual contexts (use "properly" or "correctly" instead); sounds archaic or legalistic in informal speech
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - duly authorized
   - duly appointed
   - duly elected
   - duly noted
   - duly represent
   
   **Root Analysis**: due (proper, owed) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "deu" (owed, proper) → English "due" + "-ly" → emphasizes proper or appropriate manner (formal register)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Your complaint has been duly noted and will be addressed.
   - The forms were duly signed and submitted on time.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The representatives were duly authorized to negotiate on behalf of their respective organizations.
   - The executor, duly appointed by the probate court, acts on behalf of the estate's beneficiaries.

1. **Adverb**: legitimately (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a way that conforms to the law, rules, or established standards; in a valid or authorized manner; with proper authority
   - Position: Usually mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: legally, lawfully, validly, rightfully, properly
   
   **Antonyms**: illegitimately, illegally, unlawfully, improperly
   
   **When to Use**: Use "legitimately" when emphasizing lawfulness, validity, or proper authorization; suitable across registers when emphasizing authentic authority or legal compliance
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when simple "legally" would be clearer; avoid when moral rather than legal legitimacy is meant (can be ambiguous)
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - legitimately represent
   - legitimately authorized
   - legitimately claim
   - legitimately act on behalf of
   - legitimately appointed
   
   **Root Analysis**: legitimate (legitim[ate]) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin legitimus (lawful) → English "legitimate" + "-ly" → emphasizes lawful or valid manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Can she legitimately speak for the whole group, or is that just her opinion?
   - He legitimately won the election—there was no fraud.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The attorney may legitimately act on behalf of clients within the scope of the retainer agreement.
   - Only those legitimately authorized by the board may represent the corporation in legal matters.

1. **Adverb**: collectively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - As a group; together as a whole; in a unified manner representing all members
   - Position: Usually end-position or front-position for emphasis
   
   **Synonyms**: together, jointly, as a group, as a whole, unitedly
   
   **Antonyms**: individually, separately, independently, alone
   
   **When to Use**: Use "collectively" when emphasizing group action or representation of all members together; suitable across registers when emphasizing unified representation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when individual rather than group action is meant; avoid when separate actions are being described
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - collectively represent
   - collectively decide
   - act collectively
   - collectively responsible
   - speak collectively on behalf of
   
   **Root Analysis**: collect (col- + lect) + -ive (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin collectivus (gathered together) → English "collective" + "-ly" → emphasizes group or unified manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - We collectively decided to donate the money to charity.
   - The students are collectively represented by the class president.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The board members, acting collectively on behalf of shareholders, approved the merger proposal.
   - These nations collectively represent over half the world's population at international forums.

1. **Adverb**: jointly (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Together with one or more others; in cooperation; sharing representation or responsibility with others
   - Position: Usually mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: together, collectively, in cooperation, mutually, in partnership
   
   **Antonyms**: separately, independently, individually, alone
   
   **When to Use**: Use "jointly" when emphasizing shared action, responsibility, or representation among specific parties; suitable across registers; emphasizes partnership more than "collectively"
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when separate or independent action is meant; avoid when singular representation without partners is described
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - jointly represent
   - jointly responsible
   - act jointly on behalf of
   - jointly appointed
   - jointly authorized
   
   **Root Analysis**: joint (connection, union) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Old French "joint" (joined) → English "joint" + "-ly" → emphasizes shared or cooperative manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - They're jointly organizing the event on behalf of both departments.
   - We jointly own the property, so we both need to sign.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The attorneys jointly represent the co-defendants in the civil litigation.
   - Both agencies will jointly administer the program on behalf of eligible participants.

1. **Adverb**: vicariously (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Through another person; by means of a substitute or representative; experienced in the imagination through the actions of another
   - Position: Usually mid-position or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: indirectly, through another, by proxy, second-hand
   
   **Antonyms**: directly, personally, first-hand, immediately
   
   **When to Use**: Use "vicariously" when describing action or experience through another person; emphasizes indirect or substitute nature; suitable in formal contexts and when describing psychological experience
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Less common for simple representation (use "on behalf of" phrase instead); can sound overly formal in casual contexts
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act vicariously
   - vicariously liable
   - experience vicariously
   - vicariously represent
   - live vicariously through
   
   **Root Analysis**: vicari(ous) (vicari[a] + -ous) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin vicarius (substitute, deputy) → English "vicarious" + "-ly" → emphasizes substitute or indirect manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'm living vicariously through your travel photos!
   - Parents sometimes try to live vicariously through their kids' achievements.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The corporation may be held vicariously liable for actions taken by employees on behalf of the company.
   - The principal is vicariously responsible for acts performed by the agent within the scope of authority.

1. **Adverb**: ceremonially (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In a formal or ceremonious manner; with ritual or official formality; in accordance with ceremony or protocol
   - Position: Usually end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: formally, ritually, solemnly, officially
   
   **Antonyms**: informally, casually, simply
   
   **When to Use**: Use "ceremonially" when emphasizing the ritual, formal, or symbolic nature of representation or action; suitable in formal contexts describing official events
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Too formal for most everyday contexts; avoid when practical rather than symbolic action is meant; implies pomp and ritual
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - ceremonially represent
   - ceremonially appointed
   - ceremonially present
   - act ceremonially on behalf of
   - ceremonially installed
   
   **Root Analysis**: ceremony (ceremon[y]) + -ial (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin caerimonia (religious rite) → English "ceremony" + "-ial" + "-ly" → emphasizes ritual or formal manner
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The mayor ceremonially handed over the keys to the city.
   - He was ceremonially inducted into the hall of fame.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The ambassador ceremonially presented her credentials, formally representing her nation to the host government.
   - The chief justice ceremonially administered the oath of office on behalf of the nation's citizens.

1. **Adverb**: personally (type: manner/sentence)
   **Meaning**: 
   - By oneself without the agency or intervention of another; in person rather than through a representative
   - *(Sense 2)* As far as oneself is concerned; from one's own perspective (sentence adverb)
   - Position: Front-position (sentence adverb), mid-position, or end-position
   
   **Synonyms**: in person, directly, individually, oneself
   
   **Antonyms**: through a representative, on behalf of another, vicariously, by proxy
   
   **When to Use**: Use "personally" when contrasting direct action with representation by others; emphasizes absence of intermediaries; suitable across all registers
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't use when representation through others is meant; creates opposite meaning to "on behalf of"
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - personally responsible
   - appear personally
   - personally liable
   - act personally (not through representative)
   - personally handle
   
   **Root Analysis**: person (individual) + -al (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: Latin persona (person, individual) → English "personal" + "-ly" → emphasizes direct individual action without intermediary
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - I'll personally make sure this gets done—you can count on me.
   - Do I need to appear personally, or can someone represent me?
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The defendant must appear personally in court rather than being represented solely by counsel.
   - Personally, I support the initiative, though I cannot speak on behalf of the entire committee.
