# Source - Multi-Word Expressions

1. **Expression**: primary source (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - An original document, dataset, or account that provides first-hand evidence about a topic (e.g., raw data, official records, interviews).
   - *Fixedness*: strong (core research and history term)
   
   **Synonyms**: original source, first-hand source, original document, direct evidence
   
   **Antonyms**: secondary source, summary article, commentary, review
   
   **When to Use**: Talking about materials that are closest to the event or phenomenon itselfâ€”things you could, in principle, check directly.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For textbooks, explainers, or articles that interpret earlier work; those are secondary sources.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - primary source document
   - primary data source
   - primary historical source
   - key primary source
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Primary" (first, original) and "source" (origin) naturally combine to contrast with later, derivative materials.
   
   **Root Analysis**: primary (Latin *primarius* = first in rank) + source (origin, starting point)
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Whenever you can, read the primary source instead of relying only on blog posts.
   - Interview recordings and raw survey files are primary sources for this project.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The analysis relies on primary sources, including court records and contemporaneous news coverage.
   - Primary source documents were digitized and annotated before coding.

1. **Expression**: secondary source (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A text or resource that discusses, interprets, or summarizes primary sources rather than providing original evidence.
   - *Fixedness*: strong (standard research term)
   
   **Synonyms**: review article, commentary, textbook, overview
   
   **Antonyms**: primary source, original data, first-hand account
   
   **When to Use**: Talking about literature that helps you understand primary material, such as textbooks, literature reviews, and syntheses.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For raw data, original documents, or direct measurements.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - secondary source material
   - secondary literature
   - secondary historical source
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Secondary" (second-level) signals that the source stands one step away from the original evidence.
   
   **Root Analysis**: secondary (Latin *secundarius* = following, second) + source.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - This blog post is a secondary source; it just explains somebody elses research.
   - For a quick overview, secondary sources are fineâ€”but check the primary work too.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Secondary sources were used to contextualize findings from primary administrative data.
   - The review synthesizes secondary source material from multiple disciplines.

1. **Expression**: single source of truth (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A single, authoritative dataset or system that is treated as the definitive source for a particular set of facts.
   - *Fixedness*: strong in data and systems design
   
   **Synonyms**: canonical source, authoritative source, master record, system of record
   
   **Antonyms**: multiple conflicting sources, duplicated records, fragmented sources
   
   **When to Use**: Describing architectures or processes where one database, log, or document is the official reference that others must match.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When multiple sources intentionally coexist for cross-checking or redundancy.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - establish a single source of truth
   - act as the single source of truth
   - no single source of truth
   - system of record as source of truth
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Single" + "source" + "truth" emphasizes uniqueness, origin, and correctness in one compact expression.
   
   **Root Analysis**: single (one) + source (origin) + truth (state of being true).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Right now we dont have a single source of truth for user dataâ€”everythings duplicated.
   - The data warehouse is supposed to be our single source of truth for metrics.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The data governance program aims to designate a single source of truth for each critical business entity.
   - Without a single source of truth, reconciliation between overlapping datasets becomes costly and error-prone.

1. **Expression**: off the record (type: idiom/fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *Literal/Journalistic*: Said to a journalist or listener with the expectation that it will not be published or directly attributed.
   - *Figurative*: Shared in confidence, for background only, not for public use.
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: in confidence, not for publication, background only, unofficially
   
   **Antonyms**: on the record, for attribution, publicly, officially
   
   **When to Use**: When someone is willing to share information only if their identity or exact words are kept out of public outputs.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In formal academic writing (prefer explicit confidentiality language), or when no confidentiality was agreed.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - speak off the record
   - strictly off the record
   - keep this off the record
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: The "record" is the official written or published account; "off the record" means "outside that official record."
   
   **Root Analysis**: off (not on) + the record (official account).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He told me, "This is off the record, but the deal is almost done."
   - We can talk off the record if you dont want your name used.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Several officials provided off-the-record background that informed the analysis but is not quoted directly.
   - Off-the-record conversations raised concerns that could not be fully documented.

1. **Expression**: on the record (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Said with the understanding that it may be quoted, attributed, and included in official or public documents.
   - *Fixedness*: strong, paired with "off the record"
   
   **Synonyms**: for attribution, publicly, officially, on the record as saying
   
   **Antonyms**: off the record, background only, unattributable, anonymous
   
   **When to Use**: Clarifying that statements may be used as sourced quotations, especially in interviews or negotiations.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When confidentiality is required, or when the interaction is clearly private.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - go on the record
   - speak on the record
   - put this on the record
   - stay on the record
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "On the record" mirrors legal and journalistic practice where official minutes or recordings form the record.
   
   **Root Analysis**: on (attached to) + the record (official account).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Are you willing to go on the record with that statement?
   - She insisted that her comments stay on the record.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Only on-the-record statements are quoted or attributed in the final report.
   - The spokesperson declined to comment on the record about the leaked documents.

1. **Expression**: chain of custody (type: collocation)
   **Meaning**: 
   - A documented sequence showing who has handled data, evidence, or samples, and when, from original source to final use.
   - *Fixedness*: strong in legal, forensic, and data contexts
   
   **Synonyms**: custody trail, handling record, provenance chain, audit trail
   
   **Antonyms**: undocumented handling, broken chain, unknown custody
   
   **When to Use**: Discussing rigorous tracking of sensitive sources such as forensic evidence, logs, or regulated data.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For casual file sharing where formal tracking is not needed.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - maintain chain of custody
   - break the chain of custody
   - chain-of-custody documentation
   - chain-of-custody log
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Chain" emphasizes linked steps; "custody" emphasizes responsibility for the source material.
   
   **Root Analysis**: chain (series of connected links) + custody (guardianship, care).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - If we cant show a clear chain of custody, people wont trust the files.
   - Every time you copy the data, you should document it to keep the chain of custody.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Forensic data must maintain a documented chain of custody from collection to analysis.
   - The audit found gaps in the chain of custody for several key source documents.

1. **Expression**: paper trail (type: idiom)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal: A series of documents that record actions and decisions over time.
   - Figurative: Any sequence of records (including digital logs) that reveals the true origin and path of information or decisions.
   - *Fixedness*: strong
   
   **Synonyms**: documentary record, audit trail, record trail, evidence trail
   
   **Antonyms**: no record, undocumented process, missing documentation
   
   **When to Use**: Emphasizing that actions or decisions can be traced back through documents, emails, or logs.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: For purely oral traditions or when documentation does not exist.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - leave a paper trail
   - follow the paper trail
   - no paper trail
   - clear paper trail
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For idioms*: Historically, physical paper was the main record medium; a "trail" suggests following documents back to the source.
   
   **Root Analysis**: paper (written record) + trail (path to be followed).
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Theres a long paper trail showing how this decision was made.
   - Always assume your emails are part of the paper trail.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The investigation reconstructed the paper trail using archived correspondence and meeting minutes.
   - A complete paper trail from source data to published figures improves accountability.

1. **Expression**: cite your sources (type: fixed phrase)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Literal: Provide formal references for the information or ideas you use.
   - Figurative: Be transparent about where your claims come from.
   - *Fixedness*: strong in education and research communication
   
   **Synonyms**: provide references, acknowledge sources, show your sources
   
   **Antonyms**: hide your sources, fail to cite, omit references
   
   **When to Use**: As instruction or reminder in academic, journalistic, or analytical contexts.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: In highly informal conversation where formal referencing is not expected.
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - please cite your sources
   - always cite your sources
   - cite all your sources
   
   **Why This Partnership**: 
   - *For collocations*: "Cite" and "sources" occur together so frequently in education that the phrase functions as a teaching slogan.
   
   **Root Analysis**: cite (to reference) + your (possessive) + sources.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - My teacher keeps writing "cite your sources" in the margin of my essays.
   - Online, people often say "source?" as a shorthand for "please cite your sources."
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The course guidelines repeatedly emphasize: cite your sources, even when you paraphrase.
   - Students are required to cite their sources using the departments preferred citation style.
