1. **Preposition**: before (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Earlier than a particular time or event.
   - *(Abstract)* In preparation for something that will happen later.
   
   **Synonyms**: prior to, ahead of, in advance of
   
   **Antonyms**: after, following, subsequent to
   
   **When to Use**: Talking about actions such as eating, stretching, or warming up that should happen before exercise.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Do not use for spatial meaning where "in front of" is needed; avoid in very formal writing where "prior to" might be preferred.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: before a workout; before training; before your run
   - **Verb + prep**: stretch before running; eat lightly before exercising
   - **Noun + prep**: routine before bed; snack before practice
   
   **Root Analysis**: be- (by, near) + fore (earlier, in front)
   
   **Etymology**: Old English "beforan" (in front of, earlier than); used for both time and position.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Try to drink some water before your workout.
   - I always feel stiff if I don’t warm up before I lift.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants completed a standardized warm-up before each testing session.
   - Light carbohydrate intake before intense exercise can improve performance.

1. **Preposition**: after (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Later than a particular time or event.
   - *(Abstract)* As a result or consequence of something that has finished.
   
   **Synonyms**: following, once, subsequent to
   
   **Antonyms**: before, prior to, ahead of
   
   **When to Use**: Describing recovery, stretching, eating, or rest that occurs after a workout.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Do not use when you need a cause–effect connector (prefer "because of" or "due to"); avoid for spatial meaning where "behind" is correct.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: after training; after your run; after the session
   - **Verb + prep**: cool down after exercise; stretch after lifting
   - **Noun + prep**: soreness after workouts; fatigue after intervals
   
   **Root Analysis**: Old English "æfter" (following, in pursuit of)
   
   **Etymology**: Germanic origin; widely used for time and sequence.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Make sure you eat something after a long run.
   - I like to take a hot shower after I work out.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Muscle soreness peaked 24 hours after the initial training session.
   - Participants reported feeling more relaxed after completing the yoga class.

1. **Preposition**: during (type: simple)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* Throughout the period of time when something is happening.
   
   **Synonyms**: in, throughout, over the course of
   
   **Antonyms**: before (for earlier), after (for later)
   
   **When to Use**: Referring to actions or sensations that happen in the middle of a workout, such as breathing, pain, or drinking water.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid for very brief, single moments (use "at" or "when"); do not use in place of "for" when you mean duration.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: during exercise; during the class; during the run
   - **Verb + prep**: hydrate during training; breathe steadily during each set
   - **Noun + prep**: pain during movement; heart rate during intervals
   
   **Root Analysis**: from "dur" (to last, endure) + -ing (present participle suffix)
   
   **Etymology**: From Middle English "during" meaning "lasting"; later used as a preposition meaning "throughout a period."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Sip water during your workout, not just at the end.
   - If you feel sharp pain during a set, stop immediately.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Heart rate was recorded continuously during the treadmill test.
   - Participants reported perceived exertion during each exercise bout.

1. **Preposition**: throughout (type: compound)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Temporal)* During the whole period of time from beginning to end.
   - *(Spatial)* In every part of a place or space.
   
   **Synonyms**: all through, across, from start to finish, over the whole of
   
   **Antonyms**: at one point, briefly, locally
   
   **When to Use**: Describing breathing, posture, or effort that should stay consistent all through a movement or training cycle.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when referring to a single moment or specific repetition; do not use where "between" or "among" is needed.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: throughout the workout; throughout the program; throughout the movement
   - **Verb + prep**: maintain form throughout; stay engaged throughout
   - **Noun + prep**: changes throughout the season; soreness throughout the week
   
   **Root Analysis**: through (from one side to the other) + out (completely)
   
   **Etymology**: Middle English compound meaning "through and out the other side"; used for both time and space.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Try to keep your core tight throughout the whole exercise.
   - She stayed focused throughout the entire class.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Postural alignment was monitored throughout each lifting session.
   - Adherence remained high throughout the twelve-week training period.

1. **Preposition**: along with (type: phrasal)
   **Meaning**: 
   - *(Abstract)* In addition to; together with someone or something.
   
   **Synonyms**: together with, in combination with, as well as
   
   **Antonyms**: apart from, excluding, instead of
   
   **When to Use**: Explaining combined elements of a program, such as strength work along with cardio, or stretching along with breathing exercises.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Avoid when you mean sequence instead of addition (use "after" or "then"); do not use in very formal mathematics where "+" or "and" is clearer.
   
   **Common Patterns**: 
   - **Prepositional phrases**: strength training along with cardio; stretching along with breathing work
   - **Verb + prep**: train along with friends; progress along with the group
   - **Noun + prep**: improvements along with side effects; benefits along with risks
   
   **Root Analysis**: along (together, in company) + with (accompanied by)
   
   **Etymology**: From Old English "andlang" (extended, continuous) + "with"; "along with" became a fixed phrase meaning "in addition to."
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - You should do some strength work along with your running.
   - I started tracking my sleep along with my workouts.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Participants completed mobility exercises along with their aerobic training.
   - Nutritional guidance was provided along with the exercise intervention.
