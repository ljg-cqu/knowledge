1. **Adverb**: instinctively (type: manner)
   **Meaning**: 
   - In an instinctive manner; driven by innate impulse rather than conscious thought or learning; naturally and automatically without prior experience.
   - *Position*: Usually mid-position (before main verb) or end-position.
   
   **Synonyms**: innately, naturally, automatically, intuitively, reflexively
   
   **Antonyms**: deliberately, consciously, thoughtfully, by learning
   
   **When to Use**: Describing innate animal behaviors, discussing genetic programming, explaining unlearned responses, contrasting with learned behaviors.
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When describing learned or conditioned behaviors (use "habitually" or "through training" instead).
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - act instinctively
   - react instinctively
   - respond instinctively
   - instinctively know
   - behave instinctively
   
   **Root Analysis**: instinct (from Latin in- + stinct = goaded) + -ive + -ly
   
   **Etymology**: Latin → from 'instinct' (Latin 'instinctus' = impulse) + adverbial suffix. Used in behavioral contexts since the 18th century.
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Baby birds instinctively open their mouths when they sense their parents nearby.
   - Cats instinctively know how to hunt even if they've never been taught.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Neonates respond instinctively to specific releasing stimuli without prior exposure.
   - Migration routes are followed instinctively, encoded genetically rather than transmitted culturally.

1. **Adverb**: seasonally (type: time/frequency)
   **Meaning**: 
   - At particular times of the year; in a way that varies with or depends on the seasons; periodically according to seasonal cycles.
   - *Position*: Usually end-position or front-position for emphasis.

   **Antonyms**: year-round, constantly, continuously, permanently

   **When NOT to Use**: For random or irregular occurrences (use "occasionally" instead), or year-round behaviors.
   
   **Common Phrases**: 
   - migrate seasonally
   - breed seasonally
   - vary seasonally
   - available seasonally
   - change seasonally

   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Many birds migrate seasonally, flying south for winter and north for summer.
