1. **Original Expression**: The investigation uncovered systematic violations.  
   **Context**: News article reporting investigation findings
   
   **Core Intent**: To report that an investigation revealed serious wrongdoing
   
   **Casual**: The investigation found they'd been breaking the rules big time.  
   *Characteristics*: Informal phrasal verb "found," colloquial intensifier "big time"  
   *Use when*: Casual news discussions, social media, conversation with friends
   
   **Semi-Formal**: The investigation revealed serious rule violations.  
   *Characteristics*: Neutral verb "revealed," standard adjective "serious"  
   *Use when*: Mainstream news reporting, general audience publications, broadcast news
   
   **Formal**: The investigation uncovered systematic violations of regulatory protocols.  
   *Characteristics*: Precise verb "uncovered," technical language "systematic violations," formal noun "protocols"  
   *Use when*: Official reports, quality newspapers, investigative journalism, legal contexts
   
   **Humorous**: The investigation found they'd been playing fast and loose with the rules.  
   *Characteristics*: Idiomatic expression "playing fast and loose" adds lighthearted tone  
   *Use when*: Opinion columns, satirical news, informal commentary  
   *Avoid when*: Serious wrongdoing involving victims, tragedy, or significant harm
   
   **Lively**: The investigation blew the lid off years of rule-breaking!  
   *Characteristics*: Dramatic phrasal verb "blew the lid off," exclamation mark for emphasis  
   *Use when*: Attention-grabbing headlines, social media posts, engaging storytelling
   
   **Empathetic**: The investigation uncovered violations that affected many people.  
   *Characteristics*: Adds human impact "affected many people"  
   *Use when*: Stories with victims, community impact reporting, human interest angles
   
   **Register Selection Guide**:
   - *Power Distance*: Formal for official/legal contexts; casual among peers
   - *Social Distance*: Formal for unknown audiences; casual for familiar readers
   - *Gravity of Situation*: Formal for serious wrongdoing; lighter registers for minor issues
   - *Cultural Context*: American journalism allows more variation than British
   - *Medium*: Print traditionally more formal; social media more casual
   
   **Common Mistakes**:
   - Using casual language in serious investigative pieces (undermines credibility)
   - Being too formal in accessible news (alienates general audience)
   - Mixing registers within single article (sounds inconsistent)
   
   **Cultural Notes**: British journalism typically maintains more formal register than American; tabloids vs. quality papers have distinct register expectations
   
   **Context Examples**:
   - **Situation 1**: Major newspaper investigative report → **Appropriate Variant**: Formal (establishes authority and seriousness)
   - **Situation 2**: Social media news summary → **Appropriate Variant**: Casual or Lively (engages scrolling audience)
   - **Situation 3**: Local community paper with affected residents → **Appropriate Variant**: Semi-Formal with Empathetic elements
