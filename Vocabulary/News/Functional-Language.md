1. **Functional Expression**: According to [source]... (function: attributing information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to cite sources and attribute information in news reporting
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (distances reporter from claim)
   
   **Synonyms**: As stated by [source], [Source] reports that, Per [source], [Source] says
   
   **Antonyms**: In my opinion, I believe (direct attribution to reporter)
   
   **When to Use**: Attributing information to sources, citing authorities, maintaining objectivity
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Don't overuse with same source repeatedly; avoid for common knowledge
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - According to reports...
   - According to sources familiar with...
   - According to officials...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Essential for journalistic objectivity; distances reporter from truth claims while allowing reporting of unverified information
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [Continuation with attributed information]
   - [Additional context or verification]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - According to my sources, the deal's almost done.
   - According to the police, the suspect was arrested yesterday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - According to government officials, the policy will take effect in the first quarter.
   - According to internal documents obtained by this publication, the company knew about the defects.

1. **Functional Expression**: [Source] confirmed that... (function: verifying information)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to indicate that information has been verified by authoritative source
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: direct
   
   **Synonyms**: [Source] verified that, [Source] corroborated that, [Source] authenticated that
   
   **Antonyms**: [Source] denied that, [Source] disputed that, [Source] questioned
   
   **When to Use**: Reporting verified facts, establishing credibility, multiple-source confirmation
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Unverified information (use "claimed" or "alleged"); single unconfirmed source
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - Multiple sources confirmed...
   - Officials have confirmed...
   - Independently confirmed...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Signals higher confidence level than "alleged" or "reported"; implies verification process
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [Specific confirmed details]
   - [Additional verification or context]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - The company confirmed that they're closing the plant.
   - Police confirmed the suspect was in custody.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Multiple independent sources confirmed that negotiations have reached an advanced stage.
   - The administration confirmed that the secretary will resign effective immediately.

1. **Functional Expression**: [X] allegedly [did Y] (function: reporting unproven claims)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to report accusations or claims that have not been proven
   - *Formality*: formal to neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (hedges truth claim)
   
   **Synonyms**: [X] reportedly [did Y], [X] is accused of [Y-ing], [X] purportedly [did Y]
   
   **Antonyms**: [X] definitively [did Y], [X] provably [did Y]
   
   **When to Use**: Reporting accusations, legal cases, unverified claims; legal protection against libel
   
   **When NOT to Use**: Confirmed facts; be careful not to soften serious established wrongdoing
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - allegedly involved in
   - allegedly committed
   - allegedly occurred
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Critical for legal protection; maintains presumption of innocence; distances news org from unproven claims
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [Follow with attribution or further details]
   - [Note that allegations have not been proven]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - He allegedly stole company funds, but nothing's been proven.
   - The incident allegedly happened last Tuesday.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - The official allegedly accepted bribes from contractors over a five-year period.
   - The company allegedly violated environmental regulations at multiple facilities.

1. **Functional Expression**: Sources familiar with [X] told [news org] that... (function: citing confidential sources)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to attribute information from anonymous sources while indicating their relevant knowledge
   - *Formality*: neutral
   - *Directness*: indirect (anonymous attribution)
   
   **Synonyms**: Officials with knowledge of, People briefed on, Those with direct knowledge
   
   **Antonyms**: Named officials stated, On-the-record sources confirmed
   
   **When to Use**: Confidential information, protecting sources, sensitive matters
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When on-record attribution is possible; overreliance weakens credibility
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - People familiar with the matter
   - Officials with knowledge of
   - Sources with direct knowledge
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Balances source protection with credibility; "familiar with" indicates relevant knowledge without revealing identity
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [Information from anonymous sources]
   - [Additional verification or context]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - Sources familiar with the talks said a deal is close.
   - People with knowledge of the situation told us they expect changes soon.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - Sources familiar with the negotiations told The Times that discussions have intensified in recent days.
   - Officials with knowledge of the internal deliberations indicated that significant disagreements remain.

1. **Functional Expression**: It remains unclear whether/how... (function: noting information gaps)
   **Meaning**: 
   - Used to acknowledge lack of information or unresolved questions
   - *Formality*: neutral to formal
   - *Directness*: direct (transparent about limits)
   
   **Synonyms**: It is unknown whether, Questions remain about, Uncertainty persists regarding
   
   **Antonyms**: It is clear that, Evidence confirms, Sources have established
   
   **When to Use**: Acknowledging information gaps, noting unresolved questions, maintaining transparency
   
   **When NOT to Use**: When information is actually available but unreported
   
   **Common Variations**: 
   - It is unclear...
   - It remains to be seen...
   - Questions remain about...
   
   **Pragmatic Notes**: Demonstrates journalistic honesty about reporting limitations; maintains credibility by not claiming certainty
   
   **Response Patterns**: 
   - [What is known]
   - [What efforts were made to clarify]
   
   **Examples [Casual]**: 
   - It remains unclear whether they'll accept the offer.
   - It's unclear how this will affect the timeline.
   
   **Examples [Formal]**: 
   - It remains unclear whether the administration will pursue regulatory action or legislative solutions.
   - Questions remain about how the organization will address systematic deficiencies identified in the report.
