## Glossary

- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Regulations and processes to detect and prevent money laundering, including transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting (SARs), customer due diligence (KYC), required by FATF standards and enforced by FinCEN (US), EU authorities, national regulators.
- **Chainalysis**: Blockchain intelligence company providing transaction monitoring, risk scoring, investigation tools for cryptocurrency AML compliance, 90%+ market penetration at large exchanges, $100K-$500K/year typical pricing.
- **False Negative**: Suspicious transaction incorrectly classified as legitimate (missed detection), enables illicit fund flows, measured through red team exercises and regulatory audits, target <5% rate.
- **False Positive**: Legitimate transaction incorrectly flagged as suspicious, overwhelms compliance teams (60-80% of alerts typical), causes user friction if blocking applied, target <20% rate through ML and behavior profiling.
- **FATF Travel Rule**: Financial Action Task Force Recommendation 16 requiring customer information exchange for transactions >$1,000, implemented in 85 of 117 jurisdictions, mandates <5s latency for real-time IVMS101 protocol data exchange between VASPs.
- **FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)**: US Treasury bureau enforcing Bank Secrecy Act, requires Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) filing within 30 days of detection, 1,000-3,000 SARs/year typical for mid-tier exchange, penalties $4.3B (Binance), $100M (BitMEX) for violations.
- **IVMS101 (InterVASP Messaging Standard)**: Technical protocol for Travel Rule compliance, defines customer data format and communication between Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs), enables <5s transaction processing vs 5-10min manual exchange.
- **Mixer/Tumbler**: Service obfuscating cryptocurrency transaction history by pooling funds from multiple users and redistributing, breaks on-chain traceability, flagged as high-risk by Chainalysis/Elliptic, detection via graph analysis and statistical fingerprints.
- **OFAC SDN (Office of Foreign Assets Control Specially Designated Nationals)**: US Treasury list of 10K+ sanctioned individuals and entities, mandatory 100% screening for all exchange transactions <1s latency, blocking required with reporting to OFAC within 10 days.
- **Peel Chain**: Money laundering technique splitting funds into progressively smaller amounts across multiple addresses, creates "peel" pattern in blockchain graph, detected via graph analysis and statistical clustering, common in darknet marketplace withdrawals.
- **SAR (Suspicious Activity Report)**: Regulatory filing to FinCEN (US) or equivalent authority documenting suspected money laundering or fraud, required within 30 days of detection, 1,000-3,000/year typical, $200-$500 analyst cost per filing, criminal penalties for late/missing reports.
- **TPS (Transactions Per Second)**: Throughput measure for transaction monitoring system, current 1K-5K TPS insufficient for 100K-500K daily transactions requiring 10K-50K TPS peak capacity, determines infrastructure sizing (compute, storage, network).
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: FATF terminology for cryptocurrency exchanges, wallet providers, custodians subject to Travel Rule and AML regulations, 85 of 117 jurisdictions require VASP registration and compliance programs.
