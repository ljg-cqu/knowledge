## Glossary

- **Cross-collateralization**: Practice of using Asset Type A to back liabilities in Asset Type B (e.g., using Bitcoin reserves to cover Ethereum withdrawal obligations), hidden risk in traditional PoR that only verifies total asset amounts.
- **Fractional reserve**: Banking practice of holding reserves less than 100% of liabilities, illegal for cryptocurrency exchanges under emerging regulations (US GENIUS Act, EU MiCAR), enabled by audit gaps in quarterly snapshot PoR.
- **GENIUS Act**: US legislation (signed July 18, 2025) providing stablecoin regulatory framework: 1:1 backing with liquid assets, regular audits, transparency reports mandatory for issuers.
- **Merkle tree**: Cryptographic data structure enabling efficient verification that specific data (user balance) is included in larger dataset (all user balances) without revealing entire dataset, used in PoR to prove individual account inclusion.
- **MiCAR (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU regulatory framework operational December 2024, mandates consumer protection, fund segregation, transparency protocols for crypto asset service providers, transitional period until mid-2026 for existing firms.
- **Oracle (blockchain)**: External data provider supplying off-chain information (e.g., bank account reserves, audit attestations) to on-chain smart contracts, used in continuous PoR systems to bridge real-world asset verification with blockchain transparency.
- **Proof of Reserves (PoR)**: Cryptographic attestation proving cryptocurrency exchange holds 100% of user-claimed assets by linking on-chain wallet addresses to off-chain liability databases, traditionally quarterly snapshots, evolving to continuous real-time verification.
- **Zero-knowledge proof**: Cryptographic method proving statement truth (e.g., user balance included in total) without revealing underlying data (specific balance amount or other users' data), preserves privacy in public PoR attestations.
