## Glossary

- **Base Fee**: Minimum fee per gas unit required for transaction inclusion in Ethereum block, dynamically adjusted based on network congestion (EIP-1559).
- **DeFi (Decentralized Finance)**: Financial applications on blockchain (lending, swaps, yield farming) typically requiring complex smart contract interactions with higher gas costs.
- **Gas**: Unit measuring computational work required to execute transactions on Ethereum-like blockchains; more complex operations require more gas.
- **Gas Limit**: Maximum gas units a user is willing to spend on a transaction; setting too low causes transaction failure, too high wastes funds.
- **Gwei**: Denomination of Ethereum (1 ETH = 1 billion gwei), commonly used to express gas prices (e.g., 20 gwei = 0.00000002 ETH per gas unit).
- **Layer 2 (L2)**: Scaling solutions built on top of Layer 1 blockchains (Ethereum) that process transactions off-chain and batch results to mainnet, reducing fees 90-99%.
- **MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)**: Profit extracted by block producers through transaction ordering, front-running, or sandwich attacks, often causing gas fee spikes.
- **Priority Fee (Tip)**: Additional fee users pay miners/validators for faster transaction inclusion, separate from base fee (EIP-1559).
- **Slippage**: Difference between expected and executed trade price, often caused by MEV or market volatility, sometimes correlated with gas fee competition.
