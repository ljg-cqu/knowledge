## Glossary

- **CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission)**: US federal agency regulating commodity futures and options markets, claims jurisdiction over cryptocurrency derivatives, enforcement actions against unregistered exchanges.
- **FATF (Financial Action Task Force)**: International intergovernmental organization setting standards for combating money laundering and terrorist financing, 117 member jurisdictions, Travel Rule originates from FATF Recommendation 16.
- **FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)**: US Treasury bureau enforcing Bank Secrecy Act, requires money services businesses (including crypto exchanges) to register, implement AML programs, file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs).
- **IVMS101 (InterVASP Messaging Standard)**: Technical standard for exchanging customer information between Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) to comply with FATF Travel Rule, defines data format and communication protocol.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto assets effective 2024-2025, requires licensing, €350K capital, operational resilience, consumer protection, market abuse prevention.
- **RegTech (Regulatory Technology)**: Technology solutions automating compliance processes including transaction monitoring, screening, reporting, regulatory change tracking, reduces manual effort 40-60% per vendor claims.
- **SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission)**: US federal agency regulating securities markets, claims jurisdiction over crypto assets deemed securities, enforcement actions for unregistered offerings and exchanges.
- **Travel Rule**: FATF Recommendation 16 requiring financial institutions (including crypto exchanges) to transmit customer information (originator, beneficiary details) for transactions exceeding specified thresholds (typically $1,000/€1,000).
