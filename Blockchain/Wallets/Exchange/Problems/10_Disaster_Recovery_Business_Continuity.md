## Glossary

- **AES-256 (Advanced Encryption Standard 256-bit)**: Symmetric encryption algorithm with 256-bit key length, industry standard for data protection, computationally infeasible to brute-force (2^256 possible keys).
- **DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)**: Cyberattack overwhelming system with traffic from multiple sources, causing 4-48h outages for exchanges without mitigation (Cloudflare, AWS Shield), costing $100K-$500K/hour in lost revenue.
- **Hot-standby**: Redundant infrastructure maintaining real-time synchronized state, enabling automated failover within seconds to minutes, typically 2-3x cost of single-instance architecture.
- **HSM (Hardware Security Module)**: Tamper-resistant physical device managing cryptographic keys, required for institutional custody, initialization time 15min-2h, corruption/failure requires backup HSMs for recovery.
- **ISO 27001**: International standard for information security management systems, includes business continuity management requirements, third-party certification required for enterprise clients.
- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)**: Maximum acceptable data loss measured in time (e.g., 5-minute RPO = lose at most 5 minutes of transactions), determines backup frequency requirements.
- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)**: Maximum acceptable downtime for system restoration (e.g., 1-hour RTO = restore operations within 1 hour of disaster), determines failover automation requirements.
- **Shamir's Secret Sharing**: Cryptographic algorithm splitting secret (private key) into N shares requiring M shares to reconstruct (M-of-N threshold, e.g., 3-of-5), eliminates single point of failure, complicates recovery coordination.
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Security compliance certification verifying operational effectiveness of controls over 6+ months, includes disaster recovery and business continuity criteria, required by enterprise clients and regulators.
