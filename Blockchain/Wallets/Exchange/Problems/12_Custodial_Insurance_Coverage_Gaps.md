## Glossary

- **AUC (Assets Under Custody)**: Total value of cryptocurrency assets held by exchange on behalf of users, typically measured in USD equivalent, determines insurance coverage needs and premium calculations (1-3% AUC for custody insurance).
- **Captive Insurance**: Insurance company owned by the insured entity (exchange) to retain premium income and investment returns, requires $5M-$20M regulatory capital, 12-24mo licensing, suitable for $1B+ AUC exchanges.
- **Crime/Theft Insurance**: Coverage for loss due to criminal acts including hacking, employee theft, social engineering, fraud; primary policy type for crypto exchanges with limits typically 10-30% AUC.
- **Deductible**: Initial loss amount absorbed by insured before insurance coverage begins, typically $1M-$10M for crypto exchange policies, paid from operational reserves.
- **Errors & Omissions (E&O) Insurance**: Coverage for operational failures, negligence, professional mistakes causing customer fund loss; separate from crime insurance, limits $10M-$50M typical.
- **Evertas**: Dedicated cryptocurrency insurance provider founded 2019, offers custody and crime coverage, underwriting expertise in blockchain security, $100M-$500M+ capacity per exchange.
- **Lloyd's of London**: Insurance market with multiple syndicates underwriting crypto custody policies, collective capacity $1B-$5B+ for industry, 300+ years risk assessment expertise, primary market for large exchange coverage.
- **Nexus Mutual**: Decentralized insurance alternative using Ethereum smart contracts, parametric coverage with token governance voting on claims, $10M-$50M capacity per protocol, 50-70% lower premiums than traditional but novel smart contract risk.
- **Parametric Insurance**: Automated coverage paying pre-defined amounts based on objective triggers (e.g., oracle-confirmed hack exceeding threshold), eliminates claims dispute and investigation delays, limited availability in crypto.
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Security compliance certification verifying operational effectiveness of controls over 6+ months, required by most crypto insurers as underwriting prerequisite, 6-12mo initial audit costing $100K-$300K.
- **Specie Insurance**: Coverage for physical storage of cryptocurrency assets (e.g., paper wallets, hardware devices in vaults), distinct from digital/online custody insurance, used for cold storage protection.
- **Uninsured Retention**: Portion of loss retained by exchange (self-insured) through reserves before insurance coverage applies, typically 70%+ AUC due to coverage limits, requires $50M-$100M+ reserve allocation.
