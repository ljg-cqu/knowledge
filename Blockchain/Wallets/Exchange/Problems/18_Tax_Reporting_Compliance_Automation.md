## Glossary

- **1099-DA**: IRS Form "Digital Asset Proceeds From Broker Transactions," mandatory for custodial exchanges starting 2025 tax year to report sales/exchanges of digital assets.
- **Cost Basis**: Original value paid for an asset, used to calculate capital gains/losses. For 1099-DA, mandatory 2026+ for covered assets only.
- **Covered Asset**: Digital asset acquired on/after 1/1/2026 and held continuously on same broker platform without transfers, requiring full cost basis reporting.
- **CP2000 Notice**: IRS automated mismatch notice sent when taxpayer-reported income differs from information returns (1099s), triggering audits or adjustments.
- **FIFO (First-In, First-Out)**: Default cost basis method where oldest acquired assets are deemed sold first; mandatory unless valid Specific Identification used.
- **IRC §6045**: Internal Revenue Code section requiring brokers to report proceeds from sales of securities and (now) digital assets to IRS.
- **Non-Covered Asset**: Digital asset acquired before 1/1/2026 or transferred between platforms, where cost basis reporting is optional for brokers.
- **Revenue Procedure 2024-28**: IRS guidance providing one-time safe harbor for transitioning from universal to wallet-by-wallet accounting (deadline passed for most).
- **Specific Identification**: Tax-lot selection method requiring documented pre-disposition identification of which specific asset units to sell, enabling tax optimization.
- **Universal Method**: Now-eliminated cost basis tracking treating all wallets as single pool, allowing retroactive lot selection across wallets (invalid post-1/1/2025).
- **Wallet-by-Wallet Accounting**: Mandatory 1/1/2025+ method requiring separate cost basis tracking for each distinct wallet/account, with no cross-wallet lot mixing.
