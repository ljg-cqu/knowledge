## Glossary

- **Actuarial data**: Historical loss frequency and severity statistics used by insurance underwriters to calculate premiums; cryptocurrency custody has limited data (5-7 years MPC operational history) creating pricing uncertainty.
- **Beazley**: Lloyd's of London syndicate providing specialty insurance including cyber and digital asset coverage, partnered with Munich Re to insure MPC provider Dfns in 2024.
- **Custody Rule 206(4)-2**: SEC regulation requiring investment advisers maintain client assets with "qualified custodian" demonstrating "reasonable assurances" of safety, written for traditional securities custody, application to MPC unclear.
- **DeFi insurance protocols**: Decentralized coverage platforms (Nexus Mutual, InsurAce, Unslashed Finance) using cryptocurrency pools and token holder governance for claims resolution, alternative to traditional insurance.
- **Dfns**: MPC wallet infrastructure provider insured by Beazley and Munich Re in 2024, demonstrating specialized MPC coverage availability for select providers.
- **Fidelity bond**: Insurance covering employee dishonesty (theft, embezzlement), required by crypto custody underwriters as part of comprehensive coverage package.
- **Lloyd's of London**: Insurance market where syndicates provide capacity for specialty risks including cryptocurrency custody, primary source of large-scale digital asset insurance ($100M+ policies).
- **Marsh**: Global insurance broker placing largest digital asset custody insurance facilities; March 2024 launched $825 million capacity supporting MPC solutions.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU regulation effective 2025 requiring crypto asset service providers maintain "professional indemnity insurance" or "other comparable guarantee," specifics undefined.
- **Munich Re**: Major reinsurer providing capacity to primary insurers for large risks, partnered with Beazley to insure MPC provider Dfns in 2024.
- **Operational loss**: Insurance classification for losses from internal processes, employee errors, system failures; typically excluded from crypto custody policies, creating MPC coverage gaps.
- **Parametric insurance**: Policy automatically paying claims when predefined trigger occurs (e.g., blockchain-detected exchange hack), reduces investigation time but limited coverage scope.
- **Penetration testing**: Simulated cyberattack assessing security controls, required quarterly by crypto custody insurance underwriters to verify defenses remain effective.
- **Professional indemnity insurance**: Coverage for losses from professional negligence or errors, EU MiCA mandates for crypto asset service providers, MPC-specific requirements undefined.
- **Reinsurance**: Insurance purchased by primary insurers to transfer catastrophic risk, enables large coverage limits; Munich Re, Swiss Re provide crypto custody reinsurance capacity.
- **SAB 121 (Staff Accounting Bulletin)**: SEC 2022 guidance requiring public companies report custodied crypto as liabilities on balance sheets, created adoption uncertainty resolved partially by 2024 Congressional action.
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Service Organization Control audit verifying security controls over ≥6 month period across Trust Services Criteria (security, availability, confidentiality, privacy, processing integrity), required by institutional custody underwriters.
- **Underwriting**: Insurance risk assessment process determining coverage terms, exclusions, and premiums; crypto custody underwriting requires 40-60 page applications documenting security architecture.
