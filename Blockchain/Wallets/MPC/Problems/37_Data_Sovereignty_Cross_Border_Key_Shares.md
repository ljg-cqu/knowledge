   - **Background and current situation**: 
     MPC security model assumes geographic distribution: Textbook MPC architecture distributes n shares across independent jurisdictions ensuring adversary compromising k-1 shares learns nothing about complete key [Cryptographic assumption: threshold security]. Institutional-grade deployments place shares strategically: User device (mobile/laptop), Enterprise server (on-premises or cloud primary region), Cloud backup (secondary region different provider), Offline HSM (geographically distant vault), Trusted third party (escrow service, different jurisdiction) [Guide: Stackup, 2025]. Key share alone mathematically useless (cannot derive private key from 1-of-3 shares, learn nothing about complete key). However: (1) Linked to user (share associated with specific wallet, user identity); (2) Combined with other shares recovers wallet (system as a whole processes personal data even if individual components don't); (3) Conservative interpretation (data controllers assume GDPR applies to avoid €20M fines). ## Glossary

- **Adequacy determination**: European Commission decision that non-EEA country provides adequate data protection, enabling transfers without additional safeguards; US lacks adequacy post-Schrems II (2020), UK/Switzerland/Japan have adequacy.
- **DPDP Act (Digital Personal Data Protection)**: India data protection regulation (2023) mandating localized storage for certain data categories, cross-border transfers require consent or government approval, penalties up to ₹250 crore/$30M.
- **FSA (Financial Services Agency)**: Japan financial regulator requiring domestic storage for crypto custody services key material per financial services regulations.
- **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**: EU regulation (2016, enforced 2018) restricting cross-border personal data transfers outside EEA unless adequacy or safeguards (SCCs), extraterritorial reach to any org processing EU residents' data, fines up to €20M or 4% global turnover.
- **Geographic distribution**: MPC security best practice of distributing key shares across independent jurisdictions (US + EU + Asia + neutral) to prevent single-government seizure, regional disasters, correlated infrastructure failures.
- **PIPL (Personal Information Protection Law)**: China data protection regulation (2021) requiring security assessments for cross-border personal information transfers by critical infrastructure operators, broadly defining personal information, penalties up to ¥50M/$7M or 5% revenue.
- **Schrems II**: EU Court of Justice case (July 2020) invalidating US-EU Privacy Shield adequacy determination due to US surveillance laws, requiring Standard Contractual Clauses + additional safeguards for US transfers.
- **Share migration**: Process of changing key share storage locations post-deployment through cryptographic refresh (regenerating shares without reconstructing master key), requires threshold participant coordination, costs $50K-$200K per major customer, 3-6 month timeline.
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**: EU-approved contract templates enabling GDPR-compliant data transfers to non-adequate countries with additional safeguards (encryption, legal challenges, transparency), alternative to adequacy determinations.
