## Glossary

- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Regulatory framework requiring financial institutions detect and report suspicious transactions potentially linked to money laundering, terrorism financing, or other illicit activities.
- **Behavioral analytics**: Machine learning-based transaction monitoring analyzing patterns (velocity, counterparty relationships, mixing, layering) to detect suspicious activity beyond simple sanctions list checks.
- **CTF (Counter-Terrorism Financing)**: Regulations preventing use of financial systems to fund terrorist activities; overlaps with AML but distinct legal framework.
- **False positive**: Legitimate transaction incorrectly flagged as suspicious by monitoring systems; 1-5% false positive rate means 10K-50K erroneous blocks per 1M transactions, generating support burden.
- **FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)**: US Treasury bureau enforcing Bank Secrecy Act, issuing AML/CTF regulations for crypto service providers including Travel Rule.
- **KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Identity verification process requiring service providers collect, verify, and maintain customer information (name, address, government ID, source of funds).
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU regulatory framework effective Q3 2025 requiring crypto service providers implement comprehensive AML/KYC controls, transaction monitoring, Travel Rule compliance; non-compliance penalties up to 5% turnover or €5M.
- **OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)**: US Treasury department maintaining Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list of ~10K sanctioned individuals, entities, addresses; transactions to/from blocked.
- **SAR (Suspicious Activity Report)**: Mandatory filing to Financial Intelligence Units when monitoring detects potentially illicit transactions; failure to file results in regulatory penalties.
- **Sanctions screening**: Real-time checking of transaction counterparties against government watchlists (OFAC, UN, EU) to block transfers to/from prohibited entities; adds 200-500ms latency.
- **Selective disclosure**: Privacy technique allowing regulators access compliance data while maintaining transaction privacy from public/other parties; potentially using zero-knowledge proofs or encrypted data sharing.
- **Travel Rule**: FATF recommendation requiring VASPs transmit originator/beneficiary information for transactions exceeding thresholds ($3K US, €1K EU); enforcement through FinCEN, MiCA, national regulations.
- **TRISA/TRP/OpenVASP**: Competing Travel Rule protocol standards for VASP-to-VASP identity data exchange; lack of interoperability creates 80% failure rate for counterparty data retrieval.
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: FATF designation for entities providing crypto exchange, custody, transfer services; includes MPC wallet providers despite "non-custodial" marketing, requiring AML/KYC compliance.
