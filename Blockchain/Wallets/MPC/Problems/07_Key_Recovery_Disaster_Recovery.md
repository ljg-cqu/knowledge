Unlike traditional wallets with 12-24 word seed phrases enabling instant recovery, MPC requires complex coordination among remaining share holders, third-party recovery services (creating new trust dependencies), or pre-distributed backup shares (stored securely across multiple geographic locations). Institutional custody providers managing est. $50B+ assets, exchanges serving 100M+ users, and enterprise treasury operations face operational outages lasting days-weeks, regulatory compliance failures (must demonstrate 24-48h recovery capability), and permanent asset loss ($964M+ private key compromises in 2024) when recovery procedures fail or backup infrastructure proves inadequate.

Traditional backup (seed phrase written on paper/metal) impossible as shares meaningless individually, full key never exists to backup [Blog: Stackup, 2024]. Current recovery approaches: (1) Redundancy: deploy n+2 parties so k remain accessible after 2 failures, but increases attack surface and coordination complexity [Blog: CoinCover, 2023]; (2) Third-party recovery services: companies like Cobo, Nemean Services store encrypted backup shares, but reintroduces trusted third party dependency MPC aimed to eliminate [Blog: Cobo, 2024]; (3) Distributed backup: pre-split backup shares stored across multiple geographic locations (HSMs, secure vaults, cloud regions), requires 24/7 access coordination and physical security [Blog: CoinCover, 2023]; (4) Social recovery: distribute shares to trusted contacts (family, colleagues), but human factors create risk (contacts unavailable, relationships deteriorate, social engineering) [Blog: Blockworks, 2023]. ## Glossary

- **Disaster Recovery (DR)**: Process of restoring data and systems after disasters (cyber-attacks, hardware failures, natural disasters); for MPC wallets, involves recovering operational access to distributed key shares.
- **Geographic distribution**: Strategy of storing backup key shares in physically separate locations (data centers, secure vaults across multiple regions/countries) to survive regional disasters.
- **HSM (Hardware Security Module)**: Tamper-resistant hardware device for secure cryptographic key storage and operations; used for protecting MPC key shares in custody infrastructure.
- **k-of-n threshold**: MPC scheme requiring k parties out of n total to collaborate for transaction signing; enables fault tolerance (n-k party failures tolerated) while maintaining security.
- **Key share**: Individual piece of distributed private key in MPC protocol; mathematically useless alone, but k shares can reconstruct signing capability through MPC protocol without materializing full key.
- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)**: Maximum acceptable time to restore operations after disaster; financial regulations typically require 24-48h RTO for custody providers.
- **Redundancy**: Deploying additional MPC parties beyond minimum threshold (e.g., 5 parties for 3-of-5) to tolerate failures; increases operational complexity and attack surface.
- **Seed phrase**: 12-24 word mnemonic enabling complete wallet recovery in traditional (non-MPC) wallets; impossible in MPC as full key never exists to backup.
- **Social recovery**: Disaster recovery approach distributing key shares to trusted individuals (family, colleagues, friends); vulnerable to human factors (unavailability, relationship changes, social engineering).
- **Third-party recovery service**: External company (e.g., Cobo, Nemean Services, CoinCover) storing encrypted backup shares for disaster recovery; reintroduces trusted third party dependency.
