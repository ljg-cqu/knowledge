## Glossary

- **Asset-only verification**: Proof-of-reserves approach demonstrating control of blockchain addresses through cryptographic signatures but not proving 1:1 backing without liability data.
- **Merkle tree**: Cryptographic data structure organizing customer balances into hierarchical tree with public root hash, enabling individual verification without revealing others' balances.
- **Point-in-time attestation**: PoR approach verifying reserves at specific snapshot moment (monthly/quarterly) but not providing continuous assurance between attestations.
- **Proof-of-Keys**: Verification process demonstrating custodian control over private keys for claimed asset holdings through cryptographic signatures.
- **Proof-of-reserves (PoR)**: Attestation demonstrating custodian holds customer assets through cryptographic verification (assets) and optionally third-party audit (liabilities).
- **Real-time verification**: Continuous on-chain monitoring of custodian wallet addresses enabling immediate detection of reserve changes versus customer claims.
- **zk-SNARK (Zero-Knowledge Succinct Non-Interactive Argument of Knowledge)**: Cryptographic proof enabling verification that statement is true (e.g., assets ≥ liabilities) without revealing underlying data.
