## Glossary

- **Avoidance actions**: Legal claims to reverse transactions deemed improper (e.g., transactions at undervalue, preferential payments, fraudulent transfers).
- **Chapter 11**: U.S. bankruptcy code chapter allowing company reorganization while continuing operations under court supervision.
- **Cold wallet**: Offline storage device (USB, hardware wallet, paper) holding private keys, disconnected from internet for security.
- **Commingled assets**: Mixed funds or assets where client property and company property are indistinguishable, complicating ownership claims.
- **Custodian**: Professional service provider holding and securing digital assets on behalf of insolvency estate with insurance coverage.
- **Digital forensics**: Investigative process identifying, recovering, and analyzing digital evidence including blockchain transactions and wallet addresses.
- **Freezing order**: Court injunction preventing asset transfer or disposal, applicable worldwide in some jurisdictions, can target "persons unknown".
- **Hot wallet**: Internet-connected wallet enabling fast transactions but vulnerable to hacking and theft.
- **In specie distribution**: Distributing assets in their original form (e.g., Bitcoin) rather than converting to fiat currency.
- **Insolvency practitioner (IP)**: Licensed professional appointed to administer bankrupt estate, recover assets, and distribute to creditors.
- **Liquidated sum**: Specific monetary amount that can be calculated precisely, required for statutory demand (not applicable to crypto debts per UKJT).
- **Private key**: Cryptographic code (12-24 characters) providing sole access to digital assets in wallet; loss results in permanent inaccessibility.
- **Property (Digital Assets etc) Bill**: UK legislation (introduced Sept 2024) confirming digital assets as property under law.
- **Proof-of-reserves**: Cryptographic verification demonstrating control of private keys for claimed holdings.
- **Seed phrase**: Sequence of 12-24 random words enabling wallet recovery if device lost or private key forgotten.
- **Statutory demand**: Formal request for debt payment; failure to comply within 21 days creates presumption of insolvency.
- **Third-party disclosure order**: Court order compelling entity (e.g., exchange) to provide information about debtor's assets.
- **UKJT (UK Jurisdiction Taskforce)**: Legal working group issuing statements on legal treatment of digital assets and smart contracts.
