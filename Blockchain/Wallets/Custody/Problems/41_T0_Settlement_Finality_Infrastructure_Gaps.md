## Glossary

- **Atomic DVP (Delivery-versus-Payment)**: Settlement mechanism ensuring asset delivery and payment occur simultaneously or neither occurs; eliminates counterparty risk by preventing one party receiving asset without other receiving payment.
- **CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency)**: Digital currency issued by central bank for wholesale or retail transactions; pilot programs exploring instant settlement (ECB digital euro, BOE digital pound).
- **GENIUS Act**: US law enacted July 2025 positioning United States as leader in digital currency and strengthening US dollar's global role through stablecoin framework.
- **Hashed Timelock Contract (HTLC)**: Smart contract-based atomic swap protocol enabling cross-chain transactions with time-bound commitment mechanism; if conditions not met by deadline, transaction reverses.
- **Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)**: Payment system processing transactions individually in real-time rather than batching; Federal Reserve FedNow and SWIFT gpi enable faster traditional payments but not atomic settlement.
- **Stablecoin**: Cryptocurrency pegged to fiat currency (typically USD) providing price stability; enables on-chain settlement within minutes; forecast $40T transaction volume 2025.
- **T+0 Settlement**: Instant settlement where trade execution and final settlement occur same day or immediately (trade date plus zero days); eliminates multi-day counterparty risk of T+1/T+2 cycles.
- **T+1 Settlement**: Trade settlement occurring on business day following trade execution (trade date plus one day); US securities moved from T+2 to T+1 May 2023.
- **T+2 Settlement**: Traditional settlement cycle where trades settle two business days after execution; standard for most global securities markets before US T+1 transition.
