## Glossary

- **Bailment**: Legal relationship where one party (bailee/custodian) holds property on behalf of another (bailor/customer) without transfer of ownership, obligating return of identical assets.
- **Bankruptcy-remote**: Legal structure designed to protect assets from inclusion in bankruptcy estate, typically through special purpose entities or trust arrangements with confirmed opinions.
- **Commingling**: Mixing customer assets with proprietary (company-owned) assets in same accounts, making individual ownership indistinguishable and creating risk of creditor claims.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU regulation effective December 2024 establishing comprehensive framework for crypto-asset service providers, including Article 70(1) mandatory segregation requirements.
- **Omnibus account**: Single blockchain address holding assets for multiple customers, providing operational efficiency but obscuring individual ownership without proper off-chain records.
- **Proof-of-reserves**: Attestation demonstrating custodian holds customer assets through cryptographic verification (assets) and third-party audit (liabilities), proving 1:1 backing.
- **Segregation**: Legal and technical separation of customer assets from custodian's proprietary assets, ensuring customer assets not available to general creditors in bankruptcy.
- **Unsecured creditor**: Party with claim against bankrupt estate without specific collateral, receiving pro-rata distribution after secured creditors (typically 10-30% in crypto bankruptcies).
