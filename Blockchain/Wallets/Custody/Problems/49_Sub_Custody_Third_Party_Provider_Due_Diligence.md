## Glossary

- **Counterparty risk**: The risk that a sub-custodian or third-party service provider fails to perform its obligations, potentially resulting in frozen or lost client assets ranging from $50M-$500M+ based on institutional portfolio sizes.
- **Material change**: Modification to custody arrangements that requires prior regulatory approval under NYDFS guidance, with approval timelines of 30-90 days; threshold for "material" not precisely defined creating compliance uncertainty.
- **NYDFS (New York Department of Financial Services)**: Primary US state regulator for crypto custody, issued updated sub-custody guidance September 30, 2025 requiring prior approval for material arrangement changes and "substantially similar" regulatory regime validation.
- **Sub-custodian**: Third-party entity to which a primary custodian delegates safekeeping of digital assets for specific blockchains, geographic regions, or asset types; 70-85% of institutional custodians use ≥1 sub-custodian relationship.
- **Substantially similar regulatory regime**: NYDFS standard requiring sub-custodians be subject to regulatory oversight comparable to NYDFS licensing and supervision; lacks formal equivalence determinations creating legal interpretation uncertainty.
- **Third-party risk management (TPRM)**: Framework for assessing and monitoring risks from vendors and service providers; traditional TPRM approaches miss blockchain-specific concerns like key management, transaction signing, and network node security.
