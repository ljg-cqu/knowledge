Institutional custody operations face similar risks when key personnel depart or emergency scenarios require immediate access outside normal authorization workflows. Need to implement robust emergency access and succession mechanisms reducing unplanned inaccessibility risk from current est. 15-25% probability over 20-year holding period to <2% within 12-18 months while maintaining security against unauthorized access.
   
   - **Background and current situation**: 
     Estate planning for digital assets requires specific strategies including detailed inventory of cryptocurrency accounts, succession planning with secure key management, and legal title transfer mechanisms [Web Search: Multiple Sources, 2025]. Estate planning documents such as wills and trusts must include specific language authorizing fiduciaries to access digital accounts per Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA) [Web Search: Blasser Law, 2025]. ## Glossary

- **CheckLockTimeVerify (CLTV)**: Bitcoin script opcode enabling time-locked transactions that become spendable only after specified time/block height; used for implementing dead man's switch mechanisms and heir access.
- **Dead man's switch**: Automated system requiring periodic user confirmation (e.g., every 6 months) to prevent predefined action (e.g., asset transfer to beneficiaries); failure to confirm triggers succession process.
- **Emergency access**: Custody wallet capability enabling authorized access outside normal signing workflows during crisis scenarios (death, incapacity, disaster); must balance security against unauthorized activation.
- **Probate**: Legal process of validating will and administering deceased person's estate; typically 6-18 months duration, complicated by cryptocurrency lacking traditional ownership documentation.
- **RUFADAA (Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act)**: US model law adopted by 45+ states providing legal framework for executors and fiduciaries to access deceased/incapacitated persons' digital accounts and assets.
- **Shamir Secret Sharing**: Cryptographic scheme splitting secret (e.g., seed phrase) into N shares where any K shares (threshold) can reconstruct original secret; enables distributed backup without single point of failure.
- **Social recovery**: Wallet recovery mechanism using pre-selected trusted contacts (guardians) who collectively authorize account recovery if primary key lost; trades security assumption for improved usability.
- **Succession planning**: Process of documenting and preparing asset transfer mechanisms for heirs/beneficiaries upon holder death or incapacity; requires legal documentation, secure key handoff, and asset location information.
- **Time-locked vault**: Custody mechanism preventing asset access until specified future date/condition; enables automatic heir access after predetermined period without primary holder action.
