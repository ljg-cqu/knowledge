## Glossary

- **CLARITY Act (Digital Asset Market Clarity Act of 2025)**: US legislation prohibiting undisclosed use of customer crypto assets for staking or blockchain services; requires express written client consent and CFTC oversight for any asset use.
- **GENIUS Act**: US legislation signed July 18, 2025 establishing stablecoin regulatory framework; explicitly prohibits rehypothecation and yield generation on stablecoins to ensure 1:1 reserve backing.
- **Rehypothecation**: Practice of custodians using client assets as collateral for their own borrowing or lending to third parties; common in traditional securities custody but prohibited for crypto custody by SEC, GENIUS Act, and CLARITY Act.
- **Securities lending revenue**: Income traditional securities custodians generate by lending client securities to short sellers and hedge funds; contributes 20-40% of custody segment revenue ($500M-$1B+ annually for major banks) but unavailable to crypto custodians.
- **Staking-as-a-service**: Custody offering where provider operates validator nodes for proof-of-stake blockchains, earning staking rewards (4-10% APY) shared with clients after custodian fee (10-25%); generates 0.10%-0.30% additional AUM revenue.
- **Break-even threshold**: Minimum assets under management ($2B-$5B+) required for institutional custody provider to cover operational costs ($2M-$10M annually) under pure fee model (0.04%-0.50% AUM); creates profitability barrier for mid-sized custodians.
- **Slashing**: Penalty mechanism in proof-of-stake blockchains where validator stakes are reduced ("slashed") for downtime or protocol violations; creates risk for staking-as-a-service custodians managing client assets.
