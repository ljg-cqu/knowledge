## Glossary

- **AUM (Assets Under Management)**: Total market value of digital assets held by custody provider on behalf of clients.
- **Cold storage**: Offline storage of private keys using hardware wallets, air-gapped systems, or paper backups, prioritizing security over accessibility.
- **Cold-to-hot replenishment**: Process of transferring assets from cold storage to hot wallet to maintain liquidity, typically requiring multi-sig authorization.
- **ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**: Investment fund traded on stock exchanges, tracking underlying assets like Bitcoin (e.g., BlackRock IBIT).
- **Hot wallet**: Online wallet connected to internet enabling fast transactions but vulnerable to hacking, used for operational liquidity.
- **HSM (Hardware Security Module)**: Dedicated cryptographic hardware device securing private keys with tamper-resistant properties.
- **Hybrid custody**: Custody model combining hot wallets (5-15% AUM for liquidity) with cold storage (85-95% AUM for security).
- **Liquidity ratio**: Percentage of total AUM maintained in hot wallets for immediate withdrawal fulfillment.
- **MPC/TSS (Multi-Party Computation/Threshold Signature Scheme)**: Cryptographic technique distributing key shares across parties, enabling transaction signing without reconstructing full key.
- **Multi-sig (Multi-signature)**: Wallet requiring multiple private keys (e.g., 2-of-3, 3-of-5) to authorize transactions, eliminating single-point-of-failure.
- **On-chain settlement**: Recording transaction on blockchain providing cryptographic proof of completion, addressing "paper BTC" concerns.
- **Paper BTC**: Term for Bitcoin IOUs (I-Owe-You) where custody provider claims holdings without on-chain proof, risking artificial price suppression.
- **Proof-of-reserves**: Cryptographic verification demonstrating custody provider controls private keys for claimed asset holdings.
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**: Contractual commitment specifying performance metrics (e.g., <12h withdrawal processing) with penalties for non-compliance.
