## Glossary

- **Beneficiary**: Person designated to receive assets upon owner's death, specified in will or inheritance mechanism like Bitkey/Casa multisig.
- **Dead Man's Switch**: Automated mechanism triggering asset transfer or key revelation after prolonged inactivity period (e.g., 12 months), indicating owner likely deceased.
- **Estate Plan**: Legal arrangement specifying asset distribution upon death, including wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and digital asset instructions.
- **Executor**: Person appointed in will to administer estate, settle debts, distribute assets to beneficiaries, requires legal authority to access cryptocurrency.
- **Multisig (Multi-Signature)**: Wallet requiring M-of-N signatures to authorize transactions, used for inheritance (e.g., 2-of-3: owner + 2 trusted contacts, where 2 can recover after owner's death).
- **Probate**: Legal process validating will, appointing executor, distributing estate assets under court supervision, complicated for cryptocurrency due to digital nature.
- **Seed Phrase (Recovery Phrase)**: 12-24 word mnemonic encoding master private key, critical for wallet recovery, creates inheritance dilemma (reveal vs. secure storage).
- **Self-Custody**: User directly controls private keys (vs. custodial services like exchanges), provides security/privacy but complicates inheritance without planning.
- **Shamir Secret Sharing**: Cryptographic technique splitting seed into N shares where M shares required to reconstruct, enables distributed inheritance without single point of failure.
- **Social Recovery**: Inheritance mechanism where trusted contacts (family, friends, attorneys) hold recovery keys, collectively enabling asset access after owner's death.
