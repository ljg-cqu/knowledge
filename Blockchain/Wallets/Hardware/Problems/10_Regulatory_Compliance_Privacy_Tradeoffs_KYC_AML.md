GDPR conflict: blockchain transparency vs. data protection rights (EDPB Guidelines 02/2025 discourage on-chain personal data). EU AMLR prohibits anonymous accounts at CASPs, impacting hardware wallet on/off-ramps. Need to achieve regulatory compliance in ≥3 major jurisdictions (EU, US, Asia) while maintaining >80% user privacy satisfaction by Q4 2026, balancing $200M-$500M compliance costs against market access to $582.98M industry.

## Glossary

- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Regulatory framework requiring financial institutions to detect and prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and illicit activity through KYC, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
- **AMLR (Anti-Money Laundering Regulation)**: EU regulation (part of AML Package) requiring CASPs to implement enhanced due diligence, prohibiting anonymous accounts, mandating risk-mitigating measures for self-hosted wallet transfers.
- **CASP (Crypto-Asset Service Provider)**: EU MiCA term for entities offering crypto services (exchanges, custodians, brokers), subject to licensing, KYC, AML, consumer protection requirements.
- **EBA (European Banking Authority)**: EU regulatory agency supervising MiCA implementation, issuing technical standards for CASPs, enforcing AML compliance.
- **EDPB (European Data Protection Board)**: EU body coordinating GDPR enforcement, issued Guidelines 02/2025 on blockchain/personal data processing, emphasizing data minimization and off-chain storage.
- **FATF (Financial Action Task Force)**: International organization setting AML/CFT standards, issued Travel Rule guidance requiring VASPs to share originator/beneficiary information for crypto transfers.
- **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**: EU data protection law granting individuals rights (access, erasure, portability), conflicts with blockchain immutability and transparency.
- **KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Identity verification process requiring users to provide name, address, government ID, source of funds, required by AML regulations, creates privacy concerns.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU comprehensive crypto regulatory framework effective Dec 30, 2024, licensing CASPs, mandating transparency, consumer protection, stablecoin reserves.
- **Self-Hosted Wallet (Non-Custodial Wallet)**: Cryptocurrency wallet where user controls private keys (vs. custodial service like exchange), historically exempt from direct KYC but facing regulatory pressure.
- **Travel Rule**: FATF standard requiring VASPs to share originator/beneficiary information (names, addresses, wallet addresses) for crypto transfers, implemented in EU Sept 2025 with no minimum threshold.
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: FATF term for crypto exchanges, wallet providers, custodians subject to AML regulations and Travel Rule compliance.
- **Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZK-Proof)**: Cryptographic method enabling one party to prove statement truth without revealing underlying data, potential privacy-preserving compliance solution (e.g., prove funds legitimate without disclosing source).
