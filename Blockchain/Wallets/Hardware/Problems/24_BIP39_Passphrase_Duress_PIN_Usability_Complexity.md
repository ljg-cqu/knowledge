## Glossary

- **$5 Wrench Attack**: Physical coercion attack where adversary uses threat of violence to extract cryptocurrency seed phrase or private keys; named after XKCD comic illustrating that physical coercion bypasses cryptographic security.
- **BIP39 (Bitcoin Improvement Proposal 39)**: Standard defining how mnemonic seed phrases (12-24 words) are generated and used to derive cryptocurrency wallet keys; passphrase is optional 25th word extending the standard.
- **Case-Sensitive**: Distinguishing between uppercase and lowercase characters; BIP39 passphrases treat "Password", "password", and "PASSWORD" as three completely different passphrases generating separate wallets.
- **Hidden Wallet (Plausible Deniability)**: Security technique where users maintain a decoy wallet (seed-only with small balance) and hidden wallet (seed + passphrase with main holdings), allowing plausible claim that decoy is the only wallet under coercion.
- **M-of-N Threshold**: Shamir Secret Sharing parameter where secret is split into N shares and any M shares are sufficient to reconstruct (e.g., 3-of-5 means any 3 of 5 shares can recover passphrase, providing redundancy against loss).
- **Passphrase (25th Word)**: Optional BIP39 feature allowing users to add a secret word/phrase (up to 50 characters) to their 24-word seed phrase; cryptographically combines to derive a completely separate wallet tree for enhanced security.
- **Plausible Deniability**: Security property allowing users to convincingly deny the existence of hidden assets or data; in hardware wallets, revealing 24-word seed under coercion while hiding passphrase-protected wallet.
- **Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS)**: Cryptographic algorithm splitting a secret (e.g., passphrase) into multiple shares where a threshold number of shares can reconstruct the secret but fewer shares reveal no information; enables distributed backup.
- **UTF-8**: Character encoding standard supporting all international characters and symbols; BIP39 passphrases accept any UTF-8 string, allowing non-English passphrases but increasing complexity and typo risk.
