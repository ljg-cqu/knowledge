## Glossary

- **5AMLD/6AMLD (5th/6th Anti-Money Laundering Directives)**: EU legislation extending AML/CFT requirements to Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs), cryptocurrency exchanges, and custodial wallet providers.
- **Custodial Wallet**: Cryptocurrency wallet where private keys are held by third-party service provider (exchange, wallet app), making provider subject to VASP regulations vs self-custody.
- **De-banking/De-risking**: Practice where traditional banks close accounts of cryptocurrency businesses due to regulatory uncertainty and perceived money laundering risks.
- **FATF (Financial Action Task Force)**: Intergovernmental organization setting global standards for combating money laundering and terrorism financing, with 190+ member jurisdictions.
- **GENIUS Act**: Proposed US legislation mandating federal licensing for stablecoin issuers, 1:1 reserves, full AML compliance, treating issuers as financial institutions under Bank Secrecy Act.
- **IVMS101**: Interoperability standard for Travel Rule data exchange, defining format for originator/beneficiary information sharing between VASPs; optional adoption led to fragmentation.
- **KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Identity verification requirements mandating VASPs collect customer name, address, date of birth, government ID; blockchain analytics estimate global KYC costs at portion of $206B annual compliance spending.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto assets, defining stablecoins, requiring authorized issuers, mandating segregated reserves and redemption guarantees.
- **Self-Custody Wallet**: Non-custodial wallet where user holds private keys directly (hardware wallet, software wallet), technically exempt from VASP regulations but faces fiat on/off-ramp KYC requirements.
- **Sunrise Issue**: Regulatory challenge where FATF Travel Rule's staggered global rollout prevents VASPs from achieving uniform compliance—adhering to one jurisdiction may violate another's privacy laws.
- **Travel Rule (FATF Recommendation 16)**: Global AML standard requiring VASPs to collect and share originator/beneficiary information (name, account, address) for all virtual asset transfers.
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: FATF definition covering custodial wallet providers, exchanges, brokers subject to AML/CFT regulations; excludes pure self-custody software developers and hardware wallet manufacturers.
