## Glossary

- **CRQC (Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer)**: Quantum computer with sufficient qubits (1000-10000 logical) and gates (10^6-10^9) to run Shor's algorithm breaking ECDSA, enabling private key derivation from public keys
- **Q-Day**: Hypothetical date when CRQCs become available to adversaries, estimated 2029-2035 with 20% probability by 2030, triggering urgent cryptographic migration
- **Shor's algorithm (1994)**: Quantum algorithm providing polynomial-time factorization and discrete logarithm solution, breaks RSA and ECDSA asymmetric cryptography, requires CRQC
- **ECDSA secp256k1**: Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm used by Bitcoin/Ethereum for transaction signatures, vulnerable to Shor's algorithm quantum attack
- **HNDL (Harvest Now, Decrypt Later)**: Adversary strategy collecting encrypted data and blockchain public keys today for future quantum decryption once CRQC available
- **ML-DSA (Module-Lattice-Based Digital Signature Algorithm)**: NIST FIPS 204 post-quantum signature scheme based on lattice cryptography, signature size 2-5 KB (30-80× ECDSA overhead)
- **ML-KEM (Module-Lattice-Based Key Encapsulation Mechanism)**: NIST FIPS 203 post-quantum key exchange, used for establishing shared secrets resistant to quantum attack
- **SLH-DSA (Stateless Hash-based Digital Signature Algorithm)**: NIST FIPS 205 post-quantum signature based on hash functions, signature size 8-50 KB, simpler security assumption than lattice schemes
- **Hybrid signature**: Combination of classical (ECDSA) and post-quantum (ML-DSA) signatures providing backward compatibility and defense-in-depth during transition period
- **Lattice cryptography**: PQC security based on hardness of Learning With Errors (LWE) problem, foundation for ML-DSA and ML-KEM, less extensively studied than RSA/ECDSA
- **Exposed public key**: Blockchain address that has broadcast public key on-chain (Bitcoin spent P2PKH, Ethereum first transaction EOA), vulnerable to quantum private key derivation
- **P2PK (Pay-to-Public-Key)**: Early Bitcoin address type directly exposing public key, used in Satoshi-era mining rewards, $50B-$100B at risk, immediately quantum-vulnerable
- **Post-quantum cryptography (PQC)**: Cryptographic algorithms designed to resist quantum computer attacks, NIST standardized ML-DSA, ML-KEM, SLH-DSA in 2024
