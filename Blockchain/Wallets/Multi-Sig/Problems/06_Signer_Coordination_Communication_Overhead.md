## Glossary

- **Alert fatigue**: Desensitization to excessive notifications causing individuals to ignore or delay responding to legitimate alerts, affects 70-80% multisig signers
- **M-of-N threshold**: Multisig configuration requiring M approvals from N total signers (e.g., 2-of-3, 3-of-5), coordination complexity scales with both M and N
- **Quorum**: Minimum number of signers (M) required to approve transaction, gathering quorum is primary coordination challenge
- **Approval bottleneck**: Single unavailable signer delaying entire transaction approval despite other M-1 signers ready
- **Time zone gap**: Hours between overlapping business hours for globally distributed team, 12-16h typical for US/Europe/Asia
- **Asynchronous communication**: Non-real-time messaging (email, Slack) with 3-24h response latency vs synchronous (phone, video) requiring simultaneous availability
- **Notification effectiveness**: Percentage of sent notifications resulting in desired action (approval, review), 40-50% typical across channels
- **Transaction batching**: Combining multiple operations into single multisig approval, reduces coordination frequency 30-50% but not per-batch latency
- **Hot wallet workaround**: Maintaining single-signature wallet for urgent operations to bypass multisig coordination delays, undermines security model
- **Delegated authority**: Granting subset of signers or single individual approval power for specific transaction categories, reduces coordination but centralizes control
- **Pre-signed transaction**: Signatures collected in advance and stored for rapid execution, inflexible and requires periodic renewal (30-90 days)
- **Coordination overhead**: Time and resources spent organizing multisig approvals (tracking, follow-ups, meetings), 20-40% treasury team time
- **Session keys**: Temporary signing authority granted for limited duration/scope via EIP-4337 account abstraction, enables automated approvals
