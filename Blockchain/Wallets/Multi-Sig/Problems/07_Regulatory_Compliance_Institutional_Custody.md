## Glossary

- **Qualified custodian**: Entity authorized to safeguard client assets under SEC Investment Advisers Act Rule 206(4)-2, includes banks, broker-dealers, registered futures commission merchants, certain trust companies
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU regulation fully enforced January 2025 establishing comprehensive framework for crypto-asset service providers including custody requirements
- **Fiduciary duty**: Legal obligation to act in client's best interest when managing others' assets, requires prudent person standard including adequate safeguarding measures
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Service Organization Control audit examining security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, privacy controls over 6-12 month period
- **AUC (Assets Under Custody)**: Total value of crypto assets held by custodian, basis for percentage-based fee calculations (0.04-0.50% typical)
- **Asset segregation**: Requirement to separate client assets from custodian's own assets in distinct accounts/addresses/records, prevents commingling and rehypothecation
- **NYDFS BitLicense**: New York State Department of Financial Services license required for virtual currency business activity including custody in New York
- **OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency)**: Federal banking regulator that charters and supervises national banks and federal savings associations, including crypto custody institutions
- **SPDI (Special Purpose Depository Institution)**: Wyoming state trust company charter designed for crypto custody, simplified vs traditional bank but federal acceptance uncertain
- **Rehypothecation**: Practice of using client assets as collateral for custodian's own obligations, generally prohibited for crypto custody without explicit client consent
- **Business continuity plan**: Documentation of disaster recovery procedures, geographic redundancy, succession planning, 99.9%+ uptime maintenance
- **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)**: Mandatory filing to FinCEN when financial institution detects potential money laundering, terrorist financing, fraud (15-25K crypto SARs annually)
- **Cold storage**: Offline key storage (HSMs, hardware wallets, paper) physically separated from internet, reduces hack risk but increases operational complexity
