## Glossary

- **Account Abstraction**: Smart contract-based wallets enabling programmable security features like social recovery, spending limits, and session keys
- **Clipboard Hijacking**: Malware monitoring clipboard and replacing legitimate wallet addresses with attacker-controlled addresses
- **Hardware Wallet**: Physical device storing private keys offline, providing isolation from internet-connected computers/phones
- **HODLing**: Cryptocurrency slang for holding assets long-term rather than selling; stolen fund "HODLing" means attackers keep funds on-chain rather than immediately laundering
- **Laundering**: Process of obscuring cryptocurrency theft origin through mixers, bridges, chain-hopping, and exchanges to prevent tracing
- **LLM-AI (Large Language Model AI)**: AI tools like ChatGPT enabling sophisticated, personalized, scalable social engineering attacks
- **Multisig Wallet**: Wallet requiring multiple private keys (e.g., 2-of-3, 3-of-5) to authorize transactions, reducing single point of failure
- **Personal Wallet Compromise**: Theft targeting individual users' self-custody wallets (vs service hacks targeting exchanges/platforms)
- **Private Key**: Cryptographic secret enabling control of blockchain address; compromise means permanent fund loss
- **Seed Phrase**: 12-24 word mnemonic backing up private keys; if exposed, attacker gains full wallet access
- **Self-Custody**: User maintains direct control of private keys (vs custodial model where third party controls keys)
- **SIM Swap Attack**: Social engineering convincing mobile carrier to transfer victim's phone number to attacker-controlled SIM, bypassing SMS 2FA
- **Social Engineering**: Psychological manipulation tricking users into revealing credentials or authorizing malicious transactions
- **Wrench Attack**: Physical violence or coercion against crypto holders to force transfer of funds
