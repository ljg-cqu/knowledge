## Glossary

- **Background Check**: Investigation of employee history including criminal records, employment verification, credit checks, performed to assess trustworthiness
- **BPO (Business Process Outsourcing)**: Third-party service providers handling customer support, KYC, back-office operations for crypto platforms
- **Bribery**: Offering money or other incentives to employees/contractors to perform unauthorized actions or provide confidential information
- **Insider Threat**: Security risk posed by individuals with authorized access (employees, contractors, partners) who abuse privileges for malicious or negligent purposes
- **Multisig Hot Wallet**: Online wallet requiring multiple employee signatures to authorize withdrawals, reducing single-employee theft risk
- **PAM (Privileged Access Management)**: Tools controlling and monitoring access to critical systems, typically requiring approval workflows and session recording
- **RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)**: Access control model granting permissions based on job roles rather than individuals, enabling principle of least privilege
- **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)**: Platform aggregating security logs from multiple sources for correlation, alerting, and forensic analysis
- **Social Engineering**: Psychological manipulation tactics tricking employees into revealing credentials or approving unauthorized actions
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Security audit standard verifying controls over 6-12 month period, common compliance requirement for crypto platforms
- **UEBA (User and Entity Behavior Analytics)**: Machine learning-based anomaly detection analyzing user/system behavior patterns to identify insider threats
- **Vishing (Voice Phishing)**: Social engineering via phone calls, often impersonating IT support or executives to extract credentials
- **Zero-Trust Architecture**: Security model assuming no user/system is trusted by default, requiring continuous verification and least-privilege access
