## Glossary

- **Coercion**: Use of threats, violence, or intimidation to force victim into involuntary action (cryptocurrency transfer, private key disclosure)
- **Database Breach**: Unauthorized access to customer information (names, addresses, phone numbers) enabling perpetrator targeting (Ledger 2020: 270K+ customers)
- **Hardware Wallet**: Physical device storing private keys offline (Ledger, Trezor); provides no defense against physical coercion
- **Multisig Wallet**: Wallet requiring multiple private keys (2-of-3, 3-of-5) to authorize transactions; all keyholders targetable under coercion
- **Operational Security (OPSEC)**: Practices minimizing information exposure enabling adversary targeting (location privacy, pseudonym use, wealth display avoidance)
- **Physical Attack**: Wrench attack subset involving bodily harm (torture, mutilation, homicide) vs pure intimidation/coercion
- **Time-Lock**: Cryptographic constraint preventing transaction execution before specified future date/block height; complicates coercion but attackers may extend hostage duration
- **Victim Profiling**: Attacker reconnaissance identifying lucrative targets via database breaches, social media surveillance, blockchain analysis, conference attendance
- **Wrench Attack**: Physical violence or coercion used to bypass digital security and force cryptocurrency transfer (term from webcomic: "$5 wrench" defeats cryptography)
