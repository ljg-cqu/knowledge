## Glossary

- **Address clustering**: Algorithmic technique linking multiple blockchain addresses to single entity using transaction patterns and heuristics
- **Anonymity set**: Group of possible transaction originators, larger sets provide better privacy (plausible deniability)
- **CoinJoin**: Bitcoin privacy technique combining multiple transactions into single larger transaction, obscuring sender-recipient links
- **Discriminatory pricing**: Charging different prices based on customer's wealth/identity, enabled by blockchain surveillance
- **Fungibility**: Property where each unit of currency is interchangeable; tainted coins lose fungibility trading at discount
- **Mixer (Tumbler)**: Service pooling and redistributing cryptocurrency to break transaction linkage, faces regulatory scrutiny
- **Privacy coin**: Cryptocurrency with built-in transaction privacy (Monero, Zcash), declining exchange/merchant acceptance
- **Tainted coin**: Cryptocurrency linked to illicit activity via blockchain analysis, trades at 10-30% discount, exchange rejection risk
- **Transaction graph analysis**: Technique visualizing fund flows across blockchain to identify patterns, track stolen funds
- **Zero-knowledge proof**: Cryptographic technique proving transaction validity without revealing amounts/parties (zk-SNARKs, zk-STARKs)
