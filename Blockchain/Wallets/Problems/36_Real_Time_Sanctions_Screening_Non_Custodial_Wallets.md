## Problem Statement

   A:

This creates operational paradox: regulators expect custodial-level screening (real-time transaction monitoring, wallet risk scoring, source of funds verification, suspicious activity reporting) while wallets maintain non-custodial architecture (no transaction control, no identity verification, user-controlled private keys). ## Glossary

- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Regulations requiring financial institutions to detect and report suspicious transactions and prevent money laundering
- **Blockchain analytics**: Services analyzing public blockchain data to identify risk, track funds, and detect illicit activity (Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs)
- **Custodial wallet**: Wallet where third-party provider controls private keys and can block transactions, enabling centralized compliance
- **FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)**: U.S. Treasury bureau enforcing anti-money laundering and financial crime regulations
- **KYT (Know Your Transaction)**: Real-time transaction monitoring analyzing blockchain transactions for risk and compliance
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto assets effective 2024-2025
- **Non-custodial wallet**: Self-custody wallet where user controls private keys, provider cannot block transactions after signing
- **OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)**: U.S. Treasury office enforcing economic sanctions including cryptocurrency address sanctions
- **SDN (Specially Designated Nationals)**: OFAC sanctions list including 10,000+ cryptocurrency addresses associated with sanctioned entities
- **Tornado Cash**: Ethereum privacy protocol sanctioned by OFAC August 2022-March 2025 for money laundering facilitation
- **Travel Rule**: FATF recommendation requiring identity information exchange for transactions >$1,000/€1,000 between VASPs
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: Regulatory term for cryptocurrency businesses including exchanges, custody, and potentially wallet providers
- **Zero-knowledge proof**: Cryptographic method proving statement truth without revealing underlying data, potential privacy-preserving compliance technique
