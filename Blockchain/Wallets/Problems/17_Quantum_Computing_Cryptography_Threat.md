## Problem Statement

Timeline uncertainty (CRQC arrival 2030-2040 with high variance) creates planning challenge. "Harvest now, decrypt later" attacks already capturing all blockchain transaction data (public keys, signatures) for future decryption when quantum computers available. Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) solutions exist (NIST-standardized algorithms) but require: (1) 5-10x larger signature sizes (5-20KB vs 64-128 bytes) impacting blockchain scalability; (2) Protocol-level changes requiring network-wide coordination; (3) Migration timeline 5-10 years for ecosystem transition; (4) Wallet backward compatibility challenges during migration period. Need to achieve >80% wallet PQC readiness and begin blockchain protocol upgrades by Q4 2026 to ensure security before CRQC arrival.

## Glossary

- **CRQC (Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer)**: Quantum computer with sufficient qubits and error correction to break current cryptography
- **CRYSTALS-Dilithium**: NIST-standardized post-quantum signature algorithm, lattice-based, 2,420 byte signatures
- **ECDSA (Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm)**: Current Bitcoin/Ethereum signature scheme vulnerable to Shor's algorithm
- **EdDSA (Edwards-curve Digital Signature Algorithm)**: Modern signature scheme used by some blockchains, also quantum-vulnerable
- **Hard fork**: Blockchain protocol upgrade requiring all nodes to upgrade, incompatible with old software
- **Harvest now, decrypt later (HNDL)**: Attack strategy capturing encrypted/signed data today for future decryption when quantum computers available
- **Hybrid address**: Wallet address supporting both classical (ECC) and post-quantum cryptography during migration period
- **Logical qubit**: Error-corrected quantum bit required for meaningful computation (vs physical qubits)
- **Post-quantum cryptography (PQC)**: Cryptographic algorithms secure against quantum computer attacks
- **Public key exposure**: Blockchain addresses revealing public keys through transactions, enabling quantum attack vector
- **Shor's algorithm**: Quantum algorithm for factoring integers and computing discrete logarithms in polynomial time
- **NIST PQC**: U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology post-quantum cryptography standardization effort
