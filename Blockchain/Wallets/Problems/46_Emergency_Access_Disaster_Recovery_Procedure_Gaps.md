## Problem Statement

## Glossary

- **Disaster Recovery (DR)**: Policies, procedures, and technologies enabling recovery of critical systems and data following catastrophic failures (hardware, infrastructure, natural disasters, cyberattacks). Crypto wallets: recovery of private keys and transaction signing capability. Key metrics: RTO (recovery time objective), RPO (recovery point objective).
- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)**: Maximum tolerable duration for system downtime after disaster before business impact becomes unacceptable. Crypto custody: traditional finance 1-4h, current crypto 24-72h (target: <4h). EU DORA mandates 2-4h RTO for critical financial infrastructure.
- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)**: Maximum tolerable data loss measured in time. Crypto custody: typically 0 (no transaction loss acceptable due to immutability; state must reflect latest confirmed transactions). Achieved via real-time backup synchronization.
- **Social Recovery**: Wallet recovery mechanism distributing trust across multiple guardians (typically 3-5 individuals/services) who collectively hold key shards or approval authority. Pioneered by Argent wallet. Benefit: eliminates seed phrase single point of failure. Challenge: guardian coordination burden, trust requirements. Adoption <1%.
- **Trustless Recovery**: Cryptographic mechanisms enabling wallet recovery without relying on trusted third parties, using threshold encryption, time-locked release, or multi-party computation. Enterprise implementations allow immediate recovery post-disaster without custodian intervention. Setup cost: $50K-$200K. Adoption <10%.
- **Dead Man's Switch**: Automated mechanism releasing wallet access instructions (or key shards) to designated beneficiaries after specified inactivity period (e.g., 6-12 months no login). Used for estate planning. Challenge: false triggers (vacation, hospitalization), beneficiary crypto literacy. Adoption <5% HNWIs.
- **Incident Response Playbook**: Step-by-step documented procedures for responding to specific disaster scenarios (hardware failure, cyberattack, key compromise, natural disaster, executive incapacitation) with defined roles, escalation paths, communication protocols. Only 20-30% of custodians have comprehensive playbooks.
- **Multi-Signature (Multi-Sig)**: Wallet requiring m-of-n key holders to approve transactions (e.g., 3-of-5 signers). Used for institutional custody to prevent single points of failure. DR challenge: coordinating key holders during disasters, maintaining geographical distribution, ensuring legal access for executors/successors.
- **Hardware Security Module (HSM)**: Tamper-resistant hardware device storing cryptographic keys and performing signing operations in isolated environment. Custodians use HSMs for key security. DR requirement: geographically distributed HSM backups (multi-region redundancy), secure key replication protocols.
- **Encrypted Backup**: Cryptographic protection (AES-256, ChaCha20) applied to key material backups stored on secondary media (USB, paper, cloud). DR challenge: encryption key management (if encryption key lost, backup unusable ‚Äî 40-50% backup recovery failures stem from encryption key loss).
- **Shamir's Secret Sharing**: Cryptographic algorithm splitting secret (private key) into n shares where any m shares reconstruct secret (e.g., 3-of-5 threshold). Used for distributed backup and social recovery. Benefit: no single share compromises security; m shares required for recovery.
