## Glossary

- **Asset Segregation**: Legal and operational separation of client assets from custodian's proprietary holdings, protecting clients in custodian insolvency
- **Bank Charter**: Regulatory license (federal OCC, state trust, EU MiCA, etc.) granting institution authority to provide custody services with prudential oversight
- **Basis Points (bps)**: 1/100th of 1%; custody fees typically expressed as bps of assets under management (100 bps = 1%)
- **Cold Storage**: Cryptocurrency stored in offline wallets/devices, isolated from internet-connected systems, providing maximum security against hacks
- **Custodian**: Financial institution holding and safeguarding client assets on their behalf, distinct from execution/advisory services
- **DeFi (Decentralized Finance)**: Smart contract-based financial protocols (lending, trading, derivatives) without intermediaries
- **HSM (Hardware Security Module)**: Tamper-resistant hardware device storing cryptographic keys, often air-gapped or in secure facilities
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU regulatory framework harmonizing crypto asset treatment across 27 member states, effective 2025
- **MPC (Multi-Party Computation)**: Cryptographic technique splitting private key across multiple parties/devices, requiring threshold cooperation to sign transactions
- **OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency)**: U.S. federal bank regulator chartering national banks, issued 2021 guidance allowing crypto custody
- **Proof-of-Reserve**: Third-party attestation verifying custodian holds assets matching client balances, preventing fractional reserve
- **Prudential Regulation**: Banking oversight focused on safety/soundness (capital reserves, liquidity, risk management) to protect depositors/clients
- **Rule 206(4)-2**: SEC custody rule requiring registered investment advisers to use qualified custodians for client assets
- **SPDI (Special Purpose Depository Institution)**: Wyoming bank charter designed for digital asset custody with full reserve requirement
- **Zero-Knowledge Proof-of-Reserve**: Cryptographic method proving custodian holds sufficient assets without revealing individual account balances or addresses
