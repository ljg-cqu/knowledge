## Glossary

- **Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)**: Nation-state or state-sponsored threat actor with sophisticated capabilities, long-term objectives, and significant resources
- **APT38**: North Korea Lazarus Group subgroup specializing in financial institution compromise and cryptocurrency theft
- **Asset freezing**: Blocking stolen cryptocurrency from being transferred by coordinating with exchanges and custodians to blacklist addresses
- **Chain-hopping**: Moving stolen funds across multiple blockchains (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Monero, etc.) to obscure origin
- **Hardware Security Module (HSM)**: Tamper-resistant physical device for cryptographic key storage and operations
- **Insider threat**: Security risk from employees or trusted personnel who have legitimate access and misuse it
- **ISAC (Information Sharing and Analysis Center)**: Industry collaboration framework for sharing cyber threat intelligence
- **Lazarus Group**: North Korea state-sponsored APT group responsible for $2B+ cryptocurrency theft in 2025
- **MPC (Multi-Party Computation)**: Cryptographic technique for splitting private keys across multiple parties eliminating single point of failure
- **Peel chain**: Money laundering technique sending small amounts to new addresses in succession to obscure transaction trail
- **Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)**: Inventory of software components and dependencies for supply chain security analysis
- **Supply chain compromise**: Attacking target by compromising trusted vendors, dependencies, or development tools
- **TraderTraitor**: North Korea APT group focused on social engineering cryptocurrency company employees
- **Zero-day exploit**: Security vulnerability unknown to software vendor, providing attacker with unpatched exploit capability
