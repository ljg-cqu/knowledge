## Glossary

- **Advanced liveness detection**: Biometric verification using texture analysis, 3D depth mapping, and challenge-response to defeat deepfakes (vs basic blink/smile tests)
- **Deepfake**: AI-generated synthetic media (photos, videos, audio) impersonating real individuals to bypass biometric authentication
- **EDPB (European Data Protection Board)**: EU authority issuing GDPR guidelines, including blockchain and biometric data compliance requirements
- **GDPR Article 17 (Right to Erasure)**: EU data protection right allowing individuals to request deletion of personal data, conflicting with immutable blockchain
- **Injection attack**: Attack where pre-recorded or manipulated media is injected into camera feed to bypass liveness detection
- **Liveness detection**: Technology verifying biometric sample comes from live person vs photo, video, or synthetic media
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**: EU regulation (active 2024) requiring KYC/AML compliance for cryptocurrency exchanges and wallet providers
- **Presentation attack detection (PAD)**: System detecting spoofing attempts (photos, masks, fake fingerprints) in biometric authentication
- **vishing (voice phishing)**: Social engineering attack using phone calls, now enhanced with AI voice cloning to bypass voice biometric authentication
- **Zero-knowledge biometric verification**: Cryptographic protocol (zk-SNARKs) enabling biometric authentication without revealing or storing biometric data
