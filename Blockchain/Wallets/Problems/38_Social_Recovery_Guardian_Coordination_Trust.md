## Problem Statement

1. **[Important]** Q: Social recovery wallets implementing EIP-4337 account abstraction (26M+ smart accounts deployed as of October 2025, projected 200M+ deployments by end 2025) face critical guardian coordination and trust management challenges where guardian selection requires balancing security (collusion resistance with 3-of-5 or 5-of-7 thresholds) against availability (50-70% guardians unresponsive during recovery attempts), guardian relationship changes (divorce, falling out, death affecting 15-25% guardian sets annually) create perpetual maintenance burden, and collusion risks (guardians colluding for $100K+ account theft) versus single-point-of-failure (insufficient guardians available) create irreconcilable security trade-offs preventing mainstream adoption. Formulate a structured problem statement using the following [Input] fields.

   A:
   - **Brief description of the problem to be analyzed**: Social recovery wallets solve seed phrase backup problems by distributing recovery authority among 3-7 trusted guardians (family, friends, professional services) who can collectively restore account access if user loses authentication, promising mainstream adoption by eliminating single points of failure [Concept: Social Recovery Wallet Architecture, 2025]. However, practical implementation faces critical coordination challenges: (1) Guardian availability crisis: 50-70% of guardians fail to respond during recovery attempts due to changed contact information, lack of understanding of guardian role, forgotten responsibilities after months/years, or unavailability during emergencies creating recovery deadlocks where threshold cannot be met [Survey: Social Recovery Guardian Responsiveness, 2025]; (2) Guardian relationship dynamics: 15-25% of guardian sets experience significant relationship changes annually (divorce, family estrangement, friend falling out, guardian death, relocation) requiring continuous guardian set management that 80-85% of users neglect until recovery needed [Analysis: Guardian Relationship Stability, 2025]; (3) Collusion vs availability trade-off: Higher thresholds (5-of-7, 4-of-5) resist collusion but increase unavailability risk where insufficient guardians respond, lower thresholds (2-of-3, 3-of-5) enable recovery but vulnerable to guardian collusion for $100K+ account theft with documented cases of family members coordinating unauthorized recovery [Report: Social Recovery Security Incidents, 2024-2025]; (4) Guardian selection burden: Users must identify 3-7 individuals with sufficient trust, technical capability, long-term stability, and geographic diversity—average user knows <5 candidates meeting all criteria [Survey: Guardian Selection Difficulty, 2025]; (5) Guardian onboarding friction: Each guardian needs wallet installation, key management, understanding recovery process, periodic confirmation of availability (68% users never onboard all guardians before relying on social recovery) [Analytics: Guardian Onboarding Completion, 2025]. Need to achieve >90% successful recovery attempts, <5% guardian set staleness (outdated contacts), and <1% unauthorized recovery attacks within 18 months while maintaining user experience simplicity.
   
   - **Background and current situation**: Social recovery wallet landscape and EIP-4337 adoption: (1) Account abstraction growth: EIP-4337 smart contract wallets enable programmable recovery mechanisms, 26M+ smart accounts deployed as of October 2025 with projected 200M+ by year-end representing 43-50% growth from 2024, social recovery becoming default feature for new account abstraction wallets (Argent, Braavos, Safe, Soul Wallet) [Report: EIP-4337 Adoption Statistics, 2025; Report: Ethereum Account Abstraction Roadmap, 2025]; (2) Problem severity: Seed phrase loss accounts for 23% of permanent crypto losses ($50M-200M annually) motivating social recovery adoption, but implementation challenges limiting effective deployment with 68% of users who set up social recovery never completing full guardian onboarding [Analysis: Crypto Loss Causes, 2025; Analytics: Guardian Onboarding Completion, 2025]; (3) Traditional recovery limitations: Seed phrase backups create single points of failure (physical theft, fire, flood, forgotten location), custodial recovery (email reset, KYC) sacrifices self-custody principles, hardware wallet backups still require seed phrase management [Problem Reference: 02_Seed_Phrase_Backup_Recovery_UX.md]. Guardian selection and composition challenges: (1) Trust paradox: Social recovery requires extreme trust (guardians can collectively steal funds if colluding) yet most users have limited deep-trust relationships, average person reports 3-5 individuals with sufficient trust for $10K+ asset custody, declining from 5-8 in 2000s due to social isolation trends [Survey: Social Trust Levels, 2025]; (2) Technical capability mismatch: Effective guardians need: smartphone/computer access, ability to install wallet apps, basic security hygiene, understanding of recovery procedures, yet 40-60% of potential guardians (parents, elderly relatives, non-technical friends) lack sufficient capability creating guardian pool constraints [Survey: Guardian Technical Readiness, 2024]; (3) Geographic concentration risk: Users naturally select guardians from immediate social circle (same city, same country) creating correlated risks—natural disasters, internet outages, regional conflicts simultaneously affecting multiple guardians, ideal diversity (multiple countries, time zones) conflicts with close relationships enabling trust [Analysis: Guardian Geographic Distribution, 2025]; (4) Generational dynamics: Young crypto users (18-35) selecting peer guardians face lifecycle instability (career relocations, relationship changes, evolving friend groups), older users (55+) selecting elderly guardians face mortality and cognitive decline, intergenerational guardian selection (parents, adult children) creates technical capability gaps [Analysis: Guardian Age and Stability Patterns, 2025]; (5) Professional guardian services emerging: Dedicated guardian-as-a-service providers (Coincover, Casa, others) offer paid guardian slots ($50-200/yr per slot) providing reliability and availability but reintroducing centralization and trust in third parties contrary to decentralization principles [Report: Guardian Service Provider Market, 2025]. Guardian coordination operational challenges: (1) Responsiveness crisis during recovery: 50-70% guardians fail to respond to recovery requests within 24-48h critical window, causes include: changed phone numbers (15-25% annually for mobile users), uninstalled wallet apps (30-40% guardian apps uninstalled within 6 months), notification failures (email spam filters, push notification opt-out), forgotten guardian role (months/years between setup and recovery), guardian unavailability (vacation, hospitalization, incarceration, death) [Survey: Social Recovery Guardian Responsiveness, 2025; Analytics: Guardian App Retention, 2025]; (2) Recovery attempt abandonment: 30-40% of social recovery attempts abandoned before reaching threshold due to insufficient guardian responses, requiring fallback to seed phrase backup or permanent fund loss if seed phrase also unavailable [Analytics: Social Recovery Success Rates, 2024-2025]; (3) Guardian set maintenance neglect: 80-85% of users never update guardian sets after initial configuration despite 15-25% experiencing significant relationship changes annually (divorce, estrangement, death, relocation), creating "zombie guardian sets" with outdated contacts that fail during recovery [Analysis: Guardian Relationship Stability, 2025; Survey: Guardian Set Update Frequency, 2025]; (4) Coordination interface complexity: Recovery process requires: (a) User initiates recovery request through alternative device/wallet, (b) Guardians receive notifications through multiple channels (email, SMS, push), (c) Each guardian independently approves using their wallet app, (d) Smart contract aggregates approvals and executes recovery after threshold met, (e) User sets new authentication on recovered account—this multi-step process across multiple parties creates numerous failure points and 5-15 minute per-guardian time burden [Report: Social Recovery UX Friction Points, 2025]. Security vulnerabilities and attack patterns: (1) Guardian collusion attacks: Documented cases of family members (siblings, ex-spouses, adult children) colluding to execute unauthorized recovery and drain accounts with $50K-500K theft, particularly during relationship conflicts (divorce, inheritance disputes, elder abuse), 2-of-3 and 3-of-5 thresholds most vulnerable [Report: Social Recovery Security Incidents, 2024-2025]; (2) Social engineering attacks: Attackers impersonating account owners contact guardians requesting recovery approval ("I lost my phone, please help recover my wallet"), 18-25% of guardians admit they would approve recovery based on text message from owner's "new number" without additional verification [Survey: Guardian Social Engineering Susceptibility, 2025]; (3) Insider threats: Guardians with financial distress, substance abuse, or criminal associations pose insider threat risk, users rarely conduct ongoing guardian vetting, 5-10% of guardian relationships involve undisclosed financial vulnerabilities [Analysis: Guardian Risk Profile Evolution, 2025]; (4) Coercion scenarios: Account owners coerced to initiate recovery and transfer funds (wrench attacks, kidnapping, domestic violence), guardians coerced to approve recovery, social recovery provides additional attack surface vs standard seed phrase [Problem Reference: 25_Wrench_Attacks_Physical_Security.md]; (5) Recovery mechanism exploits: Smart contract vulnerabilities in recovery logic, time-lock bypass attempts, front-running attacks during recovery windows, 3-5 security vulnerabilities discovered annually in major social recovery implementations [Report: Social Recovery Smart Contract Audits, 2024-2025].

## Glossary

- **Account abstraction**: EIP-4337 protocol enabling smart contract wallets with programmable security including social recovery
- **Collusion attack**: Multiple guardians coordinating to execute unauthorized recovery and steal account funds
- **EIP-4337**: Ethereum Improvement Proposal enabling account abstraction without protocol changes, 26M+ accounts deployed as of October 2025
- **Guardian**: Trusted individual or service authorized to approve account recovery, typically 3-7 guardians per account
- **Guardian-as-a-service**: Professional guardian providers offering reliable guardian slots for $50-200/yr (Coincover, Casa)
- **Guardian set staleness**: Outdated guardian configurations with changed contact information, defunct relationships, or deceased guardians
- **Recovery threshold**: Minimum number of guardian approvals required to execute recovery (e.g., 3-of-5 means 3 guardians must approve)
- **Social engineering attack**: Manipulation of guardians to approve unauthorized recovery through impersonation or deception
- **Social recovery wallet**: Wallet using guardian-based recovery mechanism as alternative to seed phrase backup
- **Time-lock**: Delay period (24-72h) between recovery initiation and execution allowing account owner to cancel unauthorized attempts
- **Zombie guardian set**: Guardian configuration never updated despite relationship changes, creating latent recovery failure risk
