## Glossary

- **Multi-signature (Multisig) Wallet**: Smart contract requiring M-of-N signers to approve transactions before execution. Eliminates single private key dependency. Common configurations: 2-of-3, 3-of-5, 5-of-9. Higher threshold = more security, slower coordination.
- **Gnosis Safe**: Most popular multisig wallet implementation securing estimated $50B-$150B. Modular architecture supporting plugins, guards, and custom transaction logic. ~60-70% multisig market share. Battle-tested security.
- **M-of-N Threshold**: Signature requirement where M approvals out of N total signers needed for transaction execution. Example: 3-of-5 requires 3 signatures from 5 designated signers. Balances security vs. operational efficiency.
- **Transaction Approval Time**: Duration from transaction proposal to sufficient signatures (M-of-N) for execution. Includes signer notification, review, signing, and submission. Current baseline 6-72 hours. Critical metric for operational efficiency.
- **Signer Availability**: Percentage of signers capable of responding to signature requests within target timeframe. Affected by time zones (8-16 hour delays), travel (10-30% unavailable), competing priorities. Low availability increases transaction failures.
- **Key Rotation**: Process of replacing multisig signer keys, either for security (compromise/loss) or membership changes. Requires coordination among signers, on-chain transaction ($50-$500 gas), verification. Current time 7-30 days.
- **Treasury Management**: Organizational workflow for managing multisig funds including transaction proposals, approval workflows, spending limits, audit trails, reporting. Platforms: Multis, Coinshift. Addresses coordination complexity for frequent transactions.
- **Transaction Simulation**: Pre-execution preview showing transaction effects (balance changes, contract state updates) before signers approve. Critical for complex contract interactions. Built into Safe UI, reduces review time and approval errors.
- **Signer Response Time**: Duration from transaction notification to first signature submission. Current baseline 8-24 hours. Depends on notification channel effectiveness, signer availability, transaction urgency. Bottleneck for time-sensitive operations.
- **Emergency Transaction**: Time-critical transaction requiring rapid approval (security incident response, exploit mitigation, market opportunity). Current approval time 12-48 hours, target <1 hour. Often requires out-of-band coordination (calls, emergency channels).
