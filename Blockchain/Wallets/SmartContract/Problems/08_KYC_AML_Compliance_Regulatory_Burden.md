## Glossary

- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Regulatory framework requiring financial institutions to detect and report suspicious transactions potentially related to money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illicit activities. Includes transaction monitoring, risk assessment, and suspicious activity reporting (SARs).
- **Custodial Wallet**: Wallet service where provider controls private keys on behalf of users, creating custodial relationship. Clearly subject to KYC/AML regulations as financial intermediary. Contrasts with non-custodial (self-hosted) wallets where users control private keys.
- **DID (Decentralized Identifier)**: W3C standard for self-sovereign identity enabling users to create and control identifiers without centralized registry. Used in proposed decentralized identity solutions for crypto compliance with selective disclosure and privacy preservation.
- **FATF (Financial Action Task Force)**: Intergovernmental organization setting international standards for combating money laundering and terrorist financing. FATF Recommendation 16 (Travel Rule) requires identity information transfer for transactions >$1000/€1000.
- **FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)**: US Treasury bureau enforcing Bank Secrecy Act, AML regulations, and money transmitter requirements. Issued guidance treating certain crypto wallet providers and DeFi protocols as money service businesses requiring registration and compliance.
- **Geofencing**: Technical restriction blocking service access based on user geographic location (typically via IP address). Used by wallet providers to avoid operating in jurisdictions with uncertain or unfavorable regulations, but circumventable through VPNs.
- **KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Identity verification process requiring collection and verification of user information (name, address, date of birth, government ID documents). Regulatory requirement for financial institutions to prevent fraud, money laundering, and terrorist financing.
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)**: EU regulation effective 2025 establishing comprehensive framework for crypto asset service providers (CASPs) including wallet providers, requiring authorization, capital requirements, consumer protection, and KYC/AML compliance.
- **Non-Custodial Wallet**: Wallet where user controls private keys directly without intermediary. Regulatory treatment ambiguous: some jurisdictions exempt from KYC requirements (no custody = no regulated financial service), others propose regulation of providers offering non-custodial wallet software.
- **SAR (Suspicious Activity Report)**: Report filed by financial institutions with regulators (FinCEN in US) when transactions meet suspicious activity thresholds. Required within 30 days of detection, includes transaction details, involved parties, and reason for suspicion.
- **Self-Hosted Wallet**: Synonym for non-custodial wallet emphasizing user's direct control and custody of private keys. Contrasts with hosted/custodial wallets where provider controls keys. Regulatory treatment varies by jurisdiction with unclear enforcement mechanisms.
- **Travel Rule**: FATF Recommendation 16 requiring Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) to obtain and transmit identity information (originator and beneficiary name, account number, address) for transactions exceeding $1000/€1000. Implementation fragmented across jurisdictions with incompatible messaging protocols.
- **VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider)**: FATF term for businesses conducting operations with virtual assets on behalf of customers, including exchanges, wallet providers (custodial), transfer services. Subject to AML/KYC regulations under FATF standards, with national implementation varying.
- **Verifiable Credential**: W3C standard enabling cryptographically verifiable claims about identity attributes. Proposed for crypto compliance enabling selective disclosure (prove age >18 without revealing birthdate) and privacy-preserving KYC, but limited regulatory acceptance and adoption.
