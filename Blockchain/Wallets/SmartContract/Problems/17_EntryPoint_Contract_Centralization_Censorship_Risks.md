## Glossary

- **Bundler**: Off-chain service collecting UserOperations from users, simulating execution, bundling multiple operations into single transaction, and submitting to EntryPoint contract; operates private mempool enabling censorship.
- **Censorship Resistance**: Property ensuring valid transactions cannot be indefinitely excluded by network participants; Ethereum achieves this via PBS and inclusion lists, but ERC-4337 bypasses these mechanisms.
- **EIP-7702**: Proposed Ethereum Improvement Proposal for native account abstraction allowing EOAs to delegate validation logic to smart contracts, reducing bundler dependency compared to ERC-4337.
- **EntryPoint Contract**: Singleton smart contract (0x5FF137D4b0FDCD49DcA30c7CF57E578a026d2789) processing all ERC-4337 UserOperations; single point of failure and upgrade coordination challenge.
- **Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)**: Market concentration measure calculated as sum of squared market shares (0-10,000 scale); >2,500 indicates "highly concentrated" per FTC guidelines.
- **MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)**: Profit extractable by reordering, inserting, or censoring transactions in blocks; ERC-4337 bundlers capture 80-90% of AA-related MEV vs. distributed validator rewards.
- **PBS (Proposer-Builder Separation)**: Ethereum mechanism separating block proposal (validators) from block building (builders) to democratize MEV and enhance censorship resistance; nullified for ERC-4337.
- **UserOperation**: ERC-4337 transaction-like object representing user intent; processed by bundlers instead of direct blockchain submission, enabling account abstraction without protocol changes.
- **UserOperation Mempool**: Off-chain transaction pool maintained by bundlers where UserOperations await inclusion; private nature enables censorship without accountability.
- **Vendor Lock-in**: Dependency on specific bundler provider due to market concentration (65-75% top-3 share) limiting competition, innovation, and user choice.
- **Verifiable Censorship Resistance**: Cryptographic proof (e.g., zk-SNARK) guaranteeing transaction inclusion or providing evidence of censorship, enabling accountability for bundlers.
