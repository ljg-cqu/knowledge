## Glossary

- **BIP-39**: Bitcoin Improvement Proposal 39 defining standard for generating deterministic wallets using 12/24-word mnemonic phrases (seed phrases) that encode wallet's master private key in human-readable format.
- **Guardian**: Trusted individual or entity in social recovery system who holds partial recovery authority. Requires M-of-N guardians to approve wallet recovery (e.g., 3 of 5 guardians).
- **MPC-TSS (Multi-Party Computation Threshold Signature Scheme)**: Cryptographic protocol enabling distributed key generation and signing without ever reconstructing full private key, core technology for seedless wallets.
- **Seed phrase (Recovery phrase, Mnemonic phrase)**: 12 or 24 words representing wallet's master private key in human-readable format per BIP-39 standard, used for wallet backup and recovery. Loss or theft leads to irreversible fund loss or theft.
- **Seedless wallet**: Wallet architecture eliminating seed phrase requirement using advanced cryptography (MPC, distributed key generation), biometric authentication, and/or encrypted cloud backups to enable recovery without single point of failure.
- **Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS)**: Cryptographic algorithm splitting secret (seed) into N shares where any M shares can reconstruct the secret (M-of-N threshold), but M-1 shares reveal nothing. Basis of SLIP-39.
- **SLIP-39**: Satoshi Labs Improvement Proposal 39 providing alternative to BIP-39 using Shamir Secret Sharing to split seed into multiple shares with threshold recovery mechanism (e.g., any 3 of 5 shares recovers wallet).
- **Social recovery**: Wallet recovery mechanism distributing recovery authority among multiple trusted guardians, requiring threshold number (M-of-N) to approve account recovery, eliminating single point of failure while maintaining user control.
