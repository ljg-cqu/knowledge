## Glossary

- **CRQC (Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer)**: Quantum computer with sufficient logical qubits (1000+), fault tolerance (<10^-6 error rate), and circuit depth to break current public-key cryptography (ECDSA, RSA, ECDH) using Shor's algorithm in practical timeframes (hours to days).
- **ECDH (Elliptic Curve Diffie-Hellman)**: Key exchange protocol based on elliptic curve discrete logarithm problem, used to establish shared secrets between parties, vulnerable to quantum computers via Shor's algorithm.
- **ECDSA (Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm)**: Digital signature algorithm using elliptic curve cryptography, standard for Bitcoin and Ethereum, derives signatures from private keys to prove transaction authenticity, vulnerable to Shor's algorithm.
- **EdDSA (Edwards-curve Digital Signature Algorithm)**: Variant of ECDSA using twisted Edwards curves for improved performance, used by Solana and Cardano, equally vulnerable to quantum attacks.
- **Fault tolerance**: Ability of quantum computer to maintain low error rates (<10^-6) through quantum error correction across Clifford and non-Clifford gates during deep circuit execution.
- **FN-DSA (FFT over NTRU Digital Signature Algorithm)**: NIST-standardized post-quantum signature algorithm based on lattice problems, 1280-byte signatures, resistant to quantum attacks.
- **Hard fork**: Blockchain protocol upgrade incompatible with previous versions, requires all nodes to upgrade or network splits into two chains (e.g., Bitcoin/Bitcoin Cash 2017).
- **HNDL (Harvest Now, Decrypt Later)**: Attack strategy where adversaries collect encrypted data today (e.g., download blockchain transactions) and store for future decryption when quantum computers become powerful enough.
- **Immutability**: Core blockchain property ensuring transaction history cannot be altered or deleted once confirmed, creates irreversible quantum exposure for historical data encrypted with vulnerable algorithms.
- **Logical qubit**: Error-corrected quantum bit constructed from multiple physical qubits, provides stable computation unit for quantum algorithms, requires 100-1000 physical qubits per logical qubit depending on error correction code.
- **ML-DSA (Module Lattice Digital Signature Algorithm)**: NIST-standardized post-quantum signature algorithm based on lattice cryptography, 2592-byte signatures (40x larger than ECDSA), quantum-resistant, formerly called CRYSTALS-Dilithium.
- **Mosca's Theorem**: Security framework stating system is vulnerable if "time to migrate to PQC + data confidentiality requirement > time to build CRQC", implies blockchain data with indefinite confidentiality requirement cannot be secured against quantum threats.
- **NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)**: U.S. federal agency responsible for cryptographic standards, ran PQC competition 2017-2024, finalized ML-DSA/SLH-DSA/FN-DSA August 2024, mandates federal PQC migration by 2035.
- **Physical qubit**: Single quantum bit without error correction, subject to decoherence and gate errors, current systems have 100-1000+ physical qubits but require aggregation into logical qubits for reliable computation.
- **PQC (Post-Quantum Cryptography)**: Cryptographic algorithms designed to resist attacks from quantum computers, typically based on lattice problems, hash functions, or code-based systems, alternative to current RSA/ECC.
- **Q-Day**: Hypothetical future date when quantum computers achieve capability to break current public-key cryptography in practical timeframes, estimated 5-15 years from 2025.
- **RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman)**: Public-key encryption/signature algorithm based on integer factorization hardness, widely used for TLS and key exchange, vulnerable to Shor's algorithm.
- **Shor's algorithm**: Quantum algorithm (1994) efficiently factoring large integers and solving discrete logarithms in polynomial time, breaks RSA, ECDSA, ECDH when run on sufficiently powerful quantum computers.
- **SLH-DSA (Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Algorithm)**: NIST-standardized post-quantum signature algorithm using hash functions, 7856-byte signatures, quantum-resistant, formerly called SPHINCS+.
