## Glossary

- **CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework)**: OECD framework introducing international tax reporting obligations for crypto-asset service providers, effective January 1, 2026 in participating jurisdictions
- **Cost basis**: Original purchase price of a crypto asset used to calculate capital gains or losses for tax purposes, requiring tracking across multiple transactions and chains
- **DAC8 (Directive on Administrative Cooperation 8)**: EU directive aligning with CARF, requiring Reporting Crypto-Asset Service Providers to collect customer self-certifications and report transactions to tax authorities
- **DeFi protocol**: Decentralized finance application providing financial services (lending, trading, yield farming) without centralized intermediary, complicating tax tracking due to smart contract interactions
- **RCASP (Reporting Crypto-Asset Service Provider)**: Entity required to collect customer data and report crypto-asset transactions to tax authorities under CARF/DAC8, including exchanges, brokers, and certain wallet providers
- **Self-certification**: Tax-related identity declaration provided by customer to RCASP, including tax residency, identification number, and relevant account information for international reporting
- **Self-custody wallet**: Non-custodial cryptocurrency wallet where user controls private keys directly, not held by third-party service, theoretically exempt from KYC/AML but increasingly subject to tax reporting requirements
- **Zero-knowledge proof**: Cryptographic method allowing one party to prove statement truth without revealing underlying data, potential solution for privacy-preserving tax reporting
