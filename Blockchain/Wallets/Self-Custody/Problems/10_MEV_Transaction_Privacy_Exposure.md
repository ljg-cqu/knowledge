## Glossary

- **Back-running**: MEV strategy executing transaction immediately after target transaction to profit from resulting state changes, third component of sandwich attacks
- **Flashbots**: Organization and infrastructure provider for private transaction relay (MEV-Geth, MEV-Share), enabling users and searchers to bypass public mempool and reduce MEV extraction harm
- **Front-running**: MEV attack where bot detects profitable pending transaction, submits identical transaction with higher gas fee to execute first, profiting from victim's intended action
- **Maximal Extractable Value (MEV)**: Profit extracted by reordering, including, or excluding transactions within blockchain blocks, exploiting public mempool transparency
- **MEV-Share**: Flashbots mechanism refunding portion of extracted MEV to transaction originators, aligning incentives between searchers and users
- **Private mempool**: Transaction relay bypassing public mempool, sending transactions directly to validators/miners via trusted intermediaries (Flashbots, Eden Network, BloXroute)
- **Public mempool**: Pool of pending blockchain transactions visible to all network participants before block inclusion, enabling MEV attacks via transaction reordering
- **Sandwich attack**: MEV strategy placing transactions before (front-run) and after (back-run) victim's trade to manipulate price, extracting profit at victim's expense, now predominant MEV attack type
- **Slippage**: Difference between expected transaction execution price and actual price, artificially increased by MEV sandwich attacks (2-5x higher than expected)
