# Case Study: English Pronunciation

Generate evidence-based pronunciation assessments comparing American and British accents (plus Indian, Australian variants).

---

# Part I: Specifications

## Scope

- **Purpose**: Evaluate pronunciation using evidence-backed phonetic reasoning
- **Context**: 200–400 words per scenario (learner L1, target accent, challenges, resources)
- **Structure**: 18–22 scenarios across 5 clusters (vowels, consonants, suprasegmentals, connected speech, accent comparison); 3–5 scenarios per cluster
- **Difficulty**: 20% Foundational / 40% Intermediate / 40% Advanced
- **Tasks**: 3–4 MECE tasks each (IPA transcription, production, perception, comparison)
- **Deliverables**: Transcriptions, ≤300-word guides, minimal pair charts, accent matrices, remediation plans

## Quality

- **Coverage**: All phonemes (24 consonants, 20+ vowels), suprasegmentals, connected speech; GA/RP comparison mandatory
- **Content**: Phonetically significant only; acoustic data, formant measurements, minimal pairs from peer-reviewed research
- **Accuracy**: IPA verified against 2+ authoritative dictionaries; acoustic data from peer-reviewed sources
- **Logic**: Articulatory descriptions consistent with acoustic evidence; acknowledge variation
- **Pedagogy**: High-frequency words, L2 error patterns, intelligibility impact, actionable cues, practice drills, self-assessment

## Citations

**Sources** (≥3 types; single ≤25%): Phonetic dictionaries, peer-reviewed journals, acoustic corpora, L2 pedagogy

**Minimums**: Glossary ≥15, Tools ≥5, Literature ≥8, APA ≥15

**Format**: APA 7th; inline `[Ref: ID]`; 80% EN, 15% ZH, 5% other

**Evidence**: ≥80% scenarios cite ≥1 source; ≥40% cite ≥2

**Quality**: ≥40% sources last 5yr (≥60% for pedagogy); IPA verified with 2+ dictionaries; all `[Ref: ID]` resolve

## Validation

Run all checks; remediate failures until PASS.

Report format: `Check | Result | PASS/FAIL`. FAIL halts submission.

## Sample Scenario: GA vs RP Vowel Distinctions

### Scenario 1: TRAP-BATH-PALM Split

**Difficulty**: Intermediate | **Focus**: Vowel quality/distribution | **Profile**: Chinese L1, advanced, IELTS 7.0, 6yr GA training, targeting RP for UK studies

**Context** 

Chinese graduate student preparing for UK studies. Current GA vowel system uses /æ/ in "bath," "dance," "can't." Must acquire RP TRAP-BATH-PALM split for social integration `[Ref: G14, A2]`.

TRAP /æ/ appears in "cat," "happy," "man" (both accents). RP underwent historical split: /æ/ → /ɑː/ before fricatives (/f, θ, s/) and nasal+consonant clusters `[Ref: L6]`. Creates accent-differentiated minimal pairs: "ant" /ænt/ vs "aunt" /ɑːnt/ (RP), but both /ænt/ (or /ɑnt/) in GA. Must learn lexical distribution without overgeneralizing—"cat" stays /æ/ in both `[Ref: T1]`.

Perception good (/æ/, /ɑː/, /ɑ/ distinguished). Production challenges: (1) lexical distribution memory, (2) /ɑː/ duration + back position, (3) avoiding hypercorrection. Resources: Cambridge Dictionary, self-recording, British flatmates `[Ref: T1, T5]`.

**Task 1: Lexical Distribution (25 pts)**

Categorize 15 words by RP vowel (TRAP /æ/ vs BATH /ɑː/): *cat, bath, laugh, castle, man, dance, path, plant, mask, can, can't, fast, cash, class, grass*

- **Expected**: TRAP /æ/ (cat, man, can, cash); BATH /ɑː/ (bath, laugh, castle, dance, path, plant, mask, can't, fast, class, grass); pattern: /ɑː/ before fricatives (/f, θ, s/) + nasal+stop `[Ref: A11]`; "can" modal: weak /kən/, strong /kæn/
- **Grading**: Full (25): All correct + pattern + exceptions | Partial (18): 12-14 + partial pattern | Minimal (10): 9-11, no pattern | Zero: <9
- **GA**: All /æ/ except "father," "palm" /ɑ/ (both accents)

**Task 2: IPA Transcription (25 pts)**

Transcribe GA and RP with primary stress:
1. "I can't dance in the bath."
2. "The class laughed fast."
3. "My aunt planted grass."
4. "The castle path is long."

- **Expected**: 
  1. GA: /aɪ kænt dæns ɪn ðə bæθ/ | RP: /aɪ kɑːnt dɑːns ɪn ðə bɑːθ/
  2. GA: /ðə klæs læft fæst/ | RP: /ðə klɑːs lɑːft fɑːst/
  3. GA: /maɪ ænt plæntɪd græs/ | RP: /maɪ ɑːnt plɑːntɪd grɑːs/
  4. GA: /ðə kæsəl pæθ ɪz lɔŋ/ | RP: /ðə kɑːsəl pɑːθ ɪz lɒŋ/
  Note: /ɑː/ vs /ɑ/, /ɔ/ vs /ɒ/, length `[Ref: T1, A12]`
- **Grading**: Full (25): All correct, stress, 3+ accent differences | Partial (18): 1-3 errors, stress mostly | Minimal (10): 4-6 errors, captures split | Zero: >6 errors or no distinction

**Task 3: Production Practice (25 pts)**

Design 5-step sequence for RP /ɑː/ acquisition: perception, articulation, progression (isolated→connected), self-assessment with tech.

- **Expected**: 
  1. **Perception**: Minimal pairs /æ/ vs /ɑː/ (GA "can't" /kænt/ vs RP /kɑːnt/) with Cambridge audio `[Ref: T1]`
  2. **Articulation**: From /æ/: lower jaw, retract tongue, neutral lips, lengthen; cue: similar to Chinese /a/ but backer/longer; compare "father" /ɑ/ (shared)
  3. **Isolated**: Sustained /ɑː/, then "bath," "laugh," "dance"
  4. **Word list**: 20 high-frequency BATH words with model repetition
  5. **Sentences**: Carrier phrases ("The ___ is..."), then natural
  6. **Self-assessment**: Praat spectrogram (F1/F2, lower F2 than /æ/) `[Ref: T5, A6]`; British flatmate feedback; intelligibility test
- **Grading**: Full (25): All 5 + articulatory cues + tech + L1 transfer | Partial (18): 4 + some detail | Minimal (10): 3, lacks progression/assessment | Zero: Generic

**Task 4: Hypercorrection (25 pts)**

Identify which should NOT use /ɑː/ in RP: *crash, math, banner, after, sample, transfer, contrast, demand*

- **Expected**: NOT /ɑː/: crash /kræʃ/, banner /bænə/, sample /sæmpl/, demand /dɪmɑːnd/ | SHOULD /ɑː/: math /mɑːθ/, after /ɑːftə/, transfer /trænsˈfɜː/, contrast /kɒntrɑːst/ | Pattern: Lexically conditioned, not fully predictable `[Ref: L6]` | Variation: Some RP speakers vary (e.g., "plastic" /plæstɪk/ or /plɑːstɪk/)
- **Grading**: Full (25): Correct + pattern limits + variation | Partial (18): Mostly (1-2 errors) + exceptions | Minimal (10): Some, lacks pattern understanding | Zero: Overgeneralization

**Common Omissions**: Vowel length (RP /ɑː/ longer, not just quality); GA variation ("aunt" /ænt/ vs /ɑnt/); sociolinguistic variation (younger RP speakers)

**Bonus (+10)**: Related splits (START /ɑː/ RP vs /ɑr/ GA); Australian (similar BATH, different TRAP); historical (Great Vowel Shift, Middle English)
