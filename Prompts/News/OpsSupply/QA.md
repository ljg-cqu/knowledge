## Purpose
Generate 4–6 decision-critical Q&As from recent industry news for operations leaders. Web search; self-contained.

**Context**: Problem (fragmented news, unclear impact), scale (1M+ users or $500K+ budget), constraints (decision-critical: blocks decisions, risk >5%, ≥2 stakeholders), timeline (6mo), resources (4–6h/mo), targets (decision time ↓30-40%, risk ↑40-50%).

**Cadence**: Monthly

**Key Terms**:
- **Decision-critical**: Action required within 6mo or risk >5%
- **Supply chain cycles**: Source (procurement) → Make (production) → Deliver (logistics) → Enable (safety/compliance)
- **MECE**: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive

**Scope**: Supply disruptions, supplier risk, cost shocks, regulatory/safety changes, capacity constraints.

**Exclusions**: R&D, marketing, incremental improvements, rumors.

**Freshness**: Majority <2mo; disruptions <1mo; regulatory <6mo.

**Decision Criticality** (≥1 per Q&A):
1. **Critical**: Blocks decisions or risk >5%
2. **Important**: Affects ≥2 stakeholders, action within 6mo, or quantified impact

---

## Framework

**Stakeholders**: COO, VP Supply Chain, Plant Manager, Procurement Head, Quality Lead

**Cycles**: Source → Make → Deliver → Enable

**Categories**: Logistics Disruptions | Supplier Risk | Manufacturing & Capacity | Safety & Compliance

## Requirements

**Output**: 4–6 Q&As, 200–300w each, cited.

**Coverage**: All 4 cycles; emphasize Logistics & Supplier Risk; multiple stakeholders.

**References**: Glossary, diverse sources (≥3 types), valid URLs.

**Visuals**: ≥2 diagrams + ≥1 table.

## Execution

### 1. Discovery & Curation
**Search**: Authoritative sources (2024+):
- Trade: Supply Chain Dive, Logistics Management, IndustryWeek
- Consulting: McKinsey Supply Chain Insights, Gartner Supply Chain Top 25
- Regulatory: OSHA.gov, FDA.gov
- Industry: CSCMP's Supply Chain Quarterly, ASCM publications

**Curate**: 
- Prefer primary sources
- ≥3 source types, multiple categories
- Each Q&A meets ≥1 criticality criterion
- Verify freshness per Purpose

**Record**: Date (YYYY-MM-DD), domain, title, source, summary, category, URL, criticality criterion.

### 2. Build References
**Glossary**: Define terms in plain language with analogies. Example: "Just-in-time inventory: ordering supplies only when needed, like a restaurant buying fresh ingredients daily". Include only terms used in Q&As.

**Citations**: Use [Ref: N#][n#] inline. Format: [n#]: Source Title. Publication/Organization (Date: YYYY-MM-DD). URL (Accessed: YYYY-MM-DD). Flag uncertainty if applicable.

### 3. Generate Q&A Structure
1. ### Qn: Question
2. #### News: What/when/why; cite with date [Ref: N#][n#]
3. #### Impact: Consequences across cycles, quantified (cost, time, capacity, risk %)
4. #### Stakeholders: Roles, concerns, actions
5. #### Options & Decision: 
   - ≥2 alternatives (costs/benefits/risks)
   - Recommendation (Adopt/Investigate/Defer/Avoid) with rationale
   - Trade-offs, limitations
   - Timeline with milestones
6. #### Actions: 
   - Next steps (owners, deadlines: 2wk/2mo/6mo)
   - Success criteria (baseline → target, measurement)
7. #### References: [n#] per §2 format

### 4. Visuals
≥2 diagrams (Mermaid: risk heatmap, decision tree, impact matrix) + ≥1 comparison table (alternatives: costs/benefits/risks).

### 5. Verification
**Self-review**: Before finalizing, verify:
- ☐ URLs valid (test each); dates current (2024+)
- ☐ Calculations/metrics accurate
- ☐ No contradictions between Q&As
- ☐ Terminology matches glossary
- ☐ Each Q&A meets ≥1 criticality criterion; priority labeled
- ☐ Uncertainty flagged where applicable
- ☐ All Validation criteria met (§Validation)
