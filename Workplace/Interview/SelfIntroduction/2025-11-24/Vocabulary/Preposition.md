# Essential Prepositions for Job Interview

> **Purpose**: Quick reference guide for prepositions commonly used in technical job interviews, with examples based on your professional experience.

---

## 1. across

**Meaning**: From one side to another; spanning multiple areas/platforms

**When to use**: Describing multi-platform deployment, cross-ecosystem experience, or wide-ranging work

**Examples**:
- I deployed smart contracts **across** 12+ blockchain networks including Ethereum, BSC, and Arbitrum.
- I have cross-ecosystem experience **across** major platforms such as EVM, Polkadot, Avalanche, and Solana.
- I coordinated multiple cross-national teams **across** Singapore, the US, and Europe.

---

## 2. on

**Meaning**: Positioned at the surface of; concerning; using as a basis/platform

**When to use**: Describing work on specific platforms, technologies, or projects

**Examples**:
- I worked **on** the uniBTC Restaking protocol development.
- The system was successfully deployed **on** Avalanche Fuji testnet.
- I developed Solana on-chain NFT membership system integrated **on** IPFS storage.
- I'm focusing **on** blockchain architecture and distributed systems.

---

## 3. with

**Meaning**: Accompanied by; using; having; managing

**When to use**: Describing tools/technologies used, amounts managed, or collaboration

**Examples**:
- I have extensive experience **with** Golang, Solidity, and Rust development.
- I managed DeFi protocols **with** million-dollar TVL.
- I built Docker-based microservice architecture **with** Prometheus monitoring systems.
- I worked **with** multiple international teams to deliver blockchain solutions.

---

## 4. for

**Meaning**: Intended to belong to; during (time period); in favor of; on behalf of

**When to use**: Describing purpose, duration, responsibility, or the entity you worked for

**Examples**:
- I worked **for** Chengdu Artest Technology Co., Ltd. from 2022 to 2024.
- I was responsible **for** smart contract development and whitepaper writing.
- I designed a system **for** decentralized key management and multi-signature.
- I have 7+ years of experience **for** blockchain industry development.

---

## 5. through

**Meaning**: From beginning to end; by means of; via

**When to use**: Describing completion of full processes or methods to achieve results

**Examples**:
- I led multiple major projects **through** full lifecycle development.
- I achieved zero Gas fee transactions **through** EVM-compatible chain design.
- I significantly improved team efficiency **through** better processes and tools.

---

## 6. from...to

**Meaning**: Starting point to ending point (time, location, or range)

**When to use**: Describing time periods, transitions, or ranges

**Examples**:
- I worked at Beijing 21Vianet **from** 2020.10 **to** 2022.04 as a Blockchain Senior Engineer.
- The TVL ranged **from** thousands of dollars **to** over 4 million dollars.
- I transitioned **from** construction engineering **to** blockchain development in 2018.

---

## 7. in

**Meaning**: Within; during; using (language/medium); having skill/experience

**When to use**: Describing industry experience, time periods, locations, or skills

**Examples**:
- I have 7+ years **in** the blockchain industry.
- I'm proficient **in** multiple programming languages including Golang and Rust.
- I participated **in** code review and submitted bug fixes.
- I completed my Master's degree **in** Mechanics at Chongqing University.

---

## 8. over

**Meaning**: More than; throughout the duration; above

**When to use**: Describing quantities exceeding a threshold or management scope

**Examples**:
- I built and managed a technical team **of over** ten members.
- I successfully deployed on **over** 12 blockchain networks.
- I have **over** 7 years of experience in blockchain development.

---

## 9. of

**Meaning**: Belonging to; containing; about; made from

**When to use**: Describing possession, composition, or quantity relationships

**Examples**:
- I led a technical team **of** over ten members.
- I have deep understanding **of** blockchain fundamentals and consensus algorithms.
- The performance improved by 33% as a result **of** algorithm optimization.

---

## 10. during

**Meaning**: Throughout the time of; at some point in

**When to use**: Describing when something happened within a time period

**Examples**:
- **During** my time at Bedrock, I led the uniBTC contract development.
- **During** the project, I solved critical security vulnerabilities.
- I coordinated international teams **during** multiple cross-national projects.

---

## 11. to

**Meaning**: In the direction of; toward; until; for the purpose of; according to

**When to use**: Describing direction, purpose, comparison, or assignment

**Examples**:
- I rapidly adapted **to** emerging technology domains.
- According **to** the whitepaper, the protocol supports multiple BTC assets.
- I deployed the system **to** production environments.
- I'm committed **to** solving complex technical challenges.

---

## 12. into

**Meaning**: To the inside of; changing to become; breaking down as parts

**When to use**: Describing transformation, division, or integration

**Examples**:
- I integrated IPFS distributed storage **into** the blockchain platform.
- I broke down complex tasks **into** smaller, manageable steps.
- I transitioned **into** blockchain development in 2018.

---

## 13. between

**Meaning**: In the space separating; comparing two things; shared by

**When to use**: Describing coordination, differences, or relationships of two entities

**Examples**:
- I solved precision differences **between** multiple currencies in cross-chain systems.
- I coordinated **between** multiple international teams across different time zones.
- The choice **between** different blockchain platforms depends on specific requirements.

---

## 14. by

**Meaning**: Through the action of; not later than; near; according to

**When to use**: Describing methods, agents, deadlines, or measurements

**Examples**:
- Performance was improved **by** 33% through algorithm optimization.
- The system was recognized **by** the Lido official team.
- I reduced manual intervention **by** implementing automated trading systems.
- Projects were delivered **by** the deadline with high quality.

---

## 15. as

**Meaning**: In the role of; while; because; to the same degree

**When to use**: Describing roles, simultaneous actions, or comparisons

**Examples**:
- I served **as** a Senior Backend Developer and Technical Mentor.
- **As** a blockchain architect, I led technology selection for multiple projects.
- I worked **as** a technical consultant during the collaborative project.
- **As** the team lead, I was responsible for overall architecture design.

---

## 16. at

**Meaning**: Located in; employed by; engaged in; at a point in time

**When to use**: Describing location, employer, activity, or specific time

**Examples**:
- I worked **at** Chengdu Artest Technology Co., Ltd. for over 2 years.
- I'm proficient **at** solving complex technical challenges.
- The project started **at** the beginning of 2024.
- I excel **at** integrating heterogeneous systems.

---

## 17. about

**Meaning**: Concerning; on the subject of; approximately

**When to use**: Describing topics or approximate numbers

**Examples**:
- I authored numerous English technical documents **about** blockchain protocols.
- I have **about** 7 years of experience in the blockchain industry.
- Let me tell you **about** my experience with multi-chain deployment.

---

## 18. within

**Meaning**: Inside; not exceeding; before the end of

**When to use**: Describing constraints, limits, or boundaries

**Examples**:
- I completed the project **within** the deadline.
- The solution operates **within** the distributed system architecture.
- I maintained high on-time delivery rate **within** multiple parallel projects.

---

## 19. after

**Meaning**: Following in time; behind in position

**When to use**: Describing sequence or timing

**Examples**:
- **After** joining Bedrock Technology, I led the uniBTC development.
- **After** the code review, I submitted multiple bug fixes.
- The system performance improved significantly **after** optimization.

---

## 20. before

**Meaning**: Earlier than; in front of; prior to

**When to use**: Describing precedence in time or order

**Examples**:
- **Before** joining the blockchain industry, I worked in construction engineering.
- I completed security audits **before** deployment to production.
- **Before** making technical decisions, I evaluate multiple solutions.

---

## Additional Interview Tips

### Common Preposition Combinations

1. **responsible for** - I was responsible for smart contract development
2. **experience with/in** - I have experience with Golang and in blockchain
3. **worked on/at/with** - I worked on projects, at companies, with teams
4. **deployed to/on/across** - deployed to networks, on platforms, across chains
5. **integrated with/into** - integrated with systems, into architecture
6. **proficient in/at** - proficient in Golang, at solving problems
7. **contributed to** - I contributed to open source projects
8. **participated in** - I participated in code reviews
9. **led through** - I led projects through full lifecycle
10. **succeeded in** - I succeeded in optimizing performance

### Time Prepositions Quick Reference

- **in** 2024, in July, in the blockchain industry
- **on** Monday, on July 15th, on the platform
- **at** 3pm, at the beginning, at the company
- **from...to** - from 2022 to 2024
- **during** - during the project, during my time at
- **for** - for 7 years, for the duration of
- **by** - by 2024, by the deadline
- **within** - within 2 weeks, within the timeframe

### Technical Context Prepositions

- **on** - platforms, blockchains, testnets (on Ethereum, on Solana)
- **with** - technologies, frameworks, amounts (with Golang, with $4M TVL)
- **in** - languages, domains, systems (in Rust, in blockchain, in production)
- **across** - multiple platforms, networks, teams (across 12+ chains)
- **through** - methods, processes (through optimization, through automation)
- **for** - purpose, duration, employer (for security, for 2 years, for Bedrock)
