Strategy & Vision

Navigating regulatory uncertainty and competitive positioning

Advanced Q1.1.1
8 min read

Responding to a New, Unfavorable Regulation (MiCA)

Scenario Context

The European Union has passed MiCA regulation requiring 100% cash reserves. Your current model with mixed assets is non-compliant. CFO estimates 15% yield reduction. Regulation takes effect in 12 months.

Key Insight

This scenario tests a senior PM's ability to navigate a high-stakes, externally imposed constraint that forces a direct trade-off between regulatory compliance, financial viability, and long-term market trust.

Framework Applied

  • • Modified RICE framework for prioritization
  • • Cross-functional task force approach
  • • Risk-reward analysis

The core challenge is transforming a regulatory mandate into a strategic advantage. The first step is establishing a cross-functional "MiCA Task Force" including product, legal, finance, and engineering leads to conduct thorough impact analysis.

The strategic decision hinges on a three-pronged approach: full compliance commitment [1], revenue model re-evaluation through new value-added services, and compliance as marketing strategy positioning us as the "most regulated and secure" option in Europe.

Success Criteria

Compliance: 100% MiCA adherence by deadline
Market Share: 10% increase in EU within 18 months
Revenue: Offset 50% of lost yield within 2 years
Trust: Measurable increase in positive sentiment
Advanced Q1.2.1

Competing Against a New, Well-Funded Stablecoin

Competitive Threat

"StableX" launches with bank backing and real-time proof of reserves. Your stablecoin faces criticism over opaque audits. Key enterprise clients are questioning your transparency commitment.

The immediate reaction might be to rush a feature to match StableX's real-time proof of reserves, but a more strategic approach is needed. Instead of a knee-jerk reaction, I would charter a dedicated "Transparency Initiative" team.

My strategy would be a two-pronged defense and offense. Defensively, address transparency head-on by developing a best-in-class solution potentially using Chainlink's Proof of Reserve [2] to provide granular, verifiable data on-chain. Offensively, double down on core strengths: liquidity and utility.

Strategic Trade-offs

Investing significant resources into transparency initiative while fueling growth engine requires careful prioritization. However, turning weakness into strength reinforces core moat.

Discovery & User Research

Understanding user trust and identifying market opportunities

Intermediate Q2.1.1
9 min read

Investigating Root Cause of "Depeg" Event from User Perspective

Crisis Scenario

Stablecoin briefly dropped to $0.95 during market volatility. Social media filled with complaints and lost trust. Users calling it a "scam" while others question reserve stability.

The immediate priority is understanding the "why" behind user reactions, not just fixing technical issues. I would initiate a rapid, multi-method discovery process combining quantitative and qualitative approaches.

Quantitative analysis would segment users based on actions during depeg: who sold, who bought more (arbitrageurs), who did nothing, and who moved funds off exchanges. This identifies different user personas and risk tolerance levels.

Research Methodology

  • In-depth interviews: Understand mental models, risk perception, and specific fears [3]
  • Social media analysis: Map narrative landscape and identify key influencers
  • Behavioral segmentation: Correlate actions with user sophistication levels
Intermediate Q2.2.1
8 min read

Exploring Viability of Remittances Use Case

Market Opportunity

Exploring new use case for stablecoin in global remittances market. Need to understand market size, competitive landscape, and build business case for executive team.

The discovery process would be grounded in market research and user validation. First, quantifying the opportunity through top-down (market reports) and bottom-up (specific corridors) analysis of Total Addressable Market.

Deep-dive user research with both senders and receivers in key corridors (U.S.-Mexico, Europe-Africa) using ethnographic studies, interviews, and surveys. Research reveals critical needs like easy cash-out options for receivers [4].

Expected Outcomes

  • • Financial model with clear ROI projections
  • • Go-to-market strategy for target corridors
  • • MVP feature requirements based on user needs
  • • Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

Prioritization & Roadmapping

Balancing competing demands and managing technical debt

Intermediate Q3.1.1
7 min read

Engineering Wants Rebuild for Scalability; Business Wants New Features

Classic Dilemma

Engineering team wants to rebuild for scalability while business team pushes for new features. Need data-driven approach to justify investment in non-user-facing work.

This fundamental tension requires a strategic, data-informed approach rather than taking sides. The first step is working with engineering to quantify the "scalability" problem and translate it into business impact.

I would use the RICE framework (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) [5] to evaluate both technical initiatives and new features against the same business objectives, creating a unified backlog.

Proposed Resolution

Phased approach dedicating percentage of each sprint to scalability project while delivering high-impact new features. Demonstrates balanced approach addressing both immediate needs and long-term platform health.

Intermediate Q3.2.1
6 min read

Critical Vulnerability Found in Third-Party Bridge

Security Crisis

Critical vulnerability discovered in third-party bridge that stablecoin relies on. Requires immediate assessment of third-party risk and coordinated response across multiple teams.

This high-severity incident requires immediate action. The first step is convening a crisis response team including engineering, security, legal, and communications leads to assess the vulnerability's potential impact on user funds.

Based on risk assessment and fix timeline from third-party provider, options include temporarily suspending the bridge (high risk, long fix timeline) or issuing public warnings advising users to avoid using the bridge until patched.

Crisis Management Principles

  • • Transparency and speed in communication
  • • Regular updates to community
  • • User fund protection as top priority
  • • Close coordination with third-party provider

Metrics & Analytics

Defining success and analyzing performance

Advanced Q4.1.1
8 min read

Setting Up North Star Metric for Stablecoin Ecosystem

Strategic Measurement

Need to define single guiding metric representing core value to entire ecosystem, balancing user utility, network health, and business objectives beyond simple transaction volume or market cap.

For a stablecoin, simple metrics like "transaction volume" are insufficient as they can be gamed and don't capture core value of stability and utility. A more holistic North Star Metric would be "Trusted Global Utility for Digital Dollars."

This conceptual North Star would be measured by a composite score of four key input metrics:

1. Peg Stability Score

Measures how closely stablecoin maintains 1:1 peg over time, with lower volatility scoring higher.

2. Network Utility

Combination of active addresses, integration diversity, and non-speculative transactions.

3. Reserve Transparency Score

Qualitative and quantitative measure of clarity and accessibility of reserve attestations and audits.

4. Ecosystem Growth

Rate of growth of new applications and use cases being built on top of stablecoin.

Strategic Value

This composite NSM ensures optimization not just for growth, but for healthy, sustainable growth that reinforces core value proposition of a trusted and useful stablecoin.

Advanced Q4.2.2
8 min read

Creating Dashboard to Monitor Reserve Health and Transparency

Trust Building Initiative

Building public-facing dashboard to monitor reserve health in real-time. Critical component for building and maintaining user trust in stablecoin project.

The dashboard would provide clear, real-time, verifiable view of reserves with core metrics including total reserve value, reserve composition breakdown, stablecoins in circulation, reserve ratio, and attestation status.

To enhance trustworthiness, data would be pulled directly from custodians and banks via API where possible, using technologies like Chainlink's Proof of Reserve to provide on-chain verification of off-chain assets.

Dashboard Design Principles

  • Clarity: Simple visualizations with clear explanations
  • Accessibility: Designed for both technical and non-technical users
  • Real-time Updates: Near real-time data refresh
  • On-chain Verification: Provable reserve backing

Stakeholder Management

Internal alignment and external relations

Intermediate Q5.1.1
9 min read

Mediating Conflict Between Legal and Engineering Over Launch Timeline

Internal Conflict

Legal team flags critical compliance issue two weeks before launch. Engineering lead states last-minute changes are infeasible and risky. CEO demands on-time launch. Need to mediate and decide path forward.

The conflict pits non-negotiable regulatory compliance against engineering realities of security and stability, all under fixed market-driven deadline pressure.

First step is de-escalating by moving from confrontational debate to collaborative problem-solving session. Convene meeting with engineering lead, head of legal, and compliance team to collectively define the problem with precision.

Potential Solutions

  • Temporary off-chain solution: Multi-sig wallet as stop-gap measure
  • Limited feature launch: Smaller initial supply to limit exposure
  • External security audit: Rapid assessment of proposed changes
  • Phased implementation: Gradual rollout of compliance features

The GENIUS Act requires stablecoin issuers to have technical capability to freeze tokens when legally required [6] [7]. This legal requirement must be balanced against technical feasibility and launch timelines.

Advanced Q5.2.1
9 min read

Managing Crisis Communication Plan After Security Breach

Security Crisis

Security breach occurs with $30 million stolen, similar to Tether's 2017 incident. Existential threat requiring coordinated response across product, engineering, legal, and communications teams.

The immediate priority is executing a pre-defined incident response plan. First step is containing the breach and preventing further loss of funds. In the Tether case, this involved quickly identifying compromised wallet and coordinating with exchanges to blacklist the stolen tokens [8].

Simultaneously, clear and transparent external communication is paramount. Initial announcement should be made as soon as breach is confirmed and immediate threat contained, acknowledging the incident and outlining mitigation steps.

Crisis Communication Principles

  • Speed: Communicate as soon as threat is contained
  • Transparency: Factual acknowledgment of incident
  • Empathy: Acknowledge user fear and frustration
  • Follow-through: Detailed post-mortem and prevention measures

Go-to-Market & Growth

Launching, scaling, and building trust

Intermediate Q6.1.1
7 min read

Planning Go-to-Market Strategy for New Blockchain Integration

Launch Strategy

Launching stablecoin on new blockchain requires comprehensive GTM plan coordinating marketing, business development, and community efforts to drive adoption beyond technical deployment.

The GTM strategy would be built around a "liquidity-first" approach. First step is partnering with at least one major centralized exchange (CEX) and one major decentralized exchange (DEX) on the new chain to ensure deep liquidity from day one.

Second step is targeted marketing campaign creating educational content about benefits (lower fees, faster transactions) and running targeted ads on crypto-native platforms. Third step is community engagement through AMAs, dedicated Discord channels, and influencer partnerships.

Success Metrics

TVL: Total value locked on new chain
Active Addresses: Number of unique users
Transaction Volume: Activity within first 90 days
Advanced Q6.2.1
8 min read

Developing Communication Strategy to Rebuild Trust After "Depeg"

Trust Restoration

After depeg event, need communication strategy that is transparent, empathetic, and proactive to rebuild user confidence and repair damaged brand.

Rebuilding trust requires communication strategy that is transparent, empathetic, and proactive. First step is acknowledging the issue quickly and honestly without defensive response.

Second step is providing regular, detailed updates through blog or social media channels, explaining progress and expected timeline for full resolution. Third step is demonstrating empathy by acknowledging user fear and frustration through CEO messages and active community engagement.

Long-term Trust Building

  • Commitment to Change: Concrete steps to prevent future incidents
  • Independent Audit: Third-party verification of systems
  • Transparency Dashboard: Real-time reserve monitoring
  • User Education: Better understanding of risk management

The goal is turning crisis into opportunity to become more trustworthy and resilient organization, demonstrating that trust is earned through actions, not just promises.
