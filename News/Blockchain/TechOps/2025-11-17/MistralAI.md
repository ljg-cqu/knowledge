## References

### Glossary (G)

- **DApps (Decentralized Applications)**: Applications that run on a peer-to-peer network without any central authority or middlemen, using blockchain as their base layer to store data and verify transactions.
- **Gas**: A fee paid to node operators on the Ethereum network to process transactions, often denominated in WEI.
- **Gwei**: The smallest denomination of the cryptocurrency ETH, where 1 ETH is worth 1 billion Gwei.
- **HODL**: An acronym for "Hold On for Dear Life," used to encourage holding onto crypto assets during market fluctuations.
- **ERC-20 and ERC-721**: Ethereum Request for Comments (ERC) standards that define the rules and conditions for cryptocurrency tokens and non-fungible tokens (NFTs) on the Ethereum blockchain.
- **PoW (Proof of Work)**: A consensus mechanism where participants submit proof of computational effort to validate transactions and create new blocks.
- **PoS (Proof of Stake)**: A consensus mechanism where participants stake their crypto to become validators of transactions within a blockchain network.
- **DeFi (Decentralized Finance)**: Financial services and products built on blockchain technology, accessible to all without centralized control.
- **DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology)**: A broad term covering systems like blockchain used to create and manage decentralized, public ledgers of transactions.
- **Oracle**: An agent that finds and verifies real-world occurrences and submits this information to a blockchain for use by smart contracts.
- **Governance**: The processes and mechanisms through which decisions are made on key risk parameters and proposed improvements in a blockchain system.
- **Genesis Block**: The first block in a blockchain, also referred to as 'block zero'.
- **ASIC (Application-Specific Integrated Circuit)**: Specialized hardware designed for mining cryptocurrencies, optimized for specific mining algorithms.
- **NGMI**: An acronym for "Not Gonna Make It," used to refer to a campaign or token unlikely to attain high value.
- **TVL (Total Value Locked)**: A metric used to determine the value of a blockchain network by measuring the total value of assets locked in its protocols.
