1. Q: Layer-2 migration can reduce costs 80-95% and increase throughput 100x-1000x, yet the analysis recommends a "phased" 3-6 month approach rather than immediate full migration. What does this reveal about the relationship between obvious economic benefits and execution risk?
   A: **False dichotomy awareness**: The recommendation challenges "if ROI is obvious, move fast" thinking—recognizing that even correct strategic directions have execution risk (security vulnerabilities, state migration bugs) that can erase gains or destroy the business. **Risk asymmetry insight**: The analysis implicitly weighs upside (80-95% cost savings) against downside (potential security exploit losing 100% of user funds)—when downside is existential and upside is incremental, slow de-risked execution beats fast execution even with strong ROI. **Systems thinking**: Reveals that technology decisions aren't just math problems (cost/benefit) but integration challenges where interactions between components (Layer-1 contracts, Layer-2 execution, bridge security, user experience) create non-obvious failure modes. **Application to other decisions**: This caution applies beyond tech—any major operational change (moving jurisdictions, replacing core systems, restructuring teams) benefits from piloting and staged rollout even when the end-state is clearly superior. Question to ask: "What could go catastrophically wrong if we're 95% right but miss a critical edge case?"
